## 1. What Line 10 is

Form 1040 line 10 is labeled **"Adjustments to income from Schedule 1, line 26"**. It is a pure carry-over from Schedule 1. It is then subtracted from line 9 (total income) to produce line 11a (adjusted gross income, AGI).

```
Form1040.line10 = Schedule1.line26   (single source of truth)
```

Schedule 1, Part II (the part that feeds Line 10) is in turn the sum of 14 individual adjustment lines plus a "less common" subtotal:

```
Schedule1.line25 = sum of lines 24a + 24b + 24c + 24d + 24e + 24f + 24g
                       + 24h + 24i + 24j + 24k + 24z

Schedule1.line26 = sum of lines 11 + 12 + 13 + 14 + 15 + 16 + 17
                       + 18 + 19a + 20 + 21 + 22 + 23
                       + line25
```

Notes on the structure:
- Lines **22** is **"Reserved for future use"** on the 2025 form — it is always 0.
- Lines **19b** (recipient Social Security Number, SSN) and **19c** (date of original divorce or separation agreement) are textual disclosure fields, not numeric, so they do not enter the sum.
- Letters **24l through 24y** do **not exist** on the 2025 Schedule 1 — the form jumps from 24k directly to 24z.

## 2. Plain-English computation per sub-line (the IRS rules — independent of our code)

For each sub-line below, the inputs come from the per-person input form `form-income-adjustments-taxpayer.xlsx` (and the matching spouse form). On a joint return, the taxpayer-side and spouse-side amounts are added together. On a Married Filing Separately (MFS) return, the spouse-side amounts must be excluded entirely (so the spouse's data on their own return is not also claimed on the taxpayer's return).

In the formulas below, when a name like `educatorExpensesLine11` appears, the **same field name** lives on both the taxpayer's and the spouse's input form and we sum them.

### Line 11 — Educator expenses

```
line11 = min(
    educatorExpensesLine11_taxpayer (if taxpayer is an eligible educator),
    EDUCATOR_EXPENSES_CAP_PER_PERSON
  )
  + min(
    educatorExpensesLine11_spouse (if spouse is an eligible educator AND MFJ),
    EDUCATOR_EXPENSES_CAP_PER_PERSON
  )
```

Reference data for 2025:
- `EDUCATOR_EXPENSES_CAP_PER_PERSON = $300`
- The combined MFJ maximum is therefore `$600` only when both spouses are eligible educators; one eligible spouse is still capped at `$300`.

**Eligible educator** means a kindergarten-through-grade-12 teacher, instructor, counselor, principal, or aide who worked at least **900 hours** during the school year. Source: 2025 Form 1040 Instructions, page for Schedule 1 line 11; IRC (Internal Revenue Code) section 62(a)(2)(D).

### Line 12 — Certain business expenses of reservists, performing artists, and fee-basis government officials

```
line12 = certainBusinessExpensesLine12_taxpayer (from Form 2106)
       + certainBusinessExpensesLine12_spouse    (from Form 2106, if MFJ)
```

This is **not** a general unreimbursed-employee-expense deduction. It is allowed only for: Armed Forces reservists traveling more than 100 miles from home for reserve duties, qualified performing artists, and fee-basis state or local government officials. Source: 2025 Form 1040 Instructions; Form 2106 instructions.

### Line 13 — Health Savings Account (HSA) deduction

```
line13 = hsaDeductionLine13_taxpayer (from Form 8889)
       + hsaDeductionLine13_spouse    (from Form 8889, if MFJ)
```

Excludes employer contributions, rollovers, and qualified HSA funding distributions from an Individual Retirement Account (IRA). Source: Form 8889 instructions; IRC §223.

### Line 14 — Moving expenses for Armed Forces

```
line14 = movingExpensesArmedForcesLine14_taxpayer (from Form 3903)
       + movingExpensesArmedForcesLine14_spouse    (from Form 3903, if MFJ)
```

Allowed only for members of the Armed Forces on active duty who moved because of a permanent change of station. The general moving-expense deduction was eliminated for non-military taxpayers by the Tax Cuts and Jobs Act (TCJA) of 2017. Source: 2025 Form 1040 Instructions; Form 3903 instructions; IRC §217.

### Line 15 — Deductible part of self-employment tax

```
line15 = deductibleSelfEmploymentTaxLine15_taxpayer (from Schedule SE)
       + deductibleSelfEmploymentTaxLine15_spouse    (from Schedule SE, if MFJ)
```

Schedule SE computes self-employment tax and the deductible half. This application's `CLAUDE.md` says self-employment is out of scope, so this line should either route the user to a tax professional or be enforced as zero. Source: Schedule SE instructions; IRC §1401.

### Line 16 — Self-employed Simplified Employee Pension (SEP), Savings Incentive Match Plan for Employees (SIMPLE), and qualified plans

```
line16 = selfEmployedRetirementPlansLine16_taxpayer
       + selfEmployedRetirementPlansLine16_spouse (if MFJ)
```

Same out-of-scope note as line 15. Source: 2025 Form 1040 Instructions; Publication 560.

### Line 17 — Self-employed health insurance deduction

```
line17 = selfEmployedHealthInsuranceLine17_taxpayer (worksheet or Form 7206)
       + selfEmployedHealthInsuranceLine17_spouse    (if MFJ)
```

For ordinary cases the user runs the Self-Employed Health Insurance Deduction Worksheet in the Form 1040 Instructions. **Form 7206** is required when: there are multiple sources of self-employment income; the taxpayer files Form 2555 (Foreign Earned Income); or qualified long-term-care insurance amounts are claimed. Use IRS Publication 974 if the plan was bought through the Health Insurance Marketplace and the Premium Tax Credit (PTC) applies. Source: 2025 Form 1040 Instructions; IRC §162(l).

### Line 18 — Penalty on early withdrawal of savings

```
line18_imported = sum of "earlyWithdrawalPenaltyAmount" across all uploaded
                  Form 1099-INT entries
                + sum of "earlyWithdrawalPenaltyAmount" across all uploaded
                  Form 1099-OID entries

line18 = line18_imported (preferred — from box 13 of 1099-INT / 1099-OID)
       + penaltyOnEarlyWithdrawalOfSavingsLine18_taxpayer (manual override)
       + penaltyOnEarlyWithdrawalOfSavingsLine18_spouse    (manual override, if MFJ)
```

This is the bank-imposed penalty for breaking a Certificate of Deposit (CD) or similar instrument before maturity. It is reported in **box 13** of Form 1099-INT or Form 1099-OID. Source: 2025 Form 1040 Instructions; IRS Publication 550.

### Line 19a — Alimony paid (with line 19b SSN, line 19c agreement date)

```
allowed = (alimonyAgreementDateLine19c <= 2018-12-31)
        AND (agreement not later modified to drop the taxable-to-recipient rule)

line19a = if allowed:
            alimonyPaidLine19a_taxpayer + alimonyPaidLine19a_spouse (if MFJ)
          else:
            0   (the deduction is disallowed for post-2018 agreements)
```

Reference data:
- `ALIMONY_DEDUCTIBLE_AGREEMENT_LATEST_DATE = 2018-12-31` (Tax Cuts and Jobs Act of 2017 effective date).

Lines 19b and 19c are mandatory text/date disclosure fields when line 19a is non-zero — the recipient's Social Security Number and the month and year of the original divorce or separation agreement. Source: 2025 Form 1040 Instructions; IRC §215 (as in effect before TCJA repeal); IRS Publication 504.

### Line 20 — Individual Retirement Account (IRA) deduction

```
line20 = iraDeductionLine20_taxpayer  (from IRA deduction worksheet)
       + iraDeductionLine20_spouse     (if MFJ)
```

Excludes rollover contributions and non-deductible contributions (the latter go on Form 8606 instead). If filing MFS and the taxpayer lived apart from the spouse for the entire year, a check-box on line 20 should be set (otherwise the income limit drops sharply). Source: IRS Publications 590-A and 590-B; IRC §219.

### Line 21 — Student loan interest deduction

This line has both a **dollar cap** and a **Modified Adjusted Gross Income (MAGI) phase-out**, plus **eligibility blockers**. Pseudocode:

```
# 1. Eligibility
if filing_status == MFS:
    line21 = 0   # IRC §221(e)(2) — fully disallowed for MFS
elif taxpayer_or_spouse_is_someone_elses_dependent:
    line21 = 0
else:
    # 2. Raw amount paid (cap at the per-return dollar limit)
    rawPaid = sum of "imported1098EBox1StudentLoanInterestTotal"
            + sum of "studentLoanInterestReceivedAmount" on 1099-E-like entries
            + studentLoanInterestDeductionLine21_taxpayer (manual override)
            + studentLoanInterestDeductionLine21_spouse    (manual override, if MFJ)

    capped = min(rawPaid, STUDENT_LOAN_INTEREST_DEDUCTION_CAP)

    # 3. MAGI phase-out
    magi = AGI (recomputed without this deduction)
         + any foreign earned income exclusion add-back
         + any foreign housing exclusion add-back
         + any Puerto Rico income exclusion add-back

    if magi <= phaseout_start[filing_status]:
        line21 = capped
    elif magi >= phaseout_end[filing_status]:
        line21 = 0
    else:
        excess  = magi - phaseout_start[filing_status]
        range   = phaseout_end[filing_status] - phaseout_start[filing_status]
        line21  = capped * (1 - excess / range)
        # round to nearest whole dollar per IRS worksheet rounding
```

Reference data for 2025:
- `STUDENT_LOAN_INTEREST_DEDUCTION_CAP = $2,500` (per return, not per loan)
- `phaseout_start = { Single: $85,000 ; HOH (Head of Household): $85,000 ; QSS (Qualifying Surviving Spouse): $85,000 ; MFJ: $170,000 }`
- `phaseout_end   = { Single: $100,000 ; HOH: $100,000 ; QSS: $100,000 ; MFJ: $200,000 }`
- `MFS filers: ineligible (line21 = 0 regardless of income)`

Use IRS Publication 970 instead of the worksheet when Form 2555 or Form 4563 is filed, or when Puerto Rico income is excluded. Source: 2025 Form 1040 Instructions; IRC §221.

### Line 22 — Reserved for future use

```
line22 = 0   (always — the IRS reserved this line on the 2025 form)
```

### Line 23 — Archer Medical Savings Account (MSA) deduction

```
line23 = archerMsaDeductionLine23_taxpayer (from Form 8853)
       + archerMsaDeductionLine23_spouse    (from Form 8853, if MFJ)
```

Source: Form 8853 instructions; IRC §220.

### Line 24a — Jury duty pay given to employer

```
line24a = juryDutyPayGivenToEmployerLine24a_taxpayer
        + juryDutyPayGivenToEmployerLine24a_spouse (if MFJ)
```

Only the amount actually turned over to an employer that kept paying the employee's salary during jury service. The matching income inclusion is on Schedule 1 line 8h. Source: IRS Publication 525.

### Line 24b — Deductible expenses tied to personal-property rental income on line 8l

```
line24b = expensesRelatedToPersonalPropertyRentalLine24b_taxpayer
        + expensesRelatedToPersonalPropertyRentalLine24b_spouse (if MFJ)
```

Capped at the line-8l income (so the activity does not create a net loss on Schedule 1 from this pairing).

### Line 24c — Non-taxable Olympic / Paralympic medals and United States Olympic Committee (USOC) prize money

```
allowed_exclusion = (parent_or_self_AGI <= OLYMPIC_EXCLUSION_AGI_CEILING[filing_status])

line24c = if allowed_exclusion:
            nontaxableOlympicParalympicLine24c_taxpayer + spouse equivalent
          else:
            0
```

Reference data for 2025:
- `OLYMPIC_EXCLUSION_AGI_CEILING_DEFAULT = $1,000,000`
- `OLYMPIC_EXCLUSION_AGI_CEILING_MFS     = $500,000`

This line backs out the income inclusion on Schedule 1 line 8m. Source: IRC §74(d).

### Line 24d — Reforestation amortization and expenses

Direct user entry. Source: IRC §194.

### Line 24e — Repayment of supplemental unemployment benefits (Trade Act of 1974)

Direct user entry. Specifically for repayments under the Trade Act of 1974 — not a generic unemployment repayment.

### Line 24f — Contributions to section 501(c)(18)(D) pension plans

Direct user entry. These are contributions to certain employer-funded plans created before June 25, 1959.

### Line 24g — Contributions by certain chaplains to section 403(b) plans

Direct user entry. IRC §403(b)(2)(B).

### Line 24h — Attorney fees and court costs for certain unlawful-discrimination claims

```
line24h = min(
    attorneyFeesUnlawfulDiscriminationLine24h_taxpayer,
    gross_income_from_the_action_taxpayer
)
+ same for spouse if MFJ
```

Capped by the gross income from the discrimination action that the taxpayer received in the year. Source: IRC §62(a)(20).

### Line 24i — Attorney fees and court costs for Internal Revenue Service (IRS) whistleblower awards

```
line24i = min(
    attorneyFeesIrsWhistleblowerLine24i_taxpayer,
    whistleblower_award_amount_in_gross_income_taxpayer
)
+ same for spouse if MFJ
```

Capped by the award amount included in gross income. Source: IRC §62(a)(21).

### Line 24j — Foreign housing deduction (Form 2555)

```
line24j = housingDeductionForm2555Line24j_taxpayer + spouse (if MFJ)
```

This is the **housing deduction** from Form 2555, distinct from Schedule 1 line **8d** (the foreign earned income exclusion). Do not confuse the two. Source: Form 2555 instructions; IRC §911(c)(4).

### Line 24k — Excess deductions under section 67(e) from Schedule K-1 (Form 1041)

```
line24k = excessDeductionsSection67eLine24k_taxpayer  (Form 1041 K-1 box 11 code A)
        + excessDeductionsSection67eLine24k_spouse     (if MFJ)
```

Source: Schedule K-1 (Form 1041) instructions; IRC §67(e).

### Line 24z — Other adjustments (write-in)

```
line24z = sum of (description, amount) pairs that a SPECIFIC current IRS
          instruction directs the taxpayer to put on line 24z
```

**Important behavioral rule:** the 2025 Form 1040 Instructions tell taxpayers to leave line 24z blank **unless** another IRS instruction specifically directs a write-in adjustment. This line is **not** a generic catch-all for any extra deduction the taxpayer wants to claim. Source: 2025 Form 1040 Instructions; this is verified in the local copy at `i1040gi_2025.pdf`.

### Final aggregation

```
line25 = line24a + line24b + line24c + line24d + line24e + line24f + line24g
       + line24h + line24i + line24j + line24k + line24z

line26 = line11 + line12 + line13 + line14 + line15 + line16 + line17
       + line18 + line19a + line20 + line21 + line22 + line23
       + line25

Form1040.line10 = line26
```

## 3. Computation as implemented in the code (`TaxReturnComputeService.java`)

Entry point: `computeIncomeAdjustments(...)` at approximately line 13354. It populates a `Schedule1Adjustments` output object whose `totalAdjustmentsToIncome` field is what Form 1040 line 10 ultimately reads.

### Step-by-step transcription of what the code does

```
# Spousal data shadowing for MFS — this works correctly.
incomeAdjustmentsSpouse = null     if isMfsReturn else incomeAdjustmentsSpouseRaw

# Out-of-scope blocking flags for self-employment lines 15 / 16 / 17.
# Flags are emitted with blocking=true (so compute fails downstream) but
# field aggregation continues.
if (taxpayer.hasDeductibleSelfEmploymentTaxLine15OutOfScope == true)
    OR (spouse.hasDeductibleSelfEmploymentTaxLine15OutOfScope == true):
    emit INCOME_ADJUSTMENTS_LINE15_OUT_OF_SCOPE blocking flag
# Same pattern for line 16 + line 17.

# Per-line aggregation: taxpayer + spouse (spouse is null on MFS).
line11  = taxpayer.educatorExpensesLine11 + spouse.educatorExpensesLine11
line12  = taxpayer.reservistPerformingArtistFeeBasisExpensesLine12 + spouse.…
line13  = taxpayer.hsaDeductionLine13 + spouse.…
line14  = taxpayer.movingExpensesArmedForcesLine14 + spouse.…
line15  = taxpayer.deductibleSelfEmploymentTaxLine15OutOfScopeAmount + spouse.…
line16  = taxpayer.selfEmployedRetirementPlanAdjustmentLine16OutOfScopeAmount + …
line17  = taxpayer.selfEmployedHealthInsuranceAdjustmentLine17OutOfScopeAmount + …

# Line 18 sources three places: manual override + imported summaries +
# direct sum across uploaded 1099-INT and 1099-OID entries.
line18_manual     = taxpayer.penaltyOnEarlyWithdrawalOfSavingsLine18 + spouse.…
line18_imported   = taxpayer.imported1099IntEarlyWithdrawalPenaltyTotal + …
                  + taxpayer.imported1099OidEarlyWithdrawalPenaltyTotal + …
line18_statements = sum(form1099IntEntries[i].earlyWithdrawalPenaltyAmount)
                  + sum(form1099OidEntries[i].earlyWithdrawalPenaltyAmount)
line18 = line18_manual + line18_imported + line18_statements

line19a = taxpayer.alimonyPaidLine19a + spouse.…
line19b = first non-blank of (taxpayer.alimonyRecipientSsnLine19b,
                              spouse.alimonyRecipientSsnLine19b)
line19c = first non-blank of (taxpayer.alimonyAgreementDateLine19c,
                              spouse.alimonyAgreementDateLine19c)

line20 = taxpayer.iraDeductionLine20 + spouse.…

# Line 21 sources three places — but NO cap, NO phase-out, NO MFS block.
line21_manual   = taxpayer.studentLoanInterestDeductionLine21 + spouse.…
line21_imported = taxpayer.imported1098EBox1StudentLoanInterestTotal + spouse.…
                + sum(form1099EEntries[i].studentLoanInterestReceivedAmount)
line21 = line21_manual + line21_imported

line22 = null      # hard-coded null per "Reserved for future use"

line23 = taxpayer.archerMsaDeductionLine23 + spouse.…

# Lines 24a through 24k follow the same taxpayer + spouse pattern.
line24a = taxpayer.otherAdjustmentJuryDutyPayLine24a + spouse.…
line24b = taxpayer.otherAdjustmentDeductibleExpensesRelatedTo8lLine24b + …
line24c = taxpayer.otherAdjustmentNontaxableOlympicParalympicLine24c + …
line24d = taxpayer.otherAdjustmentReforestationAmortizationLine24d + …
line24e = taxpayer.otherAdjustmentTradeActRepaymentLine24e + …
line24f = taxpayer.otherAdjustmentContributions501c18dLine24f + …
line24g = taxpayer.otherAdjustmentChaplain403bContributionsLine24g + …
line24h = taxpayer.otherAdjustmentAttorneyFeesUnlawfulDiscriminationLine24h + …
line24i = taxpayer.otherAdjustmentAttorneyFeesIrsAwardLine24i + …
line24j = taxpayer.otherAdjustmentHousingDeductionForm2555Line24j + …
line24k = taxpayer.otherAdjustmentExcessDeductionsSection67eLine24k + spouse.…
        + taxpayer.importedK1Section67eExcessDeductionsTotal + spouse.…

# Line 24z is built by walking each per-row description+amount item the user
# entered, prefixing the description with "Taxpayer:" or "Spouse:".
line24z = sum of (entry.amount) over all rows whose description is non-blank

line25 = line24a + … + line24k + line24z   (null-as-zero summation)
line26 = (line11 + line12 + … + line23) + line25
                                     (outer is null-preserve, inner null-as-zero)

Form1040.line10 = line26
```

The output is stored into `Schedule1Adjustments.totalAdjustmentsToIncome` and propagated to `form1040.adjustments.adjustmentsSchedule1` and then to AGI.

## 6. Input-field cross-reference (alphabetical, user-facing field name from `form-income-adjustments-taxpayer.xlsx` / `…-spouse.xlsx`)

Each of these is read with `getAmount(incomeAdjustmentsTaxpayer, "…")` and the matching spouse field with `getAmount(incomeAdjustmentsSpouse, "…")`.

| Field name (input form) | Drives Schedule 1 line |
|---|---|
| `educatorExpensesLine11` | 11 |
| `reservistPerformingArtistFeeBasisExpensesLine12` | 12 |
| `hsaDeductionLine13` | 13 |
| `movingExpensesArmedForcesLine14` | 14 |

## 8. Missing input fields the application does not yet collect

These are the fields the application would need before it can correctly enforce all the IRS rules listed in §2 above:

1. **`taxpayerIsEligibleEducator` and `spouseIsEligibleEducator`** (boolean) — gates whether line 11 is allowed at all and whether the MFJ-both-qualify $600 cap applies. Today the app trusts the user-entered amount and applies no cap.
2. **`taxpayerIsClaimedAsDependent` / `spouseIsClaimedAsDependent`** (boolean) — needed to disallow line 21 student loan interest when the taxpayer or spouse is someone else's dependent.
3. **`line21EligibilityFlag` derivation from filing status** — the MFS disallow rule for line 21 needs a guard that zeroes out line 21 when `filingStatus == MARRIED_FILING_SEPARATELY`. The current null-shadow only protects against spouse leakage, not against the MFS taxpayer claiming the deduction on their own.
4. **`form2555HousingExclusionAddBack` / `form4563IncomeAddBack` / `puertoRicoExclusionAddBack`** — operands of the MAGI used in the line 21 phase-out worksheet. Modified AGI ≠ AGI for this line.
5. **`line19aAgreementModificationFlag`** — captures whether a pre-2019 agreement was later modified to drop the taxable-to-recipient rule. The form should at minimum collect the agreement date; today there is no validator that disallows line 19a when the date is after 2018-12-31.
6. **`line24h_grossIncomeFromAction`** and **`line24i_whistleblowerAwardInGrossIncome`** — the income caps for the attorney-fee adjustments. Today the user could enter $50,000 of attorney fees against a $10,000 award.
7. **`line20MfsLivedApartAllYear`** — already-present-in-the-app boolean (used by line 4abc IRA forms) needs to be wired into the line 20 IRA deduction worksheet check-box on Schedule 1.
8. **`line24zAuthorizedTypes`** — an enumerated list of IRS-authorized line 24z write-in types (e.g., "Section 87 alcohol-fuel credit", etc.). The current free-form list cannot enforce the "only when a specific IRS instruction directs it" rule.

## 10. Summary verdict

The structural arithmetic for line 10 is implemented correctly: the right sub-lines feed the right totals, the MFS spouse-shadow is in place, and self-employment lines 15 / 16 / 17 are appropriately blocked. The compute returns null when no Part II input exists (good — Schedule 1 is then not generated).

**However**, several IRS-mandated *limits, caps, and eligibility rules* are not enforced anywhere in the back-end:

- **Educator expenses ($300/$600 cap)** — silent over-deduction risk.
- **Alimony paid post-2018 disallowance** — silent over-deduction risk; high-dollar audit risk.
- **Student loan interest cap ($2,500), MAGI phase-out ($85k/$170k start), MFS disallow, dependent disallow** — four separate gaps; high-dollar over-deduction risk; the audit comment in the source claims the MFS block is enforced but only spouse data is shadowed — the taxpayer's own amount still flows through.
- **Olympic / Paralympic AGI ceiling** — constants exist but are not consulted at line 24c.
- **Line 24h / 24i income caps** — not enforced.
- **Line 24z free-form** — backend accepts arbitrary write-ins; only the UI advisory keeps this honest.

These are not arithmetic mistakes; they are **missing eligibility and limit enforcement**. The arithmetic implementation should be regarded as "correct only when the user enters values that already comply with the IRS limits". Until the gaps above are closed, the app can compute and file a return that the IRS will subsequently reject or adjust.

Top three to close first, in priority order of dollar exposure and IRS-audit likelihood:

1. **Student loan interest** (line 21) — apply the $2,500 cap, the MAGI phase-out, and the MFS disallow.
2. **Alimony paid** (line 19a) — block when the agreement date is after 2018-12-31.
3. **Educator expenses** (line 11) — clamp to $300 per eligible educator.

Once these are in `ReferenceData` and enforced at compute time, the structural correctness of line 10 documented in §3 will translate to *actual* correctness of the filed return.
