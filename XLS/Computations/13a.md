**One-line summary of line 13a:** the smaller of (a) 20% of qualified business income (with limitations) plus 20% of qualified Real Estate Investment Trust (REIT) dividends and qualified Publicly Traded Partnership (PTP) income, OR (b) 20% of the taxpayer's taxable income minus net capital gain and qualified dividends — never less than zero.

---

## 1. What Line 13a is

Form 1040 line 13a is labeled **"Qualified business income deduction from Form 8995 or Form 8995-A"**. It is a one-line carry-over: whichever of those two forms applies to the taxpayer's situation, the bottom-line deduction from that form is what appears on line 13a.

```
Form1040.line13a = Form8995.line15  (when the simpler Form 8995 applies)
                 OR
                 Form8995A.line39    (when the full Form 8995-A applies)
```

Then, together with line 12e (the standard or itemized deduction) and line 13b (Schedule 1-A additional deductions), it is subtracted from line 11b (adjusted gross income) to produce line 15 (taxable income).

The deduction itself is created by Internal Revenue Code section 199A. It is meant to give pass-through-business owners a deduction roughly comparable to the lower corporate tax rate enacted in the same legislation.

## 2. Plain-English computation per ingredient (the IRS rules — independent of our code)

### 2.1 Which form applies — the threshold decision

Per the 2025 Form 8995 instructions, the simpler **Form 8995** can be used only when **all three** of the following are true:

1. The taxpayer has Qualified Business Income, qualified Real Estate Investment Trust dividends, or qualified Publicly Traded Partnership income or loss.
2. The taxpayer's taxable income before the Qualified Business Income deduction is **not more than**:
   - `$394,600` if Married Filing Jointly;
   - `$197,300` for every other filing status (Single, Married Filing Separately, Head of Household, Qualifying Surviving Spouse).
3. The taxpayer is **not** a patron of a specified agricultural or horticultural cooperative.

If any of those is false, the taxpayer must use the more complex **Form 8995-A**.

### 2.2 The compute-order dependency

Per the 2025 Form 8995 instructions line 11 and the 2025 Form 8995-A taxable-income instructions:

```
taxable_income_before_qbi = Form1040.line11a - Form1040.line12e - Form1040.line13b
```

This means line 13a **cannot** be finalized until line 12e and line 13b are final. The application must compute:
- line 12e (standard or itemized deduction) → final;
- line 13b (Schedule 1-A additional deductions total) → final;
- only then can line 13a be computed correctly.

This is a one-pass dependency: line 13a does NOT feed back into line 12e or line 13b.

### 2.3 Net Qualified Business Income (the QBI component)

```
net_qualified_business_income = max(0, current_year_qualified_business_income
                                     - prior_year_qualified_business_loss_carryforward)
```

If `(current year QBI − prior year carryforward)` is negative:
- the negative amount is **preserved** as the next-year prior-year carryforward (so the loss is not lost);
- but the input to the 20% multiplication is clamped at zero (so the current-year deduction is never negative).

This dual semantic — preserve the loss for next-year storage AND clamp to zero for current-year computation — is the key rule that produces the `line13a ≥ 0` invariant required by Internal Revenue Code section 199A(c)(2).

### 2.4 Net qualified REIT / PTP component

Same dual-semantic pattern, applied to the separate REIT-dividend / PTP-income stream:

```
net_qualified_reit_and_ptp = max(0, current_year_qualified_reit_and_ptp_amount
                                     - prior_year_reit_ptp_loss_carryforward)
```

### 2.5 Tentative deduction (before the taxable-income limit)

```
qbi_deduction_component   = 20% × net_qualified_business_income
reit_ptp_deduction_component = 20% × net_qualified_reit_and_ptp
tentative_deduction       = qbi_deduction_component + reit_ptp_deduction_component
```

This is the simple Form 8995 formula. The Form 8995-A path applies additional limitations to the `qbi_deduction_component` before this sum — see section 4 below.

### 2.6 Taxable-income limitation (the 20% ceiling)

Per Form 8995 line 11–14 and Form 8995-A line 32–37, the deduction is capped at 20% of taxable income minus net capital gain and qualified dividends:

```
net_capital_gain_and_qualified_dividends = max(0, Schedule_D_net_capital_gain) + qualified_dividends
taxable_income_limit_base = max(0, taxable_income_before_qbi - net_capital_gain_and_qualified_dividends)
taxable_income_limit      = 20% × taxable_income_limit_base
```

The reason for subtracting net capital gain and qualified dividends: those amounts are already taxed at the preferential long-term-capital-gains rate, so the IRS does not allow them to inflate the QBI-deduction ceiling.

### 2.7 Final line 13a

```
Form1040.line13a = max(0, min(tentative_deduction, taxable_income_limit))
```

The outer `max(0, ...)` is structurally redundant when the prior dual-semantic clamps are in place but documents the line 13a ≥ 0 invariant.

## 3. Reference data needed to compute line 13a (2025 amounts — these change every tax year)

**Qualified Business Income deduction rate** (set by Internal Revenue Code section 199A; same every year since 2018):

```
QBI_DEDUCTION_RATE = 20%
```

**Taxable-income thresholds** (the "below threshold / above threshold" decision):

```
QBI_THRESHOLD_MARRIED_FILING_JOINTLY = $394,600
QBI_THRESHOLD_ALL_OTHER_FILING_STATUSES = $197,300
```

**Phase-in ranges** (the band above the threshold where the Specified Service Trade or Business — abbreviated SSTB — disallowance and the W-2 wage / Unadjusted Basis Immediately After Acquisition limitation phase in):

```
QBI_PHASEIN_RANGE_MARRIED_FILING_JOINTLY = $100,000
QBI_PHASEIN_RANGE_ALL_OTHER_FILING_STATUSES = $50,000
```

**Top of the phase-in band** (computed, not stored separately):

```
QBI_PHASEIN_TOP_MARRIED_FILING_JOINTLY = $394,600 + $100,000 = $494,600
QBI_PHASEIN_TOP_ALL_OTHER_FILING_STATUSES = $197,300 + $50,000 = $247,300
```

**W-2 wage / Unadjusted Basis Immediately After Acquisition limit rates** (Form 8995-A only; section 199A(b)(2)(B)):

```
QBI_W2_WAGE_LIMIT_RATE = 50%   (50% of W-2 wages paid by the qualifying business)
QBI_W2_WAGE_LIMIT_ALTERNATIVE_WAGES_RATE = 25%   (25% of W-2 wages PLUS …)
QBI_W2_WAGE_LIMIT_ALTERNATIVE_UBIA_RATE  = 2.5%  (… 2.5% of Unadjusted Basis Immediately After Acquisition)
```

The IRS takes the **larger** of these two limits as the W-2 / UBIA limit — the taxpayer always gets the more favorable choice.

## 4. Above-threshold limitations (Form 8995-A only)

When the taxpayer must use Form 8995-A (income above the threshold), three additional limitations apply per-business:

### 4.1 W-2 wage / UBIA limit (per business)

```
wage_ubia_limit = max(
    50% × W2_wages_of_qualifying_business,
    25% × W2_wages_of_qualifying_business + 2.5% × ubia_of_qualified_property
)
```

The deduction for the business is then limited to:

```
deduction_for_business = min(20% × QBI_for_business, wage_ubia_limit)
```

### 4.2 SSTB phase-in / disallowance

A **Specified Service Trade or Business** (SSTB) means health, law, accounting, actuarial science, performing arts, consulting, athletics, financial services, brokerage services, or any trade where the principal asset is the reputation or skill of one or more employees or owners.

For SSTB activities:
- below the threshold: no reduction (treated as a normal business);
- in the phase-in band: the deduction is multiplied by an "applicable percentage" = `1 − (taxable_income − threshold) / phase_in_range`;
- at or above the top of the phase-in band: the SSTB deduction is **zero** (fully disallowed).

### 4.3 Non-SSTB phase-in in the band

For non-SSTB activities in the phase-in band, the difference between `20% × QBI` and the W-2 / UBIA limit is reduced by the same phase-in percentage. Above the top of the band, the full W-2 / UBIA limit applies with no relief.

## 5. Out-of-scope paths (Internal Revenue Code section 199A but not supported here)

Per `CLAUDE.md`:

- **Schedule C (sole proprietorship business income)** is out of scope. Self-employment QBI from Schedule C cannot be entered.
- **Schedule F (farm income)** is out of scope. Self-employment QBI from Schedule F cannot be entered.
- **Cooperative patron deduction (Form 8995-A Schedule D)** is out of scope. A taxpayer who is a patron of a specified agricultural or horticultural cooperative cannot use this application.

A blocking flag (`hasScheduleCOrScheduleFQbiSources` or `isCooperativePatronOfAgriculturalHorticulturalCooperative`) is emitted when the user attests to either of these on the input form.

What IS supported:
- Section 199A items from **Schedule K-1 (Form 1065)** — partnership pass-through;
- Section 199A items from **Schedule K-1 (Form 1120-S)** — S-corporation pass-through;
- Section 199A items from **Schedule K-1 (Form 1041)** — estate or trust pass-through;
- **Form 1099-DIV box 5** — qualified REIT dividends;
- Manual supplemental adjustments entered on the QBI personal form;
- Prior-year QBI loss carryforward and prior-year REIT/PTP loss carryforward.

## 6. Carryforward dual semantic

This is the most subtle rule on line 13a and the one most likely to be misimplemented.

Per Internal Revenue Code section 199A(c)(2) + the 2025 Form 8995 instructions for lines 3 and 7:

If the net Qualified Business Income (current-year amount minus prior-year carryforward) is negative:
- **for the current year**: the negative number is clamped to zero before the 20% multiplication, so line 13a stays at or above zero;
- **for next year**: the negative number is preserved so it can be reported as next year's prior-year QBI loss carryforward (Form 8995 line 3, entered as a negative on the user's printed return).

The same rule applies independently to the REIT / PTP component (Form 8995 line 7).

Worked example:

```
current_year_qualified_business_income = $10,000
prior_year_qualified_business_loss_carryforward = $50,000

net_qualified_business_income (preserved value) = $10,000 - $50,000 = -$40,000
qbi_component_base (clamped for current year) = max(-$40,000, 0) = $0
qbi_deduction_component = 20% × $0 = $0
→ Form 1040 line 13a contribution from QBI = $0 (correct: line13a ≥ 0)
→ next year's prior_year_qualified_business_loss_carryforward = $40,000
```

If the clamp were applied at the subtraction site (instead of after), the −$40,000 would be lost and the taxpayer would forfeit the carryforward.

## 9. Computation as implemented by the code (TaxReturnComputeService.java)

### 9.1 Two-pass invocation

Because line 13a depends on line 13b which is computed AFTER line 12e, the code uses a two-pass pattern:

```
PASS 1 (inside computeLine12 at the end of the standard-deduction branch):
    line13a_preliminary = computeLine13a(line13b = null)
    // taxable_income_before_qbi computed as (line 11a − line 12e − 0)

PASS 2 (in prepare() after Schedule 1-A is computed):
    line13a_final = computeLine13a(line13b = actual_schedule_1a_line_38)
    // taxable_income_before_qbi now correctly excludes Schedule 1-A
```

The preliminary line 13a is used as the line-14 (total deductions) operand in the first-pass write so the response shape is well-formed. The second-pass overwrites it.

### 9.2 Core `computeLine13a` logic

```
1. Gate: if neither taxpayer nor spouse has hadQualifiedBusinessIncomeInputs → return null
2. Collect per-person inputs (with the MFS guard that null-shadows spouse data on MFS)
3. Validate statement-upload gating + out-of-scope flags → may emit blocking flags
4. Compute aggregate working values:
     qualified_business_income_amount = taxpayer + spouse (with MFS guard)
     prior_year_carryforward          = taxpayer + spouse (with MFS guard)
     net_qbi (PRESERVE NEGATIVE)      = subtractNonNegativeAllowNegative(qbi, carryforward)
     qualified_reit_and_ptp_amount    = taxpayer + spouse
     prior_year_reit_ptp_carryforward = taxpayer + spouse
     net_reit_ptp (PRESERVE NEGATIVE) = subtractNonNegativeAllowNegative(reit_ptp, carryforward)
5. Compute taxable income before QBI:
     agi                    = Form1040.line11b
     taxable_income_before_qbi = max(0, agi - line12e - line13b)
6. Compute net capital gain + qualified dividends:
     net_capital_gain_and_qualified_dividends = max(0, Schedule_D_gain) + qualified_dividends
7. Decide form: shouldUseForm8995A(filing_status, taxable_income_before_qbi)
8. Validate threshold path (cooperative patron / SSTB blockers if applicable)
9. Compute components:
     qbi_component_base       = max(net_qbi, 0)                                 // CURRENT-YEAR clamp
     qbi_deduction_component  = Form 8995-A path: per-business with SSTB + W-2/UBIA limit
                                Form 8995 path:   20% × qbi_component_base
     reit_ptp_component_base  = max(net_reit_ptp, 0)
     reit_ptp_deduction       = 20% × reit_ptp_component_base
     tentative_deduction      = qbi_deduction_component + reit_ptp_deduction
10. Compute taxable-income limit:
     taxable_income_limit_base = max(0, taxable_income_before_qbi - net_capital_gain_and_qualified_dividends)
     taxable_income_limit      = 20% × taxable_income_limit_base
11. Final: line13a = min(tentative_deduction, taxable_income_limit)
12. Build either Form8995 or Form8995A output object with all working values
```

### 9.3 `compute8995AQbiDeductionComponent` (the above-threshold per-business loop)

For each business activity (taxpayer + spouse combined):

```
phase_in_percentage = (taxable_income_before_qbi - threshold) / phase_in_range
                       clamped to [0, 1]

if activity is an SSTB (Specified Service Trade or Business):
    if taxable_income_before_qbi >= upper_threshold:
        contribution = $0   (fully phased out)
    else:
        applicable_pct = 1 - phase_in_percentage
        adjusted_qbi   = qbi × applicable_pct
        adjusted_w2    = w2_wages × applicable_pct
        adjusted_ubia  = ubia × applicable_pct
        tentative_for_sstb = 20% × adjusted_qbi
        wage_ubia_limit_for_sstb = max(50% × adjusted_w2,
                                        25% × adjusted_w2 + 2.5% × adjusted_ubia)
        contribution = min(tentative_for_sstb, wage_ubia_limit_for_sstb)
else (non-SSTB):
    tentative_qbi_deduction = 20% × qbi
    wage_ubia_limit = max(50% × w2_wages, 25% × w2_wages + 2.5% × ubia)
    if taxable_income_before_qbi > upper_threshold:
        contribution = min(tentative_qbi_deduction, wage_ubia_limit)
    else (in the phase-in band):
        excess = max(0, tentative_qbi_deduction - wage_ubia_limit)
        reduction = excess × phase_in_percentage
        phased_amount = tentative_qbi_deduction - reduction
        contribution = max(min(tentative_qbi_deduction, wage_ubia_limit), phased_amount)
```

The four-case taxonomy (below threshold, in phase-in band SSTB, in phase-in band non-SSTB, above the band) mirrors the IRS Form 8995-A Cases A through D.

### 9.4 Output assembly

Whichever form is used, the output sub-object is populated with all 10 echo fields (input echoes + tentative + limit + final), and `Form1040.deductions.qualifiedBusinessIncomeDeduction` is set to the final `line13a` value.

## 13. End-to-end worked example (Married Filing Jointly, above threshold, SSTB)

Inputs:
- Filing status: Married Filing Jointly
- Combined wages: $400,000
- QBI from K-1 (taxpayer is a doctor — Specified Service Trade or Business): $80,000
- W-2 wages paid by the medical practice (Box 1 of the practice's W-3): $30,000
- UBIA of qualified property in the practice: $50,000
- Standard deduction (line 12e): $31,500
- Schedule 1-A line 38 (line 13b): $0

IRS computation:

```
agi                          = $400,000 + $80,000 = $480,000
taxable_income_before_qbi    = $480,000 − $31,500 − $0 = $448,500
$448,500 > $394,600 threshold AND $448,500 ≤ $494,600 upper → in phase-in band, use Form 8995-A

phase_in_percentage = ($448,500 − $394,600) / $100,000 = 53,900 / 100,000 = 0.539
applicable_pct      = 1 − 0.539 = 0.461

adjusted_qbi   = $80,000 × 0.461 = $36,880
adjusted_w2    = $30,000 × 0.461 = $13,830
adjusted_ubia  = $50,000 × 0.461 = $23,050

tentative_for_sstb = 20% × $36,880 = $7,376
wage_ubia_limit_for_sstb = max(50% × $13,830, 25% × $13,830 + 2.5% × $23,050)
                         = max($6,915, $3,457.50 + $576.25)
                         = max($6,915, $4,033.75) = $6,915

contribution_to_qbi_component = min($7,376, $6,915) = $6,915

tentative_deduction           = $6,915 + $0 (no REIT/PTP) = $6,915

taxable_income_limit_base     = max(0, $448,500 − $0 − $0) = $448,500
taxable_income_limit          = 20% × $448,500 = $89,700

Form 1040 line 13a            = min($6,915, $89,700) = $6,915
```

Above the upper threshold ($494,600 MFJ) the entire SSTB deduction would be $0; here we're in the phase-in band so the deduction is reduced but not eliminated.
