## 1. What Line 13b is

Form 1040 line 13b is labeled **"Additional deductions from Schedule 1-A, line 38"**. It is a one-line carry-over from a brand-new 2025 schedule (Schedule 1-A) that consolidates the four "no-tax-on-X" deductions enacted by the One Big Beautiful Bill Act (OBBBA) of 2025.

```
Form1040.line13b = Schedule1A.line38
```

Then, together with line 12e (standard or itemized deduction) and line 13a (Qualified Business Income deduction), it is subtracted from line 11b (Adjusted Gross Income) to produce line 15 (taxable income).

```
Form1040.line14 = line12e + line13a + line13b
Form1040.line15 = max(0, line11b − line14)
```

Schedule 1-A itself is composed of six parts:

| Part | Title | Final line |
|---|---|---|
| I | Modified Adjusted Gross Income (MAGI) Amount | line 3 |
| II | No Tax on Tips | line 13 |
| III | No Tax on Overtime | line 21 |
| IV | No Tax on Car Loan Interest | line 30 |
| V | Enhanced Deduction for Seniors | line 37 |
| VI | Total Additional Deductions | line 38 (= line 13 + line 21 + line 30 + line 37) |

Part I is a base that feeds Parts II through V (each part subtracts a phase-out determined from the Part I result).

**Important compute-order rule for 2025**: Line 13b must be finalized **before** Line 13a, because the Form 8995 / 8995-A computation for line 13a uses `taxable_income_before_qbi = line11a − line12e − line13b`. See `lines/13ab.md` §9.

## 2. Plain-English computation per Part (the IRS rules — independent of our code)

### 2.1 Part I — Modified Adjusted Gross Income (line 3)

Schedule 1-A Part I rebuilds Adjusted Gross Income (AGI) with four kinds of excluded foreign or territorial income added back. That sum is the base for every phase-out in Parts II–V.

```
Schedule1A.line1  = Form1040.line11b           (the taxpayer's AGI)
Schedule1A.line2a = excluded_Puerto_Rico_income (income excluded under §933)
Schedule1A.line2b = Form2555.line45            (foreign earned income exclusion)
Schedule1A.line2c = Form2555.line50            (foreign housing exclusion)
Schedule1A.line2d = Form4563.line15            (American Samoa excluded income)
Schedule1A.line2e = line2a + line2b + line2c + line2d
Schedule1A.line3  = Schedule1A.line1 + Schedule1A.line2e   (this is "MAGI")
```

The point of the add-backs: these exclusions reduce AGI but are still "income" in substance, so they count toward the phase-out thresholds. Otherwise, a high-income foreign worker could get a big tip deduction that Congress intended to phase out.

### 2.2 Part II — No Tax on Tips (line 13)

Available when the filer received **qualified tips** in 2025 in an occupation listed at `IRS.gov/TippedOccupations`, has a valid Social Security Number, and (if married) is filing jointly.

```
line4a = tips reported on Form W-2 box 7 (social security tips)
line4b = unreported tips from Form 4137 line 4
line4c = line4a + line4b
         (★ Multi-employer worksheet: if tips were received from more than one
            employer, lines 4a and 4b are entered as 0 and line 4c is computed
            from the IRS allocation worksheet. Aggregating-then-capping at the
            $25,000 limit produces the same final answer, so it is acceptable
            to combine before the cap.)
line5  = qualified tips received in a trade or business
         (from Form 1099-NEC / 1099-MISC / 1099-K, capped by that business's
          net income limitation if applicable)
line6  = line4c + line5                                 (raw qualified tips)
line7  = min(line6, $25,000)                            (combined cap)
line8  = Schedule1A.line3                               (MAGI)
line9  = $150,000  ($300,000 if MFJ)                    (phase-out threshold)
line10 = max(0, line8 − line9)                          (excess MAGI)
line11 = floor(line10 / $1,000)                         (whole-$1,000 buckets, round DOWN)
line12 = line11 × $100                                  (phase-out reduction)
line13 = max(0, line7 − line12)                        (★ final tips deduction)
```

**Combined cap rule:** if both spouses on a Married-Filing-Jointly return have qualified tips, the $25,000 cap is combined, not per spouse.

### 2.3 Part III — No Tax on Overtime (line 21)

Available when the filer received **qualified overtime compensation** in 2025, has a valid Social Security Number, and (if married) is filing jointly.

```
line14a = qualified overtime portion of Form W-2 box 1
line14b = qualified overtime from non-W-2 sources (e.g., 1099-NEC box 1, 1099-MISC box 3)
line14c = line14a + line14b                              (raw qualified overtime)
line15  = min(line14c, cap)                             where:
            cap = $12,500   if single / HOH / QSS / MFS
            cap = $25,000   if MFJ
line16  = Schedule1A.line3                              (MAGI)
line17  = $150,000  ($300,000 if MFJ)                   (phase-out threshold)
line18  = max(0, line16 − line17)                       (excess MAGI)
line19  = floor(line18 / $1,000)                        (whole-$1,000 buckets, round DOWN)
line20  = line19 × $100                                 (phase-out reduction)
line21  = max(0, line15 − line20)                       (★ final overtime deduction)
```

**Why W-2 box 1 is the source:** 2025 W-2 forms were *not* required to separately identify the qualified overtime amount (the rule was too new). The IRS instructions explicitly allow worksheet-based or reasonable-method reconstruction by the taxpayer.

### 2.4 Part IV — No Tax on Car Loan Interest (line 30)

Available when the filer paid or accrued **Qualified Passenger Vehicle Loan Interest (QPVLI)** in 2025. This Part is **not** restricted to Married-Filing-Jointly when married — it is the only Part of Schedule 1-A that runs on Married-Filing-Separately returns.

**Qualifying loan requirements (all must be true):**
- Loan originated **after December 31, 2024**.
- Loan originated by the taxpayer (not assumed from another buyer).
- Loan was used to purchase an "applicable passenger vehicle".
- Vehicle is for **personal use** (not predominantly business or commercial).
- Loan is secured by a **first lien** on the purchased vehicle.

**Qualifying vehicle requirements:**
- **New to the taxpayer** (used vehicles do not qualify).
- Car, minivan, van, SUV, pickup truck, or motorcycle under the vehicle-weight limit.
- **Final assembly in the United States**.

**Per-vehicle iteration over a list of vehicles (each with its own VIN):**

```
for each vehicle:
   paid = vehicleLoanInterestPaidAmount

   ★ Refinancing limitation:
   if vehicleWasRefinanced and originalLoanPrincipal < refinancedLoanBalance:
       paid = paid × (originalLoanPrincipal / refinancedLoanBalance)
       (only the portion attributable to purchase-money principal qualifies;
        cash-out refinances inflate the loan beyond the qualifying portion)

   ★ No-double-deduction:
   net_for_vehicle = paid − interestAlreadyDeductedOnScheduleE
                          − interestAlreadyDeductedOnScheduleC
                          − interestAlreadyDeductedOnScheduleF

line23 = sum(net_for_vehicle for all vehicles)
line24 = min(line23, $10,000)                            (cap)
line25 = Schedule1A.line3                                (MAGI)
line26 = $100,000  ($200,000 if MFJ)                     (phase-out threshold)
line27 = max(0, line25 − line26)                         (excess MAGI)
line28 = ceil(line27 / $1,000)                           (whole-$1,000 buckets, round UP — ★ NOT DOWN)
line29 = line28 × $200                                   (phase-out reduction)
line30 = max(0, line24 − line29)                         (★ final car-loan-interest deduction)
```

The Vehicle Identification Number (VIN) for each car must be reported on the return. The IRS will reject e-filed returns that claim Part IV without VINs.

### 2.5 Part V — Enhanced Deduction for Seniors (line 37)

Available when the filer (or spouse, on MFJ) was born **before January 2, 1961**, has a valid Social Security Number, and (if married) is filing jointly.

```
taxpayer_qualifies = bornBeforeJan2_1961 AND validSSN
spouse_qualifies   = MFJ AND spouseBornBeforeJan2_1961 AND spouseValidSSN
eligibleCount      = (taxpayer_qualifies ? 1 : 0) + (spouse_qualifies ? 1 : 0)
                     (0, 1, or 2)

base_amount = eligibleCount × $6,000             (max $12,000 if both qualify on MFJ)

line31 = Schedule1A.line3                        (MAGI)
line32 = $75,000  ($150,000 if MFJ)              (phase-out threshold)
line33 = max(0, line31 − line32)                 (excess MAGI)
line34 = line33 × 0.06                           (★ continuous 6% phase-out — NO bucketing)
line35 = max(0, $6,000 − line34)                 (per-person amount after phase-out)
        (effectively: per-person max is $6,000; phase-out is shared across both spouses)
line36a = $6,000 if taxpayer_qualifies else 0
line36b = $6,000 if spouse_qualifies else 0
line37  = max(0, (line36a + line36b) − line34)   (★ final senior deduction)
        equivalently: max(0, eligibleCount × $6,000 − line34)
```

**"Born before Jan 2, 1961" rule:** per Internal Revenue Code §63(f)(1)(A), a person is treated as reaching age 65 on the day before the 65th birthday. For the 2025 tax year, "age 65 by end of year" therefore means born on or before January 1, 1961, which the IRS expresses as "born before January 2, 1961".

**Death-before-65 exclusion:** if a taxpayer dies in 2025 before reaching age 65 under that rule, the taxpayer does not qualify, even though they would have turned 65 by year-end if alive.

### 2.6 Part VI — Total Additional Deductions (line 38)

```
Schedule1A.line38 = Schedule1A.line13   (tips)
                  + Schedule1A.line21   (overtime)
                  + Schedule1A.line30   (car loan interest)
                  + Schedule1A.line37   (enhanced senior)

Form1040.line13b = Schedule1A.line38
```

## 5. Out-of-scope paths (not supported by this product)

- **Form 2555 manually-entered fallback amounts.** The code prefers the computed Form 2555 line 45 / line 50 when Form 2555 is filled out; otherwise the user can enter `form2555Line45ExcludedIncome` and `form2555Line50HousingExclusion` directly. Manual entry remains supported.
- **Form 4563 (American Samoa exclusion).** The product does not compute Form 4563; the `form4563Line15ExcludedIncome` field is a manual override only.
- **Tips received as a self-employed worker beyond the 1099-NEC channel.** Schedule C and Schedule F self-employment paths are out of scope for the entire return, so the Schedule 1-A code accepts the per-employer "trade tips net income cap" defensively but does not attempt to compute it. Manual entry is the only path.
- **Vehicles owned partly by another taxpayer (proportional VIN claim).** The code treats every entered vehicle as 100% owned by the taxpayer. Joint ownership prorations would need an extra input field.

## 8. Computation as implemented by the code (`TaxReturnComputeService.java`)

Entry point: `computeSchedule1A(...)` at `src/main/java/com/ustax/microservices/TaxReturnComputeService.java:28271`. Invoked from `prepare()` around line 1098 with a defensive Married-Filing-Separately guard at line 1058 that null-shadows `deductionsSpouse` and `form2555Spouse` on MFS to prevent spouse-data leakage into the taxpayer's MAGI.

### 8.1 Screening gate

```java
boolean taxpayerHadInputs = Boolean.TRUE.equals(getBoolean(deductionsTaxpayer, "hadAdditionalDeductions"));
boolean spouseHadInputs   = Boolean.TRUE.equals(getBoolean(deductionsSpouse, "spouseHasAdditionalDeductionInputs"));
if (!taxpayerHadInputs && !spouseHadInputs) return null;        // entire Schedule 1-A skipped
```

If both screening flags are false, the entire schedule is skipped and `Form1040.deductions.additionalDeductions` stays null. The line-14 / line-15 totals then naturally collapse to the line-12e + line-13a sum.

### 8.2 Filing-status gating

```java
String status = normalizeFilingStatus(getString(filingStatus, "filingStatus"));
boolean isMfj      = "Married filing jointly".equalsIgnoreCase(status);
boolean isMarried  = isMfj || "Married filing separately".equalsIgnoreCase(status);
// Parts II, III, V require MFJ if married; MFS filers cannot claim them.
boolean eligibleForPerPersonParts = !isMarried || isMfj;
```

Single / HOH / QSS filers automatically have `eligibleForPerPersonParts = true`. MFJ → true. MFS → false. Part IV does NOT check this flag.

### 8.3 Part I — MAGI (line 3)

```java
BigDecimal agi = adjustments.getLine11bAmountFromLine11aAdjustedGrossIncome();
BigDecimal puertoRico = getAmount(deductionsTaxpayer, "puertoRicoExcludedIncome");

// Prefer computed Form 2555 values when Form 2555 is computed:

    form2555Line50 = getAmount(deductionsTaxpayer, "form2555Line50HousingExclusion");

form4563Line15 = getAmount(deductionsTaxpayer, "form4563Line15ExcludedIncome");

BigDecimal magi = roundMoney(agi + puertoRico + form2555Line45 + form2555Line50 + form4563Line15);
```

### 8.4 Part II — Tips (line 13)

For each spouse independently, gate by:
- `eligibleForPerPersonParts` (MFJ-when-married rule)
- SSN present
- `hadInputs` opt-in
- `taxpayerReceivedQualifiedTips` (or `spouseReceivedQualifiedTips`)
- `taxpayerTippedOccupationConfirmed` (or `spouseTippedOccupationConfirmed`)

Raw tips combine FOUR channels per spouse:

```java
BigDecimal w2Box7         = sumW2Box7TipsForSsn(w2Entries, taxpayerSsn);
BigDecimal form4137       = tips.form4137Taxpayer().getLine4UnreportedTips();
BigDecimal manual         = getAmount(deductionsTaxpayer, "taxpayerManualTipsFromNonStatementSources");
BigDecimal nec1099Trade   = sum1099NecTipsForSsn(form1099NecEntries, taxpayerSsn);
if (tradeTipsCap != null && tradeTipsCap > 0) {
    nec1099Trade = min(nec1099Trade, tradeTipsCap);    // self-employment net-income limitation
}
taxpayerRawTips = w2Box7 + form4137 + manual + nec1099Trade;
```

Combined cap + phase-out:

```java
BigDecimal totalRaw = taxpayerRawTips + spouseRawTips;
BigDecimal capped   = min(totalRaw, SCHEDULE_1A_TIPS_CAP);                  // $25,000
BigDecimal threshold = isMfj ? SCHEDULE_1A_TIPS_PHASEOUT_MFJ                // $300,000
                              : SCHEDULE_1A_TIPS_PHASEOUT_SINGLE;           // $150,000
BigDecimal phaseout = computeTipsOvertimePhaseout(magi, threshold,
                                                  SCHEDULE_1A_TIPS_OVERTIME_STEP_AMOUNT);
                      // = floor(max(0, magi − threshold) / 1000) × $100
line13TipsDeduction = max(0, capped − phaseout);
```

The `1099-NEC` auto-import filters by `isTipIncome=true` AND `recipientTIN` matching the filer's SSN, then sums `nonemployeeCompensationAmount`.

### 8.5 Part III — Overtime (line 21)

Same gating as Part II (minus the tipped-occupation confirmation, replaced by `taxpayerReceivedQualifiedOvertime`).

```java
taxpayerRawOvertime = getAmount(deductionsTaxpayer, "taxpayerOvertimeFromW2Box1Amount")
                    + getAmount(deductionsTaxpayer, "taxpayerOvertimeFromNonW2SourcesAmount");
// (no statement auto-import — manual entries only)

BigDecimal totalRaw = taxpayerRawOvertime + spouseRawOvertime;
BigDecimal cap = isMfj ? SCHEDULE_1A_OVERTIME_CAP_MFJ                       // $25,000
                       : SCHEDULE_1A_OVERTIME_CAP_SINGLE;                   // $12,500
BigDecimal capped   = min(totalRaw, cap);
BigDecimal threshold = isMfj ? SCHEDULE_1A_TIPS_PHASEOUT_MFJ                // $300,000
                              : SCHEDULE_1A_TIPS_PHASEOUT_SINGLE;           // $150,000
BigDecimal phaseout = computeTipsOvertimePhaseout(magi, threshold,
                                                  SCHEDULE_1A_TIPS_OVERTIME_STEP_AMOUNT);
                      // shared helper with Part II — same FLOOR rounding, same $100/$1k step
line21OvertimeDeduction = max(0, capped − phaseout);
```

### 8.6 Part IV — Car loan interest (line 30)

**Does not** check `eligibleForPerPersonParts` — runs on Single / MFJ / MFS / HOH / QSS.

```java
if (taxpayerHadInputs && Boolean.TRUE.equals(getBoolean(deductionsTaxpayer, "paidCarLoanInterestOnNewVehicle"))) {
    BigDecimal totalNetInterest = 0;
    for (each vehicle in carLoanVehicles) {
        BigDecimal paid = getAmount(vehicle, "vehicleLoanInterestPaidAmount");

        // ★ Refinancing proportionalization
        if (vehicleWasRefinanced && originalLoanPrincipal < refinancedLoanBalance) {
            paid = paid × (originalLoanPrincipal / refinancedLoanBalance);
        }

        // ★ Subtract anything already deducted on Schedule C / E / F
        BigDecimal exclusions = scheduleE + scheduleC + scheduleF;
        BigDecimal net_for_vehicle = max(0, paid − exclusions);

        totalNetInterest += net_for_vehicle;
    }

    BigDecimal capped = min(totalNetInterest, SCHEDULE_1A_CAR_LOAN_CAP);            // $10,000
    BigDecimal threshold = isMfj ? SCHEDULE_1A_CAR_LOAN_PHASEOUT_MFJ                // $200,000
                                  : SCHEDULE_1A_CAR_LOAN_PHASEOUT_SINGLE;           // $100,000
    BigDecimal phaseout = computeCarLoanPhaseout(magi, threshold,
                                                  SCHEDULE_1A_CAR_LOAN_STEP_AMOUNT);
                          // ★ = ceil(max(0, magi − threshold) / 1000) × $200 — ROUND UP
    line30CarLoanDeduction = max(0, capped − phaseout);
}
```

### 8.7 Part V — Senior (line 37)

For each spouse independently, eligibility is derived as:

```java
Boolean bornBefore = getBoolean(deductionsTaxpayer, "taxpayerBornBeforeJan2_1961");
if (bornBefore == null) {
    bornBefore = isBornBeforeJan2_1961(getString(you, "dateOfBirth"));
    // helper parses YYYY-MM-DD; returns true if dob ≤ 1961-01-01
}
taxpayerSeniorEligible = Boolean.TRUE.equals(bornBefore);
```

Combined deduction:

```java
int eligibleCount = (taxpayerSeniorEligible ? 1 : 0) + (spouseSeniorEligible ? 1 : 0);
BigDecimal seniorBase = SCHEDULE_1A_SENIOR_BASE_PER_PERSON × eligibleCount;        // 0, $6,000, or $12,000
BigDecimal threshold = isMfj ? SCHEDULE_1A_SENIOR_PHASEOUT_MFJ                     // $150,000
                              : SCHEDULE_1A_SENIOR_PHASEOUT_SINGLE;                // $75,000
BigDecimal phaseout = computeSeniorPhaseout(magi, threshold, SCHEDULE_1A_SENIOR_PHASEOUT_RATE);
                      // = max(0, magi − threshold) × 0.06       (CONTINUOUS — no bucketing)
line37SeniorDeduction = max(0, seniorBase − phaseout);
```

### 8.8 Part VI — Total (line 38) and the carry-over to Form 1040 line 13b

```java
BigDecimal line38Total = line13TipsDeduction + line21OvertimeDeduction
                       + line30CarLoanDeduction + line37SeniorDeduction;
```

## 12. End-to-end worked example (Married Filing Jointly, both spouses senior, phase-outs bind)

**Inputs:**
- Filing status: Married Filing Jointly
- AGI (line 11b): `$180,000`
- Form 2555 line 45: `$0`; Form 2555 line 50: `$0`; Puerto Rico: `$0`; Form 4563: `$0`
- Both spouses born before January 2, 1961: Yes
- Both spouses have valid SSNs
- Both spouses opted in (`hadAdditionalDeductions = Yes` and `spouseHasAdditionalDeductionInputs = Yes`)
- No tips, no overtime, no car loan interest

**Hand calculation:**

```
Part I — MAGI
   line3 (MAGI) = $180,000

Part V — Senior (only Part with any amounts)
   eligibleCount = 2
   seniorBase = 2 × $6,000 = $12,000
   threshold (MFJ) = $150,000
   excess MAGI = $180,000 − $150,000 = $30,000
   phase-out (continuous 6%) = $30,000 × 0.06 = $1,800
   ★ line37 = max(0, $12,000 − $1,800) = $10,200

Part VI — Total
   line38 = $0 + $0 + $0 + $10,200 = $10,200

→ Form 1040 line 13b = $10,200
```

If a Married-Filing-Separately filer entered the same inputs, `eligibleForPerPersonParts` would be false and Part V would be `$0`. Part IV (car-loan-interest) would still run on MFS if any vehicles had been entered.

This filer's line 13b is **$10,200**. The 6% continuous phase-out is exactly what the IRS instructions describe: every dollar of MAGI above $150,000 reduces the deduction by 6¢ — smoother than the bucketed phase-outs of Parts II / III / IV.

This matches the canonical lock-in test `schedule1ASeniorDeductionMfjBothEligibleWithPhaseout`.

## 13. AMT note

Tips (Part II), Overtime (Part III), and Car Loan Interest (Part IV) reduce **both** regular tax (via line 13b) AND Alternative Minimum Tax (no add-back on Form 6251). The Enhanced Senior Deduction (Part V) reduces regular tax but is **added back** to Alternative Minimum Taxable Income (AMTI) on Form 6251 line 1w. This is by congressional design: high-income seniors who would otherwise use the $6,000 to drop below the AMT floor get the add-back to keep them in AMT. See the cross-reference in `TaxReturnComputeService.computeLine17` ~line 8093.
