## 1. What Line 16 is

Form 1040 line 16 is labeled **"Tax"** with three checkboxes / write-in areas next to it:

- **Check box 1** if any of the tax is from **Form 8814** (parent's election to report a child's interest and dividends).
- **Check box 2** if any of the tax is from **Form 4972** (tax on lump-sum distributions to participants born before January 2, 1936).
- **Check box 3** if any of the tax is from a numbered write-in code: `962` (Section 962 election), `ECR` (Education Credit Recapture), `1291TAX` (Form 8621 line 16e), `Form 8978` (line 14 positive), or `965INC` (triggered Section 965(i) deferred tax).

Line 16 is the **only** line on Form 1040 where this many different tax-computation methods can converge. Every taxpayer reaches line 16 through one of seven different paths.

**Important rule** (from the 2025 Form 1040 instructions): line 16 is NOT automatically zero when line 15 is zero. If line 15 is $0 but any Box 1, Box 2, or Box 3 tax applies, line 16 is positive.

```
Form1040.line16 = tax_on_taxable_income
                + Form8814_extra_tax    (Box 1)
                + Form4972_lump_sum_tax (Box 2)
                + Box3_writein_total    (Box 3)
```

Then line 16 is used at line 18 (line 18 = line 16 + line 17 AMT) and ultimately rolls into line 24 (total tax). Line 16 also acts as the comparison base for line 17 AMT computation: line 17 = max(0, AMT − regular_tax_only); the AMT add is reduced by the regular tax that's already on line 16's `tax_on_taxable_income` component.

## 2. Plain-English computation per branch (the IRS rules — independent of our code)

### 2.1 The seven-branch decision tree (controls `tax_on_taxable_income`)

Per the 2025 Form 1040 instructions, the **first matching branch wins**:

```
1.  If line 15 ≤ 0                                       → tax = $0                                 (method = ZERO)
2.  Else if the taxpayer filed Form 2555                 → tax = Foreign Earned Income Tax Worksheet line 6  (method = FOREIGN_EARNED_INCOME)
3.  Else if the taxpayer must use Form 8615 (kiddie tax) → tax = Form 8615 line 18                  (method = FORM_8615)
4.  Else if the Schedule D Tax Worksheet is required     → tax = Schedule D Tax Worksheet result    (method = SCHEDULE_D_TAX_WORKSHEET)
5.  Else if the Qualified Dividends + Capital Gain
      Tax Worksheet is required                          → tax = QDCG Worksheet line 25             (method = QDCG)
6.  Else if Schedule J is elected (farming/fishing)      → tax = Schedule J result                  (method = SCHEDULE_J)
7.  Else if line 15 < $100,000                           → tax = Tax Table lookup                   (method = TAX_TABLE)
8.  Else (line 15 ≥ $100,000)                            → tax = Tax Computation Worksheet bracket  (method = TAX_COMPUTATION_WORKSHEET)
```

Branches 7 and 8 use the same bracket formulas. The IRS publishes the Tax Table for under-$100k filers as a pre-calculated $50-bucket lookup; the Tax Computation Worksheet at $100k+ is the formula directly. Mathematically they produce identical (or near-identical to the nearest dollar) results.

### 2.2 Branch 2 detail — Foreign Earned Income Tax Worksheet (controlling method for Form 2555 filers)

When the taxpayer (or spouse, on a joint return) claims the foreign earned income exclusion, foreign housing exclusion, or foreign housing deduction on **Form 2555**, the IRS REQUIRES use of the Foreign Earned Income Tax Worksheet. This prevents tax-rate arbitrage — without the worksheet, the excluded income would never affect the marginal rate on the included income.

```
line1  = Form1040 line 15 (taxable income)
line2a = Form2555 line 45 (foreign earned income exclusion)        [taxpayer + spouse on MFJ]
line2b = Form2555 line 50 (foreign housing exclusion)              [taxpayer + spouse on MFJ]
       + housing deduction (if any)
       − deductions disallowed against excluded income             [the project treats this as $0]
line2c = line2a + line2b
line3  = line1 + line2c                                            (the "stack")
line4  = tax on line3 using the applicable ordinary OR special
         method (Tax Table / Tax Computation Worksheet, OR QDCG /
         Schedule D / Form 8615 if otherwise applicable)
line5  = tax on line2c using Tax Table or Tax Computation Worksheet
line6  = max(0, line4 − line5)                                     (this is the line-16 tax)
```

**If line 15 is zero, do NOT complete this worksheet.** Decision-tree Branch 1 fires first, so this case is structurally prevented.

### 2.3 Branch 3 detail — Form 8615 (kiddie tax)

Use Form 8615 to compute the tax-on-taxable-income component when ALL FIVE of these are true:

1. The taxpayer had **more than $2,700 of unearned income** (2025 threshold per Revenue Procedure 2024-40).
2. The taxpayer is required to file a return.
3. The taxpayer is either:
   - under age 18 at the end of 2025; or
   - age 18 at the end of 2025 and did not have earned income greater than half of support; or
   - a full-time student age 19 through 23 at the end of 2025 and did not have earned income greater than half of support.
4. At least one parent was alive at the end of 2025.
5. The taxpayer does not file a joint return for 2025.

Form 8615 itself runs a complex computation that compares the child's tax at the child's bracket against the child's share of the tax that would apply at the parent's bracket (a sort of "income-shifting" anti-abuse rule). Line 18 of Form 8615 is the final figure that lands on Form 1040 line 16.

This product does NOT compute Form 8615 internally. The user fills out Form 8615 externally (on paper or in another tool) and enters the line 18 result into `childFinalTaxLine18` on the Kiddie Income personal form.

### 2.4 Branch 4 detail — Schedule D Tax Worksheet

Required when Schedule D has either:
- Line 18 (28% rate gain on collectibles + Section 1202 stock with 50% exclusion) > 0, OR
- Line 19 (Unrecaptured Section 1250 gain — depreciation recapture on real estate, capped at 25%) > 0,

AND both Schedule D line 15 (long-term net) and line 16 (overall total) are positive gains.

The worksheet computes tax as the sum of:
- ordinary rates on the ordinary-income portion;
- 0% / 15% / 20% preferential rates on regular long-term capital gain and qualified dividends;
- 28% on the 28%-rate gain portion;
- 25% on the unrecaptured Section 1250 gain portion.

Not used when Form 2555 was filed (use the Foreign Earned Income Tax Worksheet instead).

### 2.5 Branch 5 detail — Qualified Dividends and Capital Gain Tax Worksheet

Required when the Schedule D Tax Worksheet is NOT required AND any of:
- qualified dividends are reported on Form 1040 line 3a;
- the taxpayer did not file Schedule D but reported capital gain distributions on line 7a;
- the taxpayer filed Schedule D with both lines 15 and 16 positive.

The worksheet separates income into ordinary and "preferential" (long-term capital gain + qualified dividends) and applies three brackets — 0%, 15%, 20% — to the preferential portion based on where total taxable income falls relative to the 2025 thresholds.

**2025 QDCG thresholds:**

| Filing status | 0% rate ceiling | 20% rate floor |
|---|---:|---:|
| Single | $48,350 | $533,400 |
| Married filing jointly / Qualifying surviving spouse | $96,700 | $600,050 |
| Married filing separately | $48,350 | $300,025 |
| Head of household | $64,750 | $566,700 |

Below the 0% ceiling, all preferential income is taxed at 0%. Between the 0% ceiling and the 20% floor, the preferential income is taxed at 15%. Above the 20% floor, that portion is taxed at 20%. Ordinary income is taxed at the normal bracket rates regardless.

Not used when Form 2555 was filed (use the Foreign Earned Income Tax Worksheet instead).

### 2.6 Branch 6 detail — Schedule J (farm or fishing income averaging)

Available to taxpayers with income from farming or fishing who elect to use income averaging across the prior three years. **This product treats Schedule J as out of scope** per `CLAUDE.md` (farming/fishing is not supported). Spec §7 documents the branch for completeness but the implementation omits it. Tracked as informational only — taxpayers electing Schedule J would need to use a different tool.

### 2.7 Branches 7 and 8 — Tax Table and Tax Computation Worksheet (the default)

For the great majority of filers — no Form 2555, no kiddie tax, no qualified dividends or capital gains, no Schedule J — the tax-on-taxable-income is computed from the 2025 brackets.

**2025 brackets (subtraction-constant formula, all four filing statuses):**

| Filing status | 10% bracket | 12% | 22% | 24% | 32% | 35% | 37% |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Single | $0–$11,925 | $11,925–$48,475 (−$238.50) | $48,475–$103,350 (−$5,086) | $103,350–$197,300 (−$7,153) | $197,300–$250,525 (−$22,937) | $250,525–$626,350 (−$30,452.75) | over $626,350 (−$42,979.75) |
| Married filing jointly / Qualifying surviving spouse | $0–$23,850 | $23,850–$96,950 (−$477) | $96,950–$206,700 (−$10,172) | $206,700–$394,600 (−$14,306) | $394,600–$501,050 (−$45,874) | $501,050–$751,600 (−$60,905.50) | over $751,600 (−$75,937.50) |
| Married filing separately | $0–$11,925 | $11,925–$48,475 (−$238.50) | $48,475–$103,350 (−$5,086) | $103,350–$197,300 (−$7,153) | $197,300–$250,525 (−$22,937) | $250,525–$375,800 (−$30,452.75) | over $375,800 (−$37,968.75) |
| Head of household | $0–$17,000 | $17,000–$64,850 (−$340) | $64,850–$103,350 (−$6,825) | $103,350–$197,300 (−$8,892) | $197,300–$250,500 (−$24,676) | $250,500–$626,350 (−$32,191) | over $626,350 (−$44,718) |

The subtraction-constant formula at bracket B is: `tax = income × rate_B − constant_B`. Each constant is chosen so the bracket function is continuous at every break point.

**$100,000 boundary**: per IRS instructions, the Tax Table is used below $100,000 and the Tax Computation Worksheet at $100,000 or more. In this product the bracket formula is identical across both ranges and the boundary check ONLY affects the `computationMethod` label string. The mathematical result is the same.

### 2.8 Box 1 — Form 8814 extra tax

When the taxpayer ELECTS to report a child's interest and dividends on the parent's return (Form 8814), the child's income is added to the parent's regular tax PLUS an extra tax computed on Form 8814 line 15 (10% of the child's unearned income above the IRS-set threshold, with a cap). One Form 8814 per child whose gross income is under $13,500.

```
Box1_tax = sum over all children of Form8814 line 15 extra tax
```

### 2.9 Box 2 — Form 4972 lump-sum distribution tax

When the participant was born before January 2, 1936 and elects 10-year averaging or the 20% capital-gain method on a qualifying lump-sum distribution from a qualified retirement plan, the resulting tax goes here. NOT for IRAs or 403(b) plans (those use ordinary rates or the early-distribution penalty on Form 5329).

```
Box2_tax = Form4972 (taxpayer) line 30 total + Form4972 (spouse) line 30 total
```

### 2.10 Box 3 — write-in additional taxes

The 2025 Form 1040 instructions list FIVE codes that go in Box 3:

| Code | Source | What it represents |
|---|---|---|
| `962` | Section 962 election | Tax on a Controlled Foreign Corporation's earnings under the election to be taxed as a domestic corporation. Includes the net amount after any Form 1118 foreign tax credits. |
| `ECR` | Education Credit Recapture | Recapture of an American Opportunity or Lifetime Learning Credit when the underlying refund was reduced after the fact. |
| `1291TAX` | Form 8621 line 16e | Tax on excess distributions from a Passive Foreign Investment Company (PFIC). |
| `Form 8978` | Form 8978 line 14 (positive) | Tax pushed out to a partner from a BBA partnership audit. **Only positive amounts go on line 16; negative amounts go to Schedule 3 line 6l.** |
| `965INC` | Section 965(i) triggered tax | Tax that became due when a previously-deferred Section 965 inclusion was triggered (e.g., the taxpayer left the United States). |

```
Box3_tax = section962_tax_net_of_Form1118_credits
         + education_credit_recapture
         + Form8621.line16e
         + max(0, Form8978.line14)
         + section965i_triggered_tax
```

When any Box 3 amount applies, the code (e.g., "ECR") is written in the line-16 entry space next to the dollar amount.

### 2.11 Final assembly

```
Form1040.line16 = tax_on_taxable_income (one of 7 branches)
                + Box1_tax (Form 8814)
                + Box2_tax (Form 4972)
                + Box3_tax (write-in)
```

The boxes are checked / unchecked independently. A return may have Box 1 + Box 3 checked but not Box 2.

## 4. Routing rules (which branch wins on which return)

| If this is true... | Then `tax_on_taxable_income` is computed by... |
|---|---|
| `line 15 ≤ 0` | $0 (Branch 1) |
| Form 2555 present (taxpayer or spouse on MFJ) | Foreign Earned Income Tax Worksheet (Branch 2) |
| `hasKiddieTaxUnearnedIncome = true` on the kiddie-income form | Form 8615 line 18, user-entered (Branch 3) |
| Schedule D line 18 > 0 OR line 19 > 0, AND Schedule D lines 15+16 both positive | Schedule D Tax Worksheet (Branch 4) |
| Qualified dividends present OR cap gain distributions on line 7a without Schedule D OR Schedule D filed with lines 15+16 both positive | QDCG Worksheet (Branch 5) |
| Schedule J elected | Schedule J — OUT OF SCOPE for this product |
| line 15 < $100,000 (default) | Tax Table (Branch 7) — bracket formula |
| line 15 ≥ $100,000 (default) | Tax Computation Worksheet (Branch 8) — bracket formula (same math as Branch 7) |

Mutual exclusivity is enforced **structurally by the order of the if/else if chain** — when an earlier branch fires, no later branch is reached.

## 5. Out-of-scope paths (acknowledged limitations)

- **Schedule J** (farm/fishing income averaging) — Branch 6 in the IRS spec. Omitted per the product's out-of-scope rule for self-employment farming.
- **Box 3 codes other than ECR** — `962`, `1291TAX`, `Form 8978`, and `965INC` are documented in the IRS instructions but not yet wired into the compute path. Only `ECR` is implemented today. The spec mentions this as Box 3 ⚠️ PARTIAL IMPLEMENTATION.
- **Form 1118 foreign tax credit netting against Section 962 tax** — required when the `962` Box 3 code is in use; not wired because the upstream `962` code itself is not wired.
- **The "deductions disallowed against excluded income" line 2b of the Foreign Earned Income Tax Worksheet** — assumed to be $0 in the code (Gap #12 below). This understates tax in the rare case where a Form 2555 filer had Schedule A miscellaneous deductions or similar items allocable to excluded foreign income.
- **Form 8615 internal computation** — the user fills out Form 8615 externally and enters line 18 into `childFinalTaxLine18`. The product does not auto-compute the parent-rate comparison.
- **The Form 2555 + Form 8615 cross-product** — if BOTH apply (rare combination: child with foreign earned income exclusion), the current Foreign Earned Income Tax Worksheet uses TCW or QDCG for its line 4 rather than feeding Form 8615 line 18 in. The spec mentions this as a KNOWN LIMITATION.

## 8. Computation as implemented by the code (`TaxReturnComputeService.java`)

Entry point: `computeLine16(...)` at `src/main/java/com/ustax/microservices/TaxReturnComputeService.java:2711`. Invoked from `prepare()` around line 1587 with a defensive MFS guard at line 1560 that null-shadows `form2555Spouse` and `form4972Spouse` on MFS to prevent spouse-data leakage into the taxpayer's line 16.

### 8.1 Quick-exit when line 15 is null

```java
BigDecimal line15 = form1040.getDeductions().getLine15TaxableIncome();
if (line15 == null) return;             // upstream line-15 failure → no line 16
```

### 8.2 Decision tree — 7 branches

```java
if (line15.compareTo(ZERO) <= 0) {
    regularTax = ZERO;
    computationMethod = "ZERO";
} else if (form2555Taxpayer != null || form2555Spouse != null) {
    regularTax = computeForeignEarnedIncomeTaxWorksheet(...);
    computationMethod = "FOREIGN_EARNED_INCOME";
} else if (Boolean.TRUE.equals(getBoolean(kiddieIncomeTaxpayer, "hasKiddieTaxUnearnedIncome"))) {
    BigDecimal kiddie = getAmount(kiddieIncomeTaxpayer, "childFinalTaxLine18");
    if (kiddie != null) {
        regularTax = roundMoney(kiddie);
        computationMethod = "FORM_8615";
    } else {
        // Defensive fallback: log warning, fall through to bracket
        regularTax = computeTaxBracket(line15, statusStr);
        computationMethod = line15 < 100000 ? "TAX_TABLE" : "TAX_COMPUTATION_WORKSHEET";
    }
} else if (isScheduleDTaxWorksheetRequired(scheduleD)) {
    regularTax = computeScheduleDTaxWorksheet(line15, statusStr, scheduleD, qualDiv, line7a);
    computationMethod = "SCHEDULE_D_TAX_WORKSHEET";
} else if (isQDCGWorksheetRequired(form1040, scheduleD)) {
    regularTax = computeQDCGWorksheet(line15, statusStr, qualDiv, scheduleD, line7a, line7bScheduleDNotRequired);
    computationMethod = "QDCG";
} else {
    regularTax = computeTaxBracket(line15, statusStr);
    computationMethod = line15 < 100000 ? "TAX_TABLE" : "TAX_COMPUTATION_WORKSHEET";
}
```

Notable observations:
- Branch 6 (Schedule J) is structurally absent — there is no else-if for Schedule J. Out of scope per `CLAUDE.md`.
- Branch 3 (Form 8615) has a defensive fallback to the bracket formula when `childFinalTaxLine18` is null — protects against half-filled forms.
- The bracket method label is determined by the $100,000 boundary AFTER the computation, even though the math is identical above and below.

### 8.3 Foreign Earned Income Tax Worksheet (`computeForeignEarnedIncomeTaxWorksheet`)

```java
totalExclusion = (form2555Taxpayer line 45 + line 50) + (form2555Spouse line 45 + line 50)
lineC = line15 + totalExclusion                      // the stack
lineD = isScheduleDTaxWorksheetRequired(scheduleD)
          ? computeScheduleDTaxWorksheet(lineC, ...)
        : (qualDiv > 0 OR LTCG present)
          ? computeQDCGWorksheet(lineC, ...)
        : computeTaxBracket(lineC, filingStatus)     // ordinary path
lineE = computeTaxBracket(totalExclusion, filingStatus)  // ALWAYS ordinary bracket
lineF = max(0, lineD - lineE)
```

Fallback: when `totalExclusion = 0` (no Form 2555 amounts), returns `computeTaxBracket(line15, filingStatus)` — degrades to Branch 7/8.

### 8.4 QDCG Worksheet (`computeQDCGWorksheet`)

```java
// Determine net long-term capital gain that gets the preferential rate.
if (Schedule D present and both lines 15 + 16 positive) {
    netLtcg = min(schedDLine15, schedDLine16)
} else if (line7bScheduleDNotRequired and capitalGainLoss > 0) {
    netLtcg = capitalGainLoss
} else {
    netLtcg = 0
}
preferentialIncome = min(taxableIncome, qualDiv + netLtcg)
ordinaryIncome = taxableIncome - preferentialIncome

// 2025 thresholds per filing status
zeroCeiling = SINGLE: 48350 | MFJ/QSS: 96700 | MFS: 48350 | HOH: 64750
twentyFloor = SINGLE: 533400 | MFJ/QSS: 600050 | MFS: 300025 | HOH: 566700

// Allocate preferential income to the three rate buckets
zeroPortion   = max(0, min(preferentialIncome, zeroCeiling - ordinaryIncome))
twentyPortion = max(0, taxableIncome - max(twentyFloor, ordinaryIncome + zeroPortion))
fifteenPortion = preferentialIncome - zeroPortion - twentyPortion

total = computeTaxBracket(ordinaryIncome, filingStatus)
      + 0 * zeroPortion
      + 0.15 * fifteenPortion
      + 0.20 * twentyPortion
```

### 8.5 Schedule D Tax Worksheet (`computeScheduleDTaxWorksheet`)

Handles 28%-rate gain (collectibles) and unrecaptured §1250 gain (depreciation recapture) by treating them as subsets of the long-term gain and applying their respective rates. The product's implementation is a simplified version of the full IRS worksheet — see Gap #14 below.

### 8.6 Tax-bracket formula (`computeTaxBracket`)

Per filing status, applies the bracket formula `tax = income × rate − subtraction_constant`. Returns identical results across both Tax Table (< $100k) and Tax Computation Worksheet (≥ $100k) ranges. Defaults to Single brackets when the filing status is not recognized.

### 8.7 Box 1, 2, 3 add-ons

```java
// Box 1: Form 8814 — sum across children
form8814AddonTax = sum(f8814.getLine15ExtraTax() for f8814 in form8814List)

// Box 2: Form 4972 — taxpayer + spouse
form4972AddonTax = (form4972Taxpayer.line30Total ?? 0) + (form4972Spouse.line30Total ?? 0)
// Note: form4972Spouse is null-shadowed on MFS at the call site

// Box 3: ECR only (the other 4 codes are not wired)
ecrAddonTax = hasEcrRecapture && ecrAmount > 0 ? ecrAmount : 0
box3Code = ecrAddonTax > 0 ? "ECR" : null
```

### 8.8 Final assembly

```java

taxAndCredits.setRegularTax(regularTax)
taxAndCredits.setComputationMethod(computationMethod)
taxAndCredits.setBox1Form8814Tax(box1Checked ? form8814AddonTax : null)
```

## 12. End-to-end worked example #2 (MFJ with qualified dividends, QDCG Worksheet)

**Inputs:**
- Filing status: Married filing jointly
- Line 11b (AGI): $200,000
- Line 12e: $31,500
- Line 13a/13b: $0
- Line 15 = $200,000 − $31,500 = $168,500
- Qualified dividends (line 3a): $20,000
- No Form 2555, no Schedule D (so no 28%-rate gain or §1250 gain), no kiddie tax, no Box 1/2/3

**Decision tree:** Branch 5 (QDCG Worksheet — qualified dividends > 0).

**QDCG calculation:**
```
netLtcg = $0 (no Schedule D, no line 7a cap gain distributions)
preferentialIncome = min($168,500, $20,000 + $0) = $20,000
ordinaryIncome = $168,500 − $20,000 = $148,500

MFJ thresholds: 0% ceiling = $96,700; 20% floor = $600,050

zeroPortion = max(0, min($20,000, $96,700 − $148,500)) = max(0, min($20,000, −$51,800)) = $0
twentyPortion = max(0, $168,500 − max($600,050, $148,500 + $0)) = max(0, $168,500 − $600,050) = $0
fifteenPortion = $20,000 − $0 − $0 = $20,000

tax on $148,500 ordinary (MFJ, 22% bracket $96,950–$206,700):
  = $148,500 × 0.22 − $10,172 = $32,670 − $10,172 = $22,498

total = $22,498 + $0 × 0 + $20,000 × 0.15 + $0 × 0.20
      = $22,498 + $3,000
      = $25,498
```

**Line 16 = $25,498.** `regularTax = $25,498`, `computationMethod = "QDCG"`. No Box checks.

(For comparison: if computed using ordinary brackets only on the full $168,500, tax would be $168,500 × 0.22 − $10,172 = $26,898 — about $1,400 higher. The QDCG Worksheet saves the user $1,400 by taxing the qualified dividends at the lower 15% preferential rate.)

## 13. End-to-end worked example #3 (Form 2555 stacking — Foreign Earned Income Tax Worksheet)

**Inputs:**
- Filing status: Single
- Line 11b (AGI): $40,000
- Line 12e: $15,750
- Line 13a/13b: $0
- Line 15 = $24,250
- Form 2555 line 45 (foreign earned income exclusion): $50,000
- Form 2555 line 50 (housing exclusion): $0
- No qualified dividends, no capital gains, no kiddie tax, no Box 1/2/3

**Decision tree:** Branch 2 (Form 2555 controls).

**Foreign Earned Income Tax Worksheet:**
```
totalExclusion = $50,000 + $0 = $50,000
lineC = $24,250 + $50,000 = $74,250          (the "stack")
lineD = computeTaxBracket($74,250, Single)   (no QDCG / Schedule D path)
      = $74,250 × 0.22 − $5,086 = $16,335 − $5,086 = $11,249
lineE = computeTaxBracket($50,000, Single)
      = $50,000 × 0.22 − $5,086 = $11,000 − $5,086 = $5,914
lineF = max(0, $11,249 − $5,914) = $5,335
```

**Line 16 = $5,335.** `regularTax = $5,335`, `computationMethod = "FOREIGN_EARNED_INCOME"`. No Box checks.

(For comparison: if the foreign earned income exclusion were ignored and the brackets applied to the $24,250 taxable income directly, tax would be $24,250 × 0.12 − $238.50 = $2,672. The worksheet result is HIGHER ($5,335 vs $2,672) because the stacking rule prevents the taxpayer from using a low marginal rate on the income that wasn't excluded. The exclusion still helps — without it, line 11b would have been $90,000 with line 15 of $74,250 and tax of $11,249 — but it doesn't let the taxpayer arbitrage the bracket structure.)
