## 1. What Line 19 is

Form 1040 line 19 is labeled **"Child tax credit or credit for other dependents from Schedule 8812"**.

Two separate credits flow through this single line:

- The **Child Tax Credit** — $2,200 per qualifying child for tax year 2025. A qualifying child must be under age 17 at the end of the year, a United States citizen / national / resident alien, have a valid Social Security Number (SSN) issued by the due date of the return, and meet the standard qualifying-child dependency rules.
- The **Credit for Other Dependents** — a flat $500 per dependent who cannot be used for the Child Tax Credit (typically dependents 17 or older, or dependents who hold an Individual Taxpayer Identification Number instead of a Social Security Number).

Both amounts are added together to form a tentative credit, the tentative credit is then phased out as Modified Adjusted Gross Income (Modified AGI) exceeds a filing-status-dependent threshold, and finally the result is capped at the remaining tax on Form 1040 line 18 (after subtracting earlier nonrefundable credits on Schedule 3).

A related refundable credit (the **Additional Child Tax Credit**, computed on Schedule 8812 Part II) ends up on **Form 1040 line 28**, not line 19. The two credits share most inputs and are produced together, so this document covers both for completeness.

```
Form 1040 line 19 = Schedule 8812 line 14         (nonrefundable Child Tax Credit + Credit for Other Dependents)
Form 1040 line 28 = Schedule 8812 line 27         (refundable Additional Child Tax Credit)
```

**Important rule** (from the 2025 Schedule 8812 Instructions): if Form 1040 line 18 is zero, Schedule 8812 line 14 will also be zero (no tax remains to absorb the nonrefundable credit), but that does NOT automatically eliminate the refundable Additional Child Tax Credit on line 28.

## 2. Plain-English computation (the IRS rules — independent of our code)

The Internal Revenue Service prescribes a three-part Schedule 8812:

- **Part I (lines 1–14)** computes the nonrefundable Child Tax Credit and Credit for Other Dependents that flow to Form 1040 line 19.
- **Part II-A (lines 15–20)** is the simpler refundable-credit path used by most filers (typically fewer than three qualifying children).
- **Part II-B (lines 21–27)** is the alternative refundable-credit path used when (a) three or more qualifying children are claimed, or (b) the filer is a bona fide resident of Puerto Rico.

### 2.1 Part I — Lines 1 through 14 (the nonrefundable credit that becomes Form 1040 line 19)

```
line1   = Form 1040 line 11b (Adjusted Gross Income)
line2a  = Puerto-Rico-excluded income that needs to be added back for this computation
            (out of project scope — assumed $0)
line2b  = Form 2555 foreign earned income exclusion + Form 2555 foreign housing exclusion
            (taxpayer + spouse on a joint return)
line2c  = Form 4563 line 15 (American Samoa exclusion add-back; out of project scope — assumed $0)
line2d  = line2a + line2b + line2c
line3   = line1 + line2d                                      (Modified AGI)

line4   = count of dependents whose "Child tax credit" box is checked on Form 1040
line5   = line4 × $2,200                                      (2025 Child Tax Credit per child)

line6   = count of dependents whose "Credit for other dependents" box is checked on Form 1040
line7   = line6 × $500                                        (2025 Credit for Other Dependents per dependent)

line8   = line5 + line7                                       (tentative combined credit)

line9   = $400,000 if filing status is Married Filing Jointly
        = $200,000 for every other filing status
            (Single, Head of Household, Married Filing Separately, Qualifying Surviving Spouse)

line10  = max(0, line3 − line9)                               (Modified AGI in excess of threshold)
            ★ If line10 > 0, round UP to the next $1,000 BEFORE multiplying on line 11.
line11  = (line10 rounded up to next $1,000) ÷ 1,000 × $50    (phase-out reduction at $50 per $1,000)
line12  = max(0, line8 − line11)                              (credit after phase-out)

line13  = Credit Limit Worksheet A result                     (see Worksheet A below)
line14  = min(line12, line13)                                 ★ Form 1040 line 19 OUTPUT
```

### 2.2 Credit Limit Worksheet A (used to produce line 13)

This worksheet caps the nonrefundable credit at "remaining tax after the earlier nonrefundable credits on Schedule 3 have already been used."

```
wA1 = Form 1040 line 18                                       (tax before credits — sum of line 16 + line 17)
wA2 = sum of these earlier 2025 Schedule 3 lines (those that the return actually uses):
        Schedule 3 line 1  — Form 1116 Foreign Tax Credit
        Schedule 3 line 2  — Form 2441 Credit for Child and Dependent Care Expenses
        Schedule 3 line 3  — Form 8863 Education Credits (the nonrefundable Lifetime Learning portion)
        Schedule 3 line 4  — Form 8880 Retirement Savings Contributions Credit (Saver's Credit)
        Schedule 3 line 5b — Form 5695 Part II Energy Efficient Home Improvement Credit
        Schedule 3 line 6d — Schedule R Credit for the Elderly or Disabled
        Schedule 3 line 6f — Clean Vehicle Credit (Form 8936 Schedule A)
        Schedule 3 line 6m — Previously Owned Clean Vehicles Credit
wA3 = max(0, wA1 − wA2)
wA4 = Worksheet B adjustment, if Worksheet B applies (see §2.5); otherwise 0
wA5 = max(0, wA3 − wA4)
Schedule 8812 line 13 = wA5
```

### 2.3 Part II-A — Lines 15 through 20 (refundable Additional Child Tax Credit, earned-income path)

The refundable Additional Child Tax Credit exists ONLY for qualifying children (line 4 count); the Credit for Other Dependents NEVER becomes refundable.

**Preconditions — line 27 is set to $0 if any of the following are true:**

1. line4 = 0 (no qualifying children).
2. The return files Form 2555 (foreign earned income exclusion blocks the refundable portion entirely — Internal Revenue Code §24(d)(5)(B)).
3. The 2025 filer-identification rule fails (see §2.7 below).
4. line16a = 0 (the entire tentative credit was already absorbed by tax on line 14).

**Line 15 is reserved for future use in 2025 and is NOT an opt-out checkbox.** See §4 for how the project handles this.

**If preconditions pass:**

```
line16a = max(0, line12 − line14)                             (excess credit not used on line 14)
line16b = line4 × $1,700                                      (2025 Additional Child Tax Credit cap per child)
line17  = min(line16a, line16b)                               (Additional-Child-Tax-Credit potential)

line18a = earned income for Additional-Child-Tax-Credit purposes
            ≈ Form 1040 line 1z (total wages) plus the elective nontaxable combat pay
              addition from line 1i; precise definition is the Internal Revenue Service
              earned-income chart for line 18a.
line18b = total nontaxable combat pay received in 2025
            (informational disclosure — the 2025 form uses line 18a, not line 18a + line 18b,
             for the line-19 computation below).

if line18a ≤ $2,500:
    line19 = 0
    line20 = 0
else:
    line19 = line18a − $2,500                                 (earned income above the floor)
    line20 = line19 × 15%                                     (15% Additional-Child-Tax-Credit rate)
```

**Routing decision after line 20:**

```
if line16b < $5,100 (i.e. fewer than three qualifying children):
    if the taxpayer is a bona fide resident of Puerto Rico:
        go to Part II-B (line 21)
    else:
        line27 = min(line17, line20)
        (DONE — Form 1040 line 28 = line27)

else (three or more qualifying children):
    if line20 ≥ line17:
        line27 = line17                                       (earned-income method already covers it)
        (DONE — Form 1040 line 28 = line27)
    else:
        go to Part II-B (line 21)
```

The threshold $5,100 = 3 × $1,700; it is the ACTC potential for exactly three qualifying children. Below $5,100, Part II-B cannot increase the credit so it is skipped.

### 2.4 Part II-B — Lines 21 through 27 (alternative refundable path: Social-Security-tax method)

For filers with three or more qualifying children (or bona fide Puerto Rico residents), the refundable Additional Child Tax Credit may be larger than the earned-income method allows. Part II-B compares the Social-Security-tax withheld during the year against the year's refundable credits already claimed, and uses the excess (if any) as an alternative basis.

```
line21 = Social-Security tax withheld + Medicare tax withheld during 2025
            (Form W-2 box 4 + box 6; Form 8919 line 6 if any; for railroad workers
             the equivalent Tier-1 Railroad Retirement amount; plus the employee's
             half of Schedule SE if self-employment is in scope)
line22 = Schedule 1 line 15 (deductible half of self-employment tax)
       + Schedule 2 line 5  (uncollected Social Security and Medicare tax on tip income — Form 4137)
       + Schedule 2 line 6  (uncollected Social Security and Medicare tax on wages — Form 8919)
       + Schedule 2 line 13 (Additional Medicare Tax from Form 8959)
line23 = line21 + line22

line24 = Form 1040 line 27a (Earned Income Credit)
       + Schedule 3 line 11 (refundable Schedule 3 credits — chiefly refundable Form 8863
                             American Opportunity Credit when claimed)

line25 = max(0, line23 − line24)                              ★ SUBTRACTION with a zero floor
                                                                (the IRS instruction says
                                                                 "Subtract line 24 from line 23.
                                                                  If zero or less, enter -0-.")
line26 = max(line20, line25)                                  (better of earned-income or Social-Security path)
line27 = min(line17, line26)                                  ★ Form 1040 line 28 OUTPUT
```

### 2.5 Credit Limit Worksheet B — when it applies (instead of Worksheet A)

For 2025, **Worksheet B replaces Worksheet A** when ALL THREE of the following are true:

1. The return claims one or more of these specific credits:
   - Form 8396 Mortgage Interest Credit
   - Form 8839 Qualified Adoption Expenses (the nonrefundable portion that lands on Schedule 3 line 6c)
   - Form 5695 Part I Residential Clean Energy Credit
   - Form 8859 District of Columbia First-Time Homebuyer Credit
2. The return is NOT filing Form 2555.
3. Schedule 8812 line 4 > 0 (the return has at least one qualifying child).

Worksheet B uses a more elaborate sequencing of credits and also reads Schedule 1 line 15, Schedule 2 lines 5, 6, and 13, Form 1040 line 27a, Schedule 3 line 11, and Schedule 3 lines 5a, 6c, 6g, and 6h.

### 2.6 Form 8862 recertification

If the taxpayer's Child Tax Credit, Additional Child Tax Credit, or Credit for Other Dependents was disallowed for any tax year after 2015 (for a reason other than an arithmetic error), the taxpayer must file Form 8862 (Information To Claim Certain Credits After Disallowance) with the return to reclaim the credit. If Form 8862 is required and not filed, the credit is forfeit.

### 2.7 2025 filer-identification (Social Security Number / Individual Taxpayer Identification Number) rule

Beginning tax year 2025, the rule for who may CLAIM the Child Tax Credit and Additional Child Tax Credit on a return changed:

- **Single, Head of Household, Married Filing Separately, Qualifying Surviving Spouse, or Nonresident filer:** the filer must have a **valid Social Security Number** issued for employment by the due date of the return (including extensions). An Individual Taxpayer Identification Number (a tax-processing number assigned to individuals not eligible for a Social Security Number — these numbers always begin with the digit "9") does NOT qualify.
- **Married Filing Jointly:** **only one spouse must have a valid Social Security Number.** The other spouse may have a Social Security Number, an Individual Taxpayer Identification Number, or an Adoption Taxpayer Identification Number (an interim number issued for an adopted child whose Social Security Number is not yet available).

If this filer-identification rule fails, the return cannot claim the Child Tax Credit or the Additional Child Tax Credit. The Credit for Other Dependents is NOT blocked by this rule (it uses the older, more permissive Social Security Number / Individual Taxpayer Identification Number / Adoption Taxpayer Identification Number rule).

### 3.1 Step-by-step

```
STEP 1. Form 8862 gate
   if ctcScreening.ctcPreviouslyDenied == true:
       if Form 8862 is missing or its claimsCTC flag is not true:
           emit blocking flag "FORM_8862_CTC_REQUIRED"
           return an empty Schedule 8812 (all zeros)
       if Form 8862 ctcEligible is not true:
           return an empty Schedule 8812 (all zeros)

STEP 2. Modified AGI (Part I lines 1–3)
   agi    = form1040.adjustments.line11bAmountFromLine11aAdjustedGrossIncome
   line2b = (form2555Taxpayer.foreignEarnedIncomeExclusion + .housingExclusionAmount)
          + (form2555Spouse  .foreignEarnedIncomeExclusion + .housingExclusionAmount)
   magi   = agi + line2b
   ★ Puerto Rico exclusion (line 2a) and Form 4563 line 15 (line 2c) are NOT added — out of scope.

STEP 3. Count dependents (Part I lines 4 + 6)
   for each DependentRecord d in dependents:
       if d.qualifiesForCTC():       numCtcChildren++
       else if d.qualifiesForODC():  numOdcDependents++
   (Mutual exclusion is structural — a dependent cannot satisfy both predicates because of the else-if.)

STEP 4. Tentative credit (Part I lines 5 + 7 + 8)
   line5 = numCtcChildren × $2,200                         (2025 OBBBA / Rev. Proc. 2024-40 §3.08)
   line7 = numOdcDependents × $500
   line8 = line5 + line7

STEP 5. Phase-out (Part I lines 9–12)
   threshold = $400,000 if filing status == "Married filing jointly", else $200,000
   excess    = magi − threshold
   line10Raw = max(0, excess)                              (stored on Schedule 8812 line 10 for PDF display)
   line10Rounded = ceil(line10Raw / 1000) × 1000           (only used to multiply on line 11)
   line11 = line10Rounded × 0.05                           (5% = $50 per $1,000)
   if line8 ≤ line11:
       line12 = 0
       line14 = 0
       line27 = 0
       return                                              (early exit — credit fully phased out)
   line12 = line8 − line11

STEP 6. Credit Limit Worksheet A (line 13)
   wA1 = form1040.taxAndCredits.totalTaxBeforeCredits      (= Form 1040 line 18)
   wA3 = Schedule 3 nonrefundable credits already claimed:
           foreignTaxCredit                                (Schedule 3 line 1)
         + childDependentCareCredit                        (Schedule 3 line 2)
         + educationCredits                                (Schedule 3 line 3)
         + retirementSavingsContributionsCredit            (Schedule 3 line 4)
         + elderlyDisabledCredit                           (Schedule 3 line 6d)
         + energyEfficientHomeImprovementCredit            (Schedule 3 line 5b)
         + cleanVehicleCredit                              (Schedule 3 line 6f)
         + creditPreviouslyOwnedCleanVehicles              (Schedule 3 line 6m)
   worksheetA = max(0, wA1 − wA3)
   line13 = worksheetA

STEP 7. Line 14 (Form 1040 line 19 output)
   line14 = min(line12, worksheetA)
   write line14 to taxAndCredits.childTaxCredit            ★ Form 1040 line 19 OUTPUT

STEP 8. "Elects no Additional Child Tax Credit" gate
   if ctcScreening.electsNoActc == true:
       line27 = 0
       return                                              (no IRS basis — see §4 G4)

STEP 9. Part II-A preconditions
   hasFiling2555 = (form2555Taxpayer != null OR form2555Spouse != null)
   if numCtcChildren == 0 OR hasFiling2555:
       line27 = 0
       return

STEP 10. 2025 SSN / ITIN rule
   identify ITIN by stripping non-digits and checking for leading "9":
       Single / Head of Household / Married Filing Separately / Qualifying Surviving Spouse:
           if taxpayer has ITIN → emit flag SCHEDULE_8812_ITIN_ACTC_BLOCKED; line27 = 0; return
       Married Filing Jointly:
           if BOTH taxpayer AND spouse have ITINs → emit flag; line27 = 0; return

STEP 11. Part II-A lines 16–17
   line16a = max(0, line12 − line14)
   if line16a == 0: line27 = 0; return
   line16b = numCtcChildren × $1,700                       (2025 OBBBA cap)
   line17  = min(line16a, line16b)

STEP 12. Part II-A earned income (lines 18a and 18b)
   line18a = form1040.income.totalWages                    (= Form 1040 line 1z)
   line18b = form1040.income.nontaxableCombatPayElection   (line 1i election; disclosure only)
   line18a += line18b                                      (combat pay is added INTO line 18a)

STEP 13. Part II-A lines 19 + 20
   if line18a > $2,500:
       line19 = line18a − $2,500
       line20 = line19 × 0.15
   else:
       line19 = 0
       line20 = 0

STEP 14. Routing
   isPuertoRicoResident = ctcScreening.isBonafidePuertoRicoResident == true
   if (line16b < $5,100) AND NOT isPuertoRicoResident:
       line27 = min(line17, line20)                        (fewer than 3 children, no Puerto Rico)
   else if line20 ≥ line17:
       line27 = line17                                     (earned-income method already covers it)
   else:
       proceed to Part II-B (STEP 15)

STEP 15. Part II-B (3+ children OR Puerto Rico resident, earned-income method insufficient)
   line21 = sum of W-2 socialSecurityTaxWithheldAmount across all W-2 entries
   line22 = schedule2.otherTaxes.uncollectedSocialSecurityMedicareTaxOnWages       (Schedule 2 line 5)
          + schedule2.otherTaxes.uncollectedSocialSecurityMedicareRrtaTax           (Schedule 2 line 6)
          + schedule2.otherTaxes.additionalMedicareTax                              (Schedule 2 line 13)
          (Schedule 1 line 15 self-employment tax NOT added — out of project scope)
   line23 = line21 + line22
   line24 = payments.earnedIncomeCredit                                              (Form 1040 line 27a)
          + payments.americanOpportunityCredit                                       (refundable AOTC)
          (Schedule 3 line 11 NOT added — deferred)

   line25 = line23 + line24                                ★ ADDITION  — see §4 BUG 1 below

STEP 16. Wire output

```

## 4. Identified gaps, bugs, and shortcomings

> Per the project rule "validate the application against the Internal Revenue Service documents, not the other way around," every discrepancy below treats the Internal Revenue Service rule as correct and flags the code.

### ✅ BUG 1 — Schedule 8812 line 25 used addition instead of subtraction (HIGH severity — **FIXED 2026-06-08**)

**Was at:** `TaxReturnComputeService.java` line 29991
**Pre-fix code:**

```java
BigDecimal line25 = line23.add(line24);
```

**What the Internal Revenue Service requires** (2025 Schedule 8812, line 25):

> "Subtract line 24 from line 23. If zero or less, enter -0-."

**Fix applied:**

```java
BigDecimal line25 = line23.subtract(line24).max(BigDecimal.ZERO);
```

**Impact (pre-fix):** Inflated line 25 by twice the value of line 24 in every Part II-B case (3+ qualifying children, or a Puerto Rico resident). Through `line26 = max(line20, line25)` and `line27 = min(line17, line26)`, this OVER-CLAIMED the refundable Additional Child Tax Credit on Form 1040 line 28 inside the `numCtcChildren × $1,700` ceiling.

**Evidence the code's own verification comment already cited the correct formula:** at line 29911 the inline verification breadcrumb stated `// line25 = max(0, line23 − line24)`, exactly matching the IRS rule. The implementation a few dozen lines below had been contradicting its own comment.

**Lock-in tests added 2026-06-08:**

### G4 (project history) — "Elects no Additional Child Tax Credit" opt-out has no IRS basis (LOW — closed as accepted design decision, NOT addressed in this pass)

The 2025 Schedule 8812 has no provision for a taxpayer to elect not to claim the refundable Additional Child Tax Credit. Line 15 of the form is explicitly marked "Reserved for future use." The code at line ~29683 and the front-end `form-ctc-actc-screening.component.ts` expose `electsNoActc` as a user-facing checkbox that, when true, sets `line27 = 0`. Recommendation if revisited: remove the opt-out from both the front-end and the back-end so users are not led to believe the choice has tax-law meaning.

### G6 (project history) — Credit Limit Worksheet A is correct only for currently-implemented Schedule 3 lines (LOW — open by design, NOT addressed in this pass)

Worksheet A is required to subtract Schedule 3 lines 5b (Form 5695 Part II Energy Efficient Home Improvement Credit), 6d (Schedule R Credit for the Elderly or Disabled), 6f (Form 8936 Schedule A Clean Vehicle Credit), and 6m (Previously Owned Clean Vehicles Credit) — all of which the code DOES subtract. It also references Schedule 3 lines that would belong to forms not yet in project scope: Form 8396 Mortgage Interest Credit (line 6l), Form 5695 Part I Residential Clean Energy Credit (line 5a), and Form 8859 District of Columbia First-Time Homebuyer Credit (line 6g). **Not changed in this pass** because each of those forms is itself out of project scope; adding subtractions for them now would silently zero them (no input), gaining nothing. Recommendation: extend the Worksheet A subtractions one by one as each Form 8396 / 5695 Part I / 8859 implementation lands.

### G7 (project history) — 2025 Social Security Number / Individual Taxpayer Identification Number rule uses a leading-"9" heuristic (LOW — sufficient for current scope, NOT addressed in this pass)

The 2025 filer-identification rule (see §2.7) distinguishes Social Security Numbers from Individual Taxpayer Identification Numbers. The code uses the IRS-documented heuristic: identifiers whose first digit (after non-digit stripping) is "9" are Individual Taxpayer Identification Numbers per IRS Publication 1635 (the range is 9XX-50 through 9XX-65, 9XX-70 through 9XX-88, 9XX-90 through 9XX-92, and 9XX-94 through 9XX-99). The Adoption Taxpayer Identification Number range (9XX-93-XXXX) ALSO begins with "9" but is only ever issued to dependents (adopted children) — it never appears on the filer's Social-Security-Number field. **Not changed in this pass** because the current behavior is correct for filers: a leading "9" on the filer's identifier reliably indicates an Individual Taxpayer Identification Number. The dependent-side Adoption / Individual / Social Security Number distinction is already handled by the upstream dependent intake (`DependentRecord.qualifiesForCTC()` / `.qualifiesForODC()`). The "issued on or before the due date of the return" timing rule is delegated to intake forms (the user attests by checking the per-dependent Child-Tax-Credit / Other-Dependents box).
