# Form 1040 — Line 1a — Total amount from Form(s) W-2, box 1

**Tax year: 2025**

This document explains how the dollar amount that goes on **Line 1a** of Form 1040 is calculated. Line 1a represents the wages, tips, and other compensation that the taxpayer (and the spouse on a joint return) earned during the year as reported by employers on **Form W-2** (Wage and Tax Statement — the year-end pay summary an employer sends).

The document is organized in three parts:

1. **The IRS rule.** What the controlling IRS publications say must happen. This is the source of truth.
2. **The code implementation.** What the application's backend Java code currently does.
3. **The comparison.** A side-by-side check showing whether the code follows the IRS rule honestly, and where the two diverge.

---

## Part 1 — The IRS rule (the source of truth)

### 1.1 Authority

The IRS publications used to write this document are in `C:\us-tax\docs\books\`:

- **2025 Instructions for Form 1040 / 1040-SR** (`i1040gi_2025.pdf`) — the controlling rule for the Line 1a label and routing.
- **Publication 17 — Your Federal Income Tax** (`p17.pdf`) — the master guide for individuals filing Form 1040; Chapter 5 covers wages and salaries.
- **Publication 525 — Taxable and Nontaxable Income** — defines which kinds of pay are taxable, including the carve-out for nonqualified deferred compensation plans and section 457 plans.
- **General Instructions for Forms W-2 and W-3** — defines W-2 box 1 (Wages, tips, and other compensation), box 8 (Allocated tips), box 11 (Nonqualified plans), and box 13 (Statutory employee).
- **About Form 4852** — describes the substitute-W-2 workflow used when the actual W-2 is unavailable.

### 1.2 The basic rule

From the 2025 Instructions for Form 1040, line 1a:

> Enter the total amount from Form(s) W-2, box 1. If a joint return, also include your spouse's income from Form(s) W-2, box 1.

In plain English: Line 1a is the **total of all eligible Form W-2 box 1 amounts** for the taxpayer and (on a joint return) the spouse, after the carve-outs described in section 1.4.

### 1.3 What "eligible" means

A Form W-2 box 1 amount is **eligible for Line 1a** when all four of these are true:

1. The amount appears in **box 1** of a Form W-2 (the box labeled "Wages, tips, other compensation").
2. The Form W-2 belongs to the taxpayer, or to the spouse if the filing status is *Married Filing Jointly*.
3. The Form W-2 is **not** marked as belonging to a *statutory employee* (a worker in a special IRS category whose income must be reported on Schedule C — Profit or Loss From Business — instead of as ordinary wages). The Statutory employee checkbox is box 13 on the W-2.
4. The amount is **not** subject to one of the carve-outs in section 1.4 below.

### 1.4 The IRS carve-outs (what to take OUT of Line 1a and report elsewhere)

The 2025 Instructions for Form 1040 tell the taxpayer to move two specific kinds of W-2 box 1 amounts out of Line 1a and onto a different line. In addition, the Schedule C instructions handle statutory-employee W-2s.

#### Carve-out A — Wages earned while incarcerated → Schedule 1, line 8u

If the taxpayer earned wages while serving time in a penal institution (prison or jail), those wages are reported on **Schedule 1, line 8u** (Wages earned while incarcerated) instead of Line 1a. Schedule 1 is the supplementary schedule for additional income and adjustments.

#### Carve-out B — Nonqualified deferred compensation or nongovernmental section 457 plan → Schedule 1, line 8t

If part of the W-2 box 1 amount represents a pension or annuity from a **nonqualified deferred compensation plan** (a retirement-style plan that did not meet all of the tax-favored requirements) or a **nongovernmental section 457 plan** (a deferred compensation plan for employees of tax-exempt organizations and similar entities), that amount is reported on **Schedule 1, line 8t** (Pension or annuity from a nonqualified deferred compensation plan or a nongovernmental section 457 plan) instead of Line 1a.

The W-2 reports this amount in **box 11** (Nonqualified plans). The IRS General Instructions for Forms W-2 and W-3 state that box 11 amounts are already included inside box 1. So when this carve-out applies, the box 11 amount must be subtracted from the box 1 amount before it contributes to Line 1a.

#### Carve-out C — Statutory employee W-2s → Schedule C (out of scope for this application)

If a W-2 has the **Statutory employee** box checked in box 13, the Schedule C instructions require the income to be reported on Schedule C, not on Line 1a. Schedule C (and self-employment income generally) is out of scope for this application; a statutory-employee W-2 must therefore be flagged so the return cannot finalize.

### 1.5 What does NOT go on Line 1a (because it goes on a different sub-line)

Form 1040 line 1 is split into ten sub-lines (1a through 1i, plus the total 1z). Each sub-line carries a different category of earned income. These do **not** go on Line 1a:

| Type of income | Goes on |
|---|---|
| Household employee wages **not** reported on a Form W-2 | Line 1b |
| Tip income not in W-2 box 1, and W-2 box 8 allocated tips (W-2 box 8 is never inside box 1) | Line 1c |
| Taxable Medicaid waiver payments not in W-2 box 1 | Line 1d |
| Taxable dependent care benefits from Form 2441 line 26 | Line 1e |
| Employer-provided adoption benefits from Form 8839 line 31 | Line 1f |
| Wages from Form 8919 line 6 (uncollected Social Security and Medicare tax on wages) | Line 1g |
| Other earned income (such as scholarship pay, jury duty pay, etc.) | Line 1h |
| Nontaxable combat pay election | Line 1i |

### 1.6 Form 4852 — substitute Form W-2 when the actual W-2 is unavailable

**Form 4852** (Substitute for Form W-2, Wage and Tax Statement) is used when the taxpayer cannot obtain a real Form W-2 in time to file — the employer never sent it, sent a wrong one, or did not correct an error. The taxpayer estimates the wages and withholding from a final pay stub or similar records.

When Form 4852 is used in place of a missing W-2, the wages reported on **Form 4852 line 7a** (Wages, tips, and other compensation) are added to Line 1a in the same way as a W-2 box 1 amount.

On a *Married Filing Separately* return, only the taxpayer's own Form 4852 flows into the taxpayer's Line 1a; the spouse's Form 4852 (if any) does not, because each spouse files a separate return.

### 1.7 The complete IRS equation (plain English)

Putting all parts together, the IRS rule for Line 1a is:

```text
For each Form W-2 belonging to the taxpayer or (on a joint return) the spouse:
    if the W-2 has the Statutory employee box checked (box 13):
        skip this W-2 entirely — it belongs on Schedule C, not Line 1a
    otherwise:
        contribution = W-2 box 1 amount
        if W-2 box 11 (Nonqualified plans) is positive:
            contribution = contribution − W-2 box 11 amount
            (the box 11 amount is routed to Schedule 1 line 8t instead)
        add contribution to the running total

Add the taxpayer's Form 4852 line 7a wages, if a Form 4852 substitute is used.
If the return is NOT Married Filing Separately, also add the spouse's Form 4852 line 7a wages.

If the taxpayer (and the spouse on a joint return) earned wages while incarcerated:
    running total = running total − inmate-wages amount
    (do not let the running total go below zero)
    (the inmate-wages amount is routed to Schedule 1 line 8u instead)

Line 1a = the resulting running total
```

The order of the last two steps matters in the implementation but not in the math: subtracting inmate wages **after** Form 4852 wages have been added correctly handles the rare case where the taxpayer's only wages come from a prison-issued Form 4852 substitute (no real W-2). The running total still ends up at zero with the inmate amount fully routed to Schedule 1 line 8u.

### 1.8 Whole-dollar rounding

The IRS allows whole-dollar rounding on Form 1040 as long as it is applied consistently across the return. The recommended practice is to keep cents while adding and round to whole dollars only when printing the final return.

### 1.9 Reference data (constants that may change year to year)

**Line 1a has no tax-year-specific numeric constant** — no rate, no threshold, no limit appears inside the Line 1a computation. Line 1a is a pure summation with carve-outs. The carve-out destinations (Schedule 1 line 8t and line 8u) are line labels, not numeric constants, and they have not changed between tax years 2024 and 2025.

The $2,800 IRS W-2 reporting threshold for household-employee wages that sometimes appears in Line 1a discussions actually belongs to **Line 1b**, not Line 1a.

## Part 2 — The code implementation

The backend code is in `C:\us-tax\us-tax-be\src\main\java\com\ustax\microservices\TaxReturnComputeService.java`. Line 1a is calculated by two helper methods plus a call site that combines them.

### 2.1 Methods involved

| Method name | Location | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| `computeLine1aWages(w2Entries, flags, uid)` | `TaxReturnComputeService.java:14573-14614` | Sums all Form W-2 box 1 amounts with Carve-out C (statutory employee → skip and emit blocking flag) and Carve-out B (W-2 box 11 nonqualified plans subtraction). Does NOT apply the inmate-wages subtraction (that happens at the call site so it can run after Form 4852 wages are added). |
| `extractForm4852Line1aWages(form4852Data)` | `TaxReturnComputeService.java:18417` | Reads `line7aWagesTipsOtherCompensation` from a Form 4852 personal form when `needsForm4852` is `true` and the substitute form type resolves to `"w2"`. |
| `buildIncome(...)` call site | `TaxReturnComputeService.java:5787-5815` | Combines the two above: calls `computeLine1aWages(...)`, adds the taxpayer's Form 4852 wages, adds the spouse's Form 4852 wages (unless Married Filing Separately), then subtracts the combined inmate-wages amount. |

### 2.4 The code equation (step by step)

In plain English the code does this. The order shown is the **actual code order**, which differs from the IRS narrative in section 1.7 because the inmate-wages subtraction is deferred to the call site so it applies after the Form 4852 wages are added.

```text
Step 1 — Call computeLine1aWages(w2Entries, flags, uid) at TaxReturnComputeService.java:14573
  Step 1.0  If there are no W-2 entries at all, return null and stop.
  Step 1.1  Initialize the running total to null (meaning "no value yet").
  Step 1.2  For each W-2 entry:
    Step 1.2a  If the entry is null, skip it.
    Step 1.2b  If the W-2's statutoryEmployee field is true:
                 - Emit the blocking flag STATUTORY_EMPLOYEE_W2_OUT_OF_SCOPE
                 - Log a warning
                 - Skip this W-2 entry entirely (do not add anything for it)
                 - Continue to the next W-2 entry
    Step 1.2c  Read wagesTipsOtherCompAmount (W-2 box 1) into a local value.
                 If it is null, skip this entry and continue.
    Step 1.2d  Read nonqualifiedPlansAmount (W-2 box 11).
                 If it is greater than zero, subtract it from the box 1 value
                 (clamped at zero — the result cannot go below zero).
    Step 1.2e  Add the resulting box 1 amount to the running total.
                 If the running total is currently null, this becomes the first value.
  Step 1.3  Return the running total (which may be null if every W-2 was skipped or null).

Step 2 — In buildIncome(...), after Step 1 returns, at TaxReturnComputeService.java:5787-5805
  Step 2.1  Determine isMfsReturn = (filingStatus equals "Married filing separately").
  Step 2.2  Add the taxpayer's Form 4852 line 7a wages to the running total by calling
            extractForm4852Line1aWages on the taxpayer's Form 4852 data.
  Step 2.3  If the return is NOT Married Filing Separately, also add the spouse's
            Form 4852 line 7a wages by the same method.

Step 3 — Inmate-wages subtraction at TaxReturnComputeService.java:5810-5815
  Step 3.1  Add together inmateWagesAmount from form-employment-taxpayer and
            inmateWagesAmount from form-employment-spouse.
  Step 3.2  If the running total is not null AND the combined inmate-wages amount is positive:
              Subtract the inmate-wages amount from the running total (clamped at zero).

Step 4 — Final storage
  Step 4.1  Round to whole dollars at PDF render time (the runtime value keeps full precision).
  Step 4.2  Store on Form1040.income.wages (the Line 1a slot).
  Step 4.3  A separate defensive coercion in buildIncome promotes null to BigDecimal.ZERO
            when hasEmploymentFlag is true (the user said they had employment income but
            no W-2 was found). This distinguishes "user claimed wages but they computed
            to 0" from "user did not claim wages."
```

### 2.5 Reference data constants used by the code for Line 1a

**None.** `computeLine1aWages` does not read any value from `ReferenceData.java`. The $2,800 household-employee threshold visible in the surrounding code at `TaxReturnComputeService.java:5792` is a Line 1b concern, not a Line 1a one.

### 3.1 Items the code does NOT support (intentional out-of-scope decisions)

1. **Form W-2C (Corrected Wage and Tax Statement)** — there is no separate statement type for a corrected W-2. If a W-2C arrives *before filing*, the user enters the corrected numbers directly on the regular W-2 statement entry. If the W-2C arrives *after filing*, the amended-return process (Form 1040-X) is out of scope for this application.
2. **Statutory-employee returns** — Schedule C is out of scope, so the application refuses to process a statutory-employee W-2 (emits a blocking flag).
3. **Form 1040-X (Amended U.S. Individual Income Tax Return)** — out of scope.

### 3.2 Items where the code is STRICTER than the IRS rule (defensive measures)

The code requires the user to explicitly answer whether their work was *household work* on the Employment Income form. If they say they had employment income but answer "no" to household work, and the application cannot find a W-2 matching their Social Security number (and no Form 4852 substitute is present), the blocking flag `MISSING_W2_EMPLOYMENT_INCOME_*` is emitted. The IRS rule does not require this — it is a defensive check to prevent filing a return that is silently missing a W-2.

### 3.3 Items where the code is INTENTIONALLY PERMISSIVE (taxpayer-controlled inputs)

Per the User Responsibility for Input Accuracy rule (recorded in `rules.md`), the items below are routine consequences of the upload-then-verify workflow and are not application defects. The application captures values from uploaded statements via AI extraction and asks the taxpayer to review, correct, and save. The taxpayer is responsible for the final values.

| Item | Detail |
|---|---|
| The Statutory employee checkbox (W-2 box 13) is not marked required and defaults to unchecked. | The taxpayer must verify this field during the post-upload W-2 review step. Whether the AI extractor captured box 13 correctly is exactly the kind of value the taxpayer is expected to verify. |
| Inmate wages can be entered twice (once on the Employment Income form via the W-2 picker, and once on the Other Incomes form as a non-W-2 amount). | This dual-source design is intentional — some inmates have both W-2 prison wages and separate non-W-2 cash wages, and both legitimately add together for Schedule 1 line 8u. The non-blocking flag `INMATE_WAGES_DUPLICATE_RISK_TAXPAYER` (or `_SPOUSE`) prompts the user to verify no overlap. |

## Part 4 — Summary

**Form 1040 Line 1a = sum of W-2 box 1 amounts (for the taxpayer and, on a joint return, the spouse), minus statutory-employee W-2s, minus W-2 box 11 nonqualified plans amounts, plus Form 4852 line 7a substitute-W-2 wages (with the spouse excluded on Married Filing Separately returns), minus wages earned while incarcerated.**

The code in `computeLine1aWages` (`TaxReturnComputeService.java:14573`) plus `extractForm4852Line1aWages` (`TaxReturnComputeService.java:18417`) plus the call site in `buildIncome` (`TaxReturnComputeService.java:5787-5815`) implements this rule honestly. The only differences from the IRS narrative are intentional design choices documented above:

- The inmate-wages subtraction happens at the call site **after** Form 4852 wages are added (so prison wages reported via Form 4852 are still correctly removed from Line 1a).
- Inmate wages live on the Employment Income personal form (derived from a W-2 picker) rather than on the W-2 entry itself.
- W-2C handling is consolidated into the regular W-2 entry rather than as a separate statement type.
- Statutory-employee W-2s are blocked rather than routed to Schedule C (because Schedule C is out of scope).
