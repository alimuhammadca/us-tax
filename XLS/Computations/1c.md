# Form 1040 — Line 1c — Tip income not reported on line 1a

**Tax year: 2025.**
**Last verified against the code on 2026-05-30.**

This document explains how the dollar amount that goes on **Line 1c** of Form 1040 is calculated. Line 1c reports the *taxable* portion of a worker's tip income that was **not already included on Line 1a** — that is, tips that did not flow through the worker's Form W-2 Box 1.

The document is organized in three parts:

1. **The IRS rule.** What the controlling IRS publications say must happen. This is the source of truth.
2. **The code implementation.** What the application's backend Java code currently does.
3. **The comparison.** A side-by-side check showing whether the code follows the IRS rule honestly, and where the two diverge.

A note on vocabulary used in this document:

- **Tips.** Payments made by customers directly to the worker (cash tips, electronic / charge tips written on a payment slip, or non-cash tips such as tickets, passes, or merchandise) on top of the bill for service. Tips are taxable income — they are not gifts.
- **Reported tips.** Tips a worker handed over to the employer on a written tip report. The employer is then required to withhold Social Security, Medicare and federal income tax on those tips and to include them in the worker's Form W-2 Box 1.
- **Unreported tips.** Cash and charge tips the worker did NOT report to the employer. The worker still owes income tax on them — and also the Social Security and Medicare tax that the employer would have withheld if the tips had been reported. The Social Security / Medicare side of that tax is computed on **Form 4137** (a companion form), not on Line 1c.
- **Allocated tips (Form W-2 Box 8).** An estimate the employer printed in Box 8 of the worker's Form W-2 when the worker's tip report came in below the IRS-published share of the establishment's gross receipts. Box 8 amounts are NOT in Box 1, so they go onto Line 1c (with one exception described below).
- **Form 4137 (Social Security and Medicare Tax on Unreported Tip Income).** The IRS form that computes the worker's Social Security (6.2 percent) and Medicare (1.45 percent) tax on unreported tips. Form 4137 line 13 flows to **Schedule 2 line 5** — a separate line on a separate page of the return. Form 4137 line 6 also feeds **Form 8959** (Additional Medicare Tax) when the worker's earnings exceed the high-earner threshold. None of those downstream flows change the dollar amount that appears on Line 1c itself.

## Part 1 — The IRS rule (the source of truth)

### 1.1 Authority

The IRS publications used to write this document are in `C:\us-tax\docs\books\` unless noted otherwise:

- **2025 Instructions for Form 1040 / 1040-SR** (`i1040gi_2025.pdf`) — the controlling rule for the Line 1c label and the three categories of tip income that belong on the line.
- **Publication 17 — Your Federal Income Tax** (`p17.pdf`) — the master guide for individuals filing Form 1040. The wages-and-other-earnings chapter restates the rule that all tips are taxable, including tips below the $20-per-month employer-reporting threshold.
- **Publication 525 — Taxable and Nontaxable Income** (`p525.pdf`) — confirms that all forms of tip income (cash, charge, non-cash) are taxable.
- **Publication 531 — Reporting Tip Income** (`p531.pdf`) — the IRS's *authoritative* publication on tip income: source for what counts as a tip versus a service charge, how to keep a daily tip diary, how to use the diary to override Form W-2 Box 8 (the *adequate-records* exception), and how non-cash tips are valued. The same rules are also restated in the 2025 Form 1040 instructions and in the Form 4137 instructions.
- **Form 4137 — Social Security and Medicare Tax on Unreported Tip Income** (`f4137--2025.pdf`) — defines the Form 4137 line-by-line computation that runs alongside Line 1c (income side) and lands on Schedule 2 line 5 (tax side). The $20-per-month reporting threshold is defined here and applies to Form 4137 line 5 only — not to Line 1c. Instructions are embedded on the form (Form 4137 is short).

Supplementary general-purpose references in `docs/books/`: `J.K. Lasser's Your Income Tax 2025` and `Taxes For Dummies 2024 Edition`. Both restate the same rule in lay terms; neither is the controlling source.

The local file `C:\us-tax\lines\1c.md` is the project's developer-facing specification for Line 1c. It cites the same publications. The companion knowledge file at `C:\us-tax\knowledge\line-1c-tip-income.md` and the dependency map at `C:\us-tax\dependencies\1c.md` track the same rules at finer-grained level (per-employer fields, downstream forms, test coverage).

### 1.2 The basic rule

From the 2025 Instructions for Form 1040, line 1c:

> Enter tip income you didn't report to your employer (other than the value of any noncash tips, such as tickets, passes, or merchandise) on line 1c. Also include any allocated tips from your Form(s) W-2 shown in box 8, unless you can prove that your unreported tips are less than the amount in box 8. (Include the value of any noncash tips in line 1c.)

In plain English, the IRS rule for Line 1c is:

```text
Line 1c = unreported cash and charge tips
        + tips shown in Form W-2 Box 8 (allocated tips), with one exception
        + the fair market value of any non-cash tips received
```

Each of the three categories is explained in detail below.

### 1.3 Category one — Unreported cash and charge tips

These are tips the worker received in cash or as charged amounts on a customer's bill and did NOT report to the employer on a written tip report.

The rule in plain English: any cash or charge tips the worker received during the tax year that were not turned in to the employer on a written tip report belong on Line 1c, regardless of amount. There is no minimum and no maximum.

There is an *adjacent* rule that applies to a **different** line (Form 4137 line 5, not Line 1c): tips totaling less than **$20** for a given employer for a given month are exempt from the Social Security / Medicare side of the tax. The $20-per-month threshold reduces Form 4137 line 6, but it does NOT reduce Line 1c — those sub-twenty-dollar tips are still taxable income.

### 1.4 Category two — Allocated tips from Form W-2 Box 8

When the workplace participates in the IRS Tip Rate Determination Agreement (or a similar allocation program), and the worker reported fewer tips than 8 percent (or the establishment-specific rate) of the employer's gross receipts, the employer is required to print an *allocated* amount in **Box 8** of the worker's Form W-2. Box 8 amounts are NOT included in Box 1, so they must be added separately on Line 1c.

There is one exception. Publication 531 (Reporting Tip Income) gives the worker a way out of using the Box 8 figure: if the worker keeps a **daily tip diary** (or other adequate written record) and that record shows the actual unreported tips were *less than* the Box 8 allocation, the worker may report the **smaller, substantiated** figure on Line 1c instead. To use the adequate-records exception the worker must:

1. Have kept a daily record (or equivalent) of tips received.
2. Be able to substantiate the lower amount on request.
3. Attach a statement to the return explaining why Box 8 is being overridden.

If the adequate-records claim fails any of these conditions — for example the worker says they have adequate records but cannot produce the substantiated amount — the Box 8 figure is used.

### 1.5 Category three — Non-cash tips (fair market value)

Tickets, passes, merchandise, room nights, and other property received as tips count as taxable income at their fair market value. They go on Line 1c.

Non-cash tips are an **income-only** category: the worker does NOT owe Social Security or Medicare tax on them (only cash and charge tips trigger the Form 4137 computation), and they do NOT appear on Form 4137.

### 1.6 What does NOT go on Line 1c

| Type of tip | Goes on | Why |
|---|---|---|
| Cash and charge tips that were reported to the employer on a written tip report | Line 1a (already included in Form W-2 Box 1) | Avoid double-counting |
| Tips less than $20 per month per employer (reporting threshold) — same character as unreported cash/charge tips | Still on Line 1c for income tax | The $20/month rule reduces Form 4137 line 6, not Line 1c |
| Federal/state/local government tips on which Medicare-only tax applies (no Social Security) | Still on Line 1c for income tax | The Medicare-only treatment affects Form 4137 line 10, not Line 1c |
| Service charges (the establishment added a fixed gratuity) | Line 1a as ordinary wages | Service charges are wages, not tips |
| Tips the worker passed on to other employees under a tip-sharing or tip-pooling arrangement | Not reported by this worker (the receiving worker reports them) | Only the tips the worker actually keeps are reported |
| Self-employment tip income | Schedule C — Profit or Loss From Business | Out of scope for this application |
| Uncollected Social Security or Medicare tax on tips already reported to the employer (Form W-2 Box 12 code A or code B) | Schedule 2 line 13 — via a separate Uncollected Social Security / Medicare form | Not the Line 1c income placement |

### 1.7 The Married Filing Separately rule

On a Married Filing Separately return, each spouse reports only their own tip income. Do NOT aggregate the other spouse's tips. The Form 4137 computation, the Schedule 2 line 5 contribution and the Form 8959 line 6 base are likewise per-filer on a Married Filing Separately return.

### 1.8 The complete IRS equation (plain English)

```text
For each separate tip employer the worker had during the tax year:

    unreported_for_income(employer):
        unreported = max(0, total cash and charge tips received
                              - tips reported to the employer)
        if Form W-2 Box 8 (allocated tips) > 0:
            if the worker has adequate records (a daily tip diary)
               AND the substantiated unreported amount is < Box 8 allocation:
                unreported_for_income = substantiated amount
            else:
                unreported_for_income = max(unreported, Box 8 allocation)
        else:
            unreported_for_income = unreported

    add unreported_for_income(employer) to a running per-person total.
    add non_cash_tips_fair_market_value(employer) to the same per-person total.

For a Married Filing Separately return:
    include only the filer's own tip employers.
    Do not aggregate the spouse's tip employers.

For a joint return (Married Filing Jointly):
    sum the taxpayer's per-person total + the spouse's per-person total.

Line 1c = the resulting total, rounded to whole dollars at the very end
          (see Section 12 of the Form 1040 instructions on "Rounding Off
          to Whole Dollars" — rounding is optional but, if used, must be
          applied consistently throughout the return).
```

### 1.9 Form 4137 — the companion tax, computed separately

Line 1c is the *income-tax* placement of unreported tips. The matching *Social Security and Medicare* tax on the same cash-and-charge tips (not the non-cash component) is computed on Form 4137 and routed to Schedule 2 line 5 — a separate line on a separate page of the return. The two are linked because they share most of their inputs, but the dollar amount that lands on **Line 1c specifically** is governed only by the equation in Section 1.8 above.

## Part 2 — The code implementation

The backend Java code that calculates Line 1c lives in `TaxReturnComputeService.java`:

- **`computeTips(...)`** — aggregates the taxpayer's amount and the spouse's amount, applying the Married Filing Separately guard.
- **`computeTipsForPerson(...)`** — calculates one person's tip-income total by walking the list of tip employers entered on that person's Tip Income form, with a fallback that auto-builds employer rows from Form W-2 Box 8 when the user has not yet filled the Tip Income form.

These methods are invoked once per compute pass. The aggregated result is stored on the Form 1040 output model via `income.setTipIncome(line1c)` after a final `roundMoney(...)` step in the caller (the `buildIncome(...)` method).

> Convention: backend references in this document use method names rather than source-code line numbers (line numbers drift with refactors). Established 2026-05-06; restated here for clarity. See knowledge file `line-1c-tip-income.md` §4.

### 2.3 Reference data (constants that may change year-to-year)

For **Line 1c itself**, the only "constant" that influences how a worker decides what to enter is the $20-per-month per-employer reporting threshold — and that threshold gates a *different* line (Form 4137 line 5), not Line 1c. The Line 1c math is a pure sum.

The constants that feed the companion Form 4137 computation are listed here for completeness, because they ride along with the same input forms:

| Reference constant | 2025 value | Where it lives | Where it is used |
|---|---|---|---|
| Social Security wage base | $176,100 | `ReferenceData.SOCIAL_SECURITY_WAGE_BASE` (also exposed via `/api/reference-data`) | Form 4137 line 7 (the maximum amount of wages subject to the 6.2 percent Social Security tax in 2025). |
| Social Security tax rate | 6.2 percent | `new BigDecimal("0.062")` inline constant in `computeTipsForPerson(...)` | Form 4137 line 11. |
| Regular Medicare tax rate | 1.45 percent | `new BigDecimal("0.0145")` inline constant in `computeTipsForPerson(...)` | Form 4137 line 12. |
| $20-per-month tip-reporting threshold | $20 | Not a numeric constant in code; the worker enters the resulting `line5Under20PerMonthTips` amount directly | Documented on the input form and on Form 4137 line 5. Not used in the Line 1c math. |
| Additional Medicare Tax rate | 0.9 percent | `new BigDecimal("0.009")` in Form 8959 code | Form 8959 / Schedule 2 line 11 — downstream of Line 1c through Form 4137 line 6. Not used in the Line 1c math. |
| Additional Medicare Tax thresholds | $200,000 Single / Head of Household / Qualifying Surviving Spouse; $250,000 Married Filing Jointly; $125,000 Married Filing Separately | `ReferenceData.ADDITIONAL_MEDICARE_THRESHOLD_*` | Form 8959 only. Not used in the Line 1c math. |

The 2024 Social Security wage base was $168,600. Whenever the IRS updates this number for a future tax year, the value in `ReferenceData.SOCIAL_SECURITY_WAGE_BASE` must be updated.

### 2.4 The code equation (Line 1c portion)

In plain English, the code does this (matching the IRS rule from Section 1.8):

```text
Step A (in the caller — buildIncome / buildForm1040) — determine the
       Married Filing Separately flag.
    Read filing-status.filingStatus (normalize whitespace and capitalization).
    If it equals "Married filing separately" then set isMfsReturn = true.
    Otherwise set isMfsReturn = false.

Step B (in the caller) — invoke computeTips(...).
    computeTips returns a TipComputation record carrying:
        line1c          — the unrounded sum (taxpayer + spouse, if applicable)
        form4137Taxpayer
        form4137Spouse
        totalTipTax     — the combined Form 4137 line 13 totals (for Schedule 2 line 5)

Step C (back in the caller, inside buildIncome) — round and store.
    line1cRounded = roundMoney(tipIncome from TipComputation)
    if line1cRounded is not null:
        income.tipIncome = line1cRounded
    (When line1c is null, the field is left unset and is omitted from
     the computed JSON output — Line 1c on the form prints blank.)
```

And **`computeTips(...)`** itself is:

```text
Compute the taxpayer's TipResult:
    taxpayer = computeTipsForPerson(tip-income-taxpayer, w2Entries, taxpayerSsn)

Compute the spouse's TipResult, but ONLY when not Married Filing Separately:
    if isMfs is true: spouseResult = TipResult(null, null, null)  (skip the spouse entirely)
    if isMfs is false: spouseResult = computeTipsForPerson(tip-income-spouse, w2Entries, spouseSsn)

Aggregate the Line 1c subtotals across the two persons:
    rawLine1c = addNonNull(taxpayer.line1c, spouseResult.line1c)
    (A null subtotal contributes nothing; if BOTH are null, rawLine1c is null.)

Aggregate the Form 4137 line 13 totals:
    totalTipTax = addNonNull(taxpayer.tipTax, spouseResult.tipTax)

Return TipComputation(rawLine1c, taxpayer.form4137, spouseResult.form4137, totalTipTax).
```

And **`computeTipsForPerson(data, w2Entries, ssn)`** for one person is:

```text
Soft gate — explicit "No" exclusion.
    Read hasUnreportedTips. If it is exactly false:
        return TipResult(null, null, null).
    (The gate is "soft" — null or unset answers fall through to the auto-fill
     step below so the Form W-2 Box 8 auto-fill convenience still works for
     users who have not yet opened the Tip Income form.)

Auto-fill step — build synthetic employer rows from Form W-2 Box 8.
    Read entries = tipsByEmployer.
    If entries is empty:
        Walk every Form W-2 in the return whose employee Social Security Number
        matches the person's SSN AND whose Box 8 allocated tips > 0:
            create a synthetic employer row populated with employer name,
            employer EIN, allocated tips, and the Social Security wage/tip boxes.
    If entries is STILL empty:
        return TipResult(null, null, null).

Per-employer loop — for each employer entry:
    received       = parseAmount(entry.totalTipsReceived)
    reported       = parseAmount(entry.totalTipsReportedToEmployer)
    allocated      = parseAmount(entry.allocatedTipsW2Box8)
    substantiated  = parseAmount(entry.substantiatedUnreportedTipsIfLessThanAllocated)
    hasAdequate    = entry.hasAdequateRecordsUnreportedLessThanAllocated

    unreportedCashCharge = max(0, received - reported)   (a null received contributes null)

    if allocated > 0:
        if hasAdequate is true AND substantiated is missing:
            mark `adequateRecordsClaimedButNoSubstantiatedAmount = true`
            (this raises a blocking flag after the loop; compute still proceeds
             through the loop using the allocated-tips override as a fallback)

        if hasAdequate is true AND substantiated is present AND substantiated < allocated:
            unreportedForIncome = substantiated         (adequate-records reduction)
        else:
            base = unreportedCashCharge or 0
            unreportedForIncome = max(base, allocated)  (allocated-tips override)
    else:
        unreportedForIncome = unreportedCashCharge

    accumulate per-employer totals:
        totalCashChargeTips          += received
        totalReportedTips            += reported
        totalUnreportedTipsForIncome += unreportedForIncome
        totalUnderTwentyTips         += entry.line5Under20PerMonthTips
        totalNonCashTips             += entry.nonCashTipsFmv
        totalMedicareOnlyTips        += entry.medicareOnlyGovernmentTips
        totalRrtaComp                += entry.rRTACompensationW2Box14
        totalAllocatedTips           += allocated

    build a Form4137Employer row (name, EIN, total tips received, reported tips)
    and add it to the per-person `employers` list (used by the Form 4137 output).

After the per-employer loop:

Compose Line 1c amount for this person:
    line1cIncome = totalUnreportedTipsForIncome + totalNonCashTips
    (this is the taxpayer's or the spouse's contribution to Line 1c.)

Adequate-records flag check (post-loop):
    if adequateRecordsClaimedButNoSubstantiatedAmount is true:
        emit blocking flag ADEQUATE_RECORDS_MISSING_SUBSTANTIATED_AMOUNT_{TAXPAYER,SPOUSE}.

Compose Form 4137 (only when needed — see gate below):
    Line 4 of Form 4137 = totalUnreportedTipsForIncome
    Line 5 = totalUnderTwentyTips
    Line 6 = max(0, Line 4 − Line 5)
    Line 7 = SOCIAL_SECURITY_WAGE_BASE  (2025: $176,100)
    Line 8 = (W-2 boxes 3 + 7 for the matching SSN, or the form-input fallback)
             + totalRrtaComp
    Line 9 = max(0, Line 7 − Line 8)
    Line 10 = min((Line 6 − totalMedicareOnlyTips), Line 9)
    Line 11 = Line 10 × 6.2 percent     (Social Security tax)
    Line 12 = Line 6 × 1.45 percent     (Medicare tax)
    Line 13 = Line 11 + Line 12         (total tax — flows to Schedule 2 line 5)

Form 4137 production gate:
    Create the Form 4137 object ONLY when:
        Line 4 > 0 AND (Line 6 > 0 OR totalAllocatedTips > 0).
    Otherwise leave Form 4137 null. (All-noncash and all-under-$20 paths get
    a Line 1c amount but no Form 4137.)

Missing-SS-W-2 flag check:
    If Line 6 > 0 AND the Social Security wage base from the W-2 step is null:
        emit blocking flag MISSING_SS_W2_DATA_{TAXPAYER,SPOUSE}.
        Line 11 (Social Security tax) is set to null; Line 12 (Medicare tax)
        still computes; the blocking flag prevents the return from being persisted.

Missing-employer-EIN flag check (post Form-4137-creation):
    If Form 4137 was created AND any employer row has a blank EIN:
        emit blocking flag MISSING_EMPLOYER_EIN_FORM_4137_{TAXPAYER,SPOUSE}.

return TipResult(line1cIncome, line13TotalTax, form4137).
```

### 2.5 Validation flags the code may emit

All three flags are **blocking**, in accordance with the save-vs-compute principle: the user can always save the Tip Income form (the user interface never refuses a save), but the return cannot be produced until the user resolves the underlying issue. The blocking flag causes the compute endpoint to return HTTP 409 with the flag list in the response body, so the user interface can present a clear "what's wrong and how to fix it" message.

| Flag name | Blocking? | When it fires | Effect on Line 1c |
|---|---|---|---|
| `MISSING_SS_W2_DATA_TAXPAYER` (or `_SPOUSE`) | Yes — blocking | Form 4137 line 6 > 0 AND Social Security wages/tips are unavailable from any matching Form W-2 in the system | Line 1c is computed normally — Medicare tax (line 12) still computes; Social Security tax (line 11) is suppressed until the user supplies the Box 3 / Box 7 data through the Form W-2 or via the form-input fallback fields. |
| `ADEQUATE_RECORDS_MISSING_SUBSTANTIATED_AMOUNT_TAXPAYER` (or `_SPOUSE`) | Yes — **non-overrideable** per `rules.md` §17 (since 2026-05-30; previously blocking-but-overrideable since 2026-05-19) | The worker claimed `hasAdequateRecordsUnreportedLessThanAllocated = true` but did not enter `substantiatedUnreportedTipsIfLessThanAllocated` | Line 1c is computed using the **allocated-tips override** as a defensive fallback (the higher Form W-2 Box 8 amount). Because the computed Line 1c is wrong — the user has said the lower number applies — the flag is blocking. The user must either enter the substantiated total (to use the lower amount) or change the "adequate records" answer to No (to use the W-2 Box 8 amount). Passing `overrideFlags=true` does NOT bypass this flag (added to `NonOverrideableFlags.CODES` on 2026-05-30). |
| `MISSING_EMPLOYER_EIN_FORM_4137_TAXPAYER` (or `_SPOUSE`) | Yes — blocking (since 2026-05-19) | A Form 4137 was produced AND any employer row on it has a blank or missing EIN | Line 1c and Form 4137 are mathematically correct. The flag is blocking because IRS e-file would reject a Form 4137 with a blank EIN cell — producing the return without the EIN would only mislead the user. The user enters the EIN from box b of the employer's Form W-2 in every row that triggers Form 4137. |

The user interface shows an advisory hint next to each affected field when the condition is met, so users see clear "enter this to compute your return" guidance directly on the form — but the field is never made required and Save never refuses.

### 2.6 Married Filing Separately guard

The aggregator `computeTips(...)` accepts a boolean `isMfs` argument. When true:

- The spouse's `computeTipsForPerson(...)` call is **skipped**.
- The spouse's contribution to `tipIncome` (Line 1c), `form4137Spouse`, and the spouse's share of Schedule 2 line 5 and Form 8959 line 6 are all suppressed.

This mirrors the same pattern used on Line 1b (household-employee wages). The user interface disables the spouse Tip Income form on non-Married-Filing-Jointly returns; the backend gate is a defense in depth for direct-API submissions that could otherwise persist stale spouse data.

### 2.7 Auto-fill from Form W-2 Box 8

A user who has only **allocated** tips (Box 8 with no further data entry) is not required to open the Tip Income form. `computeTipsForPerson(...)` invokes a helper (`buildTipEntriesFromW2(...)`) that builds synthetic employer rows from every Form W-2 in the return that has a non-zero Box 8 and a matching employee Social Security Number. The synthetic row carries the allocated amount through to Line 1c and Form 4137.

There is a documented defensive bug in this helper (tracked in `outstanding.md`): when the helper is called for the spouse on a return that has no spouse identification record, the spouse's Social Security Number is null and the SSN filter is bypassed — every Form W-2 is then absorbed into the spouse's tip computation. In practice this is mitigated by (a) the Married Filing Separately guard which skips the spouse entirely on Married Filing Separately, and (b) the user interface requiring spouse identification before the spouse Tip Income form is reachable. A future fix should make the helper return empty when called with a null or empty SSN.

## Part 3 — Comparison between the IRS rule and the code

Reading the two parts side-by-side, the code follows the IRS rule faithfully on every point that affects the dollar amount on Line 1c. The table below maps each IRS requirement from Part 1 to the corresponding code step from Part 2.

| IRS requirement | Code behavior | Faithful? |
|---|---|---|
| Include unreported cash and charge tips (received minus reported) | Per-employer formula `unreportedCashCharge = max(0, received - reported)` in `computeTipsForPerson(...)` | Yes |
| Include allocated tips from Form W-2 Box 8 | `allocated > 0` branch overrides `unreportedForIncome` to at least the Box 8 amount, unless the adequate-records exception applies | Yes |
| Apply the adequate-records exception (use a substantiated lower amount when the worker has a daily tip diary) | When `hasAdequateRecordsUnreportedLessThanAllocated = true` AND `substantiatedUnreportedTipsIfLessThanAllocated < allocated`, the substantiated amount replaces the Box 8 figure | Yes — and the code surfaces the "claimed records but missing substantiated amount" inconsistency through the blocking `ADEQUATE_RECORDS_MISSING_SUBSTANTIATED_AMOUNT_*` flag at compute time |
| Include the fair market value of non-cash tips | `totalNonCashTips` from each employer row is added to `line1cIncome` | Yes |
| Sub-$20-per-month tips do not reduce Line 1c (only Form 4137 line 6) | `line5Under20PerMonthTips` is accumulated into `totalUnderTwentyTips` and subtracted on Form 4137 line 6, NOT from `line1cIncome` | Yes |
| Government-employer Medicare-only tips do not reduce Line 1c (only Form 4137 line 10) | `medicareOnlyGovernmentTips` is accumulated into `totalMedicareOnlyTips` and subtracted on Form 4137 line 10, NOT from `line1cIncome` | Yes |
| On a Married Filing Separately return, do not include the spouse's tip income | The `computeTips(...)` aggregator skips the spouse call when `isMfs` is true | Yes |
| On a Married Filing Jointly return, include both spouses' tip income — each on its own Form 4137 | Two separate `computeTipsForPerson(...)` calls produce independent `TipResult`s; Line 1c sums them and each spouse gets a distinct Form 4137 object on the output | Yes |
| Whole-dollar rounding at the very end of the computation | `roundMoney(...)` is applied in `buildIncome(...)` to the `TipComputation.line1c` value before storing on `income.tipIncome` | Yes |

### 3.1 Items the code does NOT support (intentional out-of-scope decisions)

The following are documented as out of scope and not bugs:

1. **Form W-2 Box 12 code A / code B** — *uncollected* Social Security or Medicare tax on tips already reported to the employer. This is a separate path (Schedule 2 line 13). It is captured on the *Uncollected Social Security / Medicare* form (`uncollected-ss-medicare-taxpayer.xlsx` / `…-spouse.xlsx`) and computed independently of Line 1c. It does not change the Line 1c amount.
2. **Self-employment tip-receiving businesses** (an independent contractor who receives tips while running their own Schedule C business) — Schedule C is out of scope for this application.

### 3.2 Items where the code is stricter than the IRS rule (defensive measures)

1. **Soft gate on `hasUnreportedTips = false`.** When the user explicitly answers "No" to the "Did you receive tip income not fully reported to an employer?" question, the code returns null immediately and skips both Line 1c and Form 4137. This is "soft" — null or unset answers fall through to the auto-fill step so the Form W-2 Box 8 auto-fill still works for new users who have not yet opened the Tip Income form.
2. **Form W-2 is canonical for the Social Security wage base on Form 4137 line 8.** The form-input fallback fields `socialSecurityWagesW2Box3` and `socialSecurityTipsW2Box7` fire only when no matching Form W-2 exists in the system. When a matching Form W-2 has an explicit zero in Box 3 / Box 7, the Form W-2 wins (returns zero, not null) and the form-input fallback is silently ignored. The reasoning is that a typo'd Form W-2 should be corrected at the source, not patched through the Tip Income form. This is documented in `lines/1c.md` §7.6 and locked in by the unit test `line1cSsWageBaseW2IsCanonicalEvenWhenZero`. (This rule affects Form 4137 only; it does not change the Line 1c amount.)
3. **Employer EIN required on Form 4137 rows.** The IRS Form 4137 requires the EIN for each employer listed. The backend emits a blocking flag at compute time whenever Form 4137 is produced with any blank-EIN row (since 2026-05-19, in line with the save-vs-compute principle).
4. **Adequate-records compute-time enforcement.** When the user claims `hasAdequateRecordsUnreportedLessThanAllocated = true` without entering the substantiated amount, the backend emits a blocking flag at compute time so the resulting (wrong) Line 1c is not persisted (since 2026-05-19). The user can resolve this by entering the substantiated amount or changing the "adequate records" answer to No.

### 3.3 Items captured in the input form but not used in the Line 1c math

| Field | What it does today | Why it is captured anyway |
|---|---|---|
| `line5Under20PerMonthTips` (per-employer) | Reduces Form 4137 line 6 (the Social Security / Medicare base). Does not reduce Line 1c. | Required for Form 4137 line 5 disclosure. |
| `medicareOnlyGovernmentTips` (per-employer) | Reduces Form 4137 line 10 (the Social Security base only). Does not reduce Line 1c. | Required for Form 4137 line 10. |
| `rRTACompensationW2Box14` (per-employer) | Adds to Form 4137 line 8 (the wage base subtracted from the Social Security ceiling). Does not change Line 1c. | Captures railroad-retirement compensation that is otherwise invisible from Form W-2 boxes 3 / 7. |
| `socialSecurityWagesW2Box3` / `socialSecurityTipsW2Box7` (per-employer) | Form 4137 line 8 fallback when no matching Form W-2 is in the system. Does not change Line 1c. | Lets a user complete Form 4137 even if their Form W-2 is not in the system; the user interface marks these "FALLBACK ONLY". |

These are honest, intentional non-uses — not bugs.

### 3.4 Missing fields or possible gaps

**For Line 1c itself: none identified.**

- The three IRS income categories (unreported cash/charge, allocated Box 8, non-cash) each have a corresponding input field on the Tip Income form (`totalTipsReceived` minus `totalTipsReportedToEmployer`, `allocatedTipsW2Box8`, and `nonCashTipsFmv`).
- The adequate-records exception is captured by the pair `hasAdequateRecordsUnreportedLessThanAllocated` / `substantiatedUnreportedTipsIfLessThanAllocated`.
- The output field `tipIncome` on the Form 1040 model is wired to the front-end Form 1040 preview and to the data-flow diagram at `c:/us-tax/diagrams/1c.drawio`.
- The Married Filing Separately guard is implemented and tested by `line1cMfsGuard_spouseTipIncomeAndForm4137NotAggregatedOnMfs` in `TaxReturnComputeServiceTest.java`.
- The Form 4137 production gate (line 4 > 0 AND (line 6 > 0 OR `totalAllocatedTips` > 0)) and the all-noncash / all-under-$20 negative paths are tested by `line1cNoncashOnly_lineIncomeReportedButNoForm4137` and `line1cAllUnder20PerMonth_lineIncomeReportedButNoForm4137`.

**Documentation gaps (not implementation gaps):**

None as of 2026-05-30 — Publication 531 (`p531.pdf`) and Form 4137 (`f4137--2025.pdf`) both downloaded to `docs/books/`. The full IRS citation chain for Line 1c is now locally available.

**Defensive bug (tracked in `outstanding.md`, low priority):** the null-SSN fallthrough in the Form W-2 auto-fill helper (described in Section 2.7). It does not affect Line 1c on returns where the Married Filing Separately guard fires or where the spouse identification is properly populated — which is every return reachable through the user interface.

## Part 4 — Summary

**Form 1040 Line 1c = sum, across the taxpayer and (on a non-Married-Filing-Separately return) the spouse, of, for each tip employer: the *unreported-for-income* amount (cash and charge tips not reported to the employer, with the Form W-2 Box 8 allocated-tips override unless the worker has adequate records substantiating a lower figure), plus the fair market value of any non-cash tips, rounded to whole dollars at the very end.**

The $20-per-month per-employer reporting threshold, the government-employer Medicare-only treatment, and the Form 4137 computation of Social Security and Medicare tax on the unreported portion are computed alongside Line 1c but route to **Schedule 2 line 5** and **Form 8959** — they do not change the dollar amount on Line 1c itself.
