# Form 1040 — Line 1d — Medicaid waiver payments not reported on Form(s) W-2, box 1

**Tax year: 2025.**
**Last verified against the code on 2026-05-30.**

This document explains how the dollar amount that goes on **Line 1d** of Form 1040 is calculated. Line 1d reports a special category of earned income: payments a person received as a *Medicaid waiver* caregiver — typically a person caring for a family member at home under a state Medicaid program — that were **not already included in Form W-2 box 1**.

Line 1d is unusual among the wage lines because some of these payments are *taxable* (and stay on Line 1d as ordinary earned income), while others are *non-taxable* under a special IRS rule called **Notice 2014-7** — and those non-taxable amounts can still be reported on Line 1d, but only when the worker makes an election to count them as earned income for two specific credits, and only because they are then **backed out** on a separate line on Schedule 1 (line 8s) so the net effect on income tax is zero.

The document is organized in three parts:

1. **The IRS rule.** What the controlling IRS publications say must happen. This is the source of truth.
2. **The code implementation.** What the application's backend Java code currently does.
3. **The comparison.** A side-by-side check showing whether the code follows the IRS rule honestly, and where the two diverge.

A note on vocabulary used in this document:

- **Medicaid waiver payment.** A payment made by a state, or a state's agent, to a worker who provides personal care services in a private home to an *eligible individual* under a state Medicaid Home and Community-Based Services waiver program (legal citation: section 1915(c) of the Social Security Act). The payment is "for" the worker but is conditioned on the eligible individual living in the worker's own home.
- **Difficulty-of-care payment.** The broader Internal Revenue Code category, in IRC §131, that includes Medicaid waiver payments. The exclusion from gross income comes from §131.
- **Notice 2014-7.** The 2014 IRS guidance document that established that qualifying Medicaid waiver payments are treated as difficulty-of-care payments under IRC §131, and therefore can be excluded from gross income — even when paid by a state rather than a foster-care agency.
- **The earned-income election (or "Notice 2014-7 election").** A choice the worker can make to *include* their otherwise-excludable Medicaid waiver payments in earned income, **for the limited purpose** of computing the Earned Income Credit and the Additional Child Tax Credit. The election is made by entering the amount on Line 1a or Line 1d (depending on whether it was in Form W-2 box 1), and then entering the same amount as a *negative number* on Schedule 1 line 8s so it does not affect taxable income.
- **All-or-none, per spouse.** The election is not a partial-amount choice. The worker either includes *all* of their eligible Notice 2014-7 payments in earned income or *none* of them. On a Married Filing Jointly return each spouse makes the choice separately — one spouse may elect while the other does not.
- **Form W-2 box 12 code II.** Starting with payments made in 2024, the IRS requires payers (state Medicaid programs and their agents) to report non-taxable Medicaid waiver amounts on Form W-2 in box 12 with code II ("eye-eye"). Box 1 should be zero or blank in that case.
- **Schedule C path.** If the worker runs a separate trade or business of providing home-care services (typical of a self-employed caregiver), the IRS routes the income to Schedule C rather than Line 1d, with the Notice 2014-7 exclusion taken as a Schedule C expense labelled "Notice 2014-7". Schedule C is out of scope for this application.

### 1.1 Authority

The IRS publications used to write this document are in `C:\us-tax\docs\books\` unless noted otherwise:

- **Publication 17 — Your Federal Income Tax** (`p17.pdf`) — the master guide for individuals filing Form 1040. The wages-and-other-earnings chapter restates the rules around Medicaid waiver payments and the earned-income election.
- **Publication 525 — Taxable and Nontaxable Income** (`p525.pdf`) — establishes the difficulty-of-care payment exclusion under IRC §131 and the resulting earned-income inclusion option.
- **IRS Notice 2014-7** (`notice-2014-7.pdf`, 8 pages) — the authoritative guidance extending the §131 exclusion to qualified Medicaid waiver payments paid by states and their agents, with the requirement that the care recipient must live in the worker's home. The IRS does NOT publish a standalone PDF for the Schedule 1 instructions (the standalone URL `https://www.irs.gov/instructions/i1040sch1` returns HTTP 404); the line 8s instructions are embedded inside `i1040gi_2025.pdf` (already cited above) in the "Lines 8a–8z" subsection of Schedule 1.

### 1.2 The basic rule

From the 2025 Instructions for Form 1040, line 1d:

> Enter on line 1d the total of your wage payments received as a Medicaid waiver payment described in Notice 2014-7 that you choose to include in earned income for purposes of the Earned Income Credit (EIC) or the Additional Child Tax Credit (ACTC), and any other taxable Medicaid waiver payments not reported on Form(s) W-2, box 1.

In plain English, the IRS rule for Line 1d is:

```text
Line 1d = taxable Medicaid waiver payments not already in W-2 box 1
        + non-taxable Medicaid waiver payments not already in W-2 box 1
          (but ONLY if the worker has chosen to include them in earned income
           for the Earned Income Credit and Additional Child Tax Credit;
           this choice is all-or-none for each spouse)
```

Whatever amount lands on Line 1d that is *non-taxable* must be matched by a negative entry of the same amount on **Schedule 1 line 8s** so that the net effect on income tax is zero. The Line 1d entry is what makes the amount count as earned income for the two specific credits; the Schedule 1 line 8s entry is what makes sure the amount is not taxed.

### 1.3 Component one — Taxable Medicaid waiver payments not in W-2 box 1

These are Medicaid waiver payments that did NOT qualify for the Notice 2014-7 exclusion (for example, the care recipient did not live in the worker's home; the worker had a separate home; the payment was vacation pay; or the per-individual cap in IRC §131(c) was exceeded — see Section 1.6 below).

These amounts are ordinary earned income. They go on Line 1d permanently — no offset on Schedule 1 line 8s.

### 1.4 Component two — Non-taxable Medicaid waiver payments and the earned-income election

If the payments qualify for the Notice 2014-7 exclusion AND the payer correctly kept them out of Form W-2 box 1 (either by reporting them only in box 12 code II, or on Form 1099-MISC / 1099-NEC, or on a year-end statement, or by not issuing any tax form at all):

- The worker may **elect** to include those amounts in earned income for the Earned Income Credit and Additional Child Tax Credit. The election is all-or-none for each spouse — partial elections are not allowed.
- When the election is on, the *full non-taxable amount* goes on Line 1d and the *same full non-taxable amount* goes on Schedule 1 line 8s as a negative number.
- When the election is off, the non-taxable amount does NOT go on Line 1d, and no Schedule 1 line 8s entry is needed.

This is the only legitimate reason to put a non-taxable amount on a *wage* line of the Form 1040.

### 1.5 What does NOT go on Line 1d

| Type of payment | Goes on | Why |
|---|---|---|
| Medicaid waiver wages already reported in Form W-2 box 1 | Line 1a (already there) | They are taxable wages; Line 1a covers them |
| Non-taxable Medicaid waiver amounts on Form W-2 box 12 code II, when the worker is NOT electing to include them in earned income | Nowhere on the return | The IRS instructions explicitly say the worker does not need to report them or attach the Form W-2 |
| Medicaid waiver income earned in a separate home-care **business** (taxpayer is a sole proprietor) | Schedule C, with a Notice 2014-7 offset as an expense | This path is out of scope for this application |
| Other types of foster-care or difficulty-of-care payments that are NOT Medicaid waiver payments | Schedule 1 line 8 series (different line) | Different rule path |

### 1.6 Eligibility for the Notice 2014-7 exclusion (high level)

The Notice 2014-7 exclusion applies to *qualified* Medicaid waiver payments. Key conditions:

1. The payments come from a state Medicaid Home and Community-Based Services waiver program described in section 1915(c) of the Social Security Act (or a similar program — facts-and-circumstances test).
2. The care recipient is an *eligible individual* under that program.
3. The care is provided in the worker's own home — that is, the eligible individual lives in the worker's home under the plan of care.
4. The worker does not have a separate home elsewhere.
5. Vacation pay does NOT qualify for the exclusion.
6. The exclusion applies even if the worker is related to the care recipient (e.g., an adult child caring for a parent).

Determining whether each payment qualifies is a factual judgement on the user's part — the application asks the user to enter the qualified amount directly rather than re-computing it from facts.

### 1.7 The per-individual cap in IRC §131(c)

The §131 exclusion has a per-individual ceiling:

- The exclusion is lost on payments received for the care of **more than 5 individuals age 19 or older**.
- The exclusion is lost on payments received for the care of **more than 10 individuals under age 19**.

Amounts received for individuals above those caps are taxable and would go to Line 1d as the *taxable* component (Section 1.3). In practice, Medicaid waiver programs structurally involve **one** recipient per household, so this cap rarely comes up; the application does not currently apply the cap automatically (see Part 3 — gaps).

### 1.8 The Married Filing Separately rule

On a Married Filing Separately return each spouse reports only their own Medicaid waiver payments and makes their own all-or-none election. Do not aggregate the other spouse's amounts. This applies identically to both Line 1d and the Schedule 1 line 8s offset.

### 1.9 The complete IRS equation (plain English)

```text
For each Medicaid waiver payment entry that the worker received during the tax year:

    qualified         = the worker's stated non-taxable qualified amount (Notice 2014-7)
    included_in_w2    = portion of the qualified amount already in Form W-2 box 1
                        (zero when the payment was reported on Form W-2 box 12 code II,
                         or on Form 1099-MISC / 1099-NEC, or on a year-end statement,
                         or with no tax form at all)
    not_in_w2         = max(0, qualified - included_in_w2)
    taxable_not_in_w2 = the worker's stated taxable amount not in Form W-2 box 1

    Per entry:
        contribution_to_line1d_taxable_part   = taxable_not_in_w2
        contribution_to_line1d_qualified_part = not_in_w2 (only when election is true)
        contribution_to_line8s_total          = qualified  (only when election is true)

Per spouse:
    line1d_person = sum of taxable_not_in_w2
                  + (if election is true: sum of not_in_w2)
    line8s_person = (if election is true: -(sum of qualified)
                     else: not reported)

For a Married Filing Separately return:
    include only the filer's own Medicaid waiver entries.
    Do not aggregate the spouse's entries.

Form 1040 Line 1d  = sum of line1d_person across taxpayer and (when not MFS) spouse,
                     rounded to whole dollars at the very end.
Schedule 1 Line 8s = sum of line8s_person across taxpayer and (when not MFS) spouse,
                     a NEGATIVE number, rounded to whole dollars at the very end.
```

### 1.10 Whole-dollar rounding

The Form 1040 Instructions allow whole-dollar rounding (drop amounts below 50 cents; round amounts of 50 cents through 99 cents up to the next whole dollar). Rounding is optional but, if used, must be applied consistently throughout the return. Computed sums should add the precise amounts first and round only the final results.

## Part 2 — The code implementation

The backend Java code that calculates Line 1d (and the matching Schedule 1 line 8s entry) lives in `TaxReturnComputeService.java`:

- **`computeMedicaidWaiverPayments(...)`** — aggregates the taxpayer's amounts and the spouse's amounts, applying the Married Filing Separately guard.
- **`computeMedicaidForPerson(...)`** — calculates one person's Line 1d and line 8s contributions by walking the list of Medicaid waiver payment entries on that person's *Medicaid waiver payments* form.

These methods are invoked once per compute pass. The aggregated Line 1d amount lands on `income.medicaidWaiverPayments` on the Form 1040 output model via `income.setMedicaidWaiverPayments(line1d)` in `buildIncome(...)` after a standard `roundMoney(...)` step. The aggregated line 8s amount lands on `additionalIncome.otherIncomeMedicaidWaiverPayments` on the Schedule 1 output model.

> Convention: backend references in this document use method names rather than source-code line numbers (line numbers drift with refactors). Established 2026-05-06.

### 2.3 Reference data (constants that may change year-to-year)

For **Line 1d itself**, the application uses **no tax-year-specific computation constants**. The math is a pure sum of user-entered amounts and Form W-2 box 1 lookups; no dollar thresholds, no rates, no statutory floors or ceilings are applied.

For completeness, the *rules* that govern the computation (which the application *does* implement but which require no per-year constants) come from the following statutory references:

| Reference | Where it appears | Used in |
|---|---|---|
| IRS Notice 2014-7 | Defines what qualifies as a non-taxable Medicaid waiver payment | The user enters the qualified amount directly; no code constant |
| IRC §131 (foster care / difficulty-of-care exclusion) | Authority for the income-tax exclusion | Same — no code constant |
| IRC §131(c) per-individual cap (10 under age 19 / 5 age 19 or older) | Limits the exclusion when the worker cares for too many individuals | `ReferenceData.IRC_131C_CAP_INDIVIDUALS_UNDER_19` (= 10), `ReferenceData.IRC_131C_CAP_INDIVIDUALS_19_OR_OLDER` (= 5). Statutory constants — NOT tax-year-specific. Applied in `computeMedicaidForPerson(...)` as pro-rata reduction (added 2026-05-31). |
| IRC §32(c)(2) and Notice 2014-7 election scope | Defines the all-or-none election for Earned Income Credit and Additional Child Tax Credit | The application reads the boolean `includeQualifiedPaymentsInEarnedIncomeForEIC_ACTC` directly |
| 2024+ payer-side reporting standard (Form W-2 box 12 code II) | Informational — drives the reconciliation flag only | No code constant |

### 2.4 The code equation (Line 1d portion)

In plain English, the code does this (matching the IRS rule from Section 1.9):

```text
Step A (in the caller — buildIncome / buildForm1040) — determine the
       Married Filing Separately flag.
    Read filing-status.filingStatus (normalize whitespace and capitalization).
    If it equals "Married filing separately" then set isMfsReturn = true.
    Otherwise set isMfsReturn = false.

Step B (in the caller) — invoke computeMedicaidWaiverPayments(...).
    computeMedicaidWaiverPayments returns a MedicaidComputation record carrying:
        line1d_taxpayer  + line1d_spouse → summed via addNonNull
        line8s_taxpayer  + line8s_spouse → summed via addNonNull
        (skipped entirely for the spouse when isMfsReturn = true)

Step C (back in the caller, inside buildIncome) — round and store.
    line1dRounded = roundMoney(line1d aggregate)
    if line1dRounded is not null:
        income.medicaidWaiverPayments = line1dRounded
    (When line1d is null, the field is left unset and is omitted from the
     computed JSON output — Line 1d on the form prints blank.)

    line8sRounded = roundMoney(line8s aggregate)
    if line8sRounded is not null:
        schedule1.additionalIncome.otherIncomeMedicaidWaiverPayments = line8sRounded
```

And **`computeMedicaidWaiverPayments(...)`** itself is:

```text
Compute the taxpayer's MedicaidResult:
    taxpayer = computeMedicaidForPerson(medicaid-waiver-payments-taxpayer,
                                        w2Entries, taxpayerSsn, ...)

Compute the spouse's MedicaidResult, but ONLY when not Married Filing Separately:
    if isMfs is true: spouseResult = MedicaidResult(null, null)
    if isMfs is false: spouseResult = computeMedicaidForPerson(medicaid-waiver-payments-spouse,
                                                                 w2Entries, spouseSsn, ...)

Aggregate both spouses' contributions:
    rawLine1d = addNonNull(taxpayer.line1d, spouseResult.line1d)
    rawLine8s = addNonNull(taxpayer.line8s, spouseResult.line8s)
    (A null contribution contributes nothing; if BOTH are null the aggregate is null.)

Return MedicaidComputation(rawLine1d, rawLine8s).
```

And **`computeMedicaidForPerson(data, w2Entries, ssn, flags, label, uid)`** for one person is:

```text
If the input form is null (the person has not filled the Medicaid waiver form):
    return MedicaidResult(null, null).

Gate 1 — outer "received any payments?" gate (HARD).
    Read receivedMedicaidWaiverPayments. If it is not exactly true:
        return MedicaidResult(null, null).
    (Hard gate: null or false both produce a null result. Unlike line 1c's
     soft gate, there is no auto-fill convenience to preserve here.)

Gate 2 — Schedule C gate (BEFORE the entries-empty check; broadened 2026-05-20).
    Read hasTradeOrBusinessProvidingHomeCare.
    If true:
        Emit blocking flag MEDICAID_WAIVER_SCHEDULE_C_<person>.
        return MedicaidResult(null, null).
    (Fires REGARDLESS of whether any payment entries have been added. The
     Yes-to-business declaration alone routes the return off Line 1d, so a
     user who answers Yes without yet entering any payments is still blocked
     — exactly the case the rule is meant to catch.)

Gate 3 — entries-list gate.
    Read entries = medicaidWaiverPayments.
    If the list is empty:
        return MedicaidResult(null, null).

Per-entry loop — for each entry:
    payerName       = entry.payerName
    payerTin        = entry.payerTIN
    sourceType      = entry.sourceType
    isW2            = sourceType matches "W-2" (case insensitive)
    codeIIAmount    = entry.w2Box12CodeIIAmount
    qualifiedAmount = entry.qualifiedNotice2014_7Amount

    If codeIIAmount is provided AND qualifiedAmount is missing:
        Emit blocking flag MEDICAID_WAIVER_CODE_II_MISSING_QUALIFIED_<person>.
        (Tells the user: you said there was a W-2 box 12 code II amount,
         but did not enter the matching Notice 2014-7 qualified amount.)

    includedInW2 = entry.qualifiedAmountIncludedInW2Box1
    missingIncludedAmount = false

    If isW2:
        Look up the matching W-2 in the system by SSN + payer TIN + payer name.
        Read its box 1 amount (wagesTipsOtherCompAmount).
        If includedInW2 is missing:
            If the matched W-2 has no box 1 amount or box 1 <= 0:
                includedInW2 = 0
                (Modern 2024+ reporting standard: box 12 code II, box 1 = 0.)
            Else:
                missingIncludedAmount = true
                Emit blocking flag MEDICAID_WAIVER_W2_BOX1_UNSPECIFIED_<person>.

    If qualifiedAmount and includedInW2 are both present AND
       includedInW2 > qualifiedAmount:
        Emit blocking flag MEDICAID_WAIVER_W2_OVERAGE_<person>.

    qualifiedNotIncluded = (if missingIncludedAmount: null
                            else: max(0, qualifiedAmount - includedInW2))

    Accumulate per-entry totals into running sums:
        qualifiedTotal        += qualifiedAmount
        qualifiedIncludedInW2 += includedInW2
        qualifiedNotInW2      += qualifiedNotIncluded
        taxableNonW2          += entry.taxablePaymentsNotInW2Box1

Compute Line 1d for this person:
    line1d = taxableNonW2
    If election (includeQualifiedPaymentsInEarnedIncomeForEIC_ACTC) is true:
        If qualifiedTotal is not positive:
            Emit blocking flag MEDICAID_WAIVER_ELECTION_NO_QUALIFIED_<person>.
        line1d += qualifiedNotInW2

Compute Schedule 1 line 8s for this person:
    line8s = null
    If election is true:
        offset = qualifiedIncludedInW2 + qualifiedNotInW2
        If offset is not null:
            If offset > qualifiedTotal:
                Emit blocking flag MEDICAID_WAIVER_LINE8S_EXCEEDS_QUALIFIED_<person>.
                (Internal-consistency error: offset cannot exceed qualified total.)
            line8s = -offset   (the negative entry inside the preprinted parentheses
                                on Schedule 1 line 8s)
        Else:
            Emit blocking flag MEDICAID_WAIVER_LINE8S_MISSING_<person>.

return MedicaidResult(line1d, line8s).
```

### 2.5 Validation flags the code may emit

All flags below are **blocking** per the save-vs-compute principle: the user can always save the Medicaid waiver form (the user interface never refuses a save in these states); the return cannot be produced until the user resolves the underlying issue. The blocking flag causes the compute endpoint to return HTTP 409 with the flag list in the response body so the user interface can present a clear "what's wrong and how to fix it" message. The user interface also shows an inline advisory hint next to the affected field as soon as the inconsistency exists, so users see the guidance directly on the form.

| Flag name | Blocking? | When it fires | Effect on Line 1d |
|---|---|---|---|
| `MEDICAID_WAIVER_CODE_II_MISSING_QUALIFIED_TAXPAYER` (or `_SPOUSE`) | Yes — blocking | A Form W-2 box 12 code II amount was provided on the entry, but the matching Notice 2014-7 qualified amount was not | Line 1d for this entry uses only the taxable amount until the user enters the qualified amount or clears the Box 12 code II value. |
| `MEDICAID_WAIVER_W2_BOX1_UNSPECIFIED_TAXPAYER` (or `_SPOUSE`) | Yes — blocking | The entry's `sourceType` is W-2, the matched W-2 has positive box 1 wages, and the user did not say how much of the qualified amount is already in box 1 | The entry's qualified-not-in-W-2 portion is excluded from the Line 1d / line 8s totals (`missingIncludedAmount = true`) until the user fills in `qualifiedAmountIncludedInW2Box1`. |
| `MEDICAID_WAIVER_W2_OVERAGE_TAXPAYER` (or `_SPOUSE`) | Yes — blocking | `qualifiedAmountIncludedInW2Box1` exceeds `qualifiedNotice2014_7Amount` | Internal data-entry inconsistency — the included-in-W-2 portion cannot exceed the total qualified amount. The user must either reduce the included-in-W-2 portion to the actual overlap, or increase the total qualified amount if it was understated. |
| `MEDICAID_WAIVER_SCHEDULE_C_TAXPAYER` (or `_SPOUSE`) | Yes — **non-overrideable** per `rules.md` §17 (since 2026-05-30; previously blocking-but-overrideable) | `hasTradeOrBusinessProvidingHomeCare` is true (broadened 2026-05-20 — fires regardless of whether any amounts have been entered) | Line 1d for this person is suppressed entirely. The user must either change the "Do you run a home-care business" answer to No, OR remove the Medicaid waiver entries and file Schedule C separately (this application does not produce Schedule C). Passing `overrideFlags=true` does NOT bypass this flag — filing as-is would leave Line 1d empty AND produce no Schedule C either, so the entire Medicaid waiver income would be missing from the return and the IRS would catch the gap via a CP-2000 document-matching notice. |
| `MEDICAID_WAIVER_ELECTION_NO_QUALIFIED_TAXPAYER` (or `_SPOUSE`) | Yes — blocking | The earned-income election is on but no qualified amounts were entered | The user must either enter the qualified amount in at least one row, or turn off the earned-income election. |
| `MEDICAID_WAIVER_LINE8S_EXCEEDS_QUALIFIED_TAXPAYER` (or `_SPOUSE`) | Yes — blocking | Internal-consistency check: the computed Schedule 1 line 8s offset exceeds the total qualified amount | Internal guardrail — should never fire under normal flow. |
| `MEDICAID_WAIVER_LINE8S_MISSING_TAXPAYER` (or `_SPOUSE`) | Yes — blocking | Election is on but the offset computes to null | Internal guardrail — should never fire under normal flow. |
| `MEDICAID_WAIVER_LINE1D_TAXABLE_OVERLAPS_W2_BOX1_TAXPAYER` (or `_SPOUSE`) | Yes — **non-overrideable** per `rules.md` §17 (added 2026-05-30) | An entry has `sourceType = W-2`, the matched W-2 has positive Box 1 wages, AND `taxablePaymentsNotInW2Box1 >= matched-W-2 Box 1` — the same dollars would appear on Line 1a (via the W-2 statement) AND Line 1d (via this entry) | The entry is processed normally inside the per-employer loop, but the blocking flag prevents the return from being persisted because filing as-is would over-report wages by at least the Box 1 amount. The user fixes by either (a) moving the amount from `taxablePaymentsNotInW2Box1` to `qualifiedNotice2014_7Amount` + setting `qualifiedAmountIncludedInW2Box1` to the same value (Notice 2014-7 path — Line 8s offset backs out the Line 1a portion); or (b) deleting the Medicaid waiver entry entirely (the W-2 wages are already correctly on Line 1a). Picker pre-fill was also fixed (2026-05-30) so new entries no longer start in this state. |

**Save-vs-compute pivot (2026-05-20).** The `CODE_II_MISSING_QUALIFIED` and `W2_BOX1_UNSPECIFIED` flags previously had a UI-side save block (the form refused to save in those states and showed a red error). The save block was removed; both flags now fire only at compute time, the user interface shows advisory hints that explain the fix, and Save unconditionally succeeds. This matches the same pivot applied on 2026-05-19 to the Form 4137 EIN and adequate-records flags on line 1c, and to the Form 8919 firm-ID flag on line 1g.

### 2.6 Married Filing Separately guard

The aggregator `computeMedicaidWaiverPayments(...)` accepts a boolean `isMfs` argument. When true, the spouse's `computeMedicaidForPerson(...)` call is **skipped** and the spouse's contributions to both Line 1d and Schedule 1 line 8s are suppressed.

The user interface disables the spouse Medicaid waiver form on non-MFJ returns; the backend gate is a defense in depth for direct-API submissions that could otherwise persist stale spouse data. Mirrors the same pattern used by `computeTips` on line 1c.

### 2.7 Cross-form algebraic invariant (verified)

When the earned-income election is on, the application's Line 1d and Schedule 1 line 8s entries always sum to exactly cancel the *non-taxable* portion of Medicaid waiver payments out of the calculation of Adjusted Gross Income:

- Line 1d gross inclusion of `qualifiedNotInW2`, plus
- Schedule 1 line 8s offset of `-(qualifiedIncludedInW2 + qualifiedNotInW2)`,
- combined with the fact that `qualifiedIncludedInW2` is already part of Line 1a,

nets out to exactly zero on the income side of the return.

In other words: electing the earned-income inclusion has **zero effect on income tax** but a *positive* effect on the Earned Income Credit and Additional Child Tax Credit (the amount counts as earned income for those credit computations). This is exactly the IRS-intended treatment.

There is a known but currently-tolerated residual risk: if the user accidentally enters the same real-world payment as two separate entries (e.g., the same $1,000 payment is recorded against two ostensibly-different payers), the duplication does not affect Adjusted Gross Income (because the line 8s offset doubles too), but it does inflate the earned-income basis used for the Earned Income Credit and the Additional Child Tax Credit. A user interface heuristic to detect cross-entry duplicates is tracked in `outstanding.md` as a deferred enhancement.

**Auto-extract rules** when the user clicks "Bring into form" in section 3:

| Source | What gets pre-filled into the new entry row |
|---|---|
| W-2 with Box 12 code II > 0 | `payerName`, `payerEIN`, `sourceType = W-2`, `w2Box12CodeIIAmount`, `qualifiedNotice2014_7Amount = code II amount`, `qualifiedAmountIncludedInW2Box1 = 0` (this auto-create runs on `ngOnInit`; the picker lists it only if the user explicitly removed the row) |
| W-2 without Box 12 code II | `payerName`, `payerEIN`, `sourceType = W-2` only. **No amount is pre-filled** (changed 2026-05-30 — see `MEDICAID_WAIVER_LINE1D_TAXABLE_OVERLAPS_W2_BOX1_*` in §2.5 for the bug this prevents). The user enters the amount based on whether Notice 2014-7 applies. |
| 1099-MISC Box 3 (Other Income) > 0 | `payerName`, `payerTIN`, `sourceType = 1099-MISC`, `taxablePaymentsNotInW2Box1 = Box 3 amount` |
| 1099-MISC Box 6 (Medical/health care) > 0 | `payerName`, `payerTIN`, `sourceType = 1099-MISC`, `taxablePaymentsNotInW2Box1 = Box 6 amount` (shown as a second picker entry when both Box 3 and Box 6 are populated) |
| 1099-NEC Box 1 (Nonemployee compensation) > 0 | `payerName`, `payerTIN`, `sourceType = 1099-NEC`, `taxablePaymentsNotInW2Box1 = Box 1 amount` |

**Default-to-taxable rule.** For every non-code-II source, the picker pre-fills `taxablePaymentsNotInW2Box1` rather than `qualifiedNotice2014_7Amount`. This is intentional: Notice 2014-7 qualified status is a *legal-eligibility* determination (shared home, §1915(c) waiver, etc.) that the application cannot verify automatically. Treating the amount as taxable by default is the safe direction — over-reporting income is recoverable; auto-classifying as qualified when it isn't would be misrepresentation. The form shows a clear advisory note above the entries list: *"Amounts brought in from the statements above default to 'Taxable payments not in W-2 box 1'. If Notice 2014-7 applies to a payment, move that amount to 'Qualified Medicaid waiver payments' in the same row."*

**Picker dedup.** The picker hides any statement whose payer (matched by EIN or fuzzy name) is already represented by an existing entry row. Removing an entry row immediately re-surfaces the statement in the picker.

## Part 3 — Comparison between the IRS rule and the code

Reading the two parts side-by-side, the code follows the IRS rule faithfully on every point that affects the dollar amount on Line 1d (and the matching Schedule 1 line 8s entry). The table below maps each IRS requirement from Part 1 to the corresponding code step from Part 2.

| IRS requirement | Code behavior | Faithful? |
|---|---|---|
| Include taxable Medicaid waiver payments not in W-2 box 1 | `taxablePaymentsNotInW2Box1` from each entry is unconditionally added to `taxableNonW2`, which becomes the starting value of `line1d` | Yes |
| Include non-taxable Medicaid waiver payments not in W-2 box 1, ONLY when the election is on | When `includeQualifiedPaymentsInEarnedIncomeForEIC_ACTC` is true, `qualifiedNotInW2` is added to `line1d`; otherwise it is not | Yes |
| Generate a Schedule 1 line 8s negative offset for the *full* non-taxable amount when the election is on | When the election is true, `line8s = -(qualifiedIncludedInW2 + qualifiedNotInW2)`. This backs out both the portion already in Line 1a (via W-2 box 1) and the portion added to Line 1d | Yes |
| Do not back out taxable amounts on Schedule 1 line 8s | Only `qualifiedTotal` and its split components are used in the line 8s formula; `taxableNonW2` is never included | Yes |
| The election is all-or-none | The code reads a single boolean; partial-amount elections are not representable in the input | Yes |
| Each spouse makes the election independently | Two separate `computeMedicaidForPerson(...)` calls; each reads its own form's election flag | Yes |
| If the worker has a separate home-care business, route the income to Schedule C | Schedule C blocking flag fires; Line 1d is suppressed; the user is forced to handle Schedule C separately (out of scope for the application) | Yes — and clearly surfaced to the user via the blocking flag |
| Avoid double-counting between Line 1a and Line 1d for amounts already in W-2 box 1 | When `sourceType` is `W-2` and the matched W-2 has positive box 1 wages, the user is required to enter `qualifiedAmountIncludedInW2Box1`; the computation splits the qualified amount cleanly between "already in Line 1a" and "needs Line 1d" | Yes |
| On a Married Filing Separately return, do not include the spouse's amounts | The `computeMedicaidWaiverPayments(...)` aggregator skips the spouse call when `isMfs` is true. The single MFS guard cascades to both Line 1d and Schedule 1 line 8s via the same `addNonNull` aggregation | Yes |
| Whole-dollar rounding at the very end of the computation | `roundMoney(...)` is applied in `buildIncome(...)` before storing on `income.medicaidWaiverPayments`; same for the Schedule 1 line 8s aggregate | Yes |

### 3.1 Items the code does NOT support (intentional out-of-scope decisions)

The following are documented as out of scope and not bugs:

1. **Schedule C path** — when the user has a separate home-care business, the backend emits a blocking flag (`MEDICAID_WAIVER_SCHEDULE_C_*`), suppresses Line 1d entirely, and expects the user to handle the Notice 2014-7 expense offset on Schedule C themselves. Schedule C as a whole is out of scope for this application.
2. **IRC §131(c) per-individual cap** (more than 10 individuals under age 19, or more than 5 age 19 or older) — not currently applied automatically. Medicaid Home and Community-Based Services waiver programs structurally involve a single recipient per household, so this cap rarely applies in practice. The user is expected to manually separate the taxable and non-taxable portions of their payments if the cap is exceeded. Tracked in `outstanding.md`.
3. **IRA contribution treatment of excluded Medicaid waiver payments** (which may count as compensation for IRA contributions even though excluded from income) — not currently surfaced. This is a downstream completeness issue, not a Line 1d issue.
4. **Form 4137 / Form 8919 interaction** — Notice 2014-7 payments are not subject to Social Security or Medicare tax under the non-employee path, so they do not interact with Form 4137 (unreported tip Social Security/Medicare) or Form 8919 (uncollected Social Security/Medicare on wages). The code does not need to coordinate with those forms.

### 3.2 Items where the code is stricter than the IRS rule (defensive measures)

1. **Hard gate on `receivedMedicaidWaiverPayments`.** The code requires the user to explicitly answer "Yes" — null or false both produce an empty result. There is no auto-fill convenience to preserve.
2. **Schedule C gate fires before any entries exist (2026-05-20 broadening).** A user who answers "Yes" to "Do you run a home-care business?" without yet entering any payments is still blocked by the `MEDICAID_WAIVER_SCHEDULE_C_*` flag. This catches the case where the user is mid-data-entry but already off-path.
3. **W-2 box 1 inclusion amount required when source is W-2 and box 1 wages exist.** A blocking flag (`MEDICAID_WAIVER_W2_BOX1_UNSPECIFIED_*`) prevents the user from leaving this ambiguous. Without this, a user with a 2024-era W-2 that included Medicaid waiver wages in box 1 could accidentally double-count the qualified portion on both Line 1a and Line 1d.
4. **Code II reconciliation flag.** If the user entered a value for `w2Box12CodeIIAmount` but did not enter a matching `qualifiedNotice2014_7Amount`, the code blocks the return until the user supplies the qualified amount. This catches a common data-entry oversight from the 2024-onward standard.
5. **Election-with-no-qualified-amounts flag.** Selecting the election with no qualified amounts to elect is nonsensical; the code surfaces this so the user can either uncheck the election or supply the missing amounts.
6. **Internal-consistency guardrails on the line 8s offset.** Two flags (`MEDICAID_WAIVER_LINE8S_EXCEEDS_QUALIFIED_*` and `MEDICAID_WAIVER_LINE8S_MISSING_*`) catch impossible-state arithmetic from the line 8s calculation. They should never fire under normal usage; they exist to fail loudly rather than silently produce a wrong return.

### 3.3 Items captured in the input form but not used in the Line 1d math

| Field | What it does today | Why it is captured anyway |
|---|---|---|
| `payerName`, `payerTIN` (per-entry) | Used to look up the matching W-2 for the `sourceType = W-2` branch | Required for the double-count prevention rule, but does not change the Line 1d total directly |
| `programName` (form-level) | Display only | Helps the user remember which Medicaid program they were paid under (HCBS §1915(c) waiver, etc.) |
| `careRecipientRelationship` (form-level) | Display only | Helps the user remember whether Notice 2014-7 likely applies. The Notice applies even to related caregivers, so the relationship does not change the math |
| `livesWithCareRecipient` (form-level) | Display only — drives a user-interface warning banner, not the math | The Notice 2014-7 exclusion requires shared-home care. The application trusts the user's stated `qualifiedNotice2014_7Amount` directly and uses this flag only to nudge the user with a warning if they entered a qualified amount while saying they did not share a home |
| `w2Box12CodeIIAmount` (per-entry) | Drives the `MEDICAID_WAIVER_CODE_II_MISSING_QUALIFIED_*` flag only | The qualified amount is the field that flows into the math; the code II amount is captured for cross-reference and reconciliation |
| `notes` (per-entry) | Free text for the user's own records | Stored but never read by the math |

These are honest, intentional non-uses — not bugs.

### 3.4 Missing fields or possible gaps

**For Line 1d itself: implementation complete as of 2026-05-31.**

- **IRC §131(c) per-individual cap is applied automatically (added 2026-05-31).** When the user's per-spouse counts (`qualifiedIndividualsUnder19Count` and `qualifiedIndividualsAge19OrOlderCount`) exceed the IRS thresholds (10 / 5), the code applies a pro-rata reduction: `effectiveCap = min(under19, 10) + min(age19plus, 5)`; `excludableFraction = effectiveCap / totalIndividuals`. The over-cap portion of `qualifiedTotal` is added to Line 1d as ordinary taxable income with no Schedule 1 line 8s offset. The within-cap portion follows the usual election rules. When counts are absent or both within thresholds, the cap math is a no-op (backward-compatible with pre-existing returns). Pro-rata is the standard interpretation when amounts aren't separable by individual; the IRS does not specify an allocation method in §131(c) itself.

All other gating fields and per-entry fields have a corresponding input on the Medicaid waiver form:

- The all-or-none election is captured by `includeQualifiedPaymentsInEarnedIncomeForEIC_ACTC`.
- The W-2 box 1 inclusion amount is captured by `qualifiedAmountIncludedInW2Box1`.
- The Schedule C path gate is captured by `hasTradeOrBusinessProvidingHomeCare`.
- The Married Filing Separately guard is implemented and tested (`line1dMfsGuard_spouseMedicaidWaiverNotAggregatedOnMfs`).
- Both per-spouse independent election cases are tested (`line1dMfj_bothSpousesElect`, `line1dMfj_taxpayerElectsSpouseDoesNot_perSpouseElectionRule`).
- The W-2 cross-match has tests for the matched-positive-box-1, matched-zero-box-1 (modern 2024+ reporting), and no-match branches.

**Documentation gaps (not implementation gaps):**

None as of 2026-05-30 — Notice 2014-7 was downloaded to `docs/books/notice-2014-7.pdf` (8 pages, 28 KB). The IRS does not publish a standalone Schedule 1 instructions PDF; the line 8s instructions are embedded in `i1040gi_2025.pdf` already in the books folder.

## Part 4 — Summary

**Form 1040 Line 1d = sum, across the taxpayer and (on a non-Married-Filing-Separately return) the spouse, of, for each Medicaid waiver payment entry: the taxable amount that is not in Form W-2 box 1, plus (only when the worker has chosen to include qualified Notice 2014-7 payments in earned income for the Earned Income Credit and Additional Child Tax Credit) the non-taxable qualified amount that is not in Form W-2 box 1.**

The choice to include qualified Notice 2014-7 amounts in earned income is **all-or-none for each spouse** and produces a matching negative entry on **Schedule 1 line 8s** equal to the full qualified amount (including any portion already in Form W-2 box 1 that flows through Line 1a). Net effect of the elected amounts on Adjusted Gross Income is exactly zero; the only lasting effect is that the qualified amounts count as earned income for the two credits. Taxable Medicaid waiver payments stay on Line 1d permanently with no offset.

When the user runs a separate home-care **business**, the income is routed to Schedule C with a Notice 2014-7 expense offset instead — that path is out of scope for this application and is enforced by a blocking flag that fires the moment the user answers "Yes" to the home-care business question (broadened 2026-05-20 — fires regardless of whether any payment entries have been added).
