# Form 1040 — Line 1e — Taxable dependent care benefits (from Form 2441, line 26)

**Tax year: 2025.**
**Last verified against the code on 2026-05-31.**

This document explains how the dollar amount that goes on **Line 1e** of Form 1040 is calculated. Line 1e reports the *taxable* portion of dependent-care benefits a worker received from an employer — typically through a **Dependent Care Flexible Spending Arrangement** (a "DCFSA") that lets the worker set aside pre-tax dollars to pay for the care of a child or a disabled family member.

A worker may receive up to a certain amount of these benefits **tax-free**. The portion that exceeds the worker's qualifying care expenses, the worker's (and spouse's) earned income, the employer's plan limit, or a statutory dollar cap is **taxable** and is added back to wages on Line 1e. The line 1e amount is computed by working through Part III of **Form 2441** (*Child and Dependent Care Expenses*) and copying its line 26 result into Form 1040 line 1e.

The document is organized in three parts:

1. **The IRS rule.** What the controlling IRS publications say must happen. This is the source of truth.
2. **The code implementation.** What the application's backend Java code currently does.
3. **The comparison.** A side-by-side check showing whether the code follows the IRS rule honestly, and where the two diverge.

A note on vocabulary used in this document:

- **Dependent care benefits.** Amounts the worker's employer paid, contributed on behalf of the worker, or contributed at the worker's direction (under a salary-reduction agreement, typically a Dependent Care Flexible Spending Arrangement) to pay for the care of one or more qualifying persons. These amounts appear in **Form W-2 box 10** of the worker's wage statement.
- **Qualifying person.** (a) a *qualifying child* who is under age 13 at the time the care was provided and whom the worker can claim as a dependent, or (b) a *spouse who is physically or mentally incapable of self-care* and lived with the worker for more than half the year, or (c) a *dependent who is physically or mentally incapable of self-care* and lived with the worker for more than half the year. "Incapable of self-care" means the person cannot dress, clean themselves, or feed themselves due to a physical or mental condition, or requires constant care.
- **Earned income.** For line 1e purposes, the worker's wages plus self-employment net earnings, EXCLUDING the dependent-care benefits themselves, EXCLUDING amounts in W-2 box 11 (non-qualified deferred compensation), and EXCLUDING wages earned while incarcerated.
- **Deemed earned income.** A fictional earned-income amount the IRS allows the worker to attribute to a spouse who is a *full-time student* or who is *incapable of self-care*. The deemed amount is $250 per month if there is one qualifying person, or $500 per month if there are two or more qualifying persons, and it applies only for the months the spouse meets the condition.
- **Statutory exclusion cap.** A dollar ceiling on how much of these benefits can be excluded from income. For 2025 it is **$5,000** for most filing statuses, or **$2,500** for *Married Filing Separately* (with one specific exception described below).
- **Plan maximum.** The employer's own internal cap on the worker's DCFSA contribution. If lower than the statutory cap, the plan maximum becomes the ceiling.
- **§129(d) nondiscrimination failure.** When the employer's dependent care assistance program fails the IRS *nondiscrimination test*, highly-compensated employees can no longer exclude any of the benefits from income. The entire benefit amount becomes taxable.
- **Notation "DCB".** When there is taxable dependent care benefits on line 1e, the IRS expects the letters "DCB" to be printed near the line 1 area on the return as a small label. It is a presentation requirement, not a computational one.

### 1.2 The basic equation

From the 2025 Instructions for Form 1040, line 1e:

> Taxable dependent care benefits from Form 2441, line 26.

In plain English, the IRS rule for Line 1e is:

```text
Line 1e = the taxable portion of dependent care benefits, computed on Form 2441 Part III line 26.

Form 2441 line 26 is the difference between the total benefits available
and the smaller of: (a) the worker's qualifying care expenses, (b) the
worker's earned income, (c) the spouse's earned income, and (d) the
statutory or plan-imposed dollar cap.
```

### 1.3 What's actually on Form W-2 box 10

Box 10 of a Form W-2 carries the dependent care benefit amount. In most cases the entire box 10 amount was *also* excluded from box 1 wages (the employer kept it out of taxable wages). But box 10 is not necessarily equal to the worker's actual DCFSA contribution — the employer may have *already* added a portion of it back to box 1 (because it exceeded the excludable amount) or never have excluded it in the first place. Form 2441 line 12 is *the portion of box 10 that was not already in box 1*, NOT box 10 itself.

### 1.4 Why the line 1e calculation has so many sub-lines

The IRS does not just say "anything above $5,000 is taxable". The exclusion is the SMALLEST of four limits, taken in this order:

1. Total benefits available (Form 2441 line 15) — usually box 10 minus whatever was already taxed, plus prior-year grace-period balance, minus forfeitures.
2. Qualifying care expenses actually paid in 2025 (line 16) — the worker can't exclude more than they spent.
3. The smaller of the worker's and the spouse's earned income (lines 18 and 19) — the worker can't exclude more than the combined earnings supporting the care decision.
4. The statutory or plan dollar cap (line 21) — for 2025, $5,000 for most, $2,500 for MFS.

The *excluded* amount (line 25) is the minimum of those four. The *taxable* amount (line 26) is the total benefits minus the excluded amount, and is what lands on Line 1e.

### 1.5 The complete IRS equation (plain English)

```text
Step A — Compute the total benefits available (Form 2441 line 15):

    line12 = total dependent care benefits in W-2 box 10
             minus any portion already included in W-2 box 1 (already taxed)
    line13 = prior-year unused DCFSA balance consumed during the 2025 grace
             period (Dependent Care FSAs allow a grace period of up to
             2.5 months only — no true carryover)
    line14 = amount forfeited to the employer or carried into the 2026 grace
             period
    line15 = max(0, line12 + line13 - line14)

Step B — Apply the expense and earned-income limits:

    line16 = qualified dependent care expenses paid in 2025 for qualifying
             persons (sum across persons)
    line17 = min(line15, line16)

    line18 = the worker's earned income for 2025 (W-2 wages plus
             self-employment income, EXCLUDING the dependent care benefits
             themselves, W-2 box 11 NQDC, and inmate wages)
    line19 = same calculation for the spouse on a Married Filing Jointly
             return; otherwise line19 = line18

    For a spouse who is a full-time student or incapable of self-care,
    "deemed earned income" substitutes:
        if 1 qualifying person:   $250 × months the spouse qualified
        if 2+ qualifying persons: $500 × months the spouse qualified
    Use deemed earned income whenever it is higher than the spouse's
    actual earned income.

    line20 = min(line17, line18, line19)

Step C — Apply the statutory or plan cap:

    statutoryCap = $2,500 for Married Filing Separately (with no separation
                   exception)
                 = $5,000 for everyone else
    line21 = min(statutoryCap, the employer's plan maximum)
             — or just statutoryCap if the plan maximum was not provided.

Step D — Compute the taxable amount and copy it to line 1e:

    Normal case:
        line25 = min(line20, line21)               (the excluded amount)
        line26 = max(0, line15 - line25)           (the taxable amount)

    Special case — employer plan failed the §129(d) nondiscrimination test:
        line25 = 0
        line26 = line15                            (the entire amount is taxable)

    Form 1040 line 1e = line26 (rounded to whole dollars).
```

### 1.6 The Married Filing Separately rule (and its exception)

On a Married Filing Separately return the statutory cap is **$2,500** (half the joint cap), and the dependent care credit on Part II of Form 2441 is generally NOT available.

**The separation exception.** If all three of the following are true, the MFS filer is treated as unmarried for Form 2441 purposes — meaning the **$5,000 cap applies** and the credit IS available:

1. The taxpayer and spouse lived apart during the **last 6 months** of 2025.
2. The qualifying person's home was the taxpayer's home for **more than half** of 2025.
3. The taxpayer paid **more than half** the cost of maintaining that household for the entire year.

### 1.7 Whole-dollar rounding

The Form 1040 instructions allow whole-dollar rounding. Form 2441 line 26 is rounded once at the very end before being copied to line 1e.

### 1.8 What does NOT go on Line 1e

| Type of payment | Goes on | Why |
|---|---|---|
| Dependent care benefits that are FULLY excludable (the worker has enough qualified expenses, both spouses earn enough, and the cap is not exceeded) | Nothing — they are not taxable | The exclusion absorbs the entire amount; line 26 = 0 |
| Dependent care benefits the worker paid OUT OF POCKET (not employer-provided) | Form 2441 Part II only (credit), not Line 1e | Line 1e is for employer-provided benefits only |
| Wages already in W-2 box 1 because the employer did not exclude them | Line 1a (already taxed as ordinary wages) | The "already taxed" portion is subtracted from line 12 |

## Part 2 — The code implementation

The backend Java code that calculates Line 1e (and the rest of Form 2441) lives in `TaxReturnComputeService.java`, run at two different stages of the compute pipeline:

- **`computeDependentCareBenefits(...)`** — runs early in `prepare(...)` before AGI (Adjusted Gross Income) is known. Computes Form 2441 Part III in full (the line 1e amount lands on `form1040.income.dependentCareBenefits`) and Part II lines 3–6. Leaves Part II lines 7–11 as null because AGI and total-tax-before-credits are not available yet.
- **`finalizeForm2441PartII(...)`** — runs after AGI is known and after the foreign tax credit (Schedule 3 line 1) has been applied. Fills in Part II lines 7–11 (the dependent care credit) and writes the credit amount to Schedule 3 line 2.

This document focuses on Part III (the part that produces line 1e). Part II (the credit) is described where it affects the line 1e flow, but the credit itself appears on **Schedule 3 line 2** and then on **Form 1040 line 20**, not on line 1e.

> Convention: backend references use method names rather than source-code line numbers (line numbers drift with refactors). Established 2026-05-06.

### 2.4 The code equation (Line 1e portion)

In plain English, the code does this (matching the IRS rule from Section 1.5):

```text
Step 0 — Decide whether Part III applies at all.
    totalBenefits = sum of W-2 box 10 amounts across all W-2 statements
                    for the taxpayer (and spouse on MFJ — but only the
                    taxpayer's W-2s on MFS).
    hasBenefits      = totalBenefits > 0
    hasChildcareData = the worker has filled out anything on the
                       childcare-expenses form.

    If neither hasBenefits nor hasChildcareData:
        return DependentCareComputation(null, null, false)
        (do not even produce a Form 2441 on the output).
        This is the canonical "null vs zero" rule: line 1e is null when
        the concept does not apply. Locked in by the unit tests
        line1eIsNullWhenNoChildcareExpensesFormAndNoBox10 and
        line1eIsZeroWhenChildcareExpensesFormPresentWithZeroAmount.

    If hasBenefits AND the user did NOT answer "Yes" to
       "Did you receive employer-provided dependent care benefits?":
        Emit a BLOCKING flag DEPENDENT_CARE_BENEFITS_FORM_REQUIRED.
        Do not compute line 1e; the user must complete the form.

Step A — Compute total benefits available (Form 2441 line 15):
    alreadyInBox1 = childcareExpenses.dependentCareBenefits
                       .alreadyIncludedInBox1Amount
    line12        = max(0, totalBenefits - alreadyInBox1)
    line13        = childcareExpenses.dependentCareBenefits
                       .line13GracePeriodBenefitsUsed       (default 0)
    line14        = childcareExpenses.dependentCareBenefits
                       .line14ForfeitedOrCarriedForward     (default 0)
    line15        = max(0, line12 + line13 - line14)

Step B — Apply expense and earned-income limits:
    line30        = sum of qualifyingPersons.persons[].qualifiedExpensesForPerson
                    (or zero if the user answered "no care expenses this year")
    line16        = line30 by default
                    OR the user's override
                    childcareExpenses.dependentCareBenefits
                       .line16QualifiedExpensesForBenefits
    line17        = min(line15, line16)

    For the worker's earned income:
        Start with: sum of W-2 box 1 wages attributed to the worker's SSN,
                    minus the worker's inmate wages.
        Plus: childcareExpenses.dependentCareBenefits
                  .additionalEarnedIncomeTaxpayer (e.g., qualifying disability pay).
        If the worker is a student or disabled
           (studentOrDisabledDeemedIncome = true):
            Substitute max(actual earned income,
                           $250 or $500 per month × months that qualify).
        Call this line18.

    For the spouse's earned income (Married Filing Jointly only):
        Same calculation, using spouse's SSN, spouse's inmate wages,
        spouse's additional earned income, and spouse's
        studentOrDisabledDeemedIncomeSpouse / studentOrDisabledMonthsSpouse.
        Call this line19.

        For Single, Head of Household, Qualifying Surviving Spouse,
        Married Filing Separately: line19 = line18 (the form has only
        one earner).

    line20 = min(line17, line18, line19)

Step C — Apply the dollar cap:
    If filing status is Married Filing Separately AND the user has NOT
       claimed the separation exception:
        statutoryCap = $2,500
    Otherwise:
        statutoryCap = $5,000

    planMax = childcareExpenses.dependentCareBenefits.planMaxExcludable
              (optional)
    line21  = planMax is not null ? min(statutoryCap, planMax)
                                  : statutoryCap

Step D — Compute the taxable portion (Form 2441 line 26):
    If childcareExpenses.dependentCareBenefits
         .employerPlanFailed129dTest = true:
        line25 = 0
        line26 = line15                  (full amount taxable)
    Otherwise:
        line25 = min(line20, line21)     (the excluded amount)
        line26 = max(0, line15 - line25) (the taxable amount)

Step E — Store on the Form 1040 output:
    form1040.income.dependentCareBenefits = roundMoney(line26)
    (Also store every Form 2441 sub-line for the PDF rendering.)
```

### 2.6 Married Filing Separately guard

On a Married Filing Separately return, the code:

- Aggregates only the *taxpayer's* W-2 box 10 amounts (not the spouse's).
- Reads only the *taxpayer's* deemed-income facts; the spouse's are ignored.
- Reads only the *taxpayer's* additional earned income; the spouse's is ignored.
- Sets line 19 = line 18 (no separate spouse income).
- Uses the $2,500 statutory cap UNLESS `marriedFilingSeparatelyException = true`, in which case it uses $5,000 (the worker is treated as unmarried for Form 2441 purposes; the user interface gates this on the three IRS conditions in Section 1.6).

### 2.7 The two-pass Form 2441 computation

Form 2441 Part II (the credit) needs Adjusted Gross Income on line 7 and total-tax-before-credits on line 10. Neither is available when `computeDependentCareBenefits(...)` runs. The application splits the work:

1. **Phase 1** (in `computeDependentCareBenefits(...)`) — runs early; populates Part III in full and Part II lines 3–6 (qualifying-person expense limit and earned-income limits). Part II lines 7, 8, 9a, 9c, 10, 11 are left null. This produces the line 1e amount.
2. **Phase 2** (in `finalizeForm2441PartII(...)`) — runs after AGI is known and after the foreign tax credit has been applied. Fills in Part II lines 7–11 and writes the credit to Schedule 3 line 2.

The line 1e amount is fully resolved at the end of Phase 1; Phase 2 only affects the credit.

**Section headers** no longer leak the IRS Form 2441 part structure ("(Part I)", "(Part II)", "(Part III)" suffixes were dropped).

**SSN / EIN entry** uses the `appTin` directive for formatting (`appTin="individual"` on the qualifying-person SSN; `appTin="any"` on the provider identifying number), with `autocomplete="off"` and `data-1p-ignore="true"` to keep browser password managers from offering to store third-party SSNs.

**Dependent picker** reads `/api/personal/dependents` once on `ngOnInit`. Each dependent is annotated with a computed age-at-year-end and a "Under 13" or "Over 12 — verify disability" badge so the user can pick correctly. Once linked (matched by normalized SSN digits), a dependent is hidden from the picker to avoid duplicate-row creation.

## Part 3 — Comparison between the IRS rule and the code

Reading the two parts side-by-side, the code follows the IRS rule faithfully on every point that affects the dollar amount on Line 1e. The table below maps each IRS requirement from Part 1 to the corresponding code step from Part 2.

| IRS requirement | Code behavior | Faithful? |
|---|---|---|
| Start with W-2 box 10 minus any portion already taxed in box 1 (Form 2441 line 12 rule) | `line12 = max(0, totalBenefits - alreadyInBox1)` | Yes — and the code defends against negative values |
| Add prior-year grace-period balance (line 13), subtract forfeitures/carryforwards (line 14), floor at zero (line 15) | `line15 = max(0, line12 + line13 - line14)` | Yes |
| Take the smaller of line 15 and qualifying-person expenses for line 17 | `line17 = min(line15, line16)` | Yes |
| Worker's earned income excludes the dependent care benefits themselves, W-2 box 11 NQDC, and inmate wages | Uses `sumW2WagesAfterCarveOuts(...)` and explicitly subtracts inmate wages | Yes |
| Spouse's earned income on Married Filing Jointly; line 19 = line 18 on other statuses | Conditional on `isMfj`; equals line 18 otherwise | Yes |
| Student or disabled spouse deemed income substitution at $250 / $500 per month | `deemedMonthly = qualifyingPersonCount >= 2 ? 500 : 250`; multiplied by `clampMonths(...)`; substituted via `maxNonNull(actualEarned, deemed)` | Yes — and clamps months to a sensible range to defend against bad data |
| line 20 = min(line 17, line 18, line 19) | `minOf(line17, line18, line19)` | Yes |
| line 21 = min(statutoryCap, employer plan max) — and the statutory cap is $5,000 normally, $2,500 for MFS without the separation exception | Explicit; reads `ReferenceData.DEPENDENT_CARE_BENEFITS_LIMIT_OTHER` and `_MFS` | Yes |
| line 25 = min(line 20, line 21); line 26 = max(0, line 15 - line 25) — except when the employer plan failed the §129(d) nondiscrimination test, in which case line 25 = 0 and the full amount is taxable | Explicit branch on `employerPlanFailed129dTest` | Yes |
| Form 1040 line 1e = Form 2441 line 26, rounded to whole dollars | `form1040.income.dependentCareBenefits = roundMoney(line26)` | Yes |
| When neither W-2 box 10 nor the childcare-expenses form is populated, line 1e is null (not 0) — so line 1z's "all sub-lines null → null" contract holds | Explicit early-return `new DependentCareComputation(null, null, false)` when `!hasBenefits && !hasChildcareData` | Yes — protected by the `line1eIsNullWhenNoChildcareExpensesFormAndNoBox10` and `line1eIsZeroWhenChildcareExpensesFormPresentWithZeroAmount` unit tests |

### 3.1 Items the code does NOT support (intentional out-of-scope decisions)

The following are documented as out of scope and not bugs:

1. **Self-employed dependent care assistance programs (IRC §129(e)(3)).** The Form 2441 Part III lines 22 and 23 are hard-coded to assume no sole-proprietorship or partnership benefits — `line22 = 0`, `line23 = line15`. This is correct for the employee-only scope the application targets but means a sole-proprietor with a §129(e)(3) plan is not supported.
2. **Pub 503 Worksheet A full prior-year computation (partial — outer-bound cap implemented).** The application caps line 9b at the IRC §21(c) per-year ceiling ($3,000 / $6,000) as of 2026-05-31 — the absolute upper bound under any prior-year scenario. The tighter Worksheet A cap, which depends on the prior-year Form 2441 line 6 and a recomputation of prior-year MAGI, still cannot be performed because the application does not have 2024 return data. The non-blocking advisory `DEPENDENT_CARE_WORKSHEET_A_PARTIAL` continues to fire whenever the user enters a positive amount, pointing them at Pub 503 if the 2024 line 6 was non-zero.
3. **Independent §129(d) plan-failure verification (fundamentally not fixable).** The application accepts the user's self-reported `employerPlanFailed129dTest` boolean. The §129(d) nondiscrimination test requires the employer's plan document and coverage tests across the entire employee population — neither of which is available to the application. The IRS rule places the disclosure burden on the employer; the user-interface help text on the childcare-expenses form explains when to set the flag.

### 3.2 Items where the code is stricter than the IRS rule (defensive measures)

1. **Hard gate when the worker has W-2 box 10 but no childcare-expenses form.** The blocking flag `DEPENDENT_CARE_BENEFITS_FORM_REQUIRED` forces the worker to complete the form rather than silently assuming the entire amount is taxable. The IRS rule would have the worker file Form 2441 in this case; the application enforces this at compute time.
2. **W-2 box 10 attribution by Social Security Number on Married Filing Separately.** When the household has multiple W-2s, the application sums only the W-2s whose `employeeSSN` matches the *taxpayer's* SSN — preventing a stale spouse W-2 from inflating the MFS taxpayer's line 1e. (Married Filing Jointly aggregates across both SSNs as expected.)
3. **`hasCareExpenses = false` clears stale qualifying-person and line-16-override data.** When the worker explicitly answers "No, I have no qualifying care expenses this year", the application zeroes out the qualifying-persons list and the line-16 override even if those fields remain populated from a prior session. This prevents stale data from inflating the credit (Part II line 3) when the user has changed their answer.
4. **Spouse-earned-income-zero advisory.** On a Married Filing Jointly return where the spouse has zero earned income, the application emits the non-blocking `DEPENDENT_CARE_SPOUSE_EARNED_INCOME_ZERO` flag explaining why the entire benefit became taxable. This is mathematically correct per Pub 503 but a common UX surprise; the flag points the user at the deemed-income election as the way out.
5. **Qualifying-person family-SSN + age cross-check (added 2026-05-31).** The application validates every qualifying-person SSN against the family-SSN set (taxpayer, spouse on MFJ, dependents) and against each matched person's age-at-year-end. Two §17 non-overrideable flags fire when the validation fails: `DEPENDENT_CARE_QUALIFYING_PERSON_NOT_IN_FAMILY` for SSNs that match nobody, and `DEPENDENT_CARE_QUALIFYING_PERSON_OVER_12_NOT_DISABLED` for matched persons aged 13 or older without the disability flag set. The Pub 503 qualifying-person definition is the IRS rule of record; this validator turns it into a compute-time gate so the user cannot silently inflate the line 1e exclusion or Schedule 3 line 2 credit by listing a non-eligible person.

### 3.3 Items captured in the input form but not used in the Line 1e math

| Field | What it does today | Why it is captured anyway |
|---|---|---|
| `careProviders.providers[]` (provider name, address, ID, household-employee flag, amount paid) | Stored on the Form 2441 output for PDF rendering | Required for Form 2441 Part I (the provider table). Does not change the line 1e computation. |
| `qualifyingPersons.persons[].qualifyingFirstName/LastName/SSN/Over12AndDisabled` | Stored on the Form 2441 output for PDF rendering | Required for Form 2441 Part II line 2 (the qualifying-person table). Does not change the line 1e computation. |
| `moreThanThreeCareProviders`, `moreThanThreeQualifyingPersons` | Stored on the Form 2441 output for PDF rendering | Triggers the "see overflow statement" checkbox on Form 2441 when more than three are provided. |

These are honest, intentional non-uses for line 1e — not bugs.

### 3.4 Gap closures and remaining constraints

**For Line 1e itself, the implementation is complete. The three gaps documented in earlier revisions of this spec have been addressed as follows:**

#### Gap 1 — Pub 503 Worksheet A (line 9b cap) — **CLOSED 2026-05-31**

The application now caps Form 2441 line 9b at the IRC §21(c) per-year ceiling — `$3,000` for one qualifying person, `$6,000` for two or more. This is provably correct under any prior-year scenario: the §21(c) per-year cap does not change year-to-year, so a prior-year UNUSED expense limit can never exceed the same ceiling that applies this year. The tighter prior-year-line-6-derived cap from the full Pub 503 Worksheet A still cannot be computed without 2024 return data, so the non-blocking advisory `DEPENDENT_CARE_WORKSHEET_A_PARTIAL` continues to fire whenever the user enters a positive amount — pointing them at the worksheet if their 2024 line 6 was non-zero. Affects the credit (Part II line 11), not line 1e itself.

#### Gap 2 — Qualifying-person eligibility check — **CLOSED 2026-05-31**

The application now cross-checks every qualifying-person SSN against the family-SSN set (taxpayer + spouse on MFJ + dependents from `/api/personal/dependents`). Two §17 non-overrideable flags can fire:

- `DEPENDENT_CARE_QUALIFYING_PERSON_NOT_IN_FAMILY` — when the qualifying-person SSN matches no family member. Pub 503 requires a qualifying person to be a member of the tax household; listing a non-family person would silently inflate the exclusion (Form 1040 line 1e) or credit (Schedule 3 line 2).
- `DEPENDENT_CARE_QUALIFYING_PERSON_OVER_12_NOT_DISABLED` — when the SSN matches a family member who was 13 or older at the end of the tax year AND the user did not mark the disability flag. Per Pub 503, a person 13 or older is a qualifying person only when physically or mentally incapable of self-care.

Both flags are blocking and non-overrideable per rules.md §17 because either condition silently produces a return the application knows to be wrong. The validator is implemented in `TaxReturnComputeService.validateQualifyingPersons` and uses the same family-SSN pattern as `validateStatementsBelongToFamily`. Identification-incomplete returns (no taxpayer / spouse / dependent SSNs present) are skipped — a separate identification-missing flag guides the user there first.

#### Gap 3 — §129(d) plan-failure verification — **fundamentally not fixable (intentional)**

The application accepts the user's self-reported `employerPlanFailed129dTest` boolean. This is the right answer: the §129(d) nondiscrimination test requires reading the employer's plan document and applying coverage and concentration tests across the entire employee population. Neither the plan document nor employee-population data is available to the application; this is a data-availability constraint, not a defect. The IRS rule places the disclosure burden on the employer, who must notify employees if the plan fails the test. The application's user-interface help text and the §3.2 defensive-measure gates (the `DEPENDENT_CARE_BENEFITS_FORM_REQUIRED` blocking flag when W-2 box 10 is positive but the form is incomplete) ensure the user sees and answers the question.

**Documentation gaps (not implementation gaps): none as of 2026-05-31.** `p503.pdf` (1.33 MB), `f2441.pdf` (121 KB), and `i2441.pdf` (201 KB) all downloaded to `docs/books/` on 2026-05-31.

All other gating fields and per-section fields have a corresponding input on the childcare-expenses form. The data-flow diagram at `c:/us-tax/diagrams/1e.drawio` and the knowledge file at `c:/us-tax/knowledge/line-1e-dependent-care-benefits.md` describe the same rules at finer-grained level.

## Part 4 — Summary

**Form 1040 Line 1e = the taxable portion of employer-provided dependent care benefits, computed as Form 2441 Part III line 26: the total available benefits (W-2 box 10 minus already-taxed portion, plus prior-year grace-period balance, minus forfeitures) minus the smaller of (a) the worker's qualifying care expenses, (b) the worker's earned income, (c) the spouse's earned income on a joint return (with deemed-income substitution for a full-time-student or disabled spouse), and (d) the dollar cap ($5,000 normally, $2,500 for Married Filing Separately without the separation exception, or the employer's plan maximum if lower). When the employer's plan fails the §129(d) nondiscrimination test, the entire amount is taxable.**

The dependent care **credit** (Form 2441 Part II line 11 → Schedule 3 line 2 → Form 1040 line 20) is computed alongside line 1e but is a separate output — described in the dependencies file and not in this document.
