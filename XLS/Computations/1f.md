# Form 1040 — Line 1f — Taxable adoption benefits (from Form 8839, line 31)

**Tax year: 2025.**
**Last verified against the code on 2026-05-31.**

This document explains how the dollar amount that goes on **Line 1f** of Form 1040 is calculated. Line 1f reports the *taxable* portion of dependent-care... no — of *employer-provided adoption benefits* a worker received from an employer that runs a **written qualified adoption-assistance program** (a "Section 137 plan"). The plan lets the employer pay adoption agencies, lawyers, courts, and travel costs on the employee's behalf — or reimburse the employee for amounts already paid — and the employee can exclude the qualifying portion from federal income tax.

The amount that the law lets the employee exclude is computed on **Form 8839 Part III** (Qualified Adoption Expenses) and depends on three things: how much the employer paid (Form W-2 box 12 code T), the employee's Modified Adjusted Gross Income (a high-income phaseout reduces the exclusion), and per-child rules (special-needs status, foreign-child finality, prior-year benefit consumption). The portion the law does NOT let the employee exclude is the *taxable adoption benefits* — that's exactly what goes on Form 1040 line 1f. Form 8839 line 31 is identical to Form 1040 line 1f; whatever ends up on Form 8839 line 31 is copied to Form 1040 line 1f.

The document is organized in three parts:

1. **The IRS rule.** What the controlling IRS publications and Internal Revenue Code sections say must happen. This is the source of truth.
2. **The code implementation.** What the application's backend Java code currently does.
3. **The comparison.** A side-by-side check showing whether the code follows the IRS rule honestly, and where the two diverge.

A note on vocabulary used in this document:

- **Employer-provided adoption benefits.** Amounts the worker's employer paid, contributed on the worker's behalf, or reimbursed (under a Section 137 written qualified adoption-assistance program) to cover qualified adoption expenses. Reported on Form W-2 box 12 with letter code **T**. The code T total includes pre-tax salary-reduction contributions the employee made through a cafeteria plan under Internal Revenue Code §125, not only direct employer dollars.
- **Qualified adoption expenses.** Adoption agency fees, attorney fees, court costs, travel expenses (including meals and lodging while away from home), re-adoption expenses for a foreign child, and other expenses directly related to and for the principal purpose of the legal adoption of an eligible child. Costs of adopting a spouse's child and surrogate-parenting arrangements are NOT qualified expenses.
- **Eligible child.** A child under age 18, OR a person of any age who is physically or mentally incapable of self-care. (Internal Revenue Code §23(d)(2).)
- **Child with special needs.** A child who is a United States citizen or resident whom a state has determined (i) cannot or should not be returned to the parents' home, and (ii) is unlikely to be adopted without assistance because of a specific factor or condition such as ethnic background, age, medical condition, sibling-group membership, or physical/mental/emotional handicap. (Internal Revenue Code §23(d)(3).)
- **Foreign child.** A child who was not a United States citizen or resident at the time the adoption effort began.
- **Adoption assistance program.** A separate written plan of an employer for the exclusive benefit of its employees, providing adoption assistance — meeting the requirements of Internal Revenue Code §137(c).
- **Modified Adjusted Gross Income (Modified AGI).** Adjusted Gross Income from Form 1040 line 11 PLUS certain add-backs: Form 2555 (Foreign Earned Income) lines 45 and 50, Form 4563 (American Samoa) line 15, Puerto Rico excluded income, the student-loan-interest deduction (Schedule 1 line 21), the Series EE / I U.S. savings bond interest exclusion (Schedule B line 3), and the self-employed foreign-housing deduction (Schedule 1 line 24j).
- **Phaseout.** The exclusion amount tapers from full to zero as Modified Adjusted Gross Income rises from one statutory threshold to another. For 2025, the phaseout runs from $259,190 to $299,190.
- **Notation "PYAB".** When line 1f is *negative* (a retroactive exclusion of prior-year benefits exceeds current-year benefits), the 2025 Form 1040 instructions ask the filer to write the letters "PYAB" (Prior-Year Adoption Benefits) on the dotted line to the left of the line 1f amount, similar to "DCB" for line 1e.

### 1.2 The basic equation

From the 2025 Instructions for Form 1040, line 1f:

> Enter the taxable amount of employer-provided adoption benefits from Form 8839, line 31.

In plain English, the IRS rule for Line 1f is:

```text
Line 1f = the taxable portion of employer-provided adoption benefits,
          computed on Form 8839 Part III line 31.

Form 8839 line 31 is the difference between:
  - the total employer-provided adoption benefits the worker received
    this year (line 23), and
  - the total amount of those benefits the law lets the worker exclude
    after applying the per-child cap, the special-needs rule, the
    foreign-child rule, and the high-income phaseout (line 30).

Line 1f may be ZERO, POSITIVE, or NEGATIVE. A negative amount reduces
the worker's wages — it can occur when prior-year benefits become
retroactively excludable in the year a foreign adoption is finalized.
```

### 1.3 What's actually on Form W-2 box 12 code T

Box 12 with letter code **T** carries the *employer-provided adoption benefits* amount. In practice:

- This amount was generally NOT included in W-2 box 1 (federal taxable wages) by the employer, because the law presumes most of it can be excluded;
- It IS generally included in W-2 boxes 3 and 5 (Social Security and Medicare wages), because adoption assistance is subject to FICA taxes even when it is excluded from federal income tax. (Internal Revenue Code §3121(a)(18); Publication 15-B.)
- The code T total can include pre-tax salary-reduction contributions the employee made through a §125 cafeteria plan for adoption expenses, not just employer-funded amounts.
- Completing Form 8839 Part III is what determines how much of the code T amount stays excluded (line 30) and how much becomes taxable (line 31). If the line 30 amount is less than the code T amount, the difference is added back to wages on Form 1040 line 1f.

### 1.4 Why the line 1f calculation has so many sub-lines

The IRS does not simply say "anything above $17,280 is taxable". The exclusion is the SMALLEST of two limits, taken per child, then summed:

1. The **per-child lifetime cap** of $17,280 minus the amount of employer-provided benefits already excluded for the same child in prior tax years (Form 8839 line 21 — remaining lifetime exclusion).
2. The **current-year benefit allocated to that child** from the W-2 box 12 code T total (line 22). Exception: if the child has special needs AND the adoption became final in 2025, the worker may exclude the entire remaining lifetime cap regardless of how much code T was paid this year (the special-needs rule, Internal Revenue Code §137(a)(3)).

After the per-child smaller-of-two-numbers (line 24) is found, the **high-income phaseout** can further reduce the exclusion (lines 25 through 28). The result per child is the amount actually excluded (line 29). Summing across children gives line 30. Subtracting line 30 from the current-year total benefits (line 23) gives line 31 — the taxable amount that lands on Form 1040 line 1f.

### 1.5 The complete IRS equation (plain English)

For each eligible child (let `i` be the 1-based child index):

```text
Step A — Per-child remaining lifetime exclusion (Form 8839 lines 19, 20, 21):

    line19[i] = $17,280
                (per-child lifetime cap; reduced if the same child is being
                 adopted with another non-spouse adult — section 1.10)
    line20[i] = amount of employer-provided adoption benefits already
                excluded for this same child in prior tax years
    line21[i] = max(0, line19[i] - line20[i])
                (remaining lifetime exclusion still available for this child)

Step B — Current-year benefits allocated to this child (Form 8839 line 22):

    line22[i] = portion of the W-2 box 12 code T total this worker is
                claiming for child i this year
                (sum of line22 across children must equal the code T total,
                 with a small rounding tolerance; see section 1.5)

Step C — Smaller-of-two-numbers, with the special-needs exception
         (Form 8839 line 24):

    if hasSpecialNeeds[i] AND adoptionFinal2025OrEarlier[i]:
        line24[i] = line21[i]
                    (special-needs rule: exclude the full remaining cap
                     even if line22[i] is zero — IRC §137(a)(3))
    else if isForeignChild[i] AND adoptionFinal2025OrEarlier[i]
           AND NOT hasSpecialNeeds[i]:
        line24[i] = min(line21[i], line22[i] + deferredPriorYear[i])
                    (foreign-child finality rule: in the finality year, prior-
                     year benefits paid before finalization become
                     retroactively excludable — IRC §137(e))
    else:
        line24[i] = min(line21[i], line22[i])

Step D — Per-child Modified Adjusted Gross Income phaseout
         (Form 8839 lines 25-28):

    line25 = Modified Adjusted Gross Income for the return
    if line25 <= $259,190:
        phaseoutFraction = 0
    elif line25 >= $299,190:
        phaseoutFraction = 1.0000
    else:
        line26 = line25 - $259,190
        phaseoutFraction = round(line26 / $40,000, at least 3 decimals)

    line28[i] = line24[i] * phaseoutFraction
                (the part lost to the high-income phaseout)

Step E — Per-child excluded amount and return totals
         (Form 8839 lines 29, 30, 31):

    line29[i] = line24[i] - line28[i]
                (the part actually excluded from this worker's wages)

    line23 = sum over all children of line22[i]
             (total current-year employer-provided adoption benefits)
    line30 = sum over all children of line29[i]
             (total excluded from wages this year)
    line31 = line23 - line30
             (the residual = the taxable portion; may be negative)

Step F — Carry to Form 1040:

    Form 1040 line 1f = line31 (rounded to whole dollars, sign preserved)
```

### 1.6 Negative line 1f — the foreign-finality and special-needs cases

The 2025 Form 8839 instructions explicitly contemplate `line31 < 0`. The instruction is: when `line30 > line23`, subtract line 23 from line 30 and enter the result as a NEGATIVE number on both Form 8839 line 31 and Form 1040 line 1f. The 2025 Form 1040 instructions further ask the filer to write the letters "PYAB" (Prior-Year Adoption Benefits) on the dotted line to the left of line 1f.

Two scenarios produce a negative line 1f:

1. **Foreign-child finality (Internal Revenue Code §137(e)).** Employer-provided adoption benefits for a foreign child are NOT excludable until the year the adoption becomes final. In all earlier years, those benefits were taxed as wages. In the finality year, the worker can retroactively exclude those prior-year benefits — they flow through Form 8839 line 22 (augmented by the deferred prior-year amount) or directly into the special-needs branch. If the retroactive exclusion exceeds the current-year code T total, line 31 is negative — it reduces the worker's current-year wages on line 1f to undo the prior-year over-taxation.
2. **Special-needs off-W-2 (Internal Revenue Code §137(a)(3)).** When a special-needs adoption finalizes in 2025 under a written qualified adoption-assistance program, the worker may exclude the full remaining lifetime cap (`line21[i]`) even if the employer paid nothing in 2025 (code T = 0). With `line23 = 0` and `line30 > 0`, line 31 is negative.

Therefore the IRS rule requires:

- Line 1f must support negative values.
- Wages aggregation logic must not clamp line 1f to zero.
- Line 1z (the sum across lines 1a through 1i) and downstream totals must absorb the signed amount correctly.
- The "PYAB" annotation should appear on the rendered Form 1040 when line 1f is negative.

### 1.7 The Married Filing Separately rule (Internal Revenue Code §137(f))

If the worker is married, they must generally file a joint return to take the §137 exclusion. Married Filing Separately filers cannot claim the exclusion, unless:

- the taxpayer is legally separated under a decree of divorce or separate maintenance, OR
- the taxpayer lived apart from the spouse for the last 6 months of the tax year.

When neither exception applies on a Married Filing Separately return, the worker must report the entire W-2 box 12 code T amount as taxable wages on line 1f.

### 1.8 The more-than-2% S corporation owner rule (Internal Revenue Code §1372)

Internal Revenue Code §1372 treats an employee who owns more than 2% of the stock (or voting power) of a Subchapter S corporation as a partner of that corporation for fringe-benefit purposes. The §137 adoption-benefit exclusion is one of the fringe benefits affected. A more-than-2% S-corporation-owner-employee CANNOT exclude employer-provided adoption benefits from that S corporation; the full code T amount from that employer becomes taxable wages on line 1f.

Stock ownership for this rule includes both direct ownership and constructive ownership under Internal Revenue Code §318 (attribution from spouses, parents, children, and grandchildren).

### 1.9 The same-expenses rule (Internal Revenue Code §137(d))

The worker can claim both the Form 8839 Part III **exclusion** (employer-provided benefits) and the Form 8839 Part II **credit** (out-of-pocket expenses) in the same year — but NOT for the same dollar of qualified adoption expense. Expenses reimbursed by the employer are not "qualified" for the credit; only the worker's out-of-pocket amount is.

In practice: when the worker computes the Part II credit base, the amount of employer reimbursement received for that child must be subtracted from gross qualified expenses before applying the credit.

### 1.10 Shared adoption with a non-spouse (Internal Revenue Code §137(b)(1) + §23(d)(1)(A))

When the same eligible child is being adopted by the taxpayer and another adult who is NOT the spouse on a joint return (for example, an unmarried partner, or two unmarried siblings adopting together), the per-child $17,280 cap must be divided between the two adopting parties in any way they both agree. The same shared cap applies to both the Part III exclusion (line 19) and the Part II credit (line 2) for that child.

The IRS relies on the parties to agree honestly; there is no centralized verification.

### 1.11 More than three eligible children

The Form 8839 face has room for three children. When the worker is adopting more than three eligible children, the IRS instructions require attaching additional Forms 8839 — one continuation form per additional set of three children. For Part III specifically:

- Complete lines 19–22, 24, 28, and 29 for each child on whichever Form 8839 covers that child.
- Complete the return-level totals (lines 23, 25–27, 30, 31) on only ONE main form.
- Line 23 on the main form must equal the combined total of line 22 across all attached forms.
- Line 30 on the main form must equal the combined total of line 29 across all attached forms.

### 1.12 Whole-dollar rounding

The Form 1040 instructions allow whole-dollar rounding. Form 8839 lines 28, 29, and 31 are rounded once at the end before being carried to Form 1040 line 1f. The phaseout fraction itself (line 27) is computed to at least 3 decimal places (the IRS minimum); using more precision is acceptable.

### 1.13 What does NOT go on Line 1f

| Type of payment | Goes on | Why |
|---|---|---|
| Employer-provided adoption benefits that are FULLY excludable (within the per-child cap, low enough income, special-needs rule satisfied) | Nothing — they are not taxable | The exclusion absorbs the entire amount; line 31 = 0 or negative |
| Out-of-pocket adoption expenses paid by the worker | Form 8839 Part II (credit) — feeds Schedule 3 line 6c, not Form 1040 line 1f | Line 1f is for the *exclusion* (employer-provided benefits) only |
| Employer-provided adoption benefits already in W-2 box 1 | Line 1a (already taxed as ordinary wages) | The employer chose not to apply the exclusion at the payroll level; the exclusion can still be claimed on Form 8839, but the math runs differently — see Form 8839 instructions for "Box 1 inclusion" |
| Costs of adopting a spouse's child or surrogate-parenting arrangements | Not adoption expenses at all (Internal Revenue Code §137(d)(2)) | These do not qualify under §137 |

## Part 2 — The code implementation

The backend Java code that calculates Line 1f (and the rest of Form 8839 Part III) lives in `TaxReturnComputeService.java`, run at two different stages of the compute pipeline:

- **`computeAdoptionBenefits(...)`** — runs early in `prepare(...)` before Adjusted Gross Income is known. Computes Form 8839 Part III in full (the line 1f amount lands on `form1040.income.adoptionBenefits`), using a user-entered Modified Adjusted Gross Income if available, or a phaseout fraction of 0 if not.
- **`applyAdoptionCredit(...)`** — runs after Adjusted Gross Income is known and after the foreign tax credit has been applied. When the user did NOT enter a manual Modified Adjusted Gross Income, this second pass (called "G1" in the code comments) auto-derives Modified Adjusted Gross Income from Adjusted Gross Income plus the IRS-required add-backs, recomputes the per-child phaseout, and corrects line 1f.

This document focuses on Part III (the part that produces line 1f). Part II (the credit) is described where it affects the line 1f flow, but the credit itself appears on **Schedule 3 line 6c** and then on **Form 1040 line 20**, not on line 1f.

> Convention: backend references use method names rather than source-code line numbers (line numbers drift with refactors).

| Phaseout start (Modified Adjusted Gross Income threshold) | $259,190 | `ReferenceData.ADOPTION_PHASEOUT_START` | Form 8839 Part III line 25 — when Modified Adjusted Gross Income is at or below this, the phaseout fraction is 0 |
| Phaseout range (width of the phaseout) | $40,000 | `ReferenceData.ADOPTION_PHASEOUT_RANGE` | Form 8839 Part III line 27 — `phaseoutFraction = min(1, max(0, (magi - start) / range))` |
| Phaseout end (derived: start + range) | $299,190 | derived in code | At or above this Modified Adjusted Gross Income, the phaseout fraction is 1 (entire amount taxable) |
| Refundable cap (Part II line 11b — credit only) | $5,000 | `ReferenceData.ADOPTION_LINE11B_REFUNDABLE_CAP` | NOT used in line 1f math; used by Form 8839 Part II line 11b (the refundable portion of the adoption credit) |

The 2024 values were `$16,810 / $252,150 / $292,150`. The 2026 values will be set by Revenue Procedure 2025-32 when published. None of these values are hard-coded in the line 1f compute logic — they are all read from `ReferenceData`.

### 2.4 The code equation (Line 1f portion)

In plain English, the code does this (matching the IRS rule from Section 1.5):

```text
Step 0 — Decide whether Form 8839 Part III applies at all.

    Sum W-2 box 12 code T across W-2 statements:
      - On Married Filing Separately: sum only W-2s whose employeeSSN
        matches the taxpayer's SSN (the spouse's W-2s belong on the
        spouse's return).
      - On every other filing status: sum W-2s whose employeeSSN matches
        either the taxpayer's or the spouse's SSN.
      - If neither SSN is populated: legacy fallback sums all code T.
    Call this w2BoxTAmount.

    hasBenefits         = w2BoxTAmount > 0
    hasAdoptionInputs   = any child entry, any per-child allocation, or
                          any prior-year amount is populated
    hasSpecialNeedsFinal = any child has hasSpecialNeeds=true AND
                           adoptionFinal2025OrEarlier=true
    hasDeferredForeign   = any child has isForeignChild=true AND
                           adoptionFinal2025OrEarlier=true AND
                           NOT hasSpecialNeeds=true AND
                           deferredPriorYearForeignBenefits > 0

    If hasBenefits AND user did not answer "Yes" to
       "Did you adopt a child or receive adoption assistance in 2025?":
        Emit BLOCKING flag ADOPTION_BENEFITS_FORM_REQUIRED.

    If NOT hasBenefits AND NOT hasAdoptionInputs:
        Return AdoptionComputation(line1f = null, form8839 = null).
        (Line 1f is null — Form 8839 is not generated.)

Step 1 — Married Filing Separately gate (Internal Revenue Code §137(f)).

    If filing status is MFS AND
       form-filing-status.mfsOrHohLivedApartOrLegallySeparated is NOT true:
        Emit non-blocking advisory ADOPTION_BENEFITS_MFS_EXCLUSION_DISALLOWED.
        line1f = roundMoney(w2BoxTAmount)        (entire amount is taxable)
        Return AdoptionComputation(line1f, form8839 = null).

Step 2 — IRC §1372 partition (more-than-2% S corporation owner).

    Use computeSection1372DisallowedFromEligibleW2BoxT to split
    w2BoxTAmount into:
      - section1372DisallowedAmount = sum of code T from W-2s flagged
        as employers in which the worker is a more-than-2% owner
      - eligibleW2BoxTAmount = w2BoxTAmount minus section1372DisallowedAmount

    The Form 8839 Part III math below uses eligibleW2BoxTAmount only.
    The section1372DisallowedAmount stacks directly onto line1f as
    taxable wages at the end.

    If section1372DisallowedAmount > 0:
        Emit non-blocking advisory ADOPTION_BENEFITS_SECTION_1372_DISALLOWED.

Step 3 — Allowance gate (open Part III when any of three triggers apply).

    allowPartIII = hasBenefits OR hasSpecialNeedsFinal OR hasDeferredForeign

    If NOT allowPartIII:
        line1f = (hasAdoptionInputs ? 0 : null) + section1372DisallowedAmount

Step 4 — Smart-default allocation across children.

    If hasBenefits AND no per-child current-year allocation is set,
    evenly divide eligibleW2BoxTAmount across the children; the last
    child absorbs the rounding remainder. User can override.

Step 5 — Per-child loop (Form 8839 Part III line numbers).

    For each child i (1 through up to 6):
        perChildCap = resolveSharedAdoptionCap($17,280, i, adoptionData)
                      (returns the user's allocated share if shared
                       adoption applies for this child, else $17,280)

        line19[i] = perChildCap
        line20[i] = priorYearEmployerBenefitsByChild["child{i}PriorYearBenefits"]
                    (default 0)
        line21[i] = max(0, line19[i] - line20[i])
        line22[i] = currentYearBenefitsAllocation["child{i}Benefits2025"]
                    (default 0)

        specialNeeds       = hasSpecialNeeds == true
        foreignChild       = isForeignChild == true
        adoptionFinal      = adoptionFinal2025OrEarlier == true
        deferred           = deferredPriorYearForeignBenefitsByChild
                              ["child{i}DeferredPriorYearForeignBenefits"]

        if specialNeeds AND adoptionFinal:
            line24[i] = line21[i]
        else if foreignChild AND adoptionFinal AND NOT specialNeeds:
            line24[i] = min(line21[i], line22[i] + deferred)
        else:
            line24[i] = min(line21[i], line22[i])

        (Per-child gate: when NOT hasBenefits, line24 must come ONLY
         from the special-needs path or the foreign-deferred path; if
         line20[i] or line22[i] is positive without one of those paths,
         emit BLOCKING flag ADOPTION_BENEFITS_OFF_W2_NOT_SPECIAL_NEEDS_CHILD_i.)

Step 6 — Modified Adjusted Gross Income and phaseout (Phase 1).

    magi              = imports.magiForAdoptionBenefitsExclusion
                        (user-entered; null in most returns)
    phaseoutFraction  = computeAdoptionPhaseoutFraction(magi)
                        (null when magi is null; 0 when magi <= $259,190;
                         rounded to 4 decimals; capped at 1.0)
    phaseoutForCalc   = phaseoutFraction == null ? 0 : phaseoutFraction

    line28[i] = line24[i] × phaseoutForCalc
    line29[i] = line24[i] - line28[i]

Step 7 — Aggregation (Form 8839 lines 23, 30, 31).

    totalPart3Line22 = sum of line22[i]
    line23           = totalPart3Line22

    Allocation-match check:
      If hasBenefits AND |eligibleW2BoxTAmount - totalPart3Line22| > $1:
        Emit BLOCKING flag ADOPTION_BENEFITS_ALLOCATION_MISMATCH.

    line30 = sum of line29[i]
    line31 = line23 - line30

Step 8 — Store on the Form 1040 output.

    line1f = roundMoney(line31) + section1372DisallowedAmount
    form1040.income.adoptionBenefits = line1f  (signed; may be negative)
    (Also store every Form 8839 sub-line for the PDF rendering.)

Step 9 — Phase 2 / G1 fallback (in applyAdoptionCredit).

    If form8839.part2Line7ModifiedAgi is null (no manual entry in Phase 1)
       AND AGI is known:
      autoMagi = AGI
               + Form 2555 line 45 (taxpayer + spouse)
               + Form 2555 line 50 (taxpayer + spouse)
               + Form 4563 line 15
               + Puerto Rico excluded income
               + studentLoanInterestDeduction          (Schedule 1 line 21)
               + savingsBondInterestExclusion          (Schedule B line 3)
               + foreignHousingDeduction              (Schedule 1 line 24j)
      newPhaseoutFraction = computeAdoptionPhaseoutFraction(autoMagi)
      Re-run the per-child Step 6 and Step 7 with newPhaseoutFraction.
      Overwrite form1040.income.adoptionBenefits with the corrected line1f
      (still + section1372DisallowedAmount).
```

A difference larger than $100 usually means the manual entry is from a prior tax year, missing an add-back, or contains a typo. |

### 2.6 Married Filing Separately guard

On a Married Filing Separately return without the legal-separation / lived-apart-6-months exception, the code:

- Skips the entire Form 8839 Part III computation.
- Emits the non-blocking `ADOPTION_BENEFITS_MFS_EXCLUSION_DISALLOWED` advisory.
- Sets `line1f = roundMoney(w2BoxTAmount)` — the entire code T amount becomes taxable wages.
- Does not produce a Form 8839 output object (`form8839 = null`).

This matches §137(f) faithfully — except that the per-W-2 SSN filter for `w2BoxTAmount` is still applied first, so a spouse's W-2 doesn't inflate the disallowed amount on the taxpayer's return.

When the exception is true (`mfsOrHohLivedApartOrLegallySeparated = true`), the Married Filing Separately filer is treated as unmarried for §137 purposes and the regular Part III computation runs.

### 2.7 The two-pass Form 8839 computation

Form 8839 Part III line 25 (Modified Adjusted Gross Income) cannot be computed until after Adjusted Gross Income is known. Adjusted Gross Income is not known when `computeAdoptionBenefits(...)` runs, because line 1f is itself an input to Adjusted Gross Income (via line 1z → line 9 → line 11).

The application breaks the chicken-and-egg by running the compute in two passes:

1. **Phase 1** (in `computeAdoptionBenefits`) — runs early; uses the user-entered `imports.magiForAdoptionBenefitsExclusion` if available, else defaults the phaseout fraction to 0 (full exclusion in this pass). Produces a *preliminary* line 1f that feeds line 1z, line 9, and line 11.
2. **Phase 2 / G1** (in `applyAdoptionCredit`) — runs after Adjusted Gross Income is final and the foreign tax credit has been applied. If Phase 1 used no manual Modified Adjusted Gross Income, this phase auto-derives Modified Adjusted Gross Income from Adjusted Gross Income plus all the IRS-required add-backs (Section 2.4 Step 9), recomputes the per-child phaseout, and overwrites `form1040.income.adoptionBenefits` with the corrected line 1f.

Manual entry of Modified Adjusted Gross Income wins over G1 — Phase 2 only fires when the user left the field blank.

### 3.1 Items the code does NOT support (intentional out-of-scope decisions)

The following are documented as out of scope and not bugs:

1. **More than 6 children in a single return.** Form 8839 face-form covers 3 children; the application handles 6 inline. Returns with more than 6 eligible children emit the non-blocking `ADOPTION_BENEFITS_TOO_MANY_CHILDREN` advisory and the worker must attach extra Forms 8839 manually. The compute correctly handles the first 6.
2. **Multi-form 8839 attachment math.** Even within the 6-child scope, the IRS rule for line 23 and line 30 on the *main* form versus *attached* forms (Section 1.11) is not modelled because the application produces only one Form 8839 output, not a stack.
3. **Independent §1372 §318 constructive-ownership verification.** The §1372 outer screening question wording asks the worker to include attributed stock ("directly or through attribution to a spouse, parents, or children"), but the application does not separately compute §318 attribution. The worker's self-report is trusted.
4. **Centralized inter-party shared-adoption-total enforcement.** Under §137(b)(1), the two adopting parties must agree that their two allocated shares of the $17,280 cap sum to no more than $17,280. The application clamps each side at $17,280 individually but cannot detect a coordinated over-claim (the other party's return is on a different account / a different software).
5. **Independent verification of the employer's "written qualified adoption-assistance program" (§137(c)).** For the §137(a)(3) special-needs branch, the worker's self-reported `hasSpecialNeeds=true` + `adoptionFinal2025OrEarlier=true` is trusted. The application cannot read the employer's plan documents. A non-blocking reminder advisory (`ADOPTION_BENEFITS_WRITTEN_QAAP_REMINDER`) fires when line 1f is negative — the case in which the §137(c) written-program requirement is most consequential.

### 3.2 Items where the code is stricter than the IRS rule (defensive measures)

1. **Allocation-match blocking flag.** The IRS does not require the sum of per-child line 22 allocations to equal the W-2 code T total exactly. The application enforces `|w2BoxTAmount - sumLine22| ≤ $1` and emits the `ADOPTION_BENEFITS_ALLOCATION_MISMATCH` blocking flag otherwise. The ±$1 tolerance absorbs rounding artefacts from the even-split smart default.
2. **Per-W-2 SSN filter on Married Filing Separately.** When multiple W-2s are present, only those whose `employeeSSN` matches the taxpayer's SSN feed `w2BoxTAmount` on a Married Filing Separately return. This prevents a stale spouse W-2 from inflating the disallowed amount on the taxpayer's return.
3. **Per-child off-W-2 gate (Internal Revenue Code §137(a)(3))**. The `ADOPTION_BENEFITS_OFF_W2_NOT_SPECIAL_NEEDS_CHILD_{i}` blocking flag refuses to compute a child's line 22 or line 20 when there is no code T amount AND the child is neither special-needs-final nor foreign-deferred. The IRS would simply produce a wrong number; the application stops the user from filing it.
4. **§1372 outer screening required when code T > 0.** The UI blocks the save of `form-adoption-expenses` until `section1372AnyEmployer` is answered Yes or No on any return where any W-2 has code T > 0. The IRS does not have a paper form analogue; this is a UX gate.
5. **Shared-adoption outer screening required when any child has Part II or Part III amount.** Same shape as the §1372 screening — UX gate not present on the paper form.

## Part 4 — Summary

**Form 1040 Line 1f = the taxable portion of employer-provided adoption benefits, computed as Form 8839 Part III line 31: the total current-year benefits (W-2 box 12 code T, SSN-filtered on Married Filing Separately) minus the per-child sum of (smaller-of the remaining lifetime cap and the current-year allocation, with the special-needs and foreign-finality branches allowing line 24 = line 21 or line 22 + deferred respectively), reduced by the Modified Adjusted Gross Income phaseout running from $259,190 to $299,190 in 2025. The amount may be NEGATIVE (the foreign-finality and special-needs branches can produce a retroactive prior-year exclusion that exceeds current-year benefits); when negative, the "PYAB" annotation is rendered. More-than-2% S corporation owner disqualification (§1372) and the Married Filing Separately disallowance (§137(f)) stack the disallowed amount directly on line 1f as taxable wages. The same dollar cannot be claimed for both the Part III exclusion and the Part II credit (§137(d)) because the Part II input is structurally split into gross expenses minus employer reimbursement.**

The adoption **credit** (Form 8839 Part II → Schedule 3 line 6c → Form 1040 line 20) is computed alongside line 1f but is a separate output — described in the dependencies file (`C:\us-tax\dependencies\1f.md`) and not in this document.
