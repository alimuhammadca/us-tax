# Form 1040 — Line 1h — Other earned income

**Tax year: 2025.**
**Last verified against the code on 2026-06-01.**
**Gap status overview: 4 of 8 RESOLVED** (Gaps 1, 5, 6, 7 — the 4 documented overstatement bugs) — see [Status overview](#status-overview-refreshed-2026-06-01) in Part 3.

This document explains how the dollar amount that goes on **Line 1h** of Form 1040 is calculated. Line 1h is the catch-all wages slot for **earned income that belongs in the wages section but is not covered by lines 1a through 1g**. The 2025 IRS instructions identify exactly four categories of money that go here:

1. **Strike or lockout benefits** (paid by a union to a worker during a strike or lockout, other than bona fide gifts).
2. **Excess elective deferrals** (the amount by which a worker's pre-tax retirement contributions for the year exceeded the IRS annual limit).
3. **Disability pension payments** received from an employer plan **before** the worker reaches the plan's minimum retirement age (reported on Form 1099-R with distribution code 3).
4. **Corrective distributions** from a retirement plan (Form 1099-R with distribution code 8 for the **current year only**; code P is for a prior year and goes on the prior year's return).

Line 1h is a **return-level** number — there is no separate taxpayer Form 1h and spouse Form 1h on Form 1040 (unlike Form 8919, which is per-spouse). But several of the source records (W-2 statements, 1099-R statements) are per-person, and the application must correctly attribute each one to the taxpayer or spouse before computing the return-level total.

The document is organized in three parts:

1. **The IRS rule.** What the controlling IRS publications and Internal Revenue Code sections say must happen. This is the source of truth.
2. **The code implementation.** What the application's backend Java code currently does.
3. **The comparison.** A side-by-side check showing whether the code follows the IRS rule honestly, and where the two diverge.

A note on vocabulary used in this document:

- **Elective deferral.** A worker's pre-tax contribution to a retirement plan such as a 401(k), 403(b), SIMPLE IRA, or 457(b). Pre-tax means the contribution is subtracted from the worker's wages **before** the wages are reported on the W-2 in box 1 — so the contribution is not in box 1 wages. On the W-2, elective deferrals appear in box 12 with a one- or two-letter code (D, E, F, S, G, etc.). Roth contributions are NOT elective deferrals for line 1h purposes — they are after-tax and already included in W-2 box 1.
- **402(g) limit.** Internal Revenue Code §402(g) caps the total amount a worker can defer in a year across all 401(k), 403(b), SARSEP, and similar plans combined. For 2025 the base limit is **$23,500**.
- **SIMPLE plan.** Savings Incentive Match Plan for Employees of Small Employers. A simpler retirement plan than a 401(k); has a lower annual deferral limit. For 2025 the base limit is **$16,500**.
- **457(b) plan.** A deferred-compensation plan for state and local government workers and certain tax-exempt organizations. For 2025 the base limit is **$23,500**.
- **Catch-up contribution.** An additional amount a worker age 50 or older may defer above the base limit. For 2025: $7,500 extra for 402(g)-type plans, $3,500 extra for SIMPLE plans, $7,500 extra for 457(b) plans.
- **Enhanced catch-up contribution.** A higher catch-up amount for workers age 60, 61, 62, or 63 at year end, added by the SECURE 2.0 Act §603. For 2025: $11,250 extra for 402(g)-type plans, $5,250 extra for SIMPLE plans, $11,250 extra for 457(b) plans. Reverts back to the regular $7,500 / $3,500 / $7,500 catch-up amounts at age 64.
- **Excess elective deferral.** The amount by which a worker's total pre-tax elective deferrals for the year exceeded the applicable annual limit (base limit + catch-up if eligible). The excess is taxable wages on Form 1040 line 1h. The same excess withdrawn from the plan in a later year is **not** taxed again — the line 1h reporting is the worker's recognition of income for the deferral year.
- **Designated Roth contribution.** A retirement-plan contribution made on an **after-tax** basis. On the W-2 these appear in box 12 with code AA (Roth 401(k)) or BB (Roth 403(b)). Because Roth contributions are after-tax, they are already in W-2 box 1 wages — including them in line 1h would double-count the income. The 2025 Form 1040 instructions explicitly direct the filer to exclude Roth amounts from the line 1h excess-deferral computation.
- **Minimum retirement age.** The earliest age at which a worker can begin drawing a pension or annuity from a specific employer plan without it being treated as an early disability payment. The plan document defines this age — there is no single nationwide number. Disability pension payments received **before** this age are wages on line 1h; payments received **at or after** this age are pension income on lines 5a / 5b.
- **Form 1099-R distribution codes.** Single- or two-letter codes the payer enters in box 7 of Form 1099-R to describe the type of distribution. Relevant to line 1h:
  - **Code 3** — Disability. The distribution is a disability pension payment.
  - **Code 8** — Excess contributions plus earnings, taxable in the **current** year.
  - **Code P** — Excess contributions plus earnings, taxable in a **prior** year (so the amount does NOT go on the current year's line 1h).
  - Codes 3 and 8 can appear together on the same 1099-R; when they do, the application routes the entry through the disability path only (to prevent the same amount from being counted twice).
- **IRA / SEP / SIMPLE corrective distribution.** A corrective distribution from a *traditional or Roth IRA* (not from an employer 401(k) / 403(b) / 457(b)). These do NOT go on line 1h — they belong on Form 1040 line 4a / 4b. The Form 1099-R issuer marks them by checking the box labeled "IRA / SEP / SIMPLE" on the form. The application reads this as the boolean `iraSepSimple`.
- **PSO (Public Safety Officer) exclusion.** A retired public safety officer who pays for health or long-term-care insurance directly from the pension distribution may exclude up to $3,000 from the pension's taxable amount. If the same officer is reporting a disability pension on line 1h, the **excluded** amount is removed from the line 1h figure and the letters "PSO" are written next to line 1h on the paper return. The application does not currently implement PSO — see Gap 1 below.
- **Strike / lockout benefits.** Money or property a union pays its members during a labor strike or employer lockout. Treated as wages unless the union's facts clearly establish the payment was a bona fide gift. Reported on line 1h.

## Part 1 — The IRS rule (the source of truth)

### 1.1 Authority

The IRS publications and Internal Revenue Code sections used to write this document are in `C:\us-tax\docs\books\` unless noted otherwise:

- **2025 Instructions for Form 1040 / 1040-SR** (`i1040gi_2025.pdf`) — the controlling rule for the Line 1h label. Defines Line 1h as "Other earned income" and lists exactly four categories: strike/lockout benefits, excess elective deferrals, disability pensions (1099-R code 3 before minimum retirement age), and corrective distributions (1099-R code 8 current year).
- **Publication 17 — Your Federal Income Tax** (`p17.pdf`) — the "Wages, Salaries, and Other Earnings" chapter covers all four line 1h categories in lay terms. Specifically discusses disability pensions and minimum retirement age (chapter 5, "Disability Pensions"), strike and lockout benefits (chapter 5, "Strike and lockout benefits"), and excess elective deferrals (chapter 5, "Elective Deferrals").
- **Publication 525 — Taxable and Nontaxable Income** (`p525.pdf`) — the deeper reference for line 1h categories. Sections "Disability Pensions", "Strike and lockout benefits", "Excess Salary Deferrals", and "Corrective Distributions" each map directly to one of the four line 1h sub-amounts.
- **Publication 575 — Pension and Annuity Income** (NOT local; available at <https://www.irs.gov/pub/irs-pdf/p575.pdf>) — explains the minimum-retirement-age boundary between line 1h (wages) and lines 5a / 5b (pension). Documentation gap: not bundled in `docs/books/`.
- **Instructions for Forms 1099-R and 5498** (NOT local; available at <https://www.irs.gov/instructions/i1099r>) — the procedural source for the distribution-code definitions. Specifies that code 3 = disability, code 8 = current-year excess, code P = prior-year excess, and that codes 3 and 8 may appear together. Documentation gap: not bundled in `docs/books/`.
- **Internal Revenue Code §402(g)(1)** — the annual elective deferral limit. For 2025 the indexed limit is **$23,500** (Rev. Proc. 2024-40; IRS Notice 2024-80).
- **Internal Revenue Code §414(v)(2)(B)(i)** — the regular age-50 catch-up amount. For 2025: **$7,500** for 402(g)-type plans and **$3,500** for SIMPLE plans.
- **Internal Revenue Code §414(v)(2)(B)(iii)** (added by **SECURE 2.0 Act §109**, Pub. L. 117-328) — the enhanced catch-up for workers age 60 through 63. For 2025: **$11,250** for 402(g)-type plans and **$5,250** for SIMPLE plans (150% of the regular catch-up).
- **Internal Revenue Code §408(p)(2)(E)** — the SIMPLE plan annual deferral limit. For 2025: **$16,500**.
- **Internal Revenue Code §457(e)(15)** — the 457(b) annual deferral limit. For 2025: **$23,500** (same as the §402(g) limit).
- **Internal Revenue Code §72(t)(2)(A)(iii)** — defines the disability exception for early distributions. Disability for §72(t) is "unable to engage in any substantial gainful activity by reason of any medically determinable physical or mental impairment which can be expected to result in death or to be of long-continued and indefinite duration".

Supplementary general-purpose references in `docs/books/`: `J.K. Lasser's Your Income Tax 2025` chapter on wages and retirement-plan distributions; `Taxes For Dummies 2024 Edition`. Both restate the same rules in lay terms; neither is the controlling source.

The local file `C:\us-tax\lines\1h.md` is the project's developer-facing specification for Line 1h. The companion knowledge file at `C:\us-tax\knowledge\line-1h-other-earned-income.md` and the dependency map at `C:\us-tax\dependencies\1h.md` track the same rules at finer-grained level (per-section field tables, attribution rules, downstream form integration, test coverage).

### 1.2 The basic equation

From the 2025 Instructions for Form 1040, line 1h:

> Other earned income. Use the *Wages, Salaries, and Tips* section of Pub. 17 and the *Other Earned Income* part of the 1040 instructions to figure the amounts to include.

The four categories aggregate into a single line 1h amount:

```text
Line 1h = disabilityPensionWages                  (Category 1)
        + excessElectiveDeferrals                 (Category 2)
        + correctiveDistributionsCurrentYear      (Category 3)
        + strikeOrLockoutBenefits                 (Category 4)
```

Each component is defined in sections 1.3 through 1.6 below. If all four components are zero or null, line 1h itself is null (blank on the printed return).

### 1.3 Category 1 — Disability pension wages

#### What the IRS says

> Disability pensions. Generally, you must report as income any amount you receive for your disability through an accident or health insurance plan that is paid for by your employer. You must report your taxable disability payments on line 1h of Form 1040 or 1040-SR until you reach minimum retirement age. (Publication 17, chapter 5, "Disability Pensions"; same rule in the 2025 1040 instructions.)

In plain English:

- A worker who retires on disability and receives a pension from an employer plan reports those payments as **wages** on **Line 1h** (NOT as pension income on lines 5a / 5b) until the worker reaches the plan's **minimum retirement age**.
- After minimum retirement age, the same payments become **pension income** on lines 5a / 5b instead. The amount stops flowing through Line 1h.
- Payments by reason of an occupational sickness or injury under a federal, state, or local statute are not taxable wages and do not go on line 1h at all (a separate exclusion).

#### Inputs (from Form 1099-R)

For each Form 1099-R the worker receives:

```text
recipientTIN          — the Social Security Number of the person who received
                        the payment; tells the application whether this is the
                        taxpayer's 1099-R or the spouse's 1099-R
distributionCodes     — box 7 of the 1099-R; if it contains "3" this is a
                        disability pension
taxableAmountAmount   — box 2a; the taxable portion (the payer has already
                        backed out any non-taxable basis)
grossDistributionAmount — box 1; the gross amount before basis recovery,
                        used only as a fallback when box 2a is blank
```

And from the taxpayer's `other-earned-income` personal form, per 1099-R entry:

```text
disabilityPensions.entries[i].source1099RId    — links the selection to the
                                                  1099-R statement entry
disabilityPensions.entries[i].owner            — "taxpayer" or "spouse",
                                                  required only when the
                                                  recipientTIN matches
                                                  neither family Social Security
                                                  Number
disabilityPensions.entries[i].isBelowPlanMinimumRetirementAge
                                                — Yes / No; the gating answer.
                                                  Cannot be derived from the
                                                  1099-R because the plan's
                                                  minimum retirement age is
                                                  plan-specific and not on the
                                                  form
```

#### Equation

For each Form 1099-R with distribution code 3:

```text
if isBelowPlanMinimumRetirementAge == Yes:
    amountForThis1099R = (box2aTaxableAmount IS NULL ?
                          box1GrossDistribution :
                          box2aTaxableAmount)
else:
    amountForThis1099R = 0  (the payment goes on lines 5a / 5b instead)

disabilityPensionWages = sum over all qualifying 1099-Rs of amountForThis1099R
```

The box 2a → box 1 fallback applies because disability pension payments are **fully taxable wages** — there is no basis recovery in the wages-on-line-1h path. If the issuer left box 2a blank, the worker is expected to use the gross amount.

#### Validation

- If a 1099-R with code 3 is present and `isBelowPlanMinimumRetirementAge` has not been answered, the return **cannot compute** correctly. The application must block the compute and ask the user to answer the question. (This is a blocking flag — see Part 2.)
- If the `recipientTIN` matches neither the taxpayer nor the spouse Social Security Number, the application **cannot tell** which family member the payment belongs to. The user must select an owner; without it, the compute is blocked.

### 1.4 Category 2 — Excess elective deferrals

#### What the IRS says

> If you participate in a section 401(k), 403(b), or 457(b) plan or a SIMPLE IRA, your elective deferral limit for 2025 is $23,500 ($16,500 for SIMPLE plans). If the total deferred for all plans in 2025 was more than the limit, the excess is included on Form 1040 line 1h. (2025 Form 1040 instructions, line 1h, paragraph on "Excess salary deferrals".)

Two further rules apply:

- **Roth amounts are excluded.** The 2025 1040 instructions explicitly say: "Do not include the excess attributable to designated Roth contributions on line 1h, because those amounts are already included in box 1 of your Form W-2." The application must compute the excess from **pre-tax** deferral codes only.
- **Catch-up contributions are excluded from the cap.** A worker age 50 or older may add $7,500 (or $3,500 for SIMPLE; or $11,250 / $5,250 for ages 60 through 63 under SECURE 2.0 §603) on top of the base limit before any excess is recognized.

#### Inputs (from W-2 box 12)

For each W-2 statement attributed to the taxpayer or spouse:

```text
employeeSSN              — used for attribution: must match taxpayer Social
                           Security Number for taxpayer pool, or spouse Social
                           Security Number for spouse pool
box12Entries[].code      — one of D, E, F, S, G — see code table below
box12Entries[].box12Amount  — the dollar amount deferred under that code
```

The applicable W-2 box 12 codes for line 1h's excess-deferral pool:

| Code | Plan type | Pool |
|---|---|---|
| D | 401(k) elective deferral (pre-tax) | 402(g) |
| E | 403(b) elective deferral (pre-tax) | 402(g) |
| F | SARSEP §408(k)(6) elective deferral | 402(g) |
| S | SIMPLE plan §408(p) elective deferral | SIMPLE |
| G | §457(b) governmental / tax-exempt deferred-compensation contribution | 457(b) |

**Excluded codes** (Roth — already in W-2 box 1, so excluded from the line 1h excess-deferral pool):

| Code | Plan type |
|---|---|
| AA | Roth 401(k) contribution |
| BB | Roth 403(b) contribution |

The 2025 1040 instructions also reference (but the application does not yet support):

- **EE** — Roth 457(b) contribution (also excluded from line 1h)
- Higher SIMPLE 2.0 base limit of $17,600 with $3,850 catch-up that **certain** SIMPLE plans may elect under SECURE 2.0 §117

#### Constants (2025 reference data)

| Constant name | 2025 IRS value | Source |
|---|---|---|
| `ELECTIVE_DEFERRAL_402G_LIMIT` | **$23,500** | IRC §402(g); Rev. Proc. 2024-40; IRS Notice 2024-80 |
| `ELECTIVE_DEFERRAL_402G_CATCHUP` | **$7,500** | IRC §414(v)(2)(B)(i) |
| `ELECTIVE_DEFERRAL_402G_ENHANCED_CATCHUP` | **$11,250** | IRC §414(v)(2)(B)(iii) — SECURE 2.0 §109 |
| `ELECTIVE_DEFERRAL_SIMPLE_LIMIT` | **$16,500** | IRC §408(p)(2)(E); Rev. Proc. 2024-40 |
| `ELECTIVE_DEFERRAL_SIMPLE_CATCHUP` | **$3,500** | IRC §414(v)(2)(B)(ii) |
| `ELECTIVE_DEFERRAL_SIMPLE_ENHANCED_CATCHUP` | **$5,250** | IRC §414(v)(2)(B)(iii) — SECURE 2.0 §109 |
| `ELECTIVE_DEFERRAL_457B_LIMIT` | **$23,500** | IRC §457(e)(15) |
| `ELECTIVE_DEFERRAL_457B_CATCHUP` | **$7,500** | IRC §414(v)(2)(B)(i) |
| `ELECTIVE_DEFERRAL_457B_ENHANCED_CATCHUP` | **$11,250** | IRC §414(v)(2)(B)(iii) — SECURE 2.0 §109 |

These constants live in `ReferenceData.java`. They are *the* IRS values for 2025; the application reads them from one place so a 2026 update changes only that file.

#### Equation

For each person (taxpayer and spouse separately):

```text
ageAtYearEnd  = year(taxYearLastDay) - year(dateOfBirth)

# Choose the catch-up amount based on age
if ageAtYearEnd in [60, 61, 62, 63]:
    catchup402g   = ELECTIVE_DEFERRAL_402G_ENHANCED_CATCHUP   = $11,250
    catchupSimple = ELECTIVE_DEFERRAL_SIMPLE_ENHANCED_CATCHUP = $5,250
    catchup457b   = ELECTIVE_DEFERRAL_457B_ENHANCED_CATCHUP   = $11,250
elif ageAtYearEnd >= 50:
    catchup402g   = ELECTIVE_DEFERRAL_402G_CATCHUP   = $7,500
    catchupSimple = ELECTIVE_DEFERRAL_SIMPLE_CATCHUP = $3,500
    catchup457b   = ELECTIVE_DEFERRAL_457B_CATCHUP   = $7,500
else:
    catchup402g = catchupSimple = catchup457b = $0

# Sum the per-person pre-tax deferrals from W-2 box 12
total402g   = sum of W-2 box 12 amounts with codes D, E, F (this person's SSN)
totalSimple = sum of W-2 box 12 amounts with code S         (this person's SSN)
total457b   = sum of W-2 box 12 amounts with code G         (this person's SSN)

# Apply each pool's limit
limit402g   = $23,500 + catchup402g
limitSimple = $16,500 + catchupSimple
limit457b   = $23,500 + catchup457b

excess402g   = max(0, total402g   − limit402g)
excessSimple = max(0, totalSimple − limitSimple)
excess457b   = max(0, total457b   − limit457b)

excessForThisPerson = excess402g + excessSimple + excess457b

# Combine the two people
excessElectiveDeferrals = excessForTaxpayer + excessForSpouse
```

On a Married Filing Separately return, only the filing spouse's W-2 entries (matched by `employeeSSN`) are counted. The other spouse files separately.

### 1.5 Category 3 — Corrective distributions (current year)

#### What the IRS says

> Corrective distributions. If you received a corrective distribution from your retirement plan that included excess elective deferrals or excess contributions (plus earnings) and they were taxable in 2025, include the corrective distribution on line 1h. (2025 Form 1040 instructions, line 1h.)

In plain English:

- When a worker over-contributes to a 401(k), 403(b), SIMPLE, or 457(b) plan, the plan administrator may **return** the excess (plus the earnings on it) to the worker. That returned amount is a *corrective distribution* and is reported on Form 1099-R with distribution code **8** (current year) or **P** (prior year).
- Only **code 8** amounts go on the current year's line 1h. Code P amounts go on the **prior** year's return (the year the deferral was originally made) — and are excluded from the current year's line 1h.
- **IRA / SEP / SIMPLE corrective distributions** (when the 1099-R issuer has checked the "IRA / SEP / SIMPLE" box) go on Form 1040 lines 4a / 4b, NOT line 1h.

#### Inputs (from Form 1099-R)

```text
recipientTIN          — attribution to taxpayer or spouse
distributionCodes     — box 7; line 1h requires "8" (not "P", not just "3")
taxableAmountAmount   — box 2a; the taxable portion (which is what line 1h
                        receives — no fallback to box 1)
iraSepSimple          — the boolean derived from the form's IRA/SEP/SIMPLE
                        checkbox; if True, the entry is excluded from line 1h
```

And per-entry from `correctiveDistributions.entries[]` on the `other-earned-income` form:

```text
correctiveDistributions.entries[i].source1099RId  — the linked 1099-R entry
correctiveDistributions.entries[i].owner          — "taxpayer" or "spouse",
                                                    required only when the
                                                    recipientTIN matches
                                                    neither family Social
                                                    Security Number
```

#### Equation

```text
For each Form 1099-R with code 8 AND not code 3 AND iraSepSimple == False:
    contribution = box2aTaxableAmount   # NO fallback to box 1
correctiveDistributionsCurrentYear = sum over all qualifying 1099-Rs of contribution
```

**Why no fallback to box 1.** The IRS Instructions for Forms 1099-R require the payer to populate box 2a for code-8 distributions because the excess deferral must be split from any non-taxable basis. If box 2a is blank, treating the gross amount as taxable risks taxing return-of-basis. The correct behavior is to **exclude** that entry (and surface an issuer-error advisory) rather than fall back to box 1.

#### Overlap rule (codes 3 and 8 on the same 1099-R)

A single Form 1099-R can legitimately carry both code 3 (disability) and code 8 (excess contribution). When both codes are present, the entry is routed through the disability path only and **not** through the corrective-distribution path — the same dollar amount is never counted twice.

### 1.6 Category 4 — Strike or lockout benefits

#### What the IRS says

> Strike and lockout benefits. Benefits paid to you by a union as strike or lockout benefits, including both cash and the fair market value of other property, are usually included in your income as compensation. You can exclude these benefits from your income only when the facts clearly show that the union intended them as gifts to you. (Publication 17, chapter 5, "Strike and lockout benefits".)

In plain English: strike and lockout payments from a union are reported on line 1h as wages, unless the worker can clearly establish the payment was a bona fide gift.

#### Inputs

Strike benefits can arrive in two ways:

- **Imported from Form 1099-MISC.** When the worker has a 1099-MISC statement on the return whose box 3 has been **tagged as strike benefits** (the `box3IsStrikeBenefits` flag set to true), the box 3 amount (`otherIncomeAmount`) is automatically included.
- **Manually entered.** Strike benefits not reported on any tax statement are entered as a single dollar amount on the `other-earned-income` form (`strikeBenefits.manualStrikeBenefitsAmount`).

#### Equation

```text
importedStrike = sum of otherIncomeAmount over all 1099-MISC entries where
                 box3IsStrikeBenefits == True
manualStrike   = the value in strikeBenefits.manualStrikeBenefitsAmount
                 (a single number on the form)
strikeOrLockoutBenefits = importedStrike + manualStrike
```

If both sources are null, this category contributes nothing.

### 1.7 What does NOT go on line 1h

| Type of income | Goes on | Why |
|---|---|---|
| Disability pension at or after the plan's minimum retirement age | Form 1040 line 5a (gross) and line 5b (taxable) | The IRS reclassifies the payment as pension income once the minimum-retirement-age threshold is crossed. |
| IRA distributions (any kind) | Form 1040 line 4a (gross) and line 4b (taxable) | Separate statutory path for IRAs. The 1099-R issuer marks the IRA path via the IRA/SEP/SIMPLE box. |
| Corrective distributions from an IRA, SEP-IRA, or SIMPLE IRA | Form 1040 lines 4a / 4b | Same as above — the iraSepSimple flag is True. |
| Corrective distributions for excess contributions in a *prior* year (Form 1099-R code P) | The prior year's Form 1040 line 1h | The IRS rule for code P is that the income belongs to the prior year, not the current year. |
| Designated Roth elective deferrals (W-2 box 12 codes AA, BB, EE) | Form 1040 line 1a (already in W-2 box 1) | Roth deferrals are after-tax. Box 1 wages already include them; counting them again on line 1h would double-count. |
| Catch-up contributions within the catch-up cap | Form 1040 line 1a (already in W-2 box 1) | The catch-up adds room to the cap; an amount within the catch-up cap is NOT an excess. |
| Scholarship or fellowship grants not on a W-2 | Schedule 1 line 8r | Separate routing for scholarship/fellowship income; line 1h does not collect it. |
| Wages already correctly reported as W-2 box 1 income | Form 1040 line 1a | Already wages; no reclassification needed. |
| Nonqualified deferred-compensation amounts shown on W-2 box 11 | Form 1040 line 1a (and Schedule 1 line 8t for the box 11 subtraction) | Separate carve-out; line 1h does not collect them. |

### 1.8 Public Safety Officer (PSO) exclusion notation

When a retired public safety officer reports a disability pension on line 1h **and** elects the PSO exclusion (up to $3,000 of premiums paid for health or long-term-care insurance directly from the pension), the 2025 1040 instructions require:

```text
amountForThis1099R_paper = box2aTaxableAmount − amountOfPsoExclusion
write "PSO" next to line 1h
```

The PSO exclusion is fully implemented on the pension path (lines 5a / 5b path) but NOT on the line 1h disability path. See Gap 1 in Part 3.

### 2.1 Where the code lives

The line 1h compute is in:


Specifically:

### 2.2 Reference-data constants

The 2025 constants used by `computeExcessDeferralsForPerson` live in `ReferenceData.java`:

```text
TAX_YEAR                                          = 2025

ELECTIVE_DEFERRAL_402G_CATCHUP                    =  7500
```

### 2.3 Step-by-step compute (as the code runs it)

```text
1. Read the user's `other-earned-income` form:
       strikeBenefits.manualStrikeBenefitsAmount
       disabilityPensions.entries[]
       correctiveDistributions.entries[]

2. Resolve identity:
       taxpayerSsn   = you.ssn
       spouseSsn     = isMfsReturn ? null : spouse.ssn
       taxpayerAge   = computeAgeAtYearEnd(you.dateOfBirth)
       spouseAge     = isMfsReturn ? null : computeAgeAtYearEnd(spouse.dateOfBirth)

3. Load statements (server-side, via `statementDataService.listEntriesWithData`):
       rEntries      = all 1099-R entries on the return
       miscEntries   = all 1099-MISC entries on the return
       w2Entries     = (already loaded by the caller, passed in)

4. Compute strike total:
       manualStrike   = strikeBenefits.manualStrikeBenefitsAmount      (may be null)
       importedStrike = sumStrikeBenefitsFrom1099Misc(miscEntries)     (may be null)
       strikeTotal    = manualStrike + importedStrike                  (null-safe add)

5. Compute excess deferrals (per person):
       taxpayerTotals  = computeDeferralTotalsForSsn(w2Entries, taxpayerSsn)
                         (sums D/E/F → 402g, S → SIMPLE, G → 457b)
       spouseTotals    = computeDeferralTotalsForSsn(w2Entries, spouseSsn)
       taxpayerExcess  = computeExcessDeferralsForPerson(taxpayerTotals, taxpayerAge)
       spouseExcess    = computeExcessDeferralsForPerson(spouseTotals, spouseAge)
       excessDeferrals = taxpayerExcess + spouseExcess

6. Walk the 1099-R list once:
       For each entry:
         distributionCodes  = parseDistributionCodes(entry.distributionCodes)
         recipientSsn       = entry.recipientTIN
         (MFS guard: skip the entry entirely if the return is MFS and the
          recipientTIN matches the spouse SSN — that 1099-R belongs on the
          spouse's separate return)
         owner              = resolveOwnerFromRecipientSsn(recipientSsn,
                                                           taxpayerSsn,
                                                           spouseSsn)
                              (may return "taxpayer", "spouse", or null)

         If codes contains "3":            ## DISABILITY
             # If owner cannot be auto-resolved, read the user's selection
             # from disabilityPensions.entries[].owner; if still missing,
             # mark missingDisabilityOwner = true
             belowMinAge = entry's selection's isBelowPlanMinimumRetirementAge
             if belowMinAge is null:
                 missingDisabilityAge = true
             elif belowMinAge == true:
                 # Box 2a → box 1 fallback (Cat 3 only)
                 amount = entry.taxableAmountAmount ?? entry.grossDistributionAmount
                 disabilityTotal += amount
             else:
                 # Post-min-age; goes on lines 5a/5b instead, not here
                 (no contribution to line 1h)

         If codes contains "8" OR codes contains "P":      ## CORRECTIVE
             # Only when NOT also code 3 (the disability path already handled it)
             # and NOT IRA/SEP/SIMPLE
             if entry.iraSepSimple == true:
                 (skip — goes on lines 4a/4b)
             elif codes contains "3":
                 (skip — already counted in the disability path)
             elif codes contains "8":  ## current year
                 # Same owner-resolution path; if still missing, mark
                 # missingCorrectiveOwner = true
                 # NO box-2a-to-box-1 fallback for Cat 4
                 amount = entry.taxableAmountAmount
                 correctiveTotal += amount   (null-safe add — null is dropped)
             # codes contains "P" (prior year) entries fall through here
             # silently and contribute nothing (the prior year owns them)

7. Emit blocking flags when required:
       missingDisabilityOwner          → LINE1H_DISABILITY_OWNER_REQUIRED
       missingDisabilityAge            → LINE1H_DISABILITY_MIN_RETIREMENT_AGE_REQUIRED
       missingCorrectiveOwner          → LINE1H_CORRECTIVE_OWNER_REQUIRED

8. Sum the four categories and round:
       line1hRaw = disabilityTotal + excessDeferrals + correctiveTotal + strikeTotal
       line1h    = roundMoney(line1hRaw)            # HALF_UP rounding to whole dollar

9. Build the statement-descriptions list (used for the printed-return text):
       if disabilityTotal != null && != 0  →  add "Disability pension wages"
       if excessDeferrals  != null && != 0  →  add "Excess elective deferrals"
       if correctiveTotal  != null && != 0  →  add "Corrective distributions (current year)"
       if strikeTotal      != null && != 0  →  add "Strike/lockout benefits"

10. Return OtherEarnedIncomeComputation(line1h, statements).
```

#### Gap 1 — Public Safety Officer (PSO) exclusion on the line 1h disability path

**IRS rule.** A retired public safety officer reporting a disability pension on line 1h who also pays for accident, health, or long-term-care insurance directly from the pension may exclude up to $3,000 of those premiums from the line 1h amount. The paper return is annotated with "PSO" next to the line 1h figure and the excluded amount is subtracted from the taxable amount before it enters line 1h.

**Resolution status: RESOLVED 2026-06-01** (Phase 1a backend + Phase 1b UI/YAML/e2e/docs).

**Backend resolution (Phase 1a — 2026-06-01).** ✅ Compute path implemented.
- New constant `ReferenceData.PSO_PREMIUM_EXCLUSION_CAP = $3,000` (statutory; not annually-indexed).
- New helper `computeLine1hPsoExclusion(psoForm, perPersonDisabilityTotal)` reads three fields (`isEligibleRetiredPublicSafetyOfficer`, `electsPsoPremiumExclusion`, `totalQualifyingPsoPremiumsPaid`) and returns the per-person exclusion (`min(premiums, $3,000)`, further clamped to the person's disability total via `subtractNonNegative`).
- `computeOtherEarnedIncome` now tracks per-person disability totals (`disabilityTotalTaxpayer` / `disabilityTotalSpouse`), applies per-person PSO, and recomposes the combined disability total **after** PSO subtraction.
- The statement-descriptions list emits `"Disability pension wages (PSO)"` instead of `"Disability pension wages"` when any PSO exclusion was applied (the IRS-required PSO notation on the printed return).
- Four new unit tests lock in: under-cap exclusion ($2,400 PSO → line 1h $2,600); over-cap exclusion ($5,000 PSO capped at $3,000 → line 1h $7,000); clamp-to-disability-total edge case (line 1h disability portion → $0); double-gate ineligibility (eligible=false → no exclusion).
- 597 / 597 TaxReturnComputeServiceTest cases pass after the change. No regression.
- Form structure reads `data.disabilityPensions.psoExclusionTaxpayer` and `data.disabilityPensions.psoExclusionSpouse` (skipped on MFS to honor the spouse-isolation guard).

**Frontend resolution (Phase 1b — 2026-06-01).** ✅ Angular form + YAML + e2e + docs all shipped.

- `form-other-earned-income.component.ts`: added `PsoElection` interface, default model now includes
  `psoExclusionTaxpayer` and `psoExclusionSpouse` sub-objects, mergeDefaults / normalizeForSave
  handle them, helper methods `hasTaxpayerDisabilityEntry()` / `hasSpouseDisabilityEntry()`
  control conditional rendering.
- `form-other-earned-income.component.html`: per-person PSO sections render only when the person
  has at least one disability entry. Progressive disclosure: eligibility → election → premium
  amount. Help text reminds the user of the $3,000 cap.
- `yamls/1h-other-earned-income.yaml`: documented as sections `Step 3a` (taxpayer) and `Step 3b`
  (spouse) with `showIf` gates on the respective disability ownership.
- E2E test `PSO premium exclusion reduces line 1h disability wages and adds PSO statement label`
  in `line1h-other-earned-income.spec.ts` drives the new UI end-to-end and verifies both the
  reduced line 1h amount and the `"Disability pension wages (PSO)"` statement label.
- Updated `lines/1h.md`, `knowledge/line-1h-other-earned-income.md`, `dependencies/1h.md`.

**Known limitation (deferred enhancement).** The IRS rule shares one $3,000 PSO cap per person across both line 1h AND line 5b — a person who has both a below-min-age disability 1099-R AND an above-min-age pension 1099-R in the same year must split the $3,000 between the two lines. The current implementation enforces the $3,000 cap on each line independently. In practice a single 1099-R goes to line 1h XOR line 5b based on the below-min-age gate, so the independent caps produce the correct answer in the common case. The cross-line shared-cap enforcement is documented as a future enhancement.

#### Gap 2 — Prior-year code P entries are dropped silently (no advisory)

**IRS rule.** A 1099-R with code P represents excess deferrals that were taxable in a **prior** year. The amount belongs on the prior year's amended return, not the current year. The IRS expects the worker to know this and act on it (typically by amending the prior year).

**Code behavior.** The current compute correctly **excludes** code-P amounts from current-year line 1h (correct). But it does NOT raise any advisory message telling the user that a code-P 1099-R is present and that the prior year's return likely needs to be amended.

**Impact.** Low risk of overstating the *current* year's line 1h (this part is correct). Risk that the user is unaware they need to amend the prior year.

**Fix.** Add a non-blocking advisory flag (e.g., `LINE1H_PRIOR_YEAR_CORRECTIVE_DISTRIBUTION_DETECTED`) when at least one 1099-R has code P and is not also code 3 and is not IRA / SEP / SIMPLE. Surface the message "A 1099-R with prior-year corrective distribution code (code P) was found. The amount belongs on your previous year's return — consider filing Form 1040-X to amend that year if you have not already done so." This is tracked in the project's `outstanding.md` file.

#### Gap 3 — No advisory when issuer leaves box 2a blank on a code-8 corrective distribution

**IRS rule.** The 2025 Instructions for Forms 1099-R require the issuer to populate box 2a for corrective distributions. If a code-8 1099-R lacks box 2a, the issuer has erred.

**Code behavior.** The corrective path correctly does NOT fall back to box 1 — instead the entry contributes null and is dropped. But no advisory is surfaced to the user, so the user has no signal that an issuer-error situation exists.

**Impact.** Low — the silent-drop is the safest behavior (better than risking taxing return-of-basis). But the user may miss a refund opportunity if box 2a should have been populated but the issuer left it blank.

**Fix.** Add a non-blocking advisory flag (e.g., `LINE1H_CORRECTIVE_BOX2A_MISSING_ADVISORY`) when a code-8 1099-R with `iraSepSimple == false` has a blank box 2a. Tracked in `outstanding.md`.

#### Gap 4 — Designated Roth 457(b) (code EE) is not explicitly excluded

**IRS rule.** Code EE on W-2 box 12 represents a designated Roth contribution to a governmental 457(b) plan, added by SECURE 2.0 §603 (effective 2024). The amount is already in W-2 box 1 wages and must NOT be counted in the line 1h excess-deferral pool.

**Code behavior.** The current `DEFERRAL_457B_CODES = { G }` covers only the pre-tax pool. Code EE is not in any code set, so it is **not** included anywhere — which happens to give the correct line 1h answer (zero contribution).

**Impact.** None to line 1h today (the omission produces the correct behavior). But the rule is brittle — if a future change accidentally adds EE to `DEFERRAL_457B_CODES` thinking it is the 457(b) pool, the code would start including Roth in the line 1h excess pool and double-count Roth amounts (which are already in W-2 box 1).

**Fix (defensive).** Add code EE to a documented `DEFERRAL_ROTH_CODES = { AA, BB, EE }` constant in `TaxReturnComputeService.java` and reference it in the comments above `DEFERRAL_457B_CODES` to make the exclusion explicit. No behavioral change. This is documentation hygiene.

#### Gap 8 — No advisory when the same code (D, E, etc.) appears on W-2s from **different** employers

**IRS rule.** The 402(g), SIMPLE, and 457(b) limits are **per-person**, not per-plan or per-employer. A worker who deferred $20,000 to a 401(k) at employer A and $10,000 to a 401(k) at employer B has total deferrals of $30,000 — which exceeds the $23,500 limit by $6,500 even though neither plan independently exceeded the limit.

**Code behavior.** The summation **is** per-person (`sumW2Box12ByCodes(w2Entries, codes, ssn)` walks all W-2s of the same SSN), which is correct. So the excess is calculated correctly. There is no advisory telling the user *why* their line 1h has an excess when no single plan exceeded the limit, but the math is right.

**Impact.** None to the line 1h number. Possible user-confusion only.

**Fix.** Optional UX nicety — surface a non-blocking informational note such as "Your $30,000 in 401(k) deferrals across all employers exceeds the 2025 annual limit of $23,500 by $6,500." Not required for correctness.

### 3.3 Verified-correct items

- Four IRS categories and their routing rules — implemented.
- 2025 reference-data limits — match Rev. Proc. 2024-40 and IRS Notice 2024-80.
- Age-50 standard catch-up and age-60-to-63 enhanced catch-up — implemented.
- Roth code AA / BB exclusion from line 1h — implemented (excluded from the 402(g) code set).
- Box 2a → box 1 fallback for Category 1 (disability) only — implemented.
- No box 2a → box 1 fallback for Category 3 (corrective) — implemented (correctly excludes).
- IRA / SEP / SIMPLE corrective distribution exclusion — implemented.
- Codes 3 + 8 overlap (disability wins) — implemented.
- Strike-benefits dual sources (manual + 1099-MISC `box3IsStrikeBenefits`) — implemented.
- MFS guard against spouse-attributed W-2 / 1099-R amounts — implemented.
- Blocking flags for missing owner and missing minimum-retirement-age answer — implemented.
- Per-SSN attribution for W-2 box 12 (no spillover between spouses) — implemented.
- Statement descriptions correctly listed when each category has a non-zero contribution — implemented.

### 3.4 Summary

For tax year 2025, the **line 1h compute is honest in the common case AND in the edge cases that were the documented overstatement bugs.** The 4 real overstatement bugs (Gaps 1, 5, 6, 7) have all been resolved as of 2026-06-01. Specifically:

- A worker with a disability pension from a 1099-R code 3, below the plan's minimum retirement age, gets the correct amount on line 1h.
- A retired Public Safety Officer who pays qualifying premiums from their disability pension correctly reduces line 1h by up to $3,000 (Gap 1, resolved).
- A worker with excess 401(k) / 403(b) / SIMPLE / 457(b) deferrals (pre-tax codes only) gets the correct excess on line 1h, including:
  - Age-based catch-up ($7,500 age 50+, $11,250 ages 60–63 per SECURE 2.0 §603).
  - Elevated SIMPLE limit for SECURE 2.0 §117 plans ($17,600 / $3,850 / $5,775, Gap 5 resolved).
  - 457(b) 3-year special pre-retirement catch-up ($47,000 cap, replaces age-50/enhanced; Gap 6 resolved).
  - 403(b) 15-year-rule extra contribution ($3,000 bonus, stacks with age-50; Gap 7 resolved).
- A worker with a code-8 corrective distribution from a non-IRA plan gets the correct amount on line 1h.
- A worker with strike or lockout benefits (manual or tagged 1099-MISC) gets the correct amount on line 1h.
- The four amounts are summed and rounded to a whole dollar before flowing to the printed return.

**Remaining work.** The 4 OPEN gaps (Gaps 2, 3, 4, 8) are all UX/documentation polish — they do not affect the line 1h number. They can be addressed when convenient.

### 3.5 UI parity (added 2026-06-01)

The `form-other-earned-income` Angular form now mirrors the backend compute end-to-end in real time:

- A running line 1h total banner at the top of the form displays `formatCurrency(line1hTotal())` and recomputes on every input change. The local computation honors all four resolved gaps' opt-in flags:
  - PSO premium exclusion (Gap 1) — `computeLivePsoExclusion(pso, perPersonDisabilityTotal)` mirrors `TaxReturnComputeService.computeLine1hPsoExclusion` (per-person cap, clamp-to-disability semantics).
  - SECURE 2.0 §117 SIMPLE elevated limit (Gap 5) — `computeLiveExcessForPerson` swaps in `ELECTIVE_DEFERRAL_SIMPLE_117_LIMIT/CATCHUP/ENHANCED_CATCHUP` when the worker's `usesSimple117ElevatedLimits` flag is true.
  - IRC §457(b)(3) 3-year special catch-up (Gap 6) — `computeLiveExcessForPerson` replaces the regular 457(b) cap with `ELECTIVE_DEFERRAL_457B_3YEAR_SPECIAL_CATCHUP_LIMIT` and suppresses age-50/enhanced catch-up for the 457(b) pool only.
  - IRC §402(g)(7) 403(b) 15-year-rule bonus (Gap 7) — `computeLiveExcessForPerson` adds `ELECTIVE_DEFERRAL_403B_15YEAR_RULE_BONUS` to the 402(g) limit when the worker's `uses403b15YearRule` flag is true.
- The UI's `ReferenceDataService.ReferenceData` interface now exposes the 6 corresponding constants (added 2026-06-01 alongside the running-total banner).
- The compute service is unchanged. The local UI computation is for *display only* — the actual line 1h amount written to the tax return is produced by `TaxReturnComputeService.computeOtherEarnedIncome` as before. The two are required to agree.

The form also surfaces user-visible certification language on each Yes path of the four self-certifications (PSO eligibility + §117 + §457(b)(3) + §402(g)(7)) so the IRS audit-risk implications are explicit at the point of selection. See `test_cases/1h.md` Round D for the full UX-change inventory.
