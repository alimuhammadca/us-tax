# Form 1040 Line 1i — Nontaxable Combat Pay Election

**Tax year:** 2025
**Form 1040 / 1040-SR field:** Line 1i — "Nontaxable combat pay election"
**Backend output field:** `Income.nontaxableCombatPayElection`
**Audit date:** 2026-06-01

This document audits Form 1040 line 1i (Nontaxable combat pay election) against the Internal Revenue Service (IRS) source rules and against the application's computed output. The objective is to validate the application against the IRS documents — not the other way around. Any disagreement between the two is documented as a gap to be fixed in the application.

The structure of the document:

1. **Part 1 — The IRS rule (the source of truth)** — what line 1i represents, where it is sourced from, and what the IRS instructions require.
2. **Part 2 — The code implementation (what the application actually does)** — step-by-step description of the Java compute method.
3. **Part 3 — The comparison** — side-by-side match of IRS rule against the code; gaps and verified-correct items.
4. **Field reference** — exact field names from the input and output spreadsheet manifests.
5. **Reference data constants** — values that change from one tax year to another.

Acronyms used in this document are spelled out the first time they appear and listed in the glossary at the bottom.

## Part 1 — The IRS rule (the source of truth)

### 1.1 Authority

The line 1i election is governed by the following Internal Revenue Service authorities:

- **2025 Instructions for Form 1040 and Form 1040-SR** (file `i1040gi_2025.pdf` in `docs/books/`), the section on the Earned Income Credit, page 108: *"If you elect to include nontaxable combat pay in figuring your EIC, enter the amount on Form 1040 or 1040-SR, line 1i."*
- **Internal Revenue Code (IRC) §32(c)(2)(B)(vi)** — the statutory election that nontaxable combat pay may be included in earned income for the Earned Income Credit at the taxpayer's option.
- **Internal Revenue Code (IRC) §24(d)(1)(B)** — the Additional Child Tax Credit (ACTC) earned-income formula, which also reads nontaxable combat pay as part of earned income.
- **IRS Publication 17 (Your Federal Income Tax for Individuals)** — chapter on the Earned Income Credit; reinforces that the election may *decrease* the credit for taxpayers in the phaseout range and recommends computing the credit both ways before deciding.
- **IRS Publication 590-A (Contributions to Individual Retirement Arrangements)** — paragraph titled "Nontaxable combat pay" — informs that the same combat pay counts as compensation for Individual Retirement Arrangement (IRA) contribution purposes. This is a *separate* provision from line 1i and is mentioned in this document for completeness only.

### 1.2 What line 1i is

Line 1i is the dollar amount of nontaxable combat pay that the filer *elects* to include in earned income for the purpose of computing two credits:

- The **Earned Income Credit (Form 1040 line 27a)**.
- The **Additional Child Tax Credit (Schedule 8812 Part II-A)**.

Line 1i is **credit-only**. The amount entered on line 1i:

- Does **not** make the combat pay taxable.
- Does **not** add to wages on lines 1a through 1h.
- Does **not** add to **line 1z** (total wages).
- Does **not** add to **Adjusted Gross Income (AGI)**, taxable income, or any other line.
- Is used **only** when downstream credit computations read earned income.

### 1.3 The election rules

The IRS imposes three structural rules on the election:

1. **Optional** — the taxpayer must affirmatively choose to make the election. There is no automatic inclusion.
2. **All-or-none, per person** — when the election is made, the filer must include the *entire* amount of nontaxable combat pay received. Partial amounts are not permitted.
3. **Spouse independence on a joint return** — on a Married Filing Jointly (MFJ) return, each spouse decides independently. All four combinations are valid (taxpayer yes + spouse yes; taxpayer yes + spouse no; taxpayer no + spouse yes; taxpayer no + spouse no).

The 2025 Instructions explicitly caution that the election may *decrease* the Earned Income Credit for taxpayers whose earned income is already in the credit's phaseout range. Taxpayers in that situation are advised to compute the credit with and without combat pay and choose the larger result.

### 1.4 What goes on line 1i (and what does not)

Line 1i is sourced from **only one** input: nontaxable combat pay reported on a Form W-2, **box 12, with code letter "Q"**.

These items do **not** belong on line 1i:

- Basic Allowance for Housing (BAH).
- Basic Allowance for Subsistence (BAS).
- Other tax-free military allowances reported on W-2 box 14 or elsewhere.
- Military retirement pay.
- Any other military earned-income items outside of W-2 box 12 code Q.

### 1.5 The equation per IRS rule

Define the following inputs (per person):

- `combatPayTotal_taxpayer` = sum of the W-2 box 12 amounts where the box-12 code letter is "Q" *and* the W-2 was issued to the taxpayer.
- `combatPayTotal_spouse` = sum of the W-2 box 12 amounts where the box-12 code letter is "Q" *and* the W-2 was issued to the spouse.
- `electCombatPay_taxpayer` = the taxpayer's Yes/No election (Boolean).
- `electCombatPay_spouse` = the spouse's Yes/No election (Boolean).

Then per the IRS rule:

```
electedCombatPay_taxpayer = electCombatPay_taxpayer ? combatPayTotal_taxpayer : 0
electedCombatPay_spouse   = electCombatPay_spouse   ? combatPayTotal_spouse   : 0

Form1040.Line1i = electedCombatPay_taxpayer + electedCombatPay_spouse
```

If neither spouse elects, line 1i is blank on the printed return (treated as zero for downstream computations).

### 1.6 Downstream consumers of line 1i

After line 1i is computed, two other tax-return computations read it:

#### Earned Income Credit (Form 1040 line 27a)

The Earned Income Credit is determined from the IRS Earned Income Credit Table. The table is looked up using earned income that *includes* the elected combat pay:

```
earnedIncomeForEarnedIncomeCredit = wagesAndSelfEmploymentEarnedIncome + Form1040.Line1i
```

The credit returned by the table is then compared against the credit computed using Adjusted Gross Income (AGI) at the phaseout threshold, and the *smaller* of the two values is the actual credit. Because combat pay increases the earned-income side of the comparison but does *not* increase the AGI side, the election can *decrease* the credit when earned income (without combat pay) is below the phaseout start but earned income (with combat pay) is past the phaseout start.

#### Additional Child Tax Credit (Schedule 8812 line 18a → line 20)

For the 2025 Additional Child Tax Credit, the earned-income piece of the formula is:

```
Schedule8812.Line18a = wagesAndSelfEmploymentEarnedIncome + Form1040.Line1i

If Schedule8812.Line18a is at or below $2,500:
    Schedule8812.Line19 = 0
    Schedule8812.Line20 = 0
Else:
    Schedule8812.Line19 = Schedule8812.Line18a − $2,500
    Schedule8812.Line20 = Schedule8812.Line19 × 15%   (rate per IRC §24(d)(1)(B)(ii))

Refundable credit cap per qualifying child = $1,700 for 2025.
```

Combat pay can only **increase** the result of this formula (the formula has no phaseout side that combat pay also drives), so the ACTC election is always non-harmful for filers below the per-child cap.

### 1.7 Out-of-scope related provision — Individual Retirement Arrangement contributions

The same nontaxable combat pay also counts as "compensation" for purposes of contributing to an Individual Retirement Arrangement (IRA), per IRS Publication 590-A. This is a statutory exception to the general rule that nontaxable income cannot support an IRA contribution. **This provision is unrelated to Form 1040 line 1i** — the IRA contribution limit reads the combat pay directly from the W-2, not from the line 1i election — and is documented here only to flag the planning consideration. The line 1i election does not trigger any IRA computation in this application.

### 1.8 Statement / filing behavior

The election is made *by populating line 1i* on Form 1040. No separate IRS election form is filed. The 2025 instructions do not require any "Nontaxable Combat Pay" (NCP) annotation written elsewhere on the return.

## Part 2 — The code implementation (what the application actually does)

### 2.1 Where the code lives

| Concern | Location |
|---|---|
| Compute method | `us-tax-be/src/main/java/com/ustax/microservices/TaxReturnComputeService.java`, method `computeCombatPay` |
| Code-set constant | Same file, `private static final Set<String> COMBAT_PAY_CODES = Set.of("Q");` |
| Per-person reader of box 12 code Q | Same file, helper `sumW2Box12ByCodes(w2Entries, codes, ssn)` |
| Final result holder | Same file, `private record CombatPayComputation(BigDecimal line1i)` |
| Output assembly | Same file, `buildIncome` calls `income.setNontaxableCombatPayElection(combatPay.line1i())` |
| Output model field | `us-tax-be/src/main/java/com/ustax/model/output/Income.java`, field `nontaxableCombatPayElection` (BigDecimal, nullable) |

### 2.2 Reference data constants used by the code

Line 1i itself has **no** reference-data constants — the computation is a Boolean-gated sum of inputs sourced from the W-2. The downstream consumers do use reference-data constants:

| Constant | 2025 value | Where used | Source |
|---|---|---|---|
| Earned-income floor for ACTC (`Schedule 8812 Part II-A line 19`) | `$2,500` | `computeSchedule8812` | Internal Revenue Code §24(d)(1)(B)(i) |
| ACTC formula rate | `15%` | `computeSchedule8812` | Internal Revenue Code §24(d)(1)(B)(ii) |
| Refundable ACTC cap per qualifying child | `$1,700` | `computeSchedule8812` | American Rescue Plan extension as adjusted for 2025 |

These constants belong to the Additional Child Tax Credit and the Earned Income Credit table lookups, not to line 1i itself, but they are relevant because they determine the downstream effect of the line 1i amount.

### 2.3 Step-by-step compute (as the code runs it)

The method `computeCombatPay(taxpayerData, spouseData, you, spouse, w2Entries, isMfsReturn, uid)` runs the following steps (with the source line numbers in the Java file in parentheses):

**Step 1 — Read Social Security Numbers (lines 14556–14561).**

- `taxpayerSsn` is read from the resolved `you` map's `ssn` field.
- `spouseSsn` is read from the resolved `spouse` map's `ssn` field, *unless* the filing status is Married Filing Separately (MFS), in which case `spouseSsn` is set to `null`. The Married Filing Separately guard prevents spouse-attributed W-2 amounts and a stale `combat-pay-spouse` form from a prior Married Filing Jointly year from leaking into the current taxpayer's line 1i.

**Step 2 — Sum W-2 box 12 code Q amounts per person (lines 14572–14577).**

- `taxpayerTotal = sumW2Box12ByCodes(w2Entries, {"Q"}, taxpayerSsn)` — but only if `taxpayerSsn` is non-blank. If the taxpayer's SSN is missing (incomplete onboarding), `taxpayerTotal` is set to `null`. This is a defensive guard: the helper `sumW2Box12ByCodes` treats a null SSN parameter as "no filter" (sum *all* code-Q entries on the return), which would falsely absorb code Q amounts that belong to nobody. The explicit null guard at this call site prevents that.
- `spouseTotal` follows the same logic, additionally guarded by Married Filing Separately (no spouse total on a separate return).

**Step 3 — Read the election Booleans (lines 14579–14580).**

- `electTaxpayer = taxpayerData.electCombatPay` — read from the personal form `combat-pay-taxpayer`.
- `electSpouse = spouseData.electCombatPay` — read from the personal form `combat-pay-spouse`, except on Married Filing Separately where the field is forced to `null`.

**Step 4 — Apply the all-or-none election (lines 14590–14595).**

- `taxpayerElected = (electTaxpayer == true) ? (taxpayerTotal ?? BigDecimal.ZERO) : null`
- `spouseElected = (electSpouse == true) ? (spouseTotal ?? BigDecimal.ZERO) : null`

The deliberate use of `BigDecimal.ZERO` when elected-but-no-W-2 distinguishes the user state "I elected but no code Q W-2 was found" (zero) from the user state "I did not elect" (null). The IRS rule is satisfied in both cases because zero is the correct election amount when no combat pay was received.

**Step 5 — Round and emit (lines 14606–14616).**

- `line1iRaw = addNonNull(taxpayerElected, spouseElected)` — `addNonNull` returns null only when both operands are null; otherwise it treats a null operand as zero.
- `line1i = roundMoney(line1iRaw)` — rounded to whole-dollar precision (BigDecimal scale 0).
- The result is wrapped in a `CombatPayComputation(line1i)` record and returned.

The orchestrator then calls `income.setNontaxableCombatPayElection(combatPay.line1i())` inside `buildIncome` to expose the value on the Form 1040 output payload.

### 2.4 Line 1z exclusion (explicit)

Line 1z on Form 1040 is the total of wage lines 1a through 1h. The code that computes line 1z does **not** include line 1i in that sum:

```
line1z = addNonNull(line1a, line1b, line1c, line1d, line1e, line1f, line1g, line1h)
```

This matches the IRS instruction that line 1i is a separate, credit-only field.

### 2.5 Downstream consumption — Earned Income Credit

Inside `computeLine27aEIC`, the earned-income input is built by adding `Form1040.Line1i` to the wage and self-employment earned income:

```
earnedIncomeForEarnedIncomeCredit = wagesAndSelfEmploymentEarnedIncome + income.nontaxableCombatPayElection
```

The Earned Income Credit lookup is then performed against this combined value. The smaller-of-two-comparisons rule (table credit using earned income vs. table credit using Adjusted Gross Income) is implemented inside the same method.

### 2.6 Downstream consumption — Additional Child Tax Credit

Inside `computeSchedule8812`, the Schedule 8812 Part II-A line 18a is built as:

```
Schedule8812.Line18a = income.totalWages + income.nontaxableCombatPayElection
```

The $2,500 floor and 15% rate are applied to produce line 20 (ACTC earned-income amount), which is then capped per qualifying child at $1,700 (2025 value).

### 3.4 Summary

For tax year 2025, the **line 1i compute is faithful to the IRS rule**. The amount written to Form 1040 line 1i for any combination of inputs (taxpayer election + spouse election + per-person W-2 box 12 code Q amounts) matches what the 2025 IRS instructions would produce.

Gap 1 — the user-decision support of computing the Earned Income Credit both ways — was the only remaining "should we do this?" item. It was closed on 2026-06-02 with an inline EIC comparison panel on both combat-pay forms. The remaining gaps (2 / 3 / 4) are low-severity downstream-credit polish items that do not affect line 1i correctness or the election decision.

## Glossary

- **ACTC** — Additional Child Tax Credit.
- **AGI** — Adjusted Gross Income.
- **BAH** — Basic Allowance for Housing (a tax-free military allowance, not on line 1i).
- **BAS** — Basic Allowance for Subsistence (a tax-free military allowance, not on line 1i).
- **EIC** — Earned Income Credit (sometimes called the Earned Income Tax Credit, EITC). Form 1040 line 27a.
- **IRA** — Individual Retirement Arrangement (a private retirement savings account). The IRA contribution rule for combat pay is mentioned in this document for completeness only; it is out of scope for line 1i.
- **IRC** — Internal Revenue Code (Title 26 of the United States Code).
- **MFJ** — Married Filing Jointly.
- **MFS** — Married Filing Separately.
- **NCP** — Nontaxable Combat Pay (the IRS shorthand; not required to be written on the 2025 return).
- **SSN** — Social Security Number.
