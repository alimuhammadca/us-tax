> Tax year: **2025**.

## Quick orientation

The Form 1040 label for line 23 is:

> **23. Other taxes, including self-employment tax, from Schedule 2, line 21**

This line is a **single pass-through transfer** from Schedule 2 (Additional Taxes). It carries a grand total of every "other tax" the taxpayer owes that is not the ordinary income tax (line 16), the Alternative Minimum Tax (folded into line 17), or the excess Premium Tax Credit repayment (also line 17). These "other taxes" include the self-employment tax, the 10% additional tax on early retirement distributions, the household employer's payroll tax, the 0.9% Additional Medicare Tax, the 3.8% Net Investment Income Tax, and roughly a dozen other niche items.

Two things to keep in mind throughout:

1. Line 23 has **no logic of its own**. All of the work happens on Schedule 2 Part II (lines 4 through 21). Line 23 is just the printed copy of Schedule 2 line 21.
2. The IRS 2025 Schedule 2 line 21 formula **does not include line 20** (the Section 965 net tax liability installment from Form 965-A). Line 20 is reported separately on Schedule 2 but is excluded from the printed sum on line 21.

## 1. The IRS-canonical computation (what the form really says to do)

### 1a. Equation for line 23

```
Form 1040 line 23 = Schedule 2 line 21
```

### 1b. Equation for Schedule 2 line 21 (per the printed 2025 form text)

The literal 2025 Schedule 2 instruction for line 21 says:

> **Add lines 4, 7 through 16, 18, and 19.**

So:

```
Schedule 2 line 21 = Schedule 2 line 4
                   + Schedule 2 line 7
                   + Schedule 2 line 8
                   + Schedule 2 line 9
                   + Schedule 2 line 10   (reserved for future use; treat as 0)
                   + Schedule 2 line 11
                   + Schedule 2 line 12
                   + Schedule 2 line 13
                   + Schedule 2 line 14
                   + Schedule 2 line 15
                   + Schedule 2 line 16
                   + Schedule 2 line 18
                   + Schedule 2 line 19
```

Notice four important details that are easy to get wrong:

| Detail | What it means |
|---|---|
| Lines 5 and 6 are **not** added directly into line 21 | Their sum is already captured on line 7, and only line 7 is added. Adding 5 and 6 again would double-count. |
| Line 10 is reserved | It is printed on the form but always zero in 2025. |
| Lines 17a through 17z (the "other additional tax" write-ins) are not added directly | Their sum is captured on line 18, and only line 18 is added. Adding 17a through 17z again would double-count. |
| Line 20 is excluded | Section 965 installment is a separate enforcement track and the 2025 form text deliberately omits it from line 21. |

## 2. The computation as actually implemented in the code

> File: `C:\us-tax\us-tax-be\src\main\java\com\ustax\microservices\TaxReturnComputeService.java`

The implementation splits the work across three writer methods, one finalizer method, and one downstream method. Each writer touches only its own Schedule 2 Part II sub-fields; the finalizer aggregates them; the downstream method reads the finalized total into Form 1040 line 23.

### 2a. Writer #1 — `applyAdditionalSocialSecurityMedicareTaxes()` (line 21225)

Called from `prepare()` around line 958. Reads three upstream computations and writes four Schedule 2 sub-fields:

```
input  tipTax              = TipComputation.totalTipTax()           // from Form 4137 path
input  uncollectedTax      = UncollectedComputation.line6Tax()      // from Form 8919 path
input  additionalMedicareTax = computeAdditionalMedicareTax(...)    // Form 8959 Part I only (initial estimate)

if tipTax              not null  →  Schedule2OtherTaxes.unreportedTipIncomeTax                       (line 5)
if uncollectedTax      not null  →  Schedule2OtherTaxes.uncollectedSocialSecurityMedicareTaxOnWages  (line 6)
if additionalMedicareTax not null →  Schedule2OtherTaxes.additionalMedicareTax                       (line 11)
line7Subtotal = nullSafeAdd(tipTax, uncollectedTax)
if line7Subtotal not null  →  Schedule2OtherTaxes.totalAdditionalSocialSecurityMedicareTax           (line 7)
```

The method does **not** touch `totalOtherTaxes` or `TaxAndCredits.otherTaxes`. Both are left for the finalizer.

### 2b. Writer #2 — Form 8959 line 18 override (after `buildForm8959()` at lines 963–969)

After the full Form 8959 (Parts I and III) is built, the Part-I-only Additional Medicare Tax estimate from writer #1 is **superseded** by the full Form 8959 line 18 total (Part I wages and tips plus Part III Railroad Retirement Tax Act compensation):

```
if form8959.line18TotalAdditionalMedicareTax not null
    Schedule2OtherTaxes.additionalMedicareTax = form8959.line18TotalAdditionalMedicareTax   (line 11, corrected)
```

This was the G3 fix on 2026-04-19; it ensures Railroad Retirement Tax Act compensation is included.

### 2c. Writer #3 — `applyForm5329TaxToSchedule2()` (line 21275)

Called from `prepare()` around line 1003. Reads the Form 5329 model produced by the pension/IRA path and writes one Schedule 2 sub-field:

```
input  iraTax = form5329.getAdditionalTaxOnEarlyDistributions()
if iraTax > 0  →  Schedule2OtherTaxes.additionalTaxOnIras   (line 8)
```

### 2d. Finalizer — `finalizeSchedule2OtherTaxes()` (line 21390)

Called from `prepare()` at line 2022, after all writers have finished and just **before** `computeLine20ThroughLine24()` at line 2024.

```
ot = schedule2.getOtherTaxes()
if ot is null  →  return                              // nothing to finalize

totalAdditionalTaxes = sum (treating null as zero)
                       of  ot.additionalTaxOnIras                            // line 8
                          , ot.householdEmploymentTaxes                       // line 9   (always null today)
                          , ot.additionalMedicareTax                          // line 11
                          , ot.netInvestmentIncomeTax                         // line 12  (always null today)
                          , ot.uncollectedSocialSecurityMedicareRrtaTax       // line 13  (always null today)
                          , ot.interestOnInstallmentSalesResidentialLots      // line 14  (always null today)
                          , ot.interestOnDeferredTaxInstallmentSalesOver150k  // line 15  (always null today)
                          , ot.recaptureLowIncomeHousingCredit                // line 16  (always null today)
if totalAdditionalTaxes > 0
    ot.totalAdditionalTaxes = round(totalAdditionalTaxes)                     // → Schedule 2 line 18

grandTotal = sum (treating null as zero)
             of  ot.selfEmploymentTax                              // line 4  (always null today)
                , ot.totalAdditionalSocialSecurityMedicareTax       // line 7
                , totalAdditionalTaxes                              // (the value just computed)
                , ot.recaptureNetEpeForm4255Line1dColL              // line 19 (always null today)

if grandTotal > 0
    ot.totalOtherTaxes = round(grandTotal)                          // → Schedule 2 line 21
    form1040.taxAndCredits.otherTaxes = round(grandTotal)           // → Form 1040 line 23
```

### 2e. Downstream consumer — `computeLine20ThroughLine24()` (line 25975)

Called from `prepare()` at line 2024. Reads `tac.otherTaxes` (already populated by the finalizer) and uses it for the line 24 total:

```
line23 = safeAmount(tac.getOtherTaxes())            // safeAmount turns null into 0
line24 = round(line22 + line23)
tac.setTotalTax(line24 > 0 ? line24 : 0)
```

That is the entire line 23 path in the code.

```

963   buildForm8959(); if not null, override line 11   → overwrites Schedule2OtherTaxes.additionalMedicareTax with Form 8959 line 18 total
```

## 4. Identified gaps and shortcomings

> **Update — 2026-06-09 (V58):** Gaps **G1, G3 (stub), G5, G6, G7, G8 (stub), G9, G10, G11** have been closed; details are in §8 below.
> **Update — 2026-06-09 (V59):** Remaining write-in / partial-field gaps have been closed: **Schedule 2 lines 17a, 17b, 17e–17j, 17l–17n, 17p, 17q** (stub intakes); **Schedule 2 line 17k** (auto-derived from W-2 box 12 code K); **Form 8960 lines 3, 5b, 5c, 6, 7, 9a, 9b, 9c, 10** (taxpayer-entered overrides). See §9 below.
> The only remaining open items are **G2 (Schedule SE)** and **G4 (Form 8959 Part II self-employment)**, both blocked by the explicit out-of-scope policy in `C:\us-tax\CLAUDE.md` for self-employment.

These are listed roughly in order of taxpayer-impact severity. Tax-impact severity is how often a real 2025 individual return would be wrong, and by how much, if the gap goes unaddressed.

### G1 — `totalAdditionalTaxes` (Schedule 2 line 18) is mis-populated

**Severity:** Medium (printed-PDF correctness, not totals correctness today).

The IRS Schedule 2 line 18 is defined as "Total additional taxes" = sum of lines 17a through 17z. In the code, `finalizeSchedule2OtherTaxes()` instead computes `totalAdditionalTaxes` as the sum of lines 8 + 9 + 11 + 12 + 13 + 14 + 15 + 16 and stores that into `Schedule2OtherTaxes.totalAdditionalTaxes` (the field the Schedule 2 PDF prints on line 18).

Two consequences:

1. The arithmetic line-21 grand total comes out **correct** today, because every Schedule 2 sub-line between 8 and 16 is added exactly once — just not under the IRS's structural layout. If 17a–17z ever start being populated, they will be **dropped** from the grand total (the finalizer never reads them), causing a real under-statement of line 23.
2. The Schedule 2 PDF will print a non-zero value on **line 18** that is actually the sum of lines 8 through 16. A reviewer reading the printed Schedule 2 will see a line 18 amount with no supporting 17a–17z detail, which looks wrong to an IRS reader.

**Fix:** Rename / restructure `finalizeSchedule2OtherTaxes()` so that:
- `totalAdditionalTaxes` (line 18) sums only 17a-through-17z fields, and
- `grandTotal` (line 21) sums line 4 + line 7 + line 8 + line 9 + line 11 + line 12 + line 13 + line 14 + line 15 + line 16 + line 18 + line 19 directly.

### G2 — Schedule SE (line 4) not computed

**Severity:** High in the general population (self-employed taxpayers); zero in this codebase's stated scope.

Self-employment tax is the largest single contributor to line 23 for any taxpayer who has net earnings from self-employment of $400 or more (per Form 1040 instructions Table 1-3 condition 3). The application is explicitly scoped to W-2 wage earners and does not have intake forms for Schedule C (Profit or Loss From Business), Schedule F (Profit or Loss From Farming), or Schedule SE. Any return with self-employment income produced by this application will be wrong on lines 4, 11 (Part II self-employment portion), and 23.

**Missing input field:** an `form-self-employment-*.xlsx` intake covering net earnings from self-employment, the church-employee threshold ($108.28), and the optional methods.

**Reference:** Schedule SE instructions; Publication 334 (Tax Guide for Small Business — present at `C:\us-tax\docs\books\p334.pdf`).

### G3 — Schedule H (line 9) not computed

**Severity:** High for household employers (nannies, housekeepers, caregivers) paying cash wages of $2,800 or more.

Form 1040 instructions Table 1-3 condition 1c explicitly calls out household employment taxes as a reason a taxpayer must file. There is no intake form (`form-household-employment-*.xlsx` is missing), no model class, no computation.

**Missing input fields:** household employee count, total cash wages paid, federal income tax withheld for the employee, state unemployment tax indicator.

**Reference:** Publication 926 (Household Employer's Tax Guide).

### G4 — Form 8959 Part II (self-employment portion) not computed

**Severity:** Medium when self-employment is in scope; zero today (gated by G2).

The Additional Medicare Tax on Schedule 2 line 11 includes three parts: wages and Railroad Retirement Tax Act compensation (Parts I and III, both implemented), and self-employment income (Part II, not implemented). When Schedule SE is brought into scope, Form 8959 Part II must also be implemented and line 11 recomputed.

### G5 — Form 8960 Net Investment Income Tax (line 12) not computed

**Severity:** Medium for high-income investors (modified AGI above $200,000 / $250,000 / $125,000 thresholds with material interest, dividends, capital gains, or passive income).

The 3.8% Net Investment Income Tax is currently dropped. Any return with substantial dividend, interest, or capital-gain income above the modified-AGI threshold will under-report line 23.

## 5. Validation rules the implementation already enforces (positive findings)

- `Form 1040 line 23 ≥ 0` — the finalizer only sets `tac.otherTaxes` when `grandTotal > 0`; downstream `safeAmount(...)` treats null as zero. Negative line 23 is impossible.
- `Schedule 2 line 7 = line 5 + line 6` — the line-7 subtotal is computed inside `applyAdditionalSocialSecurityMedicareTaxes()` from the same `tipTax` and `uncollectedTax` values that are written to lines 5 and 6, so the equality is structurally guaranteed.
- `Schedule 2 line 21 grand total never double-counts lines 5 and 6` — only `totalAdditionalSocialSecurityMedicareTax` (line 7) is added in the finalizer, never the components.
- `Schedule 2 line 21 grand total never double-counts lines 17a–17z` — vacuously, since none are written today.
- `Schedule 2 line 20 (Section 965) is excluded from line 21` — the finalizer does not read `section965NetTaxLiabilityInstallment`.
- `Form 1040 line 24 = line 22 + line 23` — `computeLine20ThroughLine24()` enforces this with `roundMoney(line22.add(line23))`.

## 7. Bottom line for line 23 correctness in this codebase today

For the **W-2-wage-earner population in scope**, line 23 is computed correctly when only the following are present: unreported tips (Form 4137), misclassified-employee wages (Form 8919), Form 5329 10% early-distribution penalty, and Form 8959 Additional Medicare Tax. The grand-total arithmetic flowing into `TaxAndCredits.otherTaxes` is right for those cases.

For any return outside that profile (self-employed, household employer, taxpayer above the Net Investment Income Tax threshold with investment income, taxpayer with Health Savings Account distributions that were not for qualified medical expenses, taxpayer with W-2 box 12 codes A/B/M/N, taxpayer with low-income-housing credit recapture, taxpayer with Form 4255 net Elective Payment Election recapture), line 23 will be **under-reported** because the contributing sub-items are dropped to zero.

The most impactful gap to close — apart from the out-of-scope self-employment work — is **G5 (Form 8960 Net Investment Income Tax)**, because it can affect any high-income investor in the wage-earner population the application already serves.

The most impactful **structural bug** to close is **G1**: the misuse of the `totalAdditionalTaxes` field. It does not affect the arithmetic of line 23 today (because lines 17a–17z are all zero), but it will silently start dropping items from line 23 the moment any 17-series field is wired up.
