## Quick orientation

The Form 1040 label for line 25a is:

> **25a. Federal income tax withheld from Form(s) W-2**

This line is one of three sub-lines (25a, 25b, 25c) whose sum becomes line 25d, which is the total federal income tax that was withheld from the taxpayer's pay during the year. Line 25d is then added with estimated tax payments (line 26) and other refundable credits (line 32) to produce the total payments on line 33. The IRS subtracts total payments from total tax (line 24) to determine whether the taxpayer owes more (line 37) or gets a refund (line 34).

Line 25a is the **only** sub-line that carries withholding originating from Form W-2 (the standard wage statement an employee receives from each employer). Withholding from any other source belongs on line 25b or 25c, not line 25a.

Three things to keep in mind throughout:

1. Line 25a has **no rate**, **no threshold**, and **no formula** beyond "add up the dollar amounts in box 2 of every Form W-2 on the return."
2. On a joint return, **both spouses' W-2 amounts** are combined onto the same line 25a.
3. Form W-2 box 2 is the same number whether the wage was income tax withheld at the federal-employee rate, the standard wage-bracket rate, or the percentage method — the IRS does not break out withholding by method on Form 1040.

## 1. The IRS-canonical computation (what the form really says to do)

### 1a. Equation for line 25a

```
Form 1040 line 25a = sum of (Form W-2 box 2 federal income tax withheld) across every Form W-2 on the return
                    + sum of (Form 4852 line 7e federal income tax withheld) across every Form 4852 (W-2 substitute) on the return
```

### 1b. Word version

For every Form W-2 the taxpayer (and the spouse on a joint return) received, find the amount in box 2 labeled "Federal income tax withheld." Add every such amount together. If the taxpayer is missing a Form W-2 and has prepared a substitute on Form 4852, also add the amount the taxpayer reported on Form 4852 line 7e. The total is Form 1040 line 25a.

### 1c. Where each input comes from

| Input | Per-row IRS source | Project intake form |
|---|---|---|
| W-2 box 2 federal income tax withheld | Form W-2 box 2 | `form-w-2.xlsx` (statement, multiple-per-return) — canonical field name `federalIncomeTaxWithheldAmount` |
| Form 4852 line 7e federal income tax withheld (W-2 substitute) | Form 4852 line 7e | `form-form4852.xlsx` (personal, single-per-return container, may hold multiple W-2-substitute entries) — canonical field name `line7eFederalIncomeTaxWithheld` per entry whose `substituteFormType` is `"w2"` |

### 1d. Reference data (2025 constants that drive line 25a)

**None.** Unlike most Form 1040 lines, line 25a has no tax-year-dependent constant — no rate, no threshold, no inflation-indexed amount. The IRS simply sums the dollar amounts the taxpayer's employers (or the taxpayer's own Form 4852 substitute) already withheld. The withholding amounts themselves embed the relevant 2025 wage-bracket and percentage-method tables, but those tables apply to the **payer** (the employer running payroll), not to the line-25a aggregation step.

### 1e. Output mapping

The Schedule 2-related PDF semantic name (canonical from `C:\us-tax\pdfs\f1040_field_mapping_semantic.csv`) for the line that produces line 25a is:

| Form 1040 line | PDF semantic field | Acroform leaf | Page / rectangle |
|---|---|---|---|
| **25a** | **`line25a_federal_income_tax_withheld_w2`** | **`f2_17[0]`** | **Page 2 / (410.4, 504, 481.65, 516)** |

The Java model carrier is:

| Concept | Java class / field |
|---|---|
| Form 1040 line 25a amount | `Payments.withholdingW2` (`getWithholdingW2()` / `setWithholdingW2(BigDecimal)`) — `com.ustax.model.output.Payments` |
| Form 1040 carrier | `Form1040.payments` of type `Payments` |
| Form 1040 line 25d amount (downstream consumer) | `Payments.totalWithholding` |

### 1f. Filing-status interaction

- **Single, Head of household, Qualifying surviving spouse, Married filing jointly:** Sum every Form W-2 belonging to the filer (and the spouse, on a joint return).
- **Married filing separately:** Sum only the Form W-2 entries belonging to the taxpayer who is filing this separate return. The spouse's Form W-2 withholding belongs on the spouse's own separate Form 1040 line 25a, **not** on this taxpayer's return.

The Form 4852 substitute follows the same rule: a Form 4852 the spouse prepared for their own missing Form W-2 belongs on the spouse's return on a Married-filing-separately filing, not on the taxpayer's return.

## 2. The computation as actually implemented in the code

> File: `C:\us-tax\us-tax-be\src\main\java\com\ustax\microservices\TaxReturnComputeService.java`

The implementation is concentrated inside one method (`computeLine31ThroughLine38`) and uses one helper (`sumFederalWithholdingFromEntries`) plus one Form-4852-specific helper (`extractForm4852Line25aWithholding`).

### 2a. Helper — `sumFederalWithholdingFromEntries(entries)` (line 27430)

```
input  entries  = a list of statement entry maps (each map represents one statement, like one Form W-2)
output total    = the sum of every entry's federalIncomeTaxWithheldAmount field, considering only positive amounts; null if the list is empty or all amounts are zero/null

for every entry:
    amount = parseAmount(entry.federalIncomeTaxWithheldAmount)
    if amount is not null and amount > 0:
        total = (total is null ? amount : total + amount)
return total
```

The helper is form-agnostic: it can sum any list of statement entries that follow the canonical W-2-mapper field name `federalIncomeTaxWithheldAmount`. The Java code reuses this helper for line 25a (W-2 entries), line 25b (1099-series entries), and line 25c (W-2-G entries).

### 2b. Form-4852-specific helper — `extractForm4852Line25aWithholding(form4852Data)` (line 24479)

```
input  form4852Data = the Form 4852 personal-form payload (taxpayer or spouse), or null
output total        = the sum of line7eFederalIncomeTaxWithheld across every Form 4852 entry whose substituteFormType is "w2"; null if the form is not active or there are no W-2 substitute entries

if form4852Data is null OR form4852Data.needsForm4852 is not TRUE:
    return null
for every entry in form4852Data.entries:
    substituteFormType = normalize(entry.substituteFormType, default-fallback-by-line-8-presence)
    if substituteFormType is not "w2":
        skip
    total = addNonNull(total, parseAmount(entry.line7eFederalIncomeTaxWithheld))
return total
```

The default-fallback (when `substituteFormType` is missing) treats a row that has any line-8 (1099-R substitute) input as a 1099-R; otherwise it defaults to W-2. This is the "absence of explicit type → infer from filled fields" rule.

### 2c. Composer — line 25a section of `computeLine31ThroughLine38` (line 26613)

```
input  w2Entries              = list of W-2 statement entries belonging to this return
input  form4852TaxpayerData   = taxpayer's Form 4852 personal-form payload, or null
input  form4852SpouseData     = spouse's Form 4852 personal-form payload, or null
input  filingStatusData       = filing-status personal-form payload
output Payments.withholdingW2 = the line 25a amount written to the Form 1040 model

withholdingW2 = sumFederalWithholdingFromEntries(w2Entries)
withholdingW2 = addNonNull(withholdingW2, extractForm4852Line25aWithholding(form4852TaxpayerData))
isMfsForForm4852 = (filingStatusData.filingStatus == "Married filing separately")
withholdingW2 = addNonNull(withholdingW2,
                            isMfsForForm4852 ? null : extractForm4852Line25aWithholding(form4852SpouseData))
payments.setWithholdingW2(withholdingW2)
```

Three things to notice in the composer:

1. **`w2Entries` is not filtered by Social Security Number.** It is the full list of every Form W-2 saved on this return. On a Married-filing-jointly return both spouses' W-2 entries appear in `w2Entries`; both contribute. On a Married-filing-separately return, the data layer separates each spouse's statements at storage time (each spouse has their own user identity), so `w2Entries` for the filing taxpayer's return already excludes the spouse's W-2 entries.
2. **Form 4852 taxpayer-side withholding is always added.** No filing-status guard.
3. **Form 4852 spouse-side withholding is gated by Married-filing-separately.** On MFS the spouse files a separate return; their Form 4852 substitute withholding belongs on their own line 25a.

### 2d. Downstream consumer — line 25d total (line 26654)

```
totalWithholding = round(safeAmount(withholdingW2) + safeAmount(withholding1099) + safeAmount(withholdingOther))
payments.setTotalWithholding(totalWithholding > 0 ? totalWithholding : null)
```

Line 25d is the simple sum of all three withholding sub-lines, rounded to whole dollars, and stored as null when the total is zero (Java null-when-zero canonical convention).

### 2e. Compute order (relevant call sites in `prepare()`)

```
~200    statementDataService.listEntriesWithData(uid, "w-2")   → builds w2Entries
~255    personalForms.get("form4852-taxpayer")                  → form4852TaxpayerData
~258    personalForms.get("form4852-spouse")                    → form4852SpouseData
~3400   computeLine31ThroughLine38(...)                         → writes Payments.withholdingW2
```

### 2f. Frontend display

Line 25a is displayed on the Form 1040 PDF preview at the Acroform field `f2_17[0]` (page 2, line 25a) via the semantic name `line25a_federal_income_tax_withheld_w2`. The frontend reads `form.payments?.withholdingW2` from the compute response and writes the formatted amount into that field. There is no client-side recomputation — the backend value is the source of truth.

## 4. Identified gaps and shortcomings

These are listed roughly in order of taxpayer-impact severity. None are blocking for the wage-earner population in scope.

### G1 — Negative or zero W-2 box 2 amounts are silently discarded

**Severity:** Cosmetic.

`sumFederalWithholdingFromEntries` adds an entry only if the parsed amount is `> 0`. A negative box 2 amount would indicate corrupt data and is appropriately skipped, but a zero box 2 is also skipped, which is arguably correct for an aggregation that produces null when the total is zero. There is no observable taxpayer-impact difference, but the code's intent could be clearer: the IRS rule is "treat blanks and zeros the same as missing." The current code does treat them that way.

**Fix:** None needed; this is an intent-clarity note, not a defect.

### G2 — Line 25a sub-total is not rounded to whole dollars before being stored

**Severity:** Low.

The composer (`Payments.withholdingW2 = ...`) stores the raw `BigDecimal` sum without an explicit `roundMoney(...)` wrap. The downstream line 25d total does call `roundMoney`. In practice the W-2 box 2 amounts are stored as integer dollars (the W-2 mapper writes `BigDecimal("123")` style values from the `inputnumber` field), so rounding at the sub-line is a no-op today. The minor risk: if a future statement mapper writes sub-dollar precision (say, from an automated W-2 import that preserves cents), the line 25a value could end up displayed as `12345.67` while line 25d is `12346` (rounded). The PDF render would handle this by formatting, but the JSON payload would look inconsistent.

**Fix (1-line):** Add `withholdingW2 = roundMoney(withholdingW2);` before `payments.setWithholdingW2(withholdingW2)` at `TaxReturnComputeService.java:26621`.

### G3 — Zero line 25a is stored as zero, not as null

**Severity:** Low (canonical-null-zero deviation).

The project's `canonical_null_zero_semantic` rule says a BigDecimal field should be **null** when the concept does not apply (no W-2 entries on the return at all) and **zero** when the concept applies but the value sums to zero (e.g., the taxpayer's only Form W-2 has $0 in box 2 — a child summer-camp counselor whose payroll had no federal withholding). The current implementation correctly returns null when `w2Entries` is empty (the `sumFederalWithholdingFromEntries` helper returns null if the input list is empty or has no positive amounts) — so this is actually the same null behavior in both cases, not a deviation. **Not a real gap on re-review.**

**Fix:** None needed; the `null OR concept-applies-but-zero` distinction is the convention's escape hatch but for line 25a both cases collapse to null because the helper short-circuits.

### G4 — No explicit handling of Form W-2c (corrected W-2)

**Severity:** Low (uncommon).

The project's W-2 statement intake has no `wasCorrected` flag or replaced-by-W2c indicator. If a taxpayer receives a corrected W-2c partway through the year, the application expects the user to update the original W-2 entry with the corrected box 2 value, not to add a second W-2 entry. If a user mistakenly enters both the original and the corrected W-2 as separate entries, line 25a would double-count.

**Fix:** Long term, add a `priorW2WasCorrected` flag or a `replacedByCorrectedW2cId` link to the W-2 statement schema. Short term, this is a user-education issue and not a hidden defect — the symptom is visible in the line 25a total.

### G5 — Form 4852 `substituteFormType` inference fallback can misclassify edge cases

**Severity:** Low.

When the user has not explicitly set `substituteFormType` on a Form 4852 row and the row also has no line-8 (1099-R) inputs, the code defaults to `"w2"`. This is correct behavior 99% of the time. The remaining 1% — a partially-filled 1099-R substitute with neither `substituteFormType` set nor any line-8 input yet — would be incorrectly summed into line 25a as W-2 withholding. The risk is bounded by the save-vs-compute gating in the Form 4852 UI, which prompts the user to choose a substitute type before saving a complete row.

**Fix:** Make `substituteFormType` a required field at save time; remove the inference fallback. Defer until a defect is observed; today the fallback hides only rare edge cases.

For every taxpayer in the project's W-2-wage-earner scope, line 25a is computed correctly. ## 9. §8 update — G6 (newly identified) closure

A critical second-pass re-review of the Form 4852 path uncovered **one previously-unidentified gap (G6)** that the §8 closure had missed.

### 9a. G6 (FIXED) — `extractForm4852Line25aWithholding` does not filter non-positive line 7e values

- **Discovery:** While auditing the §8 closure for completeness, comparing the W-2 path (`sumFederalWithholdingFromEntries` at `TaxReturnComputeService.java:27430`, which has `if (withheld > 0)` filter) against the Form 4852 path (`extractForm4852Line25aWithholding` at line 24588, which did `addNonNull(total, parseAmount(...))` with no positive-value filter) revealed an inconsistency. A Form 4852 entry whose `line7eFederalIncomeTaxWithheld` is null/zero/negative would either silently sum a zero (no observable effect) or subtract from the running total (silent line-25a under-statement).
- **IRS rule:** Form 4852 line 7e is "Federal income tax withheld" — by definition a non-negative quantity. The 2025 Form 4852 instructions describe it as the amount the employer would have shown in box 2 of the missing W-2, which is always ≥ 0. A negative entry is corrupt data; the taxpayer-impact-correct treatment is to discard it, matching the W-2 path.
