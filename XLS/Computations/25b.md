# Form 1040 (2025) — Line 25b: Federal income tax withheld from Form(s) 1099

> Plain-English specification of the Internal Revenue Service (IRS) canonical computation for Form 1040 line 25b, followed by the as-implemented computation in this codebase, with a gap analysis at the end.
>
> Tax year: **2025**.
>
> Sources consulted: 2025 Form 1040; the 2025 Instructions for Form 1040 (`C:\us-tax\docs\books\i1040gi_2025.txt`); Publication 17 (`C:\us-tax\docs\books\p17.pdf`); Publication 575 (Pension and Annuity Income); Publication 590-A and 590-B (Individual Retirement Arrangements); the 2025 Form W-2 + W-2 box instructions; the 2025 Form 4852 (Substitute for Form W-2 or Form 1099-R) instructions; plus the project's own `lines\25abcd.md`, `dependencies\25abcd.md`, and `knowledge\line-25abcd-federal-withholding.md`.

## Quick orientation

The Form 1040 label for line 25b is:

> **25b. Federal income tax withheld from Form(s) 1099**

This is the second of three sub-lines (25a, 25b, 25c) whose sum becomes line 25d, the total federal income tax that was withheld from the taxpayer's payments during the year. Line 25d is then added with estimated tax payments (line 26) and other refundable credits (line 32) to produce the total payments on line 33. The IRS subtracts total payments from total tax (line 24) to determine whether the taxpayer owes more (line 37) or gets a refund (line 34).

Line 25b is the **only** sub-line that carries withholding originating from the Form 1099 series (the standard information statement an income payer issues for non-wage payments) and from two railroad / social-security forms (Form SSA-1099 and Form RRB-1099). Withholding from a Form W-2 belongs on line 25a; withholding from a Form W-2G or from Form 8959 Part V belongs on line 25c.

Three things to keep in mind throughout:

1. Line 25b has **no rate**, **no threshold**, and **no formula** beyond "add up every federal income tax withheld amount on every Form 1099 (and the railroad / social-security forms that the IRS routes through 25b instead of 25c) on the return."
2. On a joint return, **both spouses' Form 1099 amounts** are combined onto the same line 25b.
3. The 2025 IRS instruction text routes **backup withholding** (a 1099 box that fills only when the payee failed to furnish a Taxpayer Identification Number under Internal Revenue Code §3406) onto line 25b the same as ordinary withholding — see the i1040gi-2025 text at lines 4369–4375 ("If you were subject to backup withholding on income you received during 2025, include the amount withheld, as shown on your Form 1099, in the total on line 25b of Form 1040 or 1040-SR.").

## 1. The IRS-canonical computation (what the form really says to do)

### 1a. Equation for line 25b

```
Form 1040 line 25b = sum of (every Form 1099 federal income tax withheld box on the return)
                    + sum of (Form SSA-1099 box 6 voluntary federal income tax withheld)
                    + sum of (Form RRB-1099 box 10 federal income tax withheld)
                    + sum of (Form RRB-1099-R box 9 federal income tax withheld)
                    + sum of (Form 4852 line 8f federal income tax withheld, when the Form 4852 entry is a 1099-R substitute)
```

In a single phrase: **"add every withholding box on every 1099-style information statement on the return."**

### 1b. Word version

For every Form 1099 the taxpayer (and the spouse on a joint return) received, find the federal income tax withheld box. Different 1099 forms put it in different boxes:

- Form 1099-R box 4 (the most common one — pension and retirement distributions)
- Form 1099-INT box 4 (interest)
- Form 1099-DIV box 4 (dividends)
- Form 1099-B box 4 (broker proceeds)
- Form 1099-OID box 4 (Original Issue Discount)
- Form 1099-G box 4 (state refunds, unemployment compensation, and other government payments)
- Form 1099-NEC box 4 (nonemployee compensation backup withholding)
- Form 1099-MISC box 4 (miscellaneous payments backup withholding)
- Form 1099-K box 4 (payment card / third-party network backup withholding)
- Form 1099-DA box 2a (Digital Asset proceeds — NEW for 2025; the IRS placed the withholding box at 2a, not 4)
- Form 1099-PATR box 4 (taxable distributions received from cooperatives, backup withholding)
- Form SSA-1099 box 6 (Social Security Benefit Statement, voluntary withholding)
- Form RRB-1099 box 10 (Railroad Retirement Board social security equivalent, federal income tax withheld)
- Form RRB-1099-R box 9 (Railroad Retirement Board non-social-security tier 1, federal income tax withheld)
- Form 4852 line 8f (Substitute for Form 1099-R federal income tax withheld — only when the Form 4852 entry is a 1099-R substitute, not a W-2 substitute)

Add every such amount together. The total is Form 1040 line 25b.

### 1d. Reference data (2025 constants that drive line 25b)

**None.** Line 25b has no tax-year-dependent constant — no rate, no threshold, no inflation-indexed amount. The IRS simply sums the dollar amounts the taxpayer's payers already withheld. The withholding amounts themselves embed the relevant 2025 backup-withholding rate (24% per Internal Revenue Code §3406(a)(1)) and any voluntary-withholding rates the taxpayer elected, but those rates apply to the **payer** (the entity running the payment), not to the line-25b aggregation step.

For context (constants that affect the AMOUNT in each box at the payer level, NOT the line-25b sum itself):

| Constant | Value (2025) | Source |
|---|---|---|
| Backup withholding rate | 24% | Internal Revenue Code §3406(a)(1) — applied by the payer when the payee fails to furnish a Taxpayer Identification Number |
| SSA-1099 voluntary withholding rates | 7%, 10%, 12%, or 22% | Internal Revenue Code §3402(p)(1)(B); chosen on Form W-4V (Voluntary Withholding Request) |
| 1099-R default pension withholding | 10% on periodic; 20% on eligible rollover distributions | Internal Revenue Code §3405 |

The aggregation step on Form 1040 line 25b does NOT recompute these — it accepts the dollar amounts the payer printed on each form.

### 1f. Filing-status interaction

- **Single, Head of household, Qualifying surviving spouse, Married filing jointly:** Sum every Form 1099-series entry belonging to the filer (and the spouse, on a joint return).
- **Married filing separately:** Sum only the Form 1099-series entries belonging to the taxpayer who is filing this separate return. The spouse's Form 1099 withholding belongs on the spouse's own separate Form 1040 line 25b, **not** on this taxpayer's return.

The Form 4852 substitute follows the same rule: a Form 4852 the spouse prepared for their own missing Form 1099-R belongs on the spouse's return on a Married-filing-separately filing, not on the taxpayer's return.

## 2. The computation as actually implemented in the code

> File: `C:\us-tax\us-tax-be\src\main\java\com\ustax\microservices\TaxReturnComputeService.java`

The implementation is concentrated inside one method (`computeLine31ThroughLine38`) and uses three helpers (`sumFederalWithholdingFromMultipleLists`, `sumSsa1099Withholding`, `extractForm4852Line25bWithholding`).

### 2a. Helper — `sumFederalWithholdingFromEntries(entries)` (line 27550)

```
input  entries  = a list of statement entry maps (each map represents one statement, like one Form 1099-R)
output total    = the sum of every entry's federalIncomeTaxWithheldAmount field, considering only positive amounts; null if the list is empty or all amounts are zero or null

for every entry:
    amount = parseAmount(entry.federalIncomeTaxWithheldAmount)
    if amount is not null and amount > 0:
        total = (total is null ? amount : total + amount)
return total
```

This is the same helper that line 25a uses for Form W-2 summation; it is form-agnostic and reads the canonical field name `federalIncomeTaxWithheldAmount` from each entry. This is why **every 1099-series mapper must expose its withholding field under that exact key** for the aggregation to include it. (See Gap A — 1099-K deviates.)

### 2b. Helper — `sumSsa1099Withholding(entries)` (line 27570)

```
input  entries  = a list of SSA-1099 entry maps
output total    = the sum of every entry's voluntaryFederalIncomeTaxWithheldAmount field, considering only positive amounts; null if no contribution

for every entry:
    amount = parseAmount(entry.voluntaryFederalIncomeTaxWithheldAmount)
    if amount is not null and amount > 0:
        total = addNonNull(total, amount)
return total
```

This is a dedicated reader because the SSA-1099 frontend stores box 6 under `voluntaryFederalIncomeTaxWithheldAmount` (the wording matches "voluntary federal income tax withheld" on the form itself), not under the standard `federalIncomeTaxWithheldAmount` that the generic helper reads.

### 2c. Variadic wrapper — `sumFederalWithholdingFromMultipleLists(lists...)` (line 27587)

```
input  lists = a varargs of lists; each list is the entries for one statement form type
output total = the sum of sumFederalWithholdingFromEntries(list) across every list, null when no contribution

total = null
for each list:
    total = addNonNull(total, sumFederalWithholdingFromEntries(list))
return total
```

This wrapper exists only so the composer can pass a single line of code rather than chain a dozen `addNonNull(...)` calls. It delegates entirely to `sumFederalWithholdingFromEntries`.

### 2d. Form-4852-specific helper — `extractForm4852Line25bWithholding(form4852Data)` (line 24621)

```
input  form4852Data = the Form 4852 personal-form payload (taxpayer or spouse), or null
output total        = the sum of line8fFederalIncomeTaxWithheld across every Form 4852 entry whose substituteFormType is "1099-r"; null when there is no contribution

if form4852Data is null OR form4852Data.needsForm4852 is not TRUE:
    return null
for every entry in form4852Data.entries:
    substituteFormType = normalize(entry.substituteFormType, default-fallback-by-line-8-presence)
    if substituteFormType is not "1099_r":
        skip
    total = addNonNull(total, parseAmount(entry.line8fFederalIncomeTaxWithheld))
return total
```

Note: the helper does NOT have the "positive amounts only" filter that the line-25a Form 4852 helper got in the §9 G6 closure documented in `25a.md`. A negative line 8f value would silently subtract from line 25b. (See Gap C.)

### 2e. Composer — line 25b section of `computeLine31ThroughLine38` (line 26743)

```
input  form1099REntries, formRrb1099REntries, form1099IntEntries, form1099DivEntries,
       form1099BEntries, form1099OidEntries, form1099GEntries, form1099NecEntries,
       form1099KEntries, form1099MiscEntries, formRrb1099Entries, form1099DaEntries  = entry lists
input  formSsa1099Entries  = entry list (handled specially)
input  form4852TaxpayerData, form4852SpouseData  = Form 4852 payloads
input  isMfsForForm4852    = filing status flag

withholding1099 = sumFederalWithholdingFromMultipleLists(
        form1099REntries, formRrb1099REntries,
        form1099IntEntries, form1099DivEntries,
        form1099BEntries, form1099OidEntries,
        form1099GEntries, form1099NecEntries, form1099KEntries,
        form1099MiscEntries, formRrb1099Entries, form1099DaEntries)
withholding1099 = addNonNull(withholding1099, sumSsa1099Withholding(formSsa1099Entries))
withholding1099 = addNonNull(withholding1099, extractForm4852Line25bWithholding(form4852TaxpayerData))
withholding1099 = addNonNull(withholding1099,
                              isMfsForForm4852 ? null : extractForm4852Line25bWithholding(form4852SpouseData))
payments.setWithholding1099(withholding1099)
```

Six things to notice in the composer:

1. **`form1099PtrEntries` is NOT in the variadic call.** The 1099-PATR statement entries are never loaded into compute and therefore never reach line 25b. (See Gap B.)
2. **`form1099KEntries` IS in the variadic call**, but the 1099-K mapper exposes its box 4 withholding as `federalIncomeTaxWithheld4Amount`, not the canonical `federalIncomeTaxWithheldAmount`. `sumFederalWithholdingFromEntries` reads only the canonical key and so reads null from every 1099-K entry. (See Gap A.)
3. **No filtering by Social Security Number.** On a Married-filing-jointly return both spouses' 1099 entries appear in the lists; both contribute. On a Married-filing-separately return, the data layer separates each spouse's statements at storage time (each spouse has their own user identity), so the lists for the filing taxpayer's return already exclude the spouse's 1099 entries.
4. **Form 4852 taxpayer-side withholding is always added.** No filing-status guard.
5. **Form 4852 spouse-side withholding is gated by Married-filing-separately.** On Married-filing-separately the spouse files a separate return; their Form 4852 substitute withholding belongs on their own line 25b.
6. **The line-25b sub-total is NOT rounded to whole dollars before being stored.** Only the line 25d total is `roundMoney`-ed. Line 25a was rounded as part of the §8b G2 closure but the parallel change was not applied here. (See Gap D.)

### 2f. Downstream consumer — line 25d total (line 26654)

```
totalWithholding = round(safeAmount(withholdingW2) + safeAmount(withholding1099) + safeAmount(withholdingOther))
payments.setTotalWithholding(totalWithholding > 0 ? totalWithholding : null)
```

Line 25d is the simple sum of all three withholding sub-lines, rounded to whole dollars, and stored as null when the total is zero (Java null-when-zero canonical convention).

## 4. Identified gaps and shortcomings

Listed in order of taxpayer-impact severity. Two are real bugs that under-state line 25b for affected taxpayers; the remaining items are consistency parity gaps with the line-25a path.

### Gap A — 1099-K box 4 backup withholding is silently dropped from line 25b

**Severity:** HIGH (bug — under-states the credit position for any taxpayer with 1099-K backup withholding).

**What the IRS rule says.** Form 1099-K box 4 is "Federal income tax withheld" — backup withholding the payment-card or third-party-network payer applied under Internal Revenue Code §3406 when the recipient failed to furnish a Taxpayer Identification Number. The 2025 Instructions for Form 1040 (`i1040gi_2025.txt`, lines 4369–4375) say: "include the amount withheld, as shown on your Form 1099, in the total on line 25b of Form 1040 or 1040-SR."

**What the code does.** `Form1099KMapper.loadOne(...)` at `Form1099KMapper.java:155` reads `federalIncomeTaxWithheld4Amount` from the payload and at `Form1099KMapper.java:244` emits the same key `federalIncomeTaxWithheld4Amount` in the response. The composer at `TaxReturnComputeService.java:26749-26754` passes `form1099KEntries` into `sumFederalWithholdingFromMultipleLists`, which calls `sumFederalWithholdingFromEntries`, which reads only `federalIncomeTaxWithheldAmount` (canonical) and reads null from every 1099-K entry. **Result: every 1099-K box 4 amount is silently dropped from line 25b.**

**Why it happened.** The 1099-K mapper uses field names that match the IRS field labels with box numbers in the names (`box1aGrossAmount`, `federalIncomeTaxWithheld4Amount`, etc.) rather than the project's canonical-by-concept convention. The composer and helper were written assuming the canonical convention.

**Fix (mapper-output alias, same pattern as the 2026-06-10 1099-DA fix at `Form1099DaMapper.java:248-256`):** add a single line to `Form1099KMapper.loadOne(...)` immediately after the existing `federalIncomeTaxWithheld4Amount` output:
```java
if (row.federalIncomeTaxWithheld4Amount != null) {
    out.put("federalIncomeTaxWithheldAmount", row.federalIncomeTaxWithheld4Amount);
}
```
This exposes the same value under the canonical key the generic helper reads. No mapper-input change, no DB migration, no UI change.

### Gap B — 1099-PATR box 4 backup withholding is entirely excluded from line 25b

**Severity:** HIGH (bug — under-states the credit position for any taxpayer with 1099-PATR backup withholding).

**What the IRS rule says.** Form 1099-PATR (Taxable Distributions Received From Cooperatives) box 4 is "Federal income tax withheld" — backup withholding the cooperative applied under Internal Revenue Code §3406. The 2025 Instructions for Form 1040 (general 1099 rule, `i1040gi_2025.txt` line 4374): "include the amount withheld, as shown on your Form 1099, in the total on line 25b."

**What the code does.** `Form1099PatrMapper` exists and exposes the canonical `federalIncomeTaxWithheldAmount` output key. `StatementSsnRule` for `"1099-ptr"` exists at `TaxReturnComputeService.java:4416`. But there is **no `statementDataService.listEntriesWithData(uid, "1099-ptr")` call anywhere in `prepare()`**, so `form1099PtrEntries` (or whatever the variable would be named) does not exist, and the composer's variadic call does not include 1099-PATR entries.

**Result: every 1099-PATR box 4 amount is excluded from line 25b** — even if the user has entered 1099-PATR statements via the UI, that data never reaches the line-25b computation.

**Why it happened.** Form 1099-PATR is the only 1099 form whose statement-loading and composer wiring was overlooked when the 1099-series suite was wired up. The mapper, SSN rule, and frontend exist; only the load-and-pass-to-composer step is missing.

**Fix:** in `prepare()` near the other 1099 listEntriesWithData calls, add:
```java
List<Map<String, Object>> form1099PtrEntries =
        statementDataService.listEntriesWithData(uid, "1099-ptr");
```
Then add `form1099PtrEntries` to the variadic list at `TaxReturnComputeService.java:26749-26754`.

### Gap C — `extractForm4852Line25bWithholding` lacks the positive-value filter

**Severity:** Low (defensive — would only matter on corrupt data).

**What the IRS rule says.** Form 4852 line 8f is "Federal income tax withheld" — by definition a non-negative quantity. A negative entry indicates corrupt data.

**What the code does.** At `TaxReturnComputeService.java:24634`, the helper uses `addNonNull(total, parseAmount(entry.get("line8fFederalIncomeTaxWithheld")))` — directly summing whatever value the user entered. A negative line 8f value would silently subtract from line 25b, under-stating the credit position. The line-25a Form 4852 helper got a positive-filter as part of the `25a.md` §9 G6 closure on 2026-06-10; the parallel change was not applied to the line-25b path.

**Fix (one-line, mirrors the §9 G6 closure):**
```java
BigDecimal withheld = parseAmount(entry.get("line8fFederalIncomeTaxWithheld"));
if (withheld != null && withheld.compareTo(BigDecimal.ZERO) > 0) {
    total = addNonNull(total, withheld);
}
```

### Gap D — Line 25b sub-total is not rounded to whole dollars before being stored

**Severity:** Low (cosmetic / future-proofing).

**What the IRS rule says.** The form prints whole-dollar amounts; the IRS accepts either whole-dollar or actual-cent reporting on each line.

**What the code does.** The composer at `TaxReturnComputeService.java:26749-26759` stores the raw `BigDecimal` sum without an explicit `roundMoney(...)` wrap. The downstream line 25d total at line 26654 DOES call `roundMoney`. In practice every 1099-series mapper writes whole-dollar BigDecimal values from `inputnumber` UI fields, so rounding at the sub-line is a no-op today. The minor risk: if a future statement mapper writes sub-dollar precision (for example, from an automated statement import that preserves cents), the line 25b value could end up displayed as `12345.67` while line 25d is `12346` (rounded). The PDF render would handle this by formatting, but the JavaScript Object Notation (JSON) payload would look inconsistent.

The line-25a path got `roundMoney` as part of the §8b G2 closure on 2026-06-10; the parallel change was not applied to the line-25b path.

**Fix (one-line, mirrors the §8b G2 closure at `TaxReturnComputeService.java:26740`):** insert
```java
withholding1099 = withholding1099 == null ? null : roundMoney(withholding1099);
```
immediately before `payments.setWithholding1099(withholding1099);` at line 26759.

## 7. Bottom line for line 25b correctness in this codebase today

Line 25b is **mostly correct, with two real bugs that under-state the credit for affected taxpayers**:

- **A taxpayer with 1099-K box 4 backup withholding** will see line 25b under-stated by the box 4 amount (the project intake captures the value but the compute path silently drops it). Probably rare in practice but not zero.
- **A taxpayer with 1099-PATR box 4 backup withholding** will see line 25b under-stated by the box 4 amount (the form never reaches compute at all). Affected taxpayer population: anyone receiving taxable cooperative distributions AND failing to furnish a Taxpayer Identification Number to the cooperative. Even rarer than the 1099-K case but the magnitude is unbounded.

They are not user-observable today because the underlying values are always whole-dollar non-negative entries from the inputnumber UI fields. They should be closed for code-consistency reasons.

The four-form OOS bucket (K-1, 1042-S, 8805, 8288-A) belongs on line 25c per the IRS rule, not line 25b — so it does not affect line-25b correctness.

### 9h. Lessons captured

Two general patterns surfaced during this audit that recur across the 25-line cluster:

1. **Mapper-output-key drift.** When a mapper's storage column / Java field name embeds the box number (`box4`, `federalIncomeTaxWithheld4Amount`), the generic helpers — written against the by-concept canonical name (`federalIncomeTaxWithheldAmount`) — silently fail. Two instances now: 1099-DA (closed 2026-06-10 in the line-25a session), 1099-K (closed in this session). Future audit: grep every statement mapper's `loadOne` output and confirm the canonical key is exposed.

2. **Compute-wiring drift.** A statement form can pass through every other registry (`StatementFormCatalog`, `UserDataBulkDelete.PARENT_TABLES_UID_CASCADE`, the mapper, the entity, the frontend component) and still be silently excluded from compute because the `prepare()` step never calls `listEntriesWithData(...)` for it. 1099-PATR was such a case for line 25b. Future audit: cross-check every 1099-series form in `StatementFormCatalog` against the list of `prepare()` loads.

Both lessons follow the same principle: **a complete cross-registry inventory beats a single-file read** when a statement form has multiple registration points. The Java unit + e2e pair caught both issues without trial-and-error.

### 10h. Lessons captured from §10

The same diagnostic shortcut that surfaced 25a.md Gap G also surfaced 25b.md Gaps F, G, H: **after the first-pass closure list is written, re-read the composer code and ask "what composer paths have logic but no test?"**

Specifically:
- Read every conditional / ternary in the composer for the line being audited.
- For each one, identify which test in the end-to-end suite exercises the true branch and which exercises the false branch.
- A composer path with both branches untested is a real coverage gap regardless of whether it was enumerated in §11.

For line 25b, the composer at `TaxReturnComputeService.java:26756-26758` has three structurally distinct contributions:
1. Taxpayer Form 4852 line 8f (unconditional add) — needed a positive-flow test.
2. Spouse Form 4852 line 8f gated by Married-filing-separately (ternary, true branch).
3. Spouse Form 4852 line 8f else-branch on Married-filing-jointly.

The first-pass closure focused on the bug-level mapper / wiring gaps and did not enumerate these three composer-path branches. The second pass enumerated them explicitly and added one end-to-end test per branch.

**Future audit shortcut:** Before declaring a line audit complete, grep the composer for conditional expressions and verify each branch has at least one end-to-end test that takes it.
