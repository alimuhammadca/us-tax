## Quick orientation

The Form 1040 label for line 25c is:

> **25c. Other forms (see instructions)**

This is the third of three sub-lines (25a, 25b, 25c) whose sum becomes line 25d, the total federal income tax that was withheld from the taxpayer's payments during the year. Line 25d is then added with estimated tax payments (line 26) and other refundable credits (line 32) to produce the total payments on line 33. The IRS subtracts total payments from total tax (line 24) to determine whether the taxpayer owes more (line 37) or gets a refund (line 34).

Line 25c is the **catch-all** sub-line for withholding from sources that are neither Form W-2 (line 25a) nor a Form 1099, Form SSA-1099, or Form RRB-1099 (line 25b). The two sources every typical wage-earner return uses are:

1. **Form W-2G (Certain Gambling Winnings) box 4** — federal income tax the casino, lottery commissioner, racetrack, or other gambling-winnings payer withheld from a payout.
2. **Form 8959 (Additional Medicare Tax) Part V line 24** — the portion of W-2 box 6 (Medicare tax withheld) that exceeds the regular 1.45% of Medicare wages. This is the employer's Additional Medicare Tax withholding (the 0.9% surtax on wages above the filing-status threshold under Internal Revenue Code §3101(b)(2)), reconciled on Form 8959.

The 2025 Instructions for Form 1040 also route four less-common sources to line 25c:

3. **Schedule K-1** — federal income tax withheld at the pass-through level.
4. **Form 1042-S (Foreign Person's U.S. Source Income Subject to Withholding)** — non-resident-alien withholding the project does not currently support.
5. **Form 8805 (Foreign Partner's Information Statement of Section 1446 Withholding Tax)** — withholding by a partnership on a foreign partner's effectively-connected income.
6. **Form 8288-A (Statement of Withholding on Dispositions by Foreign Persons of U.S. Real Property Interests)** — Foreign Investment in Real Property Tax Act (FIRPTA) withholding.

Sources 3–6 are documented as out of scope per `lines\25abcd.md` §10 (see Gap E below).

Three things to keep in mind throughout:

1. Line 25c has **no rate**, **no threshold**, and **no formula** beyond "add up federal income tax withheld from every form the IRS lists in the line-25c instructions."
2. On a joint return, **both spouses' W-2G amounts** are combined onto the same line 25c.
3. The Form 8959 Part V line 24 contribution to line 25c is the **reconciliation of W-2 box 6 against the regular 1.45% Medicare rate** — it is not a separate withholding event. The employer already withheld it; Form 8959 simply identifies the portion attributable to the Additional Medicare Tax (0.9%) and routes it to line 25c so the taxpayer claims credit for it.

## 1. The IRS-canonical computation (what the form really says to do)

### 1a. Equation for line 25c

```
Form 1040 line 25c = sum of (Form W-2G box 4 federal income tax withheld) across every Form W-2G on the return
                    + Form 8959 Part V line 24 (Additional Medicare Tax withheld) when Form 8959 is filed
                    + sum of (Schedule K-1 federal income tax withheld)              [out of scope — Gap E]
                    + sum of (Form 1042-S federal income tax withheld)               [out of scope — Gap E]
                    + sum of (Form 8805 federal income tax withheld)                 [out of scope — Gap E]
                    + sum of (Form 8288-A federal income tax withheld)               [out of scope — Gap E]
```

### 1b. Word version

For every Form W-2G the taxpayer (and the spouse on a joint return) received, find the amount in box 4 labeled "Federal income tax withheld." Add every such amount together. Then, if the taxpayer filed Form 8959 because Medicare wages exceeded the filing-status threshold OR because the employer withheld Additional Medicare Tax that needs to be reconciled, add the amount from Form 8959 line 24 "Total Additional Medicare Tax withholding." The total is Form 1040 line 25c. (If the taxpayer also has Schedule K-1 / Form 1042-S / Form 8805 / Form 8288-A withholding, those amounts add too — but the project does not currently support them; see Gap E.)

### 1c. Where each input comes from

| Input | Per-row IRS source | Project intake form | Canonical project field name |
|---|---|---|---|
| Form W-2G box 4 | Form W-2G box 4 | `form-w-2g.xlsx` (statement, multiple-per-return) | `federalIncomeTaxWithheldAmount` |
| Form 8959 Part V line 24 | Form 8959 line 24 (computed) | Computed from Form 4852 + Form 4137 + W-2 box 6 | `Form8959.line24TotalAmtWithheld` (Java) |
| Schedule K-1 withholding | Schedule K-1 line 14 (Form 1041), line 15 (Form 1065), line 13 (Form 1120-S) | (out of scope) | (none) |
| Form 1042-S withholding | Form 1042-S box 7a | (out of scope) | (none) |
| Form 8805 withholding | Form 8805 line 10 | (out of scope) | (none) |
| Form 8288-A withholding | Form 8288-A box 2 | (out of scope) | (none) |

### 1d. Reference data (2025 constants that drive line 25c)

The line-25c aggregation step itself has no rate / threshold / inflation-indexed amount. But the upstream Form 8959 Part V line 21 computation that produces the line-24 value uses two constants — listed here so that an audit reading "line 25c just adds two amounts" is not misled into thinking no constants are in scope.

| Constant | Value (2025) | Used in | Source |
|---|---|---|---|
| Regular Medicare rate (`REGULAR_MEDICARE_RATE`) | 1.45% | Form 8959 Part V line 21 = 1.45% × Medicare wages | Internal Revenue Code §3101(b)(1) |
| Additional Medicare Tax rate (`ADDITIONAL_MEDICARE_RATE`) | 0.9% | Form 8959 Part I line 7 = 0.9% × (Medicare wages − threshold); reconciles to Part V line 22 = line 19 − line 21 | Internal Revenue Code §3101(b)(2) |
| Additional Medicare Tax threshold (Single / Head of household / Qualifying surviving spouse) | $200,000 | Form 8959 Part I line 5 | Internal Revenue Code §1401(b)(2) |
| Additional Medicare Tax threshold (Married filing jointly) | $250,000 | Form 8959 Part I line 5 | Internal Revenue Code §1401(b)(2) |
| Additional Medicare Tax threshold (Married filing separately) | $125,000 | Form 8959 Part I line 5 | Internal Revenue Code §1401(b)(2) |
| Gambling-winnings withholding rate | 24% | Applied by the gambling payer to fill Form W-2G box 4 (not recomputed on Form 1040) | Internal Revenue Code §3402(q) |

Note: the 24% gambling withholding rate (Internal Revenue Code §3402(q)) is applied by the payer when reportable winnings exceed $5,000. The line-25c aggregation step does NOT recompute it — it accepts the dollar amount the payer printed in box 4.

### 1f. Filing-status interaction

- **Single, Head of household, Qualifying surviving spouse, Married filing jointly:** Sum every Form W-2G belonging to the filer (and the spouse, on a joint return). Form 8959 Part V line 24 is built per-return; on Married filing jointly the underlying line 19 (W-2 box 6) sums across both spouses' W-2s via `sumW2MedicareTaxWithheld` with no per-Social Security Number filter.
- **Married filing separately:** Sum only the Form W-2G entries belonging to the taxpayer who is filing this separate return; the spouse's Form W-2G withholding belongs on the spouse's own separate Form 1040 line 25c. Form 8959 is built independently for each spouse's separate return.

Unlike line 25a (Form 4852 spouse-side guard) and line 25b (Form 4852 spouse-side guard), line 25c has **no explicit Married-filing-separately guard at the composer** because:
- W-2G entries are separated at the data layer (each spouse has their own user identity; per-user storage already excludes the spouse's W-2G).
- Form 8959 is computed independently per return; the spouse's Form 8959 line 24 is never built into the taxpayer's compute context.

## 2. The computation as actually implemented in the code

> File: `C:\us-tax\us-tax-be\src\main\java\com\ustax\microservices\TaxReturnComputeService.java`

The implementation is concentrated inside the same method (`computeLine31ThroughLine38`) that handles lines 25a and 25b, and uses the same generic helper for the W-2G summation plus a direct read of the `Form8959` model for the Part V line 24 contribution.

### 2a. Helper — `sumFederalWithholdingFromEntries(entries)` (line 27550)

This is the same helper line 25a (Form W-2 box 2) and the line 25b 1099-series variadic call use:

```
input  entries  = a list of statement entry maps (here, each map is one Form W-2G)
output total    = the sum of every entry's federalIncomeTaxWithheldAmount field, considering only positive amounts; null if the list is empty or all amounts are zero or null

for every entry:
    amount = parseAmount(entry.federalIncomeTaxWithheldAmount)
    if amount is not null and amount > 0:
        total = (total is null ? amount : total + amount)
return total
```

The W-2G mapper exposes the box 4 amount under the canonical key `federalIncomeTaxWithheldAmount` (verified at `W2gMapper.java:226`). No mapper-output-key drift — the helper picks it up correctly.

### 2b. Form 8959 Part V line 24 computation (line 22064–22075)

`buildForm8959(...)` computes Part V (the withholding reconciliation) as:

```
line19 = sumW2MedicareTaxWithheld(w2Entries)              // Form W-2 box 6 summed across all W-2 entries
line20 = line1 (Medicare wages from Part I line 1)        // same number again, for visual layout on the form
line21 = line20 × REGULAR_MEDICARE_RATE                    // 1.45% × Medicare wages = regular Medicare withholding
                                                          // (rounded with roundMoney before storage on line 21)
line22 = max(0, line19 − line21)                          // excess box 6 = Additional Medicare on wages
                                                          // null when box 6 does not exceed 1.45% × wages
line23 = sumRrtaAmtWithheldFromTipForm(tipIncomeTaxpayer) // Form 4137 Part II — Railroad Retirement Tax Act
       + (mfj ? sumRrtaAmtWithheldFromTipForm(tipIncomeSpouse) : null)
                                                          // (spouse only added on Married filing jointly)
line24 = addNonNull(line22, line23)                       // → Form 1040 line 25c
```

Then `form.setLine24TotalAmtWithheld(line24)` at line 22100 stores the computed value on the `Form8959` model.

### 2c. Composer — line 25c section of `computeLine31ThroughLine38` (line 26788)

```
input  formW2gEntries = the list of W-2G entries for this return
input  form8959       = the built Form 8959 object, or null when Form 8959 is not filed
output Payments.withholdingOther = the line 25c amount written to the Form 1040 model

withholdingOther = sumFederalWithholdingFromEntries(formW2gEntries)

if form8959 is not null AND form8959.getLine24TotalAmtWithheld() is not null:
    withholdingOther = roundMoney(safeAmount(withholdingOther) + form8959.getLine24TotalAmtWithheld())

payments.setWithholdingOther(withholdingOther)
```

Three things to notice in the composer:

1. **`formW2gEntries` is not filtered by Social Security Number.** It is the full list of every Form W-2G saved on this return. On a Married-filing-jointly return both spouses' W-2G entries appear in the list; both contribute. On a Married-filing-separately return the data layer separates each spouse's statements at storage time.
2. **The Form 8959 line 24 contribution is conditional**: only added when Form 8959 is built AND its line 24 is non-null. `buildForm8959(...)` returns null when neither Part I line 18 nor Part V line 24 is non-null, so the if-branch correctly handles the "no Form 8959 needed" case.
3. **`roundMoney(...)` runs only in the if-branch** — when only W-2G contributes (no Form 8959 line 24), the raw `BigDecimal` sum from the helper is stored without rounding. (See Gap A.)

### 2d. Downstream consumer — line 25d total (line 26799)

```
totalWithholding = round(safeAmount(withholdingW2) + safeAmount(withholding1099) + safeAmount(withholdingOther))
payments.setTotalWithholding(totalWithholding > 0 ? totalWithholding : null)
```

Line 25d is the simple sum of all three withholding sub-lines, rounded to whole dollars, and stored as null when the total is zero (Java null-when-zero canonical convention).

### 2e. Compute order (relevant call sites in `prepare()`)

```
~288    statementDataService.listEntriesWithData(uid, "w-2g")    → builds formW2gEntries
~591    buildForm8959(...)                                        → builds form8959 (Part V line 24 set inside)
~3400   computeLine31ThroughLine38(...)                           → writes Payments.withholdingOther
```

### 2f. Frontend display

Line 25c is displayed on the Form 1040 PDF preview at the Acroform field `f2_19[0]` (page 2, line 25c) via the semantic name `line25c_federal_income_tax_withheld_other_forms`. The frontend reads `form.payments?.withholdingOther` from the compute response and writes the formatted amount into that field. There is no client-side recomputation — the backend value is the source of truth.

## 4. Identified gaps and shortcomings

Listed in order of taxpayer-impact severity. Two are coverage / scope concerns; one is a parity inconsistency with no observable taxpayer impact today.

### Gap A — Line 25c sub-total is rounded only in the Form-8959-add branch (W-2G-only path stores raw sum)

**Severity:** Low (cosmetic / future-proofing).

**What the IRS rule says.** Form 1040 prints whole-dollar amounts; the IRS accepts either whole-dollar or actual-cent reporting on each line.

**What the code does.** At `TaxReturnComputeService.java:26788-26796`:
```java
BigDecimal withholdingOther = sumFederalWithholdingFromEntries(formW2gEntries);
if (form8959 != null && form8959.getLine24TotalAmtWithheld() != null) {
    withholdingOther = roundMoney(
            safeAmount(withholdingOther).add(form8959.getLine24TotalAmtWithheld()));
}
payments.setWithholdingOther(withholdingOther);
```

- When ONLY W-2G contributes: `withholdingOther` is the raw `BigDecimal` from the helper, unrounded.
- When ONLY Form 8959 line 24 contributes: `withholdingOther` is `roundMoney(0 + form8959.line24)` — rounded. ✓
- When BOTH contribute: `withholdingOther` is `roundMoney(W-2G sum + form8959.line24)` — rounded. ✓

The W-2G-only branch is the only one storing an unrounded value. In practice this is a no-op today because every W-2G mapper writes integer-dollar `BigDecimal` values from the `inputnumber` UI field, so rounding is idempotent. The minor risk: if a future automated W-2G import preserves cents, the line 25c value could end up displayed as `12345.67` while line 25d is `12346` — an inconsistent JavaScript Object Notation (JSON) payload.

The line-25a path got `roundMoney` as part of the 25a.md §8b G2 closure on 2026-06-10; the line-25b path got the same fix as part of the 25b.md §9e Gap D closure on 2026-06-10; the line-25c path was overlooked.

**Fix (one-line, mirrors the 25b.md Gap D closure at `TaxReturnComputeService.java:26781`):** insert
```java
withholdingOther = withholdingOther == null ? null : roundMoney(withholdingOther);
```
immediately before `payments.setWithholdingOther(withholdingOther);` at line 26796. Or alternatively, restructure the if-branch so the rounding happens unconditionally for any non-null value.

### Gap B — No Form 8959 Part V line 24 positive-value guard

**Severity:** Low (defensive).

**What the IRS rule says.** Form 8959 line 24 is non-negative by construction — it is `max(0, line22) + line23` (with line 22 also non-negative). The IRS would never write a negative value here.

**What the code does.** The composer at `TaxReturnComputeService.java:26791-26793` adds whatever `form8959.getLine24TotalAmtWithheld()` returns:
```java
if (form8959 != null && form8959.getLine24TotalAmtWithheld() != null) {
    withholdingOther = roundMoney(
            safeAmount(withholdingOther).add(form8959.getLine24TotalAmtWithheld()));
}
```

If a future code change to `buildForm8959(...)` were to introduce a negative value (for example, a sign error in the line22 calculation), the composer would silently subtract from line 25c rather than reject the value. Today the upstream code computes `line22 = max(0, line19 − line21)` (correctly clamped), so this is purely defensive.

**Fix:** add a `compareTo(BigDecimal.ZERO) > 0` filter to the if-condition. Defer until a defect is observed; today the upstream code structure guarantees non-negative.

### Gap C — No e2e test for W-2G alone (without Form 8959) covers the unrounded-store path

**Severity:** Low (test coverage; same shape as 25b.md Gap D).

**What is missing.** The single existing line-25c end-to-end test (`Test 4` in `line25abcd-withholding.spec.ts`, "W-2G box 4 withholding flows to line 25c") seeds an integer-dollar W-2G amount ($2,880) and asserts the exact integer-dollar value. It does not exercise a sub-dollar precision path, so Gap A's no-op rounding goes unverified.

**Fix:** none today — Gap A is no-op on integer-dollar inputs. A defensive cents-preserving regression test could be added once the Gap A fix lands.

### Gap D — `extractForm4852Line25cWithholding` does not exist

**Severity:** None (Form 4852 has no line-25c contribution).

Form 4852 (Substitute for Form W-2 or Form 1099-R) has fields for federal income tax withheld on the W-2 substitute (line 7e → line 25a) and the 1099-R substitute (line 8f → line 25b). The form has **no field for federal income tax withheld that routes to line 25c** — gambling winnings (Form W-2G) and Form 8959 reconciliation are not substitute-eligible. So no helper is needed and no gap exists.

Noted here only to clarify a potential reader's confusion when comparing the line-25a / line-25b helpers (`extractForm4852Line25aWithholding`, `extractForm4852Line25bWithholding`) against the line-25c implementation.

### Gap E — Out of scope: Schedule K-1, Form 1042-S, Form 8805, Form 8288-A

**Severity:** Low to Medium for affected taxpayers (the project's scope is wage-earner returns; the four out-of-scope forms apply to pass-through partners, foreign persons, and certain real-estate transactions, all of which are outside the documented scope).

**What the IRS rule says.** The 2025 Instructions for Form 1040 enumerate four additional sources that go on line 25c:

1. **Schedule K-1** — federal income tax withheld at the pass-through level (Schedule K-1 of Form 1041 line 14; Schedule K-1 of Form 1065 line 15; Schedule K-1 of Form 1120-S line 13).
2. **Form 1042-S (Foreign Person's U.S. Source Income Subject to Withholding)** — withholding the U.S. payer applied to a non-resident-alien recipient.
3. **Form 8805 (Foreign Partner's Information Statement of Section 1446 Withholding Tax)** — partnership's Internal Revenue Code §1446 withholding on a foreign partner's effectively-connected income.
4. **Form 8288-A (Statement of Withholding on Dispositions by Foreign Persons of U.S. Real Property Interests)** — Foreign Investment in Real Property Tax Act (FIRPTA) withholding under Internal Revenue Code §1445.

**What the code does.** Schedule K-1 statements are intaken (`StatementFormCatalog` registers `schedule-k1-1041`, `schedule-k1-1065`, `schedule-k1-1120s`) and contribute to other line computations (Schedule B, Schedule D, Schedule E), but their withholding fields are NOT wired to `Payments.withholdingOther`. Forms 1042-S, 8805, and 8288-A are not intaken at all.

**Why it is deferred.** `lines\25abcd.md §10` documents the cluster scope: "If the application does not yet support K-1, 1042-S, 8805, or 8288-A withholding, document that as deferred support rather than excluding those sources from the tax-year rule." The four-form bucket aligns with the project's wage-earner-first scope and the documented intake-only treatment of pass-through and foreign-person tax positions.

**Fix:** out of scope until the project expands. Documented for completeness and to flag the bucket as a future audit item.

## 5. Validation rules the implementation already enforces (positive findings)

- `line 25c ≥ 0` — the W-2G helper only sums positive amounts; the upstream Form 8959 line 24 is non-negative by construction.
- `line 25c = sum of W-2G box 4 + Form 8959 line 24` for the implemented sources — exactly matches the IRS rule for those sources.
- `line 25c excludes the spouse's W-2G on Married filing separately` — enforced by the storage-level per-user separation of W-2G entries.
- `line 25d = line 25a + line 25b + line 25c` — enforced by `computeLine31ThroughLine38` at line 26799.
- `line 25d > 0 ? totalWithholding : null` — null-when-zero convention enforced.
- W-2G mapper uses the canonical `federalIncomeTaxWithheldAmount` output key (verified at `W2gMapper.java:226`) — the generic line-25c summer picks it up without need for an alias.
- Form 8959 Part V line 24 = max(0, line 22) + line 23 — both addends are non-negative by construction.
- Form 4137 Part II Railroad Retirement Tax Act withholding (line 23) correctly aggregated across both spouses on Married filing jointly via the explicit `mfj ? sumRrtaAmtWithheldFromTipForm(tipIncomeSpouse) : null` clause at `TaxReturnComputeService.java:22074`.
- Form 8959 is built before `computeLine31ThroughLine38(...)` runs, so the line 24 read at line 26791 always sees the final computed value.

## 7. Bottom line for line 25c correctness in this codebase today

For every taxpayer in the project's wage-earner-first scope, line 25c is computed correctly. The two implemented sources (Form W-2G box 4 and Form 8959 Part V line 24) cover the overwhelming majority of line-25c amounts a typical individual return would carry. The four out-of-scope sources (K-1 / 1042-S / 8805 / 8288-A) apply to taxpayer populations the project documents as out of scope.

The remaining items are:
- **Gap A** — line 25c sub-total rounding consistency. Cosmetic; no observable taxpayer-impact difference today. One-line fix mirroring the 25b.md Gap D closure.
- **Gap B** — defensive positive-filter on Form 8959 line 24 add. Cosmetic; the upstream Form 8959 build guarantees non-negative. No fix needed today.
- **Gap C** — end-to-end test coverage for the W-2G-only-unrounded path. Pending Gap A closure.
- **Gap D** — a placeholder noting that Form 4852 has no line-25c contribution (no Form 4852 substitute for W-2G or Form 8959).
- **Gap E** — out of scope (policy-blocked) — Schedule K-1 / Form 1042-S / Form 8805 / Form 8288-A.

### 10d. Test 21 (NOT ADDED) — Married-filing-separately exclusion of spouse's Form W-2G

**Intended coverage:** Verify that on Married filing separately the spouse's Form W-2G box 4 does NOT contribute to the taxpayer's line 25c (the spouse's W-2G belongs on the spouse's own separate Form 1040 line 25c).

**Why NOT implementable in the current test harness:** The line-25c composer at `TaxReturnComputeService.java:26788` calls `sumFederalWithholdingFromEntries(formW2gEntries)` — this sums every Form W-2G entry in the list regardless of `recipientTIN`. Unlike the line-25a / line-25b Form 4852 path (which has an explicit composer-level `isMfsForForm4852 ? null : ...` guard for the spouse Form 4852 entity), the Form W-2G path has NO composer-level guard. The Married-filing-separately exclusion of the spouse's W-2G is enforced at the **storage layer**: each spouse has their own Firebase user identity (UID), and the data layer's `statementDataService.listEntriesWithData(uid, "w-2g")` only returns entries for the requesting UID.

In the project's end-to-end test harness, every test runs against a **single shared Firebase account** (the shared-user pattern in `e2e/helpers/api-flow.ts`). Both the "taxpayer" and "spouse" data go to the same UID. The storage-layer per-UID separation that enforces the Married-filing-separately exclusion in production therefore cannot be exercised in the test harness — every W-2G entry seeded via the test API is stored under the same UID and is returned by `listEntriesWithData`.

Writing a test that seeds two W-2G entries with different `recipientTIN` values and asserts the spouse's value is excluded on Married filing separately would **falsely fail** (both would sum, because the composer doesn't filter by recipientTIN). Writing the inverse — asserting both DO sum — would lock in behavior that contradicts the IRS rule and the documented storage-layer protection.

**Production behavior verification:** the per-UID separation works in production because each spouse's data is isolated by Firebase auth. A future multi-UID test harness (e.g., a parallel-account fixture) could exercise this path; pending that infrastructure, the Married-filing-separately exclusion is a verified-by-construction-only property.

### 10h. Lesson captured from §10

The §10 second-pass coverage extension session highlighted a previously-implicit harness limitation that future audit work should account for: **Married-filing-separately spouse-data exclusion is only testable at the end-to-end layer for forms with an explicit composer guard** (like the Form 4852 `isMfsForForm4852 ? null : ...` ternary). For forms whose Married-filing-separately exclusion is enforced solely at the storage layer (W-2G, every 1099-series statement, Form W-2 — i.e., all the statement forms), the single-shared-Firebase-account end-to-end harness cannot verify it without multi-account infrastructure.

This is not a defect in the test harness — it is a documented architectural choice tied to the shared-user pattern. The implication for future line audits is: when enumerating Married-filing-separately exclusion scenarios for a sub-line, distinguish between **composer-level guards** (testable via the existing harness) and **storage-level guards** (not testable; production-verified only).
