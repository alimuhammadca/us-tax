## Quick orientation

The Form 1040 label for line 27b is:

- **On-form short label (visible on the printed Form 1040 page 2):** "b Clergy filing Schedule SE (see instructions)"
- **Full rule as stated in the 2025 Instructions for Form 1040:** "Check the box on line 27b if you, or your spouse if filing jointly, were a member of the clergy or had church employee income, AND have an amount on Schedule SE (Form 1040), line 4c, that is also included on Form 1040 or 1040-SR, line 1z."

The on-form short label uses the word "Clergy" generically; the full instructions then expand "Clergy" to cover both category A (members of the clergy in the strict religious-credentials sense) and category B (lay employees with church employee income). The on-form short label is what a taxpayer sees while filling out the return; the full rule in section 1b is what determines when the box should actually be checked.

In one sentence: line 27b is a single **checkbox** on Form 1040 page 2 sitting next to line 27a. It carries **no dollar amount** of its own. It is a signal to the Earned Income Credit computation that the special clergy / member-of-religious-order rules apply so that the same dollars of compensation are not counted twice when figuring the earned-income base for the Earned Income Credit.

Two important contrasts to clear up upfront:

1. **Line 27b is NOT a separate credit, exclusion, or addition.** It does not change line 32 (other refundable credits), line 33 (total payments), or line 34 (refund). It only changes *how* line 27a is computed — and only on returns where a Schedule SE (Self-Employment Tax) is filed.
2. **Line 27b is structurally different from line 27a, 27c, and the line 1i combat-pay election.** Line 27a is a dollar amount, line 27c is a checkbox describing the *taxpayer's* election regarding the Earned Income Credit, and the combat-pay election (line 1i with its own intake form) is the taxpayer's choice to add nontaxable combat pay into the earned-income base for the Earned Income Credit. Line 27b is none of those — it is a *technical adjustment flag* triggered by a specific overlap between Form 1040 line 1z and Schedule SE line 4c.

## 1. The Internal Revenue Service canonical rule for line 27b

### 1a. What the IRS says, in plain English

For a member of the clergy (a duly ordained, commissioned, or licensed minister of a church; a member of a religious order who has not taken a vow of poverty; or a Christian Science practitioner or reader) who is also self-employed, the same dollars of ministerial compensation can show up in two different places on the tax return:

- On **Form 1040 line 1z** (the total of wages, salaries, tips, and other employee compensation), if the church reported the minister's salary on a Form W-2 (Wage and Tax Statement).
- On **Schedule SE (Form 1040) line 4c** (Total net earnings from self-employment), because ministers' earnings are treated as self-employment earnings for Social Security and Medicare tax purposes even when paid as W-2 wages.

If the Earned Income Credit's earned-income worksheet simply added the line 1z amount AND the Schedule SE line 4c amount together as earned income, it would double-count the minister's compensation. This would inflate earned income and produce too large an Earned Income Credit.

Line 27b is the checkbox the taxpayer uses to tell the Internal Revenue Service: "I am in this overlapping situation. Please use the adjusted earned-income rules for clergy when figuring my Earned Income Credit."

### 1b. The three conditions for checking line 27b

All three must be true for the taxpayer (or for the spouse on a joint return):

1. The taxpayer falls in **either** of the following categories (it does not have to be both):
   - **Category A — Member of the clergy**: a duly ordained, commissioned, or licensed minister of a church; a member of a religious order who has NOT taken a vow of poverty; or a Christian Science practitioner or reader.
   - **Category B — Had church employee income**: a lay employee of a church or qualified church-controlled organization that has elected exemption from Social Security taxes under Internal Revenue Code section 3121(w). Such employees receive a Form W-2 from the church but their wages are excluded from Social Security and Medicare withholding via Form 941 and instead reported on Schedule SE for self-employment tax. They are *not* clergy in the religious-credentials sense, but the same overlap problem (W-2 wages on line 1z that are also on Schedule SE line 4c) applies.
2. The taxpayer is filing Schedule SE (Self-Employment Tax) with the return.
3. The amount reported on Schedule SE line 4c (Total net earnings from self-employment) **includes** an amount that is **also** included on Form 1040 line 1z (wages, salaries, tips, and other employee compensation).

If any one of the three conditions fails, line 27b stays unchecked.

**Why two categories?** The two trigger paths exist because the overlap problem (W-2 box 1 wages reappearing on Schedule SE line 4c) has two distinct origins. For Category A, the overlap arises from the special tax treatment of ministers' compensation under Internal Revenue Code section 1402(c) — ministerial earnings are self-employment income for Social Security purposes even when paid as W-2 wages. For Category B, the overlap arises from the church-employer's election under Internal Revenue Code section 3121(w) to opt the entire workforce out of the Federal Insurance Contributions Act, which then routes lay-employee Social Security and Medicare tax through Schedule SE instead. Either origin produces the same line-1z-and-line-4c overlap; checking line 27b signals "use the adjusted earned-income worksheet" regardless of which category the taxpayer is in.

### 1c. What checking line 27b changes

Checking line 27b does not by itself add or subtract a dollar amount on the return. It causes a downstream effect on the Earned Income Credit worksheet only:

- When computing the earned-income base for the Earned Income Credit, the worksheet starts from line 1z and adds net earnings from self-employment.
- When line 27b is checked, the worksheet **subtracts** the portion of ministerial compensation that is on BOTH line 1z and Schedule SE line 4c, so that the same dollars are not added twice.
- The Earned Income Credit amount on line 27a is then computed from this adjusted earned-income base.

The mathematics for the adjustment:

```text
earnedIncomeForEicWorksheet =
      line1z                                         // wages, salaries, tips, other employee compensation
    + scheduleSE.line4c                              // total net earnings from self-employment
    - (if line27b = true) overlapAmount              // the portion that appears on BOTH
```

Where `overlapAmount` is the dollar value of ministerial compensation that the church reported as W-2 wages AND that also went into Schedule SE line 4c.

### 1d. Equation for line 27b itself

Because line 27b is a checkbox, its "value" is Boolean (true / checked, or false / unchecked). The first condition is satisfied by EITHER of two category checks:

```text
taxpayerCategoryATriggered =                                  // member of clergy
        taxpayerIsOrdainedMinister
     OR taxpayerIsMemberOfReligiousOrderWithoutVowOfPoverty
     OR taxpayerIsChristianSciencePractitioner

taxpayerCategoryBTriggered =                                  // had church employee income
        taxpayerHadChurchEmployeeIncomeUnderIrc3121w

taxpayerLine27bApplies =
        (taxpayerCategoryATriggered OR taxpayerCategoryBTriggered)
    AND taxpayerFilesScheduleSE
    AND (scheduleSE.line4c overlapsWith form1040.line1z for the taxpayer)
```

On a joint return the rule is checked separately for each spouse, and line 27b is checked if it is true for **either** spouse.

```text
line27b = taxpayerLine27bApplies
          OR (filingJoint AND spouseLine27bApplies)
```

### 1e. Additional related rule — clergy earned-income inclusion choice

Publication 517 (Social Security and Other Information for Members of the Clergy and Religious Workers) also tells ministers that for Earned Income Credit purposes, the net earnings from self-employment from ministerial services count as earned income even if the minister has elected to be exempt from self-employment tax under Internal Revenue Code section 1402(e). Line 27b is not the same as that election — it is a different technical adjustment, specific to the overlap problem described above.

## 5. The Internal Revenue Service canonical computation, step by step

For a reader who wants to manually compute line 27b for a 2025 tax return:

1. **Identify whether the taxpayer falls in either of the two Internal Revenue Service categories.**
   - **Category A** — clergy:
     - Is the taxpayer a duly ordained, commissioned, or licensed minister of a church? (Yes / No)
     - OR is the taxpayer a member of a religious order who has NOT taken a vow of poverty? (Yes / No)
     - OR is the taxpayer a Christian Science practitioner or reader? (Yes / No)
     - If the answer to all three is No → set `taxpayerCategoryATriggered = false`; otherwise set it to `true`.
   - **Category B** — church employee income:
     - Was the taxpayer a lay employee of a church or qualified church-controlled organization that has elected exemption from the Federal Insurance Contributions Act under Internal Revenue Code section 3121(w)? (Yes / No)
     - If Yes → set `taxpayerCategoryBTriggered = true`; otherwise set it to `false`.
   - `taxpayerTriggersFirstCondition = (taxpayerCategoryATriggered OR taxpayerCategoryBTriggered)`.
2. **Determine whether the taxpayer is filing Schedule SE.**
   - Is a Schedule SE (Self-Employment Tax) being included with the return for the taxpayer? (Yes / No)
   - Store as `taxpayerFilesScheduleSE`.
3. **Determine whether Schedule SE line 4c overlaps Form 1040 line 1z for the taxpayer.**
   - Look at the Schedule SE that the taxpayer filed. Find the dollar amount on Schedule SE line 4c (Total net earnings from self-employment).
   - Compare to Form 1040 line 1z (wages, salaries, tips, and other employee compensation).
   - If any portion of Schedule SE line 4c was ALSO reported on a Form W-2 that contributes to line 1z (typical for a minister paid a salary by their church), set `taxpayerScheduleSeOverlapsLine1z = true`.
4. **Compute the taxpayer-side checkbox value:**
   ```text
   taxpayerLine27bApplies = taxpayerTriggersFirstCondition
                            AND taxpayerFilesScheduleSE
                            AND taxpayerScheduleSeOverlapsLine1z
   ```
5. **On a joint return, repeat steps 1 through 4 for the spouse**, producing `spouseLine27bApplies`.
6. **Final value of line 27b:**
   ```text
   line27b = taxpayerLine27bApplies
             OR (filingJoint AND spouseLine27bApplies)
   ```
7. **Cascade effect on line 27a** (this is downstream of line 27b but the entire reason line 27b exists):
   - If `line27b = false`, the Earned Income Credit earned-income worksheet uses the regular formula: `earnedIncome = line1z + scheduleSE.line4c + nontaxableCombatPayElection + otherEarnedIncome`.
   - If `line27b = true`, the worksheet subtracts the overlap so the same dollars are not added twice: `earnedIncome = line1z + scheduleSE.line4c + nontaxableCombatPayElection + otherEarnedIncome − overlapAmount`.
   - The Earned Income Credit on line 27a is then looked up from this adjusted earned-income base.

## 6. The computation as implemented in this codebase

Line 27b is **not implemented**. It is structurally a degenerate constant — the value of `false` is treated as a static, always-true assumption rather than as the result of any computation.

### 6a. What is in the code today

The only references to line 27b in the production source tree are two inline comments in `TaxReturnComputeService.java`:

There are no Java methods named `computeLine27b`, `computeClergyAdjustment`, `getLine27b`, or anything analogous. There is no front-end binding in `form-tax-return-1040.component.ts` that reads or displays a line 27b value. There are no unit tests or end-to-end tests for line 27b.

### 6b. The implemented "formula"

Expressed in the same notation as section 5:

```text
line27b = false                  // unconditional; always
```

There are zero inputs, zero decisions, and zero conditional branches. The constant is implied by the absence of code, not asserted by any explicit assignment.

### 6c. The cascade effect on line 27a in the current implementation

Because line 27b is treated as a permanent `false`, the Earned Income Credit earned-income computation in `computeLine27aEIC` does not subtract any overlap amount. The implemented earned-income formula (from line 27388 onward in `TaxReturnComputeService.java`) is:

```java
earnedIncome = sumW2WagesBySsn(w2Entries, ...)      // from Form 1040 line 1z W-2 box 1
             − inmateWagesExclusion                  // IRC section 32(c)(2)(B)(iv)
             + eicTaxpayer.otherEarnedIncome         // jury duty pay, strike benefits, etc.
             + form1040.line1i nontaxableCombatPayElection  // combat pay election
             + (MFJ only) eicSpouse.spouseOtherEarnedIncome
```

There is no `+ scheduleSE.line4c` term and no `− overlapAmount` term. Both are absent because Schedule SE is out of project scope.

## 10. Gap analysis

The gap inventory for line 27b is short because the entire line is policy-blocked. Each gap is labelled with the same letters used in the project's combined `lines/27abc.md` document.

### Gap G9 — Schedule SE (Self-Employment Tax) is out of project scope

**Status:** Deferred. Documented in `outstanding.md` and in `lines/27abc.md` section 4.

**Description:** Self-employment income (Schedule C, Schedule F, Schedule SE) is out of project scope as currently set. Until Schedule SE is implemented, line 27b cannot have any value other than `false`, because two of its three preconditions (filing Schedule SE; Schedule SE line 4c overlapping line 1z) are unreachable.

**Impact on the return:** For an actual clergy filer who is paid a salary by a church AND files Schedule SE AND has overlap between line 1z and Schedule SE line 4c, the application would compute the Earned Income Credit on an inflated earned-income base. The line 27a Earned Income Credit value in such a return would be too large compared with the Internal Revenue Service canonical value. The difference can be material — a minister paid $30,000 by their church and whose Schedule SE line 4c includes the same $30,000 would otherwise have their Earned Income Credit worksheet add the $30,000 twice (once from line 1z, once from Schedule SE), producing an Earned Income Credit table lookup at an inflated income that may push the filer past the phaseout end of the Earned Income Credit when in fact they qualify.

**Closure conditions:** Closure of Gap G9 requires:
1. Adding a Schedule SE intake form and Schedule SE compute helper (separate from Earned Income Credit work).
2. Adding a clergy declaration field on the taxpayer's personal data form.
3. Adding an overlap declaration mechanism (or computing the overlap automatically from W-2 box 14 codes plus Schedule SE).
4. Updating `computeLine27aEIC` to subtract the overlap when line 27b is `true`.
5. Adding a `line27b` Boolean output field on `Payments.java`.
6. Adding the rendering binding in `form-tax-return-1040.component.ts` so the checkbox can be drawn on the printed page (note that the Internal Revenue Service Acroform field is absent — rendering would be a custom overlay rather than a form-fill).
7. Adding unit and end-to-end tests.

**Decision today:** Deferred. The project explicitly excludes Schedule SE / self-employment paths.

## 12. Reader's takeaway

For somebody with basic arithmetic and programming knowledge, the situation for line 27b can be summarized in three sentences:

1. **What it is:** A checkbox on Form 1040 page 2 that ministers use to tell the Internal Revenue Service to subtract a double-counted overlap of ministerial wages from the earned-income base for the Earned Income Credit.
2. **What the Internal Revenue Service expects:** The checkbox should be true when, and only when, all three conditions hold (clergy member; filing Schedule SE; Schedule SE line 4c overlap with Form 1040 line 1z). On a joint return either spouse can trigger it.
3. **What this codebase does:** It hardcodes the value to `false` and assumes the overlap does not exist, because Schedule SE is not implemented. For a true clergy filer with overlap, the Earned Income Credit on line 27a is overstated. The project tracks this as Gap G9 (Schedule SE out of scope) — explicitly deferred — and does not currently support the clergy / Schedule SE path.

### 14a. Gap O — Stale §9e in `XLS/Computations/27a.md`

### 14b. Gap P — IRS rule incompleteness: "church employee income" path missing

**Root cause.** Both this spec (§1b, §5) and `lines/27abc.md §4` and the knowledge file's row-27b summary described line 27b's first condition as covering ONLY members of the clergy. The IRS 2025 Form 1040 instructions for line 27b actually say: "Check the box on line 27b if you, or your spouse if filing jointly, **were a member of the clergy or had church employee income**, and have an amount on Schedule SE (Form 1040), line 4c, that is also included on Form 1040 or 1040-SR, line 1z." The "or had church employee income" path covers lay employees of churches that have elected exemption from the Federal Insurance Contributions Act under Internal Revenue Code section 3121(w). Their wages are subject to self-employment tax via Schedule SE instead of Social Security and Medicare withholding via Form 941, which produces the same line-1z-and-Schedule-SE-line-4c overlap as the clergy path.

**Closure applied.** Rewrote §1b in this spec to split the first condition into Category A (clergy) and Category B (church employee income under Internal Revenue Code section 3121(w)), with an explanation of why both paths exist and how they produce the same overlap. Updated §1a to distinguish the on-form short label from the full IRS instructions text. Updated §1d to compute `taxpayerTriggersFirstCondition = (Category A OR Category B)`. Updated §5 step 1 to walk through both categories separately.

Note: the rule expansion does not change the implemented behavior at all (line 27b is still a degenerate constant `false`). The IRS-correctness risk surface — what scenarios the application gets wrong when Gap G9 is unblocked — is now correctly characterized as "ANY clergy filer OR church employee filer with the overlap" rather than the narrower "ANY clergy filer with the overlap." The taxpayer-impact magnitude is larger than previously documented but the policy-blocking is unchanged.

### 14g. Lesson captured

**Two specs can drift in opposite directions from the same underlying defect.** The mislabeled semantic name `line27a_from_schedule_eic` caused 27a.md §9e to invent a non-existent "Schedule EIC attached" feature AND caused 27b.md §4 (first pass) to claim the line 27b checkbox didn't exist at all. The same root cause can produce N drift symptoms in N specs.
