## Quick orientation

The Form 1040 label for line 28 (per the actual 2025 IRS Form 1040 page 2, extracted via `pdftotext -layout`):

> **"28   Additional child tax credit (ACTC) from Schedule 8812. If you do not want to claim the ACTC, check here ............... 28"**

The line carries two distinct fields on the printed form:

| Field | Type | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| **Line 28 dollar amount** | Text field — the Additional Child Tax Credit value carried from Schedule 8812 line 27 | The main refundable credit |
| **Line 28 ACTC opt-out checkbox** | CheckBox — "If you do not want to claim the ACTC, check here" | The taxpayer's voluntary opt-out from the refundable component |

In one sentence: line 28 is the **refundable** portion of the family of child-related credits — a dollar amount carried from Schedule 8812 line 27 onto Form 1040 page 2, accompanied by a checkbox that the taxpayer marks to decline the refundable portion while keeping the nonrefundable Child Tax Credit on line 19.

Three facts to keep in mind throughout:

1. **Line 28 is refundable; line 19 is not.** A refundable credit can produce a refund larger than the taxpayer's tax owed (the Internal Revenue Service sends the excess as a payment). A nonrefundable credit can only reduce tax to zero. Schedule 8812 computes both portions: line 14 is the nonrefundable credit (carried to Form 1040 line 19), and line 27 is the refundable Additional Child Tax Credit (carried to Form 1040 line 28).
2. **Only the Child Tax Credit becomes refundable.** The Credit for Other Dependents — the $500-per-dependent credit available for dependents who do not meet the Child Tax Credit definition of "qualifying child" — is always nonrefundable. It can flow into line 19 but cannot flow into line 28.
3. **Line 28 flows to line 32 (other refundable credits subtotal) and from there to line 33 (total payments).** It does not directly reduce tax. It reduces the amount the taxpayer owes by increasing the total-payments side of the return.

The equation:

```text
Form 1040 line 28 = Schedule 8812 line 27
```

Schedule 8812 line 27 is itself the result of a multi-step computation described in the rest of this document.

## 1. The Internal Revenue Service canonical computation

The Additional Child Tax Credit is computed in two parts of Schedule 8812:

- **Part I** establishes the total Child Tax Credit + Credit for Other Dependents tentative credit after the Modified Adjusted Gross Income phaseout, then determines how much of it can be absorbed by tax (the nonrefundable portion on line 14).
- **Part II-A** computes the "excess" portion that did not fit into tax (line 16a), caps it at the Additional Child Tax Credit ceiling (line 16b = number of qualifying children × $1,700), and computes the earned-income-method potential (line 20 = 15% of earned income above $2,500).
- **Part II-B** is an alternative method for families with three or more qualifying children, or bona fide residents of Puerto Rico. It uses the Social Security and Medicare taxes the taxpayer paid through payroll as a substitute base when the earned-income method is insufficient.

### 1a. Part I — Modified Adjusted Gross Income and the tentative credit

**Line 3 — Modified Adjusted Gross Income (MAGI).**

```text
magi = form1040.line11b adjustedGrossIncome
     + (if Form 2555 filed) foreignEarnedIncomeExclusion + housingExclusionAmount
     + (Puerto Rico AGI exclusion — DEFERRED in this codebase per spec §11)
```

In plain English: start with Adjusted Gross Income, then add back any foreign earned income exclusion taken on Form 2555 and any Puerto Rico income exclusion claimed by bona fide Puerto Rico residents. The result is the Modified Adjusted Gross Income for the Child Tax Credit purposes.

**Lines 4 and 6 — qualifying counts.**

```text
numQualifyingChildren = number of dependents on this return where qualifiesForCTC == true
numOtherDependents    = number of dependents where qualifiesForODC == true
```

A "qualifying child" for the Child Tax Credit is a person who satisfies all six tests in Internal Revenue Code section 24(c) and section 152(c) (relationship, age under 17, residency, support, joint return, and identification). An "other dependent" qualifies for the $500 Credit for Other Dependents if they are a dependent on the return but do not meet the qualifying-child age or identification requirements.

**Lines 5, 7, 8 — tentative dollar amounts.**

```text
line5 (CTC potential) = numQualifyingChildren × $2,200
line7 (ODC potential) = numOtherDependents    × $500
line8 (total)         = line5 + line7
```

The $2,200 amount is new in 2025 per the One Big Beautiful Bill Act (it was $2,000 in 2024). The $500 amount is unchanged.

**Lines 9, 10, 11 — phaseout.**

```text
line9  (threshold) = $400,000 if Married Filing Jointly; otherwise $200,000
line10 (excess)    = max(0, magi − line9)
line11 (reduction) = ceil(line10 / $1,000) × $1,000 × 5%
```

In plain English: if Modified Adjusted Gross Income exceeds the threshold, the credit is reduced by $50 for each $1,000 (or fraction thereof) of the excess. The "round up to the next $1,000" treatment means a taxpayer $1 over a $1,000 boundary loses the full $50 for that bracket. The 5% phaseout rate has not changed across the 2024 to 2025 transition.

**Line 12 — credit after phaseout.**

```text
line12 = max(0, line8 − line11)
```

If the phaseout eliminates the entire credit, the rest of Schedule 8812 short-circuits to zero.

### 1b. Part I — Credit Limit Worksheet A and the nonrefundable component

**Credit Limit Worksheet A** (computed at the bottom of Part I, between lines 12 and 14) determines how much of the tentative credit can be absorbed by tax. It works by subtracting from Form 1040 line 18 (the total tax before credits) every nonrefundable credit that comes before the Child Tax Credit in the form's order: foreign tax credit (Schedule 3 line 1), child and dependent care credit (Schedule 3 line 2), education credits (Schedule 3 line 3), retirement savings credit (Schedule 3 line 4), elderly and disabled credit (Schedule R), and any other credits enumerated in the Schedule 8812 instructions.

```text
worksheetA = max(0, form1040.line18 − foreignTaxCredit
                                    − childAndDependentCareCredit
                                    − educationCreditsNonrefundable
                                    − retirementSavingsCredit
                                    − elderlyAndDisabledCredit
                                    − otherEarlierCredits)
```

This represents the tax capacity available to absorb the Child Tax Credit.

**Line 14 — nonrefundable Child Tax Credit + Credit for Other Dependents.**

```text
line14 = min(line12, worksheetA)
```

This is what flows onto Form 1040 line 19. It is *not* what we are computing in this spec — it is documented here only because line 14 directly affects how much remains for the refundable portion at line 16a.

### 1c. Part II-A — Excess Child Tax Credit and the Additional Child Tax Credit ceiling

**Line 16a — excess credit over tax.**

```text
line16a = max(0, line12 − line14)
```

If the entire line 12 fits inside the tax capacity, line 16a is zero and there is no refundable Additional Child Tax Credit. The taxpayer has already absorbed the full credit via line 19.

**Line 16b — Additional Child Tax Credit ceiling.**

```text
line16b = numQualifyingChildren × $1,700
```

The $1,700 amount is new in 2025 per the One Big Beautiful Bill Act (it was $1,600 in 2024). Critically, the Credit for Other Dependents portion is excluded from this ceiling — only qualifying children count. The Credit for Other Dependents can never become refundable, even if some of its $500 amount is unabsorbed by tax.

**Line 17 — Additional Child Tax Credit potential.**

```text
line17 = min(line16a, line16b)
```

This is the absolute maximum that line 27 can be. The next step (the earned-income method on lines 18a through 20) determines how much of this potential the taxpayer can actually claim based on their earned-income base.

### 1d. Part II-A — Earned-income method

**Lines 18a, 18b — earned income.**

```text
line18a = form1040.line1z totalWages
        + form1040.line1i nontaxableCombatPayElection
        + (self-employment net earnings — DEFERRED in this codebase per spec §11)
line18b = form1040.line1i nontaxableCombatPayElection  (disclosure only)
```

Earned income for Schedule 8812 purposes is the same earned income that feeds the Earned Income Credit worksheet: wages, salaries, tips, plus the elected combat pay amount, plus net earnings from self-employment. Self-employment is out of project scope.

**Lines 19, 20 — earned-income-method potential.**

```text
if line18a > $2,500:
    line19 = line18a − $2,500
    line20 = line19 × 15%
else:
    line19 = 0
    line20 = 0
```

In plain English: only earnings above $2,500 count, and 15 cents of every additional dollar of earnings becomes available to be refunded as Additional Child Tax Credit (up to the line 17 cap and the line 16b ceiling). The $2,500 floor and the 15% rate are unchanged between 2024 and 2025.

### 1e. Part II-A — Routing decision

After computing line 20 (earned-income-method potential), Schedule 8812 decides whether the simple earned-income method is sufficient or whether the alternative Social-Security-withholding method (Part II-B) is needed:

```text
if line16b < $5,100 AND NOT a bona fide Puerto Rico resident:
    line27 = min(line17, line20)
elif line20 >= line17:
    line27 = line17
else:
    proceed to Part II-B (lines 21 through 27)
```

The $5,100 threshold equals $1,700 × 3 — when there are fewer than three qualifying children, the earned-income method always reaches the ceiling whenever it is going to reach it at all, so Part II-B is unnecessary. Bona fide residents of Puerto Rico are routed to Part II-B regardless of child count, because their Form 1040 line 1z wages may exclude Puerto Rico-source income that should still count toward the refundable Additional Child Tax Credit.

### 1f. Part II-B — Social Security and Medicare withholding alternative method

This part computes an alternative base from payroll taxes the taxpayer has already paid. The base is the total Social Security and Medicare taxes withheld, minus any Earned Income Credit and refundable American Opportunity Tax Credit already producing refunds for the taxpayer.

**Line 21 — withheld payroll taxes.**

```text
line21 = sum over all Wage and Tax Statement (Form W-2) entries of:
             box 4 (Social Security tax withheld)
           + box 6 (Medicare tax withheld)
       + (Tier 1 Railroad Retirement Tax Act equivalent — DEFERRED per project scope)
```

**Line 22 — other taxes added back.**

```text
line22 = Schedule 1 line 15 (one-half of self-employment tax — DEFERRED per project scope)
       + Schedule 2 line 5  (uncollected Social Security and Medicare tax on wages, from Form 8919)
       + Schedule 2 line 6  (uncollected Social Security and Medicare tax on tips, from Form 4137)
       + Schedule 2 line 13 (additional Medicare tax, from Form 8959)
```

**Lines 23, 24, 25, 26 — alternative base.**

```text
line23 = line21 + line22
line24 = form1040.line27a earnedIncomeCredit
       + form1040.line29  refundableAmericanOpportunityCredit
line25 = max(0, line23 − line24)
line26 = max(line20, line25)
```

In plain English: take the Social Security and Medicare taxes paid, subtract the refundable credits already producing a refund, and take the larger of (the earned-income-method result, the payroll-tax-excess result). This gives the taxpayer the *better* of the two methods. Part II-B exists precisely to give large families an alternative when the earned-income method falls short of the Additional Child Tax Credit ceiling.

**Line 27 — Additional Child Tax Credit.**

```text
line27 = min(line17, line26)
```

This is the final refundable Additional Child Tax Credit. It is bounded above by line 17 (which is itself the smaller of "excess credit over tax" and "qualifying children × $1,700"), so it cannot exceed the ceiling under any path.

### 1g. Equation for Form 1040 line 28 itself

```text
Form 1040 line 28 = Schedule 8812 line 27
                  = (the rest of the computation above)
```

If line 27 is zero, Form 1040 line 28 is blank on the printed form (the Internal Revenue Service convention is "do not write zero in a blank field"; the project's implementation respects this by writing the value only when greater than zero).

## 2. The reference data (constants) for line 28 in 2025

All constants below come from Internal Revenue Service Revenue Procedure 2024-40 §3.08 (the official 2025 inflation-adjusted parameter publication) and the One Big Beautiful Bill Act of 2025 (which raised both the Child Tax Credit and Additional Child Tax Credit caps starting in tax year 2025). They live in this codebase at `ReferenceData.java` lines 594 through 604.

| Constant | Value | Statutory basis | Changed from 2024? |
|---|---|---|---|
| Child Tax Credit per qualifying child | **$2,200** | One Big Beautiful Bill Act of 2025; Rev. Proc. 2024-40 §3.08 | Yes — was $2,000 in 2024 |
| Credit for Other Dependents per dependent | $500 | Internal Revenue Code §24(h)(4); Rev. Proc. 2024-40 §3.08 | No (unchanged since 2018) |
| **Additional Child Tax Credit maximum per qualifying child** | **$1,700** | One Big Beautiful Bill Act of 2025; Rev. Proc. 2024-40 §3.08 | **Yes — was $1,600 in 2024** |
| Phaseout threshold — Married Filing Jointly | $400,000 | IRC §24(b)(2) | No |
| Phaseout threshold — all other filing statuses | $200,000 | IRC §24(b)(2) | No |
| Phaseout rounding increment | $1,000 | IRC §24(b)(2) | No |
| Phaseout rate | 5% (0.05) | IRC §24(b)(2) | No |
| Additional Child Tax Credit earned-income floor | $2,500 | IRC §24(d)(1)(B)(i) | No |
| Additional Child Tax Credit rate (15% of earnings over floor) | 15% (0.15) | IRC §24(d)(1)(B)(ii) | No |
| Schedule 8812 Part II-B routing threshold | $5,100 (derived as 3 × $1,700) | Implicit in the Schedule 8812 routing decision | Yes — implicitly changes with the Additional Child Tax Credit max |

**Important note on the One Big Beautiful Bill Act change.** The two Additional Child Tax Credit constants that depend on the One Big Beautiful Bill Act are `CTC_PER_QUALIFYING_CHILD` (used in line 5 = Part I tentative credit) and `ACTC_MAX_PER_QUALIFYING_CHILD` (used in line 16b = refundable ceiling). The Schedule 8812 Part II-B routing threshold is derived from the Additional Child Tax Credit max times three, so it shifts in lockstep. If the One Big Beautiful Bill Act sunsets or is amended, all three constants must be updated together; the project centralizes them in `ReferenceData.java` to make this update straightforward.

The compute helper sets the Schedule 8812 line 27 value and then conditionally promotes it onto Form 1040 line 28. The promotion logic is:

```java
BigDecimal actc = schedule8812.getLine27ActcCredit();
if (actc != null && actc.compareTo(BigDecimal.ZERO) > 0) {
    payments.setAdditionalChildTaxCredit(actc);
}
```

When the Additional Child Tax Credit is zero (or null) the `payments.additionalChildTaxCredit` field remains null, which the frontend renders as a blank cell on the printed PDF.

The frontend renderer at `form-tax-return-1040.component.ts` writes both the line 28 dollar amount and the line 28 ACTC opt-out checkbox:

```typescript
values['line28_additional_child_tax_credit'] = this.formatAmount(form.payments?.additionalChildTaxCredit);
values['line28_no_schedule8812_claim'] = (schedule8812.electsNoActc === true);
```

The checkbox value reflects the `electsNoActc` Boolean from the Schedule 8812 model (set via the project's CTC/ACTC screening intake form when the user explicitly opts out of the Additional Child Tax Credit). Per the §10 G4 design decision, this is a project-specific conservative opt-out — no Internal Revenue Service basis but documented as accepted because it preserves user agency.

## 5. The Internal Revenue Service canonical computation, step by step

For a reader who wants to manually compute Form 1040 line 28 for a 2025 tax return:

1. **Establish the Modified Adjusted Gross Income.** Start with Form 1040 line 11b (Adjusted Gross Income). Add back the foreign earned income exclusion from Form 2555 (if any) and the Puerto Rico income exclusion (if a bona fide Puerto Rico resident).
2. **Count the dependents.**
   - Count how many dependents on this return are qualifying children for the Child Tax Credit (`numQualifyingChildren`).
   - Count how many are qualifying for the Credit for Other Dependents only (`numOtherDependents`).
3. **Compute the tentative credit.**
   - Line 5 = numQualifyingChildren × $2,200.
   - Line 7 = numOtherDependents × $500.
   - Line 8 = line 5 + line 7.
4. **Apply the phaseout.**
   - Line 9 = $400,000 if Married Filing Jointly, otherwise $200,000.
   - Line 10 = max(0, MAGI − line 9).
   - Line 11 = ceil(line 10 / $1,000) × $1,000 × 5%.
5. **Compute the credit after phaseout.** Line 12 = max(0, line 8 − line 11). If zero, the entire credit is eliminated; line 28 is zero.
6. **Apply Credit Limit Worksheet A.** Subtract from Form 1040 line 18 all the nonrefundable credits that come before the Child Tax Credit in the form order. Call the result `worksheetA`.
7. **Compute the nonrefundable component.** Line 14 = min(line 12, worksheetA). This becomes Form 1040 line 19; it is NOT line 28 but it determines what is left for the refundable portion.
8. **Check for early-exit conditions.** Any of the following sets line 27 (= line 28) to zero immediately:
   - `numQualifyingChildren == 0` (no Additional Child Tax Credit without qualifying children — the Credit for Other Dependents is never refundable).
   - The taxpayer or the spouse filed Form 2555 (the foreign earned income exclusion blocks the Additional Child Tax Credit per Internal Revenue Code §24(d)(5)(B); on Married Filing Separately the spouse's Form 2555 is null-shadowed because the spouses file independently).
   - The 2025 One Big Beautiful Bill Act filer identification rule fails: the taxpayer has an Individual Taxpayer Identification Number on Single / Head of Household / Married Filing Separately / Qualifying Surviving Spouse; or both spouses have Individual Taxpayer Identification Numbers on Married Filing Jointly. (In this case the Child Tax Credit on line 19 is also blocked.)
   - The taxpayer elected to not claim the Additional Child Tax Credit via `electsNoActc = true` (a project-specific conservative opt-out; no Internal Revenue Service basis but documented under Gap G4 as an accepted design decision).
   - Line 16a = max(0, line 12 − line 14) is zero. This means tax fully absorbed the credit; nothing is left to refund.
9. **Compute the Additional Child Tax Credit ceiling.** Line 16b = numQualifyingChildren × $1,700. Note: Credit for Other Dependents does not count toward this ceiling.
10. **Compute Additional Child Tax Credit potential.** Line 17 = min(line 16a, line 16b).
11. **Compute the earned-income-method potential.**
    - Line 18a = Form 1040 line 1z (total wages) + Form 1040 line 1i (nontaxable combat pay election).
    - If line 18a > $2,500: line 19 = line 18a − $2,500; line 20 = line 19 × 15%. Otherwise both are zero.
12. **Decide the routing.**
    - If line 16b < $5,100 AND not a bona fide Puerto Rico resident: line 27 = min(line 17, line 20). Skip Part II-B.
    - Else if line 20 >= line 17: line 27 = line 17. Skip Part II-B (the earned-income method already reaches the ceiling).
    - Else: proceed to Part II-B.
13. **(Part II-B only) Compute the alternative base.**
    - Line 21 = sum across all Wage and Tax Statement entries of box 4 (Social Security tax withheld) + box 6 (Medicare tax withheld). (One-half of Schedule SE self-employment tax is deferred per project scope.)
    - Line 22 = Schedule 2 line 5 + Schedule 2 line 6 + Schedule 2 line 13.
    - Line 23 = line 21 + line 22.
    - Line 24 = Form 1040 line 27a (Earned Income Credit) + Form 1040 line 29 (refundable American Opportunity Tax Credit).
    - Line 25 = max(0, line 23 − line 24).
    - Line 26 = max(line 20, line 25).
    - Line 27 = min(line 17, line 26).
14. **Form 1040 line 28 = Schedule 8812 line 27.** Round to nearest dollar.
15. **Line 28 ACTC opt-out checkbox.** Independent of the dollar value, also write the line 28 opt-out checkbox per the user's election:
    - If the user explicitly set `electsNoActc = true` on the CTC/ACTC screening intake form → check the box on the rendered Form 1040.
    - Otherwise → leave the box unchecked.
    Note: when `electsNoActc = true`, step 8 also forces line 27 (and therefore line 28) to zero. The two PDF fields are then internally consistent: a checked opt-out box paired with a blank dollar value.

### A worked example

A Married Filing Jointly couple with 2 qualifying children, Form 1040 line 11b Adjusted Gross Income of $60,000, Form 1040 line 1z wages of $60,000 (no other income), Form 1040 line 18 total tax of $1,500, no other nonrefundable credits, and no Earned Income Credit:

1. MAGI = $60,000 + $0 (no Form 2555) = $60,000.
2. numQualifyingChildren = 2; numOtherDependents = 0.
3. Line 5 = 2 × $2,200 = $4,400. Line 7 = 0. Line 8 = $4,400.
4. Line 9 = $400,000 (MFJ). Line 10 = max(0, $60,000 − $400,000) = $0. Line 11 = $0.
5. Line 12 = $4,400 − $0 = $4,400.
6. Worksheet A = $1,500 − $0 (no earlier nonrefundable credits) = $1,500.
7. Line 14 = min($4,400, $1,500) = $1,500. This becomes Form 1040 line 19.
8. Early-exit checks: 2 > 0 qualifying children, no Form 2555, no Individual Taxpayer Identification Number, did not elect to opt out, line 16a check below.
9. Line 16a = max(0, $4,400 − $1,500) = $2,900. Not zero — continue.
10. Line 16b = 2 × $1,700 = $3,400. Line 17 = min($2,900, $3,400) = $2,900.
11. Line 18a = $60,000 + $0 = $60,000. Line 19 = $60,000 − $2,500 = $57,500. Line 20 = $57,500 × 15% = $8,625.
12. Routing: line 16b = $3,400 < $5,100 AND not Puerto Rico → line 27 = min($2,900, $8,625) = **$2,900**.
13. Part II-B is skipped.
14. **Form 1040 line 28 = $2,900.**

The taxpayer claims $1,500 nonrefundable Child Tax Credit on line 19 and $2,900 refundable Additional Child Tax Credit on line 28. The line 28 amount flows to line 32, then to line 33 (total payments), and increases the refund by $2,900.

### 6b. The orchestrator ordering

The orchestrator must run several helpers BEFORE `computeSchedule8812` so that the upstream values are populated when Schedule 8812 reads them. Per the G1 fix (recorded in `lines/19.md §10` and locked in 2026-04-18):

1. `computeForm8863` (education credits) runs FIRST so `payments.americanOpportunityCredit` is populated for Schedule 8812 line 24.
2. `computeLine27aEIC` (Earned Income Credit) is pre-set into `payments.earnedIncomeCredit` so Schedule 8812 line 24 sees a non-null value.
3. The Married Filing Separately defensive guard null-shadows `form2555Spouse` (per the 19 #1 fix) so a stale spouse Form 2555 does not erroneously block the taxpayer's Additional Child Tax Credit on Married Filing Separately.
4. Then `computeSchedule8812` runs, populating both line 14 (→ line 19) and line 27 (→ line 28).

### 6d. The output wiring

After the helper returns, the orchestrator promotes the Schedule 8812 line 27 value onto Form 1040 line 28:

```java
BigDecimal actc = schedule8812.getLine27ActcCredit();
if (actc != null && actc.compareTo(BigDecimal.ZERO) > 0) {
    Payments payments = form1040.getPayments();
    if (payments == null) {
        payments = new Payments();
        form1040.setPayments(payments);

    payments.setAdditionalChildTaxCredit(actc);

```

The frontend renderer then writes:

```typescript
values['line28_additional_child_tax_credit'] = this.formatAmount(form.payments?.additionalChildTaxCredit);
```

When the Additional Child Tax Credit is zero, the `additionalChildTaxCredit` field on `Payments` stays null, and the PDF cell is rendered blank (the formatter returns empty string for null values).

## 8. Validations and gating rules

The helper enforces several invariants by construction:

| Invariant | How enforced |
|---|---|
| Additional Child Tax Credit ≤ qualifying children × $1,700 | Line 17 = min(line 16a, line 16b); line 27 = min(line 17, line 26). Cannot exceed line 16b. |
| Additional Child Tax Credit ≤ excess credit over tax | Line 17 ≤ line 16a; line 27 ≤ line 17. |
| Additional Child Tax Credit ≥ 0 | All differences use `max(0, ...)` per Internal Revenue Service "if zero or less, enter -0-" rule. |
| Credit for Other Dependents never becomes refundable | Line 16b excludes Credit for Other Dependents dependents; only qualifying children counted. |
| Form 2555 blocks the entire Additional Child Tax Credit | Early-exit at line 30496 when `hasFiling2555` is true. |
| Married Filing Separately spouse's Form 2555 does not block | 19 #1 defensive guard null-shadows `form2555Spouse` at the call site. |
| Filer-identification rule failure blocks BOTH Child Tax Credit (line 19) and Additional Child Tax Credit (line 28) | BUG 3 2026-06-08 fix zeros both `line14CtcOdcCredit` and `line27ActcCredit`. |

The codebase emits two `TaxReturnFlag` advisory flags related to line 28:

- `FORM_8862_CTC_REQUIRED` — emitted when `ctcPreviouslyDenied=true` and Form 8862 is missing.
- `SCHEDULE_8812_ITIN_ACTC_BLOCKED` — emitted when the 2025 One Big Beautiful Bill Act filer-identification rule fails.

Both are advisory (non-blocking).

## 12. Reader's takeaway

For somebody with basic arithmetic and programming knowledge, the situation for line 28 can be summarized in four sentences:

1. **What it is:** A refundable credit on Form 1040 page 2 that lets families with qualifying children receive a payment from the Internal Revenue Service when the Child Tax Credit is larger than the tax they owe. The amount is capped at $1,700 per qualifying child for tax year 2025.

### 13f. Lesson captured

**The "unmapped_*" semantic name is a generator-script tell.** When the project's PDF semantic field map contains a name starting with `unmapped_`, that almost always indicates a generator-script path mismatch: the raw IRS field path the script processed did not exactly match any entry in the script's mapping table, and the generator fell through to its default. This is structurally similar to the line 27b mislabeling closed in `XLS/Computations/27b.md §13` (where the misleading name was `line27a_from_schedule_eic` rather than `unmapped_*`) — both originated in the generator's mapping table not being kept in sync with the actual IRS PDF structure.

**Diagnostic shortcut to add to the workflow:** when documenting any line that has Acroform checkboxes (typically the EIC family on line 27, the ACTC opt-out on line 28, the Schedule R checkbox on line 19, etc.), grep the semantic CSV for `unmapped_*` near the line of interest. Each `unmapped_*` is a candidate for a Gap-BB-style rename closure. The grep should run AGAINST THE ACTUAL CSV, not against the generator's mapping table — the table only shows what the generator INTENDED to produce, not what it actually emitted.
