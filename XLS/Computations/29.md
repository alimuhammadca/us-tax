## Quick orientation

The Form 1040 label for line 29 (per the actual 2025 IRS Form 1040 page 2):

> **"29   American opportunity credit from Form 8863, line 8 ............... 29"**

In one sentence: line 29 is the **refundable** portion of the American Opportunity Tax Credit — a dollar amount carried directly from Form 8863 line 8 onto Form 1040 page 2. The Internal Revenue Service treats it as a payment (just like withholding): if it exceeds the tax owed, the excess is paid back to the taxpayer as a refund. The companion non-refundable portion of the same credit appears on Schedule 3 line 3 and is the topic of `XLS\Computations\20.md` rather than this file.

Four facts to keep in mind throughout:

1. **Line 29 is only the refundable share of the American Opportunity Tax Credit.** It is not the Lifetime Learning Credit (which is always non-refundable and flows only through Schedule 3 line 3). It is not the full education credit. It is not the per-student maximum amount. It is the 40 percent slice of an already-computed Form 8863 amount, unless a specific under-age-24 restriction reduces it to zero.

2. **It comes only from Form 8863 — never re-derived on Form 1040.** Form 1040 line 29 is a transcription of Form 8863 line 8. If Form 8863 line 8 is zero or blank, Form 1040 line 29 is zero or blank. Recomputing line 29 independently from input fields outside Form 8863 would be wrong.

3. **The same Form 8863 helper also produces the non-refundable line 19 amount.** A single helper computes both line 8 (refundable, going to Form 1040 line 29) and line 19 (non-refundable, going to Schedule 3 line 3 then Form 1040 line 20). The two outputs cannot be split apart because the non-refundable line 9 begins as `line 7 - line 8`, and the Credit Limit Worksheet that produces line 19 depends on line 18 which includes line 9 plus the phased Lifetime Learning Credit.

4. **Line 29 flows into line 32 (other refundable credits subtotal) and from there into line 33 (total payments).** It does not directly reduce tax. It reduces the amount the taxpayer owes by increasing the total-payments side of the return.

The equation:

```text
Form 1040 line 29 = Form 8863 line 8
```

Form 8863 line 8 is itself the result of a multi-step computation across Part I, Part II, and Part III of Form 8863, described in the rest of this document.

## 1. The Internal Revenue Service canonical computation

The American Opportunity Tax Credit is computed in three parts of Form 8863:

- **Part III** — Student and Educational Institution Information. For each student, the disqualifier questions on lines 23-26 are answered. If the student survives those gates, the per-student credit on line 30 is computed from the adjusted qualified education expenses.
- **Part I** — Refundable American Opportunity Credit. The per-student line 30 amounts are summed to line 1, the Modified Adjusted Gross Income phaseout fraction is applied (lines 2 through 7), and 40 percent of the phased amount becomes the refundable share on line 8. The under-age-24 restriction can force line 8 to zero and shift the entire amount to the non-refundable side.
- **Part II** — Non-refundable Education Credits. The Lifetime Learning Credit and the non-refundable share of the American Opportunity Tax Credit are combined, then capped at the remaining tax liability via the Credit Limit Worksheet. The result is line 19 (carried to Schedule 3 line 3, not line 29).

For Form 1040 line 29 only Part III and Part I matter. Part II is described briefly here because the same helper computes it.

### 1a. Eligibility gates (must all pass before any credit is computed)

In plain English, before a single dollar can flow to line 29:

1. The taxpayer's filing status must not be Married Filing Separately. Per Internal Revenue Code section 25A(g)(6), spouses who file separately cannot claim any education credit.
2. The taxpayer must not be claimed as a dependent on another person's return.
3. The taxpayer (and the spouse on a joint return) must not be a non-resident alien for any part of the year unless the resident-treatment election is in place.
4. The taxpayer must have a Taxpayer Identification Number issued by the due date of the return including extensions. A Taxpayer Identification Number obtained later — for example, on an amended return — cannot revive the credit.
5. The Modified Adjusted Gross Income must be below the upper phaseout ceiling described in section 1c below.
6. If the American Opportunity Tax Credit was denied or reduced in a prior year (after 2015) for any reason other than a math or clerical error, Form 8862 Part IV must be completed and the per-student eligibility column must mark the student as eligible.

### 1b. Modified Adjusted Gross Income for Form 8863

In plain English: start with Adjusted Gross Income (Form 1040 line 11b), then add back four amounts of excluded income that would otherwise let high earners with excluded income claim the credit:

```text
magiForPhaseout
   = form1040.line11bAmountFromLine11aAdjustedGrossIncome
   + magiAddBackForm2555ExcludedIncome      // foreign earned income exclusion (Form 2555)
   + magiAddBackForm2555HousingExclusion    // foreign housing exclusion (Form 2555)
   + magiAddBackForm4563ExcludedIncome      // American Samoa exclusion (Form 4563)
   + magiAddBackPuertoRicoExcludedIncome    // bona fide Puerto Rico resident excluded income
```

These four add-backs are the same four listed in the 2025 Form 8863 instructions for line 3.

### 1c. The Modified Adjusted Gross Income phaseout (Form 8863 Part I lines 2-6 / Part II lines 14-18)

In plain English, the credit is full when income is below a lower threshold, is reduced linearly within a narrow band, and is zero at or above an upper threshold. The thresholds are fixed by statute (Internal Revenue Code section 25A(d)) and are **not** adjusted for inflation:

| Filing status | Lower threshold (full credit at or below) | Upper threshold (no credit at or above) | Phaseout band width |
|---|---|---|---|
| Married Filing Jointly | $160,000 | $180,000 | $20,000 |
| Single, Head of Household, Qualifying Surviving Spouse, Married Filing Separately | $80,000 | $90,000 | $10,000 |

The phaseout fraction is computed:

```text
phaseoutUpper   = $180,000 if Married Filing Jointly, else $90,000
phaseoutRange   = $20,000  if Married Filing Jointly, else $10,000

If magiForPhaseout >= phaseoutUpper:
   phaseoutFraction = 0
Else:
   numerator = phaseoutUpper - magiForPhaseout
   phaseoutFraction = min(1, numerator / phaseoutRange)
   Round to at least 3 decimal places.
```

The 2025 Form 8863 instruction for the phaseout division (line 6 and line 18) is: "Divide ... Enter the result as a decimal (rounded to at least three places)." This is ordinary half-up rounding, not truncation.

Plain-language consequence: a Married Filing Jointly couple with $170,000 of Modified Adjusted Gross Income has a fraction of $10,000 / $20,000 = 0.500 — exactly half the credit. A single filer at $85,000 has $5,000 / $10,000 = 0.500 — exactly half.

### 1d. Per-student American Opportunity Credit amount (Form 8863 Part III lines 20-30)

For each student listed in Part III, the following gates run in sequence. If any gate fails, the student is removed from the AOTC computation (the student may still be claimed for the Lifetime Learning Credit on line 31 by re-choosing the credit type, but the same student cannot generate both credits in the same year):

```text
line 23 — "Has the American opportunity credit been claimed for this student for any 4 prior tax years?"
   If YES → Stop. Skip lines 24-30 for this student.

line 24 — "Was the student enrolled at least half-time for at least one academic period that began (or is treated as having begun) in 2025 in a program leading to a postsecondary degree, certificate, or other recognized postsecondary educational credential?"
   If NO  → Stop. Skip lines 24-30 for this student.

line 25 — "Did the student complete the first 4 years of postsecondary education before 2025?"
   If YES → Stop. Skip lines 24-30 for this student.

line 26 — "Was the student convicted, before the end of 2025, of a felony for possession or distribution of a controlled substance?"
   If YES → Stop. Skip lines 24-30 for this student.
```

If the student passes all four gates, the per-student credit is computed from the adjusted qualified education expenses. "Adjusted" means already reduced by tax-free educational assistance such as scholarships, Pell grants, employer-paid assistance, and Department of Veterans Affairs assistance — the user enters the post-reduction amount.

```text
adjustedQualifiedEducationExpenses = expenses entered by user, already reduced by tax-free assistance

line 27 = min(adjustedQualifiedEducationExpenses, 4000)
   In plain English: enter the smaller of the expenses or $4,000.

line 28 = max(0, line 27 - 2000)
   In plain English: subtract $2,000 from line 27. If zero or less, enter zero.

line 29Form8863 = line 28 * 0.25
   In plain English: multiply line 28 by 25 percent. (This is the Form 8863 line 29, not the Form 1040 line 29 that this document is about.)

line 30 = IF line 28 == 0 THEN line 27 ELSE (line 29Form8863 + 2000)
   In plain English: this is the line where the floor versus formula split occurs. Reading the 2025 Form 8863 verbatim: "If line 28 is zero, enter the amount from line 27. Otherwise, add $2,000 to the amount on line 29 and enter the total." Equivalently:
     - If the student's expenses are at most $2,000, line 30 equals the expenses themselves (the 100 percent bracket only).
     - If the student's expenses exceed $2,000, line 30 equals $2,000 plus 25 percent of the next slice up to $4,000.
   The maximum value of line 30 per student is $2,500 (reached at $4,000 of expenses).
```

Concrete examples:

| Adjusted qualified education expenses | line 27 | line 28 | line 29Form8863 | line 30 (per-student) |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| $0     | $0     | $0     | $0     | $0     |
| $500   | $500   | $0     | $0     | $500   |
| $1,000 | $1,000 | $0     | $0     | $1,000 |
| $2,000 | $2,000 | $0     | $0     | $2,000 |
| $2,500 | $2,500 | $500   | $125   | $2,125 |
| $3,000 | $3,000 | $1,000 | $250   | $2,250 |
| $4,000 | $4,000 | $2,000 | $500   | $2,500 |
| $5,000 | $4,000 | $2,000 | $500   | $2,500 |

### 1e. Form 8863 Part I — Refundable American Opportunity Credit

```text
line 1Form8863 = sum of all per-student line 30 amounts across taxpayer + spouse students (MFJ only)

line 2Form8863 = phaseoutUpper from section 1c
line 3Form8863 = magiForPhaseout from section 1b
line 4Form8863 = max(0, line 2Form8863 - line 3Form8863)
line 5Form8863 = phaseoutRange from section 1c
line 6Form8863 = min(1, line 4Form8863 / line 5Form8863) rounded to at least 3 decimals = phaseoutFraction

line 7Form8863 = line 1Form8863 * line 6Form8863
   In plain English: the phased-out total American Opportunity Credit across all students.

line 8Form8863 = the refundable share computation:
   If the under-age-24 refundable restriction applies:
      line 8Form8863 = 0
      line 9Form8863 = line 7Form8863      (full credit shifts to the non-refundable side)
   Otherwise:
      line 8Form8863 = round(line 7Form8863 * 0.40)
      line 9Form8863 = line 7Form8863 - line 8Form8863    (approximately 60 percent stays for line 19)

Form 1040 line 29 = line 8Form8863
```

### 1f. The under-age-24 refundable restriction (Form 8863 line 7 — special box)

The 2025 Form 8863 instructions for line 7 list a compound test. The refundable portion is forced to zero if **all** of the following are true:

1. The taxpayer was either:
   - under age 18 at the end of 2025, **or**
   - age 18 at the end of 2025 and the taxpayer's earned income was less than half of the taxpayer's support, **or**
   - over age 18 and under age 24 at the end of 2025, **and** was a full-time student, **and** earned income was less than half of support.
2. **AND** at least one of the taxpayer's parents was alive at the end of 2025.
3. **AND** the taxpayer is not filing a joint return for 2025.

If all three conditions hold:

```text
Form 8863 line 8 = 0
Form 8863 line 9 = line 7Form8863    (whole credit moves to non-refundable side)
Check the box above Form 8863 line 8.
Form 1040 line 29 = 0
```

This is sometimes called "the kiddie-tax-like rule for AOTC". The plain-language idea is: a young taxpayer who is supported mostly by their parents should not receive a refundable check from the Internal Revenue Service for the education credit; if the credit applies at all, it should only reduce tax owed.

### 1g. Form 8862 recertification after prior disallowance

If the American Opportunity Tax Credit was disallowed or reduced in a prior tax year after 2015 for any reason other than a math or clerical error, the taxpayer must complete Form 8862 ("Information To Claim Certain Credits After Disallowance") Part IV and check the boxes for each student who is eligible. If Form 8862 Part IV is not filed:

- The American Opportunity Tax Credit is disallowed for the year. Form 1040 line 29 is zero.
- The Lifetime Learning Credit on Schedule 3 line 3 is not affected by the Form 8862 AOTC gate (it has its own gate).

If a Form 8862 disallowance was due to reckless or intentional disregard of the rules, the credit is barred for 2 years; if fraudulent, 10 years.

### 1h. Form 1098-T and institution Employer Identification Number

In general, the taxpayer must have received Form 1098-T from the eligible educational institution to claim the credit. The 2025 Form 8863 instructions list specific exceptions, including:

- The institution was not required to furnish Form 1098-T.
- The institution was required to furnish Form 1098-T but failed to do so before the return was filed, **and** the taxpayer satisfied the request-and-cooperation steps in the instructions, **and** can substantiate enrollment and qualified expenses through other documentation.

The institution's Employer Identification Number must be entered on Form 8863 Part III line 22 for the American Opportunity Tax Credit (this is not required for the Lifetime Learning Credit).

### 1i. Credit Limit Worksheet (Form 8863 instructions) — for completeness

This worksheet does not directly produce line 29; it produces line 19 (non-refundable). It is mentioned here because the same helper computes both outputs:

```text
Worksheet line 1 = Form 8863 line 18
   line 18 = round(line 9Form8863 + (line 12Form8863 * phaseoutFraction))
   In plain English: non-refundable AOTC remainder plus phased Lifetime Learning Credit.
Worksheet line 2 = Form 1040 line 18 (totalTaxBeforeCredits = line16 + line17)
Worksheet line 3 = Schedule 3 line 1 (foreign tax credit)
                 + Schedule 3 line 2 (credit for child and dependent care)
                 + Schedule 3 line 6d (credit for the elderly or disabled)
                 + Schedule 3 line 6l (amount from Form 8978 Schedule A line 6)
Worksheet line 4 = max(0, Worksheet line 2 - Worksheet line 3)
Form 8863 line 19 = min(Worksheet line 1, Worksheet line 4)

Schedule 3 line 3 = Form 8863 line 19
Form 1040 line 20 = Schedule 3 line 8 (which includes Schedule 3 line 3)
```

### 1j. Lifetime Learning Credit (Form 8863 Part II lines 10-12) — for completeness

This does not flow to Form 1040 line 29; it appears here because the same helper computes it:

```text
line 10Form8863 = sum of adjusted qualified education expenses across all Lifetime Learning Credit students
line 11Form8863 = min(line 10Form8863, 10000)   // return-level cap, not per-student
line 12Form8863 = round(line 11Form8863 * 0.20) // tentative Lifetime Learning Credit
```

The Lifetime Learning Credit is then phased through the same phaseout fraction as the American Opportunity Tax Credit.

### 1k. Form 1040 downstream wiring

```text
Form 1040 line 29 = Form 8863 line 8
Form 1040 line 32 = line 27a (Earned Income Credit) + line 28 (Additional Child Tax Credit) + line 29 + line 30 (refundable adoption credit) + line 31 (Schedule 3 line 15)
Form 1040 line 33 = line 25d (withholding) + line 26 (estimated tax payments) + line 32
```

Important guardrail: line 29 is included once through line 32. It is never added again separately into line 33.

## 2. The as-implemented computation in this codebase

The single computation entry point is `computeForm8863` in `TaxReturnComputeService.java` (currently at line 26125). It is called from `prepare()` at line 1749, after Form 8862 is computed and before Schedule 8812 runs. The output goes into two destinations through two assignments:

- `Form8863.line8RefundableAotc` → `payments.americanOpportunityCredit` at line 26841, which is the field that becomes Form 1040 line 29.
- `Form8863.line19NonrefundableEducationCredits` → `Schedule3NonrefundableCredits.educationCredits` via `applyForm8863ToSchedule3` at line 1750, which becomes Schedule 3 line 3 and then contributes to Form 1040 line 20.

The relevant input fields all come from two personal forms — `education-credits-taxpayer` and `education-credits-spouse` — plus the upstream-computed Adjusted Gross Income and total-tax-before-credits values.

### 2a. As-implemented eligibility gates

The code at lines 26136-26165 checks the following in order:

1. `educationCreditsTaxpayer != null && claimsEducationCreditsOnReturn == true` — if not, returns `null` (no Form 8863 at all).
2. If `aotcPreviouslyDenied == true`:
   - If `form8862 == null` or `form8862.isClaimsAOTC() == false`: emit `FORM_8862_AOTC_REQUIRED` non-blocking flag and set `aotcStudentEligible = empty list` (all students blocked from AOTC; Lifetime Learning Credit unaffected).
   - Else: use `form8862.getAotcStudentEligible()` as a per-index eligibility list.
3. If filing status is "Married filing separately" (case-insensitive): emit `EDUCATION_CREDITS_MFS_INELIGIBLE` non-blocking flag and return `null` (no Form 8863 computed at all).
4. `isMfj = "Married filing jointly".equalsIgnoreCase(status)` — used for both phaseout threshold selection and spouse-student merge.

The code does **not** check:

- Whether the taxpayer is claimed as a dependent on another return.
- Whether the taxpayer or spouse is a non-resident alien.
- Whether the Taxpayer Identification Number was issued by the due date of the return (no due-date semantics in the data model).
- Whether the student's Taxpayer Identification Number was issued by the due date.

These omissions are documented gaps; see section 4.

### 2b. As-implemented Modified Adjusted Gross Income

Code at lines 26167-26177:

```java
agi = form1040.getAdjustments().getLine11bAmountFromLine11aAdjustedGrossIncome()  // fallback 0
magi = agi
     + magiAddBackPuertoRicoExcludedIncome   // from educationCreditsTaxpayer personal form
     + magiAddBackForm2555ExcludedIncome     // from educationCreditsTaxpayer personal form
     + magiAddBackForm2555HousingExclusion   // from educationCreditsTaxpayer personal form
     + magiAddBackForm4563ExcludedIncome     // from educationCreditsTaxpayer personal form
```

All four add-back fields are read from the `education-credits-taxpayer` personal form as user-entered amounts. The user-interface boolean `hasMagiAddBacks` is a display gate only; the back end sums whichever numeric add-back fields are non-null. This add-back set matches the four add-backs in the canonical Form 8863 instructions for line 3.

### 2c. As-implemented phaseout fraction

Code at lines 26180-26195:

```java
phaseoutUpper = isMfj ? 180000 : 90000
phaseoutRange = isMfj ? 20000  : 10000

If magi >= phaseoutUpper:
   phaseoutFraction = 0
Else if (phaseoutUpper - magi) >= phaseoutRange:
   phaseoutFraction = 1
Else:
   phaseoutFraction = (phaseoutUpper - magi) / phaseoutRange, divided with scale 3 and RoundingMode.DOWN (truncated to 3 decimals)
```

The code truncates the fraction with `RoundingMode.DOWN`. The 2025 Form 8863 line 6 instruction reads "rounded to at least three places" — ordinary rounding rather than truncation. The numerical effect is small (at most one part in 1,000 of the credit) but is technically incorrect.

The Qualifying Surviving Spouse case is handled correctly: `isMfj` strictly matches only "Married filing jointly", so a Qualifying Surviving Spouse uses the Single thresholds ($80,000 / $90,000), which is the IRS rule. (This was a real bug — gap G1 in the dependencies file — fixed on 2026-04-20.)

### 2d. As-implemented per-student loop

Code at lines 26220-26293. Taxpayer students are read from `educationCreditsTaxpayer.taxpayerEducationCreditStudents`. Spouse students are merged only when `isMfj == true` **and** `spouseHasAdditionalEducationCreditStudents == true` on the `education-credits-spouse` form.

For each student, an output view (`Form8863Student`) is always added to the result list (so the Portable Document Format export retains the student name even if the student is later disqualified). The view fields populated:

- `studentName` from `studentFirstNameLine20 + " " + studentLastNameLine20`
- `studentSsn` from `studentTinLine21`
- `creditType` from `creditTypeRequested` (the string "aotc" or "llc")
- `adjustedQualifiedEducationExpenses` from the user-entered amount
- `institution1Name` from `institution1NameLine22`
- `institution1Address` from `institution1AddressLine22`

If `creditTypeRequested == "aotc"`, the AOTC disqualifier gates run:

1. Form 8862 per-index eligibility check (only if `aotcPreviouslyDenied == true`).
2. `aotcClaimedFourPriorYearsLine23 == true` → skip student (Form 8863 line 23 disqualifier).
3. `studentWasAtLeastHalfTimeLine24 != true` → skip student (Form 8863 line 24 disqualifier).
4. `studentCompletedFirstFourYearsBefore2025Line25 == true` → skip student (Form 8863 line 25 disqualifier).
5. `studentHadFelonyDrugConvictionLine26 == true` → skip student (Form 8863 line 26 disqualifier).

If the student passes all four gates, the per-student credit is computed:

```java
line27 = min(adjustedQualifiedEducationExpenses, 4000)
line28 = max(0, line27 - 2000)
line29 = line28 * 0.25
line30 = line29 + 2000      // <-- always adds 2000; see gap G-NEW-1
sv.setPerStudentLine30(roundMoney(line30))
totalAotcBeforePhaseout += line30
```

If `creditTypeRequested == "llc"`:

```java
sv.setPerStudentLlcExpenses(expenses)
totalLlcExpenses += expenses
```

### 2e. As-implemented Part I (refundable side)

Code at lines 26295-26311:

```java
line1 = totalAotcBeforePhaseout
line7 = round(line1 * phaseoutFraction)

If refundableAotcRestrictionApplies == true:
   line8 = 0
   line9 = line7
Else:
   line8 = round(line7 * 0.40)
   line9 = line7 - line8
```

The under-age-24 restriction is gated by a single user-entered boolean `refundableAotcRestrictionApplies` on the `education-credits-taxpayer` personal form. The code does not validate the three-part compound test from section 1f of this document; it trusts whatever value the taxpayer answered on the screening form.

### 2f. As-implemented Part II (non-refundable side)

Code at lines 26313-26321:

```java
line10 = totalLlcExpenses
line11 = min(line10, 10000)
line12 = round(line11 * 0.20)
llcPhased = round(line12 * phaseoutFraction)
line18 = round(line9 + llcPhased)
```

### 2g. As-implemented Credit Limit Worksheet

Code at lines 26323-26339:

```java
totalTaxBeforeCredits = form1040.taxAndCredits.totalTaxBeforeCredits   // = Form 1040 line 18
priorCredits = schedule3.foreignTaxCredit            // Schedule 3 line 1
             + schedule3.childDependentCareCredit    // Schedule 3 line 2
             + schedule3.elderlyDisabledCredit       // Schedule 3 line 6d
             + schedule3.amountFromForm8978Line14    // Schedule 3 line 6l

line19 = min(line18, creditLimit)
```

The four Schedule 3 components that are subtracted match exactly the four lines the 2025 Form 8863 Credit Limit Worksheet calls out. The code does not subtract any other Schedule 3 credit (saver's credit, residential energy credits, clean vehicle credits, etc.), which is correct.

### 2h. As-implemented wiring to Form 1040 line 29

Code at lines 26839-26842:

```java

        && form8863.getLine8RefundableAotc().compareTo(BigDecimal.ZERO) > 0) {
```

### Gap G-NEW-1 (HIGH severity — corrects credit dollars)

**Per-student line 30 over-states the credit when adjusted qualified education expenses are at most $2,000.**

**IRS canonical (Form 8863 line 30 verbatim):**

> "If line 28 is zero, enter the amount from line 27. Otherwise, add $2,000 to the amount on line 29 and enter the total."

**As implemented (`TaxReturnComputeService.java:26283`):**

```java
BigDecimal line30 = line29.add(new BigDecimal("2000")); // $2,000 minimum; max $2,500 per student
```

**Numerical examples of the divergence:**

| Adjusted qualified education expenses | IRS-correct line 30 | Code line 30 | Code error |
|---|---|---|---|
| $0    | $0    | $2,000 | **+$2,000 too high** |
| $500  | $500  | $2,000 | **+$1,500 too high** |
| $1,000 | $1,000 | $2,000 | **+$1,000 too high** |
| $1,500 | $1,500 | $2,000 | **+$500 too high** |
| $2,000 | $2,000 | $2,000 | $0 (matches) |
| $3,000 | $2,250 | $2,250 | $0 (matches) |
| $4,000 | $2,500 | $2,500 | $0 (matches) |

**Downstream effect on Form 1040 line 29 (assuming no phaseout, no under-24 restriction, single student):**

- Code line 30 = $2,000 → line 8 = round($2,000 × 40%) = $800. The taxpayer receives a refundable check for $800 that they are not entitled to.
- IRS line 30 = $500 → line 8 = $200. The correct refundable amount.
- Difference: **$600 over-refund per student** in the $500-expenses case.

**Why this slipped through:** unit tests `computeForm8863_aotcExpensesBelow2000_minimumCreditApplies` and the corresponding e2e test were written to **assert the buggy behavior** ($1,000 expenses → line 30 = $2,000, line 8 = $800). The tests codify the bug instead of catching it. This is a textbook violation of the project's `feedback_principled_diagnosis_over_test_tweaking` discipline — the test was matched to observed output rather than to the IRS form.

**Code fix sketch:**

```java
BigDecimal line30;
if (line28.compareTo(BigDecimal.ZERO) == 0) {
    line30 = line27;
} else {
    line30 = line29.add(new BigDecimal("2000"));
}
```

The unit and e2e tests must then be re-derived from the IRS form values (expenses $1,000 → line 30 = $1,000 → line 8 = $400, etc.) — not from the previous code output.

### Gap G-NEW-2 (MEDIUM severity — maintainability)

**The 2025 Internal Revenue Code section 25A constants are hardcoded inline rather than centralized in `ReferenceData.java`.**

The 14 constants in section 3 above all appear as `new BigDecimal("...")` literals inside `computeForm8863`. By contrast, the standard deduction, Qualified Business Income thresholds, Social Security taxable thresholds, and other widely used statutory amounts are centralized in `ReferenceData.java`. The omission means:

- Future statutory changes require a hunt through `computeForm8863` rather than a one-line edit in `ReferenceData.java`.
- The constants are not visible to unit tests that want to parameterize boundary cases.

**Code fix sketch:** add `AOTC_*` and `LLC_*` constants to `ReferenceData.java` and reference them from `computeForm8863`.

### Gap G-NEW-3 (MEDIUM severity — IRS rounding rule)

**Phaseout fraction is truncated (`RoundingMode.DOWN`) where the IRS form instruction calls for ordinary rounding.**

The 2025 Form 8863 line 6 and line 18 instructions read: "Divide ... Enter the result as a decimal (rounded to at least three places)." This is half-up rounding, not truncation.

**As implemented (`TaxReturnComputeService.java:26193`):**

```java
phaseoutFraction = numerator.divide(phaseoutRange, 3, java.math.RoundingMode.DOWN);
```

**Numerical effect:** for a Single filer with Modified Adjusted Gross Income of $85,432, the IRS fraction is $4,568 / $10,000 = 0.4568 → rounded to 0.457. The code produces 0.456. A $2,500 credit becomes $1,140 (code) vs $1,142.50 (IRS). The under-rounding ranges from $0 to about $2.50 per $2,500 of credit — at most about a tenth of one percent of the credit.

The technically correct fix is `RoundingMode.HALF_UP`.

### Gap G-NEW-4 (MEDIUM severity — IRS-required validation missing)

**No Taxpayer Identification Number due-date validation.**

The Internal Revenue Code section 25A(g)(1) and Form 8863 instructions require that both the taxpayer and the student have a Taxpayer Identification Number issued by the due date of the return including extensions. The current data model has no "issuance date" field for either the taxpayer's Social Security Number or the student's `studentTinLine21`. The code presumes a present non-empty Taxpayer Identification Number is sufficient.

**Effect:** a taxpayer who applied for an Individual Taxpayer Identification Number after the due date could be silently allowed the American Opportunity Credit; the Internal Revenue Service would later disallow it on examination.

This is a deferred item — the canonical fix requires either an "issued date" field or a screening boolean ("Was this TIN issued by the due date of the 2025 return, including extensions?") with the appropriate help text.

### Gap G-NEW-5 (LOW severity — IRS-required validation missing)

**No dependent-claimed-by-another-return validation.**

The Internal Revenue Code section 25A(g)(3) bars the credit when the taxpayer is claimed as a dependent on another person's return. The data model has no field for this and the code does not check.

**Effect:** a young taxpayer who is in fact claimed as a dependent by a parent could file with the American Opportunity Credit claimed; the Internal Revenue Service would later disallow it.

The fix is a screening boolean on the `education-credits-taxpayer` form: "Are you claimed as a dependent on another person's return?" If yes, return null with a non-blocking flag.

### Gap G-NEW-6 (LOW severity — under-24 restriction is self-reported)

**The compound under-age-24 refundable restriction is collapsed to a single user-entered boolean.**

The IRS-canonical test in section 1f has three conjunctive conditions, the first of which is itself a three-way disjunction with age, full-time-student, support, and earned-income inputs. The code reads a single boolean `refundableAotcRestrictionApplies` that the user must self-assess. This is documented as gap G4 in the dependencies file and is deferred.

**Effect:** the user may answer incorrectly. The credit could be too high (forgot they're a dependent) or too low (the taxpayer is over 24 and answered "yes" by mistake). The fix is a four-question wizard inside the personal form that computes the boolean from age, parent-alive, joint-return, support, and earned-income inputs.

### Gap G-NEW-7 (LOW severity — Form 1098-T receipt not enforced)

**The user-interface collects 1098-T receipt status but the back end never enforces it.**

The `education-credits-taxpayer` form has the fields `receivedAny1098TForClaimedStudents`, `uploadedAll1098TStatementsForClaimedStudents`, `usingAlternativeEnrollmentDocumentationForAnyStudent`, and per-student `institution1Received1098T` / `institution1PriorYear1098TBox7Checked`. Per `computeForm8863`, none of these is read by the compute. The credit is allowed regardless of whether 1098-T was received or whether the alternative documentation election was made.

**Effect:** none on the dollar amount today (the user enters expenses regardless), but the audit trail in the saved data does not gate the credit. A taxpayer who has no 1098-T and no alternative documentation can still claim the credit through this system; the Internal Revenue Service may disallow it on examination.

The minimum fix is a non-blocking flag: when `receivedAny1098TForClaimedStudents == false` and `usingAlternativeEnrollmentDocumentationForAnyStudent == false`, emit `AOTC_1098T_NOT_RECEIVED_NO_ALTERNATIVE`. The fuller fix is to also block per-student computation when the receipt status is missing.

### Gap G-NEW-8 (LOW severity — Form 8862 flag is non-blocking)

The Form 8862 gate at `TaxReturnComputeService.java:26147-26149` emits a non-blocking flag. When `aotcPreviouslyDenied == true` and Form 8862 Part IV is not filed, the AOTC dollar amount is correctly forced to zero (good), but the flag does not block the return from being filed. A user who reads the flag as advisory may file with the AOTC claim suppressed, which is correct, but if the user also has unrelated reasons to dispute the prior denial, they may not realize Form 8862 is required to claim the credit at all.

This is a user-experience issue rather than a computational defect.

### Gap G-NEW-9 (LOW severity — AOTC-disqualified student does not auto-fall-back to Lifetime Learning Credit)

When a student requests `creditTypeRequested == "aotc"` but is disqualified by lines 23-26, the code at `TaxReturnComputeService.java:26277` short-circuits with `continue` — the student is dropped from both AOTC and Lifetime Learning Credit. The IRS rule is correct: you cannot claim both credits for the same student in the same year, and the user's choice was AOTC, so dropping is correct. But the user may not realize they should have chosen "llc" instead. This is a user-experience issue.

### Gap G-NEW-10 (LOW severity — non-resident alien gate missing)

**No non-resident alien check.**

The Internal Revenue Code section 25A(g)(7) bars the credit for non-resident aliens unless the resident-treatment election is made. The data model has no non-resident-alien status field on either taxpayer or spouse and the code does not check. Effect: the IRS would disallow on examination.

### Gap G-NEW-11 (LOW severity — phaseout examples missing from output PDF)

PDF export at lines 165-176 of the dependencies file confirms that Form 8863 Part I lines 4 and 5 (the phaseout threshold $180,000 / $90,000 and the range $20,000 / $10,000) are not filled into the PDF output even though they are computed in the helper. The user receives an incomplete Form 8863 Page 1 if they print the return. This is a presentation gap (the dollar amount on line 29 is correct), but a IRS PDF compliance gap nonetheless.

### Gap G-NEW-12 (MEDIUM severity — duplicate manual entry; under-states MAGI when education-credits add-back fields are blank)

**The four MAGI add-back fields on the education-credits personal form are user-entered, even when the same return also computes Form 2555 (foreign earned income exclusion) or Form 4563 (American Samoa exclusion). When the user enters their foreign earned income exclusion on the foreign-earned-income personal form but forgets to also type the same dollar amount on the education-credits form, the MAGI at Form 8863 line 3 is under-stated and the credit is over-allowed.**

The 2025 Form 8863 instructions for line 3 read:

> "Enter the amount from Form 1040 or 1040-SR, line 11b. But if you're filing Form 2555 or 4563, or you're excluding income from Puerto Rico, see Pub. 970 for the amount to enter instead."

The "amount to enter instead" is Adjusted Gross Income plus the four excluded amounts (Form 2555 foreign earned income exclusion, Form 2555 housing exclusion, Form 4563 American Samoa exclusion, and Puerto Rico excluded income). The application already computes Form 2555 from the upstream `foreign-earned-income-taxpayer` and `foreign-earned-income-spouse` personal forms — that data is available to Form 8863 at compute time.

**Numerical example:** a Single taxpayer with $85,000 of W-2 wages and a $20,000 foreign earned income exclusion on Form 2555 has actual MAGI = $105,000 (above the $90,000 ceiling, no credit). The application without the auto-fill produces MAGI = $85,000 (phaseout fraction 0.500, half credit — line 8 = $500 for a single AOTC student at $4,000 of expenses). The user receives $500 of refundable credit they are not entitled to.

**Code fix:** read `foreignEarnedIncomeTaxpayer` and `foreignEarnedIncomeSpouse` (Married Filing Jointly only for the spouse) in `computeForm8863`. When both manual `magiAddBackForm2555ExcludedIncome` and `magiAddBackForm2555HousingExclusion` are null, call `computeForm2555ExclusionForSsWorksheet` (which sums Form 2555 line 45 + line 50) and add that to MAGI. Manual entries take precedence so saved returns are unaffected.

### Gap G-NEW-13 (LOW severity — presentation; printed Form 8863 page 2 has empty disqualifier checkboxes)

**Form 8863 Part III "Stop!" checkboxes (Part III lines 23, 24, 25, 26) are not rendered in the PDF output.**

The 2025 Form 8863 Part III asks four yes/no questions, each with a "Yes — Stop! Go to line 31 for this student" or "No — Go to line N" instruction. The user's answers are collected (`aotcClaimedFourPriorYearsLine23`, `studentWasAtLeastHalfTimeLine24`, `studentCompletedFirstFourYearsBefore2025Line25`, `studentHadFelonyDrugConvictionLine26`) and consumed by the compute, but the corresponding PDF AcroForm cells (`c2_5[0]`/`c2_5[1]` for line 23, `c2_6[0]`/`c2_6[1]` for line 24, `c2_7[0]`/`c2_7[1]` for line 25, `c2_8[0]`/`c2_8[1]` for line 26) are not filled. The user's printed Form 8863 page 2 has empty checkboxes where the IRS expects marks.

**Code fix:** expose the four disqualifier booleans on `Form8863Student.java`; copy them in the per-student loop in `computeForm8863`; render the appropriate Yes/No checkbox per answer in the frontend writer. Null answers leave both checkboxes empty so the printed form does not assert an answer the user did not give.

## 9. What this means for the audit

The single computational bug worth fixing immediately is **G-NEW-1**: per-student line 30 over-states the credit by exactly `$2,000 − expenses` whenever adjusted qualified education expenses are between $0 and $2,000. For a single student with $500 of expenses, the refundable check goes out at $800 instead of $200 — a $600 over-refund per affected student.

The bug is masked by two passing tests that codify it rather than catch it (`computeForm8863_aotcExpensesBelow2000_minimumCreditApplies` and the corresponding e2e). Both tests must be rewritten against the IRS Form 8863 line 30 verbatim wording before the code is changed, then the code change becomes a one-line `if (line28 == 0) ... else ...` branch.

The remaining gaps (G-NEW-2 through G-NEW-11) are real, but they do not affect dollar amounts for the common case. G-NEW-4 (Taxpayer Identification Number due-date), G-NEW-5 (claimed-as-dependent), G-NEW-7 (1098-T received), and G-NEW-10 (non-resident alien) are all IRS-required eligibility validations that are silently passing today; they should be added as either screening fields or non-blocking flags so the saved return data carries the user's attestation. G-NEW-2 (constants in `ReferenceData.java`) and G-NEW-3 (ordinary rounding rather than truncation) are maintainability and precision improvements with no impact on the common credit amount.
