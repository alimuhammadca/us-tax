# Form 1040 — Line 2a — Tax-exempt interest

**Tax year: 2025**

This document explains how the dollar amount that goes on **Line 2a** of Form 1040 is calculated. Line 2a reports the total **tax-exempt interest** the taxpayer (and the spouse on a joint return) received during the year. *Tax-exempt* means the interest is not subject to federal income tax — typically interest on bonds issued by U.S. state and local governments under Internal Revenue Code §103. Line 2a is **informational only**: it does NOT flow into total income or taxable income. It does feed two downstream computations: Form 6251 (Alternative Minimum Tax) and the Publication 915 worksheet that determines the taxable portion of Social Security benefits.

The document is organized in three parts:

1. **The IRS rule.** What the controlling IRS publications say must happen. This is the source of truth.
2. **The code implementation.** What the application's backend Java code currently does.
3. **The comparison.** A side-by-side check showing whether the code follows the IRS rule honestly, and where the two diverge.

### 1.2 The basic rule

From the 2025 Instructions for Form 1040, Line 2a:

> **Line 2a — Tax-exempt interest.** Enter the total of any:
>
> - Tax-exempt interest from box 8 of Form 1099-INT (including any tax-exempt interest received as a nominee that you must allocate to others).
> - Exempt-interest dividends from box 12 of Form 1099-DIV.
> - Tax-exempt original issue discount (OID) from box 11 of Form 1099-OID.
> - Other tax-exempt interest you received but didn't receive a Form 1099 for.

In plain English: Line 2a is the **total tax-exempt interest** the filer received, regardless of which information return it appeared on. It is reported for informational purposes (the IRS reconciles totals against 1099 information returns) but it does NOT add to total income on Line 9.

### 1.3 What counts as tax-exempt interest

A dollar of interest income qualifies for Line 2a when one of these is true:

| Source | Where it shows up | Inclusion rule |
|---|---|---|
| Interest on a bond or note issued by a U.S. state, U.S. territory, the District of Columbia, or a political subdivision (city, county, school district) | **Form 1099-INT box 8** (Tax-exempt interest) | Included as-is |
| Original issue discount (OID) on a tax-exempt bond | **Form 1099-OID box 11** (Tax-exempt OID) | Included as-is |
| Stated periodic interest on a tax-exempt OID bond (interest the bond pays *in addition to* the discount) | **Form 1099-OID box 2** (Other periodic interest) **when the bond is a tax-exempt obligation** | Taxpayer or preparer classifies the tax-exempt portion; the rest of box 2 goes to Line 2b |
| Dividends from a mutual fund that holds tax-exempt bonds | **Form 1099-DIV box 12** (Exempt-interest dividends) | Included as-is |
| Tax-exempt interest the taxpayer received but no payer issued a 1099 for | Manual entry on the personal interest form | Included as-is |

### 1.4 The double-count carve-outs (very important)

Two information-return boxes are reported as **subsets** of a larger box. The IRS specifically warns that they must NOT be added on top of the larger box:

#### Carve-out A — Form 1099-INT box 9 is INSIDE box 8

**Form 1099-INT box 9** (Specified private activity bond interest) is the portion of the box 8 tax-exempt interest that comes from bonds the federal government classifies as *private activity bonds* under Internal Revenue Code §141. Box 9 is a flag for the Alternative Minimum Tax — that subset of tax-exempt interest is treated as a *preference item* on Form 6251 line 2g — but **box 9 is already part of box 8**. Adding box 9 on top of box 8 would double-count it for Line 2a.

#### Carve-out B — Form 1099-DIV box 13 is INSIDE box 12

**Form 1099-DIV box 13** (Specified private activity bond interest dividends) is the portion of the box 12 exempt-interest dividends that came from private activity bonds. Same shape as box 9 — it is a Form 6251 line 2g preference flag, NOT a separate Line 2a contributor. **Box 13 is already part of box 12**.

#### Why this matters

Both subsets feed Form 6251 line 2g (Alternative Minimum Tax preference) separately. The IRS uses the subset boxes to identify the AMT portion. The TOTAL box 8 / box 12 still goes to Line 2a in full. The AMT subset is a *flag*, not an additional amount.

### 1.5 Premium adjustment for tax-exempt bonds

**Form 1099-INT box 13** (Bond premium on tax-exempt bond) is an adjustment, not an addition. When the taxpayer purchased a tax-exempt bond at a price above face value (a *premium*), the premium must be amortized over the bond's life and used to reduce the reported tax-exempt interest. The reduction happens once:

- If the payer (broker) already netted the premium and reported only the post-premium box 8 amount, box 13 is informational and no further adjustment applies.
- If the payer reported the gross box 8 amount and showed the premium separately in box 13, the filer subtracts box 13 from box 8 to get the net tax-exempt interest.

The IRS rule: **reduce premium at most once.** The application's `payerAlreadyReportedNetInterestOrOid` flag on the personal interest form is how the taxpayer attests which side of this fork applies.

The same logic applies to acquisition premium on tax-exempt OID bonds (Form 1099-OID), though the boxes are different.

### 1.6 The Form 1099-OID box 2 classification edge case

**Form 1099-OID box 2** (Other periodic interest) is the stated periodic interest a bond pays in addition to its discount. Whether box 2 belongs on Line 2a or Line 2b depends entirely on whether the bond itself is tax-exempt:

- If the bond is a **tax-exempt** OID bond → box 2 belongs on Line 2a.
- If the bond is a **taxable** OID bond → box 2 belongs on Line 2b.

The application asks the taxpayer to classify the tax-exempt portion of box 2 via the personal-form field `taxExemptStatedInterestFrom1099OidBox2`. The remainder is treated as taxable and goes to Line 2b.

### 1.7 Nominee tax-exempt interest

If a Form 1099-INT was issued in the taxpayer's name for interest that actually belongs to someone else (the taxpayer is a *nominee*), the taxpayer must:

1. Report the full box 8 amount on their own Line 2a (because the 1099 is in their name).
2. Then report a *nominee distribution* subtraction on Schedule B identifying the actual owner.

Per IRS Schedule B instructions, nominee interest **independently triggers Schedule B**, even if total interest is below the $1,500 threshold described in §1.10 below.

### 1.8 The complete IRS equation (plain English)

Putting all parts together:

```text
For each Form 1099-INT belonging to the taxpayer or (on a joint return) the spouse:
    contribution_INT = box 8 (Tax-exempt interest)
    if box 13 (Bond premium on tax-exempt bond) is positive
       AND the payer did NOT already net the premium:
        contribution_INT = contribution_INT − box 13
    add contribution_INT to the running total
    capture box 9 (Specified private activity bond interest) into the
        Form 6251 line 2g pile (separately; not added to Line 2a)

For each Form 1099-OID belonging to the taxpayer or (on a joint return) the spouse:
    add box 11 (Tax-exempt OID) to the running total
    note box 2 (Other periodic interest); the taxpayer-classified
       tax-exempt portion of box 2 is added to the running total

For each Form 1099-DIV belonging to the taxpayer or (on a joint return) the spouse:
    add box 12 (Exempt-interest dividends) to the running total
    capture box 13 (Specified private activity bond interest dividends)
       into the Form 6251 line 2g pile (separately; not added to Line 2a)

Add any manual tax-exempt interest the taxpayer entered that
    didn't appear on a 1099.

If the taxpayer attested that the broker did NOT already net premium:
    if `taxExemptBondPremiumAdjustmentNotInStatements` is positive:
        running total = running total − that adjustment
    (Reduce tax-exempt premium at most once — the bond-premium
     adjustment is null-shadowed when the broker pre-netted.)

Line 2a = running total
```

On a **Married Filing Separately** return, only the filing taxpayer's own 1099-INT / 1099-OID / 1099-DIV entries contribute. The spouse's entries belong on the spouse's own separate return.

### 1.9 Whole-dollar rounding

The IRS allows whole-dollar rounding on Form 1040 as long as it is applied consistently across the return. The recommended practice is to keep cents while adding and round to whole dollars only when printing the final return.

### 1.10 Reference data (constants that may change year to year)

**Line 2a has no tax-year-specific numeric constant inside its own computation.** No rate, no threshold, no inflation-adjusted limit appears in the Line 2a math itself. Line 2a is a pure summation with classification rules.

Two adjacent constants that are referenced by Schedule B (which Line 2a triggers, alongside Line 2b):

| Reference data | Value for tax year 2025 | Source | Used where |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Schedule B taxable-interest threshold** | `$1,500` | 2025 Instructions for Schedule B | Schedule B is required when **taxable** interest (Line 2b) exceeds this. The threshold has been $1,500 for many years; the IRS adjusts it by Revenue Procedure |
| **Series EE / I U.S. savings bond exclusion (Form 8815)** | Inflation-indexed phaseout — not relevant to Line 2a | 2025 Instructions for Form 8815 | Relevant only to Line 2b (the exclusion reduces taxable interest, not tax-exempt interest) |

Schedule B is also required when ANY of the following are true (per the 2025 Schedule B instructions), even if taxable interest is below $1,500:

- Seller-financed mortgage interest exists
- Accrued interest from a bond exists
- OID is reported at less than the amount shown on Form 1099-OID
- Interest is reduced below the Form 1099-INT amount because of amortizable bond premium
- Series EE / I U.S. savings bond interest exclusion is claimed under Form 8815
- Interest or dividends are received as a nominee
- A financial interest in, or signature authority over, a foreign financial account exists
- A distribution from, or grantor or transferor relationship with, a foreign trust exists

So a return with $0 taxable interest but, say, a foreign financial account or nominee tax-exempt interest still requires Schedule B.

### 1.11 Downstream consumers of Line 2a

Although Line 2a is informational and does NOT add to total income on Form 1040 Line 9, the tax-exempt interest amount IS used in three downstream calculations:

| Downstream | How it uses Line 2a |
|---|---|
| **Form 6251 line 2g** (Alternative Minimum Tax — interest from specified private activity bonds) | Uses the *box 9 + box 13 AMT subset* of Line 2a as an AMT preference item. NOT the full Line 2a — only the subsets explicitly tagged as private activity bond interest |
| **Publication 915 Worksheet** (Social Security and Equivalent Railroad Retirement Benefits) | Adds the full Line 2a to provisional income for the purpose of determining whether 0%, 50%, or 85% of Social Security benefits are taxable on Form 1040 line 6b. Tax-exempt interest can therefore *indirectly* increase taxable income for filers receiving Social Security |
| **Modified Adjusted Gross Income (MAGI)** computations | Several credits and phase-outs (e.g., Premium Tax Credit on Form 8962) require MAGI = Adjusted Gross Income + tax-exempt interest + certain other addbacks. Line 2a is the principal addback |

### 1.12 What Line 2a is NOT

- Line 2a does NOT add to Total income (Form 1040 Line 9).
- Line 2a does NOT add to Adjusted gross income (Line 11).
- Line 2a does NOT add to Taxable income (Line 15).
- Line 2a does NOT include taxable interest (that goes on Line 2b).
- Line 2a does NOT include interest from Individual Retirement Arrangements or other tax-deferred accounts (those have their own lines).
- Line 2a does NOT include tax-exempt *market discount* on a tax-exempt bond — market discount on any bond (taxable or tax-exempt) is taxable interest and belongs on Line 2b.

### 2.5 The code equation (step by step)

```text
1. For each Form 1099-INT entry that belongs to this person:
       box8 = entry.taxExemptInterestAmount
       box9 = entry.specifiedPrivateActivityBondInterestAmount
       box13 = entry.bondPremiumTaxExemptAmount
       entryTaxExemptInterest = subtractNonNegative(box8, box13)
       taxExemptInterest = addNonNull(taxExemptInterest, entryTaxExemptInterest)
       form6251Line2g = addNonNull(form6251Line2g, box9)
       (box 9 is NOT added to taxExemptInterest — that was a pre-2026-04-13 bug,
        now fixed.)

2. For each Form 1099-OID entry that belongs to this person:
       box11 = entry.taxExemptOidAmount
       taxExemptInterest = addNonNull(taxExemptInterest, box11)
       (also stores entry.otherPeriodicInterestAmount in oidBox2Total for step 4)

3. For each Form 1099-DIV entry that belongs to this person:
       box12 = entry.exemptInterestDividendsAmount
       taxExemptInterest = addNonNull(taxExemptInterest, box12)
       (entry.specifiedPrivateActivityBondInterestDividendsAmount is captured
        into form6251Line2g separately by the Form 6251 path; NOT added to
        taxExemptInterest here.)

4. Box 2 of 1099-OID classification (interest form):
       taxExemptStatedInterestFrom1099OidBox2 = personal form input
       taxExemptInterest = addNonNull(taxExemptInterest,
                                       taxExemptStatedInterestFrom1099OidBox2)

5. Manual tax-exempt interest (interest form):
       manualTaxExemptInterest = personal form input
       taxExemptInterest = addNonNull(taxExemptInterest, manualTaxExemptInterest)

6. Premium adjustment for tax-exempt bonds:
       if payerAlreadyReportedNetInterestOrOid is true:
           skip — broker already netted, no further reduction
       else:
           taxExemptBondPremiumAdjustment =
               interestForm.taxExemptBondPremiumAdjustmentNotInStatements
           taxExemptInterest = subtractNonNegative(taxExemptInterest,
                                                    taxExemptBondPremiumAdjustment)

7. Round the per-person total to whole dollars:
       taxExemptInterestForThisPerson = roundMoney(taxExemptInterest)

8. Aggregate taxpayer + spouse:
       BigDecimal taxpayerSide = computeInterestForPerson(taxpayer side)
       BigDecimal spouseSide = isMfsReturn
           ? null
           : computeInterestForPerson(spouse side)
       BigDecimal line2a = addNonNull(taxpayerSide.taxExemptInterest(),
                                       spouseSide.taxExemptInterest())

9. Round and store:
       income.setTaxExemptInterest(roundMoney(line2a))
       (When the result is null — no 1099 entries match this filer and no
        manual inputs exist — leave income.taxExemptInterest unset; the PDF
        line 2a slot then renders blank.)
```

## Glossary

- **Form 1040** — the standard United States individual federal income tax return.
- **Form 1040-SR** — the variant of Form 1040 designed for taxpayers age 65 or older; line numbers and computations are identical to Form 1040.
- **Form 1099-INT** (Interest Income) — the IRS information return a payer sends a recipient (and the IRS) to report interest income.
- **Form 1099-OID** (Original Issue Discount) — the IRS information return reporting original issue discount on bonds and similar debt instruments. Original issue discount is the difference between a bond's face value and its lower issue price; it is treated as interest income.
- **Form 1099-DIV** (Dividends and Distributions) — the IRS information return reporting dividends, capital gain distributions, and exempt-interest dividends from mutual funds.
- **Form 6251** (Alternative Minimum Tax — Individuals) — the form that computes Alternative Minimum Tax (AMT), an alternative tax system that runs parallel to regular income tax and ensures high-income filers cannot avoid all tax by using preference items.
- **Form 8815** (Exclusion of Interest From Series EE and I U.S. Savings Bonds Issued After 1989) — the form a filer uses to exclude qualified savings bond interest from taxable income when proceeds are used for higher education.
- **Form 8962** (Premium Tax Credit) — the form that computes the Premium Tax Credit for healthcare purchased through the Health Insurance Marketplace. Uses Modified Adjusted Gross Income (MAGI), which adds back tax-exempt interest.
- **Schedule B** (Interest and Ordinary Dividends) — the supplementary schedule a filer attaches when taxable interest or ordinary dividends exceed $1,500 (or any of several other conditions apply).
- **Specified private activity bond interest** — interest from bonds issued for private (rather than public) purposes that the federal government nevertheless granted tax-exempt status to under Internal Revenue Code §141. These bonds' interest is exempt from regular income tax but counts as a preference item for Alternative Minimum Tax.
- **Bond premium** — the amount by which the purchase price of a bond exceeds its face value. The premium is amortized over the bond's remaining life and reduces the reported interest income.
- **Original issue discount (OID)** — the difference between a bond's face value (what the issuer pays at maturity) and its lower issue price. OID accrues over the bond's life as interest income.
- **Acquisition premium** — the amount paid above the bond's adjusted issue price when buying an OID bond. Reduces the OID income.
- **Nominee** — a person who receives an information return (1099) in their name for income that actually belongs to someone else. The nominee reports the full amount on their own return, then subtracts the nominee distribution on Schedule B.
- **Internal Revenue Code §103** — the statute that excludes interest on state and local government bonds from federal gross income.
- **Internal Revenue Code §141** — the statute that defines private activity bonds and the conditions under which their interest remains tax-exempt.
- **Modified Adjusted Gross Income (MAGI)** — Adjusted Gross Income (line 11) plus several addbacks specified by different statutes. The Premium Tax Credit's MAGI definition includes tax-exempt interest (Line 2a).
- **Publication 17 — Your Federal Income Tax** — the IRS's master guide for individuals filing Form 1040.
- **Publication 550 — Investment Income and Expenses** — the IRS publication that covers interest, dividends, and other investment income.
- **Publication 915 — Social Security and Equivalent Railroad Retirement Benefits** — the IRS publication containing the worksheet used to determine what portion of Social Security benefits is taxable.
- **`addNonNull(a, b)`** — the application helper that returns `a + b` when both are non-null, the non-null operand when one is null, or `null` when both are null.
- **`subtractNonNegative(a, b)`** — the application helper that returns `max(0, a − b)` when both are non-null, handling null appropriately. Prevents negative tax-exempt interest from a runaway premium adjustment.
- **`roundMoney(value)`** — the application helper that rounds a BigDecimal to the nearest dollar (half-up) and returns null on a null input.
