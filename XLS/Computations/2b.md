# Form 1040 — Line 2b — Taxable interest

**Tax year: 2025**

This document explains how the dollar amount that goes on **Line 2b** of Form 1040 is calculated. Line 2b reports the total **taxable interest** the taxpayer (and the spouse on a joint return) received during the year. *Taxable* means the interest IS subject to federal income tax — typically interest from bank accounts, certificates of deposit, taxable bonds, U.S. Treasury securities, and similar sources. Unlike Line 2a (which is informational only), **Line 2b flows directly into Line 9 (Total income)** and therefore affects taxable income and the tax owed.

The document is organized in three parts:

1. **The IRS rule.** What the controlling IRS publications say must happen. This is the source of truth.
2. **The code implementation.** What the application's backend Java code currently does.
3. **The comparison.** A side-by-side check showing whether the code follows the IRS rule honestly, and where the two diverge.

### 1.2 The basic rule

From the 2025 Instructions for Form 1040, Line 2b (paraphrased):

> **Line 2b — Taxable interest.** Enter the total of any:
>
> - Taxable interest from box 1 of Form 1099-INT.
> - Interest on U.S. savings bonds and Treasury obligations from box 3 of Form 1099-INT.
> - Original Issue Discount from box 1 of Form 1099-OID.
> - Other periodic interest from box 2 of Form 1099-OID **that is not from a tax-exempt obligation**.
> - Distributions called "dividends" from cooperative banks, credit unions, domestic building and loan associations, domestic savings and loan associations, federal savings and loan associations, and mutual savings banks (reported in Form 1099-INT box 1, but commonly called dividends).
> - Interest from accounts that did not generate a Form 1099-INT (you are required to report it even without the form).
> - Interest you received from a seller-financed mortgage where the buyer used the property as a personal residence.
> - Money market fund distributions when they are *interest* (most money market distributions are dividends and go on Line 3b instead).
> - Interest on U.S. obligations, tax refunds, condemnation awards, installment sale payments, and similar miscellaneous sources.

In plain English: Line 2b is the **total taxable interest** the filer received from every taxable interest-bearing source, regardless of which information return it appeared on (or whether any return was issued at all).

### 1.3 What counts as taxable interest

A dollar of interest income qualifies for Line 2b when one of these is true:

| Source | Where it shows up | Inclusion rule |
|---|---|---|
| Interest from a bank account, certificate of deposit, savings bond, or other personal account | **Form 1099-INT box 1** (Interest income) | Included as-is |
| Interest on U.S. savings bonds (Series EE, I, HH), U.S. Treasury bills, notes, and bonds | **Form 1099-INT box 3** (Interest on U.S. savings bonds and Treasury obligations) | Included as-is, **before** the Form 8815 exclusion reduces it |
| Original Issue Discount on a taxable bond or note | **Form 1099-OID box 1** (Original Issue Discount) | Included as-is, **before** any acquisition-premium reduction |
| Stated periodic interest paid by a taxable Original Issue Discount bond (interest the bond pays *in addition to* the discount) | **Form 1099-OID box 2** (Other periodic interest) **when the bond is a taxable obligation** | The taxable portion goes to Line 2b; the tax-exempt portion goes to Line 2a |
| Original Issue Discount on a U.S. Treasury obligation | **Form 1099-OID box 8** (Original Issue Discount on U.S. Treasury obligations) | Included as-is (subject to refiguring if purchased after original issue with premium or acquisition premium) |
| "Dividends" from a cooperative bank, credit union, mutual savings bank, etc. | **Form 1099-INT box 1** (these payers report as interest even though the colloquial term is "dividends") | Included as-is |
| Interest the taxpayer received but no payer issued a 1099 for | Manual entry on the personal interest form | Included as-is — required even without the information return |
| Interest received from a seller-financed mortgage where the buyer used the property as a personal residence | Per-buyer entry on the personal interest form | Included as-is; **also** triggers Schedule B regardless of amount |
| Interest on tax refunds, condemnation awards, deferred-payment installment sales, life insurance dividends left to accumulate, prepaid insurance premiums applied to coverage, gifts for opening accounts (when over the $10 / $20 thresholds) | Manual entry or a 1099-INT depending on the payer | Included as-is per Publication 17 chapter 6 |

### 1.4 What is subtracted from taxable interest

The IRS rule allows the filer to **reduce** the gross interest by several specific items. Each reduction has a single-application rule (must not be double-counted).

| Reduction | Where it shows up | Rule |
|---|---|---|
| **Bond premium on a taxable bond** | Form 1099-INT box 11 (Bond premium) | Subtracted from Form 1099-INT box 1 once. When the payer (broker) already netted the premium and reported a post-premium amount in box 1, no further reduction applies; when the payer reported the gross amount, the filer subtracts box 11 |
| **Bond premium on a U.S. Treasury obligation** | Form 1099-INT box 12 (Bond premium on Treasury) | Same logic as box 11, applied to Treasury bonds |
| **Acquisition premium on an Original Issue Discount bond** | Form 1099-OID box 6 (Acquisition premium) | Subtracted from Form 1099-OID box 1 once. Same payer-already-netted rule as bond premium |
| **Accrued interest paid to a bond seller** between coupon dates | Manual entry on the personal interest form | When the filer buys a bond between interest-payment dates, part of the purchase price represents interest the seller had already earned. The buyer reports the gross interest and subtracts the accrued portion paid to the seller. The buyer reports the subtraction on Schedule B as "Accrued interest" |
| **Nominee interest** | Manual entry on the personal interest form | When a Form 1099-INT is issued in the filer's name but the interest actually belongs to another person, the filer reports the full amount on their own Line 2b and subtracts the portion belonging to the other person as "Nominee distribution" on Schedule B |
| **Form 8815 exclusion of Series EE / I U.S. savings bond interest** | Form 8815 line 14 | When the filer used qualified savings bond proceeds for qualified higher-education expenses, a phased-out exclusion reduces taxable interest. The exclusion amount appears on Schedule B Part I Line 3 and reduces Line 2b. Subject to phaseout based on Modified Adjusted Gross Income |
| **Original Issue Discount refigured below the 1099 amount** | Per the filer's refigured Original Issue Discount | When the filer refigures Original Issue Discount per Publication 1212 (because the debt instrument was purchased after original issue with premium or acquisition premium, or it is a stripped bond), the reduced amount is reported on Line 2b. The reduction below the 1099 amount independently triggers Schedule B |

### 1.5 The Form 1099-OID box 2 classification edge case

**Form 1099-OID box 2** (Other periodic interest) is the stated periodic interest a bond pays *in addition to* its discount. Whether box 2 belongs on Line 2b or Line 2a depends entirely on whether the bond itself is tax-exempt:

- If the bond is a **taxable** Original Issue Discount bond → box 2 belongs on Line 2b.
- If the bond is a **tax-exempt** Original Issue Discount bond → box 2 belongs on Line 2a.

The application asks the taxpayer to classify the tax-exempt portion of box 2 via the personal-form field `taxExemptStatedInterestFrom1099OidBox2`. The remainder of box 2 is treated as taxable and goes to Line 2b. The personal-form field `taxablePortionFrom1099OidBox2Override` lets the taxpayer override the residual when needed.

### 1.6 The Form 8815 savings-bond exclusion

Internal Revenue Code §135 allows a qualified filer to **exclude** from gross income the interest portion of redeemed Series EE bonds (issued after 1989) and Series I bonds when the proceeds are used to pay qualified higher-education expenses for the filer, spouse, or dependent. The exclusion is **phased out** based on Modified Adjusted Gross Income.

**2025 phaseout thresholds (Revenue Procedure 2024-40):**

| Reference data | Single / Head of Household / Qualifying Surviving Spouse | Married Filing Jointly |
|---|---|---|
| **Phaseout start** | `$99,500` | `$149,250` |
| **Phaseout end** | `$114,500` | `$179,250` |
| **Phaseout range (divisor)** | `$15,000` | `$30,000` |

**Married Filing Separately filers cannot take the exclusion** per Internal Revenue Code §135(d)(2).

The Form 8815 exclusion amount is reported on Schedule B Part I Line 3 and **reduces Line 2b**. Form 8815 line 14 is the final exclusion amount the filer claims.

### 1.7 The seller-financed mortgage rule

Per the 2025 Instructions for Schedule B, when the filer received interest from a buyer of a property the filer sold under a seller-financed mortgage **AND** the buyer used the property as a personal residence:

- The interest IS taxable interest and is included on Line 2b.
- Schedule B IS required regardless of the amount (no $1,500 threshold).
- Each row must list the buyer's full name, complete address, and Social Security Number or Employer Identification Number on Schedule B Part I, listed FIRST (before other interest payers).

### 1.8 The Schedule B trigger conditions

Schedule B (Form 1040) Part I must be filed when ANY of the following are true (per the 2025 Instructions for Schedule B):

1. Taxable interest income is more than `$1,500`.
2. The filer is claiming the Education Savings Bond Program exclusion (Form 8815).
3. The filer received interest from a seller-financed mortgage and the buyer used the property as a personal residence.
4. The filer received a Form 1099-INT for U.S. savings bond interest that includes amounts the filer reported in a previous tax year.
5. The filer received, as a nominee, interest that actually belongs to someone else.
6. The filer received a Form 1099-INT for interest on frozen deposits.
7. The filer received a Form 1099-INT for interest on a bond purchased between interest-payment dates.
8. The filer is reporting Original Issue Discount in an amount less than the amount shown on Form 1099-OID.
9. The filer is reducing interest income from bonds by amortizable bond premium.
10. The filer received tax-exempt interest or ordinary dividends in any of the situations listed in the Schedule B Part III foreign-account / foreign-trust questions.

Note that conditions 2–10 trigger Schedule B even when taxable interest is below `$1,500`.

### 1.9 The complete IRS equation (plain English)

Putting all parts together:

```text
For each Form 1099-INT belonging to the taxpayer or (on a joint return) the spouse:
    box1 = Interest income
    box3 = Interest on U.S. savings bonds and Treasury obligations
    box11 = Bond premium
    box12 = Bond premium on Treasury
    contribution_INT = (box1 + box3) − (box11 + box12)
    add contribution_INT to the running total

For each Form 1099-OID belonging to the taxpayer or (on a joint return) the spouse:
    box1 = Original Issue Discount
    box2 = Other periodic interest
    box6 = Acquisition premium
    contribution_OID = box1 − box6
    add contribution_OID to the running total
    note box2 for the classification step below

For the 1099-OID box 2 classification:
    taxable_box2 = total_box2_across_all_1099_OIDs − taxpayer_classified_tax_exempt_portion
       OR the explicit taxpayer override
    add taxable_box2 to the running total

Add manual taxable interest the taxpayer entered that did not appear on a 1099.

Add interest received from seller-financed mortgages (per-buyer row).

If the taxpayer attested that the broker did NOT already net premium on the 1099 statements:
    subtract `taxableBondPremiumAdjustmentNotInStatements`
    subtract `treasuryBondPremiumAdjustmentNotInStatements`
    subtract `oidAcquisitionPremiumAdjustmentNotInStatements`
    (Reduce premium at most ONCE — these manual fields are null-shadowed when the broker pre-netted.)

Subtract accrued interest paid to a bond seller between coupon dates.

Subtract nominee interest that belongs to another person.

Subtract the Form 8815 line 14 exclusion of Series EE / I U.S. savings bond interest.

Line 2b = running total (floored at zero — taxable interest cannot be negative)
```

On a **Married Filing Separately** return, only the filing taxpayer's own 1099-INT / 1099-OID / 1099-DIV entries contribute. The spouse's entries belong on the spouse's own separate return. Also, the Form 8815 exclusion is forbidden on a Married Filing Separately return.

### 1.10 Whole-dollar rounding

The IRS allows whole-dollar rounding on Form 1040 as long as it is applied consistently across the return. The recommended practice is to keep cents while adding and round to whole dollars only when printing the final return.

### 1.12 Downstream consumers of Line 2b

Unlike Line 2a (which is informational only), **Line 2b flows directly into Total income**:

| Downstream | How it uses Line 2b |
|---|---|
| **Form 1040 Line 9 (Total income)** | Line 9 = Line 1z + **Line 2b** + Line 3b + Line 4b + Line 5b + Line 6b + Line 7 + Line 8. Line 2b is one of the eight summands that defines Total income |
| **Form 1040 Line 11 (Adjusted gross income)** | Line 11 = Line 9 − Line 10 (Adjustments). Therefore Line 2b indirectly drives Adjusted gross income |
| **Form 1040 Line 15 (Taxable income)** | Line 15 = Line 11 − Line 14 (Total deductions). Therefore Line 2b indirectly drives taxable income and ultimately the tax owed |
| **Schedule B Part I Line 2** | When Schedule B is required, Schedule B Part I Line 2 must equal the gross taxable interest BEFORE the Form 8815 exclusion. Schedule B Part I Line 3 is the Form 8815 exclusion. Schedule B Part I Line 4 then equals Line 2b on the 1040 |
| **Form 6251 (Alternative Minimum Tax)** | Line 2b indirectly enters Form 6251 line 1b (Adjusted gross income minus deductions) which feeds the Alternative Minimum Taxable Income |
| **Publication 915 worksheet** (Social Security benefits taxability) | Does NOT use Line 2b directly (uses Line 2a instead) — listed here only to clarify that the taxable-interest path is separate from the Pub 915 add-back |

### 1.13 What Line 2b is NOT

- Line 2b does NOT include tax-exempt interest (that goes on Line 2a).
- Line 2b does NOT include ordinary dividends from corporations (those go on Line 3b — even when the payer is technically called a "mutual fund" or "regulated investment company").
- Line 2b does NOT include interest from Individual Retirement Arrangements or other tax-deferred accounts until those accounts make distributions (then it is reported on Lines 4a/4b or 5a/5b, not Line 2b).
- Line 2b does NOT include interest on insurance dividends left on deposit with the Department of Veterans Affairs (that interest is not taxable).
- Line 2b does NOT include the dividends that are actually dividends (those go on Line 3b) — but it DOES include payments called "dividends" by cooperative banks, credit unions, mutual savings banks, etc., which the Internal Revenue Code treats as interest.
- Line 2b does NOT include the tax-exempt market discount on a tax-exempt bond — wait, this requires care: **market discount IS taxable interest even on a tax-exempt bond**. So market discount DOES belong on Line 2b regardless of the underlying bond's tax-exempt status. The exemption applies to the bond's stated interest, not to gains realized by buying the bond at a discount.

### 2.2 The Married Filing Separately guard

Per the 2026-05-11 audit (lines/2ab.md §7.2), `computeInterestIncome` accepts a `boolean isMfsReturn` parameter and null-shadows three spouse reads when the filing status is Married Filing Separately:

```java
interestIncomeSpouse = isMfsReturn ? null : interestIncomeSpouseRaw;
dividendIncomeSpouse = isMfsReturn ? null : dividendIncomeSpouseRaw;
spouseSsn = isMfsReturn ? null : normalizeSsn(getString(spouse, "ssn"));
```

This single guard at the orchestrator level prevents spouse-attributed 1099 statements from leaking into the filing taxpayer's Line 2a, Line 2b, Line 3a, Line 3b, Form 6251 line 2g, and Schedule B Parts I & II. The 2a / 2b lock-in test is `mfsExcludesSpouseInterestFromLine2a` in `TaxReturnComputeServiceTest.java` (the test asserts Line 2a but the same single-guard cascade protects Line 2b).

### 2.5 The code equation (step by step)

```text

           addNonNull(taxableInterestBeforeExclusion, entryTaxableOidExcludingBox2)
       oidBox2Total = addNonNull(oidBox2Total, box2)
       addScheduleBItem(...)

       taxExemptStatedInterestFrom1099OidBox2 = personal form input
       taxablePortionFrom1099OidBox2Override = personal form input

           if taxablePortionFrom1099OidBox2Override is not null:
               taxablePortionFrom1099OidBox2Override
           else:

           addNonNull(taxableInterestBeforeExclusion, taxableBox2)

       manualTaxableInterest = interestForm.manualTaxableInterestNotOnStatements

           addNonNull(taxableInterestBeforeExclusion, manualTaxableInterest)

   broker already netted premium (payerAlreadyReportedNetInterestOrOid = true),
   the three "*NotInStatements" premium fields are null-shadowed so they don't
   double-subtract premium that the 1099 already netted in box 1 / box 11 /
   box 12 / box 6 above. Accrued and nominee adjustments are NOT premium and
   are always honored.
       accruedInterestPaidAdjustment = interestForm.accruedInterestPaidAdjustment
       nomineeInterestAdjustment = interestForm.nomineeInterestAdjustment
       taxableBondPremiumAdjustment = payerAlreadyNetted
           ? null
           : interestForm.taxableBondPremiumAdjustmentNotInStatements
       treasuryBondPremiumAdjustment = payerAlreadyNetted
           ? null
           : interestForm.treasuryBondPremiumAdjustmentNotInStatements
       oidAcquisitionPremiumAdjustment = payerAlreadyNetted
           ? null
           : interestForm.oidAcquisitionPremiumAdjustmentNotInStatements

                                            nomineeInterestAdjustment,
                                            taxableBondPremiumAdjustment,
                                            treasuryBondPremiumAdjustment,
                                            oidAcquisitionPremiumAdjustment)

           subtractNonNegative(taxableInterestBeforeExclusion, taxableAdjustments)

6. Seller-financed mortgage rows (per-buyer):
       for each row in interestForm.sellerFinancedLoans:
           buyerName = row.buyerName
           buyerAddress = row.buyerAddress
           buyerSsnOrEin = row.buyerSsnOrEin
           interestAmount = row.interestAmount
           if any required field is missing or amount is not positive:
               emit SELLER_FINANCED_LOAN_MISSING_REQUIRED_FIELDS_{TAXPAYER,SPOUSE}
                   flag (non-overrideable per rules.md §17) and DO NOT add
           else:
               taxableInterestBeforeExclusion =
                   addNonNull(taxableInterestBeforeExclusion, interestAmount)
               addScheduleBItem with buyer name as the label

7. Round the per-person total to whole dollars:
       perPersonTotal = roundMoney(taxableInterestBeforeExclusion)
       return InterestPersonTotals(taxableInterestBeforeSavingsBondExclusion =
                                     perPersonTotal, ..., scheduleBInterestItems,
                                     sellerFinancedScheduleBItems)

8. Aggregate at the orchestrator level (computeInterestIncome):
       taxpayerSide = computeInterestForPerson(taxpayer)
       spouseSide = isMfsReturn ? null : computeInterestForPerson(spouse)
       taxableBeforeExclusion =
           addNonNull(taxpayerSide.taxableInterestBeforeSavingsBondExclusion(),
                      spouseSide.taxableInterestBeforeSavingsBondExclusion())

9. Apply the Form 8815 savings-bond exclusion (override path only — see Gap #3):
       scheduleBLine3SavingsBondExclusion =
           getAmount(interestIncomeTaxpayer, "savingsBondExclusionAmount")
       taxableInterest = subtractNonNegative(taxableBeforeExclusion,
                                             scheduleBLine3SavingsBondExclusion)
       (Floor at zero — taxable interest cannot be negative.)

10. Round and store:
        line2b = roundMoney(taxableInterest)
        income.setTaxableInterest(line2b) when line2b != null

11. Include in Total income (Line 9):
        line9 = addNonNull(addNonNull(addNonNull(addNonNull(addNonNull(addNonNull(
                  addNonNull(line1z, line2b), line3b), line4b), line5b), line6b),
                  line7a), line8)
```

## Glossary

- **Form 1040** — the standard United States individual federal income tax return.
- **Form 1040-SR** — the variant of Form 1040 designed for taxpayers age 65 or older; line numbers and computations are identical to Form 1040.
- **Form 1099-INT** (Interest Income) — the Internal Revenue Service information return a payer sends a recipient (and the Internal Revenue Service) to report interest income. Boxes 1 and 3 are the primary Line 2b inputs; boxes 8 and 9 are Line 2a / Form 6251 inputs; boxes 11 and 12 are taxable-bond premium reductions; box 13 is a tax-exempt-bond premium reduction.
- **Form 1099-OID** (Original Issue Discount) — the Internal Revenue Service information return reporting Original Issue Discount on bonds and similar debt instruments. Box 1 is the primary Line 2b input; box 2 splits between Line 2a and Line 2b per taxpayer classification; box 5 is market discount (default not read; conditional on the Internal Revenue Code §1278(b) election — see §3.2 Gap #7); box 6 is the acquisition-premium reduction; box 8 is Treasury Original Issue Discount (Line 2b — closure 2026-06-03); box 10 is bond premium on covered taxable Original Issue Discount bonds (reduction — closure 2026-06-03); box 11 is tax-exempt Original Issue Discount (Line 2a).
- **Form 1099-DIV** (Dividends and Distributions) — the Internal Revenue Service information return reporting dividends, capital gain distributions, and exempt-interest dividends from mutual funds. **Not read by Line 2b**; the exempt-interest-dividend boxes 12 and 13 feed Line 2a and Form 6251.
- **Form 8815** (Exclusion of Interest From Series EE and I U.S. Savings Bonds Issued After 1989) — the form a filer uses to exclude qualified savings bond interest from taxable income when the proceeds are used for qualified higher-education expenses. The line 14 exclusion amount reduces Line 2b.
- **Schedule B** (Interest and Ordinary Dividends) — the supplementary schedule a filer attaches when taxable interest or ordinary dividends exceed `$1,500` (or any of several other conditions apply per §1.8). Part I details the per-payer interest items, including the Form 8815 exclusion as Line 3 and the net taxable interest as Line 4 (which equals Form 1040 Line 2b when Schedule B is filed).
- **Original Issue Discount** — the difference between a bond's face value (what the issuer pays at maturity) and its lower issue price. Original Issue Discount accrues over the bond's life as interest income.
- **Acquisition premium** — the amount paid above the bond's adjusted issue price when buying an Original Issue Discount bond after original issuance. Reduces the Original Issue Discount income.
- **Bond premium** — the amount by which the purchase price of a bond exceeds its face value. The premium is amortized over the bond's remaining life and reduces the reported interest income.
- **Accrued interest** — interest that accrued on a bond before the buyer purchased it but was paid to the buyer at the next coupon date. The buyer subtracts the accrued portion paid to the seller on Schedule B and reports only the post-purchase portion on Line 2b.
- **Nominee** — a person who receives an information return (1099) in their name for income that actually belongs to someone else. The nominee reports the full amount on their own return, then subtracts the nominee distribution on Schedule B identifying the actual owner.
- **Seller-financed mortgage interest** — interest the filer receives from a buyer of a property the filer sold under a seller-financed mortgage, when the buyer used the property as a personal residence. Each row triggers Schedule B regardless of amount and requires the buyer's name, address, and Social Security Number or Employer Identification Number.
- **Modified Adjusted Gross Income (MAGI)** — Adjusted Gross Income (Line 11) plus several addbacks specified by different statutes. For Form 8815 purposes, Modified Adjusted Gross Income is Adjusted Gross Income plus several specific add-backs per Publication 970 chapter 10.
- **Internal Revenue Code §135** — the statute that grants the Series EE / I U.S. savings bond exclusion (Form 8815).
- **Publication 17 — Your Federal Income Tax** — the Internal Revenue Service's master guide for individuals filing Form 1040; Chapter 6 covers Interest Income.
- **Publication 550 — Investment Income and Expenses** — the Internal Revenue Service publication covering interest, dividends, Original Issue Discount, premium amortization, market discount, and other investment income topics.
- **Publication 1212 — Guide to Original Issue Discount (OID) Instruments** — the Internal Revenue Service publication detailing how to refigure Original Issue Discount when the debt instrument was purchased after original issue with premium or acquisition premium, or when the instrument is a stripped bond.
- **`addNonNull(a, b)`** — the application helper that returns `a + b` when both are non-null, the non-null operand when one is null, or `null` when both are null.
- **`subtractNonNegative(a, b)`** — the application helper that returns `max(0, a − b)` when both are non-null, handling null appropriately. Prevents negative taxable interest from a runaway adjustment.
- **`roundMoney(value)`** — the application helper that rounds a BigDecimal to the nearest dollar (half-up) and returns null on a null input.
- **`hasPositiveAmount(value)`** — the application helper that returns true when `value` is non-null and strictly greater than zero.
- **§17 flag** — a non-overrideable blocking flag per `rules.md §17`. The application refuses to produce a tax return when a §17 flag is present, regardless of the user's `overrideFlags=true` setting, because filing the return as-is would either silently under-report income (Internal Revenue Service would catch via document-matching and issue a CP-2000 notice) or produce a return that fails Internal Revenue Service e-file validation. Examples in the Line 2b context: `OID_BOX2_TAX_EXEMPT_EXCEEDS_TOTAL_{TAXPAYER,SPOUSE}` and `SELLER_FINANCED_LOAN_MISSING_REQUIRED_FIELDS_{TAXPAYER,SPOUSE}`.
