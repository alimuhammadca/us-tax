## Quick orientation

The Form 1040 label for line 30 (per the actual 2025 IRS Form 1040 page 2):

> **"30   Refundable adoption credit from Form 8839, line 13 ............... 30"**

In one sentence: line 30 is the **refundable** portion of the 2025 Form 8839 Adoption Credit — a dollar amount carried directly from Form 8839 line 13 onto Form 1040 page 2. The Internal Revenue Service treats it as a payment (just like withholding): if it exceeds the tax owed, the excess is paid back to the taxpayer as a refund. The companion non-refundable portion of the same credit appears on Schedule 3 line 6c and is the topic of `XLS\Computations\20.md` rather than this file.

Five facts to keep in mind throughout:

1. **Line 30 is new for tax year 2025.** Through tax year 2024 the entire adoption credit was non-refundable: any excess of the credit over the taxpayer's tax liability could only be carried forward to future years. The One Big Beautiful Bill Act of 2025 split the credit into a refundable share (capped at $5,000 per child) and a non-refundable share (the remainder). The refundable share flows to Form 1040 line 30; the non-refundable share continues to flow through Schedule 3 line 6c to Form 1040 line 20 as before.

2. **The $5,000 refundable cap is per qualifying child, not per return.** A family adopting two children may receive up to $10,000 of refundable credit on line 30. The cap is computed at Form 8839 Part II line 11b for each child separately.

3. **Line 30 comes only from Form 8839 — never re-derived on Form 1040.** Form 1040 line 30 is a transcription of Form 8839 line 13. If Form 8839 line 13 is zero or blank, Form 1040 line 30 is zero or blank. There is no Form 1040-level recomputation.

4. **Line 30 carries only the current-year refundable amount.** A prior-year carryforward of unused non-refundable adoption credit enters Form 8839 line 15 and stays on the non-refundable side; it does not become refundable in a later year. The refundable cap applies only to the current year's qualified adoption expenses.

5. **Line 30 flows into line 32 (other refundable credits subtotal) and from there into line 33 (total payments).** It does not directly reduce tax. It reduces the amount the taxpayer owes by increasing the total-payments side of the return.

The equation:

```text
Form 1040 line 30 = Form 8839 line 13 = Form 8839 line 11c
                  = sum over qualifying children of min(per-child credit after phaseout, $5,000)
```

Form 8839 line 13 is itself the result of a multi-step computation across Part II of Form 8839, described in the rest of this document.

## 1. The Internal Revenue Service canonical computation

The Adoption Credit is computed in two parts of Form 8839 that interact with Form 1040 at three different lines:

- **Part II** — Adoption Credit. Per qualifying child: maximum credit, prior-year offset, qualified expenses, phaseout, refundable cap. Aggregates into line 13 (refundable → Form 1040 line 30) and line 18 (non-refundable → Schedule 3 line 6c → Form 1040 line 20).
- **Part III** — Employer-Provided Adoption Benefits. Per qualifying child: maximum exclusion, prior-year offset, benefits received, phaseout. Aggregates into line 31 (taxable benefits → Form 1040 line 1f).

For Form 1040 line 30 only Part II matters. Part III is described briefly because Part III taxable benefits at line 31 are an upstream input to Form 1040 line 1f (wages) which then feeds Adjusted Gross Income at line 11b which then feeds Modified Adjusted Gross Income at Form 8839 line 7 — completing the dependency loop.

### 1a. Who can claim the credit

A taxpayer can claim the Adoption Credit on Form 8839 only if all of the following apply (Internal Revenue Code section 23, Publication 17 chapter 25, J.K. Lasser §25.9):

1. The taxpayer paid qualified adoption expenses, or adopted (or attempted to adopt) an eligible child.
2. Filing status is Single, Head of Household, Qualifying Surviving Spouse, or Married Filing Jointly — OR Married Filing Separately and meets the considered-unmarried exception (the taxpayer lived apart from the spouse for the last 6 months of 2025 AND the child lived in the taxpayer's home more than half of 2025 AND the taxpayer provided more than half the cost of keeping up the home).
3. Modified Adjusted Gross Income is below the upper phaseout ceiling ($299,190 for 2025).
4. The eligible child has an identifying number (Social Security Number, Adoption Taxpayer Identification Number, or Individual Taxpayer Identification Number) entered on Part I.
5. For foreign children, the adoption is final before the year of credit OR an Internal Revenue Service safe harbor applies (Revenue Procedure 2005-31 or 2010-31). Domestic children with special needs are eligible even if expenses paid were less than the maximum credit.

### 1b. Modified Adjusted Gross Income for Form 8839

Per the 2025 Form 8839 Modified Adjusted Gross Income Worksheet (referenced in the Instructions for Form 8839 line 7 and Part III line 25):

```text
magiForAdoptionCredit
   = form1040.line11bAmountFromLine11aAdjustedGrossIncome
   + form2555.foreignEarnedIncomeExclusion (line 45 of Form 2555)
   + form2555.housingExclusionAmount (line 50 of Form 2555)
   + form4563.excludedIncomeAmount (American Samoa income exclusion)
   + puertoRicoExcludedIncome (bona fide Puerto Rico resident excluded income)
   + schedule1.studentLoanInterestDeduction (Schedule 1 line 21)
   + scheduleB.excludableInterestSeriesEeI (Schedule B line 3 — Series EE / I savings bond exclusion under Internal Revenue Code section 135)
   + schedule1.otherAdjustmentHousingDeduction (Schedule 1 line 24j — Form 2555 self-employed housing deduction)
```

All seven add-backs come from the 2025 Form 8839 Modified Adjusted Gross Income Worksheet. The same Modified Adjusted Gross Income is used for both Part II (Adoption Credit) at line 7 and Part III (Employer-Provided Adoption Benefits) at line 25.

### 1c. The Modified Adjusted Gross Income phaseout (Form 8839 Part II lines 7-10)

In plain English, the credit is full when income is at or below a lower threshold, is reduced linearly through a $40,000 band, and is zero at or above an upper threshold. The thresholds are inflation-adjusted under Internal Revenue Code section 23(h)(2):

| Filing status | Lower threshold (full credit at or below) | Upper threshold (no credit at or above) | Phaseout band width |
|---|---|---|---|
| All filing statuses (Single, Married Filing Jointly, Head of Household, Qualifying Surviving Spouse) | $259,190 | $299,190 | $40,000 |

The phaseout fraction is computed:

```text
If magiForAdoptionCredit <= $259,190:
   phaseoutFraction = 0   (no phaseout; full credit)
Else:
   excess = magiForAdoptionCredit - $259,190
   If excess >= $40,000:
      phaseoutFraction = 1.000   (fully phased out; no credit)
   Else:
      phaseoutFraction = excess / $40,000
      Round to at least 3 decimal places, capped at 1.000.
```

The 2025 Form 8839 line 9 instruction reads verbatim: *"Divide line 8 by $40,000. Enter the result as a decimal (rounded to at least three places). Do not enter more than 1.000."*

Plain-language consequence: a Single filer with $279,190 of Modified Adjusted Gross Income has excess $20,000, so phaseoutFraction = 0.500 — exactly half the credit phases out. A filer at $299,190 or above has the entire credit phased out.

Important semantic note: `phaseoutFraction` here represents the **fraction lost**, not the fraction kept. Form 8839 line 10 multiplies the per-child credit base by this fraction to get the *phased-out amount*, then line 11a subtracts that loss from the base to get what remains.

### 1d. Per-child Adoption Credit amount (Form 8839 Part II lines 2-11b)

For each eligible child listed in Part I, the per-child credit is computed:

```text
line 2 = $17,280
   The 2025 statutory maximum per child, inflation-adjusted under Internal Revenue Code section 23(h)(1).

line 3 = priorYearAdoptionCreditByChild.child{n}PriorYearCredit
   Any amount the taxpayer already used to figure an Adoption Credit for the same child in a prior tax year.

line 4 = max(0, line 2 - line 3)
   Remaining maximum credit available for this child in 2025.

line 5 = qualifiedAdoptionExpensesByChild.child{n}QualifiedExpensesGross
         - qualifiedAdoptionExpensesByChild.child{n}EmployerReimbursementToSubtract
   Qualified adoption expenses for this child, net of employer reimbursement. Per Internal
   Revenue Code section 137(d), expenses reimbursed by an employer under a written qualified
   adoption assistance program are NOT qualified adoption expenses for the credit.
   Special rule per Internal Revenue Code section 23(a)(3): for an adoption of a domestic
   child with special needs that became final in 2025, line 5 is treated as if it equals
   the line 2 maximum even when actual expenses paid were less.

line 6 = smaller of line 4 or line 5
   The credit base for this child, before phaseout. Special-needs final-adoption override:
   line 6 = line 4 (not min(line 4, line 5)) so the taxpayer gets the full available credit
   amount regardless of actual expenses paid.

line 10 = line 6 × phaseoutFraction
   The phased-out portion of this child's credit base.

line 11a = max(0, line 6 - line 10)
   Per-child credit after phaseout (still subject to the refundable cap at line 11b and
   the credit-limit cap at line 17 for the non-refundable side).

line 11b = smaller of line 11a or $5,000
   The refundable share per child. This is the One Big Beautiful Bill Act of 2025 cap that
   makes the adoption credit partly refundable for the first time.
```

Concrete examples for a Single filer with Modified Adjusted Gross Income $200,000 (no phaseout) and one qualifying child:

| Qualified adoption expenses (line 5) | line 4 | line 6 | line 11a | line 11b | Form 1040 line 30 contribution |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| $0     | $17,280 | $0     | $0     | $0     | $0 |
| $3,000 | $17,280 | $3,000 | $3,000 | $3,000 | $3,000 |
| $5,000 | $17,280 | $5,000 | $5,000 | $5,000 | $5,000 |
| $10,000 | $17,280 | $10,000 | $10,000 | $5,000 | $5,000 (refundable); $5,000 carries to non-refundable line 12 |
| $17,280 | $17,280 | $17,280 | $17,280 | $5,000 | $5,000 (refundable); $12,280 carries to non-refundable line 12 |
| $20,000 | $17,280 | $17,280 (capped) | $17,280 | $5,000 | $5,000 (refundable); $12,280 carries to non-refundable line 12 |

Special-needs final 2025 example: a domestic child with special needs, adoption finalized in 2025, qualified expenses paid = $500, no prior year. Line 4 = $17,280; line 6 = $17,280 (override; not $500); line 11a = $17,280 (no phaseout); line 11b = $5,000 (refundable cap). Form 1040 line 30 contribution = **$5,000** even though actual expenses paid were only $500.

### 1e. Form 8839 Part II — Aggregation across children (lines 11c, 12, 13)

```text
line 11c = sum of line 11b across all eligible children
   The total refundable credit base.

line 12  = sum of line 11a across all eligible children
   The total credit after phaseout, before the refundable split.

line 13  = line 11c
   The refundable Adoption Credit. Goes to Form 1040 line 30.
```

The line 13 = line 11c equality reflects the One Big Beautiful Bill Act of 2025 design: every dollar of the per-child amount that fits under the $5,000 cap becomes refundable; whatever remains stays non-refundable.

### 1f. Form 8839 Part II — Non-refundable side (lines 14-18, for completeness)

```text
line 14 = max(0, line 12 - line 13)
   The non-refundable portion of the current-year credit.

line 15 = creditCarryforwardFromPriorYears
   Any unused non-refundable Adoption Credit from prior tax years (carryforward worksheet
   in the prior-year Form 8839 instructions). Stays non-refundable per Internal Revenue Code
   section 23(c).

line 16 = line 14 + line 15
   Total non-refundable Adoption Credit before the Credit Limit Worksheet.

line 17 = Credit Limit Worksheet line 5 (described in section 1g below).

line 18 = smaller of line 16 or line 17
   The final non-refundable Adoption Credit. Goes to Schedule 3 line 6c, then Schedule 3
   line 8, then Form 1040 line 20.
```

### 1g. Credit Limit Worksheet (for the non-refundable side at line 17, for completeness)

Per the 2025 Instructions for Form 8839:

```text
Worksheet line 1 = Form 8839 line 16
Worksheet line 2 = Form 1040 line 18 (totalTaxBeforeCredits = line 16 + line 17)
Worksheet line 3 = Form 1040 line 19 (Child Tax Credit + Credit for Other Dependents)
                 + Schedule 3 line 1 (foreign tax credit)
                 + Schedule 3 line 2 (credit for child and dependent care)
                 + Schedule 3 line 3 (education credits)
                 + Schedule 3 line 4 (Saver's Credit)
                 + Schedule 3 line 5a (residential clean energy credit)
                 + Schedule 3 line 5b (energy efficient home improvement credit)
                 + Schedule 3 line 6b (prior-year minimum tax credit)
                 + Schedule 3 line 6d (credit for the elderly or disabled)
                 + Schedule 3 line 6f (clean vehicle credit)
                 + Schedule 3 line 6g (alternative fuel vehicle refueling property credit)
                 + Schedule 3 line 6l (amount on Form 8978 Schedule A line 6)
                 + Schedule 3 line 6m (credit for previously-owned clean vehicles)
Worksheet line 4 = max(0, Worksheet line 2 - Worksheet line 3)
Worksheet line 5 = smaller of Worksheet line 1 or Worksheet line 4 = Form 8839 line 17
```

Schedule 8812 substitution: when the Credit Limit Worksheet at line 17 is otherwise too restrictive, the 2025 Instructions for Form 8839 direct the taxpayer to a substitute computation using Schedule 8812 Credit Limit Worksheet B line 14 in place of Form 1040 line 19.

### 1h. Filing-status rule for Married Filing Separately

Per Internal Revenue Code section 137(f) and Internal Revenue Code section 23 (referencing section 137 for the joint-return rule), Married Filing Separately filers cannot claim the Adoption Credit OR the employer-benefits exclusion **unless** the considered-unmarried exception applies. The 2025 exception requires:

1. The taxpayer lived apart from the spouse for the last 6 months of 2025.
2. The eligible child lived in the taxpayer's home more than half of 2025.
3. The taxpayer provided more than half the cost of keeping up the home.

If all three conditions hold, the Married Filing Separately filer is treated as not married for adoption-credit purposes and may claim the credit.

### 1i. More than three eligible children

The 2025 Form 8839 has three child columns in Part I and Part II. For more than three children:

- Attach as many Forms 8839 as needed.
- Enter "See Attached" by the caution below Form 8839 line 1.
- Complete per-child lines 2-6, 10, 11a, and 11b for each child on each attached Form 8839.
- Complete return-level lines 7-9, 11c, and 12-18 on only one Form 8839 using the combined totals.

The combined line 11c becomes Form 8839 line 13 and flows to Form 1040 line 30.

### 1j. Form 1040 downstream wiring

```text
Form 1040 line 30 = Form 8839 line 13
Form 1040 line 32 = line 27a (Earned Income Credit) + line 28 (Additional Child Tax Credit) + line 29 (refundable American Opportunity Credit) + line 30 + line 31 (Schedule 3 line 15)
Form 1040 line 33 = line 25d (withholding) + line 26 (estimated tax payments) + line 32
```

Important guardrail: line 30 is included once through line 32. It is never added again separately into line 33.

## 2. The as-implemented computation in this codebase

The computation is split across two helpers in `TaxReturnComputeService.java` because the Modified Adjusted Gross Income is not known at the time the helper that builds Form 8839 first runs (Form 8839 needs the Modified Adjusted Gross Income, but the Modified Adjusted Gross Income depends on Form 1040 line 1f, which depends on Form 8839 Part III).

### 2a. Entry point and orchestration

- `computeAdoptionBenefits()` (line 18464) — called early in `prepare()` at line 424, BEFORE the Adjusted Gross Income is computed. Produces a Form 8839 with Part II lines 1-13 and Part III lines 19-31 using the user's manually-entered Modified Adjusted Gross Income (if present). Returns `AdoptionComputation { line1f, refundableAdoptionCredit, form8839 }`.
- `applyAdoptionCredit()` (line 22188) — called at line 1929 AFTER Form 1040 line 18 (totalTaxBeforeCredits) and Schedule 3 lines 1-6b are populated. When the manually-entered Modified Adjusted Gross Income was absent, this helper auto-derives it from Form 1040 line 11b plus the seven add-backs and re-runs the per-child Part II and Part III loops. Then it computes the Credit Limit Worksheet at Form 8839 line 17 and sets the non-refundable line 18.
- Line 27009-27023 (inside `computeLine31ThroughLine38()`) — wires `form8839.part2Line13RefundableAdoptionCredit` into `Payments.refundableAdoptionCredit`, which becomes Form 1040 line 30.

### 2b. As-implemented Modified Adjusted Gross Income (manual entry path)

Code at line 18668:

```java
BigDecimal magi = parseAmount(getMap(data, "imports").get("magiForAdoptionBenefitsExclusion"));
```

The first pass reads a single manually-entered value `imports.magiForAdoptionBenefitsExclusion` from the `adoption-expenses` personal form. This is a user-typed dollar amount; the user is expected to compute the Modified Adjusted Gross Income themselves per the 2025 Form 8839 Modified Adjusted Gross Income Worksheet. When that field is null, the first pass leaves `magi = null` and skips lines 7-13; the auto-derivation in section 2g below picks up the slack.

### 2c. As-implemented Modified Adjusted Gross Income (auto-derivation path inside `applyAdoptionCredit`)

Code at lines 22233-22264. When `part2Line7ModifiedAgi` is still null after the first pass, the helper derives it from upstream computed values:

```java
autoMagi = agi (Form 1040 line 11b)
         + puertoRicoExcludedIncome
         + (form2555Taxpayer + form2555Spouse) foreignEarnedIncomeExclusion (Form 2555 line 45)
         + (form2555Taxpayer + form2555Spouse) housingExclusionAmount (Form 2555 line 50)
         + form4563ExcludedIncome
         + schedule1.studentLoanInterestDeduction (Schedule 1 line 21)
         + scheduleB.line3ExcludableInterestSeriesEeI (Schedule B line 3)
         + schedule1.otherAdjustmentHousingDeduction (Schedule 1 line 24j)
```

All seven add-backs from the 2025 Form 8839 Modified Adjusted Gross Income Worksheet are included (1f Gap #8 closure on 2026-05-24). The Form 2555 spouse contribution is summed for joint returns only; for Married Filing Separately the helper has already short-circuited at the section 1h gate.

### 2d. As-implemented phaseout fraction

Code at lines 21191-21195 (helper `computeAdoptionPhaseoutFraction`):

```java
excess = max(0, magi - $259,190)
If excess >= $40,000:
   fraction = 1.000
Else:
   fraction = excess / $40,000, scale 4, RoundingMode.HALF_UP
```

The 2025 Form 8839 line 9 instruction reads "rounded to at least three places". The code uses 4 decimal places with half-up rounding — strictly more precise than the minimum, and within the IRS rule. The semantic matches the IRS rule: `fraction` represents the phased-out portion (fraction lost), not the kept portion.

### 2e. As-implemented per-child loop (lines 18742-18819)

For each child read from `adoption-expenses.children.entries[]`:

```java
perChildCap = $17,280 — but reduced by IRC §137(b)(1) shared-adoption split when the child
              is marked as adopted jointly with a non-spouse adult (1f Gap #2 closure).

line 2  = perChildCap
line 3  = priorYearAdoptionCreditByChild.child{n}PriorYearCredit
line 4  = max(0, line 2 - line 3)
line 5  = max(0, qualifiedExpensesGross - employerReimbursementToSubtract)
              Per IRC §137(d) the user enters gross + reimbursement separately to prevent
              double-counting the same dollar in both the §137 exclusion (Part III) and the
              §23 credit (Part II). (1f Gap #3 closure 2026-05-23.)
line 6  = (specialNeeds && adoptionFinal) ? line 4 : min(line 4, line 5)
              Special-needs final-adoption override per IRC §23(a)(3).
line 10 = line 6 × phaseoutFraction
line 11a = max(0, line 6 - line 10)
line 11b = min(line 11a, $5,000)         // ADOPTION_LINE11B_REFUNDABLE_CAP
```

The per-child amounts are written back to the `Form8839Child` view (`setPart2Line2MaxCredit`, `setPart2Line3PriorYearAmount`, etc.) so they appear on the printed Form 8839 page 1.

### 2f. As-implemented Part II aggregation (lines 18960-18969)

```java
line 11c = sum of per-child line 11b → setPart2Line11cTotalRefundableBase
line 12  = sum of per-child line 11a → setPart2Line12TotalAfterPhaseout
```

When `applyAdoptionCredit` derives the Modified Adjusted Gross Income (section 2c), it re-runs the per-child line 10 / 11a / 11b loop using the previously-stored line 6 values, then re-aggregates line 11c, line 12, line 13, line 14, and line 16. This ensures the refundable line 13 on Form 1040 line 30 reflects the auto-derived Modified Adjusted Gross Income when the user did not enter the value manually.

### 2h. As-implemented Credit Limit Worksheet (lines 22318-22447, only the relevant pieces)

```java

clwLine2 = form1040.taxAndCredits.totalTaxBeforeCredits

           OR form1040.taxAndCredits.childTaxCredit (Form 1040 line 19)

priorCredits = Schedule 3 line 1 (foreign tax credit)
             + Schedule 3 line 2 (credit for child and dependent care)
             + Schedule 3 line 3 (education credits)
             + Schedule 3 line 4 (saver's credit)
             + Schedule 3 line 5a (residential clean energy)
             + Schedule 3 line 5b (energy efficient home improvement)
             + Schedule 3 line 6b (prior-year minimum tax)
             + Schedule 3 line 6d (elderly / disabled)
             + Schedule 3 line 6f (clean vehicle)
             + Schedule 3 line 6g (alternative fuel vehicle refueling property)
             + Schedule 3 line 6l (amount from Form 8978 Schedule A line 6)
             + Schedule 3 line 6m (previously-owned clean vehicles)
```

The non-refundable line 18 is then written to `schedule3.nonrefundableCredits.adoptionCredit`, which becomes Schedule 3 line 6c. This does NOT affect Form 1040 line 30 directly; it is documented here because both lines come out of the same `applyAdoptionCredit` call.

### 2i. As-implemented wiring to Form 1040 line 30

Code at lines 27012-27023:

```java
if (adoption != null) {
    BigDecimal refundableCredit = null;
    if (adoption.form8839() != null
            && adoption.form8839().getPart2Line13RefundableAdoptionCredit() != null) {
        refundableCredit = adoption.form8839().getPart2Line13RefundableAdoptionCredit();
    } else if (adoption.adoptionCredit() != null) {
        refundableCredit = adoption.adoptionCredit();
    }
    if (hasPositiveAmount(refundableCredit)) {
        payments.setRefundableAdoptionCredit(refundableCredit);
    }
}
```

The helper prefers the value on the Form 8839 output model because that value may have been updated by the `applyAdoptionCredit` re-pass after Modified Adjusted Gross Income auto-derivation. The fallback to `adoption.adoptionCredit()` handles the (now uncommon) path where `form8839` is absent.

### 2j. Married Filing Separately gate

Code at lines 18556-18581. When filing status is "Married filing separately" AND the considered-unmarried exception is NOT marked, the helper emits a non-blocking `ADOPTION_BENEFITS_MFS_EXCLUSION_DISALLOWED` flag and returns early with `refundableAdoptionCredit = null`. Form 8839 is not produced; Form 1040 line 30 is null/$0.

Note: the flag is non-blocking because the resulting return (the full W-2 box 12 code T amount on line 1f as taxable wages, no adoption credit) is a valid Internal Revenue Service return. Blocking it would prevent the user from filing correctly when the considered-unmarried exception genuinely does not apply.

These constants are required to compute Form 8839 line 13 (and therefore Form 1040 line 30) correctly for the 2025 tax year. The maximum credit and the phaseout thresholds are inflation-adjusted each year per Internal Revenue Code section 23(h); the refundable cap is statutory under the One Big Beautiful Bill Act of 2025 and is not inflation-adjusted.

**Finding:** all 5 numeric constants are centralized in `ReferenceData.java` (lines 82-86) — good. The phaseout-fraction rounding rule (3 decimals minimum) is implemented as 4 decimals (above the minimum). The implementation is statute-compliant.

This is the *same* dollar value as the Part III maximum exclusion under Internal Revenue Code section 137(b)(1) — Internal Revenue Code section 23(a)(1) and section 137(b)(1) tie the credit cap to the exclusion cap. The code reuses `ADOPTION_MAX_EXCLUSION_PER_CHILD` for both purposes, which is correct.

## 4. Gap analysis — where the implementation diverges from the IRS canonical computation

Listed in severity order (HIGH affects credit dollars; MEDIUM affects IRS-required validation; LOW affects display, maintainability, or edge-case completeness). All previously-documented gaps G1 through G6 have been fixed; G7 is documented as partial; G8 is presentation-only. The gaps below are NEW findings from this re-audit on 2026-06-11.

### Gap G-NEW-1 (LOW severity — UI cap on number of children)

**The intake form `adoption-expenses` supports up to 6 children. The Internal Revenue Service rule supports more than 3 children through attached additional Forms 8839 with combined totals on one return.**

The 2025 Form 8839 instructions for line 1 read:

> "If you adopted or tried to adopt more than three eligible children, see the instructions for more information." (The instructions then direct the filer to attach as many additional Forms 8839 as needed.)

The code accepts up to 6 children in the per-child fields `child1QualifiedExpensesGross` through `child6QualifiedExpensesGross` (and corresponding fields for prior-year credits and benefits). A family adopting 7 or more children at once cannot be represented in the current intake form. This is rare but legitimate.

**Effect:** under-counted Adoption Credit for families with 7+ adopted children. The application would either (a) ignore children 7+ silently or (b) require the user to combine children's expenses into one of the first 6 slots, mis-attributing the qualified expenses on the printed Form 8839.

**Code fix sketch:** extend the per-child fields to a dynamic-length array (or a higher fixed cap such as 12), and update the Form 8839 PDF writer to fill the additional column overflow per the Internal Revenue Service "See Attached" convention. Mark the boolean `moreThanThreeChildren` already on the form so the user explicitly elects the attached-form mode.

### Gap G-NEW-2 (LOW severity — Modified Adjusted Gross Income mismatch when both manual and auto are present)

**The first pass reads the manual `magiForAdoptionBenefitsExclusion`; the second pass (`applyAdoptionCredit`) only auto-derives when the manual value is null. There is no comparison or reconciliation when both are populated.**

The 2025 Form 8839 Modified Adjusted Gross Income Worksheet is a one-line definition: Adjusted Gross Income plus 7 specific add-backs. If the user enters a manual value that differs from what the worksheet would produce (off by more than $100), the user is filing a return whose Form 8839 line 7 disagrees with the verifiable computation. The existing partial cross-check at `applyAdoptionCredit` lines 22207-22212 captures `manualMagiAtPhase2Start` but does not currently emit an advisory comparing it to the auto value.

**Effect:** when the user types a manual Modified Adjusted Gross Income value that differs from the IRS-canonical worksheet value, the credit is computed using the user's number. This may be an over- or under-claim depending on direction.

**Code fix sketch:** in `applyAdoptionCredit`, after computing `autoMagi`, compare it against `manualMagiAtPhase2Start`. If both are present and they differ by more than $100, emit a non-blocking advisory flag (`ADOPTION_MAGI_MANUAL_VS_AUTO_MISMATCH`) listing both values and recommending the user verify their entry. The current code captures the input but doesn't compare.

### Gap G-NEW-3 (LOW severity — line 17 deferred Schedule 8812 Credit Limit Worksheet B substitution)

**When the taxpayer is also claiming the Additional Child Tax Credit via Schedule 8812 Part II-B, the 2025 Instructions for Form 8839 direct the user to substitute Credit Limit Worksheet B line 14 for Form 1040 line 19 in the Credit Limit Worksheet at Form 8839 line 17.**

The `applyAdoptionCredit` code at line 22327 reads `schedule8812.creditLimitWorksheetBLine14` and uses it in place of `childTaxCredit` when non-null (G7 partial fix from 2026-04-20). The Schedule 8812 helper does compute a Credit Limit Worksheet B line 14 value, but the full chain of dependencies in that worksheet (especially the interplay with the Adoption Credit itself as an input to the worksheet) has not been audited end-to-end in this codebase.

**Effect:** in returns where both the Adoption Credit and Schedule 8812 Part II-B are at issue, the non-refundable adoption credit at Schedule 3 line 6c may be under- or over-stated by the difference between Form 1040 line 19 and the substitute value. This does NOT affect the refundable Form 1040 line 30 directly (that's line 13 = line 11c), but it affects the companion non-refundable side.

**Status:** G7 PARTIAL per the existing dependencies file; full end-to-end audit deferred.

## 9. What this means for the audit

The line 30 computation is **substantively correct** for the common case. The five reference data constants are accurate for 2025, centralized in `ReferenceData.java`, and used in both the first pass and the second pass. The per-child loop, the phaseout fraction, the special-needs override, the section 1372 disqualifier, the section 137(b)(1) shared-adoption split, and the Married Filing Separately gate are all implemented and match the Internal Revenue Service canonical computation.

The new gaps identified in this audit are:

- **G-NEW-1 (LOW)** — Up to 6 children supported; the Internal Revenue Service rule supports more than 3 through attached Forms 8839 with combined totals. Affects only large adopting families.
- **G-NEW-2 (LOW)** — When a user enters Modified Adjusted Gross Income manually, the application does not cross-check that value against the auto-derived worksheet value. Affects users who mis-compute their own Modified Adjusted Gross Income.
- **G-NEW-3 (LOW)** — Schedule 8812 Credit Limit Worksheet B line 14 substitution into the Form 8839 Credit Limit Worksheet at line 17 is implemented but not audited end-to-end. Affects only the companion non-refundable Schedule 3 line 6c, NOT the refundable Form 1040 line 30.

None of these gaps affects Form 1040 line 30 for the common case. The previously-documented G1 (auto-Modified Adjusted Gross Income), G2 (Child Tax Credit in Credit Limit Worksheet B), G3 (extra Schedule 3 lines in Credit Limit Worksheet B), G4 (Married Filing Separately gate), G5 (special-needs unit test), and G6 (non-refundable E2E test) gaps are all closed.
