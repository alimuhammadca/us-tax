## Quick orientation

The Form 1040 label for line 32 (per the actual 2025 IRS Form 1040 page 2):

> **"32   Add lines 27a, 28, 29, 30, and 31. These are your total other payments and refundable credits ............... 32"**

In one sentence: line 32 is a **pure subtotal**. It is the simple arithmetic sum of five preceding lines on the payments side of Form 1040. No new credit is computed on this line and no taxpayer election or input is collected on this line. The taxpayer cannot put a number on line 32 directly; the value is mechanical.

Six facts to keep in mind throughout this document:

1. **Line 32 is arithmetic only.** It is the sum of five specific lines: 27a, 28, 29, 30, and 31. There is no rounding rule special to line 32 beyond the ordinary whole-dollar rounding that applies to every Form 1040 line.

2. **Line 32 carries the refundable side of credits and Schedule 3 Part II only.** Line 32 does **not** include withholding (line 25d), estimated tax payments (line 26), nonrefundable credits (which flow to line 22 / line 21 / line 24), or the regular tax (line 16) itself. Those belong on other lines.

3. **Line 27b and line 27c are checkboxes, not dollar addends.** Line 27b is the clergy election for Schedule SE earned income treatment. Line 27c is the prior-year earned income election for the Earned Income Credit. Neither carries a dollar amount; both are election indicators only. The five dollar addends on line 32 are line 27a (not 27b or 27c), 28, 29, 30, and 31.

4. **For tax year 2025 the IRS reassigned line 30.** In tax year 2024 line 30 was the Net Premium Tax Credit. In tax year 2025 the Net Premium Tax Credit was moved into Schedule 3 line 9 (and from there flows through Schedule 3 line 15 → Form 1040 line 31), and Form 1040 line 30 was reassigned to the **Refundable Adoption Credit** (Form 8839 Part II line 13). Any code, semantic name, or documentation that still treats line 30 as the Net Premium Tax Credit is referring to the 2024 form, not the 2025 form.

5. **Line 32 feeds line 33, which feeds the refund / amount-owed determination.** Line 32 itself does not produce a refund or an amount owed. Line 33 = line 25d + line 26 + line 32 (total payments), and then either line 33 minus line 24 produces an overpayment (refund) or line 24 minus line 33 produces an amount owed.

6. **A negative line 32 is structurally impossible.** Each component is bounded below by zero. The Earned Income Credit, the Additional Child Tax Credit, the refundable American Opportunity Credit, the Refundable Adoption Credit, and Schedule 3 line 15 are all non-negative by their own definitions. If any component computes to a negative number, the upstream computation is wrong; that should be corrected at the source, not by clamping line 32.

The equation:

```text
Form 1040 line 32 = Form 1040 line 27a
                  + Form 1040 line 28
                  + Form 1040 line 29
                  + Form 1040 line 30
                  + Form 1040 line 31

Form 1040 line 33 = Form 1040 line 25d
                  + Form 1040 line 26
                  + Form 1040 line 32
```

Each of the five source lines is described in section 1 below.

## 1. The Internal Revenue Service canonical computation

### 1a. The exact equation

```text
Form 1040 line 32 = (Form 1040 line 27a)
                  + (Form 1040 line 28)
                  + (Form 1040 line 29)
                  + (Form 1040 line 30)
                  + (Form 1040 line 31)
```

All five addends are non-negative. Each addend is rounded to whole dollars before it is placed on its own Form 1040 line; line 32 is then the whole-dollar sum.

There is no IRS reference data (no rates, no thresholds, no caps) that applies directly to line 32 itself. The reference data applies to the **upstream** computations of lines 27a, 28, 29, 30, and 31. Those constants are reproduced in section 4 below and are the only knobs that move line 32 from one tax year to the next.

### 1b. What each source line means

**Line 27a — Earned Income Credit.**

Plain English: The Earned Income Credit is a refundable credit for low-income to moderate-income workers, scaled by the number of qualifying children (zero, one, two, or three or more). The credit is computed by a separate worksheet (or by the IRS-published Earned Income Credit Table) using "earned income" (wages, salaries, tips, and certain self-employment income) and an investment-income cap.

For 2025 the maximum credit and the investment-income cap are:

```text
Maximum credit (no qualifying children)  = $649
Maximum credit (one qualifying child)    = $4,328
Maximum credit (two qualifying children) = $7,152
Maximum credit (three or more children)  = $8,046
Investment-income ceiling                = $11,950
```

If the taxpayer is a Form 2555 filer (the foreign earned income exclusion is claimed), is a nonresident alien for any part of the tax year, files married filing separately and does not meet the abandoned-spouse rule, or has been previously denied the Earned Income Credit and has not refiled the required Form 8862, line 27a is zero.

**Line 27b and line 27c — checkboxes only, not addends.**

Line 27b: The taxpayer checks this box if they are a member of the clergy and made an election to treat housing-allowance amounts as earned income for Schedule SE purposes. There is no dollar field for line 27b.

Line 27c: The taxpayer checks this box if they elected to use prior-year earned income to compute the Earned Income Credit (an election available in certain disaster-relief situations). There is no dollar field for line 27c.

Because lines 27b and 27c are checkboxes, the only **dollar** addend from the "27" group on line 32 is line 27a.

**Line 28 — Additional Child Tax Credit.**

Plain English: The Additional Child Tax Credit is the refundable portion of the Child Tax Credit. It is computed on Schedule 8812 Part II-A. The mechanism is: compute the full Child Tax Credit, apply it against the regular tax to the extent the regular tax allows (nonrefundable portion goes on line 19), and then the remaining unused Child Tax Credit (subject to the per-child refundable cap and the earned-income floor) is the Additional Child Tax Credit, which is refundable and goes on Form 1040 line 28.

For 2025:

```text
Maximum Additional Child Tax Credit per qualifying child = $1,700
Earned-income floor                                       = $2,500
Earned-income multiplier                                  = 15%
Phase-out threshold (single, head of household, MFS, QSS) = $200,000 modified AGI
Phase-out threshold (married filing jointly)              = $400,000 modified AGI
```

The earned-income floor formula is:

```text
earned_income_floor_amount = max(0, earned_income - $2,500) * 15%
ACTC                       = min(per-child cap * number of qualifying children,
                                 earned_income_floor_amount,
                                 remaining unused Child Tax Credit after Form 1040 line 19)
```

If the taxpayer is a Form 2555 filer the Additional Child Tax Credit is blocked. If the taxpayer was previously denied the Child Tax Credit and has not refiled Form 8862, line 28 is zero.

**Line 29 — Refundable American Opportunity Credit.**

Plain English: The American Opportunity Credit is a credit for qualified higher-education expenses (tuition, required fees, course materials) for a degree-seeking student in one of the first four years of post-secondary education. The credit is computed on Form 8863. Up to 40 percent of the credit is refundable and goes on Form 1040 line 29; the remaining 60 percent is nonrefundable and flows to Schedule 3 line 3 → Form 1040 line 20.

Per-student dollar mechanics for 2025:

```text
For each eligible student:
  qualified_expenses                = (tuition + required fees + course materials)
  expenses_in_100_percent_tier      = min(qualified_expenses, $2,000)
  expenses_in_25_percent_tier       = min(max(0, qualified_expenses - $2,000), $2,000)
  per_student_credit_before_phaseout = expenses_in_100_percent_tier
                                     + 25% * expenses_in_25_percent_tier
                                     (this is capped at $2,500 per student)
Maximum refundable share per student = 40% * per_student_credit_after_phaseout
                                       (so at most $1,000 per student is refundable)
```

The phase-out by modified adjusted gross income:

```text
Phase-out range (single, head of household, QSS) = $80,000 to $90,000
Phase-out range (married filing jointly)         = $160,000 to $180,000
Married filing separately taxpayers              = American Opportunity Credit blocked
```

If the taxpayer (or the qualifying student) was previously denied the American Opportunity Credit by the IRS and has not refiled Form 8862, line 29 is zero. There are additional bars under-24-with-substantial-earned-income that disallow the refundable portion under Internal Revenue Code section 25A(i)(4).

**Line 30 — Refundable Adoption Credit (new for 2025).**

Plain English: For tax years 2025 and later, a portion of the Adoption Credit is refundable. This portion is reported on Form 8839 Part II line 13 and flows to Form 1040 line 30. The Adoption Credit is for qualified adoption expenses (legal fees, court costs, agency fees, travel) paid in connection with the adoption of a child.

Per-child mechanics for 2025:

```text
Maximum qualified adoption expenses per child  = $17,280
Refundable portion cap per child               = $5,000   (per One Big Beautiful Bill Act of 2025)
Modified AGI phase-out start                   = $259,190
Modified AGI phase-out end                     = $299,190
```

The refundable Adoption Credit per child:

```text
per_child_total_credit_before_phaseout = min(qualified_adoption_expenses, $17,280)
per_child_phaseout_factor              = (modified_AGI - $259,190) / ($299,190 - $259,190)
                                         clamped between 0.0 and 1.0
per_child_total_credit                 = per_child_total_credit_before_phaseout
                                         * (1 - per_child_phaseout_factor)
per_child_refundable_credit            = min(per_child_total_credit, $5,000)
Form 1040 line 30 = sum over all eligible adopted children of per_child_refundable_credit
                    (further limited by carry-forward and prior-year claimed amounts)
```

Married filing separately taxpayers are generally blocked from claiming the Adoption Credit unless a special-needs child final-adoption-decree exception applies.

The **nonrefundable** portion of the Adoption Credit flows to Schedule 3 line 6c → Form 1040 line 20 (not to line 30). Only the refundable portion is on line 30.

**Line 31 — Amount from Schedule 3, line 15.**

Plain English: Schedule 3 has two parts; Part II is "Other Payments and Refundable Credits." Schedule 3 line 15 is the grand total of all Schedule 3 Part II amounts, and that total simply flows verbatim to Form 1040 line 31.

The composition of Schedule 3 line 15 (each addend is described in `lines\31.md`):

```text
Schedule 3 line 15 = Schedule 3 line 9          (Net Premium Tax Credit, Form 8962)
                   + Schedule 3 line 10         (Amount paid with request for extension to file, Form 4868)
                   + Schedule 3 line 11         (Excess Social Security and Tier 1 Railroad Retirement Tax)
                   + Schedule 3 line 12         (Credit for federal tax on fuels, Form 4136)
                   + Schedule 3 line 14         (Sum of Schedule 3 lines 13a through 13z)

Schedule 3 line 14 = Schedule 3 line 13a        (Form 2439 undistributed long-term capital gains)
                   + Schedule 3 line 13b        (Internal Revenue Code section 1341 claim-of-right repayment)
                   + Schedule 3 line 13c        (Form 3800 net elective payment election)
                   + Schedule 3 line 13d        (Internal Revenue Code section 965(i) deferred net tax)
                   + Schedule 3 line 13z        (Other refundable credits — write-in items)
```

For 2025 the Net Premium Tax Credit is **on Schedule 3 line 9, not on Form 1040 line 30**. The reassignment of Form 1040 line 30 (from Net Premium Tax Credit in 2024 to Refundable Adoption Credit in 2025) is one of the two largest structural changes to the 2025 Form 1040 payments section.

### 1c. The downstream relationship

Line 32 feeds line 33; line 33 feeds the refund / amount-owed determination:

```text
Form 1040 line 33 = Form 1040 line 25d
                  + Form 1040 line 26
                  + Form 1040 line 32

If Form 1040 line 33 > Form 1040 line 24, the taxpayer is overpaid:
  Form 1040 line 34 = line 33 - line 24
  Form 1040 line 35a, 35b, 35c, 35d, line 36 then distribute the overpayment
  between a direct refund and an amount applied to next year's estimated tax.

If Form 1040 line 24 > Form 1040 line 33, the taxpayer owes:
  Form 1040 line 37 = line 24 - line 33
  Form 1040 line 38 may add an estimated-tax penalty from Form 2210.

If Form 1040 line 24 = Form 1040 line 33, line 34 and line 37 are both zero.
```

The Form 2210 estimated-tax penalty computation also depends on line 33 (which depends on line 32). Therefore an arithmetic error on line 32 propagates into the penalty calculation, the refund, and any amount owed; line 32 is not a cosmetic subtotal.

## 2. The as-implemented computation in this codebase

### 2a. Backend computation (Java)

The backend computes line 32 in `TaxReturnComputeService.computeLine31ThroughLine38()` at approximately line 27262 of `src\main\java\com\ustax\microservices\TaxReturnComputeService.java`:

```java
// ── Line 32: total other payments and refundable credits ──────────────
// line32 = line27a (EIC) + line28 (ACTC) + line29 (AOTC refundable)
//        + line30 (adoption refundable) + line31 (Schedule 3 line 15)
BigDecimal line32 = roundMoney(
    safeAmount(payments.getEarnedIncomeCredit())               // line 27a
    .add(safeAmount(payments.getAdditionalChildTaxCredit()))   // line 28
    .add(safeAmount(payments.getAmericanOpportunityCredit()))  // line 29
    .add(safeAmount(payments.getRefundableAdoptionCredit()))   // line 30
    .add(safeAmount(line31)));                                 // line 31
payments.setTotalOtherPaymentsAndRefundableCredits(
    line32.compareTo(BigDecimal.ZERO) > 0 ? line32 : null);

// ── Line 33: total payments ──────────────────────────────────────────
// line33 = line25d + line26 + line32
BigDecimal line33 = roundMoney(safeAmount(totalWithholding)
    .add(safeAmount(line26))
    .add(safeAmount(line32)));
payments.setTotalPayments(
    line33.compareTo(BigDecimal.ZERO) > 0 ? line33 : null);
```

In plain English the backend does this:

1. Reads five upstream values that have already been set on the `Payments` object earlier in the compute:
   - `payments.earnedIncomeCredit` (placed there by `computeLine27aEIC()`),
   - `payments.additionalChildTaxCredit` (placed there by `computeSchedule8812()` Part II-A),
   - `payments.americanOpportunityCredit` (placed there by `computeForm8863()` line 8),
   - `payments.refundableAdoptionCredit` (placed there by `computeAdoptionBenefits()` Part II line 13 and re-confirmed by `applyAdoptionCredit()` after CLW-B),
   - the local variable `line31` (set just above this block from `finalizeSchedule3Totals()` and then copied into `payments.otherPaymentsSchedule3`).

2. Treats any `null` value as zero (this is what `safeAmount()` does — it returns `BigDecimal.ZERO` for null).

3. Adds the five values, rounds to whole dollars (this is what `roundMoney()` does), and stores the result.

4. If the result is strictly greater than zero it is stored in `payments.totalOtherPaymentsAndRefundableCredits`; if the result is zero or negative, `null` is stored instead. (The "null instead of zero" choice is a project convention, not an IRS requirement. From an IRS standpoint a zero line 32 and a blank line 32 are equivalent.)

5. Computes line 33 immediately afterward as `totalWithholding + line26 + line32` and stores it in `payments.totalPayments` (again with the same null-when-zero convention).

The compute order is documented in `dependencies\32.md`. All five upstream component values must be set before this block runs; the project enforces that ordering by calling the upstream computers earlier in `computeLine31ThroughLine38()` and its callers.

### 2c. Frontend rendering (Angular preview)

The Angular preview component `src\app\forms\form-tax-return-1040.component.ts` exposes a fallback computation (used only when the backend has not stored a value yet):

```typescript
protected line32OtherPayments(): number | null {
  const payments = this.form1040()?.payments;
  if (!payments) return null;
  const stored = this.toNumber(payments.totalOtherPaymentsAndRefundableCredits);
  if (stored !== null) return stored;
  return this.sumAmounts([
    payments.earnedIncomeCredit,
    payments.additionalChildTaxCredit,
    payments.americanOpportunityCredit,
    payments.refundableAdoptionCredit,   // line 30
    payments.otherPaymentsSchedule3      // line 31
  ]);
}
```

In plain English: if the backend has already stored a value for line 32 the frontend uses that value as-is; if the stored value is `null` the frontend recomputes the subtotal from the five component fields client-side. The fallback is for the case where an older saved return has all five components but does not yet have a stored subtotal.

The frontend then writes the value into the PDF field map under a key for the `Save as PDF` export feature (lines 837–849):

```typescript
values['line28_additional_child_tax_credit'] = this.formatAmount(form.payments?.additionalChildTaxCredit);
values['line28_american_opportunity_credit'] = this.formatAmount(form.payments?.americanOpportunityCredit);
values['line30_schedule3_line15']             = this.formatAmount(form.payments?.otherPaymentsSchedule3);
values['line31_add_lines_27a_28_29_30']       = this.formatAmount(this.line32OtherPayments());
values['line33_total_payments']               = this.formatAmount(this.line33TotalPayments());
```

The line 32 value is written to the PDF field key **`line31_add_lines_27a_28_29_30`** — that semantic name is a leftover from the 2024 Form 1040 line numbering (where line 31 was "Add lines 27 through 30") and is not the actual 2025 Form 1040 semantic name. The gap analysis in section 5 explains this in detail.

## 6. Gap analysis — bugs, shortcomings, and missing fields

> Our objective is correctness. The following gaps are written from the standpoint of validating the application against the IRS 2025 Form 1040, not the other way around.

### Gap A — Frontend writes line 32, line 31, and line 29 values to stale 2024 PDF field keys (**HIGH SEVERITY — production bug, line 32 is silently dropped from the exported PDF**)

Evidence: `src\app\forms\form-tax-return-1040.component.ts` lines 846–848.

```typescript
values['line28_american_opportunity_credit'] = this.formatAmount(form.payments?.americanOpportunityCredit);
values['line30_schedule3_line15']             = this.formatAmount(form.payments?.otherPaymentsSchedule3);
values['line31_add_lines_27a_28_29_30']       = this.formatAmount(this.line32OtherPayments());
```

The 2025 IRS Form 1040 semantic CSV (`C:\us-tax\us-tax-ui\public\irs\f1040_field_mapping_semantic.csv`) uses these field names for the 2025 form:

```text
f2_25[0] → line29_american_opportunity_credit
f2_26[0] → line30_reserved_future_use         (CSV is incorrect — see gap C)
f2_27[0] → line31_amount_from_schedule3_line15
f2_28[0] → line32_total_other_payments_refundable_credits
f2_29[0] → line33_total_payments
```

The frontend code writes to `line28_american_opportunity_credit`, `line30_schedule3_line15`, and `line31_add_lines_27a_28_29_30`. None of those three keys exist in the 2025 CSV. Therefore:

- Line 29 (American Opportunity Credit) is not rendered onto the exported PDF.
- Line 31 (Schedule 3 line 15) is not rendered onto the exported PDF.
- **Line 32 (the focus of this audit) is not rendered onto the exported PDF.**

A user who saves their return as a PDF gets a Form 1040 where the line 32 box is **blank** even though the in-app preview shows the correct subtotal.

Recommended fix:

```typescript
// Correct 2025 field keys
values['line29_american_opportunity_credit']           = this.formatAmount(form.payments?.americanOpportunityCredit);
values['line30_refundable_adoption_credit']            = this.formatAmount(form.payments?.refundableAdoptionCredit);
values['line31_amount_from_schedule3_line15']          = this.formatAmount(form.payments?.otherPaymentsSchedule3);
values['line32_total_other_payments_refundable_credits'] = this.formatAmount(this.line32OtherPayments());
```

This gap was previously noted as "FIXED 2026-04-21" in `knowledge\line-32-total-other-payments.md` (gap G1) and `dependencies\32.md`. Inspection of the current code shows the fix was **not actually applied** to the frontend — the line 32 / line 31 / line 29 keys are still the 2024 names. The 2024-era key for line 30 (refundable adoption credit) is also still missing.

### Gap B — Frontend does not emit the refundable Adoption Credit (line 30) into the PDF (**HIGH SEVERITY**)

Evidence: no `values['line30_…']` assignment for `form.payments?.refundableAdoptionCredit` exists in `form-tax-return-1040.component.ts`. The only `line30_…` write in the frontend currently goes to `line30_schedule3_line15`, which is a 2024-era key holding the wrong (line 31) value.

Effect: the exported PDF has a blank line 30 even when the backend computed a non-zero refundable Adoption Credit. The taxpayer's PDF would understate total payments by the line 30 amount.

This is the most consequential downstream effect because every 2025 adoption credit case prints incorrectly.

### Gap C — The semantic CSV still names f2_26[0] "line30_reserved_future_use" (**MEDIUM SEVERITY**)

Evidence: `C:\us-tax\us-tax-ui\public\irs\f1040_field_mapping_semantic.csv` row for `f2_26[0]`:

```text
2,topmostSubform[0].Page2[0].f2_26[0],topmostSubform[0].Page2[0].line30_reserved_future_use,line30_reserved_future_use,Line30 Reserved Future Use,Text,…
```

The 2025 Form 1040 uses line 30 for the Refundable Adoption Credit, not for "reserved for future use." The CSV semantic name is left over from the 2024 (or earlier) IRS form that left line 30 blank. The CSV should rename `f2_26[0]` to `line30_refundable_adoption_credit`, and the same rename should propagate to the canonical CSV in `C:\us-tax\pdfs\f1040_field_map_semantic.csv` (if not yet done) and to the generation script.

### Gap D — No unit test covers all five components simultaneously (**LOW SEVERITY**)

Evidence: `knowledge\line-32-total-other-payments.md` section 11 lists open gap G5. The current tests cover individual sources (Earned Income Credit + Schedule 3 line 13b; Additional Child Tax Credit alone; refundable Adoption Credit alone; three-source combination) but not a single fixture with all five sources active at once.

Effect: a regression that broke the sum when more than three sources contribute would not be caught by the existing tests. Likelihood is low because the sum is a straight `BigDecimal.add` chain, but the lack of a five-source test is a residual gap.

### Gap E — Implementation convention: line 32 stored as `null` when the sum is zero

Evidence: `TaxReturnComputeService.computeLine31ThroughLine38()` at the `payments.setTotalOtherPaymentsAndRefundableCredits(...)` call. The setter receives `null` when the computed `line32` is zero or negative.

Effect on IRS correctness: none. Storing `null` instead of `0` does not change the printed Form 1040 value (a blank box is equivalent to zero), does not change line 33 (the downstream `safeAmount(line32)` collapses `null` to zero again), and does not change the refund / amount-owed determination.

Effect on developer experience: minor — any consumer that reads `payments.totalOtherPaymentsAndRefundableCredits` and does not pass through `safeAmount()` would see `null` and may produce a runtime error or a display inconsistency. The Angular `line32OtherPayments()` fallback already handles this.

This is a project convention, not a bug. The note here is so the convention is documented in one place.

### Gap F — The CSV header in `form-other-payments.xlsx` uses informal section names ("template-scan") (**COSMETIC**)

Evidence: the dump of `form-other-payments.xlsx` shows the Section column says `(template-scan)` for every row. This is a cosmetic shortcoming of the input-forms catalog; it does not affect line 32 correctness.

### Gap G — Line 32 has no validation rule that asserts non-negativity

Evidence: no explicit check in `computeLine31ThroughLine38()` that line 32 is non-negative. The code accepts whatever sum the upstream computers produced.

Effect: if a future code change introduced a negative value into one of the five upstream components (for example, by accidentally signing the refundable Adoption Credit as negative when applying a phase-out), line 32 could go negative and the application would not flag it.

Recommended fix: add a defensive check after the `roundMoney(...)` call that logs (and does not silently swallow) a non-positive component value. This is a cheap guardrail because the components are individually non-negative by IRS construction.

## 7. Summary

The IRS canonical equation for Form 1040 line 32 in tax year 2025:

```text
Form 1040 line 32 = (Form 1040 line 27a — Earned Income Credit)
                  + (Form 1040 line 28 — Additional Child Tax Credit)
                  + (Form 1040 line 29 — refundable American Opportunity Credit)
                  + (Form 1040 line 30 — Refundable Adoption Credit, new for 2025)
                  + (Form 1040 line 31 — Amount from Schedule 3 line 15)

Form 1040 line 33 = Form 1040 line 25d
                  + Form 1040 line 26
                  + Form 1040 line 32
```

The as-implemented computation in this codebase is **arithmetically correct** at the backend: `TaxReturnComputeService.computeLine31ThroughLine38()` sums the five components in exactly the IRS-required order and stores the result in `Payments.totalOtherPaymentsAndRefundableCredits`.

The PDF rendering of line 32, however, is **broken**: the Angular component writes the line 32 value to a stale 2024-era key (`line31_add_lines_27a_28_29_30`) that does not appear in the 2025 PDF field map. Until that key is renamed to `line32_total_other_payments_refundable_credits`, every saved-as-PDF Form 1040 has a blank line 32. The same bug also blanks lines 29, 30, and 31 on the exported PDF.

The highest-priority follow-ups, ordered by taxpayer impact:

1. **Fix the frontend PDF field-key names for lines 29, 30, 31, and 32** so the exported PDF matches the in-app preview (gaps A and B). This restores correct printing of every taxpayer's refundable American Opportunity Credit, Refundable Adoption Credit, Schedule 3 line 15 amount, and the line 32 subtotal.
2. **Rename `line30_reserved_future_use` to `line30_refundable_adoption_credit` in the semantic CSV** (gap C) so the field-map is internally consistent with the 2025 Form 1040.
3. **Add a unit test and an end-to-end test with all five components contributing simultaneously** (gap D) so a future change that breaks five-way summation is caught immediately.
4. **Add a non-negativity assertion on line 32 in the backend compute** (gap G) as a low-cost guardrail against upstream sign errors.

None of the gaps above change the **IRS-canonical formula**, which remains a five-addend whole-dollar sum. The gaps are all in how the codebase wires that sum into its output channels.
