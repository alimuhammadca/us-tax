## Quick orientation

The Form 1040 label for line 35a (per the actual 2025 IRS Form 1040 page 2):

> **"35a   Amount of line 34 you want refunded to you. If Form 8888 is attached, check here ▶ ☐ ............... 35a"**

In one sentence: line 35a is a **conditional single subtraction**. It is the portion of the overpayment (line 34) that the taxpayer wants returned as a cash refund, computed as `line 35a = line 34 − line 36`. The remainder of line 34 (anything not refunded as cash) is credited forward to the next year's estimated tax on line 36. Line 35a fires only when line 34 fires (only when there is an overpayment); when there is no overpayment, both line 34 and line 35a are blank on the printed return.

Seven facts to keep in mind throughout this document:

1. **Line 35a is conditional arithmetic.** It is computed only when line 34 fires (when `line 33 > line 24`). When line 24 ≥ line 33, line 35a is blank — there is no overpayment to refund.

2. **Line 35a draws from the same overpayment pool as line 36.** Line 34 (overpaid) is the pool. Line 36 (applied to next year) and line 35a (refunded as cash) together must exhaust line 34: `line 34 = line 35a + line 36`. There is no third destination — every dollar of overpayment must either go back to the taxpayer as cash or be credited forward.

3. **Line 35a is bounded below by zero and above by line 34.** Mathematically: `0 ≤ line 35a ≤ line 34`. When line 36 equals line 34 (the taxpayer elects to credit the entire overpayment forward), line 35a is zero — which the IRS treats as blank on the printed return, not as `$0`. When line 36 is zero (no carryforward election), line 35a equals line 34.

4. **Line 35a has a sibling checkbox.** The line label says "If Form 8888 is attached, check here ▶ ☐". When the taxpayer elects to split their refund across two or three accounts using Form 8888, the checkbox MUST be checked and lines 35b/c/d (routing, account type, account number) MUST be left blank. The dollar amount on line 35a still equals `line 34 − line 36` — Form 8888 doesn't change line 35a itself; it just changes WHERE the line-35a refund is deposited (split across multiple accounts via Form 8888 instead of going to one account via lines 35b/c/d).

5. **Line 35a is the refund the taxpayer actually receives.** Lines 35b/c/d are the routing instructions for HOW the IRS sends the line-35a amount (direct deposit). Without lines 35b/c/d (or Form 8888), the IRS mails a paper check for the line-35a amount.

6. **Line 35a is bounded above by the federal Treasury offset.** If the taxpayer owes back-due federal debts (past-due tax, child support, federal student loans, certain state debts), the IRS can offset some or all of the line-35a refund against those debts before issuing what remains. The Form 1040 itself doesn't compute the offset — the IRS does that during processing. This means the "line 35a amount printed on the return" may differ from "the cash actually deposited or mailed to the taxpayer." Out of scope for this computation.

7. **Line 35a has no IRS reference data of its own.** No rates, no thresholds, no caps apply directly to line 35a. The only constants that move line 35a from one tax year to the next move it via line 34 (which depends on lines 33 and 24) or via line 36 (which is a taxpayer election).

The equation:

```text
if Form 1040 line 33 > Form 1040 line 24:
    Form 1040 line 34 = Form 1040 line 33 − Form 1040 line 24
    Form 1040 line 36 = (taxpayer's elected carry-forward amount, capped at line 34)
    Form 1040 line 35a = Form 1040 line 34 − Form 1040 line 36
else:
    Form 1040 line 34 = blank
    Form 1040 line 35a = blank
    Form 1040 line 36 = blank
```

The Form 8888 checkbox on line 35a:

```text
if (Form 8888 split refund elected) and (line 35a > 0):
    line 35a checkbox = checked
    lines 35b/c/d (routing, account type, account number) = blank
else:
    line 35a checkbox = unchecked
    lines 35b/c/d (routing, account type, account number) = populated when direct deposit elected
```

## 1. The Internal Revenue Service canonical computation

### 1a. The exact equation

```text
if (Form 1040 line 33) > (Form 1040 line 24):
    (Form 1040 line 34)  = (Form 1040 line 33) − (Form 1040 line 24)
    (Form 1040 line 36)  = min((taxpayer election amount), (Form 1040 line 34))
    (Form 1040 line 35a) = (Form 1040 line 34) − (Form 1040 line 36)
else:
    (Form 1040 line 35a) = blank
```

All three quantities (line 34, line 35a, line 36) are non-negative when they fire. Line 35a is rounded to whole dollars; the inputs (line 34 and line 36) are already rounded.

There is no IRS reference data (no rates, no thresholds, no caps) that applies directly to line 35a itself. The reference data applies to the upstream computations of line 33 (and therefore line 34) and to the taxpayer's election that sets line 36.

### 1b. What each component means

**Line 34 — Amount overpaid.**

Plain English: Line 34 is the total dollar amount by which the taxpayer overpaid their 2025 tax. It is computed as `line 33 (total payments) − line 24 (total tax)` when line 33 > line 24, and is blank otherwise. Line 34 is the pool of dollars available to be refunded or credited forward. See `XLS\Computations\34.md` for the full specification.

**Line 36 — Amount applied to 2026 estimated tax.**

Plain English: Line 36 is the dollar amount the taxpayer elects to credit toward their 2026 estimated tax payments. It is a voluntary election (the taxpayer can leave it blank if they want their entire overpayment as a cash refund). When elected, it is capped at line 34 — the taxpayer cannot credit forward more than they overpaid. Line 36 is an irrevocable election: once the return is filed, the taxpayer cannot later request that this amount be sent as a refund instead. The election is made on the personal form `36-apply-to-next-year` (or its consolidated equivalent on `refund-and-amount-owed-taxpayer`).

**Line 35a — Refund amount.**

Plain English: Line 35a is the cash refund the taxpayer wants from the IRS. It is computed as `line 34 − line 36`. When line 36 is zero (no carryforward election), line 35a equals line 34 — the entire overpayment is refunded as cash. When line 36 equals line 34 (the taxpayer elects to credit the entire overpayment forward), line 35a is zero (blank on the printed return).

### 1c. The Form 8888 split-refund election

Plain English: Form 8888 allows the taxpayer to split their line-35a refund across up to three different bank accounts (with optional purchase of paper Series I bonds in 2023 and earlier — discontinued for 2024+). When Form 8888 is attached:

- The taxpayer MUST check the box on line 35a labeled "If Form 8888 is attached, check here ▶ ☐".
- Lines 35b (routing number), 35c (account type checkbox), and 35d (account number) on Form 1040 MUST be left blank — those fields are reserved for single-account direct deposit and are mutually exclusive with Form 8888.
- Form 8888 lines 1a, 2a, 3a (the per-account amounts) must sum to the line-35a amount. If they don't, the IRS prints a notice and may convert the refund to a paper check.
- Form 8888 has its own routing, type, and account fields per account (lines 1b–1d, 2b–2d, 3b–3d).

The IRS rule is exact: line 35a is unchanged by the Form 8888 election (it's still `line 34 − line 36`); only the routing instructions change. The Form 8888 checkbox is purely informational — it tells the IRS where to look for the per-account routing.

### 1d. Why line 35a is conditional rather than always-set

The IRS leaves line 35a blank rather than printing $0 when there is no overpayment for two reasons:

1. **Mutual exclusivity with line 37 (amount owed) is visually obvious.** A blank line 35a and a non-blank line 37 reads as "this return is on the amount-owed path." Two non-blank lines or two blank lines would be confusing. Leaving line 35a blank when there is no overpayment removes any ambiguity.
2. **IRS data processing accepts blanks but rejects contradictions.** A `$0` on line 35a and a positive amount on line 37 would be self-contradictory under the IRS rule "line 34/35a and line 37 cannot both be non-blank." Treating the conditional as blank-when-not-applicable avoids the contradiction.

### 1e. Rules that apply to line 35a itself

There are exactly four:

1. **Line 35a must equal `line 34 − line 36` exactly when line 34 fires.** No other formula is allowed.
2. **Line 35a must be blank when line 34 is blank.** When `line 33 ≤ line 24`, both lines 34 and 35a are blank.
3. **Line 35a cannot be negative.** Since `0 ≤ line 36 ≤ line 34`, the difference `line 34 − line 36` is always in `[0, line 34]`.
4. **The Form 8888 checkbox MUST be checked exactly when Form 8888 is attached AND line 35a > 0.** When the checkbox is checked, lines 35b/c/d must be blank.

The IRS prints rules 1 and 4 on the actual Form 1040 line 35a label. Rules 2 and 3 are implicit but follow from the structure (line 35a inherits the blank-when-not-applicable rule from line 34).

## 2. The as-implemented computation (this codebase)

### 2a. Backend (Java) — Where line 35a is computed

Per `src\main\java\com\ustax\microservices\TaxReturnComputeService.java`, line 35a is computed inside `computeLine31ThroughLine38(...)` at lines 27348–27350 (within the overpayment branch that fires when `line33 > totalTax`):

```java
// ── Line 36: cap at overpaid; store on Refund ───────────────────────────────────────
BigDecimal line36Capped = line36.min(overpaid); // cannot exceed what was overpaid
if (line36Capped.compareTo(BigDecimal.ZERO) > 0) {
    refund.setAmountAppliedToNextYear(line36Capped);
}

// ── Line 35a: refund amount = line34 − line36 ───────────────────────────────────────
BigDecimal line35a = roundMoney(overpaid.subtract(line36Capped));
refund.setRefundAmount(line35a.compareTo(BigDecimal.ZERO) > 0 ? line35a : null);
```

What each name in this block refers to:

| Local variable | Meaning | Source |
|---|---|---|
| `overpaid` | Line 34 (amount overpaid) — computed two lines above this block as `roundMoney(line33.subtract(totalTax))` | local `BigDecimal` |
| `line36` | The taxpayer's elected amount-to-apply, read from the saved `36-apply-to-next-year` (or unified) personal form earlier in the method | local `BigDecimal` |
| `line36Capped` | The lower of `line36` and `overpaid` — implements the IRS cap rule for line 36 | local `BigDecimal` |
| `line35a` | The refund amount — computed here as `overpaid − line36Capped` | local `BigDecimal` |
| `roundMoney` | Rounds a `BigDecimal` to whole dollars (zero scale, half-even rounding) | shared utility |
| `refund` | The `Refund` Java output model, lazily created when `line33 > totalTax` (see `34.md` gap A) | shared output model |

Translated into plain English, the code does exactly what the IRS canonical equation in section 1a calls for:

1. Compute `line36Capped` as `min(line36, overpaid)`. This implements the IRS rule that line 36 cannot exceed line 34. If the taxpayer elects more than they overpaid, the cap silently truncates the election.
2. Subtract `line36Capped` from `overpaid` to get `line35a`. Round to whole dollars.
3. Store the result on `refund.refundAmount`, except: if the rounded result is zero or less, store `null` instead of `BigDecimal.ZERO`. This is the **null-when-zero storage convention** (parallel to the `33.md` gap B and `32.md` gap E conventions).

The code is inside the overpayment branch `if (line33.compareTo(totalTax) > 0)`, so it never executes when there is no overpayment — implementing the IRS rule that line 35a is blank when line 34 is blank.

### 2b. Backend — Form 8888 attached checkbox

The Form 8888 attached checkbox (`line35a_form8888_attached` on the PDF) is **not directly written by `computeLine31ThroughLine38`**. The Form 8888 split-refund computation happens separately in `computeForm8888()` (step 15 in the overall compute sequence per `dependencies\33.md`). When Form 8888 fires, the backend sets the `Form8888` Java output model but does **not** set a corresponding "Form 8888 attached" flag on the `Refund` model.

The frontend is expected to derive the checkbox from `form8888 != null && refund.refundAmount > 0` at PDF emit time — see section 2d for whether this actually happens.

### 2c. Backend — Compute order

Per `dependencies\33.md` section 3, `computeLine31ThroughLine38()` is step 13 in the overall compute sequence. Inside this method, the relevant ordering for line 35a is:

```text
13. computeLine31ThroughLine38() {
       ... (lines 25d, 26, 32, 33 computed) ...
       compute line 33 = 25d + 26 + 32
       read line 36 election (amountToApply) from saved personal form
       read totalTax (line 24) from form1040.taxAndCredits
       if (line33 > totalTax):
           line 34 = line33 − totalTax    (write to refund.overpaid)
           line36Capped = min(line36, line34)
                                          (write to refund.amountAppliedToNextYear)
           line 35a = line34 − line36Capped  ← LINE 35a COMPUTED HERE
                                          (write to refund.refundAmount, null when ≤ 0)
           lines 35b/c/d                  (direct deposit details, when no Form 8888)
       else if (totalTax > line33):
           line 37 = totalTax − line33
}
14. computeForm2210()
15. computeForm8888()                     ← Form 8888 created here (after line 35a is set)
```

Line 35a is computed exactly once per return inside step 13, only inside the `if (line33 > totalTax)` branch, and always AFTER line 36 has been capped at line 34.

### 2d. Frontend (Angular) — Where line 35a is read for the preview and PDF export

Per `src\app\forms\form-tax-return-1040.component.ts` at line 859:

```typescript

```

The frontend reads `form.refund?.refundAmount` via optional chaining (which correctly handles the null-Refund-object pattern from `34.md` gap A) and writes the formatted amount to the PDF field key `line35a_refund`.

**This PDF field key is wrong for 2025.** The 2025 PDF semantic CSV (`C:\us-tax\us-tax-ui\public\irs\f1040_field_mapping_semantic.csv` row 179) maps the line-35a dollar field as:

### 2e. Frontend (Angular) — The Form 8888 attached checkbox is not emitted

Per the same `form-tax-return-1040.component.ts` file, there is **no code that writes to the PDF field key `line35a_form8888_attached`** (mapped at `c2_15[0]` per CSV row 144). When the taxpayer elects Form 8888 split refund, the checkbox on the exported PDF is left unchecked, even though the IRS rule requires it to be checked.

This is documented as gap D below.

### 2f. The as-implemented equation in one line

```text
if line33 > totalTax:
    line35a = roundMoney(overpaid − min(line36, overpaid))
    stored as Refund.refundAmount (null when zero per the null-when-zero convention)
else:
    line35a = blank — Refund object not created when no overpayment
```

The implementation is arithmetically correct at the compute layer. The defects are entirely in the PDF emit layer (gaps C and D below).

## 6. Gap analysis — bugs, shortcomings, and missing fields

> Our objective is correctness. The following gaps are written from the standpoint of validating the application against the IRS 2025 Form 1040, not the other way around.

### Gap A — Implementation convention: line 35a stored as `null` when zero ✅ **DOCUMENTED 2026-06-13**

Evidence: `TaxReturnComputeService.computeLine31ThroughLine38()` at line 27350:

```java
refund.setRefundAmount(line35a.compareTo(BigDecimal.ZERO) > 0 ? line35a : null);
```

When the computed `line35a` is exactly zero (which happens when the taxpayer elects to credit the entire overpayment to next year via line 36), the setter receives `null` instead of `BigDecimal.ZERO`. This is the same null-when-zero convention applied to `Payments.totalPayments` (33.md gap B), `Payments.totalOtherPaymentsAndRefundableCredits` (32.md gap E), and `Refund.overpaid` (via lazy creation of the entire Refund object — 34.md gap A).

Effect on IRS correctness: **none**. A blank line 35a on the printed return equals zero in IRS terms. The convention matches the IRS rule that line 35a should be blank when the entire overpayment is credited forward.

Effect on developer experience: minor. Any consumer that reads `refund.refundAmount` without null-checking will get a `NullPointerException` in Java or a TypeError in TypeScript. The Angular frontend handles this via optional chaining (`form.refund?.refundAmount`).

This is a project convention, not a bug. Documented here for cross-reference.

**Resolution (2026-06-13):** Javadoc was added to `Refund.setRefundAmount(BigDecimal)` describing the null-when-zero storage convention, cross-referencing the class-level lazy-creation convention (`34.md` gap A), and citing this gap. Java consumers reading `refund.refundAmount` are now told in-source to null-check or pass through `safeAmount(...)` before arithmetic.

### Gap B — The Refund object lazy-creation chain extends to line 35a (**DOCUMENTED, not a bug**)

Evidence: line 35a is computed inside the overpayment branch `if (line33.compareTo(totalTax) > 0)` which is the same branch that lazily creates the `Refund` object (see `34.md` gap A). When there is no overpayment, the entire `Refund` object stays null — `refund.refundAmount` is unreachable.

Effect on IRS correctness: **none**. Matches the IRS rule that line 35a is blank when line 34 is blank.

Effect on developer experience: same as gap A — null-check `form1040.refund` before reading `refundAmount`.

This is a documented convention, not a bug.

### Gap C — Frontend writes line 35a to a stale 2024-era PDF field key ✅ **CLOSED 2026-06-13**

Evidence: `src\app\forms\form-tax-return-1040.component.ts` line 859:

```typescript

```

The 2025 IRS Form 1040 semantic CSV (`C:\us-tax\us-tax-ui\public\irs\f1040_field_mapping_semantic.csv` row 179) maps the line-35a dollar field to the semantic name `line35a_refund_amount`, not `line35a_refund`:

### Gap D — Frontend does not emit the Form 8888 attached checkbox ✅ **CLOSED 2026-06-13**


When the taxpayer elects Form 8888 split refund, the IRS rule requires the line-35a checkbox to be checked so the IRS knows where to look for the routing instructions. With the checkbox unchecked AND lines 35b/c/d blank (because Form 8888 is elected), the IRS PDF reads as "the taxpayer wants a refund of $X but didn't specify how to deliver it" — the IRS would default to mailing a paper check rather than honoring the Form 8888 split, OR would print a notice asking the taxpayer to clarify.

Effect on IRS correctness: **delays the refund** in the worst case; **may convert split direct-deposit to a paper check** in the typical case. Either way, the taxpayer's elected split is not honored as filed.

Effect on Form 8888 itself: minor — Form 8888 is still attached to the return (assuming Form 8888 PDF fill is implemented, which it is not per `dependencies\33.md` Gap G4), so the IRS may still recognize the election via the attached Form 8888 even without the checkbox. But the IRS Form 8888 instructions explicitly say to check the line-35a box, so failing to do so creates avoidable risk.

Recommended fix:

```typescript
// Add to form-tax-return-1040.component.ts at line 860 (after line35a_refund_amount fix):
values['line35a_form8888_attached'] = !!(form.form8888 && form.refund?.refundAmount);
```

The boolean coerces to "true" (checked) when Form 8888 is present AND there is a positive refund amount, and to "false" (unchecked) otherwise. The `formatAmount(...)` helper returns an empty string for null/undefined/zero, so the conditional needs explicit boolean handling rather than the typical formatter.

**Resolution (2026-06-13):** the emit was added one line after the Gap C fix at `form-tax-return-1040.component.ts`. The exported PDF now correctly checks the `c2_15[0]` Form 8888 attached box when (a) Form 8888 is present on the computed return and (b) `refund.refundAmount` is positive. Three Jasmine test cases under gap G verify all three branches: Form 8888 + positive refund → true; Form 8888 null → false; refund null → false.

## 7. Summary

The IRS canonical equation for Form 1040 line 35a in tax year 2025:

```text
if Form 1040 line 33 > Form 1040 line 24:
    Form 1040 line 34  = Form 1040 line 33 − Form 1040 line 24
    Form 1040 line 36  = min(taxpayer election, Form 1040 line 34)
    Form 1040 line 35a = Form 1040 line 34 − Form 1040 line 36
else:
    Form 1040 line 35a = blank
```

Plus the Form 8888 attached checkbox rule:

```text
line 35a checkbox = (Form 8888 attached AND line 35a > 0) ? checked : unchecked
when checked, lines 35b/c/d are blank (Form 8888 supplies routing)
```

The as-implemented computation in this codebase is **arithmetically correct at the compute layer** — `TaxReturnComputeService.computeLine31ThroughLine38()` performs `line35a = roundMoney(overpaid − min(line36, overpaid))` exactly as the IRS requires, with the null-when-zero storage convention that correctly implements the "blank not zero" IRS rule for line 35a.
