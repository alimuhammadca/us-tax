## Quick orientation

The Form 1040 label for line 37 (per the actual 2025 IRS Form 1040 page 2):

> **"37   Subtract line 33 from line 24. This is the amount you owe. For details on how to pay, go to www.irs.gov/Payments or see instructions ▶ 37"**

In one sentence: line 37 is a **conditional single subtraction** that fires when the taxpayer's total tax (line 24) exceeds their total payments (line 33). It is the dollar amount the taxpayer must remit to the Internal Revenue Service by the filing deadline (April 15, 2026) to settle the 2025 tax liability. Lines 34 (overpayment) and 37 (amount owed) are mutually exclusive — for any given return exactly one fires, or both are blank when line 24 equals line 33 exactly.

Seven facts to keep in mind throughout this document:

1. **Line 37 is conditional arithmetic.** It is computed only when `line 24 > line 33`. When line 33 ≥ line 24, line 37 is blank — there is no underpayment to remit.

2. **Line 37 is the structural mirror of line 34.** Line 34 = `line 33 − line 24` (when line 33 > line 24); line 37 = `line 24 − line 33` (when line 24 > line 33). The two cannot both fire. Line 35a (refund) and line 36 (apply-to-next-year) belong to the refund path; line 37 belongs to the amount-owed path. There is no third path.

3. **Line 37 is always non-negative when it fires.** Since the condition `line 24 > line 33` is strict, the subtraction `line 24 − line 33` is always strictly positive when written. Per Internal Revenue Code §6151(a), this positive amount becomes immediately due upon filing (subject to extension, installment, and late-payment-penalty rules documented in Publication 505).

4. **Line 37 has no IRS reference data of its own.** No rates, no thresholds, no caps apply directly to line 37. It is a pure conditional subtraction; the only constants relevant to its value move it via line 24 (total tax — see `XLS\Computations\33.md` Section 5 references to upstream constants) or via line 33 (total payments — see `XLS\Computations\33.md` Section 5).

5. **Line 37 is paid separately from line 38.** Line 37 is the underpayment of 2025 tax. Line 38 is the estimated tax penalty (computed on Form 2210 per Internal Revenue Code §6654). When both fire, the taxpayer owes their sum on April 15, 2026 — the IRS treats them as one combined remittance, but the two numbers are reported on separate lines so the taxpayer (and IRS audit) can see the underpayment-versus-penalty breakout.

6. **Line 37 carries its own late-payment risks.** Internal Revenue Code §6651(a)(2) imposes a failure-to-pay penalty of 0.5% per month (capped at 25%) on any line-37 balance not paid by April 15, 2026. Internal Revenue Code §6601 charges interest at the federal short-term rate plus 3% on the unpaid balance from April 15, 2026 forward. Neither penalty is computed on the 2025 return — both apply prospectively as the taxpayer delays payment. The project does NOT model these prospective penalties (out of scope for the filed return).

7. **Line 37 is not affected by the line-36 election.** When the taxpayer has an overpayment AND elects line 36 to credit forward to 2026, line 35a (cash refund) is reduced by the line-36 amount — but line 37 stays blank because the underlying `line 33 > line 24` condition does not change. The line-36 election is structurally inert when line 37 fires; the user interface should not allow electing line 36 in an amount-owed scenario (validated by the IRS rule that line 36 can only consume line 34, which is blank in the amount-owed branch).

The equation:

```text
if (Form 1040 line 24) > (Form 1040 line 33):
    (Form 1040 line 37) = (Form 1040 line 24) − (Form 1040 line 33)
else:
    (Form 1040 line 37) = blank
```

When the gate fails, line 37 is blank on the printed return. When line 24 equals line 33 exactly, both line 34 and line 37 are blank — the return is balanced (no refund and no amount owed). When line 24 exceeds line 33, line 37 is strictly positive.

## 1. The Internal Revenue Service canonical computation

### 1a. The exact equation

```text
if (Form 1040 line 24) > (Form 1040 line 33):
    (Form 1040 line 37) = (Form 1040 line 24) − (Form 1040 line 33)
else:
    (Form 1040 line 37) = blank
```

There is no rate, no threshold, no cap. The "computation" is a single subtraction inside a single conditional gate. Line 37 is rounded to whole dollars; the inputs (lines 24 and 33) are already rounded.

There is no IRS reference data (no rates, no thresholds, no caps) that applies directly to line 37 itself. The reference data applies to the upstream computations of line 24 (see `XLS\Computations\33.md` Section 5 references) and line 33 (see `XLS\Computations\33.md` Section 5).

### 1b. What each component means

**Line 24 — Total tax.**

Plain English: line 24 is the total 2025 federal income tax the taxpayer owes before payments are applied. It is computed earlier on the return as the sum of line 22 (the after-credits tax) plus line 23 (other taxes from Schedule 2). See `XLS\Computations\33.md` Section 4 for the full upstream chain.

**Line 33 — Total payments.**

Plain English: line 33 is the total amount the taxpayer has already paid toward 2025 federal income tax during the year. It is computed as the sum of line 25d (federal income tax withholding from W-2s, 1099s, and other sources), line 26 (estimated tax payments made directly to the IRS during 2025), and line 32 (refundable credits like the Additional Child Tax Credit, the refundable portion of the American Opportunity Credit, and other refundable credits routed through Schedule 3 Part II). See `XLS\Computations\33.md` for the full breakdown.

**Line 37 — Amount you owe.**

Plain English: line 37 is the dollar amount of unpaid 2025 federal income tax. It is the difference between what the taxpayer owes (line 24) and what they have already paid (line 33), computed only when the difference is positive. Per Internal Revenue Code §6151(a), this amount is due and payable on the return-filing date (April 15, 2026, for calendar-year filers).

### 1c. The mutual-exclusivity rule with line 34

Plain English: a single return can be in one of exactly three states:

| State | Trigger | Lines that fire |
|---|---|---|
| Refund | `line 33 > line 24` | Line 34, line 35a, line 36 (line 37 is blank) |
| Balanced | `line 33 == line 24` | None — all four lines (34, 35a, 36, 37) are blank |
| Amount owed | `line 24 > line 33` | Line 37, possibly line 38 (penalty) (line 34, 35a, 36 are blank) |

The Internal Revenue Service rule is exact: a Form 1040 with BOTH line 34 AND line 37 populated is rejected as self-contradictory. The project enforces this through the structural `if / else if` mutual-exclusion in the compute service — line 37 is set ONLY in the `else if (totalTax.compareTo(line33) > 0)` branch, which is mutually exclusive with the `if (line33.compareTo(totalTax) > 0)` refund branch that sets line 34.

### 1d. Why line 37 is conditional rather than always-set

The Internal Revenue Service leaves line 37 blank rather than printing zero when there is no amount owed for two reasons:

1. **Mutual exclusivity with line 34 / line 35a is visually obvious.** A blank line 37 paired with a non-blank line 34 reads as "this return is on the refund path." Two non-blank lines or two blank lines would be ambiguous. Treating line 37 as conditional matches the IRS rule that line 37 fires only when the taxpayer actually owes money.
2. **IRS data processing accepts blanks but rejects contradictions.** Per Internal Revenue Code §6151(a), the line-37 amount is the trigger for the IRS to expect payment by the return-filing deadline. Reporting `$0` on line 37 when no payment is due would create operational confusion (the IRS could open a collection case for `$0`). Treating line 37 as blank-when-not-applicable avoids the noise.

### 1e. Rules that apply to line 37 itself

There are exactly four:

1. **Line 37 must equal `line 24 − line 33` exactly when line 24 > line 33.** No other formula is allowed.
2. **Line 37 must be blank when line 24 ≤ line 33.** When line 33 ≥ line 24, the entire amount-owed branch (line 37 plus the optional line 38 penalty stacking) is blank.
3. **Line 37 cannot be negative.** Since the condition `line 24 > line 33` is strict, the subtraction `line 24 − line 33` is always strictly positive when written.
4. **Line 37 is reported separately from line 38.** When Form 2210 fires, the line-38 penalty is added on top of the line-37 amount in `AmountOwed.amountOwed` (the project convention documented in `lines\33.md` §4) but the two numbers are reported as separate lines on the printed Form 1040 so the taxpayer can see what they owe for the underpayment versus what they owe for the underpayment-penalty.

The Internal Revenue Service prints rules 1 and 4 on the actual Form 1040 line 37 label and on the Form 2210 instructions. Rules 2 and 3 are implicit but follow from the conditional structure.

## 2. The as-implemented computation (this codebase)

### 2a. Backend (Java) — Where line 37 is computed

Per `src\main\java\com\ustax\microservices\TaxReturnComputeService.java` at lines 27440-27449, line 37 is computed inside `computeLine31ThroughLine38(...)` in the `else if` branch that mirrors the refund branch:

```java
} else if (totalTax.compareTo(line33) > 0) {
    // Line 37: amount owed = totalTax − line33
    BigDecimal owed = roundMoney(totalTax.subtract(line33));
    AmountOwed amountOwed = form1040.getAmountOwed();
    if (amountOwed == null) {
        amountOwed = new AmountOwed();
        form1040.setAmountOwed(amountOwed);
    }
    amountOwed.setAmountOwed(owed);
}
```

What each name in this block refers to:

| Local variable | Meaning | Source |
|---|---|---|
| `totalTax` | Line 24 (total tax) — read at line 27329-27330 from `form1040.getTaxAndCredits().getTotalTax()` via the `safeAmount` null-coalescing helper | local `BigDecimal` |
| `line33` | Line 33 (total payments) — computed earlier in the same method as the sum of `totalWithholding + line26 + line32`, written to `payments.totalPayments` at line 27313 | local `BigDecimal` |
| `owed` | The amount owed — computed here as `roundMoney(totalTax − line33)` | local `BigDecimal` |
| `amountOwed` | The `AmountOwed` Java output model, lazily created when `totalTax > line33` (parallel to the lazy-Refund convention from `34.md` gap A) | shared output model |
| `roundMoney` | Rounds a `BigDecimal` to whole dollars (zero scale, half-even rounding) | shared utility |

Translated into plain English, the code does exactly what the Internal Revenue Service canonical equation in Section 1a calls for:

1. Check whether `totalTax > line33` via `totalTax.compareTo(line33) > 0`.
2. If yes, subtract `line33` from `totalTax`, round to whole dollars, and store the result on `amountOwed.amountOwed`.
3. If no, do nothing — the `AmountOwed` object stays null (lazy-creation convention).

The code is inside the `else if` branch of the `if (line33 > totalTax) ... else if (totalTax > line33) ...` structure at lines 27332-27449, so it never executes when there is a refund. This implements the Internal Revenue Service mutual-exclusivity rule that lines 34 and 37 cannot both fire.

### 2b. Backend — The lazy-creation convention for the AmountOwed object

The `if (amountOwed == null) { amountOwed = new AmountOwed(); form1040.setAmountOwed(amountOwed); }` block at lines 27443-27447 implements the same lazy-creation convention documented for the `Refund` object in `34.md` gap A: the entire `AmountOwed` container is created on `Form1040` only when there is an amount owed (line 24 > line 33). When there is no amount owed (refund or balanced), `Form1040.getAmountOwed()` returns null.

Effect on Internal Revenue Service correctness: **none**. A blank line 37 on the printed return equals null in IRS terms. The convention matches the Internal Revenue Service rule that line 37 is blank when not applicable.

Effect on developer experience: any consumer that reads `form1040.amountOwed.amountOwed` without null-checking will get a `NullPointerException` in Java or a TypeError in TypeScript. The Angular frontend handles this via optional chaining (`form.amountOwed?.amountOwed`).

### 2c. Backend — The Form 2210 stacking convention (project-specific, documented in `lines\33.md` §4)

Per `src\main\java\com\ustax\microservices\TaxReturnComputeService.java` at lines 2073-2088 (immediately AFTER `computeLine31ThroughLine38` runs), the Form 2210 underpayment penalty is **added on top of** the line-37 amount when Form 2210 fires:

```java
Form2210 form2210 = computeForm2210(form1040, priorYearTaxData, filingStatus);
if (form2210 != null && form2210.getTotalPenalty() != null
        && form2210.getTotalPenalty().compareTo(BigDecimal.ZERO) > 0
        && !"WAIVED".equals(form2210.getComputationMethod())) {
    AmountOwed ao = form1040.getAmountOwed();
    if (ao == null) {
        ao = new AmountOwed();
        form1040.setAmountOwed(ao);
    }
    ao.setEstimatedTaxPenalty(form2210.getTotalPenalty());
    // Add penalty to amount owed (line 38 = line 37 + line 38 penalty)
    BigDecimal currentOwed = safeAmount(ao.getAmountOwed());
    ao.setAmountOwed(roundMoney(currentOwed.add(form2210.getTotalPenalty())));
}
```

This means the **stored** `amountOwed.amountOwed` is NOT the pure line 37 value — it is the line 37 value plus the line 38 penalty. The "pure line 37" (the IRS canonical formula `line 24 − line 33`) can be recovered from the stored value by subtracting the separately-stored `amountOwed.estimatedTaxPenalty`:

```text
pure line 37 = amountOwed.amountOwed − amountOwed.estimatedTaxPenalty
```

Plain English: the project chose to store the combined "amount the taxpayer must pay" rather than the IRS-canonical line-37 value alone. This makes the user-facing summary correct (the printed return shows the combined total), but it means a downstream consumer that wants the pure line-37 value (to verify the IRS partition invariant `total amount payable = line 37 + line 38`) must subtract `estimatedTaxPenalty` from `amountOwed`.

This convention is documented in `lines\33.md` §4 ("Form 2210 stacking convention"), is asserted by the e2e test `line37: amount owed when underpaid` at `line33-refund-owed.spec.ts:108` (which subtracts the stored penalty from the stored amount-owed to recover the pure line-37 value before asserting against the IRS rule), and is asserted by the Java unit test `line38_penalty_is_additive_to_line37_amountOwed` at `TaxReturnComputeServiceTest.java:33007`.

### 2d. Backend — Compute order

Per `dependencies\33.md` Section 3, `computeLine31ThroughLine38()` is step 13 in the overall compute sequence. Inside this method, the relevant ordering for line 37 is:

```text
13. computeLine31ThroughLine38() {
       ... (lines 25d, 26, 27a, 32, 33 computed) ...
       compute totalTax (line 24) read from form1040.taxAndCredits.totalTax
       if (line33 > totalTax):
           // refund branch — see 34.md, 35a.md, 35d.md, 36.md
       else if (totalTax > line33):
           line 37 = roundMoney(totalTax − line33)    ← LINE 37 COMPUTED HERE
                                                       (write to amountOwed.amountOwed)
}
14. computeForm2210()
    if (Form 2210 fires AND penalty > 0 AND not waived):
        amountOwed.estimatedTaxPenalty = form2210.totalPenalty   ← LINE 38 STORED HERE
        amountOwed.amountOwed += form2210.totalPenalty           ← STACKED ONTO LINE 37
```

Line 37 is computed exactly once per return inside step 13, only inside the `else if (totalTax > line33)` branch, and always BEFORE Form 2210 runs in step 14. The Form 2210 stacking happens AFTER line 37 is finalized, so the stored value evolves in two stages:

| Stage | Stored value | After |
|---|---|---|
| Stage 1 (step 13) | `amountOwed.amountOwed = (line 24 − line 33)` | `computeLine31ThroughLine38` |
| Stage 2 (step 14, if penalty applies) | `amountOwed.amountOwed = (line 24 − line 33) + form2210.totalPenalty` | `computeForm2210` |

### 2f. The as-implemented equation in one line

```text
Stage 1 (computeLine31ThroughLine38):
    if totalTax > line33:
        amountOwed.amountOwed = roundMoney(totalTax − line33)
    else:
        amountOwed (the entire object) stays null
Stage 2 (computeForm2210, when penalty fires):
    amountOwed.amountOwed += form2210.totalPenalty
    amountOwed.estimatedTaxPenalty = form2210.totalPenalty
```

The Stage-1 logic is arithmetically correct per the Internal Revenue Service rule. The Stage-2 logic implements the project's "Form 2210 stacking" convention — the stored value carries both line 37 and line 38, and the IRS-canonical line-37 value must be recovered by subtracting `estimatedTaxPenalty`. See Gap D below for whether this convention should be re-thought.

## 3. Input field references

Line 37 itself has no taxpayer-entered input field. Its two component lines (line 24 and line 33) depend transitively on every income, deduction, credit, withholding, and estimated-tax-payment input the taxpayer makes. The most direct upstream inputs:

| Component line | Personal form | Field name | Effect on line 37 |
|---|---|---|---|
| 24 (total tax) | — | (derived from line 22 + line 23) | When `line 24 > line 33`, the difference becomes line 37 |
| 33 (total payments) | — | (derived from `line 25d + line 26 + line 32`) | When `line 33 < line 24`, the deficit becomes line 37 |
| 25d (W-2 + 1099 + other withholding) | (every W-2 / 1099 statement) | (per-statement withholding box) | Reduces line 33; thereby increases line 37 when underpayment fires |

**No personal form directly affects line 37.** The line-37 amount is a pure derived value — no checkbox or text field on any intake form sets it directly. Adding estimated tax payments, increasing withholding, or claiming more credits all reduce line 37 transitively through line 33.

The full list of upstream inputs to line 24 (total tax) is documented in `XLS\Computations\33.md` Section 3 and recurses through the tax-computation cluster (line 16 regular tax, line 17 Alternative Minimum Tax, line 22 after-credits tax, line 23 Schedule 2 other taxes). Recommended fix: add class-level Javadoc to `AmountOwed.java` documenting the lazy-creation convention.
