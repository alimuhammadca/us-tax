## Glossary

- **Form 1040 Line 3a** — "Qualified dividends." The dollar amount of dividend income that is eligible for the preferential long-term capital-gain tax rates (0%, 15%, or 20% in 2025).
- **Form 1040 Line 3b** — "Ordinary dividends." The total dollar amount of dividend income (taxable as ordinary income unless the qualified-dividend subset applies).
- **Form 1040 Line 3c** — "Check if your child's dividends are included in 1 ☐ Line 3a   2 ☐ Line 3b." A pair of disclosure checkboxes added in tax year 2025 that flag when Form 8814 child dividends are folded into Line 3a or Line 3b.
- **Form 1099-DIV** — "Dividends and Distributions." The information return brokers and payers send showing dividend amounts. Box 1a = total ordinary dividends; box 1b = qualified-dividend subset.
- **Qualified dividend** — A dividend from a U.S. corporation, or from a "qualified foreign corporation," that meets the holding-period rules under Internal Revenue Code §1(h)(11). Eligible for preferential capital-gain tax rates instead of ordinary-income rates.
- **Holding period** — How long the taxpayer owned the stock around the ex-dividend date. Common stock must be held for more than 60 days during the 121-day window centered on the ex-dividend date. Certain preferred stock requires more than 90 days during the 181-day window.
- **Nominee dividend** — A dividend paid to one person whose name is on the brokerage account but that actually belongs to another person (for example, a joint account where the co-owner pays tax on their share).
- **Form 8814** — "Parents' Election To Report Child's Interest and Dividends." When a parent elects to include a qualifying child's investment income on the parent's own return, the child's qualified-dividend share flows to Line 3a via Form 8814 line 9.
- **Schedule B** — "Interest and Ordinary Dividends." A supplemental form required when ordinary dividends exceed $1,500, or when the taxpayer received dividends as a nominee.
- **Section 199A dividends** — Box 5 of Form 1099-DIV. The portion of ordinary dividends from a Real Estate Investment Trust (REIT) that is eligible for the Qualified Business Income deduction. Does NOT change Line 3a or Line 3b; feeds Line 13a (Qualified Business Income deduction) instead.
- **Married Filing Separately (MFS)** — A filing status where each spouse files their own return. The application's MFS guard at the dividend orchestrator suppresses spouse-side dividend reads to prevent cross-contamination of one spouse's return with the other's data.

## 1. IRS rule for Line 3a (computed before reading the code)

This section states what the IRS expects the Line 3a value to be, based on the 2025 instructions and the cited authorities. The next section (§2) describes what the code actually computes, and §3 compares the two.

### 1.1 Plain-English equation

Conceptually, Line 3a = "How much of the dividend income you earned in 2025 qualifies for the lower capital-gain tax rates after removing any disallowed amounts and capping by Line 3b."

In ordinary arithmetic:

```
candidateQualified  =  sum of (1099-DIV box 1b across all your 1099-DIVs)
                   +  manual qualified dividends not reported on any 1099-DIV
                   +  Form 8814 line 9 qualified-dividend portion of each
                      child whose income you are reporting on your return

disallowedQualified =  nominee qualified dividends (amount actually belonging
                          to someone else)
                   +  qualified dividends from stock that failed the common-
                          stock 61-of-121-day holding period rule
                   +  qualified dividends from stock that failed the preferred-
                          stock 91-of-181-day holding period rule
                   +  qualified dividends tied to a related-payment obligation
                          or short-sale situation
                   +  payments-in-lieu of dividends the taxpayer knows are not
                          qualified
                   +  qualified dividends from disallowed surrogate foreign
                          corporations (Internal Revenue Code §7874)

line3a_raw         =  candidateQualified − disallowedQualified

line3a             =  max(0, min(line3a_raw, line3b))
```

The final two operations are the two IRS invariants:
- **Non-negative invariant**: Line 3a cannot be less than zero.
- **Subset invariant**: Line 3a cannot exceed Line 3b, because qualified dividends are a strict subset of ordinary dividends under Internal Revenue Code §1(h)(11).

### 1.4 What does NOT belong on Line 3a (per IRS instructions)

These are common misroutings the IRS instructions explicitly call out:
- **1099-DIV box 1a only without box 1b** — box 1a goes to Line 3b but NOT to Line 3a unless box 1b is also populated.
- **1099-DIV box 2a (capital gain distributions)** — goes to Line 7a (capital gains), never to Line 3a.
- **1099-DIV box 3 (nondividend distributions / return of capital)** — reduces the taxpayer's cost basis; not income at all.
- **1099-DIV box 5 (Section 199A dividends)** — Real Estate Investment Trust dividends eligible for the Qualified Business Income deduction; feeds Line 13a, not Line 3a.
- **1099-DIV box 12 (exempt-interest dividends)** — feeds Line 2a (tax-exempt interest), not Line 3a.
- **1099-DIV box 13 (specified private activity bond interest dividends)** — feeds Form 6251 line 2g (Alternative Minimum Tax preference items), not Line 3a directly.
- **Form 8814 line 12 (residual income)** — the residue of Form 8814 line 6 after lines 9 and 10 are removed routes to Schedule 1 line 8z (Other income) → Form 1040 Line 8, not Line 3a.

### 1.5 Decision tree (the IRS-expected calculation order)

```
Step 1: Filing status?
        - If Married Filing Separately, drop the spouse-side computation entirely
          (each spouse files independently). Otherwise, both halves run.

Step 2: For each person (taxpayer first, then spouse if applicable):
        a. Sum 1099-DIV box 1b across all 1099-DIVs attributed to that person
           by recipient Taxpayer Identification Number (recipientTIN).
        b. Add manualQualifiedDividendsNotOnStatements (per-person personal form).
        c. Subtract the five disallowed-qualified categories (per-person
           personal form): nominee, common-stock holding period, preferred-
           stock holding period, related-payment / short-sale, payments-in-lieu,
           surrogate-foreign-corporation.
        d. Apply the per-person Line 3a ≤ Line 3b cap. If qualified > ordinary
           for the person, set qualified = ordinary (silent cap; common when
           the broker mis-classified box 1b).

Step 3: Aggregate across taxpayer + spouse.

Step 4: Add Form 8814 line 9 (sum across all elected children whose Form 8814
        line 4 > $2,700). This adds to BOTH Line 3a AND Line 3b symmetrically
        because qualified dividends are a subset of ordinary dividends.

Step 5: Apply the return-level Line 3a ≤ Line 3b cap as a safety net.
        If aggregation produced Line 3a > Line 3b (rare — should be prevented
        by the per-person cap), set Line 3a = Line 3b and emit a non-blocking
        flag for user review.

Step 6: Round to whole dollars.

Result: Line 3a as printed on Form 1040.
```

### 1.6 Worked example (single filer, no spouse, no children)

| Source | Amount |
|---|---|
| 1099-DIV #1 box 1a (ordinary) | $5,000 |
| 1099-DIV #1 box 1b (qualified) | $4,200 |
| 1099-DIV #2 box 1a (ordinary) | $1,500 |
| 1099-DIV #2 box 1b (qualified) | $1,200 |
| Manual qualified not on statements | $0 |
| Nominee qualified | $300 |
| Common-stock holding-period failure | $100 |
| All other disallowed categories | $0 |

Computation:
```
candidateQualified  = $4,200 + $1,200 + $0 = $5,400
disallowedQualified = $300 + $100 = $400
line3a_raw          = $5,400 − $400 = $5,000
line3b              = $5,000 + $1,500 = $6,500   (no nominee ordinary)
line3a              = min($5,000, $6,500) = $5,000
                   ⇒ no cap; Line 3a = $5,000.
```

## 2. What the code computes (Java implementation in `TaxReturnComputeService.java`)

This section transcribes the code's actual arithmetic — line-by-line, using the same input variable names as §1 — so a reader can compare it directly to §1.

### 2.1 Compute orchestrator

`computeDividendIncome` at `TaxReturnComputeService.java:7559-7755`. Called by `computeInterestIncome` at `:7027`. Receives the two personal forms, the list of 1099-DIV statement entries, the two SSNs, two boolean had-dividend flags, the legacy-mode boolean, and `form8814QualifiedDividendsTotal` (sum across all child Form 8814 line 9 outputs).

### 2.2 Per-person dividend computation

`computeDividendForPerson` at `:7757-7884`. Called once for taxpayer and once for spouse.

```
ordinaryFromStatements   = Σ entry.totalOrdinaryDividendsAmount
                              for each 1099-DIV whose recipientTIN belongs
                              to this person via belongsToPerson()
qualifiedFromStatements  = Σ entry.qualifiedDividendsAmount  (same filter)
section199aTotal         = Σ entry.section199ADividendsAmount   (box 5)
pabDividends             = Σ entry.specifiedPrivateActivityBondInterest
                              DividendsAmount  (box 13 — feeds Form 6251 line 2g)

ordinary                 = ordinaryFromStatements
                         + manualOrdinaryDividendsNotOnStatements
candidateQualified       = qualifiedFromStatements
                         + manualQualifiedDividendsNotOnStatements
nomineeOrdinary          = nomineeOrdinaryDividends   (from personal form)
nomineeQualified         = nomineeQualifiedDividends  (from personal form)

disallowedQualifiedTotal = nomineeQualified
                         + nonQualifiedFromHoldingPeriodCommon61of121
                         + nonQualifiedFromHoldingPeriodPreferred91of181
                         + nonQualifiedFromRelatedPaymentObligationShortSale
                         + nonQualifiedPaymentsInLieu
                         + nonQualifiedSurrogateForeignCorporationDividends

line3b_perPerson         = subtractNonNegative(ordinary, nomineeOrdinary)
line3a_perPerson         = subtractNonNegative(candidateQualified,
                                                disallowedQualifiedTotal)

# Per-person Line 3a ≤ Line 3b cap (silent cap; no flag)
if line3a_perPerson > line3b_perPerson:
    line3a_perPerson = line3b_perPerson
```

Helper `subtractNonNegative(a, b)`: returns `null` if `a` is `null`; otherwise returns `max(0, a − (b ?? 0))`. Null preserves "no contribution"; zero means "concept applied, summed to zero."

### 2.3 Return-level aggregation

Continuing in `computeDividendIncome` at `:7590-7647`:

```
line3a = addNonNull(taxpayer.qualifiedDividends, spouse.qualifiedDividends)
line3b = addNonNull(taxpayer.ordinaryDividends,  spouse.ordinaryDividends)

# Form 8814 child qualified dividend symmetric addition
if form8814QualifiedDividendsTotal > 0:
    line3a += form8814QualifiedDividendsTotal
    line3b += form8814QualifiedDividendsTotal
    # Both Line 3c checkboxes will be marked TRUE further down.

# Return-level Line 3a ≤ Line 3b cap (with flag)
if line3a > 0 and line3b > 0 and line3a > line3b:
    line3a = line3b
    flags.add(QUALIFIED_DIVIDENDS_LIMITED_TO_ORDINARY_DIVIDENDS,
              blocking=false)

line3a = roundMoney(line3a)    # whole-dollar rounding
```

The result becomes `DividendComputation.line3aQualifiedDividends`, then `Income.setQualifiedDividends(line3a)` at `:6688`, then PDF field `line3a_qualified_dividends` on Form 1040 page 1.

### 2.4 Married Filing Separately (MFS) suppression

`computeInterestIncome` at `:6926-6927` sets `dividendIncomeSpouse = null` and forces `spouseHadDividend = false` whenever the filing status is Married Filing Separately. This cascades into `computeDividendForPerson("spouse", ...)`, which returns the empty `DividendPersonTotals(null, null, false, null, null, [], null)`. The aggregation at `:7590-7591` then becomes taxpayer-only — `addNonNull(taxpayer.qualifiedDividends, null) = taxpayer.qualifiedDividends`.

### 2.5 Whole-dollar rounding

`roundMoney(line3a)` at `:7747` rounds to scale 0 with banker's rounding (`RoundingMode.HALF_EVEN`). Applied once, at the final step, after all additions and caps.

### 2.6 Schedule B Part II trigger

Not directly part of Line 3a, but the dividend orchestrator computes `scheduleBRequiredFromDividends` at `:7662-7664`:
```
scheduleBRequiredFromDividends = (line3b > $1,500)
                              OR any nominee dividend present
```

### 2.7 Line 3c disclosure checkboxes (new for 2025)

At `:7728-7754`:
```
hasChildQualifiedDividends = (form8814QualifiedDividendsTotal > 0)
line3cChildDividendsInLine3a = TRUE if hasChildQualifiedDividends else null
line3cChildDividendsInLine3b = TRUE if hasChildQualifiedDividends else null
```

Both fire together because Form 8814 line 9 (the only child-dividend amount that reaches Line 3a or Line 3b) routes to both symmetrically.

## 5. Compute order and downstream consumers

### 5.1 Upstream dependencies

The Line 3a compute requires these to be ready first:
1. **Taxpayer and spouse SSN** — from `identification-taxpayer` and `identification-spouse` personal forms. Used for per-person filtering via `belongsToPerson`.
2. **Filing status** — from `filing-status` personal form. Drives Married Filing Separately suppression.
3. **1099-DIV statement entries** — from `statementDataService.listEntriesWithData("1099-div")`.
4. **Dividend personal forms** — `dividend-income-taxpayer` and `dividend-income-spouse`.
5. **Form 8814 line 9 sum** — computed by `computeChildInterestDividends` BEFORE `computeInterestIncome` runs. The `prepare()` orchestrator extracts `form8814QualifiedDividendsTotal` from `form8814List` at `:406-409` and passes it into `computeInterestIncome` at `:420`, which forwards it to `computeDividendIncome` at `:7036`.

### 5.2 Downstream consumers

Line 3a feeds:
1. **Form 1040 PDF rendering** — `Income.getQualifiedDividends()` → `line3a_qualified_dividends` text field.
2. **Form 1040 Line 16 (Tax)** — qualified dividends > 0 triggers the Qualified Dividends and Capital Gain Tax Worksheet (or Schedule D Tax Worksheet if Schedule D has cap gains/qualified divs). Line 3a is NOT taxed at ordinary rates; the worksheet applies the 0% / 15% / 20% preferential brackets. See `computeQDCGWorksheet` and `computeScheduleDTaxWorksheet` invoked from `computeLine16`.
3. **Line 3c disclosure checkboxes** — set TRUE when Form 8814 child qualified dividends are present.
4. **NOT included in Line 9 (Total income)** — Line 3a is a SUBSET of Line 3b; only Line 3b is added to Total income. Including Line 3a in Line 9 would double-count.

### 5.3 Compute call order

```
prepare()                                            # entry point
  ├─ computeChildInterestDividends(...)              # produces form8814List
  │     └─ extracts form8814QualifiedDividendsTotal
  └─ computeInterestIncome(..., form8814QualifiedDividendsTotal, ...)
        └─ computeDividendIncome(...)                # produces line3a, line3b
              ├─ computeDividendForPerson("taxpayer", ...)
              ├─ computeDividendForPerson("spouse",   ...)
              ├─ (aggregate)
              ├─ (add form8814QualifiedDividendsTotal symmetrically)
              ├─ (return-level cap with flag)
              └─ (Line 3c checkboxes)
```

## 7. Common filer mistakes (per IRS instructions and Pub. 17)

These are the mistakes the IRS instructions and Pub. 17 explicitly warn against. The application either prevents or warns about them where indicated.

| # | Mistake | What the application does |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Treating box 1b as automatically fully qualified without applying the disallowed-category subtractions | The five disallowed-category fields exist on the personal form; the user is expected to enter them when they apply. |
| 2 | Putting Line 3a > Line 3b | Two-level cap (per-person silent + return-level with flag). The user cannot accidentally save Line 3a > Line 3b in the rendered PDF. |
| 3 | Misrouting 1099-DIV box 2a (capital gain distributions) into Line 3a | Box 2a is read by the capital-gains compute (`computeCapitalForPerson`), not the dividend compute. Line 3a only reads `qualifiedDividendsAmount`. |
| 4 | Misrouting 1099-DIV box 12 (exempt-interest dividends) into Line 3a | Box 12 is read by `computeInterestIncome` and added to `taxExemptInterest`. Line 3a only reads box 1b. |
| 5 | Putting Form 8814 child ordinary dividends (line 2a) directly into Line 3b | Form 8814 routing is fixed in `computeDividendIncome`: only `form8814QualifiedDividendsTotal` (line 9) reaches Lines 3a/3b. The non-qualified ordinary portion routes via Form 8814 line 12 → Schedule 1 line 8z → Form 1040 Line 8. |
| 6 | Leaving Line 3c blank when Form 8814 is in use and child has qualified dividends | `line3cChildDividendsInLine3a` is set TRUE automatically whenever `form8814QualifiedDividendsTotal > 0`. |
| 7 | Assuming all foreign-source dividends in box 1b are qualified | The application currently has only the `nonQualifiedSurrogateForeignCorporationDividends` field. Other non-treaty foreign-corporation dividends are not separately addressable — see §3.2 Gap 1. |
| 8 | Forgetting that nominee dividends require Schedule B Part II disclosure | Nominee dividends trigger Schedule B Part II regardless of amount. The per-payer Schedule B detail emission is partial (see §3.2 Gap 5). |
