## Glossary

- **Form 1040 Line 3b** — "Ordinary dividends." The total taxable dividend income from corporate distributions of earnings and profits. Includes the qualified-dividend subset that lands on Line 3a; the qualified amounts are reported in BOTH places, not subtracted from Line 3b.
- **Form 1040 Line 3a** — "Qualified dividends." A strict subset of Line 3b that qualifies for preferential capital-gain tax rates per Internal Revenue Code §1(h)(11). Always less than or equal to Line 3b.
- **Form 1040 Line 3c** — "Check if your child's dividends are included in 1 ☐ Line 3a   2 ☐ Line 3b." Pair of disclosure checkboxes new in tax year 2025; flips on when Form 8814 line 9 contributes to Line 3a/3b.
- **Form 1099-DIV** — "Dividends and Distributions." Payer-issued information return. Box 1a = total ordinary dividends (the primary feed for Line 3b). Box 1b = qualified-dividend subset (feeds Line 3a). Box 2a = capital gain distributions (NOT on Line 3b — these go to Line 7a/Schedule D). Box 3 = nondividend distributions (basis adjustment, NOT income). Box 5 = Section 199A dividends (feeds Line 13a Qualified Business Income deduction, NOT Line 3b directly). Box 12 = exempt-interest dividends (feeds Line 2a tax-exempt interest, NOT Line 3b). Box 13 = Specified Private Activity Bond dividends (feeds Form 6251 line 2g Alternative Minimum Tax preference, NOT Line 3b).
- **Nominee dividend** — A dividend received in the taxpayer's name (because the brokerage account is in the taxpayer's name) but legally belonging to another person. The actual owner reports it on their own return; the nominee subtracts it on their Schedule B Part II.
- **Form 8814** — "Parents' Election To Report Child's Interest and Dividends." When a parent elects to include a qualifying child's investment income on the parent's own return, Form 8814 line 9 (the qualified-dividend portion above the $2,700 base) flows symmetrically to BOTH parent Line 3a and Line 3b.
- **Schedule B (Form 1040)** — "Interest and Ordinary Dividends." Supplemental form Part II of which lists each dividend payer separately and sums to Line 3b. Required when Line 3b > $1,500 OR when nominee dividends are present (regardless of amount).
- **Married Filing Separately (MFS)** — A filing status under which each spouse files an independent return. The application's MFS guard suppresses spouse-side reads from the dividend orchestrator to prevent one spouse's data from leaking onto the other's Line 3b.

## 1. IRS rule for Line 3b (computed before reading the code)

This section states what the IRS expects the Line 3b value to be, based on the 2025 instructions and the cited authorities. The next section (§2) describes what the code actually computes, and §3 compares the two.

### 1.1 Plain-English equation

Conceptually, Line 3b = "How much total ordinary dividend income you earned in 2025, after subtracting any amount you received in your name on behalf of someone else, and including the qualified-dividend portion of any child's investment income you elected to report on your own return under Form 8814."

In ordinary arithmetic:

```
totalOrdinaryDividends   =  sum of (1099-DIV box 1a across all your 1099-DIVs)
                         +  manual ordinary dividends not reported on any
                            1099-DIV (rare; usually only happens when a payer
                            is below the $10 reporting floor or is foreign)

line3b_perPerson         =  max(0, totalOrdinaryDividends − nomineeOrdinaryDividends)

line3b_return            =  taxpayer.line3b_perPerson
                         +  spouse.line3b_perPerson (only when filing
                                                     status is Married Filing
                                                     Jointly OR Qualifying
                                                     Surviving Spouse — NOT
                                                     when Married Filing
                                                     Separately)

line3b                   =  line3b_return + Form_8814_line_9_total
                            (where Form_8814_line_9_total is the sum of each
                             elected child's qualified-dividend portion above
                             the $2,700 Form 8814 base amount; the SAME
                             amount also goes on Line 3a per the Form 8814
                             instructions)
```

The two IRS invariants that bound Line 3b:
- **Non-negative invariant**: Line 3b cannot be less than zero. The nominee subtraction floors at zero rather than going negative if a user mis-enters a nominee amount that exceeds their statement totals.
- **Inclusion-of-qualified invariant**: Line 3a (qualified dividends) is a strict subset of Line 3b, so Line 3a ≤ Line 3b always. Line 3b is the larger of the two.

### 1.4 Decision tree (which path applies)

```
Did you have any dividend income for the tax year?
├── No  → Line 3b = null (blank); STOP (no Schedule B; no further work)
└── Yes ↓

Did at least one Form 1099-DIV statement get uploaded?
├── No  → BLOCKING flag DIVIDEND_STATEMENT_UPLOAD_REQUIRED; cannot compute
└── Yes ↓

Did you confirm all received 1099-DIVs are uploaded?
├── No  → BLOCKING flag DIVIDEND_1099_DIV_UPLOAD_CONFIRMATION_REQUIRED
└── Yes ↓

For each person (taxpayer; spouse only if filing status ≠ MFS):
    For each 1099-DIV whose recipientTIN matches this person's SSN:
        ordinaryFromStatements +=  totalOrdinaryDividendsAmount (box 1a)
    ordinary       =  ordinaryFromStatements + manualOrdinaryDividendsNotOnStatements
    nomineeOrd     =  nomineeOrdinaryDividends (may be null/0)
    line3b_person  =  max(0, ordinary − nomineeOrd)

line3b_aggregate   =  taxpayer.line3b_person + spouse.line3b_person  (spouse=null in MFS)

Is there a Form 8814 election for any dependent with line-4 > $2,700?
├── Yes → line3b += sum of Form 8814 line 9 across all such children
└── No  → no Form 8814 contribution

Is line3a (after its own compute) > line3b?  (defense-in-depth invariant)
├── Yes → cap line3a = line3b; emit non-blocking flag QUALIFIED_DIVIDENDS_LIMITED_TO_ORDINARY_DIVIDENDS
└── No  → no cap needed

Is line3b > $1,500 OR is any nominee-dividend signal present (boolean Yes,
  nomineeOrdinary > 0, or nomineeQualified > 0)?
├── Yes → emit Schedule B; populate Part II per-payer rows + nominee
          subtraction row; set scheduleB.line6TotalOrdinaryDividends = line3b
└── No  → no Schedule B

Round line3b to whole dollars (half-to-even) once at the end.
```

### 1.6 What MUST NOT contribute to Line 3b (explicit exclusions)

Per Publication 17 chapter "Dividends and Other Distributions" and the 2025 Form 1040 instructions, the following 1099-DIV boxes look related but do NOT belong on Line 3b. Each row says where the amount actually goes.

| 1099-DIV box | Field name | Correct destination | Reason |
|---|---|---|---|
| Box 2a (Total capital gain distributions) | `totalCapitalGainDistributionsAmount` | Form 1040 Line 7a / Schedule D | These are long-term capital gains, not dividends. Misrouting to Line 3b would tax them as ordinary income instead of at preferential capital-gain rates. |
| Box 3 (Nondividend distributions) | `nondividendDistributionsAmount` | NOT income — reduces cost basis | Per Publication 17, a return-of-capital distribution reduces basis and only becomes taxable when basis reaches zero (then it is capital gain, not dividend income). |
| Box 5 (Section 199A dividends) | `section199ADividendsAmount` | Form 1040 Line 13a (Qualified Business Income deduction) | Section 199A is the QBI-eligible subset of ordinary dividends from Real Estate Investment Trusts. It does NOT add to Line 3b independently — it is already counted in box 1a. Misrouting would double-count. |
| Box 12 (Exempt-interest dividends) | `exemptInterestDividendsAmount` | Form 1040 Line 2a (Tax-exempt interest) | These are tax-exempt municipal-bond interest passing through a mutual fund. They go on Line 2a as tax-exempt interest, not as taxable dividends. |
| Box 13 (Specified Private Activity Bond interest dividends) | `specifiedPrivateActivityBondInterestDividendsAmount` | Form 6251 line 2g (Alternative Minimum Tax preference) | Box 13 is a subset of box 12 that is taxable for Alternative Minimum Tax purposes only. Adds to AMT preferences; does not change regular-tax Line 3b. |
| Box 7 (Foreign tax paid) | `foreignTaxPaidAmount` | Form 1116 / Schedule 3 line 1 (Foreign Tax Credit) | A credit against tax, not an income amount. |

### 1.7 Worked example

Filing status: Married Filing Jointly. Two 1099-DIVs and one Form 8814 child.

| Source | Amount |
|---|---|
| Taxpayer's 1099-DIV from Broker A — box 1a | $2,300 |
| Taxpayer's 1099-DIV from Broker A — box 1b (qualified subset of $2,300) | $1,800 |
| Spouse's 1099-DIV from Broker B — box 1a | $400 |
| Spouse's 1099-DIV from Broker B — box 1b | $400 |
| Taxpayer's manual ordinary not on any 1099-DIV | $50 |
| Taxpayer's nominee ordinary (paid to a sibling's joint-account share) | $200 |
| Child's Form 8814 line 4 = $1,200 (interest) + $2,800 (box 1a) + $500 (box 1b) = $4,500; line 5 = $2,700; line 6 = $1,800; line 9 = $1,800 × ($500 / $4,000) ≈ $225 (qualified-div proportion of income above $2,700) | $225 |

Per-person computation:
- Taxpayer ordinary = $2,300 + $50 = $2,350; nominee = $200; **Taxpayer Line 3b contribution** = max(0, $2,350 − $200) = **$2,150**.
- Spouse ordinary = $400; nominee = $0; **Spouse Line 3b contribution** = **$400**.

Aggregation:
- line3b_return = $2,150 + $400 = $2,550 (spouse included because filing status is MFJ).
- line3b = $2,550 + $225 (Form 8814 line 9) = **$2,775**.

Cap check:
- line3a = $1,800 + $400 + $225 = $2,425 (no disallowed categories).
- $2,425 < $2,775 → no cap fires.
- Line 3a final = $2,425; Line 3b final = **$2,775**.

Schedule B:
- $2,775 > $1,500 → Schedule B required.
- Also: a nominee subtraction is present → Schedule B Part II shows: Broker A $2,300; Broker B $400; "Taxpayer nominee dividend distribution" −$200; subtotal = $2,500; plus manual $50 + Form 8814 $225 added to line 6 = **$2,775**.

Line 3c:
- Form 8814 contributed a non-zero qualified amount → `line3cChildDividendsInLine3a` = TRUE AND `line3cChildDividendsInLine3b` = TRUE (symmetric trigger).

## 2. Code implementation (what the code actually does)

The Line 3b compute path lives in `TaxReturnComputeService.java`. Three methods participate:

| Method | Approx. location | Role |
|---|---|---|
| `prepare()` | `:387` (sums Form 8814 line-9 totals into `form8814QualifiedDividendsTotal`) | Aggregates per-child Form 8814 line-9 amounts. |
| `computeDividendIncome()` | `:7585+` | The orchestrator. Reads both spouses' dividend forms, runs Married Filing Separately suppression, calls `computeDividendForPerson` twice, sums results, adds Form 8814 line 9 symmetrically, enforces the Line 3a ≤ Line 3b cap, derives the Schedule B trigger, emits Section 199A advisory flags, and packages outputs. |
| `computeDividendForPerson()` | `:7757-7908` | Runs once per person. Reads the dividend personal form (taxpayer or spouse), iterates over 1099-DIV entries filtered by `belongsToPerson()`, sums box 1a into `ordinary`, applies manual amounts, subtracts nominee ordinary using `subtractNonNegative` (zero-floored, null-preserving), and returns a `DividendPersonTotals` record. |

### 2.1 Step-by-step code transcription

The arithmetic shape, with code line references:

```
For person ∈ { taxpayer, spouse (suppressed under Married Filing Separately) }:

    ordinaryFromStatements          := 0  // null per the canonical 0-vs-null pattern; nulls treated as zero in addition
    for each entry in 1099-DIV entries:
        if not belongsToPerson(entry):     // :7788 — filter by recipientTIN
            continue
        entryOrdinary               := parseAmount(entry.totalOrdinaryDividendsAmount)
        ordinaryFromStatements      := addNonNull(ordinaryFromStatements, entryOrdinary)  // :7792
        // (also accumulates qualifiedFromStatements, pabDividends, section199aTotal,
        //  and appends a Schedule B per-payer row at :7804)

    if not useLegacyDividendComputation:                                    // :7814
        ordinary                    := addNonNull(ordinary,
                                                 manualOrdinaryDividendsNotOnStatements)  // :7815
        nomineeOrdinary             := getAmount(form, nomineeOrdinaryDividends)           // :7818

    line3b_person                   := subtractNonNegative(ordinary, nomineeOrdinary)      // :7874

    // 3a Gap 5 closure — emit the Pub. 550 "Nominee Distribution" negative row.
    addScheduleBItem(scheduleBDividendItems,                                                // :7886
                     personLabel + " nominee dividend distribution",
                     negateIfPresent(nomineeOrdinary))

    return DividendPersonTotals(line3a_person, line3b_person, ...)


At the orchestrator (computeDividendIncome):

    line3b                          := addNonNull(taxpayer.ordinaryDividends(),
                                                 spouse.ordinaryDividends())  // implicit aggregation through DividendPersonTotals

    hasChildQualifiedDividends      := hasPositiveAmount(form8814QualifiedDividendsTotal)  // :7621
    if hasChildQualifiedDividends:                                                          // :7622
        line3a                      := addNonNull(line3a, form8814QualifiedDividendsTotal)  // :7623
        line3b                      := addNonNull(line3b, form8814QualifiedDividendsTotal)  // :7624

    if line3a > line3b > 0:                                                                  // :7640
        line3a                      := line3b                                                // :7641
        flags.add(QUALIFIED_DIVIDENDS_LIMITED_TO_ORDINARY_DIVIDENDS, non-blocking)            // :7642-7646

    hasNomineeDividends             := taxpayer.nomineeDividendsPresent()
                                       OR spouse.nomineeDividendsPresent()                   // :7662
    scheduleBRequiredFromDividends  := (line3b > $1,500) OR hasNomineeDividends              // :7663-7664

    Final whole-dollar rounding: roundMoney(line3b) applied once when packaging the
        DividendComputation record at :7748.
```

### 2.2 Married Filing Separately guard

The orchestrator at `computeDividendIncome` is one of approximately 20 orchestrators in the codebase that participate in the single-guard Married-Filing-Separately cascade. The guard takes the form: when `isMfsReturn` is true, the spouse-side dividend form, spouse SSN, and spouse `hadDividendIncome` flag are passed as `null` / `false` so the spouse path in `computeDividendForPerson` early-returns at `:7778-7780` and contributes nothing. This guarantees that a stale spouse personal form cannot leak into the taxpayer's Line 3b on a Married Filing Separately return.

### 2.3 Canonical zero-versus-null compliance

All four BigDecimal fields on `DividendPersonTotals` (`qualifiedDividends`, `ordinaryDividends`, `disallowedQualifiedTotal`, `pabDividends`, `section199aTotal`) follow the "null when not applicable, zero when applicable but sums to zero" convention. The Line 3b path:

```
ordinaryFromStatements (null at first)
   → addNonNull (preserves null when both addends are null)
   → manual addNonNull
   → subtractNonNegative(ordinary, nomineeOrdinary)
```

`subtractNonNegative(null, anything)` returns `null` per the helper. So when no 1099-DIV entries match the person AND no manual amounts AND no nominee amounts, Line 3b stays `null` (which renders as blank on the Form 1040, not as "$0"). Zero is only produced when the user has actual dividend activity that nets to zero (e.g., $100 box 1a with $100 nominee subtraction).

### 2.4 Schedule B Part II disclosure rows

For each 1099-DIV entry that belongs to the person, a row is appended to `scheduleBDividendItems` with payerName from the 1099-DIV `payerNameAddress` field (defaulting to "1099-DIV Payer" if blank) and the gross box 1a amount. Then, after the nominee subtraction is computed, a single negative-amount row is appended with the label "{Taxpayer|Spouse} nominee dividend distribution" — this is the Publication 550 pattern required to show the IRS exactly which amount was subtracted (this closure was added on 2026-06-03 per the Gap 5 fix). The `addScheduleBItem` helper null/zero-skips, so the nominee row is omitted when there is no nominee amount.

### 2.5 Section 199A advisory (not a contribution to Line 3b)

After the orchestrator computes Line 3b for both spouses, it compares each person's Section 199A total (box 5) against that person's ordinary dividend total (box 1a). If box 5 > box 1a (which violates the IRS rule that Section 199A is a subset of ordinary), a non-blocking flag `SECTION199A_DIVIDENDS_EXCEED_ORDINARY_DIVIDENDS_TAXPAYER` or `..._SPOUSE` is added. This is purely advisory; it does NOT alter Line 3b or trigger any cap on box 5.

### 4.4 Compute order and dependencies

1. **Upstream**: `identification-{taxpayer,spouse}` (SSN for `belongsToPerson` filtering); `filing-status` (Married Filing Separately suppression); `dividend-income-{taxpayer,spouse}` personal forms (manual amounts, nominee amounts, has-nominee flag); `1099-DIV` statements; `child-interest-dividends` entries (Form 8814 line 9 producer); `1099-INT` statements with `recipientTIN` matching a dependent SSN (Form 8814 statement-driven path).
2. **Self**: `computeDividendIncome` is called from `prepare()` after `computeInterestIncome` runs. Internally calls `computeDividendForPerson` twice.
3. **Downstream**:
   - `form1040.income.ordinaryDividends` → Form 1040 Line 9 (Total income) via `buildIncome`. Note that Line 3a is a strict subset of Line 3b and is NOT independently added to Line 9 (it would double-count).
   - `scheduleB.line6TotalOrdinaryDividends` → conditional Schedule B Part II.
   - `form6251.line2gPrivateActivityBondInterest` → Form 6251 AMT preference (sourced from box 13 alongside the Line 2b interest path).
   - `form1040.income.line3cChildDividendsInLine3b` → Form 1040 PDF checkbox.

## 6. Common mistakes (Line 3b specifically)

These mistakes are NOT present in the current code, but are listed because they have historically appeared in dividend-line implementations and the verified-correct items in §3.3 protect against them:

- Adding box 1b (qualified) to Line 3b separately from box 1a (which would double-count, since box 1b is already a subset of box 1a).
- Subtracting box 1b from box 1a "to keep them separate" (which would undercount Line 3b).
- Misrouting box 2a capital gain distributions to Line 3b instead of Line 7a.
- Misrouting box 12 exempt-interest dividends to Line 3b instead of Line 2a.
- Adding box 5 Section 199A on top of box 1a (which would double-count Section 199A since it is already in box 1a).
- Adding the child's full ordinary dividends (Form 8814 line 2a) to Line 3b instead of just the line-9 qualified portion (the non-qualified portion routes to Schedule 1 line 8z via Form 8814 line 12; adding line 2a to Line 3b would double-count it).
- Failing to subtract nominee ordinary dividends from Line 3b on the nominee's return (which would over-tax the nominee on dividends that legally belong to someone else).
- Triggering Schedule B only at the $1,500 threshold and forgetting the nominee-presence trigger.
- Allowing spouse-side amounts to bleed into the taxpayer's Line 3b under Married Filing Separately.
- Forgetting to apply whole-dollar rounding once at the end (intermediate rounding produces drift).
