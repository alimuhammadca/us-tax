## Glossary

- **Form 1040 Line 4a** ŌĆö "IRA distributions." The **gross** (pre-tax-treatment) dollar amount of distributions from a traditional Individual Retirement Account, a Roth Individual Retirement Account, a Simplified Employee Pension (SEP) IRA, or a Savings Incentive Match Plan for Employees (SIMPLE) IRA. **Critically, Line 4a is left BLANK when the distribution is fully taxable** (everything goes to Line 4b instead) ŌĆö Line 4a is filled in only when something special happened (a rollover, a Qualified Charitable Distribution, a Health Savings Account Funding Distribution, a Form 8606 basis-recovery case, or some other reason the gross differs from the taxable amount).
- **Form 1040 Line 4b** ŌĆö "Taxable amount." The taxable subset of Line 4a (or the full distribution amount if Line 4a is blank). Line 4b is the amount that flows to Line 9 (total income). See `XLS/Computations/4b.md`.
- **Form 1040 Line 4c** ŌĆö Pair of checkboxes (box 1 = rollover; box 2 = Qualified Charitable Distribution) + a write-in space (box 3) that discloses the reason Line 4a differs from Line 4b. See `XLS/Computations/4c.md`.
- **Individual Retirement Account (IRA)** ŌĆö A personal retirement account. Comes in two main flavors: **traditional** (contributions are typically tax-deductible going in, distributions are taxable coming out) and **Roth** (contributions are not deductible going in, qualifying distributions are tax-free coming out). Two employer-sponsored variants ŌĆö **SEP IRA** and **SIMPLE IRA** ŌĆö are taxed like traditional IRAs for distribution purposes.
- **Form 1099-R** ŌĆö "Distributions From Pensions, Annuities, Retirement or Profit-Sharing Plans, IRAs, Insurance Contracts, etc." The information return a payer sends when they distribute money from a retirement account. **Box 1** = gross distribution. **Box 2a** = taxable amount (the payer's calculation). **Box 2b** = "taxable amount not determined" checkbox. **Box 4** = federal income tax withheld. **Box 7** = distribution code (one or two letters indicating what kind of distribution this was). The **"IRA/SEP/SIMPLE" checkbox** on the 1099-R is what routes the entry to Line 4a/4b/4c (when checked) vs. Line 5a/5b/5c (when unchecked).
- **Rollover** ŌĆö Moving money from one retirement account to another within 60 days. The amount moved is shown on Line 4a but not on Line 4b (since it isn't taxed). Internal Revenue Code ┬¦408(d)(3).
- **Qualified Charitable Distribution (QCD)** ŌĆö A direct transfer from a traditional IRA to a qualified charity, available to taxpayers age 70┬Į or older. The amount is shown on Line 4a but excluded from Line 4b (up to the $108,000 annual cap for 2025). Internal Revenue Code ┬¦408(d)(8).
- **Health Savings Account Funding Distribution (HFD)** ŌĆö A once-in-a-lifetime direct transfer from an IRA to a Health Savings Account, up to the year's HSA contribution limit. The amount is shown on Line 4a but excluded from Line 4b. Internal Revenue Code ┬¦408(d)(9).
- **Form 8606** ŌĆö "Nondeductible IRAs." A separate IRS form that tracks basis (the after-tax-money portion) in traditional IRAs and Roth IRAs. When Form 8606 applies, the taxable amount on Line 4b is computed by Form 8606's internal arithmetic ŌĆö NOT directly from 1099-R box 2a.
- **Roth distribution codes** (1099-R box 7) ŌĆö Code **Q** means a fully qualified Roth distribution (the 5-year holding period was met AND a qualifying event occurred such as age 59┬Į, death, disability, or first-time home purchase). Codes **J** and **T** mean Roth distributions that are NOT fully qualified ŌĆö these require Form 8606 Part III to compute the basis-recovery taxable amount.
- **Married Filing Separately (MFS)** ŌĆö A filing status under which each spouse files an independent return. The application's MFS guard suppresses spouse-side reads from the IRA orchestrator so spouse-attributed 1099-Rs cannot leak onto the taxpayer's Line 4a.

## 1. IRS rule for Line 4a (computed before reading the code)

### 1.1 Plain-English equation

Conceptually, Line 4a asks: "What's the total gross amount that came out of your IRAs this year, **but only if part of it didn't end up taxable** ŌĆö for example because you rolled it over, gave it to charity, transferred it to a Health Savings Account, or recovered basis through Form 8606?"

If every dollar that came out of every one of your IRAs was fully taxable (no rollover, no charity transfer, no HSA transfer, no Form 8606 basis recovery, no other exception), then **Line 4a is left blank** and the full amount goes on Line 4b only.

In ordinary arithmetic:

```
gross_ira_for_person  =  sum of (1099-R box 1) across all 1099-Rs where:
                            (a) the "IRA/SEP/SIMPLE" checkbox is checked, AND
                            (b) the recipient's TIN matches this person's SSN
                          (taxpayer or spouse, treated separately first)

gross_ira_return      =  taxpayer.gross_ira_for_person
                      +  spouse.gross_ira_for_person  (only when filing
                                                       status is Married
                                                       Filing Jointly or
                                                       Qualifying Surviving
                                                       Spouse ŌĆö NOT under
                                                       Married Filing
                                                       Separately)

any_exception_applies =  (any rollover amount > 0) OR
                         (Form 8606 basis-tracking applies for either
                            spouse ŌĆö see ┬¦1.4) OR
                         (any Qualified Charitable Distribution > 0) OR
                         (any Health Savings Account Funding Distribution
                            > 0) OR
                         (any "other" Line 4c write-in is present) OR
                         (any 1099-R has box 2b "taxable amount not
                            determined" checked AND additional facts say
                            something is non-taxable)

fully_taxable_overall =  (gross_ira_return > 0) AND
                         NOT any_exception_applies AND
                         (taxable_ira_return > 0) AND
                         (gross_ira_return == taxable_ira_return)

line_4a               =  fully_taxable_overall
                            ? null  ŌåÉ BLANK on the printed Form 1040
                            : round_to_whole_dollar(gross_ira_return)
```

The most important rule: **Line 4a is BLANK when the distribution is fully taxable.** This is unusual compared to most income lines; most lines show the gross. Line 4a follows the IRS form's instructions literally ŌĆö show gross **only** when it differs from the taxable amount.

### 1.6 What MUST NOT contribute to Line 4a (explicit exclusions)

Per Publication 17 chapter on Retirement Plans and the 2025 Form 1040 instructions:

| 1099-R or other source | Routes to | Reason |
|---|---|---|
| 1099-R with `iraSepSimple` UNCHECKED (pension, annuity, 401(k), 403(b), 457(b)) | Form 1040 Line 5a / 5b / 5c (via `computePensionAnnuityIncome`) | Lines 4a/4b are IRA-only by statute. Non-IRA retirement plans use the parallel Lines 5a/5b/5c. |
| 1099-R box 4 (federal withholding) | Form 1040 Line 25b (other federal withholding) | Withholding is a payment, not income. |
| Income reported on box 1 of a 1099-INT or 1099-DIV that happens to come from an IRA account that's mislabeled | Nowhere (data error) | An IRA distribution should arrive on a 1099-R, not a 1099-INT/DIV. The application does not look at 1099-INT/DIV for IRA distributions. |
| Direct trustee-to-trustee transfers (not a taxable distribution) | Not reported anywhere | A trustee-to-trustee transfer is not a distribution. No 1099-R should be issued. If one is issued in error, the user must not report it. |

### 1.7 Worked examples

**Example A ŌĆö Fully taxable simple case (Line 4a stays BLANK):**
- One 1099-R: IRA/SEP/SIMPLE checked, box 1 = $5,000, box 2a = $5,000, box 2b unchecked, no Roth codes.
- User confirms no rollover, no Qualified Charitable Distribution, no Health Savings Account Funding Distribution, no Form 8606 triggers, no other write-in.
- Computation: grossIra = $5,000; taxableIra = $5,000; no exceptions; gross == taxable.
- **Line 4a = blank (null in JSON).**
- Line 4b = $5,000.

**Example B ŌĆö Pure rollover (Line 4a carries gross):**
- One 1099-R: IRA/SEP/SIMPLE checked, box 1 = $20,000, box 2a = $20,000.
- User entered `totalIraRolloverAmount` = $20,000.
- Computation: grossIra = $20,000; taxableIra = subtractNonNegative($20,000, $20,000) = $0; `hasRollover = true` ŌåÆ `hasAnyException = true`; the "fully taxable" test FAILS at the `NOT hasAnyException` condition.
- **Line 4a = $20,000.**
- Line 4b = $0.
- Line 4c box 1 (rollover) = TRUE.

**Example C ŌĆö Qualified Charitable Distribution with $108k cap considerations:**
- One 1099-R: IRA/SEP/SIMPLE checked, box 1 = $50,000, box 2a = $50,000. Taxpayer is 75 years old.
- User entered `totalQcdAmount` = $50,000, `inheritedIraQcdAmount` = $0, `deductibleIraContributionsAfterAge70Half` = $0.

- Line 4c box 2 (Qualified Charitable Distribution) = TRUE.

**Example D ŌĆö Form 8606 basis recovery (Exception 2):**
- One 1099-R: IRA/SEP/SIMPLE checked, box 1 = $10,000, box 2a = $10,000.
- User entered `hasNondeductibleTraditionalBasis = true`, `basisPrevFromPriorYear8606Line14` = $30,000, `valueAllTraditionalSepSimpleIrasAtYearEnd` = $90,000.

**Example E ŌĆö Multi-exception breakout statement triggered:**

- Line 4b = $30,000 ŌłÆ $10,000 ŌłÆ $10,000 ŌłÆ $5,000 = $5,000.

**Example F ŌĆö Married Filing Separately spouse-leakage guard:**
- Filing status: Married Filing Separately.

### 2.2 Married Filing Separately guard

The orchestrator at `:7918-7939` is one of 9 orchestrators in the codebase that participate in the single-guard MFS cascade. The guard takes the form: when `isMfsReturn` is true, the spouse's `ira-income-spouse` form, the spouse SSN, and the spouse-side 1099-R attribution all become null/false ŌåÆ the spouse path inside `computeIraForPerson` produces an empty `IraPersonComputation` ŌåÆ aggregation at `:8158-8159` stays taxpayer-only. The 17-line breadcrumb explicitly cites the 7+ outputs protected through this single guard (line 4a gross, line 4b taxable, line 4c boxes 1/2/3, line 4c box 3 write-in text, both Form 8606 instances, breakout-statement / rollover-attachment / Qualified Charitable Distribution-to-split-interest-entity flags). Verified by lock-in test `mfsExcludesSpouseIraFromLine4a`.

### 2.3 IRA-vs-pension routing filter

The `iraSepSimple == true` filter at `:8242` is the cornerstone of correct Line 4a / Line 5a routing. The same `form1099REntries` list is shared between `computeIraForPerson` and `computePensionAnnuityIncome` ŌĆö each filters on the opposite side of `iraSepSimple`, so the two paths are mutually exclusive and no entry double-counts. If a 1099-R is mislabeled (the payer forgot to check the IRA/SEP/SIMPLE box on an actually-IRA distribution), it will silently route to Lines 5a/5b/5c. The user must verify the box is checked on each statement during upload; the application has no other way to detect the misrouting.

### 2.4 Per-person attribution via `belongsToPersonIra`

The TIN-matching helper at `:8245` routes each 1099-R to either the taxpayer or the spouse based on which `recipient_tin` field matches which person's SSN. There's a fallback heuristic (the "untinned" path) that assigns 1099-Rs lacking a recipient TIN to the spouse when the taxpayer has no IRA activity and the spouse does. Lock-in test `assignsUntinnedIraEntryToSpouseViaFallbackHeuristic`.

### 2.5 Roth code Q vs J/T

The two-counter design at `:8230-8276` distinguishes fully qualified Roth distributions (code Q ŌĆö no Form 8606 needed) from partially qualified Roth distributions (codes J/T ŌĆö Form 8606 Part III needed). When `rothCodeCount > 0 AND rothCodeJOrTCount == 0` (all Roth entries are code Q), `allRothEntriesFullyQualified` is true, which prevents `hasRothIraDistributions` from contributing to `hasException2` at `:8294`. Without this distinction, a code-Q-only Roth distribution would wrongly generate a Form 8606. Fix from `lines/4abc.md` ┬¦11 Known Limitation #1, applied 2026-04-14.

### 2.6 Canonical zero-vs-null compliance

All BigDecimal fields on `IraPersonComputation` (`grossIraDistributions`, `taxableIraDistributions`) follow the "null when concept does not apply, zero only when concept applies but sums to zero" convention. The gross-IRA path: `grossIra` starts null ŌåÆ `addNonNull` preserves null when both addends are null ŌåÆ `roundMoney(null)` stays null ŌåÆ constructor returns null. This is critical for the Line 4a blank-when-fully-taxable test: PDF rendering treats null as absent (line stays empty on the printed form), while zero would display "$0" ŌĆö wrong for users with no IRA activity.

### 4.4 Compute order and dependencies

   - 1099-R box 4 (federal withholding) ŌåÆ Form 1040 Line 25b via `computeWithholding`.

## 6. Common mistakes (Line 4a specifically)

These mistakes are NOT present in the current code, but are listed because they have historically appeared in IRA-distribution implementations:

- Showing the gross on Line 4a even when fully taxable (Line 4a should be blank in the fully-taxable simple case).
- Adding non-IRA 1099-R amounts (pension, 401(k), 403(b)) to Line 4a (those belong on Line 5a).
- Adding 1099-R box 4 (federal withholding) to Line 4a (withholding goes to Line 25b).
- Treating a direct trustee-to-trustee transfer as a distribution and reporting it on Line 4a.
- Letting a Married Filing Separately taxpayer's Line 4a include the spouse's distributions.
- Generating Form 8606 for a code-Q-only Roth distribution (fully qualified, no basis recovery needed).
- Applying the post-70┬Į deductible-contribution clawback to inherited-IRA Qualified Charitable Distributions (the carve-out at `:8318-8324` prevents this).
- Forgetting whole-dollar rounding once at the end (intermediate rounding produces drift).
- Forgetting to suppress the spouse path under Married Filing Separately.
