## Glossary

- **Form 1040 Line 5a** — Tax Year 2025 Form 1040 line that reports the **gross** (pre-tax-rules) amount of pensions and annuities received during the year, when reporting the gross amount is required. The line is **conditional**: it must be **blank** when every pension/annuity payment being reported is fully taxable, and it must show the **total gross amount** when any payment is partly taxable, rolled over, or otherwise subject to an exclusion.
- **Form 1040 Line 5b** — Companion line that reports the **taxable** portion. Line 5b is the line that contributes to taxable income (Line 9); Line 5a is disclosure-only.
- **Pension / annuity (non-Individual-Retirement-Account)** — Payments from a defined-benefit pension plan, defined-contribution employer plan (such as 401(k), 403(b), governmental 457(b)), qualified employee annuity, tax-sheltered annuity, military retirement pay, or other employer-sponsored retirement arrangement. Individual Retirement Account distributions (traditional, Roth, Simplified Employee Pension, SIMPLE) are reported on Form 1040 Lines 4a/4b instead and are explicitly excluded from this line.
- **Form 1099-R** — IRS statement issued by the payer of a retirement distribution. For pensions/annuities, box 1 holds the gross amount, box 2a the taxable amount as the payer computed it, box 2b a checkbox "Taxable amount not determined," box 4 federal tax withheld, box 5 employee contributions (basis) recovered tax-free, box 7 the distribution code(s), and a checkbox labeled "IRA/SEP/SIMPLE" near box 7. If the IRA/SEP/SIMPLE checkbox is checked, the distribution is reported on Lines 4a/4b, not Lines 5a/5b.
- **Form RRB-1099-R** — Railroad Retirement Board statement for railroad retirement pension/annuity income. Reports the gross paid in box 7 with detail in boxes 3 (employee contributions/cost), 4 (Non-Social-Security-Equivalent Benefit, the contributory portion), 5 (Vested Dual Benefit), and 6 (Supplemental annuity). RRB pension/annuity belongs on Form 1040 Line 5, not on Line 6 (which is the social-security equivalent portion of railroad retirement).
- **Simplified Method (Publication 575)** — Required basis-recovery worksheet for annuity payments from a qualified plan when the annuity starting date is after November 18, 1996. Uses age, total investment in the contract, and an IRS-published age factor to compute the tax-free portion of each payment.
- **General Rule (Publication 939)** — Basis-recovery method for annuities outside the Simplified Method scope (nonqualified plans, certain pre-November-19-1996 starting dates). Uses an "exclusion ratio" of investment in the contract divided by expected return.
- **Rollover** — Movement of a pension distribution to another qualified plan or to an Individual Retirement Account. A qualifying rollover is not taxable; it reduces the Line 5b taxable amount but the gross is still reported on Line 5a, and Line 5c box 1 is checked.
- **Retired Public Safety Officer (PSO) exclusion** — Internal Revenue Code §402(l). An eligible retired police officer, firefighter, emergency medical technician, etc. may exclude up to $3,000 per year of qualifying health, accident, or long-term-care insurance premiums paid from pension distributions. When the exclusion applies, Line 5c box 2 is checked.
- **Disability pension before minimum retirement age** — When a taxpayer receives a disability pension before reaching the employer plan's minimum retirement age, the payments are reported as **wages on Form 1040 Line 1h**, not on Line 5. Once the taxpayer reaches minimum retirement age, the payments switch to Line 5 reporting.
- **Married Filing Separately (MFS)** — Filing status. The application's MFS guard suppresses the spouse-side pension reads so spouse-attributed pension distributions cannot leak onto the taxpayer's separate return.
- **roundMoney** — Application helper that rounds a `BigDecimal` to whole dollars per the IRS whole-dollar convention; returns `null` when input is null.
- **addNonNull** — Application helper that adds two `BigDecimal` operands while preserving null when both are null (canonical zero-versus-null compliance).

## 1. IRS rule for Line 5a (computed before reading the code)

### 1.1 Plain-English equation

Conceptually, Line 5a asks: "How much did you receive **in total** from pensions and annuities this year?"

But the IRS only wants this number disclosed when at least one of the payments is not fully taxable. If every payment is fully taxable, Line 5a stays blank and Line 5b carries the whole amount.

```
Step 1 — collect the relevant payments
  pension_set =  every Form 1099-R distribution that is NOT marked
                 IRA/SEP/SIMPLE near box 7
              +  every Form RRB-1099-R railroad-retirement distribution
              −  every Form 1099-R already routed to Form 1040 Line 1h as
                 a disability pension before minimum retirement age
              −  every distribution belonging to a spouse on a Married
                 Filing Separately return (suppressed by the per-person guard)

Step 2 — compute per-person gross amount
  gross_for_person  =  sum of Form 1099-R box 1 (gross distribution amount)
                    +  sum of Form RRB-1099-R (box 7 when present, else the
                       sum of detail boxes 4 + 5 + 6)
                    (over every entry attributed to this person)

  gross_total  =  gross_for_taxpayer  +  gross_for_spouse
                  (spouse contribution is zero on Married Filing Separately)

Step 3 — compute per-person taxable amount
  taxable_for_person  =  the Line 5b computation chain
                         (Simplified Method or General Rule overrides,
                          then rollover reduction, then Public Safety Officer exclusion;
                          see XLS/Computations/5b.md for the full chain)

  taxable_total  =  taxable_for_taxpayer  +  taxable_for_spouse

Step 4 — decide whether Line 5a is blank
  any_exception  =  any of the following on the return:
                      • a rollover applies
                      • the Public Safety Officer exclusion applies
                      • the Simplified Method or General Rule is in use
                      • a 1099-R has "taxable amount not determined" checked (box 2b)
                      • a Line 5c box-3 other write-in applies

  fully_taxable_overall  =  (gross_total is positive)
                            AND (no exception applies)
                            AND (taxable_total is positive)
                            AND (gross_total == taxable_total)

  if fully_taxable_overall:
      line_5a  =  blank (null on the JSON output, empty on the printed form)
  else:
      line_5a  =  whole-dollar-rounded gross_total
```

### 1.4 Decision tree (when Line 5a is populated versus blank)

```
Per-person worker:

  For each Form 1099-R entry:
      if iraSepSimple == TRUE:
          skip — routes to Lines 4a/4b/4c (Individual Retirement Account)
      if entryId is in the "routed to Line 1h" set (disability before
                                                    minimum retirement age):
          skip — routes to Line 1h as wages
      if the entry does not belong to this person (per the SSN-attribution helper):
          skip
      otherwise:
          gross_for_person  +=  box 1 (grossDistributionAmount)
          taxable_for_person +=  box 2a (taxableAmountAmount), with fallback to gross when null
          employee_contributions_box5 += box 5
          if box 2b is checked: taxableNotDeterminedCount += 1
          if box 7 codes contain "1" or "S": earlyDistributionBase += taxable

  For each Form RRB-1099-R entry:
      if entry does not belong to this person:
          skip
      otherwise:
          entryGross = box 7 when present, else box 4 + box 5 + box 6
          gross_for_person  +=  entryGross
          taxable_for_person +=  entryTaxable (per the RRB box-3-gates-box-4 rule;
                                              see §1.5 below)

  Sum across spouses at the orchestrator:
      gross_total    = addNonNull(taxpayer.gross, spouse.gross)
      taxable_total  = addNonNull(taxpayer.taxable, spouse.taxable)
      any_exception  = taxpayer.hasAnyException || spouse.hasAnyException

  Blank-when-fully-taxable decision:
      fully_taxable_overall  =  (gross_total > 0)
                                AND  (NOT any_exception)
                                AND  (taxable_total > 0)
                                AND  (gross_total == taxable_total)

      if fully_taxable_overall:
          line_5a  =  null  (printed form: blank)
      else:
          line_5a  =  roundMoney(gross_total)
```

### 1.5 Railroad Retirement Board (RRB-1099-R) box-split rule

The Form RRB-1099-R reports railroad-retirement pension income in five detail boxes, each with its own taxability rule per IRS Publication 575 and the IRS RRB-1099-R instructions:

- **Box 3** — Total employee contributions (basis). When positive, box 4 is subject to partial basis recovery via the Simplified Method or General Rule.
- **Box 4** — Non-Social-Security-Equivalent Benefit. Fully taxable when box 3 is zero; partly taxable when box 3 is positive.
- **Box 5** — Vested Dual Benefit. Always fully taxable.
- **Box 6** — Supplemental annuity. Always fully taxable.
- **Box 7** — Total gross paid; fallback when detail boxes are absent.

For Line 5a (gross), the rule is simpler than for Line 5b: gross is the total amount paid regardless of how the taxable computation later splits it.

```
entryGross  =  box 7  if box 7 is present
           =  box 4 + box 5 + box 6  otherwise (fallback)
```

### 1.7 What MUST NOT contribute to Line 5a (explicit exclusions)

Per IRS 2025 Form 1040 instructions and Publication 575:

| Item | Why excluded |
|---|---|
| Form 1099-R with `iraSepSimple = TRUE` | Individual Retirement Account distributions belong on Lines 4a/4b/4c, not Line 5 |
| Form 1099-R with `isBelowPlanMinimumRetirementAge = TRUE` (disability pension before minimum retirement age) | Reported as wages on Line 1h until the taxpayer reaches minimum retirement age |
| Spouse-attributed pension on a Married Filing Separately return | Spouse files separately; spouse's pension must not leak onto the taxpayer's return |
| Social Security and Tier I Social-Security-Equivalent Benefit portion of railroad retirement | Reported on Lines 6a/6b, not Lines 5a/5b |
| Form 4972 lump-sum-distribution amount that the taxpayer elects to tax under Form 4972 special treatment | Special tax-computation path; Line 5b is reduced by the rolled-over portion but the Form 4972 portion still flows through Line 5b; only the **tax-computation path** differs (Form 4972 → Form 1040 Line 16 box 2), not the Line 5a/Line 5b reporting |
| Form 5329 early-distribution additional tax | Penalty tax; flows through Schedule 2 line 8, not into Line 5a/Line 5b |
| A trustee-to-trustee transfer not reported on a 1099-R | If no 1099-R is issued, nothing flows into Line 5 |
| A pension that was wholly returned as a corrective distribution and is reported under a separate special rule | Special distributions route per their own instructions; not blanket-included on Line 5 |

### 1.8 Worked examples

**Example A — Fully taxable, single 1099-R, Line 5a blank:**
- One Form 1099-R: box 1 (gross) = $20,000; box 2a (taxable) = $20,000; no IRA/SEP/SIMPLE box; box 7 code = "7" (normal distribution); box 2b unchecked; no rollover; no Public Safety Officer election; no Simplified Method or General Rule.
- Per the equation: `gross_total = $20,000`; `taxable_total = $20,000`; `any_exception = false`; `fully_taxable_overall = true`.
- Outputs: `line_5a = null` (blank); `line_5b = $20,000`.

**Example B — Partly taxable with rollover, Line 5a populated:**
- One Form 1099-R: box 1 = $50,000; box 2a = $40,000; box 5 = $10,000 (employee basis recovered); no IRA/SEP/SIMPLE box; box 2b unchecked.
- User declared `hadPensionRollover = true` with `totalPensionRolloverAmount = $30,000`.
- Per the equation: `gross_total = $50,000`; `taxable_total = $10,000` (per Publication 575 Gap-4 rollover formula in §1 of Line 5b spec); `any_exception = true` (rollover); `fully_taxable_overall = false`.
- Outputs: `line_5a = $50,000`; `line_5b = $10,000`; `line_5c.box1Rollover = true`.

**Example C — Multiple pensions, one fully taxable and one with Public Safety Officer exclusion:**
- Pension A: gross $30,000 = taxable $30,000 (fully taxable).
- Pension B: gross $20,000 = taxable $20,000 — but the user is an eligible retired Public Safety Officer electing the exclusion with $3,000 qualifying premiums.
- Per the equation: `gross_total = $50,000`; `taxable_total = $47,000` ($30,000 + $20,000 − $3,000); `any_exception = true` (Public Safety Officer); `fully_taxable_overall = false`.
- Outputs: `line_5a = $50,000`; `line_5b = $47,000`; `line_5c.box2Pso = true`.

**Example D — Pension + railroad retirement, both fully taxable:**
- One Form 1099-R: gross $15,000 = taxable $15,000 (fully taxable).
- One Form RRB-1099-R: box 7 (total gross paid) = $8,000; box 3 (cost) = $0; box 4 = $6,000; box 5 = $2,000.
- Railroad retirement entry: `entryGross = $8,000`; with box 3 = 0 the taxable equals box 4 + box 5 = $8,000 (fully taxable).
- Per the equation: `gross_total = $23,000`; `taxable_total = $23,000`; `any_exception = false`; `fully_taxable_overall = true`.
- Outputs: `line_5a = null`; `line_5b = $23,000`.

**Example E — Married Filing Separately spouse-leakage guard:**
- Filing status: Married Filing Separately.
- Taxpayer: one Form 1099-R for $20,000 (fully taxable).
- Spouse: one Form 1099-R for $30,000 with `iraSepSimple = false` and recipient SSN matching the spouse.
- On the taxpayer's separate return, the spouse-side pension reads are suppressed via the per-person MFS guard.
- Per the equation (taxpayer-only contribution): `gross_total = $20,000`; `taxable_total = $20,000`; `any_exception = false`.
- Outputs: `line_5a = null`; `line_5b = $20,000`. The spouse's $30,000 does not appear on the taxpayer's return.

**Example F — Disability pension before minimum retirement age, routed away:**
- One Form 1099-R: gross $10,000; taxable $10,000; box 7 code "3" (disability).
- User confirmed `isBelowPlanMinimumRetirementAge = TRUE` for this 1099-R on the other-earned-income form's disability-pensions section.
- The entry is added to `entryIdsRoutedToLine1h` and skipped by the pension worker.
- Plus one additional Form 1099-R: gross $5,000; taxable $5,000 (normal pension, fully taxable).
- Per the equation: `gross_total = $5,000`; `taxable_total = $5,000`; `any_exception = false`.
- Outputs: `line_5a = null`; `line_5b = $5,000`. The $10,000 disability pension appears on Line 1h instead.

## 2. Code implementation (what the code actually does)

The Line 5a compute path lives in `TaxReturnComputeService.java`. Three methods participate:

| Method | Approximate location | Role for Line 5a |
|---|---|---|
| `computePensionAnnuities()` | `:8821–9033` | Orchestrator. Applies the Married Filing Separately suppression, classifies disability-pension entries routed to Line 1h, calls `computePensionForPerson` twice, sums gross across spouses, evaluates the four-condition fully-taxable test, sets Line 5a to null or to the whole-dollar gross. |
| `computePensionForPerson()` | `:9035–9319` | Per-person worker. Iterates the Form 1099-R entries (skipping IRA/SEP/SIMPLE, skipping disability-routed entries, applying the SSN-attribution helper) and the Form RRB-1099-R entries; sums `gross1099R`, `grossRrb`; returns `roundMoney(grossTotal)` on the `PensionPersonComputation` record. |
| `buildIncome()` | `:6744–6745` | Final wiring. Calls `income.setPensionsAnnuities(line5a)` only when `line5a != null` (preserves blank-when-fully-taxable semantic at the JSON output layer). |

### 2.1 Step-by-step code transcription

```
At computePensionForPerson:

    gross1099R := null
    grossRrb := null

    For each Form 1099-R entry in form1099REntries:
        if entry.iraSepSimple == TRUE:
            continue                                      (route to Lines 4a/4b)
        if entry.entryId is in entryIdsRoutedToLine1h:
            continue                                      (route to Line 1h)
        if not belongsToPersonIra(entry, ...):            (generic SSN helper)
            continue
        gross1099R := addNonNull(gross1099R, parseAmount(entry.grossDistributionAmount))

    For each Form RRB-1099-R entry in formRrb1099REntries:
        if not belongsToPersonRrb1099R(entry, ...):
            continue
        box3cost := parseAmount(entry.employeeContributionsCostAmount)
        box4nsseb := parseAmount(entry.contributoryAmountPaidAmount)
        box5vdb   := parseAmount(entry.vestedDualBenefitAmount)
        box6sa    := parseAmount(entry.supplementalAnnuityAmount)
        box7gross := parseAmount(entry.totalGrossPaidAmount)
        entryGross := box7gross  if box7gross != null
                                else  addNonNull(addNonNull(box4nsseb, box5vdb), box6sa)
        grossRrb := addNonNull(grossRrb, entryGross)

    grossTotal := addNonNull(gross1099R, grossRrb)

    return new PensionPersonComputation(
        roundMoney(grossTotal),                           ← first field, used as "gross"
        ...                                               (taxable, hasRollover, hasPsoElection,
                                                           hasAnyException, line5cBox3Text,
                                                           earlyDistributionAdditionalTax)
    )

At computePensionAnnuities (orchestrator):

    pensionIncomeSpouse := null  if isMfsReturn  else  pensionIncomeSpouseRaw
    spouseSsn := null  if isMfsReturn  else  normalizeSsn(spouse.ssn)

    Build entryIdsRoutedToLine1h from other-earned-income.disabilityPensions.entries
    where isBelowPlanMinimumRetirementAge == TRUE.

    taxpayer := computePensionForPerson("taxpayer", pensionIncomeTaxpayer, ...)
    spouseResult := computePensionForPerson("spouse", pensionIncomeSpouse, ...)

    grossPensions := addNonNull(taxpayer.grossPensionsAnnuities(),
                                spouseResult.grossPensionsAnnuities())
    taxablePensions := addNonNull(taxpayer.taxablePensionsAnnuities(),
                                  spouseResult.taxablePensionsAnnuities())
    hasAnyException := taxpayer.hasAnyException() || spouseResult.hasAnyException()

    fullyTaxableOverall := hasPositiveAmount(grossPensions)
                          && !hasAnyException
                          && hasPositiveAmount(taxablePensions)
                          && grossPensions.compareTo(taxablePensions) == 0

    line5a := null  if fullyTaxableOverall  else  roundMoney(grossPensions)
    line5b := roundMoney(taxablePensions)

    Return PensionComputation(line5a, line5b, line5cBox1, line5cBox2,
                              line5cBox3, line5cText, form5329)

At buildIncome:

    if line5a != null:
        income.setPensionsAnnuities(line5a)               ← only writes when populated
```

### 2.2 Married Filing Separately guard

The orchestrator at `:8855` sets `pensionIncomeSpouse = isMfsReturn ? null : pensionIncomeSpouseRaw` and `spouseSsn = isMfsReturn ? null : normalizeSsn(spouse.ssn)`. When `pensionIncomeSpouse` is null, the spouse's `computePensionForPerson` returns an empty `PensionPersonComputation` (all gross/taxable values null) because:
- `spouseHadPension` evaluates to false (no form to read from).
- The SSN-attribution helper rejects every spouse-attributed Form 1099-R and Form RRB-1099-R because `spouseSsn` is null.

The taxpayer-only contribution therefore drives both gross and exception aggregation. Verified by lock-in test `mfsExcludesSpousePensionFromLine5a`.

### 2.3 Mutual exclusion with Lines 4a/4b at the 1099-R filter

The per-person worker skips any Form 1099-R where `iraSepSimple == TRUE` at `:9062`. The mirror filter in `computeIraForPerson` skips entries where `iraSepSimple ∈ {FALSE, null}`. Every Form 1099-R takes exactly one routing path — there is no overlap and no missed entry. A Form 1099-R with `iraSepSimple = null` (not explicitly flagged) defaults to the pension path (matching IRS convention that the "IRA/SEP/SIMPLE" checkbox must be explicitly marked).

### 2.4 Disability-pension routing carve-out

When the user marks a specific 1099-R as `isBelowPlanMinimumRetirementAge = TRUE` on the other-earned-income form's disability-pensions section, that 1099-R's `entryId` is added to `entryIdsRoutedToLine1h`. The per-person worker skips it at `:9072-9075`. This prevents the same 1099-R from being counted twice (once as wages on Line 1h, once as pension on Line 5b). Lock-in test: `Disability pension wages flow to Form 1040 line 1h`.

### 2.5 The four-condition fully-taxable test

The Line 5a blank decision uses a strict 4-condition test at `:8964-8967`:

1. `hasPositiveAmount(grossPensions)` — there must be a positive gross (no payment activity means no Line 5a, no Line 5b).
2. `!hasAnyException` — no rollover, no Public Safety Officer election, no Simplified Method or General Rule, no box-2b "not determined" flag, no Line 5c box-3 other write-in.
3. `hasPositiveAmount(taxablePensions)` — there must be a positive taxable (a $0 taxable amount means the taxpayer had a payment but nothing was taxable, which is itself an exception state).
4. `grossPensions.compareTo(taxablePensions) == 0` — gross equals taxable (no tax-free recovery applies).

If all four hold, `line5a = null`. If any fails, `line5a = roundMoney(grossPensions)`.

### 2.6 Canonical zero-versus-null compliance

The orchestrator's `line5a` is `BigDecimal` (nullable). The application convention is:
- `null` when "fully taxable" (the IRS-required blank case) or when there is no pension activity at all.
- A positive whole-dollar amount otherwise.

`Income.pensionsAnnuities` is also nullable `BigDecimal`. The `buildIncome` setter call at `:6744-6745` is gated on `line5a != null`, so the field stays unset (null) in the JSON output when Line 5a should be blank. PDF rendering correctly leaves the line blank in both cases.

### 2.7 Line 5a does NOT feed Line 9 (total income)

Line 9 is `addNonNull(... line1z, line2b, line3b, line4b, line5b, line6b, line7a ..., line8)` at `:6618-6619`. Only Line 5b participates in total income — Line 5a is disclosure-only. The application correctly excludes Line 5a from the Line 9 aggregation.

## 6. Common mistakes (Line 5a specifically)

- **Treating Line 5a as always populated.** The IRS rule explicitly requires Line 5a to be **blank** when every payment is fully taxable. A return that puts the gross on both Line 5a and Line 5b for a fully-taxable case is incorrect.
- **Treating Line 5a as never populated.** The opposite mistake — leaving Line 5a blank when a rollover, Public Safety Officer exclusion, Simplified Method, or General Rule applies — is also incorrect.
- **Including Individual Retirement Account distributions on Line 5a.** IRA / Simplified Employee Pension / SIMPLE distributions belong on Lines 4a/4b/4c. The application's mirror filter against `iraSepSimple` is the safety net.
- **Including disability pension before minimum retirement age on Line 5a.** That income is wages on Line 1h until the taxpayer reaches the employer plan's minimum retirement age. The application's `entryIdsRoutedToLine1h` carve-out handles this correctly.
- **Letting spouse pension leak onto the taxpayer's separate return on Married Filing Separately.** The single-guard cascade at the orchestrator prevents this.
- **Counting railroad retirement social-security-equivalent benefit on Line 5a.** That portion is reported on Lines 6a/6b (Social Security). Only the non-Social-Security-Equivalent Benefit / Vested Dual Benefit / Supplemental annuity portions belong on Line 5.
- **Forgetting to add Form RRB-1099-R gross when railroad retirement applies.** The application handles this via the dedicated RRB loop in `computePensionForPerson`.
- **Letting Line 5a feed Line 9 total income.** Line 5a is disclosure-only. Only Line 5b participates in total income.
- **Rounding inconsistently.** All money on Form 1040 should be whole dollars per the IRS rounding convention. The application uses `roundMoney()` consistently.
