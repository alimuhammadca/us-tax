## Glossary

- **Form 1040 Line 5b** — Tax Year 2025 Form 1040 line that reports the **taxable** portion of pensions and annuities received during the year. This is the bottom-line revenue-bearing pension line: Line 5b is included in Line 9 total income; Line 5a is gross/disclosure-only and is excluded from Line 9.
- **Form 1040 Line 5a** — Companion line that reports the **gross** amount, populated only when at least one payment is partly taxable (rollover, Public Safety Officer exclusion, basis recovery via Simplified Method or General Rule, or another exception).
- **Pension / annuity (non-Individual-Retirement-Account)** — Payments from a defined-benefit pension plan, defined-contribution employer plan (such as 401(k), 403(b), governmental 457(b)), qualified employee annuity, tax-sheltered annuity, military retirement pay, or other employer-sponsored retirement arrangement. Individual Retirement Account distributions (traditional, Roth, Simplified Employee Pension, SIMPLE) are reported on Form 1040 Lines 4a/4b instead and are explicitly excluded from this line.
- **Form 1099-R** — IRS statement issued by the payer of a retirement distribution. For pensions/annuities, box 1 holds the gross amount, box 2a the taxable amount as the payer computed it, box 2b a checkbox "Taxable amount not determined," box 4 federal tax withheld, box 5 employee contributions or designated Roth contributions (basis recovered tax-free), box 7 the distribution code(s), and a checkbox labeled "IRA/SEP/SIMPLE" near box 7.
- **Form RRB-1099-R** — Railroad Retirement Board statement for railroad retirement pension/annuity income. Reports the gross paid in box 7 with detail in boxes 3 (employee contributions/cost), 4 (Non-Social-Security-Equivalent Benefit, the contributory portion), 5 (Vested Dual Benefit), and 6 (Supplemental annuity).
- **Basis recovery** — IRS-allowed tax-free portion of an annuity payment that represents the recovery of the taxpayer's after-tax investment in the contract. Two methods exist: Simplified Method and General Rule.
- **Simplified Method (IRS Publication 575)** — Required basis-recovery worksheet for annuity payments from a qualified plan when the annuity starting date is after November 18, 1996. Per IRS Publication 575 single-life table: combined-age factor 360 (combined age ≤110), 310 (≤120), 260 (≤130), 210 (≤140), 160 (>140). The monthly nontaxable portion = cost / factor. The annual nontaxable portion = monthly × payments received.
- **General Rule (IRS Publication 939)** — Basis-recovery method for annuities outside the Simplified Method scope (nonqualified plans, certain pre-November-19-1996 starting dates). Uses an "exclusion ratio" = investment in the contract / expected return. Nontaxable portion of each payment = payment × exclusion ratio.
- **Rollover** — Movement of a pension distribution to another qualified plan or to an Individual Retirement Account. A qualifying rollover is not taxable; it reduces the Line 5b taxable amount and the IRS Line 5c box 1 (rollover) is checked on the return.
- **Retired Public Safety Officer (PSO) exclusion** — Internal Revenue Code §402(l). An eligible retired police officer, firefighter, emergency medical technician, etc. may exclude up to $3,000 per year of qualifying health, accident, or long-term-care insurance premiums paid from pension distributions. When the exclusion applies, IRS Line 5c box 2 (PSO) is checked on the return.
- **Form 4972 lump-sum-distribution election** — Internal Revenue Code §402(e). A participant born before January 2, 1936 (or a designated beneficiary) may elect to tax a qualifying lump-sum distribution at preferential rates on a separate Form 4972 instead of including it on Line 5b. When elected, the lump-sum amount is **subtracted** from Line 5a/5b and reported through Form 4972 (which feeds Form 1040 Line 16 box 2 "Form 4972").
- **Form 5329 additional tax on early distributions** — Internal Revenue Code §72(t). 10% additional tax on the taxable portion of distributions before age 59½ when no exception applies (code 1 on Form 1099-R box 7). 25% rate for SIMPLE-IRA-first-2-years early distributions per Internal Revenue Code §72(t)(6) (code S). Reported on Form 5329 Part I; feeds Schedule 2 line 8; included on Form 1040 line 23. Note: the Form 5329 additional tax DOES NOT reduce Line 5b — Line 5b carries the gross taxable amount; Form 5329 sits on top as a separate penalty tax.
- **Disability pension before minimum retirement age** — When a taxpayer receives a disability pension before reaching the employer plan's minimum retirement age, the payments are reported as **wages on Form 1040 Line 1h**, not on Line 5b. Once the taxpayer reaches minimum retirement age, the payments switch to Line 5 reporting.
- **Married Filing Separately (MFS)** — Filing status. The application's MFS guard suppresses the spouse-side pension reads so spouse-attributed pension distributions cannot leak onto the taxpayer's separate return.
- **roundMoney** — Application helper that rounds a `BigDecimal` to whole dollars per the IRS whole-dollar convention; returns `null` when input is null.
- **addNonNull** — Application helper that adds two `BigDecimal` operands while preserving null when both are null (canonical zero-versus-null compliance).
- **subtractNonNegative** — Application helper that performs `max(0, a − b)` while preserving null on the left operand.

## 1. IRS rule for Line 5b (computed before reading the code)

### 1.1 Plain-English equation

Line 5b asks: "Of the pension and annuity money you received this year, how much is **taxable**?" The IRS-allowed subtractions from the gross are: basis recovery (Simplified Method or General Rule), rollover, and the Public Safety Officer premium exclusion.

```
Step 1 — collect the relevant payments
  pension_set =  every Form 1099-R distribution that is NOT marked
                 IRA/SEP/SIMPLE near box 7
              +  every Form RRB-1099-R railroad-retirement distribution
              −  every Form 1099-R already routed to Form 1040 Line 1h as
                 a disability pension before minimum retirement age
              −  every distribution belonging to a spouse on a Married
                 Filing Separately return (suppressed by the per-person guard)

Step 2 — compute per-person taxable starting point
  For each Form 1099-R: take box 2a (taxableAmountAmount) if present, else
  fall back to box 1 (grossDistributionAmount) per IRS Pub. 575 "If the
  taxable amount isn't shown on the form, use the gross amount."

  taxable_1099R_for_person     =  sum of box 2a (or box 1 fallback)
  gross_1099R_for_person       =  sum of box 1
  box5_basis_for_person        =  sum of box 5 (employee contributions
                                  / designated Roth, already after-tax)

  For each Form RRB-1099-R per the box-split rule in §1.5 below:
  gross_RRB_for_person          =  box 7 when present, else box 4 + box 5 + box 6
  taxable_RRB_for_person        =  see §1.5

  gross_total_for_person   =  gross_1099R_for_person + gross_RRB_for_person
  taxable_base_for_person  =  taxable_1099R_for_person + taxable_RRB_for_person

Step 3 — basis-recovery override (when elected)
  If the user has elected the Simplified Method (Pub. 575):
      taxable_base_for_person  =  simplified_method_taxable(gross_total, cost,
                                                            age, payments,
                                                            prior_recovery)
  Else if the user has elected the General Rule (Pub. 939):
      taxable_base_for_person  =  general_rule_taxable(gross_total, cost,
                                                       expected_return, payments)
  Note: per Internal Revenue Code §72(d) Simplified Method and General Rule are
  mutually exclusive. The application blocks compute when both are elected for
  the same person (see XLS/Computations/5a.md §4.5).

Step 4 — rollover reduction (when applicable)
  If the user has declared a rollover:
      rollover_base       =  gross_1099R_for_person
      reduced_base        =  max(0, rollover_base − rollover_amount − box5_offset)
      taxable_after_rollover  =  reduced_base + taxable_RRB_for_person
  Else:
      taxable_after_rollover  =  taxable_base_for_person

  Note: the rollover reduction uses BOX 1 (gross) as the base, not box 2a (taxable).
  Starting from box 2a would double-deduct box 5 when the payer pre-netted the
  employee basis. The RRB amount is excluded from the rollover reduction
  (RRB distributions are not qualified-plan rollover-eligible) and re-added
  after the reduction.

Step 5 — Public Safety Officer premium exclusion (when applicable)
  If isEligibleRetiredPublicSafetyOfficer == TRUE AND electsPsoPremiumExclusion == TRUE:
      pso_exclusion       =  min(qualifying_premiums_paid, $3,000)
      taxable_after_pso   =  max(0, taxable_after_rollover − pso_exclusion)
  Else:
      taxable_after_pso   =  taxable_after_rollover

  Cap is $3,000 per person per year per Internal Revenue Code §402(l) (stable
  rule, not annually indexed).

Step 6 — sum across spouses at the orchestrator
  total_taxable  =  taxable_after_pso_for_taxpayer + taxable_after_pso_for_spouse

  (Spouse contribution is zero on Married Filing Separately due to the guard.)

Step 7 — Form 4972 lump-sum election subtraction (when applicable)
  If the taxpayer or spouse has elected Form 4972 for a qualifying lump-sum
  distribution (born before January 2, 1936, 5-year plan participation, etc.):
      total_taxable  =  max(0, total_taxable − form_4972_source_taxable_amount)
      Form 4972 is generated; its line 30 result flows to Form 1040 Line 16 box 2.
      Form 1040 Line 5c box 1 (rollover) is NOT triggered by Form 4972 — Form 4972
      is its own disclosure path (the "Form 4972" tag is on Line 16 box 2, not 5c).

Step 8 — round and emit
  line_5b  =  whole-dollar-rounded total_taxable

  Line 5b is null when there is no pension activity at all (gross_total = null
  on both spouses). Line 5b is 0 when pension activity exists but every dollar
  has been absorbed by basis recovery, rollover, PSO exclusion, or Form 4972.
  Per the 2025 Form 1040 instructions, the IRS expects "0" displayed in this
  case (not blank). The application correctly emits 0.
```

### 1.4 Decision tree (what reduces Line 5b)

```
For each person (taxpayer, spouse):

  STAGE 0 — initial taxable base
    taxable_base = (sum of box 2a or box 1 fallback)  +  taxable_RRB

  STAGE 1 — Simplified Method override (Pub. 575)
    if needsSimplifiedMethodComputation AND cost/age/payments provided:
        monthlyExclusion = (cost − priorYearTaxFreeRecovery) / age_factor
        annualExclusion  = monthlyExclusion × min(payments, 12)
        taxable_base     = max(0, gross_total − annualExclusion)
    else:
        taxable_base unchanged

  STAGE 2 — General Rule override (Pub. 939)
    if needsGeneralRuleComputation AND cost/expectedReturn/payments provided:
        exclusion_ratio   = cost / expectedReturn   (or user override)
        nontaxable_portion = paymentsReceived × exclusion_ratio
        taxable_base      = max(0, paymentsReceived − nontaxable_portion)
    else:
        taxable_base unchanged

    (Stages 1 and 2 are mutually exclusive per IRC §72(d). The application
     emits a blocking flag when both are elected for the same person.)

  STAGE 3 — Rollover reduction (Pub. 575)
    if hadPensionRollover OR totalPensionRolloverAmount > 0:
        rolloverBase        = gross_1099R                    (Box 1, NOT box 2a)
        box5Offset          = taxableEmployeeContributionsAlreadyTaxedBox5Total
                              ?? sum of box 5 across attributed 1099-R entries
        reducedBase         = max(0, rolloverBase − rolloverAmount − box5Offset)
        taxableAfterRollover = reducedBase + taxable_RRB
                              (RRB re-added because RRB is not rollover-eligible)
    else:
        taxableAfterRollover = taxable_base  (no reduction)

  STAGE 4 — Public Safety Officer exclusion (IRC §402(l))
    if isEligibleRetiredPublicSafetyOfficer AND electsPsoPremiumExclusion:
        psoExclusion        = min(totalQualifyingPsoPremiumsPaid, $3,000)
        taxableAfterPso     = max(0, taxableAfterRollover − psoExclusion)
    else:
        taxableAfterPso     = taxableAfterRollover

Orchestrator aggregation:
  total_taxable_pensions  =  taxableAfterPso_taxpayer  +  taxableAfterPso_spouse
                              (spouse contribution is zero on MFS)

Form 4972 election subtraction (when elected):
  total_taxable_pensions  =  max(0, total_taxable_pensions − sourceTaxableAmount)

Output:
  line_5b  =  roundMoney(total_taxable_pensions)
```

### 1.5 Railroad Retirement Board (RRB-1099-R) box-split rule

The Form RRB-1099-R reports railroad-retirement pension income in five detail boxes, each with its own taxability rule per IRS Publication 575 and the IRS RRB-1099-R instructions:

- **Box 3** — Total employee contributions (basis). When positive, box 4 is subject to partial basis recovery via the Simplified Method or General Rule.
- **Box 4** — Non-Social-Security-Equivalent Benefit. Fully taxable when box 3 is zero; partly taxable when box 3 is positive.
- **Box 5** — Vested Dual Benefit. Always fully taxable (no basis applies).
- **Box 6** — Supplemental annuity. Always fully taxable (no basis applies).
- **Box 7** — Total gross paid; fallback when detail boxes are absent.

Three decision branches for `taxable_RRB` computation (per entry):

```
Branch A — box 3 (cost) > 0:
    entryTaxable  =  box 5 + box 6          (box 4 deferred to basis-recovery worksheet)

Branch B — box 3 = 0 AND any of box 4, 5, 6 is present:
    entryTaxable  =  box 4 + box 5 + box 6  (box 4 fully taxable since no basis)

Branch C — box 3 = 0 AND none of box 4, 5, 6 is present:
    entryTaxable  =  box 7 gross            (treat as fully taxable)
```

Sum `entryTaxable` across attributed Form RRB-1099-R entries → `taxable_RRB_for_person`.

### 1.7 What MUST NOT reduce Line 5b (explicit non-deductions)

Per IRS 2025 Form 1040 instructions and Publication 575:

| Item | Why NOT a Line 5b reduction |
|---|---|
| Form 5329 additional tax (10% / 25%) | Separate penalty tax — sits on Schedule 2 line 8 / Form 1040 line 23, leaves Line 5b unchanged |
| Federal income tax withholding (Form 1099-R box 4) | Goes to Form 1040 line 25b, not Line 5b |
| State income tax withholding (Form 1099-R box 14) | Goes to Schedule A only if itemizing, not Line 5b |
| Form 4972 line 30 special tax | Goes to Form 1040 line 16 box 2; the underlying lump-sum AMOUNT is what reduces Line 5b, not the tax |
| Form 8606 nondeductible IRA basis | Belongs to Line 4b path, not Line 5b |
| Qualified Charitable Distribution (Internal Revenue Code §408(d)(8)) | IRA-only exclusion — applies to Line 4b, not Line 5b |
| Health Savings Account funding distribution (Internal Revenue Code §408(d)(9)) | IRA-only exclusion — applies to Line 4b, not Line 5b |

### 1.8 Worked examples

**Example A — Fully taxable single-pension, no exception:**
- One Form 1099-R: gross = $20,000; taxable = $20,000; iraSepSimple = false; box 5 = $0; distributionCodes = `7`
- No rollover, no Public Safety Officer election, no basis recovery, no Form 4972
- Per the equation: Stage 0 → taxable_base = $20,000; Stages 1–4 unchanged; total_taxable = $20,000
- Outputs: 5a = null (fully taxable rule applies — see XLS/Computations/5a.md); **5b = $20,000**

**Example B — Partly taxable with rollover (Publication 575 Gap 4 example):**
- One Form 1099-R: gross = $50,000; taxable (box 2a) = $40,000; box 5 = $10,000 (payer pre-netted $10k basis from box 2a)

### 2.2 Simplified Method computation (Stage 1 detail)

```
computePensionTaxableViaSimplifiedMethod(pensionForm, grossPension):
    cost = investmentInContractCostSimplifiedMethod
    paymentCount = numberOfAnnuityPaymentsReceivedInTaxYear
    if cost <= 0 OR paymentCount <= 0 OR grossPension <= 0:
        return null   (null-fallback to taxableBase upstream)
    priorRecovery = priorYearTaxFreeRecoveryAmount
    remainingBasis = max(0, cost − priorRecovery)
    if remainingBasis == 0:
        return grossPension   (basis fully recovered — payment fully taxable)
    annuitantAge = annuitantAgeAtStartingDate
    jointAge     = jointAnnuitantAgeAtStartingDate
    factor       = determineSimplifiedMethodFactor(annuitantAge, jointAge)
    monthlyExclusion = remainingBasis / factor    (precision 10, HALF_UP)
    annualExclusion  = monthlyExclusion × min(paymentCount, 12)
    return max(0, grossPension − annualExclusion)

determineSimplifiedMethodFactor(annuitantAge, jointAge):
    if both null: return 260   (legacy default)
    ageBasis = (annuitantAge ?? 0) + (jointAge > 0 ? jointAge : 0)
    if ageBasis ≤ 110: return 360
    if ageBasis ≤ 120: return 310
    if ageBasis ≤ 130: return 260
    if ageBasis ≤ 140: return 210
    return 160
```

### 2.3 General Rule computation (Stage 2 detail)

```
computePensionTaxableViaGeneralRule(pensionForm, grossPension):
    cost            = investmentInContractCostGeneralRule
    expectedReturn  = expectedReturnGeneralRule
    paymentsReceived = paymentsReceivedInTaxYearGeneralRule
    if cost <= 0 OR expectedReturn <= 0 OR paymentsReceived <= 0:
        return null
    exclusionRatio = exclusionRatioOverrideGeneralRule
                     ?? (cost / expectedReturn)
    nontaxablePortion = paymentsReceived × exclusionRatio
    grossBase = paymentsReceived > 0 ? paymentsReceived : grossPension
    return max(0, grossBase − nontaxablePortion)
```

### 2.4 Married Filing Separately guard

The orchestrator at `:8910` sets `pensionIncomeSpouse = isMfsReturn ? null : pensionIncomeSpouseRaw` and `spouseSsn = isMfsReturn ? null : normalizeSsn(spouse.ssn)`. When `pensionIncomeSpouse` is null:
- `spouseHadPension` evaluates to false.
- The SSN-attribution helper rejects every spouse-attributed Form 1099-R / Form RRB-1099-R because `spouseSsn` is null.

Net effect: spouse's `PensionPersonComputation` is empty (all fields null/false), the orchestrator's `addNonNull` aggregation reduces to taxpayer-only, and Line 5b reflects taxpayer-only taxable.

### 2.5 Stage ordering rationale

The IRS Publication 575 worksheet order is:
1. Basis recovery FIRST (Simplified Method or General Rule produces the FULL taxable amount including any RRB portion that doesn't have basis).
2. Rollover SECOND (operates only on the 1099-R portion; RRB is re-added because RRB is not rollover-eligible).
3. Public Safety Officer exclusion LAST (operates on the post-rollover total because the cap applies to the actual taxable amount before exclusion).

The code at `:9311–9384` implements exactly this order.

### 2.6 Form 4972 lump-sum subtraction

`applyForm4972ToPensionLines` at `:23560` runs AFTER the orchestrator has populated Income with the Stage-4 result. It subtracts:
- `sourceTaxableAmount` from Line 5b: `max(0, current5b − sourceTaxableAmount)`
- `sourceGrossDistributionAmount` from Line 5a (and clears Line 5a per the "if new5a == 0 or new5a == new5b" rule)

The Form 4972 tax itself (Form 4972 line 30) flows to Form 1040 Line 16 box 2 via `computeLine16` — that's separate from Line 5b.

### 2.7 Canonical zero-versus-null compliance

The taxable chain at every stage preserves null when no input is present, and produces a non-null `BigDecimal` (including `BigDecimal.ZERO`) when concept-applies-but-zero. End state at the orchestrator:
- `line5b = null` when there is no pension activity at all (neither spouse has any attributed 1099-R or RRB-1099-R)
- `line5b = 0` when activity exists but all dollars have been absorbed (rollover + Public Safety Officer + Form 4972, etc.)
- `line5b = positive` when taxable income remains

The IRS instructions for Line 5b accept blank when no pension activity exists and require `0` when activity exists but is fully offset. The application's `roundMoney(BigDecimal.ZERO) → 0` and the `buildIncome` setter gate on `line5b != null` correctly produce both behaviors.

### 2.8 Line 5b feeds Line 9 (total income)

The Line 9 aggregation at `:6620-6622` is:

```
line9 = line1z + line2b + line3b + line4b + line5b + line6b + line7a + line8
```

Line 5b is explicitly in the sum; Line 5a is explicitly excluded.

## 6. Common mistakes (Line 5b specifically)

- **Treating Form 5329 additional tax as a reduction to Line 5b.** Form 5329 is a separate penalty tax; it flows through Schedule 2 line 8 → Form 1040 line 23. Line 5b carries the gross taxable amount unchanged.
- **Using box 2a as the rollover reduction base.** The IRS Publication 575 worksheet uses BOX 1 (gross) as the rollover base because box 5 (employee basis) is conventionally pre-netted from box 2a by the payer. Starting from box 2a double-deducts box 5.
- **Forgetting to re-add the RRB-1099-R taxable portion after Stage 3.** Railroad-retirement distributions are not qualified-plan rollover-eligible per IRS rules. The rollover reduction operates only on the 1099-R portion; the RRB taxable must be re-added.
- **Applying the Public Safety Officer exclusion BEFORE rollover reduction.** The IRS Publication 575 worksheet order is basis recovery → rollover → PSO. Applying PSO too early under-clamps the $3,000 cap relative to the post-rollover total.
- **Letting Form 4972 election DOUBLE-COUNT.** Form 4972's lump-sum amount is subtracted from Line 5b (and 5a). If the same amount also flows through the regular Stage 0 taxable_base, the result is wrong. The Form 4972 election is a SUBSTITUTION, not an addition.
- **Including disability pension (before minimum retirement age) on Line 5b.** That income is wages on Line 1h. The application's `entryIdsRoutedToLine1h` carve-out enforces this.
- **Including Individual Retirement Account distributions on Line 5b.** IRA / SEP / SIMPLE belong on Lines 4a/4b. The `iraSepSimple` filter at `:9163-9166` enforces this.
- **Letting spouse pension leak onto the taxpayer's separate return on Married Filing Separately.** The single-guard cascade at the orchestrator prevents this.
- **Misapplying the Simplified Method age factor on a joint annuity.** Joint annuity uses the COMBINED age (annuitant + joint annuitant) to look up the factor, not the annuitant age alone.
- **Forgetting to subtract prior-year tax-free recovery from the Simplified Method cost.** When the annuity has been paying for prior years, cost has already been partially recovered. Failing to subtract `priorYearTaxFreeRecoveryAmount` from the cost over-recovers basis and under-reports Line 5b.
- **Letting Line 5b = blank when pension activity exists but is fully absorbed.** IRS expects `0`, not blank. `roundMoney(BigDecimal.ZERO) → 0` and the `buildIncome` setter on non-null produce the correct `0` display.
- **Rounding inconsistently.** All money on Form 1040 should be whole dollars per the IRS rounding convention. The application uses `roundMoney()` consistently at the orchestrator boundary.
