## Part A — IRS-prescribed computation (the rule the books require)

### A.1 What goes on line 7a

Line 7a is the **net capital gain or net capital loss** the taxpayer reports on Form 1040.

There are two possible paths:

1. A **direct-entry path** that puts a single number on line 7a and does **not** require Schedule D.
2. A **Schedule D path** that computes the number by filing Schedule D, and (when needed) Form 8949, and then copies the resulting net gain or limited loss onto line 7a.

The IRS calls the direct-entry path **Exception 1**.

### A.2 Decision: does the taxpayer qualify for Exception 1?

Per the 2025 Form 1040 instructions and Publication 17 chapter "Reporting Gains and Losses," the taxpayer can skip Schedule D and put one number directly on line 7a **only when ALL FOUR of these statements are true**:

1. The taxpayer is **not** deferring any capital gain by investing it in a Qualified Opportunity Fund, and is not reporting any Qualified Opportunity Fund activity on this return.
2. The taxpayer has **no capital losses** in this tax year.
3. The **only** capital gains the taxpayer has are **capital gain distributions** reported on Form 1099-DIV box 2a (or shown on a substitute statement). The taxpayer must have no other capital sales or exchanges.
4. **None** of the taxpayer's Forms 1099-DIV show an amount in box 2b ("Unrecaptured Section 1250 gain"), box 2c ("Section 1202 gain"), or box 2d ("Collectibles 28% rate gain").

In addition, **all of the following** Schedule D triggers must also be **absent**, because each of them independently forces Schedule D even when the four statements above hold:

- No prior-year **capital loss carryover**.
- No **Form 2439** (undistributed long-term capital gains from a mutual fund or real-estate investment trust).
- No **Form 6252** installment-sale income, no **Form 4797 Part I** business-property sale, no **Form 4684** casualty/theft, no **Form 6781** straddle, no **Form 8824** like-kind exchange.
- No capital items from **Schedule K-1** of a partnership, S corporation, estate, or trust.

If any one of those triggers is present, Schedule D is required and the taxpayer does **not** qualify for Exception 1.

### A.3 Direct-entry amount when Exception 1 applies

When Exception 1 applies:

```
line_7a = total_box_2a_capital_gain_distributions_belonging_to_the_taxpayer
          − capital_gain_distributions_received_as_a_nominee
```

A **nominee** is a taxpayer whose name and Taxpayer Identification Number appear on a 1099-DIV but who actually holds the asset on behalf of someone else; the amount belonging to the other person must be subtracted out on the nominee's own line 7a (and a separate 1099-DIV must be issued to the actual owner outside the return, per the 2025 Schedule B instructions).

If the parent elects under **Form 8814** to include the child's capital gain distributions on the parent's return, then add the amount from Form 8814 line 10 as well:

```
line_7a = (taxpayer_box_2a_total − nominee_amount)
          + form_8814_line_10
```

Then on line 7b:
- Check the **"Schedule D not required"** box (because Exception 1 applies).
- Check the **"Includes child's capital gain or (loss)"** box if Form 8814 line 10 is positive, and enter that child amount in the space provided.

### A.4 Schedule D path when Exception 1 does not apply

When any one of the Exception 1 conditions fails, the taxpayer files **Schedule D** and (in most cases) **Form 8949**.

Schedule D collects six categories of capital activity:

- **Short-term** (asset held one year or less) — Schedule D lines 1a through 7.
- **Long-term** (asset held more than one year) — Schedule D lines 8a through 15.
- **Form 8949 page totals** — split into six boxes A/B/C (short-term) and D/E/F (long-term) for assets reported on Form 1099-B and the parallel codes G/H/I (short-term) and J/K/L (long-term) for digital-asset transactions reported on Form 1099-DA.
- **Other gain sources** — Forms 2439, 6252, 4797 Part I, 4684, 6781, 8824, and Schedule K-1.
- **Capital gain distributions** from Form 1099-DIV box 2a — go on **Schedule D line 13** (long-term).
- **Prior-year capital loss carryover** — short-term portion on line 6, long-term portion on line 14.

**Schedule D arithmetic:**

```
schedule_d_line_7  = line_1a + line_1b + line_2 + line_3 + line_4 + line_5 − line_6
schedule_d_line_15 = line_8a + line_8b + line_9 + line_10 + line_11 + line_12 + line_13 − line_14
schedule_d_line_16 = schedule_d_line_7 + schedule_d_line_15
```

Then line 7a is set from Schedule D line 16:

- If Schedule D line 16 is a **gain** (positive) or **zero**, copy that amount to Form 1040 line 7a.
- If Schedule D line 16 is a **loss** (negative), the deductible loss is **limited** per IRC section 1211(b):
  - For Married Filing Separately filers, the loss limit is **`CAPITAL_LOSS_DEDUCTION_LIMIT_MFS` = $1,500**.
  - For all other filing statuses (Single, Head of Household, Married Filing Jointly, Qualifying Surviving Spouse), the loss limit is **`CAPITAL_LOSS_DEDUCTION_LIMIT_DEFAULT` = $3,000**.
  - The amount that goes on Schedule D line 21 (and therefore on Form 1040 line 7a as a negative number) is the smaller of the loss limit and the magnitude of Schedule D line 16:
    ```
    schedule_d_line_21 = minimum( loss_limit, absolute_value( schedule_d_line_16 ) )
    line_7a            = − schedule_d_line_21
    ```
  - Any disallowed loss (the part above the loss limit) becomes a **capital loss carryover** to next year per IRC section 1212(b). The carryover preserves short-term-vs-long-term character per the worksheet in the Schedule D instructions.

### A.5 Form 8814 child capital gain — Schedule D path

When Schedule D **is** required and the parent is electing to include the child's capital gain distributions via Form 8814, the child amount from Form 8814 line 10 is added to **Schedule D line 13** (capital gain distributions). It then flows through Schedule D line 15 → line 16 → Form 1040 line 7a. The "Includes child's capital gain or (loss)" checkbox on line 7b is still checked, and the child amount is still entered in the line 7b space — but it must **not** be added to line 7a again, because it is already included via Schedule D line 13.

### A.6 Statements that force Schedule D regardless of any other rule

A taxpayer cannot use Exception 1 if **any one** of these conditions is true:

- Box 2b, 2c, or 2d on any Form 1099-DIV is non-zero.
- The taxpayer has a prior-year capital loss carryover.
- The taxpayer has any of: Form 2439, Form 6252, Form 4797 Part I, Form 4684, Form 6781, Form 8824, or capital-item Schedule K-1 amounts.
- The taxpayer is deferring capital gain into a Qualified Opportunity Fund or has any other Qualified Opportunity Fund activity.

### A.7 Tax computation downstream of line 7a

After line 7a is set, the rest of the return uses it in three different ways:

1. **Form 1040 line 9** ("Total income") adds line 7a as one of its components.
2. **Social Security benefits taxable amount worksheet** uses line 7a as one of the income items that determines how much of Social Security is taxable.
3. **Form 1040 line 16** (the tax itself) uses Schedule D (when filed) to decide whether the tax is computed with the **Qualified Dividends and Capital Gain Tax Worksheet** or with the **Schedule D Tax Worksheet** (the latter is used when there are amounts subject to the 28% collectibles rate or the unrecaptured Section 1250 rate).

## Part B — Code-implemented computation (what `TaxReturnComputeService.java` actually does)

Source: `C:\us-tax\us-tax-be\src\main\java\com\ustax\microservices\TaxReturnComputeService.java`, method `computeCapitalGainLoss(...)` starting at line 10343.

### B.1 Step 0 — Married Filing Separately data isolation

When the filing status is Married Filing Separately, the spouse's capital-gain personal form, spouse Social Security number, and any spouse-attributed statements are **silenced** for this computation. This prevents the spouse's data from polluting the taxpayer's line 7a number.

```
if filing_status == "Married filing separately":
    capital_spouse = null
    spouse = null
```

### B.2 Step 1 — Gather the input collections

The method pulls per-statement lists from the database:

- `form1099DivEntries` — all 1099-DIV statements.
- `form1099BEntries` — all 1099-B statements (broker sales).
- `form1099DaEntries` — all 1099-DA statements (digital-asset sales).
- `form2439Entries` — Form 2439 from regulated investment companies or real-estate investment trusts.
- `form6252Entries`, `form4797Entries`, `form4684Entries`, `form6781Entries`, `form8824Entries` — other capital-gain feeder forms.
- `formK11041Entries`, `formK11065Entries`, `formK11120SEntries` — Schedule K-1 forms from estates/trusts, partnerships, and S-corporations.
- `form1099SEntries` — real-estate proceeds.

It also reads the two personal forms `capital-gain-loss-taxpayer` and `capital-gain-loss-spouse` for user-entered overrides and supplemental amounts.

### B.3 Step 2 — Per-person totals

`computeCapitalForPerson(...)` (called twice — once for taxpayer, once for spouse when not Married Filing Separately) returns a record `CapitalPersonTotals` with these fields used downstream:

- `capitalGainDistributions` — sum of Form 1099-DIV `totalCapitalGainDistributionsAmount` (box 2a) belonging to that person, matched by Taxpayer Identification Number.
- `nomineeCapitalGainDistributions` — user-entered `nomineeCapitalGainDistributionsToSubtract`.
- `box2bTotal`, `box2cTotal`, `box2dTotal` — sums of `unrecapturedSection1250GainAmount`, `section1202GainAmount`, and `collectibles28PercentGainAmount` respectively from Form 1099-DIV.
- `shortTermOtherFormsGainLossLine4`, `shortTermScheduleK1GainLossLine5`, `longTermOtherFormsGainLossLine11`, `longTermScheduleK1GainLossLine12` — user-entered supplemental amounts.
- `priorYearCapitalLossCarryoverShortTerm`, `priorYearCapitalLossCarryoverLongTerm`, `priorYearScheduleDLine21AllowableLoss` — user-entered prior-year carryover.
- `twentyEightPercentRateGainWorksheetAmountLine18`, `unrecapturedSection1250GainWorksheetAmountLine19` — user-entered worksheet figures.
- `hasCapitalLossesForTaxYear`, `hadCapitalAssetSalesOrExchanges`, `hasCapitalLossCarryoverFromPriorYear`, `hasOtherScheduleDSourceForms`, `hasForm2439UndistributedCapitalGains`, `hasAny1099DivSpecialBoxes` — boolean facts used in the Exception 1 gate.
- `manualForm8949Transactions` — manual per-lot transactions the user typed in.

The taxpayer's and spouse's totals are then added together field-by-field with `addNonNull(...)`.

### B.4 Step 3 — Build per-transaction list and Form 8949 pages

```
transactions = buildCapitalTransactionsFrom1099B(bEntries, ...)
             + buildCapitalTransactionsFrom1099Da(daEntries, ...)
             + buildManualTransactions(taxpayer.manualForm8949Transactions, ...)
             + buildManualTransactions(spouseResult.manualForm8949Transactions, ...)
             + addNonbusinessBadDebtTransaction(taxpayer)
             + addNonbusinessBadDebtTransaction(spouse)
```

Each transaction is classified into one of the 12 Form 8949 boxes (A through L) by `derive1099BBox(...)` or `derive1099DaBox(...)`. Boxes A/G (short-term, basis reported, no adjustments) and D/J (long-term, basis reported, no adjustments) can go directly on Schedule D line 1a or 8a; the other boxes go onto Form 8949 pages.

A transaction is forced onto Form 8949 (cannot direct-aggregate) if:
- The transaction has a wash-sale disallowed amount (`washSaleLossDisallowedAmount` on 1099-B or `copyA_box1i_wash_sale_loss_disallowed` on 1099-DA) — adjustment code "W".
- The transaction is a Qualified Opportunity Fund transaction (`proceedsFromQOF` on 1099-B or `copyA_box3b_qof` on 1099-DA).
- Any other adjustment code is present.

### B.5 Step 4 — Build Schedule D row aggregates

```
line_1a = direct-aggregate transactions in boxes A+G  (short-term, basis reported, no adjustments)
line_1b = Form-8949 transactions in boxes A+G        (anything that did not direct-aggregate)
line_2  = Form-8949 transactions in boxes B+H
line_3  = Form-8949 transactions in boxes C+I
line_8a = direct-aggregate transactions in boxes D+J  (long-term, basis reported, no adjustments)
line_8b = Form-8949 transactions in boxes D+J
line_9  = Form-8949 transactions in boxes E+K
line_10 = Form-8949 transactions in boxes F+L
```

The remaining Schedule D lines are computed from the per-person totals (Section B.3):

```
line_4_amount  = taxpayer.shortTermOtherFormsGainLossLine4  + spouse.shortTermOtherFormsGainLossLine4
line_5_amount  = taxpayer.shortTermScheduleK1GainLossLine5  + spouse.shortTermScheduleK1GainLossLine5
line_11_amount = taxpayer.longTermOtherFormsGainLossLine11  + spouse.longTermOtherFormsGainLossLine11 + legacyNetOtherAdjustments
line_12_amount = taxpayer.longTermScheduleK1GainLossLine12  + spouse.longTermScheduleK1GainLossLine12

line_18_amount = taxpayer.twentyEightPercentRateGainWorksheetAmountLine18
               + spouse.twentyEightPercentRateGainWorksheetAmountLine18
               + box2cTotal + box2dTotal
line_19_amount = taxpayer.unrecapturedSection1250GainWorksheetAmountLine19
               + spouse.unrecapturedSection1250GainWorksheetAmountLine19
               + box2bTotal

line_6_short_term_carryover  = taxpayer.priorYearCapitalLossCarryoverShortTerm + spouse...
line_14_long_term_carryover  = taxpayer.priorYearCapitalLossCarryoverLongTerm  + spouse...
line_21_carryover_fallback   = taxpayer.priorYearScheduleDLine21AllowableLoss + spouse...
```

If both short-term and long-term carryover are zero but the fallback is non-zero, the fallback is treated as a short-term carryover (defensive default per the code at line 10540).

### B.6 Step 5 — Capital-gain distributions on line 13

```
line_13_amount = capitalGainDistributions − nomineeAdjustments
```

(Initial value; see Section B.8 for the child-capital-gain adjustment.)

### B.7 Step 6 — Schedule D line 16 (net capital gain or loss)

```
line_7_amount  = line_1a_gain + line_1b_gain + line_2_gain + line_3_gain
                 + line_4_amount + line_5_amount − line_6_short_term_carryover

line_15_amount = line_8a_gain + line_8b_gain + line_9_gain + line_10_gain
                 + line_11_amount + line_12_amount + line_13_amount − line_14_long_term_carryover

line_16_amount = line_7_amount + line_15_amount
```

### B.8 Step 7 — Determine Exception 1 (Schedule D not required)

The code uses an **eight-condition AND gate** (more conservative than the IRS's nominal four-condition rule):

```
exception_1 = (no Qualified Opportunity Fund deferral)
              AND (no sales or exchanges of any kind)
              AND (no capital losses)
              AND (no prior-year capital loss carryover)
              AND (no Form 2439)
              AND (no other Schedule D feeder forms / K-1)
              AND (no 1099-DIV box 2b/2c/2d amounts)
              AND (no legacy net "other adjustments")
```

Each of the eight booleans is `false` by default when input data is missing, so when in doubt Exception 1 is `false` and Schedule D is filed — this is **safer than the IRS's stated minimum rule**.

### B.9 Step 8 — Compute `line7a`

```
loss_limit = $1,500 if filing_status == "Married filing separately" else $3,000

if exception_1:
    line_7a_base = capitalGainDistributions − nomineeAdjustments
else:
    if includesChildCapitalGainLoss AND form_8814_line_10 > 0:
        # Phase 1 fix: add child amount to Schedule D line 13, NOT directly to line 7a
        line_13_amount  = line_13_amount + form_8814_line_10
        line_15_amount  = recomputed from line_13_amount
        line_16_amount  = line_7_amount + line_15_amount

    if line_16_amount is null:
        line_16_amount = 0

    if line_16_amount < 0:
        schedule_d_line_21 = minimum(loss_limit, absolute_value(line_16_amount))
        line_7a_base       = − schedule_d_line_21
    else:
        line_7a_base       = line_16_amount

# Child amount: direct add only on Exception 1 path (Schedule D path already includes it via line 13)
if includesChildCapitalGainLoss AND exception_1:
    line_7a = line_7a_base + childAmount
else:
    line_7a = line_7a_base

line_7a = roundMoney(line_7a)
```

### B.10 Step 9 — Line 7b checkboxes

```
line_7b_schedule_d_not_required         = exception_1                       # TRUE / FALSE
line_7b_includes_child_capital_gain_loss = includesChildCapitalGainLoss      # TRUE / FALSE
line_7b_child_amount_from_form_8814_line_10 = roundMoney(childAmount)        # entry-space amount
```

The child checkbox and child amount are filled the same way in both Exception 1 and Schedule D paths — they are disclosures, not separate computations.

### B.11 Step 10 — Outputs

The computation returns a `CapitalGainLossComputation` record. The Form 1040 builder then sets these fields on the `Income` output object (file `Income.java`):

- `capitalGainLoss` ← `line_7a`
- `line7bScheduleDNotRequired` ← `exception_1`
- `line7bIncludesChildCapitalGainLoss` ← `includesChildCapitalGainLoss`
- `line7bChildAmountFromForm8814Line10` ← `childAmount`

When Schedule D is required, a full `ScheduleD` output object is also produced; when Form 8949 pages are needed, a full `Form8949` output object is produced. When Qualified Opportunity Fund activity is detected, an attachment placeholder `Form8997` is produced.
