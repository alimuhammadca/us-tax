## Part 1 — IRS rule (source-of-truth)

### 1.1 Authority

- Internal Revenue Code §135 — "Income from United States savings bonds used to pay higher education tuition and fees"
- IRS 2025 Form 8815 (Created 12/3/25)
- Publication 970 (2025) chapter 10
- Publication 550 (2025) Series EE and I bond interest

### 1.2 Eligibility (Who Can Take the Exclusion)

All four conditions must be satisfied:

1. The filer cashed qualified U.S. savings bonds in 2025 that were issued after 1989.
2. The filer paid qualified higher education expenses in 2025 for themselves, their spouse, or their dependents.
3. The filer's filing status is any status **except Married Filing Separately**. (IRC §135(d)(2))
4. The filer's modified Adjusted Gross Income (AGI) is less than:
   - **$114,500** for Single, Head of Household, or Qualifying Surviving Spouse
   - **$179,250** for Married Filing Jointly

### 1.3 Qualifying U.S. savings bonds

- Series EE bonds issued after 1989 OR Series I bonds.
- Issued in the name of the bondholder (or jointly with their spouse for MFJ).
- Bondholder must have been age 24 or older before the issue date.
- Bonds issued in the name of a child under age 24 do NOT qualify for parent's exclusion.

### 1.4 Qualified higher education expenses (line 2)

Tuition and fees required for enrollment or attendance at an eligible educational institution for the filer, spouse, or any dependent claimed on Form 1040. Includes contributions to a Coverdell ESA or QTP for the filer's dependent.

EXCLUDED (per IRS instructions):
- Room and board
- Courses involving sports, games, or hobbies that are not part of a degree- or certificate-granting program
- Expenses covered by nontaxable benefits (subtract on line 3)
- Expenses used to figure an education credit on Form 8863
- Expenses used to figure the nontaxable amount of a distribution from a Coverdell ESA or QTP

### 1.5 Line-by-line computation

See `lines/f8815.md` §1 + §2 + §5 for the canonical line-by-line formulas. Summarized:

| Line | Formula | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| 4 | `max(0, line2 − line3)` | If 0, **stop. Cannot take exclusion.** |
| 7 | `(line4 ≥ line5) ? 1.000 : roundDecimal(line4 / line5, 3)` | Ratio of qualified expenses to bond proceeds |
| 8 | `roundMoney(line6 × line7)` | Candidate exclusion amount |
| 11 | `max(0, line9 − line10)` | Excess MAGI over phaseout start |
| 12 | `(line11 ≥ phaseoutRange) ? 1.000 : roundDecimal(line11 / phaseoutRange, 3)` | Phaseout ratio |
| 13 | `roundMoney(line8 × line12)` | Phaseout reduction |
| 14 | `max(0, line8 − line13)` | Excludable savings bond interest |

### 3.2 Open gaps

| # | Gap | Severity | Closure path |
|---|---|---|---|
| 6 | Form 4563 (American Samoa exclusion) is out of scope, so users from American Samoa cannot auto-derive the Line 9 Worksheet add-back from Form 4563 line 15. Workaround: user enters the value manually via the `form8815Line9WsOtherAddbacks` catch-all field | Low | Out of scope per `outstanding.md`; workaround in place |
| 7 | **(NEW from Phase 4)** When the line-by-line path computes a non-zero line 14 but the user did NOT also fill in `savingsBondExclusionAmount`, Form 1040 line 2b is not retroactively reduced. This is because `computeInterestIncome` runs before `computeForm8815` in `prepare()` and finalizes line 2b based on `savingsBondExclusionAmount` only. Workaround: the user can copy the computed line 14 value into the manual override field. Permanent fix requires moving `computeForm8815` earlier in `prepare()` (between AGI computation and the Form 1040 income summation) | Medium | Follow-up commit beyond Phase 6 |

### 3.3 Out of scope

- Form 4563 (American Samoa exclusion) — application-wide out-of-scope.
- Coverdell ESA / QTP separate tracking — line 2 instructions cover them but the application doesn't have a dedicated intake.
- Form 8818 (Optional record-keeping) — informational only; never attached.
