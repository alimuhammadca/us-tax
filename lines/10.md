Below is a **2025-tax-year, IRS-verified, developer-ready** rule map for **Form 1040 line 10 (Adjustments to income)** and its feeder, **Schedule 1 (Form 1040), line 26**.

Reviewed against:
- **2025 Form 1040 / 1040-SR**
- **2025 Schedule 1 (Form 1040)**
- **2025 Form 1040 / 1040-SR instructions** (`i1040gi`)
- Current implementation in `TaxReturnComputeService.java` (orchestrator `computeIncomeAdjustments` at line 13840; Schedule 1 line 25 / 26 derivation at lines 14145 / 14169; Pub. 915 subset at line 14181; persistence inside `buildAdjustments` at lines 7703-7732).

# 1) Line identity (2025 Form 1040)

- **Form**: 1040 / 1040-SR
- **Year**: 2025
- **Line**: 10
- **Label**: Adjustments to income from Schedule 1, line 26

```text
Form1040.line10 = Schedule1.line26
```

Per the 2025 Schedule 1 form:

```text
Schedule1.line25 = sum(lines 24a through 24z)               # 12 sublines (24a-24k + 24z)
Schedule1.line26 = sum(lines 11 through 23) + line25        # 13 operands + 1 sub-sum
```

So line 10 is a pure carryover from Schedule 1 Part II.

# 2) Exact 2025 Schedule 1 Part II structure

## 2.1 Lines 11 through 23

- **11** Educator expenses
- **12** Certain business expenses of reservists, performing artists, and fee-basis government officials
- **13** Health savings account deduction
- **14** Moving expenses for members of the Armed Forces
- **15** Deductible part of self-employment tax — **OUT OF SCOPE** (blocking)
- **16** Self-employed SEP, SIMPLE, and qualified plans — **OUT OF SCOPE** (blocking)
- **17** Self-employed health insurance deduction — **OUT OF SCOPE** (blocking)
- **18** Penalty on early withdrawal of savings
- **19a** Alimony paid (pre-2019 agreements only)
- **19b** Recipient's SSN
- **19c** Date of original divorce or separation agreement
- **20** IRA deduction
- **21** Student loan interest deduction
- **22** Reserved for future use (hard-coded null in 2025)
- **23** Archer MSA deduction

## 2.2 Lines 24a through 24z

- **24a** Jury duty pay (repaid to employer)
- **24b** Deductible expenses related to income reported on line 8l (rental of personal property)
- **24c** Nontaxable Olympic / Paralympic medals and USOC prize money back-out (line 8m offset)
- **24d** Reforestation amortization and expenses
- **24e** Repayment of supplemental unemployment benefits under the Trade Act of 1974
- **24f** Contributions to section 501(c)(18)(D) pension plans
- **24g** Contributions by certain chaplains to section 403(b) plans
- **24h** Attorney fees and court costs for certain unlawful discrimination claims
- **24i** Attorney fees and court costs paid in connection with an IRS whistleblower award
- **24j** Housing **deduction** from Form 2555 (distinct from the 8d exclusion)
- **24k** Excess deductions of section 67(e) expenses from Schedule K-1 (Form 1041), box 11 code A
- **24z** Other adjustments (narrow — only when a specific IRS instruction directs a write-in)

## 2.3 Totals

```text
Schedule1.line25 = sum(lines 24a through 24z)               # 12 sublines
Schedule1.line26 = sum(lines 11 through 23) + Schedule1.line25
Form1040.line10  = Schedule1.line26
```

Line 22 is hard-coded null at `TaxReturnComputeService.java:14084` per the 2025 form.

# 3) Backend wiring

## 3.1 Orchestrator

- **Method**: `computeIncomeAdjustments(...)` at `TaxReturnComputeService.java:13840` — aggregates taxpayer + spouse Schedule 1 Part II inputs into an `IncomeAdjustmentsComputation` value object.
- **MFS guard**: takes a `boolean isMfsReturn` parameter and null-shadows `incomeAdjustmentsSpouse` on MFS. Critical because IRC §221(e)(2) makes line 21 unavailable on MFS and line 20 has the "MFS lived apart all year" flag.
- **Call site**: `TaxReturnComputeService.java:563`.

## 3.2 Sum sites

- **Schedule 1 line 25** at `TaxReturnComputeService.java:14145`:

  ```java
  BigDecimal line25 = sumAmounts(line24a, line24b, line24c, line24d, line24e,
                                  line24f, line24g, line24h, line24i, line24j, line24k, line24z);
  ```

- **Schedule 1 line 26** at `TaxReturnComputeService.java:14169`:

  ```java
  BigDecimal line26 = addNonNull(
      sumAmounts(line11, line12, line13, line14, line15, line16, line17, line18,
                 line19a, line20, line21, line22, line23),
      line25
  );
  ```

- **Pub. 915 subset accessor** at `TaxReturnComputeService.java:14181`: same shape as line 26 but with **line 21** and **line 22** excluded. The SS taxability worksheet uses this subset because the student-loan-interest deduction itself depends on MAGI (which depends on taxable SS) — including it would be circular. Line 22 is reserved.

## 3.3 Persistence

`buildAdjustments` at `TaxReturnComputeService.java:7703-7732` calls `roundMoney(incomeAdjustments.line10FromSchedule1Line26())`, then:

```java
adjustments.setAdjustmentsSchedule1(line10);
adjustments.setLine11aAdjustedGrossIncome(line11a);
adjustments.setLine11bAmountFromLine11aAdjustedGrossIncome(line11b);
```

When **both** `line10` and `line11a` are null, the entire Adjustments object is suppressed so the Form 1040 cells render blank rather than $0.

# 5) High-value 2025 rules by line

## 5.1 Line 11: educator expenses

- An eligible educator can deduct up to **$300** of qualified expenses.
- If filing jointly and both spouses are eligible educators, the combined maximum is **$600**, with neither spouse exceeding **$300** of that spouse's own qualified expenses.
- Eligible educator = K-12 teacher, instructor, counselor, principal, or aide who worked at least **900 hours** during a school year.

## 5.2 Line 12: reservists / performing artists / fee-basis officials

- Requires **Form 2106**. Not a general unreimbursed employee-expense deduction.

## 5.3 Line 13: HSA deduction

- Use **Form 8889**.
- Do not include employer contributions, rollovers, or qualified HSA funding distributions from an IRA.

## 5.4 Line 14: moving expenses

- Use **Form 3903**.
- Limited to members of the Armed Forces on active duty who move because of a permanent change of station.
- If claiming only qualifying storage fees during an absence from the United States, check the line-14 storage-fees box.

## 5.5 Lines 15 / 16 / 17: self-employment adjustments — OUT OF SCOPE

The orchestrator emits these blocking flags when the respective `has...OutOfScope` flag is true on either the taxpayer or spouse form:
- `INCOME_ADJUSTMENTS_LINE15_OUT_OF_SCOPE` (§1401 SE tax half)
- `INCOME_ADJUSTMENTS_LINE16_OUT_OF_SCOPE` (SEP / SIMPLE / qualified)
- `INCOME_ADJUSTMENTS_LINE17_OUT_OF_SCOPE` (§162(l) SE health insurance; Form 7206 exception cases also not supported)

The amounts are still summed into line 26 if present (so the rest of Schedule 1 still assembles), but submission is gated.

## 5.6 Line 18: penalty on early withdrawal of savings

- Comes from **Form 1099-INT** box 13 or **Form 1099-OID** box 13.
- Backend folds three layers at the orchestrator: manual user entry + `imported1099IntEarlyWithdrawalPenaltyTotal` + `imported1099OidEarlyWithdrawalPenaltyTotal` + statement-derived totals.

## 5.7 Lines 19a / 19b / 19c: alimony paid

Line 19a is allowed only if the payments were made under a divorce or separation agreement:
- entered into on or before **December 31, 2018**, and
- not later modified to provide that alimony received is not includible in the recipient's income.

If the agreement was entered into after December 31, 2018, no deduction is allowed on line 19a. Line 19b requires the recipient's SSN. Line 19c requires the **month and year** of the original agreement.

## 5.8 Line 20: IRA deduction

- Use the IRA deduction worksheet (Pub. 590-A iterative path) unless a special rule requires other treatment.
- If any traditional IRA contributions are nondeductible, **Form 8606** is required.
- Rollover contributions are not part of the line-20 deduction.
- If MFS and the taxpayer lived apart from the spouse the entire year, the box on line 20 should be checked.

## 5.9 Line 21: student loan interest deduction

- Capped at **$2,500**.
- Allowed only if:
  - interest was paid in 2025 on a qualified student loan;
  - filing status is **not MFS** (IRC §221(e)(2));
  - modified AGI is below **$100,000** (single / HOH / QSS) or **$200,000** (MFJ);
  - the taxpayer, or spouse on a joint return, is **not** claimed as a dependent.
- Phaseout starts at **$85,000** single / HOH / QSS, **$170,000** MFJ.
- Use **Pub. 970** if the taxpayer files **Form 2555** or **Form 4563**, or excludes Puerto Rico income.

The MFS guard at the orchestrator is critical here — without it, spouse-side data could leak and produce a line-21 deduction on an MFS return where it is statutorily disallowed.

## 5.10 Line 22: reserved

Hard-coded null at `TaxReturnComputeService.java:14084` for 2025. The Pub. 915 subset accessor also excludes line 22.

## 5.11 Line 23: Archer MSA deduction

- Use **Form 8853**.

## 5.12 Line 24 adjustments

- **24a** Jury duty pay given back to an employer because the employer continued paying salary.
- **24b** Expense side of the 8l rental-of-personal-property income.
- **24c** Nontaxable back-out for Olympic / Paralympic medals and USOC prize money included on 8m.
- **24e** Trade Act of 1974 supplemental unemployment benefit repayment.
- **24h** Limited to gross income from the qualifying unlawful discrimination action.
- **24i** Limited to the IRS award amount included in gross income.
- **24j** **Housing deduction** from Form 2555 — distinct from the **8d** foreign earned income / housing **exclusion**.
- **24k** From Schedule K-1 (Form 1041) box 11 code A; backend folds in `importedK1Section67eExcessDeductionsTotal`.

## 5.13 Line 24z: other adjustments

Do **not** model 24z as a generic catch-all.

- The 2025 Schedule 1 still has the printed 24z write-in line.
- The current Form 1040 instructions say to **leave 24z blank** unless another IRS instruction specifically tells the taxpayer to enter a write-in adjustment there.

The backend `buildSchedule1OtherAdjustmentItems` accepts user-entered description / amount pairs (deduped and owner-labeled) but the UI should steer users with an advisory.

# 6) Pub. 915 SUBSET versus full line 26

The Social Security taxability worksheet uses a Pub-915-specific subset of Schedule 1 Part II, NOT the full line 26:

```text
line10FromSchedule1Pub915Subset =
    sumAmounts(line11 .. line20, line23)            # excludes line 21 and line 22
  + line25
```

Why:
- **Line 21** (student loan interest) phaseout depends on MAGI, which depends on taxable SS benefits, which depends on the Pub. 915 worksheet input. Including line 21 in the SS worksheet's adjustments line would create a circular dependency.
- **Line 22** is reserved.

Two distinct accessors live on `IncomeAdjustmentsComputation`:
- `line10FromSchedule1Line26()` — full line 26 (used by Form 1040 line 10).
- `line10FromSchedule1Pub915Subset()` — the subset above (used by `computeSocialSecurityBenefits` at `TaxReturnComputeService.java:14897`).

Do not collapse these two accessors. The subset is intentionally narrower.

# 7) Inputs and supporting forms

## 7.1 Typical direct inputs by line

- educator records -> **line 11**
- **Form 2106** -> **line 12**
- **Form 8889** -> **line 13**
- **Form 3903** -> **line 14**
- **1099-INT / 1099-OID** early withdrawal penalty -> **line 18**
- divorce instrument + recipient SSN -> **line 19**
- IRA contribution records + retirement-plan coverage facts -> **line 20**
- **1098-E** and student-loan eligibility facts -> **line 21**
- **Form 8853** -> **line 23**
- **Form 2555**, **K-1 (1041)**, litigation / award records, etc. -> **lines 24a through 24k**

## 7.2 User facts often required

- educator eligibility and unreimbursed expense amount
- reservist / performing artist / fee-basis-official status
- Armed Forces active-duty PCS facts and storage-only claim facts
- alimony agreement date and recipient SSN
- IRA contribution timing, coverage, and MFS-lived-apart fact
- student loan dependency status and qualified-loan status
- whether a specific IRS instruction actually requires a **24z** write-in

# 8) Schedule 1 filing rule

Attach **Schedule 1** if any Part II adjustment is used.

Operationally:
- if any of lines 11 through 23 are nonzero, or
- if any of lines 24a through 24z are nonzero,

then Schedule 1 must be included and `Form 1040 line 10 = Schedule 1 line 26`.

# 9) Out of scope

- **Lines 15 / 16 / 17** — Schedule SE half-deduction, SE retirement plans, SE health insurance. All emit blocking out-of-scope flags. Form 7206 exception cases (multiple SE income sources, Form 2555 filer, qualified LTC) are also unsupported.
- **Pub. 974** path for line 17 (Marketplace plan with advance PTC) is not implemented.

# 10) Validation rules

## 10.1 Arithmetic validations

```text
validate Schedule1.line25 == sum(lines 24a through 24z)
validate Schedule1.line26 == sum(lines 11 through 23) + line25
validate Form1040.line10  == Schedule1.line26
```

## 10.2 Structural validations

- If line 12 is used, **Form 2106** should be present.
- If line 13 is used, **Form 8889** should be present.
- If line 14 is used, **Form 3903** should be present.
- If line 19a is nonzero, line 19b and line 19c should also be populated.
- If line 20 is used and nondeductible traditional IRA contributions exist, **Form 8606** should be generated.
- If line 24z is used, preserve the specific IRS-authorized write-in type and amount.

## 10.3 MFS guard

`computeIncomeAdjustments` is the 14th member of the single-guard MFS cascade. On MFS:
- the spouse-side Schedule 1 Part II map is null-shadowed at function entry;
- every taxpayer + spouse aggregator collapses to taxpayer-only;
- line 21 student-loan-interest deduction cannot fire from spouse data;
- the line-19b / 19c text fields use taxpayer-only `firstNonBlank`.

Lock-in test: `mfsExcludesSpouseIncomeAdjustmentsFromLine10`.

# 11) Implementation guardrails

- Do **not** treat 24z as a broad miscellaneous-adjustment bucket.
- Do **not** assume Form 7206 is always required for line 17, but do block the line-17 path in the 2025 exception cases.
- Do **not** allow line 19a for post-2018 divorce or separation agreements.
- Do **not** allow line 21 for MFS or for a taxpayer claimed as someone else's dependent.
- Do **not** confuse 24j housing **deduction** with Schedule 1 line 8d foreign earned income / housing **exclusion**.
- Do **not** include rollovers or nondeductible IRA contributions in the line-20 deduction amount.
- Do **not** treat line 22 as an active deduction line.
- Do **not** route the SS taxability worksheet through `line10FromSchedule1Line26()`; use the Pub-915 subset.
- Do **not** let spouse-side data leak on MFS; the orchestrator guard is mandatory.

# 12) Practical developer cheat sheet

- **Form 1040 line 10** = **Schedule 1 line 26**.
- **Schedule 1 line 26** = lines 11 through 23 + line 25.
- **Schedule 1 line 25** = lines 24a through 24k + 24z (12 sublines; 24l-24y absent).
- **Educator expenses**: up to **$300** each, **$600** joint if both qualify.
- **Student loan interest**: up to **$2,500**; phased out over $85k-$100k single / HOH / QSS and $170k-$200k MFJ; **MFS blocked**; dependents blocked.
- **Alimony paid**: only for qualifying pre-2019 agreements.
- **Line 22** is reserved (hard-coded null).
- **24z**: leave blank unless a specific current IRS instruction directs otherwise.
- **Pub. 915** SS worksheet uses a SUBSET (no line 21, no line 22) — separate accessor.
- **Lines 15 / 16 / 17** are blocking out-of-scope.
