# Schedule R (Form 1040) — Credit for the Elderly or the Disabled

**Tax year:** 2025
**IRS form:** Schedule R (Form 1040) (2025), Attachment Sequence No. 16, OMB No. 1545-0074, Cat. No. 11359K (created 4/14/25)
**Sources:** Schedule R (Form 1040) 2025; Instructions for Schedule R (Form 1040) 2025 (Cat. No. 11357O, Nov 14 2025); Pub. 17; J.K. Lasser's Your Income Tax 2025 Professional Edition §§ 34.7–34.8; Taxes For Dummies; IRC § 22
**Destination:** Schedule 3 (Form 1040), line 6d → Form 1040 line 20

---

## 1. Line Identity

Schedule R computes the **Credit for the Elderly or the Disabled** under **IRC § 22**. It is a **nonrefundable** personal credit against regular income tax liability. It cannot be carried forward — any unused amount is lost.

**Practical note (J.K. Lasser §34.7):** Very few taxpayers can claim this credit in practice. The base amounts and AGI phaseout thresholds have not been inflation-adjusted since 1983 while Social Security benefit amounts and regular-tax liability have grown, making the credit unavailable to most elderly taxpayers who receive even modest nontaxable Social Security.

**Cannot be claimed on Form 1040-NR** (unless the taxpayer is married to a U.S. citizen or resident alien and files jointly with a dual-status election).

**IRS "figure the credit for you" (CFE) option:** Taxpayers who meet the requirements may write "CFE" on Schedule 3, line 6d and the IRS will compute the credit. The taxpayer still completes Part I of Schedule R and, if applicable, Part II and Part III lines 11 and 13. Per Pub. 17 paper-return instructions: write "CFE" next to Schedule 3 line 6d, attach Schedule R, check the appropriate Part I box, and complete Part II and Part III lines 11 and 13 if they apply. This is an IRS-controlled paper-return option, not a system computation path.

## 2. Per-Return Filing Rules

```
Schedule R count per return = 0 or 1
```

**One Schedule R per return, not one per spouse.** Both spouses' qualifying status, disability income, and nontaxable income are all reported on a single Schedule R. Part I captures the combined age/disability situation of both spouses through nine numbered checkboxes — exactly one is checked per return.

| Filing situation | Rule |
|---|---|
| Single / HOH / QSS | At most one Schedule R per return |
| MFJ | One joint Schedule R — both spouses' data combined on one form |
| MFS — lived apart all year | At most one Schedule R; special boxes 8/9 only |
| MFS — lived with spouse any time in 2025 | **Cannot claim the credit at all** |

**Both spouses' information is required on an MFJ return** because both may independently qualify (one by age, one by disability), and both spouses' nontaxable Social Security, Railroad Retirement, and pension income must be reported on lines 13a and 13b to correctly reduce the credit base.

```
if filing_status == MFJ:
    create one ScheduleR
    gather both spouses' ages, disability status, taxable disability income,
    nontaxable social security/pension/disability income, and AGI
else:
    use taxpayer-only return data
```

## 3. Two Eligibility Paths — Qualified Individual

A taxpayer is a **qualified individual** if they are a U.S. citizen or resident alien **and** meet **one** of the following:

### Path A — Age 65 or older

- A person reaches age 65 on the **day before** their 65th birthday. A taxpayer born on January 1, 1961 is considered age 65 at the end of 2025; born **after** January 1, 1961 → under age 65 at year-end.
- A taxpayer who died in 2025 is considered age 65 at year-end if they were 65 or older on the day before their death.

### Path B — Under age 65 with permanent and total disability

All three must be true:
1. **Retired on permanent and total disability** (see §4 below).
2. **Received taxable disability income** in 2025 (see §7 below).
3. Had **not yet reached mandatory retirement age** as of January 1, 2025 (mandatory retirement age = the age at which the employer would have required the taxpayer to retire had they not become disabled).

---

## 4. Permanent and Total Disability — Definition

A person is permanently and totally disabled if **both** apply:

1. They **cannot engage in any substantial gainful activity** due to a physical or mental condition.
2. A **qualified physician** certifies the condition has lasted or can be expected to last continuously for at least **one year**, or can be expected to **result in death**.

**Substantial gainful activity:** Performance of significant duties over a reasonable period of time for pay or profit, or in work generally done for pay or profit. Full-time work (or part-time at the employer's convenience) in a competitive work situation at at least minimum wage conclusively demonstrates the ability to engage in substantial gainful activity.

**Not substantial gainful activity:** Caring for oneself, hobbies, institutional therapy, school attendance, clubs, social programs.

**Sheltered employment** at a qualified location (certified by Department of Labor, hospital, homebound, or VA-sponsored program) does **not** conclusively prove ability to engage in substantial gainful activity.

**Pre-1977 retirement exception:** A person who retired on disability before 1977 and was not permanently and totally disabled at retirement qualifies if they were permanently and totally disabled on January 1, 1976 **or** January 1, 1977.

**VA certification:** If the Department of Veterans Affairs certifies the taxpayer is permanently and totally disabled, **VA Form 21-0172** may be used as a substitute for the physician's statement. VA Form 21-0172 must be signed by a person authorized by the VA and is obtainable from the local VA regional office.

## 5. Part I — Filing Status and Age/Disability Track (Nine Checkboxes)

Exactly **one box** is checked. The box determines the initial base amount, the AGI threshold, and whether Part II (disability certification) is required.

| Box | Filing Status | Condition | Part II required? |
|---|---|---|---|
| **1** | Single, HOH, or QSS | Age 65 or older | No |
| **2** | Single, HOH, or QSS | Under 65; retired on permanent and total disability | **Yes** |
| **3** | MFJ | Both spouses age 65 or older | No |
| **4** | MFJ | Both spouses under 65; only one retired on P&T disability | **Yes** |
| **5** | MFJ | Both spouses under 65; both retired on P&T disability | **Yes** |
| **6** | MFJ | One spouse 65 or older; other under 65 and retired on P&T disability | **Yes** |
| **7** | MFJ | One spouse 65 or older; other under 65 and **not** retired on P&T disability | No |
| **8** | MFS | Age 65 or older; lived apart from spouse **all of 2025** | No |
| **9** | MFS | Under 65; retired on P&T disability; lived apart from spouse **all of 2025** | **Yes** |

**Routing after Part I:**
- Boxes 1, 3, 7, 8 → skip Part II; go directly to Part III.
- Boxes 2, 4, 5, 6, 9 → complete Part II before Part III.

**MFS restriction:** A married person who lived with their spouse at **any time** during 2025 cannot claim the credit regardless of age or disability.

---

## 6. Part II — Statement of Permanent and Total Disability

Required only when boxes 2, 4, 5, 6, or 9 are checked. Applies to each spouse who is under age 65 and claims P&T disability.

### Case A — Existing physician's statement (line 2 checkbox)

Check **line 2 of Part II** if **all three** are true:
1. A physician's statement was filed with or obtained for a return for **1983 or an earlier year**, **or** a physician's statement was obtained for a later year and the physician **signed line B** (no reasonable probability of improvement).
2. The taxpayer was permanently and totally disabled during 2025.
3. The taxpayer was unable to engage in any substantial gainful activity during 2025 because of the condition.

If line 2 is checked → **no new physician's statement is needed for 2025**.

### Case B — New physician's statement required

If line 2 cannot be checked, the taxpayer must obtain a new Physician's Statement (the form is printed in the Schedule R instructions). The physician completes:
- **Line A** — certifies the condition has lasted or can be expected to last continuously for at least one year (or result in death). **(Statement must be renewed each year.)**
- **Line B** — certifies there is no reasonable probability the condition will ever improve. **(Once signed, no future statements are needed.)**

The physician's statement:
- Names the disabled person.
- Certifies they were permanently and totally disabled on January 1, 1976 or January 1, 1977, **or** on the date they retired (taxpayer enters the retirement date if after 1976).

> **The physician's statement is NEVER attached to the return.** It is always kept by the taxpayer for their records. VA Form 21-0172 is an accepted substitute for the physician's statement.

**Box 5 (both spouses disabled):** Both spouses must each obtain their own statement for the first year a new statement is required.

---

## 7. Taxable Disability Income — Definition

Required for all disability-track boxes (2, 4, 5, 6, 9). Must meet **both**:

1. Paid under an **employer's accident or health plan or pension plan**.
2. **Included in income as wages** (or payments in lieu of wages) for time absent from work due to permanent and total disability.

**What is NOT disability income:**
- Payments from a plan that does not provide for disability retirement.
- A lump-sum payment for accrued annual leave upon retirement on disability (this is salary, not disability income).
- Amounts received **after** reaching mandatory retirement age (those amounts are pension income).

After mandatory retirement age is reached, the disability income classification ends; amounts received are pension income and no longer qualify for the disability track.

## 9. Income Ceilings — Credit Unavailable Above These Limits

From **Table 1** in the 2025 Schedule R instructions. The credit is **fully unavailable** when **either** income measure hits its ceiling:

| Filing Status / Situation | AGI ceiling (line 14) | Nontaxable SS + pensions ceiling (line 13c) |
|---|---|---|
| Single, HOH, or QSS | $17,500 | $5,000 |
| MFJ — only one spouse qualifies | $20,000 | $5,000 |
| MFJ — both spouses qualify | $25,000 | $7,500 |
| MFS — lived apart all year | $12,500 | $3,750 |

These thresholds have **not been adjusted for inflation since 1983**.

## 10. Part III — Computing the Credit (Lines 10–22)

### Line 10 — Initial Base Amount

| Box checked | Amount |
|---|---|
| Box 1, 2, 4, or 7 | **$5,000** |
| Box 3, 5, or 6 | **$7,500** |
| Box 8 or 9 | **$3,750** |

If box 2, 4, 5, 6, or 9 was checked → **must complete line 11** before line 12. Otherwise go directly to line 12.

### Line 11 — Taxable Disability Income Cap (disability boxes only)

The initial base for persons under 65 cannot exceed their total taxable disability income:

| Box checked | Line 11 = |
|---|---|
| Box 2, 4, or 9 | Total taxable disability income reported on the return |
| Box 5 | Combined taxable disability income of **both** spouses |
| Box 6 | **$5,000 plus** the taxable disability income of the spouse who is under age 65 |

### Line 12 — Credit Base

```
If line 11 completed:  line 12 = min(line 10, line 11)
If line 11 skipped:    line 12 = line 10
```

This is the credit base before nontaxable income and AGI reductions.

### Lines 13a, 13b, 13c — Nontaxable Income Reductions

These reduce the credit base dollar-for-dollar.

**Line 13a — Nontaxable Social Security and Railroad Retirement (Tier 1 SS-equivalent):**

Enter the **nontaxable** portion of:
- Social Security benefits (Form 1040 line 6a minus line 6b).
- Tier 1 Railroad Retirement benefits treated as Social Security (the Social Security Equivalent Benefit, nontaxable portion).
- Workers' compensation that reduces Social Security benefits is treated as Social Security for this line.

**Do not include:** Lump-sum death benefits paid to a surviving spouse or child's insurance benefits received as a guardian.

**Line 13b — Other nontaxable pensions, annuities, and disability income:**

Enter the nontaxable portion of:
- **Veterans' pensions** (administered by the VA or another federal law other than the IRC).
- Any other pension, annuity, or disability benefit excluded from income under any provision of **federal law other than the IRC**.

**Do NOT include on line 13b:**
- Any pension, annuity, or similar allowance for personal injuries or sickness from **active military service** (any country's armed forces, NOAA, or Public Health Service). **(Military disability pensions are excluded from both 13a and 13b.)**
- A **disability annuity** payable under § 808 of the Foreign Service Act of 1980.
- Amounts that are a **return of basis** in a pension or annuity (non-taxable recovery of cost).

**Line 13c:**
```
line 13c = line 13a + line 13b
```
If no nontaxable income of either type, enter -0-.

### Lines 14–17 — Excess AGI Reduction

**Line 14:** Enter AGI from Form 1040 (or 1040-SR) **line 11a**.

**Line 15 — AGI threshold (by box checked):**

| Box checked | Threshold |
|---|---|
| Box 1 or 2 | **$7,500** |
| Box 3, 4, 5, 6, or 7 | **$10,000** |
| Box 8 or 9 | **$5,000** |

**Line 16:**
```
line 16 = max(0, line 14 − line 15)
```

**Line 17:**
```
line 17 = line 16 × 50%   (one-half of excess AGI over threshold)
```

### Lines 18–19 — Combined Reduction and Remaining Base

**Line 18:**
```
line 18 = line 13c + line 17
```

**Line 19:**
```
line 19 = line 12 − line 18
```
If **line 19 is zero or less → stop; the credit cannot be taken.** Do not complete lines 20–22.

### Line 20 — Tentative Credit

```
line 20 = line 19 × 15%
```

### Line 21 — Credit Limit Worksheet

Line 21 comes from the **Credit Limit Worksheet** (in the instructions; kept for records, not filed):

```
Worksheet step 1:  Form 1040 (or 1040-SR) line 18 (total tax before credits)
Worksheet step 2:  Schedule 3 lines 1 + 2 + 6l
                   (foreign tax credit + child/dependent care credit +
                    residential clean energy credit)
Worksheet step 3:  line 21 = max(0, step 1 − step 2)
                   If zero or less → stop; the credit cannot be taken.
```

### Line 22 — Credit for the Elderly or the Disabled

```
line 22 = min(line 20, line 21)
```

**Enter this amount on Schedule 3 (Form 1040), line 6d.**

## 11. Full Line-by-Line Formula Summary

```
line 10   Initial base: $5,000 / $7,500 / $3,750 (by box)
line 11   Taxable disability income cap (disability boxes only)
line 12   = min(line10, line11)  [or = line10 if no disability box]
line 13a  Nontaxable SS + nontaxable Tier 1 RRB (SS-equivalent)
line 13b  Nontaxable veterans' pensions + other nontaxable federal pensions
line 13c  = line13a + line13b
line 14   AGI (Form 1040 line 11a)
line 15   AGI threshold: $7,500 (boxes 1/2) | $10,000 (boxes 3–7) | $5,000 (boxes 8/9)
line 16   = max(0, line14 − line15)
line 17   = line16 × 0.50
line 18   = line13c + line17
line 19   = line12 − line18     [if ≤ 0 → stop, no credit]
line 20   = line19 × 0.15       [tentative credit]
line 21   Credit Limit Worksheet = Form1040.line18 − (Sch3.line1 + Sch3.line2 + Sch3.line6l)
                                   [if ≤ 0 → stop, no credit]
line 22   = min(line20, line21) → Schedule 3 line 6d
```

## 12. Maximum Possible Credit

Given the 15% rate on the reduced base, and assuming zero nontaxable SS/pensions and AGI exactly at the threshold:

| Situation | Max base (line 12) | Max credit (line 20) |
|---|---|---|
| Single / HOH / QSS | $5,000 | **$750** |
| MFJ — both qualify | $7,500 | **$1,125** |
| MFS | $3,750 | **$562.50** |

In practice, the credit is further reduced (often to zero) by nontaxable Social Security benefits on line 13a, or by the Credit Limit Worksheet if tax liability is small.

**Downstream:** Line 22 → Schedule 3 line 6d → Schedule 3 line 8 → Form 1040 line 20.

## 14. Interaction with Other Tax Items

### Nontaxable Social Security (SSA-1099)
The nontaxable SS portion (Form 1040 line 6a − 6b) reduces the credit base dollar-for-dollar on line 13a. Since the line 13c ceiling for single filers is $5,000 (matching the entire base amount), a single filer receiving $5,000 or more of nontaxable SS has zero credit regardless of AGI.

### Railroad Retirement (RRB-1099)
The nontaxable portion of the Social Security Equivalent Benefit (Tier 1, treated as SS) goes on line 13a. Other Tier 1 and Tier 2 nontaxable RRB amounts may go on line 13b if they are excluded under federal law.

### Veterans' Benefits (VA)
- Nontaxable VA pension reduces the base on line 13b.
- VA Form 21-0172 may substitute for the physician's statement for the disability path.
- Military disability pensions for active service are **excluded from both line 13a and 13b** — they do not reduce the credit base.

### Workers' Compensation
Workers' compensation that reduces Social Security benefits is treated as Social Security for line 13a.

### Schedule 3 Credit Ordering
The Credit Limit Worksheet uses Schedule 3 lines 1 (foreign tax credit), 2 (child/dependent care), and 6l (residential clean energy credit) as prior credits subtracted from tax liability. Schedule R credit is computed **after** those three credits.

## 16. Out of Scope

- **Form 1040-NR filers:** Cannot claim the credit unless filing jointly as a U.S. resident under a dual-status election.
- **MFS with cohabitation:** Any MFS filer who lived with their spouse at any point in 2025 is categorically ineligible — no computation needed.
- **IRS "figure it for me" (CFE) path:** The IRS offers to compute the credit for taxpayers who qualify — write "CFE" on Schedule 3 line 6d. This is an external IRS service, not a system computation path.
- **AMT interaction:** Schedule R does not interact with AMT computations.
- **Estates and trusts:** Schedule R is not filed by estates or trusts; it is an individual-only form.
- **Pre-1977 disability retirement special rule:** The January 1, 1976 / January 1, 1977 lookback for non-disabled-at-retirement pre-1977 retirees — accepted as user-entered input; no system validation.
- **Workers' compensation reducing SS (RRB cross-treatment):** Implementation treats user-entered nontaxable SS on Form 1040 line 6a as the source and uses the standard formula (line 6a − 6b).
- **Sheltered employment determination:** Whether a taxpayer's employment constitutes "substantial gainful activity" is a facts-and-circumstances determination; treated as user-certified input.
