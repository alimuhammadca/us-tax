# Form 1116 (2025) — Foreign Tax Credit → Schedule 3 Line 1

> Sources: 2025 Form 1116 (`f1116.pdf`), 2025 Instructions for Form 1116 (https://www.irs.gov/instructions/i1116), J.K. Lasser's Your Income Tax 2025 Professional Edition (§36.13), IRS Publication 514.

---

## 1. Line Identity

**Schedule 3 line 1** — "Foreign tax credit (attach Form 1116 if required)"

- Credits foreign income taxes paid or accrued to foreign countries and U.S. territories
- **Nonrefundable** — limited to U.S. regular tax allocable to foreign-source income
- Flows: Form 1116 line 35 → Schedule 3 line 1 → Form 1040 line 20

Form 1116 is a **limitation form**. It does not simply equal foreign taxes paid. It computes:

```text
allowable_FTC_for_category =
    min(
        creditable_foreign_taxes_for_category,
        U.S. tax × (foreign-source taxable income for category / worldwide taxable income)
    )
```

---

## 2. When Form 1116 Is NOT Required — Simplified Exception

All five conditions must be met to skip Form 1116 entirely:

1. All foreign-source gross income is **passive** interest or dividends (not wages, business income, etc.)
2. All income and foreign taxes are reported on **qualified payee statements** only: Form 1099-DIV, 1099-INT, Schedule K-1, or Schedule K-3
3. Total creditable foreign taxes paid or accrued are **≤ $300** (≤ **$600** for MFJ)
4. Taxpayer is **not** filing Form 4563 and is **not** excluding Puerto Rico income
5. Taxes paid to **non-sanctioned countries** only

**When simplified exception applies:**
```text
Schedule3.line1 = min(total_foreign_taxes_paid, Form1040.line16 + Schedule2.line1a)
```
Enter directly on Schedule 3 line 1 — no Form 1116 attached.

**Key restriction:** Cannot carry unused foreign taxes to/from other years under the simplified exception. Carryovers from prior years using full Form 1116 are unaffected.

## 3. How Many Forms 1116 Per Return

**One separate Form 1116 is required for each income category (basket).**

```text
number_of_Form1116 = number_of_foreign_income_categories_requiring_separate_limitation
```

### Income Categories

| Box | Category | Description | Typical individual filer scenario |
|---|---|---|---|
| **a** | Section 951A | GILTI — amounts included under § 951A (excluding passive) | Rare; U.S. shareholder of CFC |
| **b** | Foreign branch | Business profits of U.S. persons from QBUs abroad | Rare for W-2/1099 filers |
| **c** | Passive | Dividends, interest, royalties, rents, annuities, capital gains | **Most common** — 1099-DIV box 7, 1099-INT box 6, K-3 passive |
| **d** | General | All income not in other categories — wages, active business | Applies when working for foreign employer |
| **e** | Section 901(j) | Income from sanctioned countries — **no credit allowed** | Rare; one form per sanctioned country |
| **f** | Re-sourced by treaty | U.S.-source income treated as foreign-source under a treaty | Rare |
| **g** | Lump-sum distribution | Foreign-source pension lump sums under Form 4972 | Rare |

**Multiple countries within a category:** Part I/II has 3 country columns (A, B, C). If more than 3 countries exist in one category, use additional Form 1116s for the same category.

### Filing-Count Examples

| Scenario | Forms 1116 |
|---|---|
| Simplified exception qualifies | 0 (enter directly on Schedule 3 line 1) |
| Passive 1099-DIV/INT only, > $300/$600 threshold | 1 (passive) |
| Foreign wages + foreign dividends | 2 (general + passive) |
| Foreign wages + foreign dividends + treaty re-sourced | 3 |
| Passive income from > 3 countries | 2+ (overflow to additional passive Form 1116s) |
| Income from a sanctioned country | Separate Form 1116 per sanctioned country (credit = $0) |
| Lump-sum pension distribution (Form 4972) | Separate Form 1116 (category g) |
| Section 951A (GILTI) inclusions | Separate Form 1116 (category a) |

## 4. MFJ — Per-Spouse Rule

**MFJ files ONE Form 1116 per income category per return — not per spouse.** Both spouses' foreign income and foreign taxes for the same category are combined on a single form.

| Filing status | Rule |
|---|---|
| **MFJ** | One Form 1116 per income category per return; both spouses' data combined on same form |
| **MFS** | Each spouse files their own return with their own Form 1116(s) |
| **Single / HOH / QSS** | One Form 1116 per applicable category |

**Practical result for most individual filers (passive only):**
- Combined box 7 (1099-DIV) + box 6 (1099-INT) ≤ $600 → no Form 1116, simplified exception applies
- Combined > $600 → one Form 1116 (passive category) covering both spouses' 1099 foreign taxes

If each spouse has foreign income in **different categories**, separate Form 1116s arise from the category difference — not from being separate spouses.

## 6. Part I — Taxable Income From Foreign Sources

Part I is completed **per country column** (columns A, B, C). All amounts in U.S. dollars.

| Line | Description | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **i** | Foreign country or U.S. territory name | One column per country/territory |
| **1a** | Gross foreign-source income of the checked category | Do NOT include 0%-rate QDCG items |
| **1b** | Checkbox: alternative basis for employee compensation ≥ $250k | Check and report amount if using alternative sourcing |
| **2** | Deductions **definitely related** to line 1a income | COGS, wages, depreciation directly allocable to foreign income; include Schedule K-3 pre-allocated deductions |
| **3a** | Pro-rata: standard deduction or certain itemized deductions | Rare for individual filers |
| **3b** | Pro-rata: other deductions not definitely related | Attach statement |
| **3c** | Add lines 3a and 3b | |
| **3d** | Gross foreign-source income | = line 1a total |
| **3e** | Gross income from all sources | Same amount on **all** Forms 1116 filed for the return |
| **3f** | Divide line 3d by line 3e | Apportionment ratio |
| **3g** | Multiply line 3c by line 3f | Pro-rata deduction allocated to foreign income |
| **4a** | Home mortgage interest allocated to foreign income | Use Worksheet for Home Mortgage Interest in instructions |
| **4b** | Other interest expense allocated to foreign income | If gross foreign income < $5,000: allocate by gross income ratio; otherwise by asset method |
| **5** | Losses from foreign sources | Include capital losses exceeding capital gains; § 461(l) limits apply |
| **6** | Add lines 2, 3g, 4a, 4b, and 5 | Total deductions and losses |
| **7** | Subtract line 6 from line 1a | **Net foreign-source taxable income for this category** |

**Qualified dividend and capital gain adjustment (when applicable):**
- Required when Schedule D Tax Worksheet line 18 > 0 and line 45 < line 46 (or QDCG Worksheet equivalent)
- **Adjustment exception** (not required if): foreign source capital gains + qualified dividends < $20,000 AND QDCG worksheet line 5 ≤ $394,600 MFJ / $197,300 other
- If required: multiply foreign-source qualified dividends by **0.4054** (15%-rate items) or **0.5405** (20%-rate items) before entering on line 1a
- **0%-rate items:** exclude entirely from line 1a — do not include at all

**Schedule K-3 filers:** Use a separate Form 1116 for each category and a separate column for each country/territory. Pre-allocated deductions from Schedule K-3 (Part II §2) go on line 2; interest expense from K-3 goes on line 4b.

**Form 2555 filers:** Must exclude § 911 excluded income from Part I line 1a for the general category.

**Lump-sum distribution category (g):** Skip Part I entirely — proceed to Part II.

## 7. Part II — Foreign Taxes Paid or Accrued

One row per country (rows A, B, C).

| Column | Description |
|---|---|
| (i)/(k) | **Paid** or **Accrued** election — must check one per country row; applies to all taxes for that country in that year |
| (l) | Date paid or accrued |
| (m) | Foreign currency — dividends |
| (n) | Foreign currency — rents and royalties |
| (o) | Foreign currency — interest |
| (p) | Foreign currency — other taxes |
| (q) | USD — dividends withheld |
| (r) | USD — rents and royalties withheld |
| (s) | USD — interest withheld |
| (t) | USD — other taxes paid/accrued |
| **(u)** | **Total USD** = sum of columns q through t |

**Line 8:** Sum of column (u) rows A + B + C = total foreign taxes paid/accrued for this category.

### Currency Conversion

| Method | Exchange rate to use |
|---|---|
| **Paid** | Rate on the **payment date** |
| **Accrued (standard)** | **Average annual exchange rate** for the tax year |
| **Accrued (exceptions)** | Use payment-date rate if: taxes paid > 2 years late, paid in a prior year, or denominated in inflationary currency |

Inflationary currency: ≥ 30% cumulative inflation in 36 months before year-end per IMF CPI.

### Taxes NOT Creditable (Common Cases)

- Withholding taxes on dividends if shares held < 16 days in the 31-day window around ex-dividend date
- Withholding taxes where related payments on substantially similar property exist
- Taxes from sanctioned countries (§ 901(j))
- Taxes offset by foreign tax credits against the foreign country's own tax
- **2025 new rule (P.L. 119-21 § 960(d)(4)):** 10% of foreign taxes on § 959(a) exclusions caused by § 951A(a) GILTI inclusions (for taxes accruing after 6/28/2025) — reduce on line 12

## 8. Part III — Figuring the Credit

Lines 9–24 compute the **credit limitation** for this category.

```text
line9  = line8                         // total foreign taxes from Part II
line10 = carryover from prior years    // Schedule B line 3 col (xiv) + carrybacks
line11 = line9 + line10

line12 = reductions:
           - holding period failures
           - boycott provisions
           - covered asset acquisitions
           - §965(g) disallowed taxes
           - §960(d)(4): 10% of GILTI-caused §959(a) exclusion taxes (after 6/28/2025)
line13 = taxes reclassified under high-tax kickout (passive → general)

line14 = line11 + line12 + line13      // total available foreign taxes

line15 = line7                         // net foreign-source TI from Part I
line16 = OFL/ODL recapture adjustments // overall foreign loss / domestic loss accounts
line17 = line15 + line16               // adjusted net foreign-source TI
         // If line17 ≤ 0: skip line 18, enter 0 on line 19

line18 = Form1040.line11b − Form1040.line14
         // worldwide taxable income base (AGI minus deductions)
         // 2025: REMOVE §151(d)(5)(C) age-65+ deduction ($6,000/person) IF
         //   apportionable to foreign-source income
         // If QDCG or Schedule D Tax Worksheet applies: adjust to exclude
         //   foreign qualified dividends and foreign capital gains/losses

line19 = line17 / line18               // apportionment fraction (max 1.0)

line20 = Form1040.line16 + Schedule2.line1a   // total U.S. regular tax + AMT

line21 = line20 × line19               // maximum credit (limitation)
line22 = §960(c) increase (rare)
line23 = line21 + line22

line24 = min(line14, line23)           // allowed credit for this category
```

**High-tax kickout (line 13):** If the foreign effective tax rate on passive-category income exceeds the highest U.S. marginal rate, that income is reclassified as general category. The taxes move to the general category Form 1116 via line 13.

**Carryover generated:** If line 14 > line 23, the excess (line 14 − line 23) carries back 1 year then forward 10 years. Track on Schedule B (Form 1116) for this category.

## 9. Part IV — Summary of Separate Credits

Complete Part IV on **one designated summary Form 1116** (the one with the largest line 24). **Must be completed even when filing only a single Form 1116 (2025 rule).**

| Line | Category | Source |
|---|---|---|
| 25 | Section 951A | Part III line 24 of 951A Form 1116 |
| 26 | Foreign branch | Part III line 24 of foreign branch Form 1116 |
| 27 | Passive | Part III line 24 of passive Form 1116 |
| 28 | General | Part III line 24 of general Form 1116 |
| 29 | Section 901(j) | Part III line 24 of 901(j) Form 1116 (always $0) |
| 30 | Re-sourced by treaty | Part III line 24 |
| 31 | Lump-sum distribution | Part III line 24 |
| **32** | **Total** | Sum lines 25–31 |
| **33** | Smaller of line 20 or line 32 | Overall cap: aggregate credit cannot exceed total U.S. tax |
| **34** | Reduction for international boycott | Form 5713 |
| **35** | **Foreign tax credit = line 33 − line 34** | → **Schedule 3 line 1** → Form 1040 line 20 |

## 10. Carryover and Carryback Rules

```text
Unused foreign tax credit (when line 14 > line 23):
  Carryback:   1 year prior
  Carryforward: 10 years forward

Simplified-exception years: no carryover for that year's unused taxes;
  carryovers from other years using full Form 1116 are unaffected

Pre-2018 carryforwards: special rules apply per regulations
```

**Schedule B (Form 1116):** Reconciles prior-year unused credits with current-year carry-in. Attach for each category whenever line 10 is nonzero or a new carryover is generated.

**Schedule C (Form 1116):** Filed when a **foreign tax redetermination** occurs (accrued tax differs from credited amount when finally paid; refund received; exchange rate change > $10,000 or > 2% of total accrued tax). Filed with the current-year return if no U.S. tax change; with an amended return if U.S. tax changes.

## 11. Completion Flow (Step-by-Step)

```text
Step 1 — Test simplified exception:
  if allForeignIncomeIsPassive1099
     AND totalForeignTaxes ≤ 300 (600 MFJ)
     AND notForm4563
     AND notPuertoRicoExclusion
     AND notSanctionedCountry:
       Schedule3.line1 = min(totalForeignTaxes, Form1040.line16 + Schedule2.line1a)
       STOP — no Form 1116 prepared

Step 2 — Group foreign income and taxes by income category (a–g)

Step 3 — For each category where taxes > $0:
  a. Identify countries; assign to columns A/B/C
     (if > 3 countries in category: use additional Form 1116 for overflow)
  b. Complete Part I per country → line 7 (net foreign-source TI)
  c. Complete Part II per country → line 8 (total foreign taxes)
  d. Complete Part III → line 24 (allowed credit for this category)
  e. If carryover exists or is generated: attach Schedule B for this category

Step 4 — Designate summary Form 1116 (largest line 24)
  Complete Part IV (lines 25–35) on summary form only
  → line 35 = total foreign tax credit → Schedule 3 line 1

Step 5 — If foreign tax redeterminations from prior years: attach Schedule C
```

### Upstream dependencies

| Input | Source |
|---|---|
| Form 1040 line 11b (AGI) | Part III line 18 base |
| Form 1040 line 14 (deductions) | Part III line 18 base |
| Form 1040 line 16 (total tax) | Part III line 20 |
| Schedule 2 line 1a (AMT) | Part III line 20 |
| Form 2555 exclusion amount | Reduces Part I line 1a (general category) |

### Downstream

```text
Form1116.line35 → Schedule3.nonrefundableCredits.foreignTaxCredit (line 1)
               → Schedule3.line8 (total nonrefundable credits)
               → Form1040.line20
Form 6251 line 8 — AMT foreign tax credit (separate computation, deferred)
```

## 14. Compute Pseudocode

```java
// Step 1: Test simplified exception
BigDecimal totalForeignTaxes =
    sumField(div1099Entries, "foreignTaxPaidBox7")
    .add(sumField(int1099Entries, "foreignTaxPaidBox6"));

boolean simplifiedExceptionMet =
    allForeignIncomeIsPassive1099           // only 1099-DIV/INT sources
    && totalForeignTaxes.compareTo(
           mfj ? new BigDecimal("600") : new BigDecimal("300")) <= 0
    && !form4563Filed
    && !puertoRicoExclusionClaimed;

if (simplifiedExceptionMet) {
    BigDecimal limitation = addNonNull(form1040.getTotalTax(),
                                       schedule2.getAlternativeMinimumTax());
    schedule3.getNonrefundableCredits()
             .setForeignTaxCredit(totalForeignTaxes.min(limitation));
    return;
}

// Step 2: Full Form 1116 — DEFERRED
// Requires: per-category income grouping, per-country deduction allocation,
// Part III limitation, carryover tracking (Schedule B), QDCG adjustment
// Emit TaxReturnFlag.FORM_1116_REQUIRED as blocking flag
```

## 15. 2025-Specific Rules

| Rule | Detail |
|---|---|
| **P.L. 119-21 § 960(d)(4)** | Disallow 10% of foreign taxes on § 959(a) exclusions caused by § 951A(a) GILTI inclusions, for taxes accruing after 6/28/2025. Reduce on Part III line 12. |
| **§ 151(d)(5)(C) age-65+ deduction** | $6,000 per taxpayer/spouse age 65+; remove from line 18 ONLY if apportionable to foreign-source income. Not included in base otherwise. |
| **Part IV always required** | Lines 25–32 must be completed even when only one Form 1116 is filed. |
| **QDCG adjustment exception thresholds** | $394,600 MFJ / $197,300 other; foreign capital gains + qualified dividends < $20,000. |
| **QDCG adjustment factors** | 0.4054 (15%-rate items); 0.5405 (20%-rate items); 0%-rate items excluded entirely. |

## 17. Validations

```text
Form1116.line35 >= 0                      // credit is never negative
Form1116.line24 <= Form1116.line14        // cannot exceed available foreign taxes
Form1116.line24 <= Form1116.line23        // cannot exceed limitation
Form1116PartIV.line35 <= line20           // aggregate credit ≤ total U.S. tax

§ 901(j) (sanctioned country) Form 1116: line 24 = $0 always

Simplified exception: no Form 1116 attached;
  Schedule3.line1 = min(totalForeignTaxes, line16 + Schedule2.line1a)

Part IV lines 25–32: must be completed even with single Form 1116 (2025 rule)
```
