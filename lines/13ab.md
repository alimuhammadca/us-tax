Three structural facts that drive the line 13 implementation:
- **line 13a cannot be finalized before line 13b**, because the 2025 Form 8995 / 8995-A taxable-income limitation uses `taxable_income_before_qbi = Form 1040 line 11a − line 12e − line 13b`;
- the 2025 **Schedule 1-A** rules differ from a simple form-to-line mapping — each of the four new deductions (tips, overtime, car-loan interest, enhanced senior) has its own cap, phaseout shape, and filing-status gate;
- **line 13b** is not a charitable-contribution or legacy deduction line. It is a new 2025 line sourced **only** from **Schedule 1-A line 38** (sum of Parts II + III + IV + V).

# 1) Line identities (2025 Form 1040)

- **Line 13a**: Qualified business income deduction from **Form 8995** or **Form 8995-A**
- **Line 13b**: Additional deductions from **Schedule 1-A, line 38**

2025 structural change relative to 2024:
- line **12e** is the standard deduction or itemized deductions (was line 12 in 2024);
- line **13a** is the section 199A QBI deduction (unchanged label, but its taxable-income limitation now subtracts line 13b too);
- line **13b** is the **new** 2025 Schedule 1-A total.

# 3) Line 13a: Qualified business income deduction

## 3.1 Exact mapping

```text
Form1040.line13a =
    Form8995.line15
    or
    Form8995A.line39
```

The form used depends on the 2025 threshold rules below.

## 3.2 When to use Form 8995 vs. Form 8995-A

Use **Form 8995** only if all of the following are true:
- the taxpayer has QBI, qualified REIT dividends, or qualified PTP income or loss;
- 2025 **taxable income before the QBI deduction** is not more than:
  - **$394,600** if **MFJ**;
  - **$197,300** for all other filing statuses;
- the taxpayer is **not** a patron in a specified agricultural or horticultural cooperative.

Otherwise, use **Form 8995-A**.

Backend gating helper: `shouldUseForm8995A(filingStatus, taxIncBeforeQbi)` plus `qbiThresholdForStatus(...)` at the top of `computeLine13a`.

## 3.3 Critical 2025 compute-order rule

For Form 1040 filers, Form 8995 line 11 and Form 8995-A taxable-income instructions use:

```text
taxable_income_before_qbi = Form1040.line11a − Form1040.line12e − Form1040.line13b
```

That means:
- **line 12e must be final first**;
- **line 13b must be final first**;
- only then can **line 13a** be computed correctly.

The backend implements this via a **two-pass invocation** of `computeLine13a()`:
- **Pass 1** — inside `computeLine12()` (~line 5596) with `line13b = null` (preliminary placeholder that lets the first-pass line 14 / line 15 be wired so `Line12Computation` is a well-formed object for the frontend response);
- **Pass 2** — in `prepare()` (~line 1275) after `computeSchedule1A()` has produced the authoritative line 13b. This second-pass result is the authoritative line 13a and overwrites the preliminary value.

Reference: 13a #5 two-pass breadcrumb at `TaxReturnComputeService.java:~1234`.

## 3.4 Core QBI inputs

Potential 199A sources include:
- **Schedule C** trades or businesses — OUT OF SCOPE in this codebase; emits `LINE13A_SELF_EMPLOYMENT_OUT_OF_SCOPE_*`
- **Schedule E** rental activities that qualify as a trade or business for section 199A purposes — OUT OF SCOPE
- **Schedule F** farm businesses — OUT OF SCOPE
- **K-1 (Form 1065)** partnership section 199A items — SUPPORTED
- **K-1 (Form 1120-S)** S corporation section 199A items — SUPPORTED
- **K-1 (Form 1041)** estate or trust section 199A items — SUPPORTED
- **Form 1099-DIV box 5** qualified REIT dividends — SUPPORTED via SSN-matched statement aggregation
- qualified publicly traded partnership income or loss — SUPPORTED via K-1 199A fields
- prior-year QBI and REIT/PTP carryforwards — SUPPORTED via per-person personal forms
- manual QBI / REIT / PTP adjustments — SUPPORTED below threshold; BLOCKED above threshold via `LINE13A_MANUAL_QBI_THRESHOLD_UNSUPPORTED`

## 3.5 Core QBI rules

The deduction is generally the lesser of:
- the section 199A deduction computed on Form 8995 / 8995-A, or
- **20% of taxable income before the QBI deduction minus qualified dividends and net capital gain** as the forms define that limitation.

Below the threshold, the basic structure is:
- **20% of net QBI**, plus
- **20% of qualified REIT dividends and qualified PTP income**.

Above the threshold, Form 8995-A applies the additional rules, including:
- the **W-2 wage / UBIA of qualified property** limitation (50% W-2 wages or 25% W-2 wages + 2.5% UBIA); and
- the **SSTB phaseout / disallowance** rules.

Per-status thresholds and ranges are centralized at `ReferenceData.QBI_*`:

| Constant | Value (2025) |
|---|---:|
| `QBI_DEDUCTION_RATE` | 0.20 |
| `QBI_THRESHOLD_MFJ` | $394,600 |
| `QBI_THRESHOLD_ALL_OTHER` | $197,300 |
| `QBI_PHASEIN_RANGE_MFJ` | $100,000 |
| `QBI_PHASEIN_RANGE_ALL_OTHER` | $50,000 |
| `QBI_W2_LIMIT_RATE` | 0.50 |
| `QBI_W2_LIMIT_ALTERNATIVE_WAGES_RATE` | 0.25 |
| `QBI_W2_LIMIT_ALTERNATIVE_UBIA_RATE` | 0.025 |

Upper end of the phase-in range (derived at runtime): **$494,600 MFJ** / **$247,300 other**.

## 3.6 Carryforward semantics

If the net QBI component or REIT / PTP component is negative:
- it does **not** create a current negative line 13a;
- it is carried forward to next year under the applicable form instructions;
- the negative figure IS preserved on Form 8995 line 4 (`netQualifiedBusinessIncomeAfterCarryforward`) so that next year's carryforward import is correct;
- the value is **clamped to zero before the 20% factor** for current-year purposes.

```text
Form1040.line13a >= 0
```

This dual semantic — **preserve-negative for next-year storage + clamp-to-zero for current-year deduction input** — is documented at 13a #8 (~30-line breadcrumb in the helper, plus a 5-line cross-reference at the wiring site).

## 3.7 Capital-gain base for the QBI ceiling

Form 8995 line 12 / Form 8995-A taxable-income limitation use **net capital gain plus qualified dividends** as the subtrahend in:

```text
QBI ceiling = 20% × max(0, taxIncBeforeQbi − netCapitalGainAndQualifiedDividends)
```

In this codebase, **net capital gain** is approximated by **Schedule D line 15** (net long-term capital gain or loss; positive only) — NOT Form 1040 line 7a — and the approximation is encoded as:

```text
netCapitalGainAndQualifiedDividends = max(Schedule D line 15, 0) + qualifiedDividends
```

The IRS approximation `max(line7a, 0) + qualifiedDividends` is accepted per spec §3.5 + 13a #9 with three-source coverage (spec + knowledge file + existing inline comment).

## 3.8 Important exclusions from QBI

Do **not** treat the following as QBI:
- wage income as an employee;
- reasonable compensation from an S corporation;
- guaranteed payments;
- payments to a partner other than in a partner capacity;
- capital gains or losses;
- dividends and dividend equivalents;
- interest income not properly allocable to a trade or business;
- annuities unless received in connection with the trade or business;
- qualified REIT dividends and qualified PTP income as ordinary QBI items — they belong in the separate REIT / PTP component;
- tip income excluded under the 2025 tip-deduction rules when the forms say it is excluded from QBI.

# 4) Line 13b: Additional deductions from Schedule 1-A line 38

## 4.1 Exact mapping

```text
Form1040.line13b = Schedule1A.line38
Schedule1A.line38 = Schedule1A.line13 + Schedule1A.line21 + Schedule1A.line30 + Schedule1A.line37
```

## 4.2 What Schedule 1-A contains (2025)

Schedule 1-A has:
- **Part I** — Modified Adjusted Gross Income (MAGI) Amount (lines 1 to 3)
- **Part II** — No Tax on Tips (lines 4a to 13)
- **Part III** — No Tax on Overtime (lines 14a to 21)
- **Part IV** — No Tax on Car Loan Interest (lines 22 to 30)
- **Part V** — Enhanced Deduction for Seniors (lines 31 to 37)
- **Part VI** — Total Additional Deductions (line 38 — the line that feeds Form 1040 line 13b)

## 4.3 Schedule 1-A Part I MAGI

MAGI is the phaseout base for Parts II through V:

```text
Schedule1A.line1  = Form1040.line11b   (AGI)
Schedule1A.line2a = excluded Puerto Rico income      (manual)
Schedule1A.line2b = Form 2555 line 45 (FEIE)         (prefer computed; manual fallback)
Schedule1A.line2c = Form 2555 line 50 (housing excl) (prefer computed; manual fallback)
Schedule1A.line2d = Form 4563 line 15                (American Samoa exclusion; manual)
Schedule1A.line2e = line2a + line2b + line2c + line2d
Schedule1A.line3  = line1 + line2e
```

`Schedule1A.line3` is the MAGI input to every Part II to V phaseout.

## 4.4 Filing-status gates

- **Parts II / III / V** (tips, overtime, senior) are **MFJ-only when married** — MFS filers cannot claim. Single / HOH / QSS filers always qualify.
- **Part IV** (car loan interest) has **NO MFJ restriction** — applies to any filing status.

Backend implementation: `eligibleForPerPersonParts = !isMarried || isMfj` at the top of `computeSchedule1A`; gates Parts II / III / V. Part IV runs regardless.

## 4.5 MFS defensive guard at the call site

`computeSchedule1A` is invoked once from `prepare()` (~line 1132). The call site null-shadows the spouse inputs on MFS so that stale spouse-form data cannot leak into MAGI on a return that later switches to MFS:

```java
schedule1A = computeSchedule1A(
    filingStatus,
    form1040.getAdjustments(),
    additionalDeductionsTaxpayer,
    isMfsReturn ? null : additionalDeductionsSpouse,  // 13b #1: MFS guard
    you, spouse,
    w2Entries, form1099NecEntries, form1099KEntries, tips,
    form2555Taxpayer,
    isMfsReturn ? null : form2555Spouse,              // 13b #1: MFS guard
    flags, uid);
```

`computeSchedule1A` is the **17th orchestrator** in the MFS-guard cascade (per 13b #1).

# 5) Schedule 1-A Part II: No Tax on Tips (line 13)

## 5.1 Eligibility

Fill out Part II only if:
- the taxpayer and/or spouse received **qualified tips** in 2025;
- the tips were received in an occupation listed at **IRS.gov/TippedOccupations**;
- the recipient has a **valid SSN** valid for employment and issued before the due date of the 2025 return, including extensions;
- if married, the return is **MFJ**.

Per-spouse eligibility booleans on the personal forms:
- `taxpayerReceivedQualifiedTips` + `taxpayerTippedOccupationConfirmed`;
- `spouseReceivedQualifiedTips` + `spouseTippedOccupationConfirmed` (MFJ only).

## 5.2 Inputs

Raw tips per person are summed from up to four sources:
- **W-2 box 7** (`socialSecurityTipsAmount`) — SSN-matched via `sumW2Box7TipsForSsn`;
- **Form 4137** line 4 (unreported tips) — auto-computed from W-2 box 8 allocated tips plus manual entries;
- **manual entry** (`taxpayerManualTipsFromNonStatementSources`) for tips not captured by W-2 or Form 4137;
- **1099-NEC** trade tips — SSN-matched via `sum1099NecTipsForSsn`; capped by `taxpayerTradeTipsNetIncomeCap` **whenever non-null** (the trade-tips net-income limitation applies any time the user sets a cap).

## 5.3 Important special rules

- If tips were received as an employee from **more than one employer**, the IRS instructions provide a worksheet for lines 4a and 4b allocation; the backend aggregates and applies the combined cap once — mathematically equivalent.
- Qualified tips received in a trade or business on line 5 cannot exceed that business's **net income limitation** (`taxpayerTradeTipsNetIncomeCap`).
- If both spouses on an MFJ return have qualified tips, the **$25,000** cap is **combined**, not per spouse.

## 5.4 Computation skeleton (per spec §5)

```text
rawTipsTp     = W-2box7 + Form4137.line4 + manual + (1099-NEC ∩ cap)
rawTipsSp     = same (MFJ only)
totalRaw      = rawTipsTp + rawTipsSp                  (combined cap)
line6  = totalRaw
line7  = min(line6, 25000)                             (SCHEDULE_1A_TIPS_CAP)
line8  = Schedule1A.line3
line9  = 150000 if not MFJ else 300000                 (SCHEDULE_1A_TIPS_PHASEOUT_*)
line10 = line8 − line9
line11 = floor(line10 / 1000)                          (ROUND DOWN)
line12 = line11 * 100                                  (SCHEDULE_1A_TIPS_OVERTIME_STEP_AMOUNT)
line13 = max(0, line7 − line12)
```

Reference data: `SCHEDULE_1A_TIPS_CAP` = $25,000; `SCHEDULE_1A_TIPS_PHASEOUT_SINGLE` = $150,000; `SCHEDULE_1A_TIPS_PHASEOUT_MFJ` = $300,000; `SCHEDULE_1A_TIPS_OVERTIME_STEP_AMOUNT` = $100/$1k.

# 6) Schedule 1-A Part III: No Tax on Overtime (line 21)

## 6.1 Eligibility

Fill out Part III only if:
- the taxpayer and/or spouse received **qualified overtime compensation** in 2025;
- the recipient has a **valid SSN**;
- if married, the return is **MFJ**.

## 6.2 Inputs

Manual entry only — **no statement-import path** for overtime (2025 forms were generally not required to separately identify the qualifying portion):
- `taxpayerOvertimeFromW2Box1Amount` (manual W-2 box 1 overtime portion);
- `taxpayerOvertimeFromNonW2SourcesAmount` (manual 1099 / employer-statement overtime).

The IRS instructions allow worksheet-based or reasonable-method reconstruction when the forms do not separately identify the qualifying amount.

## 6.3 Important special rules

- If both spouses on an MFJ return have qualified overtime compensation, the **$25,000** cap is **combined**, not per spouse.
- Overtime uses the **same phaseout thresholds** as tips ($150k / $300k MFJ) — the shared constants `SCHEDULE_1A_TIPS_PHASEOUT_*` are reused.
- Overtime cap is **filing-status-specific**: $12,500 single / HOH / QSS / MFS, $25,000 MFJ.

## 6.4 Computation skeleton

```text
rawOTtp = taxpayerOvertimeFromW2Box1 + taxpayerOvertimeFromNonW2Sources
rawOTsp = same (MFJ only)
line14c = rawOTtp + rawOTsp
line15  = min(line14c, 12500 if not MFJ else 25000)    (SCHEDULE_1A_OVERTIME_CAP_*)
line16  = Schedule1A.line3
line17  = 150000 if not MFJ else 300000                (same constants as tips)
line18  = line16 − line17
line19  = floor(line18 / 1000)                         (ROUND DOWN)
line20  = line19 * 100
line21  = max(0, line15 − line20)
```

# 7) Schedule 1-A Part IV: No Tax on Car Loan Interest (line 30)

## 7.1 Eligibility and scope

Fill out Part IV only if qualified passenger vehicle loan interest (**QPVLI**) was paid or accrued in 2025. This deduction is available whether the taxpayer takes the standard deduction or itemizes.

Unlike Parts II, III, and V, **Part IV does NOT impose an MFJ-only rule** — it applies to any filing status (including MFS).

## 7.2 Key 2025 qualification rules

To be QPVLI, the interest generally must be on a loan that:
- was **originated after December 31, 2024**;
- was originated by the taxpayer;
- was used to purchase an **applicable passenger vehicle**;
- was for **personal use** rather than predominantly business or commercial use;
- is secured by a **first lien** on the purchased vehicle.

The vehicle generally must:
- be **new** to the taxpayer; used vehicles do not qualify;
- be a car, minivan, van, SUV, pickup truck, or motorcycle under the vehicle-weight limit;
- have undergone **final assembly in the United States**.

## 7.3 Additional rules

- A **VIN** must be reported on the return for each applicable passenger vehicle.
- Refinanced loans can still qualify, subject to the refinancing limitation rules. Backend proportionalizes: when `vehicleWasRefinanced=true` and `vehicleRefinancedLoanBalance > vehicleOriginalLoanPrincipal`, the deductible portion is `interest × (vehicleOriginalLoanPrincipal / vehicleRefinancedLoanBalance)`. Refinancing where principal ≥ refi balance is treated as if not refinanced.
- Interest on negative equity, liability insurance, trailers, and similar nonqualifying amounts is not deductible as QPVLI.
- Interest already deducted on **Schedule C, E, or F** cannot also be deducted on Schedule 1-A — backend subtracts those from the raw interest paid before applying the cap.

## 7.4 Computation skeleton

```text
line22 = sum across vehicles of vehicleLoanInterestPaidAmount × refinancing factor
        − interest already deducted on Schedule C / E / F
line23 = sum (line 22 column iii)
line24 = min(line23, 10000)                            (SCHEDULE_1A_CAR_LOAN_CAP)
line25 = Schedule1A.line3
line26 = 100000 if not MFJ else 200000                 (SCHEDULE_1A_CAR_LOAN_PHASEOUT_*)
line27 = line25 − line26
line28 = ceil(line27 / 1000)                           (★ ROUND UP — CEILING)
line29 = line28 * 200                                  (SCHEDULE_1A_CAR_LOAN_STEP_AMOUNT)
line30 = max(0, line24 − line29)
```

The **round-UP ceiling** in line 28 is the distinguishing arithmetic feature of Part IV — tips and overtime use round-DOWN floor; car-loan interest uses round-UP ceiling.

# 8) Schedule 1-A Part V: Enhanced deduction for seniors (line 37)

## 8.1 Eligibility

Fill out Part V only if:
- the taxpayer and/or spouse was born **before January 2, 1961**;
- the person claiming the deduction has a **valid SSN**;
- if married, the return is **MFJ**.

A person is treated as reaching age 65 on the **day before** the 65th birthday (IRC §63(f)(1)(A)). Backend helper `isBornBeforeJan2_1961()` reads the explicit eligibility flag and falls back to the date-of-birth string if needed (per 12d audit precedent).

Special 2025 death rule: if a taxpayer dies in 2025 before reaching age 65 under the day-before-birthday rule, the taxpayer does **not** qualify.

## 8.2 Maximum amount and phaseout

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---:|
| Maximum per eligible person | $6,000 |
| MFJ maximum if both qualify | $12,000 |
| Phaseout threshold — Single / HOH / QSS | $75,000 |
| Phaseout threshold — MFJ | $150,000 |
| Phaseout rate (continuous) | 6% of MAGI above threshold |

The Part V phaseout is **continuous** (not bucketed) — distinct from Parts II / III (round-down bucketed) and Part IV (round-up bucketed).

## 8.3 Computation skeleton

```text
line31 = Schedule1A.line3
line32 = 75000 if not MFJ else 150000                  (SCHEDULE_1A_SENIOR_PHASEOUT_*)
line33 = max(0, line31 − line32)
line34 = line33 × 0.06                                  (SCHEDULE_1A_SENIOR_PHASEOUT_RATE)
line35 = max(0, 6000 − line34)                         (SCHEDULE_1A_SENIOR_BASE_PER_PERSON)
line36a = line35 if taxpayer qualifies else 0
line36b = line35 if MFJ spouse qualifies else 0
line37  = line36a + line36b
```

## 8.4 AMT interaction

The enhanced senior deduction (Part V line 37) is **added back to AMTI** at Form 6251 line 1w per OBBBA design — it does NOT reduce the AMT base. Implementation at `computeLine17` ~line 8093. **Tips, overtime, and car-loan-interest deductions are NOT added back** (their statutory design differs from the senior deduction).

# 9) Correct compute order for lines 13a and 13b

This is the critical dependency chain for 2025 (per the line-15 audit §8.1 compute-order cheat sheet — NOT line 15 in the inner loop because that would be circular).

## 9.1 Compute through AGI / line 12e first

```text
Steps A–C: compute 1a–1z, 2b, 3b, 4b, 5b, 6b, 7a, 8 → 9 → 10 → 11a → 11b
Step D:    compute line12e  (standard or itemized)
```

## 9.2 Compute line 13b next

Because Schedule 1-A uses **line 11b** as its MAGI base, compute:

```text
Step E: Schedule1A.line38 → Form1040.line13b
```

## 9.3 Compute line 13a after line 13b (second pass)

Only after line 13b is final, compute Form 8995 / 8995-A using:

```text
taxable_income_before_qbi = Form1040.line11a − Form1040.line12e − Form1040.line13b
Step F: Form1040.line13a = Form8995.line15 or Form8995A.line39
```

Then:

```text
Step G: line14 = line12e + line13a + line13b
Step H: line15 = max(0, line11b − line14)
```

So the 2025 inner order is:

```text
11a/11b → 12e → 13b → 13a → 14 → 15 → 16
```

NOT 13a before 13b, and NOT using line 15 as the QBI taxable-income base (which would create circularity).

# 11) Validation rules

## 11.1 Line 13a / QBI validations

- If a QBI deduction is claimed, **Form 8995 or 8995-A must be attached**.
- Use **2025 thresholds**, not prior-year thresholds.
- Enforce the correct taxable-income-before-QBI formula using **line 13b**.
- `Form1040.line13a` cannot be negative (clamp-to-zero before 20% factor).
- If the taxpayer is a patron in a specified agricultural or horticultural cooperative, route to **Form 8995-A** — currently emits `LINE13A_COOPERATIVE_PATRON_UNSUPPORTED` because the cooperative path is not yet implemented.

## 11.2 Line 13b / Schedule 1-A validations

- If any Part II through V amount is nonzero, **Schedule 1-A must be attached**.
- Enforce **MFJ-only** for Parts **II, III, and V**.
- Enforce **valid SSN** rules where the schedule requires them.
- Tips and overtime use **round down** to the next whole thousand in the phaseout computation.
- Car-loan-interest uses **round up** (ceiling) to the next whole thousand in the phaseout computation.
- Car-loan-interest deduction cannot exceed **$10,000** and cannot duplicate interest deducted on Schedule C / E / F.
- Senior deduction cannot exceed **$6,000 per eligible person**.

# 12) Implementation guardrails

- Do **not** compute line **13a** before line **13b**.
- Do **not** use Form 1040 **line 15** or any circular approximation to determine taxable income before QBI.
- Do **not** treat line **13b** as a charitable, legacy, or catch-all deduction line.
- Do **not** apply an MFJ-only rule to the **car loan interest** deduction.
- Do **not** ignore the 2025 **valid SSN** rules for tips, overtime, and the senior deduction.
- Do **not** double-deduct vehicle-loan interest already deducted on **Schedule C, E, or F**.
- Do **not** include excluded tip income in QBI when the 2025 QBI instructions tell you to exclude it.
- Do **not** drop the MFS defensive guard at `computeSchedule1A` (~line 1132) or `computeLine13a` (~line 1275). Without the guard, stale spouse data leaks into MAGI / QBI on returns that switch from MFJ to MFS.
