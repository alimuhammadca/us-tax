# Form 1040 (2025) — Line 14

## Line identity

> **"Add lines 12e, 13a, and 13b."**

Source: Form 1040 (2025), page 2, Tax and Credits section. Confirmed against the official 2025 Form 1040 (OMB No. 1545-0074, created 5/25).

---

## What changed in 2025

Line 14 is structurally new relative to 2024:

| Change | 2024 | 2025 |
|---|---|---|
| Form instruction | "Add lines 12 and 13a" | "Add lines 12e, 13a, and 13b" |
| Line 12 structure | Single line (amount + adjacent checkboxes) | Split into 12a–12d (checkboxes) + 12e (amount) |
| Line 13b | Did not exist | New — Schedule 1-A additional deductions (tips, overtime, car loan interest, senior deduction) |
| Standard deduction — Single/MFS | $14,600 (2024) | **$15,750** (2025) |
| Standard deduction — MFJ/QSS | $29,200 (2024) | **$31,500** (2025) |
| Standard deduction — HOH | $21,900 (2024) | **$23,625** (2025) |

---

# 1) Formula

```text
line14 = line12e + (line13a ?? 0) + (line13b ?? 0)
```

- **No floor on line 14.** The floor at zero is applied on line 15, not here.
- `line13a` may be `null` when a blocking flag prevents QBI computation (e.g., self-employment path out of scope). Treat `null` as `0` for the line 14 sum.
- `line13b` is `0` (not null) when no Schedule 1-A deduction applies.
- `line12e` is **always positive** — the standard deduction floor ($15,750 / $23,625 / $31,500 depending on filing status) ensures a positive value even when income is zero.

---

# 2) Input mapping

## Line 12e — Standard or itemized deduction

Line 12e is the dollar-amount result of the 2025 line-12 sub-structure:

| Sub-line | Type | Content |
|---|---|---|
| 12a | Checkbox | Someone can claim taxpayer or spouse as a dependent |
| 12b | Checkbox | Spouse itemizes on a separate return / dual-status alien |
| 12c | Checkbox | You: born before January 2, 1961 / Are blind |
| 12d | Checkbox | Spouse: born before January 2, 1961 / Is blind |
| **12e** | **Amount** | **Standard deduction or itemized deductions (from Schedule A)** |

The 12a–12d checkboxes affect the standard deduction amount (age/blind add-ons) and eligibility (dependent worksheet, MFS itemize hard zero). The actual dollar value flows into line 14 from 12e only.

**2025 base standard deduction amounts**:

| Filing status | Amount |
|---|---:|
| Single / Married filing separately | $15,750 |
| Married filing jointly / Qualifying surviving spouse | $31,500 |
| Head of household | $23,625 |

Age/blind add-on per checkbox checked (2025): $1,600 (MFJ/QSS/MFS) or $2,000 (Single/HOH).

Itemizers: 12e = Schedule A total (line 17). The standard deduction amounts are not used when itemizing.

## Line 13a — QBI deduction

Source: Form 8995 line 15 (below threshold) or Form 8995-A line 39 (above threshold).

| Threshold | Value |
|---|---|
| Below threshold: Form 8995 | Taxable income before QBI ≤ $197,300 (non-MFJ) / $394,600 (MFJ) |
| Above threshold: Form 8995-A | Taxable income before QBI > threshold |

Critical compute-order constraint: `taxable_income_before_qbi = line11a − line12e − line13b` — so line 13b must finalise **before** line 13a's authoritative pass. Backend invokes `computeLine13a()` twice (preliminary inside `computeLine12` with `line13b = null`; authoritative in `prepare()` after `computeSchedule1A`).

## Line 13b — Additional deductions (NEW in 2025)

Source: Schedule 1-A line 38 = `line13 + line21 + line30 + line37`.

| Schedule 1-A part | Deduction | Cap | Phaseout |
|---|---|---|---|
| Part II (line 13) | Qualified tips | $25,000 combined | $150k / $300k MFJ; $100 per $1k ROUND DOWN |
| Part III (line 21) | Qualified overtime | $12,500 / $25,000 MFJ | Same thresholds as tips; ROUND DOWN |
| Part IV (line 30) | Car loan interest (new 2025 vehicle) | $10,000 | $100k / $200k MFJ; $200 per $1k ROUND UP CEILING; NO MFJ-only |
| Part V (line 37) | Enhanced senior deduction | $6,000 / person | 6% of MAGI above $75k / $150k MFJ; CONTINUOUS |

# 3) Compute order

```text
Step A: computeLine11ab()      → produces line11a (AGI) + line11b carryforward
Step B: computeLine12()        → produces line12e (standard or itemized deduction)
                                 + PRELIMINARY line13a (Pass 1; line13b=null)
                                 + PRELIMINARY line14 (Site A; 2-operand)
                                 + PRELIMINARY line15 (Site α)
Step C: computeSchedule1A()    → produces line13b (Schedule 1-A line 38)
                                 + INTERIM line14 (Site B; 3-operand with preliminary line13a)
                                 + INTERIM line15 (Site β)
Step D: computeLine13a()       → AUTHORITATIVE line13a (Pass 2; uses line13b)
                                 + AUTHORITATIVE line14 (Site C; 3-operand)
                                 + AUTHORITATIVE line15 (Site γ)
                                 OR Site D (else branch) when QBI is blocked
                                 + Site δ for line 15 in the else branch
Step E: computeLine16()        → uses line15 for tax computation
```

**Critical ordering constraint:** `computeSchedule1A()` (line 13b) must run before the authoritative `computeLine13a()` pass because the QBI deduction's taxable-income limitation base is:

```text
taxableIncomeBeforeQbi = line11a − line12e − line13b
```

If line 13b is not subtracted, the QBI deduction is overstated for taxpayers with large Schedule 1-A deductions.

# 4) Wiring sites (4 sites total — line 14 has NO separate orchestrator)

Line 14 is **inline-computed at 4 wiring sites** during `prepare()` — there is no `computeLine14` orchestrator. Each site overwrites the previous via `deductions.setTotalDeductions(...)`.

| Site | Location (TaxReturnComputeService.java) | Computation | State after |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Site A** — First-pass (`computeLine12`) | ~line 5702 | `line14 = roundMoney(addNonNull(line12e, line13a_preliminary))` | PRELIMINARY (2-operand; line13b not yet known) |
| **Site B** — Schedule 1-A wiring (`prepare()`) | ~line 1183 | read currentTotal → `setTotalDeductions(addNonNull(currentTotal, line13b))` | INTERIM AUTHORITATIVE (3-operand with preliminary line13a) |
| **Site C** — Second-pass SUCCESS (`prepare()`) | ~line 1426 | `newLine14 = roundMoney(addNonNull(addNonNull(line12e, newLine13a), schedule1A.getLine38Total()))` | FINAL AUTHORITATIVE (3-operand with corrected line13a) |
| **Site D** — Second-pass ELSE degenerate (`prepare()`) | ~line 1481 | `newLine14 = roundMoney(addNonNull(line12e, schedule1A.getLine38Total()))` | FINAL when second-pass blocked (2-operand; line13a was nulled at the same block) |

Site D fires in two failure modes:
- **Mode 1**: no QBI workflow (taxpayer doesn't have QBI inputs at all);
- **Mode 2**: blocking flag from QBI validation (e.g., self-employment out-of-scope) clears line 13a to null.

Both modes are functionally correct: line 14 = line 12e + line 13b, with line 13a treated as zero.

The 4-site progressive build-up is the natural consequence of the historical evolution from 2024 (2-operand `line12 + line13a`) to 2025 (3-operand `line12e + line13a + line13b`). A single-orchestrator refactor is a candidate but deferred (risk outweighs benefit — see 14 #9 observation).

# 6) Validations

- `line14 >= 0` always (each component is non-negative; no floor needed here).
- `line12e > 0` always (standard deduction floor ensures a positive value even when income is zero).
- `line13a >= 0` always (negative QBI becomes a carryforward, not a negative deduction).
- `line13b >= 0` always (Schedule 1-A parts are individually floored at zero).
- If `line13a` is `null` (blocked by flag), line 14 is still computed using `0` for that component; the return remains partially valid.

Line 14 itself emits **no blocking flags** — it is purely additive. Upstream flags from the 12abcde cluster, line 13a, and Schedule 1-A determine which components are null vs computed, but line 14 always computes (null components substitute as 0).

---

# 7) MFS protection (inherited transitively)

Line 14 has **no MFS orchestrator guard** of its own — it is inline-computed at 4 sites and inherits MFS protection transitively from its three operands:

- **line 12e** via the 12abcde cluster — `buildStandardDeductionIndicators` (15th orchestrator in the cascade) + 12b/c/d/e structural inheritance;
- **line 13a** via `computeLine13a` call site (16th orchestrator) — null-shadows `qbiDeductionSpouse` on MFS;
- **line 13b** via `computeSchedule1A` call site (17th orchestrator) — null-shadows `additionalDeductionsSpouse` and `form2555Spouse` on MFS.

All three operands are MFS-correct before they sum into line 14; the sum itself cannot introduce new MFS leakage.

---

# 8) Out of scope

- The computation of each component (line 12e, 13a, 13b) is governed by its own spec.
- Schedule A (itemized deductions) detail is governed by the line 12 spec.
- Self-employment QBI (Schedule C / Schedule F) is out of scope for line 13a — a blocking flag is emitted and line 13a is `null`.
