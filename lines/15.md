Three structural facts that drive the line 15 implementation:
- **line 15 is not just AGI minus the standard deduction** — it is **line 11b minus line 14**, and line 14 already includes **12e + 13a + 13b**;
- **line 15 is explicitly floored at zero** on the 2025 form (different from lines 11a and 11b, which do NOT have a zero-floor);
- **line 15 is the core branch input for line 16 tax computation**, including the Tax Table, Tax Computation Worksheet, Qualified Dividends and Capital Gain Tax Worksheet, Schedule D Tax Worksheet, Form 8615, Schedule J (out of scope), and the Foreign Earned Income Tax Worksheet. The zero case at line 15 also acts as a branch gate (the FEITW must NOT be completed when line 15 = 0).

# 1) Line identity (2025 Form 1040)

- **Line 15**: `Subtract line 14 from line 11b. If zero or less, enter -0-. This is your taxable income.`

2025 structural facts:
- **line 11b** is AGI carried forward from line 11a;
- **line 14** is total deductions and equals **12e + 13a + 13b**;
- **line 15** is taxable income after those deductions, never less than zero.

---

# 2) Exact 2025 computation

Form text:

```text
Subtract line 14 from line 11b. If zero or less, enter -0-. This is your taxable income.
```

Exact mapping:

```text
rawTaxableIncome = Form1040.line11b − Form1040.line14
Form1040.line15  = max(0, rawTaxableIncome)
```

Equivalent expanded form (informational only):

```text
Form1040.line15 = max(0, Form1040.line11b − (Form1040.line12e + Form1040.line13a + Form1040.line13b))
```

The expanded form is useful for reasoning, but the return treats **line 14** as line 15's direct feeder.

# 3) Inputs

## 3.1 Direct inputs

```text
Form1040.line15 dependsOn [Form1040.line11b, Form1040.line14]
```

## 3.2 Upstream line 14 components

```text
Form1040.line14 = Form1040.line12e + Form1040.line13a + Form1040.line13b
```

So line 15 indirectly depends on:
- **12e**: standard deduction or itemized deductions;
- **13a**: qualified business income deduction from Form 8995 or Form 8995-A;
- **13b**: additional deductions from Schedule 1-A line 38.

## 3.3 Inputs that do not belong directly on line 15

Do **not** feed line 15 directly from:
- Schedule A by itself;
- Form 8995 or 8995-A by itself;
- Schedule 1-A by itself;
- AGI from line 11a without first carrying it to line 11b;
- line 12e alone.

Those items must flow through the actual Form 1040 line structure first.

## 3.4 Negative AGI passes through

If `line11b < 0` (negative AGI is allowed at line 11b — there is no floor at line 11a or 11b), the subtraction still runs and the result is negative; the zero floor at line 15 then publishes 0. Implementation: `subtractNonNegative(line11b, line14)` short-circuits to 0 when the result is < 0.

---

# 4) Floor rule

The 2025 IRS form has an explicit zero floor:

```text
if Form1040.line11b − Form1040.line14 <= 0
    Form1040.line15 = 0
```

This is different from lines **11a** and **11b**, which do **not** have a zero-floor rule.

Implementation consequence:
- store the raw subtraction if useful for diagnostics;
- publish **0** on Form 1040 line 15 whenever the subtraction is zero or negative.

All five line-15 wiring sites use the helper `subtractNonNegative(line11b, line14)` which returns 0 when the result is negative. The zero-floor is therefore **structurally enforced**, not validated at runtime.

# 6) Why line 15 matters in 2025

Line 15 is one of the most important computational lines on the return.

## 6.1 Main downstream role

- **line 15** is the starting taxable-income amount used to figure **line 16 (Tax)**.

## 6.2 Common 2025 line 16 computation paths that use line 15

Depending on facts, line 16 may be computed from line 15 using:
- the **Tax Table**;
- the **Tax Computation Worksheet**;
- the **Qualified Dividends and Capital Gain Tax Worksheet**;
- the **Schedule D Tax Worksheet**;
- **Form 8615** tax computation;
- **Schedule J** (income averaging for farmers and fishermen — **out of scope** for this project);
- the **Foreign Earned Income Tax Worksheet**.

Implementation consequence: line 15 must be finalized before any line 16 branch is chosen or calculated.

## 6.3 Special zero case — FEITW gate

The 2025 Foreign Earned Income Tax Worksheet specifically says: **if Form 1040 line 15 is zero, do NOT complete that worksheet.** That means line 15 is not only a numeric input; it also controls branch selection at line 16. The `line15 == 0` check at the top of `computeLine16` (Branch 1) covers both the spec §2.3 zero rule and the spec §6.3 FEITW gate with one comparison.

# 7) Wiring sites (5 sites total — line 15 has NO separate orchestrator)

Line 15 is **inline-computed at 5 wiring sites** during `prepare()` — there is no `computeLine15` orchestrator. Sites α / β / γ / δ pair with line 14 Sites A / B / C / D; Site ε is unique to line 15 (and retroactively also a hidden 5th line-14 site).

| Site | Location (TaxReturnComputeService.java) | Computation | Pairs with line 14 |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Site α** — First-pass (`computeLine12`) | ~line 5706 | `subtractNonNegative(agi, line14_preliminary)` | Line 14 Site A |
| **Site β** — Schedule 1-A wiring (`prepare()`) | ~line 1228 | `subtractNonNegative(agi, line14_interim)` | Line 14 Site B |
| **Site γ** — Second-pass SUCCESS (`prepare()`) | ~line 1431 | `subtractNonNegative(agiFinal, newLine14)` | Line 14 Site C |
| **Site δ** — Second-pass ELSE degenerate (`prepare()`) | ~line 1517 | `subtractNonNegative(agiFinal, newLine14)` | Line 14 Site D |
| **Site ε** — Form 8396 Schedule A reduction (recompute) | ~line 23654 | `subtractNonNegative(agi, recomputed_line14)` | (unique — no direct line 14 audit pair) |

Site ε is a **rare recompute** that fires only when Form 8396 retroactively reduces the Schedule A mortgage interest deduction (mortgage credit certificate). The reduction propagates: smaller itemized deduction → smaller line 14 → larger line 15. This is the only case where line 15 changes after `computeLine16` would otherwise run; Form 8396 lives upstream of the final tax-credit pass and the recompute keeps line 15 consistent.

**Misleading local variable name at Site β**: the assignment `BigDecimal line14Final = roundMoney(subtractNonNegative(agi13b, ...))` at ~line 1228 actually holds the LINE 15 VALUE, not line 14. The setters at the next two lines (`setTaxableIncome` + `setLine15TaxableIncome`) correctly persist the value, so the bug is purely cosmetic. A clearer name would be `line15Interim`. Refactor deferred per anti-fragmentation policy (15 #6 + 15 #9).

# 8) Validation rules

## 8.1 Core numeric validation

```text
if Form1040.line11b is missing -> error MISSING_LINE11B
if Form1040.line14 is missing -> error MISSING_LINE14
```

Line 15 itself emits NO blocking flags. Spec §7 invariants (LINE14_MISMATCH, LINE15_ZERO_FLOOR_REQUIRED, LINE15_CANNOT_BE_NEGATIVE) are **structurally enforced** by `subtractNonNegative` and by line 14's 4-site build-up — they would only fire if there were a bug elsewhere.

## 8.2 Floor validation (structural)

```text
if Form1040.line11b − Form1040.line14 <= 0
    Form1040.line15 = 0   (enforced by subtractNonNegative)
```

## 8.3 Nonnegative public-output rule

```text
Form1040.line15 >= 0    (enforced by subtractNonNegative; cannot be negative)
```

## 8.4 Null propagation

```text
Form1040.line15 = null  iff  Form1040.line11b == null
```

`subtractNonNegative` short-circuits to null at a null left operand. Line 14 (the subtrahend) is never null when line 15 is wired — it is always at least line 12e (which has the standard deduction floor).

# 9) Implementation guardrails

## 9.1 Correct compute order

```text
line11a → line11b → line12e → line13b → line13a → line14 → line15 → line16
```

Important note:
- **13b must be available before 13a** in many returns because the QBI deduction computation for line 13a uses taxable income before QBI, which depends on line 13b.
- Therefore line 15 must not be computed authoritatively until the full `11b / 12e / 13a / 13b / 14` chain is stable (Sites γ or δ).

## 9.2 Avoid these implementation mistakes

Do **not**:
- compute line 15 as `11a − 14` instead of `11b − 14`;
- compute line 15 as `11b − 12e` and forget **13a** or **13b**;
- allow a negative published value on line 15;
- skip line 14 and independently subtract stale component values from line 11b;
- branch into line 16 tax worksheets before line 15 is finalized.

# 10) MFS protection (inherited transitively)

Line 15 has **no MFS orchestrator guard** of its own — it is inline-computed at 5 sites and inherits MFS protection transitively from its 2 direct inputs:
- **line 11b** via `computeAdjustments` — itself inheriting from 14 income-territory orchestrators (the income lines 1c through 8 cluster) + `computeIncomeAdjustments` per the line 10 #1 cascade;
- **line 14** via line 12e (12abcde cluster — 15th orchestrator) + line 13a (16th) + line 13b (17th).

Both inputs are MFS-correct before they feed line 15; the subtraction + zero-floor cannot introduce new MFS leakage. Site ε at Form 8396 also operates on MFS-guarded Schedule A inputs.

# 11) Practical developer cheat sheet

```text
Form1040.line14 = Form1040.line12e + Form1040.line13a + Form1040.line13b
rawTaxableIncome = Form1040.line11b − Form1040.line14
Form1040.line15 = max(0, rawTaxableIncome)
```

Meaning:
- **11b** = AGI carryforward;
- **14** = total deductions for this section;
- **15** = taxable income after those deductions, floored at zero.

Key 2025 rules:
- **line 15 can never be negative on the filed form**;
- **line 15 drives line 16 tax computation method selection and amount**;
- **line 15 = 0 forbids completion of the FEITW**.

---

# 12) Bottom line

For tax year 2025:
- **line 15** equals **line 11b minus line 14**;
- **line 14** equals **12e + 13a + 13b**;
- if the result is zero or negative, **enter 0**;
- line 15 is the taxable-income line used to compute **line 16 tax**.
