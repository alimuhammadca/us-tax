# 1) Line identity (2025 Form 1040)

- **Line 17**: `Amount from Schedule 2, line 3`

Top-level mapping:

```text
Form1040.line17 = Schedule2.line3
Schedule2.line3 = Schedule2.line1z + Schedule2.line2
Schedule2.line2 = Form6251.line11
```

Important 2025 structural point: Schedule 2 Part I has both **line 1z additions to tax** and **line 2 AMT**.

# 2) Schedule 2 Part I scope

## 2.1 Official 2025 mapping

```text
Schedule2.line1z = sum(lines 1a through 1y)
Schedule2.line2  = Form6251.line11
Schedule2.line3  = Schedule2.line1z + Schedule2.line2
Form1040.line17  = Schedule2.line3
```

## 2.2 What can be in line 1z in 2025

- **1a** excess advance premium tax credit repayment from Form 8962 — implemented (wired to `schedule2.tax.excessAdvancePremiumTaxCreditRepayment` inside `computeLine17` at ~line 17331);
- **1b** repayment of new clean vehicle credit transferred to dealer — out of scope;
- **1c** repayment of previously owned clean vehicle credit transferred to dealer — out of scope;
- **1d** recapture of net EPE from Form 4255 — out of scope;
- **1e** excessive payments on gross EPE from Form 4255 — out of scope;
- **1f** 20% EP from Form 4255 — out of scope;
- **1y** other additions to tax — out of scope.

## 2.3 Current implementation: line 1z effective value

`Schedule2.line1z` is conceptually `Form 8962 PTC repayment + other 1a..1y items`. All 1b..1y items are deferred; therefore in practice:

```text
Schedule2.line1z (computed effective) = 0
Schedule2.line2 = Form6251.line11
Schedule2.line3 = 0 + Form6251.line11 = Form6251.line11
Form1040.line17 = Form6251.line11
```

**G-new-5 caveat**: `wireLine17ToOutputs` currently sets `TaxAndCredits.additionalTaxSchedule2 = Form6251.line11` (AMT only). When Form 8962 PTC repayment > 0 + AMT = 0, the PDF line 17 cell displays $0 even though Schedule 2 line 3 should equal the PTC repayment. The recommended fix (open) is `additionalTaxSchedule2 = line1z + line11` at the wire site (`wireLine17ToOutputs` line 17276).

# 3) When Form 6251 is required

Attach **Form 6251** to the return if any of the following 2025 statements are true:

1. **Form 6251 line 7 > line 10**.
2. The taxpayer claims any **general business credit**, and either **Form 3800 line 6** or **Form 3800 line 25** is more than zero.
3. The taxpayer claims the **qualified electric vehicle credit (Form 8834)**, the **personal-use part of the alternative fuel vehicle refueling property credit (Form 8911)**, or the **credit for prior year minimum tax (Form 8801)**.
4. The total of **Form 6251 lines 2c through 3 is negative** and **line 7 would be greater than line 10** if those negative amounts were ignored.

# 4) Form 6251 structure in 2025

The 2025 form changed because the **enhanced deduction for seniors** on **Schedule 1-A line 37** must be added back for AMT.

## 4.1 Line 1a (intermediate)

```text
Form6251.line1a = Form1040.line14 - Schedule1A.line37
```

Meaning: start with total deductions on Form 1040 line 14; remove the enhanced senior deduction (personal-exemption-style add-back for AMT). Wired at `computeLine17` ~line 17297.

## 4.2 Line 1b (AMTI starting point)

```text
Form6251.line1b = Form1040.line11b - Form6251.line1a
```

Negative permitted. The 2025 form says **subtract line 1a from Form 1040 line 11b**; the old "line 1 starts from line 15" framing is no longer correct.

Important conceptual note: this expression algebraically equals `AGI − (Total deductions − Senior add-back)`. So **AMTI = AGI + AMT preferences** — not "taxable income + preferences."

## 4.3 Line 4 AMTI

```text
Form6251.line4 = Form6251.line1b + lines2a_to_2t + line3
```

The 2025 form text: `Alternative minimum taxable income. Combine lines 1b through 3.` Line 1a is only an intermediate input; not directly added into line 4.

# 7) Tentative minimum tax computation

## 7.1 Line 7 method hierarchy (3-path priority)

Use the first matching path:

```text
if Form 2555 applies (taxpayer or non-MFS spouse):
    line7 = ForeignEarnedIncomeTaxWorksheetFor6251.result            // FEITW
elif Schedule D has qualified dividends/cap gains OR Schedule D not required + qualified div present:
    line7 = Form6251.line40                                          // PART_III (preferential rates)
else:
    line7 = direct 26%/28% computation on line 6                     // DIRECT_26_28
```

Selected path is recorded at `form6251.line7ComputationPath` (enum: `FEITW` / `PART_III` / `DIRECT_26_28`).

⚠️ **MFS Form 2555 leak fix**: `form2555Spouse` is null-shadowed at the `computeLine17` call site (line 1594-1620, 17 #1) so MFS returns do not wrongly route into FEITW because of stale spouse Form 2555 data.

## 7.2 Direct 26% / 28% computation for 2025

Non-MFS filers (breakpoint $239,100):

```text
if line6 <= 239100:
    line7 = line6 * 0.26
else:
    line7 = 239100 * 0.26 + (line6 - 239100) * 0.28
```

MFS filers (breakpoint $119,550):

```text
if line6 <= 119550:
    line7 = line6 * 0.26
else:
    line7 = 119550 * 0.26 + (line6 - 119550) * 0.28
```

Inline at `computeLine17` ~line 17481-17489. Equivalent direct-offset formulas: $4,782 non-MFS / $2,391 MFS.

## 7.4 PART III population (G-new-2 fix)

When `line7ComputationPath = PART_III`, `populateAmtPartIIIFields()` (~line 17617) fills 29 Form 6251 fields (lines 12–40) implementing IRC §1(h) 0%/15%/20% rates on qualified dividends and LTCG within the AMT base. Key fields:

- line 12 = AMT base (copy of line 6);
- line 13 = qualifiedDividends + netLtcg;
- **line 14 = Schedule D line 19 unrecaptured §1250 gain** — excluded from the preferential pool (G-new-4 fix; §1250 gets 25% rate at line 37);
- line 15 = max(0, line 13 − line 14);
- line 16 = min(line 12, line 15);
- line 17 = line 12 − line 16 (ordinary AMTI; taxed at 26%/28%);
- line 18 = AMT rate tax on line 17;
- lines 19–30 = 0% / 15% / 20% bracket arithmetic per IRC §1(h);
- line 31 = 15% pool × 0.15; line 34 = 20% pool × 0.20; line 37 = §1250 surcharge × 0.25;
- line 38 = total (line 18 + 31 + 34 + 37);
- line 39 = 26%/28% on line 12 alone;
- line 40 = min(line 38, line 39) — Part III invariant: NEVER raises tax above DIRECT_26_28.
