# Form 1040 — Line 1a

**UI label:** *Total amount from Form(s) W-2, box 1*
**Semantic 1040 field:** `line1a_wages_w2_box1` in `C:\us-tax\pdfs\f1040_field_mapping_semantic.csv`
**Output field path:** `form1040.income.wages`
**Category:** earned income → wages reported on Form W-2
**Tax year:** 2025

---

## 1) Authoritative IRS definition

The 2025 Instructions for Form 1040/1040-SR define line 1a as:

> Enter the total amount from Form(s) W-2, box 1. If a joint return, also include your spouse's income from Form(s) W-2, box 1. ([IRS][1])

That is the controlling rule for the line.

**Developer interpretation:**

* Line 1a is the return-level total of all **eligible** Form W-2 box 1 amounts.
* On MFJ returns, aggregate taxpayer + spouse W-2 box 1 amounts. ([IRS][1])
* The W-2 statement entries are aggregated WITHOUT SSN filtering — `computeLine1aWages()` iterates every W-2 entry stored on the return regardless of employeeSSN.
* This is the wage anchor for the line 1 wage group; it feeds line 1z after the other line 1 sub-lines are computed.

---

## 2) What Form W-2 box 1 means

The IRS W-2 instructions define box 1 as **wages, tips, and other compensation**. The W-2 instructions also say:

* report all tips in box 1 along with wages and other compensation; and
* **allocated tips in box 8 are not included in box 1**. ([IRS][2])

**Implementation implication:**

* If an amount is in **W-2 box 1**, it is prima facie a line 1a candidate.
* Do **not** add W-2 box 8 allocated tips to line 1a; those belong on line 1c if applicable. ([IRS][1], [2])

---

## 3) Plain-English formula (current implementation)

```text
Line 1a =
    Σ (W-2 Box 1) across every eligible (non-statutory) W-2
  − Σ W-2 Box 11 nonqualifiedPlansAmount    (re-routed to Schedule 1 line 8t)
  − Σ inmateWagesAmount from employment-income personal forms (re-routed to Schedule 1 line 8u)
  + form4852-taxpayer.line7a (when needsForm4852 = true AND substituteFormType = "w2")
  + form4852-spouse.line7a   (same conditions, EXCLUDED on MFS)
```

Whole-dollar rounding is applied at PDF render. Statutory-employee W-2s are entirely excluded and emit `STATUTORY_EMPLOYEE_W2_OUT_OF_SCOPE`.

## 4) Step-by-step computation

(See `XLS/Computations/1a.xlsx` "Main Computation" sheet for the canonical map.)

| Step | Operation | Formula | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Check Statutory-employee flag | `if statutoryEmployee == true → exclude entry, emit STATUTORY_EMPLOYEE_W2_OUT_OF_SCOPE` | Schedule C is out of scope |
| 2 | Read Box 1 from each non-statutory W-2 | `box1 = w2.wagesTipsOtherCompAmount` | Null → treat as $0 |
| 3 | Subtract Box 11 NQDC if non-zero | `box1_adjusted = box1 − w2.nonqualifiedPlansAmount` | Box 11 is already INCLUDED in Box 1 per IRS W-2 instructions |
| 4 | Sum across non-statutory W-2s | `wages_sum = Σ box1_adjusted` | No SSN filtering — both taxpayer and spouse W-2s aggregate |
| 5 | Subtract inmate wages | `wages_after_inmate = wages_sum − (employmentTaxpayer.inmateWagesAmount + employmentSpouse.inmateWagesAmount)` | Re-routed to Schedule 1 line 8u |
| 6 | Add Form 4852 (taxpayer) | `if needsForm4852 = true AND substituteFormType = "w2" → add line7aWagesTipsOtherCompensation` | Substitute W-2 path |
| 7 | Add Form 4852 (spouse) — MFJ only | spouse Form 4852 added EXCEPT on MFS | MFS guard |
| 8 | Persist | `form1040.income.wages = wages_after_inmate + form4852_total` | BigDecimal (cents); rounded at render |

## 5) Explicit IRS carve-outs

The 2025 1040 instructions contain three direct line 1a carve-outs:

### 5.1 Inmate wages

If wages were earned while the taxpayer was an inmate in a penal institution:

* do **not** report them on line 1a;
* report them on **Schedule 1, line 8u** instead. ([IRS][1])

**Implementation:** subtracted in `computeLine1aWages()` (passed via `employmentFormInmateWages` parameter). Sourced from `employment-income-{taxpayer,spouse}.inmateWagesAmount` (derived via the `inmateWageW2Ids` W-2 picker that sums Box 1 of the selected W-2 entries).

### 5.2 Nonqualified deferred compensation / nongovernmental section 457 plan amounts

If the taxpayer received a pension or annuity from a nonqualified deferred compensation plan or a nongovernmental section 457 plan and the amount was reported in W-2 box 1:

* do **not** include it on line 1a;
* report it on **Schedule 1, line 8t**. ([IRS][1])

**Implementation:** subtracted via `w2.nonqualifiedPlansAmount` (Box 11). Separately summed by `sumW2Box11Nqdc()` for Schedule 1 line 8t.

### 5.3 Statutory employee W-2s

Schedule C instructions state that if the Form W-2 has the **"Statutory employee"** box checked in box 13:

* the entire W-2 is excluded from line 1a;
* `STATUTORY_EMPLOYEE_W2_OUT_OF_SCOPE` blocking flag is emitted (one per such W-2);
* Schedule C is out of scope. ([IRS][3])

## 6) What does NOT belong on line 1a (re-routed to adjacent line-1 sub-lines)

Even if a user thinks of these amounts as "wages," they are not line 1a if the IRS gives them a different home:

* household employee wages not reported on Form W-2 → **line 1b**
* tip income not reported on line 1a (allocated tips, noncash tips) → **line 1c**
* taxable Medicaid waiver payments not reported on Form(s) W-2, box 1 → **line 1d**
* taxable dependent care benefits from Form 2441, line 26 → **line 1e**
* employer-provided adoption benefits from Form 8839, line 31 → **line 1f**
* wages from Form 8919, line 6 → **line 1g**
* other earned income items assigned by the instructions to line 1h or Schedule 1 → **not line 1a**
* nontaxable combat pay election → **line 1i**

## 7) Missing or incorrect Form W-2 workflow

### If the taxpayer does not receive a W-2

* If no W-2 arrives by the end of January, contact the employer first. ([IRS][4])
* If still missing by end of February, contact the IRS. ([IRS][4])
* If the taxpayer still doesn't have the W-2 in time to file, use **Form 4852** to estimate wages and withholding. ([IRS][4], [5])

### If the W-2 is incorrect

1. **Corrected W-2 (Form W-2C) arrives BEFORE filing.** Enter the corrected Box 1 / withholding values directly in the existing W-2 statement entry. Line 1a aggregates whatever Box 1 contains; no `w-2c` statement type is needed.
2. **W-2 is wrong but no W-2C has been issued yet.** Ask the employer. If not corrected by end of February, contact IRS and use Form 4852 if needed.
3. **Corrected W-2C arrives AFTER filing.** Form 1040-X amended-return flow is out of scope.

**Implementation status:**

* Pre-filing W-2C handled by the existing W-2 statement form.
* Form 4852 intake UI (`form4852-taxpayer` / `form4852-spouse`) accepts substitute W-2 data. `extractForm4852Line1aWages()` wires `line7aWagesTipsOtherCompensation` into line 1a. The `MISSING_W2_EMPLOYMENT_INCOME_*` blocking flag is suppressed when Form 4852 is present.
* Form 1040-X amended-return flow is broadly out of scope.

## 8) Data model — implementation field names

The W-2 statement entries are stored in Firestore under `users/{uid}/w-2/{entryId}`.

### `computeLine1aWages()` — fields read

| IRS concept | Firestore field | Required? | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Box 1 wages, tips, other comp | `wagesTipsOtherCompAmount` | Yes | Primary input; absent → $0 |
| Box 11 NQDC / nongovernmental 457 | `nonqualifiedPlansAmount` | No | When non-zero, subtracted; re-routed to Schedule 1 line 8t |
| Statutory employee flag | `statutoryEmployee` | No | `true` → entire W-2 excluded; blocking flag emitted |
| Employee SSN | `employeeSSN` | No | **NOT used for filtering** — `computeLine1aWages()` includes all W-2s. SSN is used only by `hasW2ForSsn()` for the missing-W-2 validation. |

### Form 4852 supplement to line 1a

In addition to W-2 statement entries, `buildIncome()` calls `extractForm4852Line1aWages()` after `computeLine1aWages()` returns. This reads `line7aWagesTipsOtherCompensation` from the `form4852-taxpayer` and `form4852-spouse` personal forms when `needsForm4852 == true` and the substitute form type resolves to W-2. MFS guard applies (spouse Form 4852 excluded on MFS).

### Fields NOT read by `computeLine1aWages()`

| Field | Where used instead |
|---|---|
| `allocatedTipsAmount` (W-2 Box 8) | Tip computation (`computeTips()` / Form 4137 / line 1c) only |
| `socialSecurityWagesAmount` (Box 3) | Form 8919 line 8; SS worksheet; FICA |
| `medicareWagesAndTipsAmount` (Box 5) | Form 8959 Part I |

## 9) Validation flags emitted by the line 1a pass

| Flag code | Trigger | Blocking? | Suppressed when |
|---|---|---|---|
| `MISSING_W2_EMPLOYMENT_INCOME_TAXPAYER` | `employment-income-taxpayer.hasEmploymentIncome = true` AND `householdWork ≠ true` (must be explicitly false) AND no W-2 with `employeeSSN` matching taxpayer SSN | Yes | `form4852-taxpayer` with `needsForm4852 = true` AND `substituteFormType = w2` AND `line7aWagesTipsOtherCompensation` present |
| `MISSING_W2_EMPLOYMENT_INCOME_SPOUSE` | Same for spouse on returns with a spouse form present | Yes | `form4852-spouse` satisfying the same conditions |
| `STATUTORY_EMPLOYEE_W2_OUT_OF_SCOPE` | Any W-2 has `statutoryEmployee = true` | Yes — one per statutory W-2 | Never (no support for statutory-employee path) |

The `MISSING_W2` gate requires `householdWork == false` explicitly. The UI (since 2026-05-05) requires `householdWork` to be answered when `hasEmploymentIncome == true`. The backend null-tolerance is preserved as a defensive fallback for legacy data.

## 10) Three W-2 sum methods — pick the right one

The backend maintains three separate methods that sum W-2 `wagesTipsOtherCompAmount`. Pick the one whose carve-out behavior matches the IRS rule the caller is implementing.

| Method | Carve-outs applied | Emits flags? | Used by |
|---|---|---|---|
| `computeLine1aWages()` | Yes — excludes statutory employees, subtracts box 11 NQDC, subtracts inmate wages | Yes — `STATUTORY_EMPLOYEE_W2_OUT_OF_SCOPE` | Form 1040 line 1a only |
| `sumW2WagesAfterCarveOuts()` | Same carve-outs | No | Pub 915 SS Worksheet 1 line 3; Form 2441 earned income; EIC earned income (before SSN filter) |
| `sumW2Wages()` | None — raw sum | No | Cases needing raw box 1 (SSN-filtered helpers `sumW2WagesForSsn` / `sumW2WagesBySsn`) |

`sumW2WagesAfterCarveOuts` exists because three callers (Pub 915 SS Worksheet, Form 2441 earned income, EIC earned income) need the carve-outs honored but should not re-emit the statutory-employee flag.

## 11) Side-effect outputs (re-routed wages)

| Output field | Formula | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| `schedule1.additionalIncome.otherIncomeNonqualifiedDeferredComp` (line 8t) | Σ `w2.nonqualifiedPlansAmount` (non-statutory) + `otherIncomesTaxpayer.otherIncomeNonqualifiedDeferredComp8t` + `otherIncomesSpouse.otherIncomeNonqualifiedDeferredComp8t` | Null when total is zero |
| `schedule1.additionalIncome.otherIncomeWagesWhileIncarcerated` (line 8u) | `employmentTaxpayer.inmateWagesAmount` + `employmentSpouse.inmateWagesAmount` + `otherIncomesTaxpayer.otherIncomeWagesWhileIncarcerated8u` + `otherIncomesSpouse.otherIncomeWagesWhileIncarcerated8u` | Null when zero; MFJ-only for spouse contribution |

**Cross-line invariant:**

```text
line1a + line8t_w2_portion + line8u_w2_portion == Σ w2.box1 (non-statutory)
```

### Inmate-wages dual-source (Schedule 1 line 8u)

Inmate wages flow into Schedule 1 line 8u from two separate input paths by intent:

| Source | Field | Semantics |
|---|---|---|
| Employment Income form | `inmateWagesAmount` (derived from `inmateWageW2Ids` W-2 picker) | W-2 Box 1 wages from prison-issued W-2s — subtracted from line 1a (carve-out) and re-routed to line 8u |
| Other Incomes form | `otherIncomeWagesWhileIncarcerated8u` (manual amount) | Inmate wages NOT reported on any W-2 (commissary cash, payments below the W-2 reporting threshold) |

Defenses against double entry: strengthened help text, UI cross-form warning callout, and backend non-blocking `INMATE_WAGES_DUPLICATE_RISK_{TAXPAYER,SPOUSE}` flag.

## 15) Test scenarios that should exist

### Happy path

1. Single taxpayer, one W-2 → line 1a equals W-2 box 1.
2. MFJ, multiple W-2s → line 1a equals sum of all eligible box 1 amounts.

### Carve-outs

3. Inmate wages → rerouted to Schedule 1 line 8u, not line 1a.
4. NQDC / nongov 457 amount in Box 11 → rerouted to Schedule 1 line 8t.
5. Statutory employee W-2 → entire W-2 excluded; `STATUTORY_EMPLOYEE_W2_OUT_OF_SCOPE` blocking flag.

### Adjacent-line protection

6. W-2 Box 8 allocated tips not counted in line 1a (line 1c handles).
7. Household employee wages without W-2 not synthesized into line 1a (line 1b handles).

### Missing-document path

8. Form 4852 substitute wages flow into line 1a; `MISSING_W2` flag suppressed.
9. Form 4852 on MFS — spouse Form 4852 excluded by MFS guard.

### Null contract

10. `line1aIsNullWhenNoEmploymentIndicatedAndNoW2Entries` — null when employment not indicated.
11. `line1aIsZeroWhenEmploymentIndicatedButNoW2Wages` — BigDecimal.ZERO when employment indicated but no wages.
12. `computesLine1aClampedToZeroWhenCarveOutExceedsBox1` — ZERO when carve-outs exhaust Box 1.
