# Form 1040 — Line 1c

**UI label:** *Tip income not reported on line 1a (see instructions)*
**Semantic 1040 field:** `line1c_tip_income`
**Output field path:** `form1040.income.tipIncome`
**Output PDF field:** `topmostSubform[0].Page1[0].f1_49[0]`
**Category:** earned income → tips not already included in wages
**Tax year:** 2025

---

## 1) Authoritative IRS definition

The 2025 Instructions for Form 1040/1040-SR say line 1c is for:

* tip income not reported on line 1a; and
* allocated tips shown on Form W-2, box 8; and
* noncash tips.

That is the controlling rule for the line. ([IRS][1])

**Developer interpretation:**

* Line 1c is an income line, not a payroll-tax line.
* It holds tip income that is taxable but is not already included in the line 1a wage total.
* Payroll taxes related to unreported tips are handled separately through Form 4137 (line 13 → Schedule 2 line 5). ([IRS][2], [3])

---

## 2) Plain-English formula

```text
Line 1c = Σ (per-employer unreported-for-income tips) + Σ (per-employer noncash tips FMV),
          summed across taxpayer + spouse on MFJ (separate Form 4137 per spouse).
          "unreported-for-income" applies the ALLOCATED-TIPS / ADEQUATE-RECORDS rule per employer.
```

Important distinctions:

* Line 1c is INCOME. Form 4137 (→ Schedule 2 line 5) is the related SS+Medicare TAX. Different lines, different rules.
* Tips already in W-2 box 1 belong to LINE 1a, NOT line 1c.
* Sub-$20/month tips DO belong on line 1c (they are taxable income), but they are EXCLUDED from Form 4137 line 6 (the Medicare-tax base).
* Noncash tips ARE on line 1c, but Form 4137 only computes tax on cash/charge tips — no Medicare/SS owed on noncash via this path.

---

## 3) What belongs on line 1c

### 3.1 Cash and charge tips not reported to the employer

If the taxpayer received cash or charge tips and did not report them to the employer, those tips are included on line 1c because they are not already in W-2 box 1. ([IRS][2], [3])

Includes:

* tips received directly from customers;
* tips paid by check, debit card, or credit card;
* tips distributed by the employer from electronically paid tips; and
* tips received from other employees under a tip-sharing arrangement (only what the taxpayer keeps).

### 3.2 Allocated tips from Form W-2, box 8

Allocated tips shown in box 8 are not included in box 1. They generally belong on line 1c. ([IRS][2], [4])

Per IRS Pub 531, the taxpayer should include allocated tips **unless adequate records show actual unreported tips were less than the box 8 amount**. The compute models this with `hasAdequateRecordsUnreportedLessThanAllocated` and `substantiatedUnreportedTipsIfLessThanAllocated`.

### 3.3 Noncash tips

Noncash tips (tickets, passes, merchandise, other property) are taxable. Listed on line 1c by the 2025 Form 1040 instructions. They never enter the Form 4137 FICA computation. ([IRS][1], [5])

### 3.4 Tips under the $20-per-month employer reporting threshold

Tips < $20 in a calendar month from one employer do not have to be reported to the employer. They still belong in income → line 1c. They are EXCLUDED from Form 4137 line 6 (Medicare-tax base). ([IRS][2], [3])

---

## 4) What does NOT belong on line 1c

* Tips already in W-2 box 1 → belong on line 1a.
* Tips passed on to others in a tip-pool → not the taxpayer's income.
* Self-employment tip income → Schedule C (out of scope).
* RRTA-specific railroad retirement tax → Form 4137 does NOT compute RRTA tax (employer handles). RRTA comp still adds to Form 4137 line 8 SS wage base.

## 5) Step-by-step computation (per person)

(See `XLS/Computations/1c.xlsx` "Main Computation" sheet for the canonical map.)

| Step | Operation | Formula | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Load employer entries | `employers = tipsByEmployer[]` from personal form | If empty, AUTO-FILL from W-2 entries with `allocatedTipsW2Box8 > 0` and matching employee SSN |
| 2 | Per-employer: unreported cash/charge | `unreportedCashCharge = max(0, totalTipsReceived − totalTipsReportedToEmployer)` | Negative subtraction clamped to zero |
| 3 | Per-employer: apply allocated-tips override | See decision tree below | Per IRS Pub 531 + Form 4137 instructions |
| 4 | Per-employer: accumulate per-person sums | `totalUnreportedForIncome`, `totalNonCashTips`, `totalUnderTwentyTips`, `totalMedicareOnlyTips`, `totalRrtaComp`, `totalAllocatedTips` | Each feeds different Form 4137 lines |
| 5 | Compute line 1c for this person | `line1cPerson = totalUnreportedForIncome + totalNonCashTips` | addNonNull semantics |
| 6 | Build Form 4137 (when triggered) | See Form 4137 line-by-line below | Created when `line4 > 0` AND (`line6 > 0` OR `allocatedTips > 0`) |
| 7 | Aggregate across spouses | `line1c = taxpayer.line1c + spouse.line1c` (addNonNull) | MFS guard: spouse skipped when `isMfs = true` |
| 8 | Round | `line1c = roundMoney(line1c)` | HALF_UP |
| 9 | Persist | `income.setTipIncome(line1c)` only when non-null | Field omitted when null |

### Decision tree — `unreportedForIncome` per employer (Step 3)

| Branch | Condition | Result |
|---|---|---|
| No allocated tips | `allocatedTips == null` OR `== 0` | `unreportedForIncome = unreportedCashCharge` |
| Adequate-records reduction | `allocatedTips > 0` AND `hasAdequateRecords = true` AND `substantiated < allocatedTips` | `unreportedForIncome = substantiated` |
| Allocated tips override | `allocatedTips > 0` AND (no adequate records OR `substantiated >= allocated` OR `substantiated == null`) | `unreportedForIncome = max(unreportedCashCharge, allocatedTips)` |

## 6) Form 4137 line-by-line (per person)

| Form 4137 Line | Backend computation | Setter / output field |
|---|---|---|
| Line 2 — Total cash & charge tips received | `Σ totalTipsReceived` per employer | `setLine2TotalCashChargeTips` |
| Line 3 — Tips reported to employer | `Σ totalTipsReportedToEmployer` | `setLine3ReportedTips` |
| Line 4 — Total unreported tips for income | `Σ unreportedForIncome` (per allocated-tips override) | `setLine4UnreportedTips` |
| Line 5 — Tips < $20/month | `Σ line5Under20PerMonthTips` | `setLine5UnderTwentyTips` |
| Line 6 — Tips subject to Medicare | `max(0, Line 4 − Line 5)` | `setLine6UnreportedTipsSubjectMedicare` — also feeds Form 8959 |
| Line 7 — Max SS wages | $176,100 (2025) | `setLine7MaxSocialSecurityWages` |
| Line 8 — SS wages + tips + RRTA paid | `Σ W-2 (box 3 + box 7)` SSN-filtered + `Σ rRTAComp`; fallback to form input only when no matching W-2 | `setLine8SocialSecurityWagesTipsRrta` |
| Line 9 — Remaining SS wage base | `max(0, Line 7 − Line 8)` | `setLine9RemainingSocialSecurityWageBase` |
| Line 10 — Tips subject to SS | `min(Line 6 − medicareOnlyGovernmentTips, Line 9)` | `setLine10UnreportedTipsSubjectSocialSecurity` |
| Line 11 — SS tax | `Line 10 × 6.2%` (null when `MISSING_SS_W2_DATA` fires) | `setLine11SocialSecurityTax` |
| Line 12 — Medicare tax | `Line 6 × 1.45%` (always computed) | `setLine12MedicareTax` |
| Line 13 — Total tip tax | `Line 11 + Line 12` | `setLine13TotalTax` → Schedule 2 line 5 |

### SS wage-base sourcing — W-2 is canonical

For Form 4137 line 8 the backend reads via `firstNonNull(ssWagesTipsFromW2, ssWagesTipsFromInput)`:

| Source | When used |
|---|---|
| `sumW2SocialSecurityWagesAndTipsForSsn` (W-2 boxes 3 + 7, SSN-filtered) | **Primary** — used whenever non-null, INCLUDING `BigDecimal.ZERO` |
| Per-employer `socialSecurityWagesW2Box3` + `socialSecurityTipsW2Box7` on the tip-income form | **Fallback only** — used ONLY when no matching W-2 exists |

If a matching W-2 has explicit $0 in boxes 3 + 7, the form-input fallback is silently ignored (by design — typo'd W-2 should be corrected at source).

## 7) MFS guard (since 2026-05-06)

On Married Filing Separately, the backend must NOT aggregate the spouse's tip income or Form 4137 into the taxpayer's return.

**Implementation:** `computeTips(...)` accepts an `isMfs` parameter; when true, the spouse `computeTipsForPerson` call is skipped and a null `TipResult` is substituted. The single guard cascades to all four downstream effects automatically:

| Output | MFS behavior |
|---|---|
| `form1040.income.tipIncome` (line 1c) | Taxpayer-only |
| `taxAndCredits.form4137Spouse` | null (no spouse Form 4137 attached) |
| `schedule2.otherTaxes.unreportedTipIncomeTax` (line 5) | Taxpayer Form 4137 line 13 only |
| `form8959.partI` (Additional Medicare base) | Spouse Form 4137 line 6 contribution = null |

The UI also disables the spouse tip-income form on non-MFJ returns (defense in depth).

---

## 8) `hasUnreportedTips` defensive gate (since 2026-05-06)

The first question on the tip-income form is "Did you receive tip income that was not fully reported to your employer in 2025?" The backend treats this as a SOFT gate at the top of `computeTipsForPerson`:

| `hasUnreportedTips` | Backend behavior |
|---|---|
| `false` | Return null TipResult — skip computation entirely |
| `null` | Fall through. Preserves the W-2 box 8 auto-fill convenience |
| `true` | Fall through. Existing per-employer computation runs |

Soft (not hard) gate because the auto-fill convenience must still work for users with W-2 Box 8 allocated tips who have not opened the tip-income form.

---

## 9) Adequate-records claim — dual-layer enforcement (since 2026-05-06)

When the user claims adequate records show actual unreported tips are **less than** the W-2 box 8 allocated amount (`hasAdequateRecordsUnreportedLessThanAllocated == true`), they MUST also provide the substantiated amount.

| Layer | Mechanism |
|---|---|
| UI required field | Substantiated input is marked `*` and `[required]` when adequate-records is Yes. `isValid()` blocks save. |
| Backend non-blocking flag | `ADEQUATE_RECORDS_MISSING_SUBSTANTIATED_AMOUNT_{TAXPAYER,SPOUSE}` emitted when ANY employer has `hasAdequateRecords=true` AND `substantiated=null` AND `allocatedTips>0`. Compute proceeds with the allocated-tips fallback. |

The help text strengthens IRS contemporaneous-records guidance (Form 4070A / Pub 531). No backend storage of record descriptions — IRS doesn't require them on the return.

---

## 10) Employer EIN required on Form 4137 — dual-layer enforcement (since 2026-05-06)

The IRS requires the employer's EIN for each row in the Form 4137 employer table.

| Layer | Mechanism |
|---|---|
| UI required field | `rowNeedsEinForForm4137(employer)` returns true when `received > reported` OR `allocatedTipsW2Box8 > 0`; UI applies conditional `*` + `[required]` |
| Backend non-blocking flag | `MISSING_EMPLOYER_EIN_FORM_4137_{TAXPAYER,SPOUSE}` emitted when Form 4137 is built AND any row has missing/blank EIN |

| Output field | Formula | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| `form4137Taxpayer` (full Form 4137) | See Form 4137 line-by-line | Created only when `line4 > 0` AND (`line6 > 0` OR `totalAllocatedTips > 0`) |
| `form4137Spouse` | Same logic, spouse-side | MFJ only (skipped on MFS) |

**Cross-line invariant:**

```text
line 1c = Σ unreportedForIncome (per employer) + Σ noncashTipsFmv
line 1c minus Σ noncashTipsFmv == Form 4137 line 4 (when Form 4137 created)
```

W-2 box 12 codes A and B → Schedule 2 line 13 (NOT line 5). Separate from Form 4137 path.

## 14) Validation flags

| Flag code | Trigger | Effect | Suppressed when |
|---|---|---|---|
| `MISSING_SS_W2_DATA_TAXPAYER` | Form 4137 line 6 > 0 AND no W-2 SS data for taxpayer SSN AND form input fallback also null | Blocking — line 11 (SS tax) set to null; line 12 (Medicare) still computed | Provide W-2 (boxes 3+7) or form-level fallback fields |
| `MISSING_SS_W2_DATA_SPOUSE` | Same for spouse | Blocking | Same |
| `ADEQUATE_RECORDS_MISSING_SUBSTANTIATED_AMOUNT_{TAXPAYER,SPOUSE}` | `hasAdequateRecords=true` AND `substantiated=null` AND `allocatedTips>0` | Non-blocking advisory | Provide substantiated amount or set adequate-records=No |
| `MISSING_EMPLOYER_EIN_FORM_4137_{TAXPAYER,SPOUSE}` | Form 4137 created AND any employer row has blank EIN | Non-blocking advisory | Provide EIN |

Non-flag defensive behaviors (silent skip):

* Negative unreported clamped to zero.
* Allocated-tips override forces minimum (per Pub 531).
* Form 4137 NOT created when only noncash (no FICA on noncash).
* Form 4137 NOT created when all under-$20 (no Medicare base remaining).

## 17) Test scenarios that should exist

### Income placement

1. Timely-reported cash tips already in box 1 → not on line 1c.
2. Unreported cash tips → line 1c.
3. Unreported charge tips → line 1c.
4. Noncash tips → line 1c (not Form 4137).
5. Allocated tips → line 1c unless adequate-records reduction.

### Form 4137 triggers

6. $20+/month unreported → Form 4137 created.
7. Under-$20 only → line 1c yes, Form 4137 no.
8. Allocated tips → triggers Form 4137 even without unreported cash.
9. MFJ both spouses → separate Form 4137 per spouse.

### MFS / gates

10. MFS taxpayer with stale spouse data → spouse contribution dropped.
11. `hasUnreportedTips=false` → return null; auto-fill suppressed.
12. `hasUnreportedTips=null` with W-2 Box 8 → auto-fill works.

### Cross-form

13. Form 4137 line 13 → Schedule 2 line 5.
14. Form 4137 line 6 → Form 8959 Part I base.
15. W-2 box 12 codes A and B → Schedule 2 line 13 (not Form 4137).

## 18) Out of scope

* Self-employment tip income (Schedule C).
* RRTA tax computation on tips (employer handles).
* Form 1040-X amended-return flow.

---

## 19) Final condensed rule map

1. Include unreported cash and charge tips. ([IRS][2], [3])
2. Include allocated tips from W-2 box 8 unless adequate records support a lower includible amount. ([IRS][2], [3], [4])
3. Include the value of noncash tips. ([IRS][1], [2], [5])
4. Do not include tips already in W-2 box 1. ([IRS][2], [4])
5. Use Form 4137 to compute SS + Medicare tax on unreported tip income. ([IRS][3])
6. Form 4137 line 13 → Schedule 2 line 5. ([IRS][3])
7. W-2 box 12 codes A and B → Schedule 2 line 13. ([IRS][1], [2], [3])
8. MFJ spouses → separate Form 4137 per spouse.
9. MFS → spouse tip computation skipped entirely.
