# Form 1040 — Line 1d

**UI label:** *Medicaid waiver payments not reported on Form(s) W-2, box 1*
**Semantic 1040 field:** `line1d_medicaid_waiver_payments`
**Output field path:** `form1040.income.medicaidWaiverPayments`
**Output PDF field:** `topmostSubform[0].Page1[0].f1_50[0]`
**Category:** earned income → Medicaid waiver payments outside W-2 box 1
**Tax year:** 2025

---

## 1) Authoritative IRS definition

The 2025 Instructions for Form 1040/1040-SR say line 1d is for Medicaid waiver payments not reported on Form(s) W-2, box 1. ([IRS][1])

IRS Medicaid-waiver guidance explains that some of these payments are excludable from gross income under **Notice 2014-7**, but may still be included in earned income for limited credit purposes. ([IRS][2])

The statutory exclusion authority is **IRC § 131** (foster care payments). Notice 2014-7 extends the § 131 difficulty-of-care exclusion to qualified Medicaid waiver payments by treating them as difficulty-of-care payments when received by an individual care provider for care of an eligible individual living in the provider's home. ([IRS][2])

**Developer interpretation:**

* Line 1d is the input line for Medicaid waiver payments not already in W-2 box 1.
* Some line 1d amounts are taxable.
* Some line 1d amounts are nontaxable but are temporarily included so they can be counted as earned income for EIC/ACTC and then backed out on Schedule 1, line 8s. ([IRS][1], [2])

### 1.1 What's new for 2025 (2024 reporting change)

Starting with 2024 payments, the IRS requires payers to report nontaxable Medicaid waiver payments in **W-2 box 12, code II**. ([IRS][1])

Practical effect for line 1d:

* When nontaxable payments are correctly reported via code II, box 1 is zero — line 1d (or the earned-income inclusion path) still applies if the taxpayer elects.
* Prior-year W-2s, 1099-MISC, 1099-NEC, and no-form paths remain valid for non-compliant payers.
* Code II is now the standard indicator; the other reporting paths are secondary.

## 2) Plain-English formula

```text
Line 1d = (per-person) sum of taxable Medicaid waiver payments NOT in W-2 box 1,
          PLUS — only when the user makes the all-or-none earned-income election —
          the sum of nontaxable (Notice 2014-7 qualified) payments NOT in W-2 box 1.
          Aggregated across taxpayer + spouse on MFJ (spouse skipped on MFS).

Schedule 1 line 8s = −(qualifiedIncludedInW2 + qualifiedNotInW2)  per person, when election ON
                   (backs out the FULL nontaxable amount that was included on either line 1a or line 1d)
```

The earned-income election lets the user count nontaxable Medicaid waiver payments toward EIC / ACTC. The gross amount goes on line 1d AND simultaneously a NEGATIVE entry of equal size goes on Schedule 1 line 8s — **net zero tax effect**, but counts as earned income for credit purposes.

---

## 3) Core inclusion rules

Include an amount on line 1d only if all of the following are true:

1. The payment is a Medicaid waiver payment or other payment that qualifies for the same treatment under the IRS Medicaid waiver guidance.
2. The amount is not already included in Form W-2, box 1.
3. The amount is either:
   * taxable, or
   * nontaxable but included under the taxpayer's earned-income election. ([IRS][1], [2])

### 3.1 Earned-income election — all-or-none per spouse

The IRS says the taxpayer may choose to include **all, but not part**, of the eligible payments in earned income. On MFJ, each spouse may make a separate all-or-none choice. ([IRS][2])

---

## 4) What does NOT belong on line 1d

### 4.1 Amounts already in W-2 box 1

If the payment is already in box 1, it belongs in the line 1a wage flow, not line 1d. The election still controls whether Schedule 1 line 8s backs out the (now-line-1a) nontaxable portion.

### 4.2 Nontaxable payments with no election

If nontaxable, box 1 is blank/zero, and the taxpayer is NOT electing → the amount does not need to be reported on the return and those W-2s do not need to be attached. ([IRS][2])

### 4.3 Sole proprietor home-care business

If the taxpayer is a sole proprietor in a business of providing home-care services receiving excludable payments on 1099-MISC / 1099-NEC, the IRS routes reporting to Schedule C:

* include the full amount on Schedule C line 1;
* deduct the excludable amount in Part V, Other expenses, labeled `Notice 2014-7`. ([IRS][2])

**In this application:** Schedule C is out of scope. When `hasTradeOrBusinessProvidingHomeCare = true` AND any amount > 0, `MEDICAID_WAIVER_SCHEDULE_C_{TAXPAYER,SPOUSE}` blocking flag fires and line 1d is null.

---

## 5) Eligibility rules for nontaxable treatment

Notice 2014-7 applies to certain payments to an **individual care provider** for care of an **eligible individual** under a state Medicaid Home and Community-Based Services waiver program described in section 1915(c), where the care is provided in the provider's home and the eligible individual lives there under the plan of care. ([IRS][2])

Some non-1915(c) programs may qualify based on facts and circumstances. ([IRS][2])

Key examples:

* Provider with a separate home working in the recipient's home → exclusion does NOT apply.
* Shared home with no separate provider home → exclusion may apply.
* Vacation pay → not excludable.
* Related-party provider (adult child caring for parent) → still qualifies. ([IRS][2])

### 5.1 Per-individual cap (IRC § 131(c))

Medicaid waiver payments are treated as difficulty-of-care payments under IRC § 131. Section 131(c) limits the exclusion when the provider cares for more than:

* **10 qualified individuals under age 19**, or
* **5 qualified individuals age 19 or older**.

Amounts received for individuals above those thresholds are taxable.

**Implementation status (added 2026-05-31):** The §131(c) cap IS applied by `computeMedicaidForPerson`. The user enters two form-level counts (`qualifiedIndividualsUnder19Count`, `qualifiedIndividualsAge19OrOlderCount`); when either exceeds its IRS threshold the application applies a pro-rata reduction (`excludableFraction = effectiveCap / totalIndividuals`) and routes the over-cap portion of `qualifiedTotal` to Line 1d as ordinary taxable income (no Schedule 1 line 8s offset). Constants: `ReferenceData.IRC_131C_CAP_INDIVIDUALS_UNDER_19` (= 10) and `IRC_131C_CAP_INDIVIDUALS_19_OR_OLDER` (= 5). Backward-compatible: legacy returns without the count fields treat counts as 0 (no cap fires).

### 5.2 IRA contributions

Excluded Medicaid waiver payments may nonetheless be treated as compensation for IRA contributions, filling the gap to the annual IRA contribution limit when other compensation is below it. Does not affect line 1d computation.

## 6) Step-by-step computation (per person)

(See `XLS/Computations/1d.xlsx` "Main Computation" sheet for the canonical map.)

| Step | Operation | Formula | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Outer gate | `if receivedMedicaidWaiverPayments != true → return (null, null)` | Hard gate (no auto-fill path) |
| 2 | Schedule C path check | `if hasTradeOrBusinessProvidingHomeCare = true AND any amount > 0 → emit MEDICAID_WAIVER_SCHEDULE_C_{LABEL} (blocking) and return (null, null)` | Schedule C out of scope |
| 3 | Per-entry read | `qualifiedAmount`, `taxableAmount`, `codeIIAmount` (informational), `includedInW2`, `sourceType ∈ {W-2, 1099-MISC, 1099-NEC, Other}` | One row per payer × program |
| 4 | Code II reconciliation | `if codeIIAmount > 0 AND qualifiedAmount = null → emit MEDICAID_WAIVER_CODE_II_MISSING_QUALIFIED_{LABEL}` | Box 12 code II identifies nontaxable amount |
| 5 | W-2 box 1 lookup (sourceType = W-2 only) | Find matching W-2 by SSN + payerTIN/payerName. `if W-2 box 1 > 0 AND includedInW2 = null → emit MEDICAID_WAIVER_W2_BOX1_UNSPECIFIED_{LABEL}` (blocking); exclude that entry's qualifiedNotInW2. `if W-2 box 1 ≤ 0 AND includedInW2 = null → auto-set includedInW2 = 0` | Double-count guard |
| 6 | Overage check | `if includedInW2 > qualifiedAmount → emit MEDICAID_WAIVER_W2_OVERAGE_{LABEL}` | Data-entry sanity check |
| 7 | Split qualified into included vs not-included | `qualifiedNotIncluded = max(0, qualifiedAmount − includedInW2)` and accumulate `qualifiedTotal`, `qualifiedIncludedInW2`, `qualifiedNotInW2`, `taxableNonW2` | Per-person aggregation |
| 8 | Compute line 1d for this person | `line1d_person = taxableNonW2; if election = true: line1d_person = addNonNull(line1d_person, qualifiedNotInW2)` | Nontaxable only when elected |
| 9 | Compute line 8s offset | `if election != true: line8s_person = null; else: offset = qualifiedIncludedInW2 + qualifiedNotInW2; if offset > qualifiedTotal → emit MEDICAID_WAIVER_LINE8S_EXCEEDS_QUALIFIED_{LABEL}; line8s_person = -offset; if offset = null → emit MEDICAID_WAIVER_LINE8S_MISSING_{LABEL}` | Backs out the nontaxable amount |
| 10 | Aggregate across spouses | `Form1040.line1d = taxpayer.line1d + spouse.line1d (addNonNull); Schedule1.line8s = taxpayer.line8s + spouse.line8s` | MFS guard skips spouse |
| 11 | Round + persist | `income.setMedicaidWaiverPayments(line1d)` only when non-null; `schedule1.additionalIncome.setOtherIncomeMedicaidWaiverPayments(roundMoney(line8s))` only when non-null | HALF_UP |

### Decision tree — when does an entry contribute to line 1d?

| Branch | Election | Source | qualifiedAmount | taxableAmount | Contribution to line 1d | Contribution to line 8s |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Taxable + non-W-2 source | either | any | any | > 0 | `taxableAmount` | 0 |
| Nontaxable + election ON + non-W-2 | true | W-2/1099/Other | > 0 | any | `qualifiedAmount` (when not in W-2 box 1) | −`qualifiedAmount` |
| Nontaxable + election OFF + non-W-2 | false | any | > 0 | 0 | 0 | 0 |
| Nontaxable + election ON + W-2 overlap | true | W-2 | > 0 | any | `max(0, qualifiedAmount − includedInW2)` | −(`includedInW2 + qualifiedNotInW2`) |
| Sole prop home-care business | either | any | any | any | 0 (BLOCKED) | 0 (flag emitted) |
| `receivedMedicaidWaiverPayments != true` | either | n/a | n/a | n/a | 0 | 0 |

## 9) Side-effect outputs

### Schedule 1 line 8s

| Output | Formula | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| `schedule1.additionalIncome.otherIncomeMedicaidWaiverPayments` (line 8s) | Per person (election=true): `line8s_person = −(qualifiedIncludedInW2 + qualifiedNotInW2)`; aggregated across spouses (MFS guard skips spouse) | Negative — entered in preprinted parentheses. Schedule 1 lines 8z / 9 / 10 may also go negative. |

### The line 1a / line 1d / line 8s triangle

| Component | Source | Routes to |
|---|---|---|
| Taxable MW payments not in W-2 box 1 | `taxablePaymentsNotInW2Box1` | Line 1d only (PERMANENT — no offset) |
| Nontaxable not in W-2 box 1, election ON | `qualifiedNotIncluded` | Line 1d (positive) + line 8s (negative). Net zero on income, but counts as earned income |
| Nontaxable in W-2 box 1, election ON | `includedInW2` | Already in line 1a (via W-2). Line 8s backs it out. Net zero on income |
| Nontaxable in W-2 box 1, election OFF | `includedInW2` | Stays in line 1a (W-2 sum). NO line 8s offset. Permanent inclusion |
| Nontaxable not in W-2 box 1, election OFF | `qualifiedNotIncluded` | NOT included on line 1d. NO line 8s. Excluded with no earned-income consequence |

### Cross-entry duplication invariant (verified 2026-05-06)

The per-entry W2_OVERAGE flag (`includedInW2 > qualifiedAmount`) enforces a constraint that makes line 8s algebraically equivalent to `−qualifiedTotal`:

```text
line 8s = −(Σ includedInW2 + Σ max(0, qualifiedAmount − includedInW2))
        = −Σ qualifiedAmount   (when W2_OVERAGE constraint holds per entry)
        = −qualifiedTotal
```

**Consequence — AGI is fully protected:** if a user duplicates the same real-world payment across two entries, `qualifiedTotal` is inflated AND the line 8s offset is inflated by the same amount. **Net AGI impact is zero.**

**Residual risk — earned-income basis for EIC and ACTC:** when the election is ON, the duplicated `qualifiedNotInW2` also feeds line 1d → line 1z → earned income basis. Mitigation tracked as a deferred UI heuristic.

## 10) MFS guard (since 2026-05-06)

`computeMedicaidWaiverPayments(...)` accepts an `isMfs` parameter; when true, the spouse `computeMedicaidForPerson` call is skipped and a null-pair `MedicaidResult(null, null)` substituted. Single guard cascades to BOTH outputs:

| Output | MFS behavior |
|---|---|
| `form1040.income.medicaidWaiverPayments` (line 1d) | Taxpayer-only |
| `schedule1.additionalIncome.otherIncomeMedicaidWaiverPayments` (line 8s) | Taxpayer-only |

The UI also hides the spouse Medicaid waiver form on non-MFJ returns.

## 11) Validation flags

| Flag code | Trigger | Effect | Suppressed when |
|---|---|---|---|
| `MEDICAID_WAIVER_SCHEDULE_C_{TAXPAYER,SPOUSE}` | `hasTradeOrBusinessProvidingHomeCare = true` AND any amount > 0 | Blocking — line 1d returns `(null, null)`. Schedule C path required (out of scope) | Set the flag answer to false |
| `MEDICAID_WAIVER_W2_BOX1_UNSPECIFIED_{TAXPAYER,SPOUSE}` | `sourceType = W-2` AND matched W-2 has box 1 > 0 AND `qualifiedAmountIncludedInW2Box1 = null` | Blocking — entry's `qualifiedNotInW2` excluded | Provide the included amount explicitly |
| `MEDICAID_WAIVER_CODE_II_MISSING_QUALIFIED_{TAXPAYER,SPOUSE}` | `w2Box12CodeIIAmount > 0` AND `qualifiedNotice2014_7Amount = null` | Blocking | Enter qualified amount (typically equal to code II amount) |
| `MEDICAID_WAIVER_W2_OVERAGE_{TAXPAYER,SPOUSE}` | `qualifiedAmountIncludedInW2Box1 > qualifiedNotice2014_7Amount` | Blocking | Correct one of the two amounts |
| `MEDICAID_WAIVER_ELECTION_NO_QUALIFIED_{TAXPAYER,SPOUSE}` | `election = true` AND `qualifiedTotal ≤ 0` | Blocking | Enter qualified amounts or set election=false |
| `MEDICAID_WAIVER_LINE8S_EXCEEDS_QUALIFIED_{TAXPAYER,SPOUSE}` | Computed offset > qualifiedTotal | Blocking | Internal consistency error |
| `MEDICAID_WAIVER_LINE8S_MISSING_{TAXPAYER,SPOUSE}` | `election = true` AND offset null | Blocking | Internal consistency error |

Non-flag defensive behaviors (silent skip):

* `data == null` (form not saved) — returns `(null, null)`.
* `receivedMedicaidWaiverPayments != true` — outer gate.
* Empty `medicaidWaiverPayments[]` — nothing to compute.
* Empty row (both amounts null) — gracefully ignored.
* No matching W-2 — `includedInW2` used as provided.

## 14) Test scenarios that should exist

### Basic placement

1. Taxable MW payments not in W-2 box 1 → line 1d.
2. Nontaxable code II + no election → not on line 1d or line 8s.
3. Nontaxable code II + election → line 1d positive, line 8s negative same.
4. Nontaxable in line 1a + election → line 8s backs it out.

### 1099 paths

5. 1099-MISC / 1099-NEC + no separate business → line 1d / line 8s as applicable.
6. Sole proprietor home-care business → blocked (`MEDICAID_WAIVER_SCHEDULE_C_*`).

### §131(c) cap

7. 7 individuals age 19+ → pro-rata reduction; excess taxable on line 1d.
8. 11 individuals under 19 → pro-rata reduction.
9. Counts absent → no reduction (legacy data tolerance).

### Qualification rules

10. Separate provider home → does not qualify (user-supplied qualifiedAmount but UI warns).
11. Shared home → qualifies.
12. Vacation pay → does not qualify.
13. Related-party (adult child caring for parent) → qualifies.

### MFJ / MFS behavior

14. Taxpayer and spouse make different election choices.
15. MFS taxpayer with stale spouse data → spouse contribution dropped.

### 2025 reporting change

16. W-2 box 12 code II with box 1 = 0 + election → line 1d / line 8s flow.
17. Same without election → neither line populated.

## 16) Final condensed rule map

1. Include taxable Medicaid waiver payments not already in W-2 box 1. ([IRS][1], [2])
2. Include nontaxable eligible payments on line 1d only when the taxpayer makes the earned-income election. ([IRS][1], [2])
3. That election is all-or-none for each spouse's own eligible payments. ([IRS][2])
4. If a payment is already in W-2 box 1, it belongs in line 1a, not line 1d. ([IRS][1], [2])
5. If nontaxable amounts are included on line 1a or line 1d, enter the same total on Schedule 1 line 8s as a negative number. ([IRS][1], [2])
6. No separate home-care business → 1099-MISC / 1099-NEC payments can use the line 1d / line 8s path. ([IRS][2])
7. Sole proprietor home-care business → Schedule C path (out of scope; blocking flag).
8. 2024+ payers report nontaxable amounts on W-2 box 12 code II. ([IRS][1])
9. Exclusion applies even when the provider is related to the recipient. ([IRS][2])
10. Payments above the §131(c) per-individual cap → pro-rata taxable.
