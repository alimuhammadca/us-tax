**UI label:** *Taxable dependent care benefits from Form 2441, line 26*
**Semantic 1040 field:** `line1e_taxable_dependent_care_benefits`
**Output field path:** `form1040.income.dependentCareBenefits`
**Output PDF field:** `topmostSubform[0].Page1[0].f1_51[0]`
**Category:** earned income ‚Üí employer-provided dependent care benefits (taxable portion)
**Tax year:** 2025

## Canonical null vs zero semantic ‚Äî Fixed 2026-05-10

Per `knowledge/canonical-null-zero-semantic.md` and `rules.md`:

* `income.dependentCareBenefits = null` when the user has neither filled in the `childcare-expenses` form NOR has any W-2 box 10 benefits attributable to them.
* `income.dependentCareBenefits = BigDecimal.ZERO` when the user HAS filled in the form (or has W-2 box 10 benefits) AND Form 2441 Part III legitimately computes line 26 to zero (full exclusion).

Lock-in tests: `line1eIsNullWhenNoChildcareExpensesFormAndNoBox10`, `line1eIsZeroWhenChildcareExpensesFormPresentWithZeroAmount`.

## 1) Authoritative IRS definition

The 2025 Instructions for Form 1040/1040-SR define line 1e as taxable dependent care benefits from Form 2441, line 26. ([IRS][1])

Form 2441 Part III is the source of truth for the taxable portion of employer-provided dependent care benefits; line 26 explicitly carries to Form 1040 line 1e. ([IRS][2], [3])

Authoritative sources: IRC ¬ß21 (dependent care credit); IRC ¬ß129 (employer-provided dependent care assistance); Pub 503 (Child and Dependent Care Expenses).

**Developer interpretation:**

* W-2 box 10 is an input to Form 2441 Part III.
* Line 1e is NOT the raw box 10 amount.
* Line 1e is the taxable remainder after the Form 2441 Part III exclusion computation. ([IRS][2], [3])

---

## 2) Plain-English formula

```text
Line 1e = Form 2441 line 26 (taxable portion of employer-provided dependent care benefits).
Form 2441 Part III adds back the portion of W-2 box 10 benefits that exceeds
the statutory cap, the qualified-expense limit, the earned-income limit, or the plan-defined limit.
```

### Two-pass architecture

* **Phase 1 ‚Äî `computeDependentCareBenefits()`** runs in `prepare()`, BEFORE AGI is known. Sets line 1e via Form 2441 Part III and seeds Part II lines 3‚Äì6.
* **Phase 2 ‚Äî `finalizeForm2441PartII()`** runs AFTER `buildForm1040` produces AGI + `totalTaxBeforeCredits`. Computes Part II lines 7‚Äì11 (applicable %, credit, tax-liability limit). Wires line 11 ‚Üí Schedule 3 line 2 via `applyChildDependentCareCredit()`.

## 3) Product scope vs IRS form scope

### 3.1 Product scope

Employee-only for dependent care benefits. The product does NOT support:

* sole proprietorship dependent care assistance program benefits,
* partnership dependent care assistance program benefits, or
* the meaningful user workflow for deductible benefits on Form 2441 lines 22 through 24.

### 3.2 IRS form scope still matters

Even in employee-only scope, Form 2441 still contains Part III lines 22 through 24.

For an employee-only implementation:

```text
L22 = 0
L23 = L15
L24 = 0 (deductible benefits ‚Äî sole-prop only)
```

That simplification is consistent with the form structure. ([IRS][2], [3])

## 4) Qualifying-person requirement

The dependent care exclusion and credit apply only when the care is for a **qualifying person**:

* A qualifying child under age 13 at the time the care was provided whom the taxpayer can claim as a dependent (or, under the divorced/separated-parent rule, the custodial parent's child under 13).
* A spouse incapable of self-care who lived with the taxpayer more than half the year.
* A dependent (other than a child under 13) incapable of self-care who lived with the taxpayer more than half the year.

"Incapable of self-care" means inability to dress, clean, or feed oneself due to a physical/mental condition or needing constant care.

If no qualifying person exists, Form 2441 Part III does not apply and line 1e is zero regardless of W-2 box 10. ([IRS][2], [3])

## 6) Decision tree ‚Äî when does an entry contribute to line 1e?

| Branch | Filing status | W-2 box 10 | Statutory cap | Result |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| No box 10 + no childcare form data | any | 0 | n/a | line 1e = null (Form 2441 not generated) |
| Box 10 present + form not completed | any | > 0 | n/a | BLOCKED ‚Äî `DEPENDENT_CARE_BENEFITS_FORM_REQUIRED` |
| Standard MFJ within all limits | MFJ | ‚â§ $5,000 + qualified expenses ‚â• benefits | $5,000 | line26 = 0 ‚Üí line 1e = null |
| MFJ benefits exceed $5,000 cap | MFJ | > $5,000 | $5,000 | line25 = $5,000, line26 = excess ‚Üí line 1e positive |
| MFS without separation exception | MFS | any | $2,500 | line25 capped at $2,500; excess ‚Üí line 1e |
| MFS with separation exception (Box A) | MFS | any | $5,000 | $5,000 cap applied, treated as unmarried for ¬ß21 |
| ¬ß129(d) nondiscrimination failure | any | any | n/a (bypass) | line25 = 0, line26 = line15 ‚Üí entire amount taxable |
| Earned income < benefits (low-earner spouse) | MFJ | any | $5,000 | line20 capped by lower earned income |
| Student/disabled with deemed income | any | any | normal | Deemed income substitutes (Pub 503: $250 / $500 per month) |
| Plan limit lower than statutory cap | any | any | `min(planMax, statutory)` | line21 = lesser of plan or statutory |

## 7) Filing-status rules

### 7.1 MFJ

Single Form 2441 uses both spouses' earned incomes for the line 18 / line 19 limitation.

### 7.2 Single / HOH / QSS

Single Form 2441. For line 19, the form directs most non-MFJ to enter line 18.

### 7.3 MFS

* **Part III (exclusion) ‚Äî $2,500 cap.** Statutory cap halved.
* **Part II (credit) ‚Äî generally unavailable.** IRS instructions: MFS filers "can't take the credit for child and dependent care expenses in most cases."

**Separation exception ‚Äî treated as unmarried.** MFS may still claim the credit and the $5,000 cap if ALL THREE tests are met:

1. Lived apart from spouse during the LAST 6 MONTHS of the tax year;
2. The qualifying person's home was the taxpayer's home for MORE THAN HALF the year;
3. The taxpayer paid MORE THAN HALF the cost of maintaining that household for the entire year.

When met, `marriedFilingSeparatelyException = true` ‚Üí $5,000 cap + credit available.

## 14) Test scenarios that should exist

### Basic cases

1. W-2 box 10 with sufficient expenses and earned income ‚Üí zero line 1e.
2. W-2 box 10 with limited expenses ‚Üí positive line 1e.
3. W-2 box 10 with limited spouse earned income on MFJ ‚Üí increases line 1e.
4. MFS applies the $2,500 cap.

### Important guardrails

5. Box 10 amount already in W-2 box 1 ‚Üí excluded from line 12.
6. Employer plan max below statutory cap ‚Üí reduces excludable.
7. Prior-year carryover used in grace period ‚Üí can make Part III relevant even with zero current-year box 10.
8. Forfeiture / carryforward affects line 15.

### Mapping

9. Form 2441 line 26 maps exactly to Form 1040 line 1e.
10. Line 1e never goes negative.

### Qualifying person and MFS rules

11. No qualifying person ‚Üí line 1e is zero regardless of box 10.
12. MFS without exception: $2,500 cap; credit unavailable.
13. MFS with exception: $5,000 cap; credit available.
14. One-earner MFJ + non-working spouse not student/disabled: line 19 = $0 ‚Üí full benefit taxable.
15. One-earner MFJ + spouse full-time student 9 months + 2 qualifying children: deemed = $500 √ó 9 = $4,500.

### Null contract

16. `line1eIsNullWhenNoChildcareExpensesFormAndNoBox10` ‚Äî null on truly empty input.
17. `line1eIsZeroWhenChildcareExpensesFormPresentWithZeroAmount` ‚Äî non-null when concept applies.

## 17) Final condensed rule map

1. Start with dependent care benefits not already included in W-2 box 1. ([IRS][2], [3], [4])
2. Care must be for a qualifying person. No qualifying person ‚Üí line 1e = 0. ([IRS][2], [3])
3. Compute Form 2441 Part III using current-year benefits, grace-period (line 13), forfeitures (line 14), expenses, earned-income limits, plan/statutory cap. ([IRS][2], [3])
4. In employee-only scope, `L22 = 0` and `L23 = L15`. ([IRS][2], [3])
5. For lines 18/19, deem student/disabled spouse at $250 (1 person) or $500 (2+) per month. ([IRS][2], [3])
6. MFS ordinary: $2,500 cap, no credit. MFS-with-separation-exception: $5,000 cap, credit available. ([IRS][3])
7. `Form1040.line1e = Form2441.line26`; label "DCB" on the return. ([IRS][1], [2], [3])
8. Do not use a blanket `box10 ‚àí $5,000` assumption.
9. Do not ignore the employer plan maximum.
10. DCFSA allows a grace period only (‚â§ 2¬Ω months), not a fund carryover. ([IRS][3])
