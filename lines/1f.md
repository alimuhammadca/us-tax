## 2. What line 1f is

Line 1f is the taxable portion of employer-provided adoption benefits after applying:

- the per-child exclusion limit (`$17,280` for 2025),
- the special-needs rule,
- the MAGI phaseout (`$259,190` start, `$299,190` end), and
- the foreign-child finality rules where applicable.

The line is computed on `Form 8839, Part III` and then carried to `Form 1040 line 1f`.

## 4. Core 2025 IRS rules

### 4.1 Employer-provided adoption benefits

Employer-provided adoption benefits are amounts your employer paid directly to you or to a third party for qualified adoption expenses under a written qualified adoption assistance program.

**Qualified adoption expenses** include adoption fees, attorney fees, court costs, travel expenses, and other expenses directly related to and for the principal purpose of the legal adoption.

**Expenses that do not qualify:**
- expenses for adopting your spouse's child,
- costs of a surrogate-parenting arrangement.

**Eligible child:** The child must be under age 18, or physically or mentally incapable of self-care (IRC Â§23(d)).

These benefits are shown on `Form W-2, box 12, code T`. The code T total includes pre-tax salary reduction contributions made through a cafeteria plan for adoption expenses, not just employer-funded amounts.

Per IRC Â§3121 (FICA wages) and Pub. 15-B:

- adoption benefits are generally **not** included in `W-2 box 1` (excluded from federal income tax via the Form 8839 exclusion computation),
- they are generally included in `W-2 boxes 3 and 5` (subject to Social Security and Medicare taxes), and
- you must complete `Form 8839 Part III` to determine the taxable and excludable portions.

### 4.2 Special-needs child rule â€” IRC Â§137(a)(3)

A **child with special needs** is a child who is a U.S. citizen or resident that a state has determined requires adoption assistance.

Under IRC Â§137(a)(3), you may exclude employer-provided adoption benefits even if they are **not** shown in `W-2 box 12 code T`, but only if all of the following are true:

1. the child was a child with special needs,
2. the adoption became final in 2025, and
3. the employer had a written qualified adoption assistance program.

When this exception applies, Form 8839 Part III line 24 uses the full line 21 amount (`L24[i] = L21[i]`) rather than `min(L21, L22)`.

**Implementation.** `computeAdoptionBenefits` enables Part III when any child has `hasSpecialNeeds=true && adoptionFinal2025OrEarlier=true`, even when W-2 box 12 code T is zero. With the special-needs branch (`L24 = L21`) and `L23 = $0`, Form 8839 line 31 produces a NEGATIVE value (`L23 âˆ’ L30 = $0 âˆ’ L30`) which flows to Form 1040 line 1f as a wage adjustment. Non-special-needs-final children in the same return contribute 0 (their `L22 = 0 â†’ L24 = 0`). The IRS-required "written qualified adoption-assistance program" condition is not separately verified â€” the user's special-needs-final answer is trusted.

### 4.3 Over-2% S corporation owners â€” IRC Â§1372

You cannot exclude employer-provided adoption benefits if your employer is an S corporation in which you own more than 2% of the stock or more than 2% of the voting power. Under IRC Â§1372, more-than-2% S corporation shareholders are treated as partners for fringe benefit purposes, including the adoption assistance exclusion. **Not currently enforced** by the backend (Code Validation #7); a future `ADOPTION_BENEFITS_S_CORP_OWNER_DISALLOWED` flag is tracked in `outstanding.md`.

### 4.4 Same expenses cannot be used twice â€” IRC Â§137(d)

You can claim both:

- the exclusion for employer-provided adoption benefits (Form 8839 Part III), and
- the adoption credit (Form 8839 Part II),

but not for the **same expenses**. Expenses reimbursed by the employer are not qualified adoption expenses for the credit.

**Implementation.** Per-child Part II input is split into gross qualified expenses + employer reimbursement to subtract; the backend computes `net = max(0, gross âˆ’ reimbursement)` and uses it as Form 8839 Part II line 5. The reimbursement input pre-fills from the Part III allocation; the user can override. The math enforces Â§137(d) structurally â€” the same dollar cannot reach both Part II and Part III.

### 4.5 Foreign child timing rule

If the child was a foreign child when the adoption effort began, the exclusion is available only in the year the adoption becomes final. Prior-year employer-provided benefits for a foreign adoption must be reported as taxable wage income in the year received; the exclusion is then claimed on Form 8839 in the finality year.

This can produce a negative line 31 (see Section 8) when prior-year benefits become excludable in the finality year.

### 4.6 Married filing separately restriction â€” IRC Â§137(f)

If the taxpayer is married, they must **generally file a joint return** to exclude employer-provided adoption benefits.

Exceptions that allow the exclusion on a separate return:

- the taxpayer is legally separated under a decree of divorce or separate maintenance, or
- the taxpayer lived apart from their spouse for the last six months of the tax year.

**Implementation.** When `isMfs == true` AND `mfsOrHohLivedApartOrLegallySeparated != true`, the orchestrator emits `ADOPTION_BENEFITS_MFS_EXCLUSION_DISALLOWED` (blocking) and sets `line1f = w2TotalBenefits` (full taxable, no exclusion).

## 6. Triggers

Create or compute `Form 8839 Part III` when any of the following are true:

- there is a `W-2 box 12 code T` amount,
- the taxpayer qualifies for the special-needs off-W-2 rule, or
- foreign-child finality rules require Part III for benefits tied to 2025 finality.

If none of those are present:

- `Form1040.Line1f = null` (Form 8839 not generated)

## 7. 2025 computation model â€” Form 8839 Part III

### 7.1 Per-child computation

For each child `i`:

- `L19[i] = $17,280` (or the resolved shared-adoption cap when applicable)
- `L20[i] = priorYearEmployerBenefitsByChild["child{i+1}PriorYearBenefits"] ?? 0`
- `L21[i] = max(0, L19[i] âˆ’ L20[i])`
- `L22[i] = currentYearBenefitsAllocation["child{i+1}Benefits2025"] ?? 0`
- `L24[i] = (hasSpecialNeeds && adoptionFinal) ? L21[i] : min(L21[i], L22[i])`

### 7.2 Return-level totals and phaseout

- `L23 = w2TotalBenefits` (total of W-2 box 12 code T across qualifying W-2s)
- `L25 = MAGI` (from `imports.magiForAdoptionBenefitsExclusion`)

If `L25 <= $259,190`:

- `phaseoutFraction = 0`

If `L25 > $259,190`:

- `L26 = L25 âˆ’ $259,190`
- `L27 = min(1.0000, L26 / $40,000)` (4-decimal HALF_UP rounding)
- `L28[i] = L24[i] Ã— L27`

## 8. Signed behavior â€” line 1f may be negative

For 2025, Form 8839 line 31 explicitly allows a negative amount.

The form instructs: if line 30 exceeds line 23, subtract line 23 from line 30 and enter the result as a negative number on both line 31 and Form 1040 line 1f.

This arises when:

- prior-year benefits become retroactively excludable in the foreign-child finality year, or
- the special-needs off-W-2 path produces an exclusion (`L29 > 0`) against zero current-year benefits (`L23 = 0`) â€” line 1f = `-L30`.

Therefore:

- line 1f must support negative values,
- `addNonNull` in the line 1z aggregation absorbs the signed amount (no `max(0, ...)` floor),
- line 1z, line 9, and AGI all reflect the negative; the `max(0, ...)` floor is applied only at line 15 (taxable income).

## 10. Compute order

`computeAdoptionBenefits` runs in the `prepare()` phase BEFORE AGI/MAGI is finalized. It uses `imports.magiForAdoptionBenefitsExclusion` as a placeholder; a second pass (`finalizeForm8839PartII`) at line ~22289 derives MAGI from final AGI for Part II refundable credit. **Currently for line 1f the MAGI source is read once from `imports`** â€” see Code Validation #9 (above-threshold filers may silently over-claim if `imports.magi` is absent).

Sequence:

1. `computeAdoptionBenefits` â†’ `line1f` + Form 8839 Part III lines 19-31 + Part II lines 2-13 (when MAGI available)
2. `line1z = addNonNull(... line1f ...)` at line 7361
3. `line9 = addNonNull(line1z, line2b, ...)` at line 7440
4. `finalizeForm8839PartII` re-runs Part II once final AGI is available (line ~22289)
5. AGI / taxable income downstream
