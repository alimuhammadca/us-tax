Below is a **2025-tax-year, implementation-ready** rule map for **Form 2555** (Foreign Earned Income). This document covers multiplicity, per-spouse handling, and developer-safe implementation rules.

---

# Form 2555 (2025) — Foreign Earned Income

## Form identity

* **Form 2555** is used to claim the **foreign earned income exclusion**, **foreign housing exclusion**, or **foreign housing deduction**.
* It feeds into the **Foreign Earned Income Tax Worksheet**, whose result goes on **Form 1040 line 16** (Tax) in place of the Tax Table / Tax Computation Worksheet.

---

# 1) Multiplicity — one form or two?

```text
if spouseA_claims_2555 and spouseB_claims_2555:
    create Form2555_TP   (taxpayer)
    create Form2555_SP   (spouse)
else if only one spouse claims:
    create one Form2555
```

If **both spouses have foreign earned income and both want to claim** the exclusion or deduction, they must file **two separate Forms 2555** — one for each spouse — attached to the same joint return (or to their separate returns if filing separately). The IRS states this explicitly.

| Scenario | Forms 2555 required |
|---|---|
| Only taxpayer claims | 1 (taxpayer) |
| Only spouse claims | 1 (spouse) |
| Both spouses claim | 2 (one per spouse) |

---

# 2) Person-specific answers may differ

Each spouse completes their **own** Form 2555 independently. The following questions may legitimately have different answers for each spouse:

* Which test qualifies them — **bona fide residence test** or **physical presence test**.
* Their individual **12-month qualifying period** and travel dates.
* Their employer, foreign earned income amount, and exclusion amount.
* Their own tax-home and employment facts.
* Their separately computed exclusion amount in **Part VII**.

---

# 3) Shared household facts must reconcile

If both spouses lived in the **same foreign household** and file a joint return, household facts must be consistent across the two forms. The 2025 instructions allow housing amounts to be figured **jointly or separately**:

| Housing method | Rule |
|---|---|
| **Jointly** | Either spouse — but **not both** — claims the housing exclusion/deduction. |
| **Separately** | Each spouse computes housing amounts separately; expenses may be allocated between them **with no duplication**. |

If spouses lived in **separate foreign households**, each spouse computes housing separately. Only certain arrangements qualify when the households/tax homes are not within reasonable commuting distance.

---

# 4) Developer-safe implementation rules

* **Shared-household facts must reconcile** across the two forms.
* **Qualification answers may differ** by spouse — do not enforce identical answers for spouse-specific questions.
* **Do not allow both spouses to claim the housing exclusion/deduction jointly computed** on the same form — only one spouse may claim it when jointly computed.
* **No duplication** of housing expenses when separately computed.

---

# 5) Downstream

Form 2555 values (lines 45 and 50) are inputs to the **Foreign Earned Income Tax Worksheet**, which replaces the Tax Table/Tax Computation Worksheet on Form 1040 line 16. When any Form 2555 is filed, the regular tax path on line 16 must route through that worksheet.

---

# 6) Out of scope

Schedule J (farming/fishing income averaging) is excluded from this application. All other line 16 special-method paths (Form 8615, Schedule D Tax Worksheet, QDCG Worksheet) remain in scope per their own rule maps.
