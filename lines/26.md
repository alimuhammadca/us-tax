# Form 1040 (2025) - Line 26: Estimated Tax Payments and Amount Applied From 2024 Return

> Source of truth: 2025 Form 1040, the 2025 Instructions for Form 1040, 2025 Form 1040-ES, and Pub. 505.

---

## 1. Line Identity

**Form 1040 line 26** is:

```text
2025 estimated tax payments and amount applied from 2024 return
```

Exact mapping:

```text
Form1040.line26 = total 2025 estimated federal income tax payments actually made
                + overpayment from the 2024 original return applied to 2025 estimated tax
                + overpayment from a 2024 amended return applied to 2025 estimated tax
                (aggregated across taxpayer + spouse forms on MFJ only)
```

For 2025, line 26 is part of the payments section and feeds **Form 1040 line 33** directly:

```text
Form1040.line33 = Form1040.line25d + Form1040.line26 + Form1040.line32
```

Important 2025 guardrail:

```text
Form1040.line32 = Form1040.line27a + Form1040.line28 + Form1040.line29 + Form1040.line30 + Form1040.line31
```

So `line 26` is **not** rolled into line 33 by adding lines 27 through 31 individually. It is its own separate input to line 33.

---

## 2. Core 2025 Formula

```text
line26 = Σ taxpayer  ( installment1Amount + installment2Amount + installment3Amount + installment4Amount
                     + priorYearOverpaymentCredited + amendedReturnOverpaymentCredited )
       + Σ spouse    ( same 6 fields )    [ MFJ only ]
       (per-addend positive-only; null when total ≤ 0)
```

Implementation convention:

```text
Form1040.line26 = nz(estimatedTaxPaymentsMadeFor2025)
                + nz(appliedOverpaymentFrom2024OriginalReturn)
                + nz(appliedOverpaymentFrom2024AmendedReturn)
```

If all components are zero or absent (or the screening gate is false on both forms), the application stores `null`. The PDF cell renders blank under the null-when-zero convention.

Reference implementation — `computeLine26EstimatedTax` at `TaxReturnComputeService.java:27468-27525`:

```java

        && Boolean.TRUE.equals(getBoolean(spouseForm, "madeEstimatedTaxPayments"));

        "installment1Amount", "installment2Amount",
        "installment3Amount", "installment4Amount",
        "priorYearOverpaymentCredited", "amendedReturnOverpaymentCredited");

BigDecimal total = null;
if (Boolean.TRUE.equals(taxpayerMade)) {
    for (String field : fields) {
        BigDecimal v = parseAmount(taxpayerForm.get(field));
        if (v != null && v.compareTo(BigDecimal.ZERO) > 0) {
            total = addNonNull(total, v);
        }
    }
}
if (isMfj && spouseMade) {
    for (String field : fields) {
        BigDecimal v = parseAmount(spouseForm.get(field));
        if (v != null && v.compareTo(BigDecimal.ZERO) > 0) {
            total = addNonNull(total, v);
        }
    }
}
return total != null && total.compareTo(BigDecimal.ZERO) > 0 ? roundMoney(total) : null;
```

## 3. What Counts On Line 26

Include on line 26:

- estimated federal income tax payments made for tax year 2025,
- payments made by `Form 1040-ES` voucher,
- payments made by `EFTPS`,
- payments made by `IRS Direct Pay`,
- estimated tax payments made by card through an IRS-authorized processor,
- any 2024 overpayment the taxpayer elected to apply to 2025 estimated tax on the original return (`priorYearOverpaymentCredited`),
- any additional 2024 overpayment from `Form 1040-X` that was elected to be applied to 2025 estimated tax (`amendedReturnOverpaymentCredited`).

Line 26 is a **total payments** line. It is not limited to four equal quarterly payments. A taxpayer may make one payment, four payments, or multiple uneven payments during the year. All creditable 2025 estimated-tax payments are aggregated here. The four `installmentNAmount` fields are simply convenient intake bins corresponding to the four IRS quarterly due dates.

---

## 4. What Does Not Count On Line 26

Do **not** include on line 26:

- federal income tax withholding from `W-2`, `1099`, `SSA-1099`, `RRB-1099`, `W-2G`, or other withholding forms; those belong on `lines 25a` through `25c`,
- payment made with `Form 4868`; that belongs later through `Schedule 3 line 10` and `Form 1040 line 31`,
- refundable credits such as `EIC`, `ACTC`, or the refundable `AOTC`; those belong on later payment lines (27a / 28 / 29),
- refundable adoption credit (`line 30`, new for 2025),
- state estimated tax payments,
- amounts merely intended or scheduled but not actually paid,
- an amount paid with the filed 2025 return in 2026 to satisfy a balance due,
- any amount applied to **2026** estimated tax (that is `line 36`, not `line 26`).

## 5. Payment Timing And Due Dates

The standard 2025 estimated-tax installment due dates are:

| Payment period | General due date | 2025 tax year due date |
|---|---|---|
| Jan. 1 - Mar. 31, 2025 | April 15 | April 15, 2025 (Tue) |
| Apr. 1 - May 31, 2025 | June 15 | **June 16, 2025 (Mon)** (June 15 is Sunday) |
| June 1 - Aug. 31, 2025 | Sept. 15 | September 15, 2025 (Mon) |
| Sept. 1 - Dec. 31, 2025 | Jan. 15 next year | January 15, 2026 (Thu) |

Important timing notes:

- The June installment falls on **June 16, 2025** because June 15, 2025 is a Sunday.
- If the taxpayer files the 2025 return and pays the balance due by **February 2, 2026**, the January 15, 2026 installment is not required. This is the 2025 version of the normal January-payment exception, adjusted for the weekend calendar (Jan 31 Sat → Feb 1 Sun → Feb 2 Mon).
- These due dates matter for `Form 2210` penalty analysis, but they do **not** change the line-26 arithmetic. Line 26 is still the total of all creditable estimated payments for 2025.
- The four `installmentNAmount` field labels in the UI use these due dates as captions; the field semantics are still "payment made for that period" regardless of when actually paid.

## 6. Filing Status, Joint Payments, And Former Spouse Rules

### 6a. Married filing jointly — both forms aggregated

If spouses made separate estimated-tax payments during 2025 but file a joint 2025 return, add both spouses' payments. **The helper reads both the `estimated-tax-payments-taxpayer` form and the `estimated-tax-payments-spouse` form on MFJ** and combines their 6 fields. Each spouse may enter their own payments on their respective form. If the taxpayer form was never saved (null), the spouse form is still read.

### 6b. Single / Head of household / Qualifying surviving spouse — taxpayer form only (Gap F closure)

The **Gap F closure (2026-06-10)** narrowed the spouse-aggregation guard from `!isMfs` to `isMfj`. Prior to the fix the helper read the spouse form on Single / HoH / QSS as well — incorrect because those statuses have no living spouse on the return (per Publication 17 / Publication 559). Stale spouse-form data left over from a prior MFJ filing-status selection (the project saves all personal forms under one household UID per `project_single_return`) would silently overstate line 26.

After the Gap F closure the guard is exactly:

```java
boolean spouseMade = isMfj && spouseForm != null
        && Boolean.TRUE.equals(getBoolean(spouseForm, "madeEstimatedTaxPayments"));
...
if (isMfj && spouseMade) { ... }
```

### 6c. Married filing separately — taxpayer form only

If spouses paid joint estimated tax but file separate 2025 returns, they may divide the payments any way they agree. If they cannot agree, the payments must be divided in proportion to each spouse's individual tax as shown on the separate 2025 returns. Pub. 505 discusses this allocation rule.

The helper does not perform this allocation automatically (deferred OOS — G8). It reads only the taxpayer's own form on MFS.

### 6d. Divorced taxpayers — former spouse SSN annotation

If the taxpayer made joint estimated-tax payments with a former spouse in 2025, the 2025 Form 1040 provides a dedicated space on line 26 to enter the former spouse's SSN.

The field `divorceFormerSpouseSSN` is **stored on the taxpayer form but NOT summed** by the helper — it is annotation-only. It is excluded from the `fields` list at `TaxReturnComputeService.java:27493-27496` so it cannot accidentally be parsed as a numeric amount.

If the taxpayer was divorced and remarried during 2025, the present spouse's SSN is entered on the front of the return in the normal spouse-identification area.

### 6e. Name change

If estimated-tax payments were made under a former name, the IRS instructions say to attach a statement to a paper return explaining the payments and the name(s) and SSN(s) used.

## 7. Screening Gate Convention

Line 26 is the only line in the 25-26 payments cluster that uses a **screening gate** — the per-form boolean `madeEstimatedTaxPayments`. The gate behavior:

- If **neither** form has `madeEstimatedTaxPayments = true`, the helper returns `null` immediately at line 27491 — line 26 is null regardless of any stored installment values.
- If the taxpayer form has the gate true, taxpayer fields are aggregated.
- If MFJ **and** the spouse form has the gate true, spouse fields are aggregated.
- Final guard: if the resulting `total` is null or ≤ 0, return `null` — null-when-zero convention.

This screening gate is the "Convention 5" that distinguishes line 26 from line 25 (which has no gate — withholding is always inferred from statement presence).

## 8. Prior-Year Overpayment Rules

Two distinct prior-year carryforward sources can feed line 26:

- overpayment from the **2024 original return** applied to 2025 estimated tax (field `priorYearOverpaymentCredited`), and
- overpayment from a **2024 amended return (`Form 1040-X`)** applied to 2025 estimated tax (field `amendedReturnOverpaymentCredited`).

Both are included on line 26 if they were actually elected to be applied to 2025 estimated tax. The intake stores them separately for auditability even though both fold into the same line-26 total.

Important `Form 1040-X` guardrail:

- If the taxpayer already elected on the original 2024 return to apply an overpayment to 2025 estimated tax, `Form 1040-X` cannot reverse that election. The amended-return field captures only **additional** overpayment elected via 1040-X, not the original-return election.

## 9. Implementation Guidance

The minimum correct compute model for line 26 itself is:

```text
line26 = totalEstimatedTaxPaymentsFor2025 + carryforwardCreditsFrom2024
```

This project's intake captures payments in **six fields per form × up to 2 forms = up to 12 numeric inputs** (per spec §2), plus the screening gate, plus the annotation-only `divorceFormerSpouseSSN`. Each payment field is **per-addend positive-filtered** at line 27503 / 27517 — negative or zero amounts are skipped rather than allowed to net against positive ones.

Important design guardrail:

- Do **not** assume line 26 can always be represented accurately by exactly four hard-coded quarterly fields. A taxpayer may make multiple payments within a period or make payments on irregular dates. The four `installmentNAmount` bins are a convenient intake UI, but the line-26 tax-law spec remains based on the **total of all creditable payments**.

### Separate intake forms for line 26 (UI) and Form 2210 (Schedule AI)

Line 26 reads `estimated-tax-payments-taxpayer` and `estimated-tax-payments-spouse`.

Form 2210 line 11 (estimated-tax payments per period, used in the regular-method penalty computation) reads a **separate** `prior-year-tax-taxpayer` form's fields `payment1Amount` / `payment2Amount` / `payment3Amount` / `payment4Amount`. These are distinct from the line-26 installment fields by design — Form 2210's Schedule AI / regular-method needs payment-date granularity which line 26 itself does not (see `TaxReturnComputeService.java:27628-27632, 27678`).

## 10. Upstream And Downstream Dependencies

Line 26 does **not** depend on income, AGI, deductions, tax, or credit computations. It is a payments aggregation line.

Its downstream path on the 2025 Form 1040 is:

```text
Form1040.line25d = total withholding
Form1040.line26  = estimated tax payments and amount applied from 2024 return   [THIS LINE]
Form1040.line32  = total other payments and refundable credits (27a + 28 + 29 + 30 + 31)
Form1040.line33  = Form1040.line25d + Form1040.line26 + Form1040.line32
```

Then:

```text
Form1040.line34 = overpayment if line33 > line24
Form1040.line37 = amount owed if line24 > line33
```

Form 2210 Part I `totalPayments` includes the line-26 amount transitively via the `Payments.totalPayments` (line 33) read.

---

## 11. Validation Guardrails

- All entered payment amounts should be nonnegative; negative entries are skipped per-addend at lines 27503 and 27517 (positive-only filter).
- If the product captures separate taxpayer and spouse payment records, combine them on a joint return (MFJ); ignore the spouse record on Single / HoH / QSS / MFS.
- If the product imports prior-year data, preserve whether the carryforward came from the original return or an amended return for auditability, even though both amounts feed the same 2025 line 26.
- The `divorceFormerSpouseSSN` field is annotation-only and never iterated as a payment field.

---

## 12. Summary Rule

For tax year 2025:

```text
Form1040.line26 = Σ taxpayer ( installments 1-4 + priorYearOverpaymentCredited + amendedReturnOverpaymentCredited )
                + Σ spouse   ( same 6 fields )   [ MFJ only ]
                ( positive-only per addend; null when total ≤ 0 )
```

And:

```text
Form1040.line33 = Form1040.line25d + Form1040.line26 + Form1040.line32
```
