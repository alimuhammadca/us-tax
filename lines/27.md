## 1. Line Identity

| Sub-line | 2025 Form 1040 label |
|---|---|
| `27a` | Earned income credit (EIC) |
| `27b` | Clergy checkbox |
| `27c` | Box if you don't want to claim the credit or if instructed to check |

Exact mappings:

```text
Form1040.line27a = earned income credit amount determined under the 2025 EIC instructions, worksheets, and EIC table
Form1040.line27b = checkbox used for clergy / religious worker earned-income adjustment rules
Form1040.line27c = checkbox used when taxpayer opts out of EIC or is instructed to check because EIC cannot be claimed
```

For 2025, only `line 27a` is a payment amount. It feeds `Form 1040 line 32`.

```text
Form1040.line32 = Form1040.line27a + Form1040.line28 + Form1040.line29 + Form1040.line30 + Form1040.line31
```

`Line 27b` and `line 27c` are indicator fields only. They do not add to payments.

---

## 2. Core 2025 Structure

`Line 27a` is **not** a simple source-form carryforward. It is a computed refundable credit determined from:

- EIC eligibility rules,
- AGI limits,
- earned-income limits,
- investment-income limits,
- qualifying-child rules,
- valid-SSN rules,
- Worksheet A or Worksheet B in the Form 1040 instructions, or IRS computation if the taxpayer elects that option,
- the 2025 EIC table.

So the correct high-level model is:

```text
if taxpayer is not eligible for EIC:
    Form1040.line27a = 0 / blank
    Form1040.line27c = checked when the instructions require or allow it
else:
    Form1040.line27a = EIC amount from 2025 Worksheet A or Worksheet B (or IRS-computed EIC)
```

## 3. 2025 Eligibility Thresholds

For tax year 2025, both **AGI** and **earned income** must be below the applicable threshold.

| Qualifying children with valid SSNs | Single / HOH / QSS / qualifying MFS-separated-spouse filer | MFJ |
|---|---|---|
| 0 | `< $19,104` | `< $26,214` |
| 1 | `< $50,434` | `< $57,554` |
| 2 | `< $57,310` | `< $64,430` |
| 3 or more | `< $61,555` | `< $68,675` |

2025 investment-income limit:

```text
investment income must be $11,950 or less
```

2025 maximum credit amounts:

- no qualifying children: `$649`
- 1 qualifying child: `$4,328`
- 2 qualifying children: `$7,152`
- 3 or more qualifying children: `$8,046`

Important guardrail:

- These maximums do **not** create a direct formula. The actual credit amount comes from the worksheet / table process.

## 4. What Makes A Taxpayer Ineligible

The taxpayer cannot claim EIC for 2025 if any of the following applies:

- taxpayer or joint-return spouse lacks a valid SSN issued by the due date of the 2025 return, including extensions,
- taxpayer files `Form 2555`,
- taxpayer fails the filing-status rules,
- taxpayer is a nonresident alien for part of 2025 and does not qualify for the special MFJ resident-election path,
- investment income exceeds `$11,950`,
- AGI is at or above the applicable threshold,
- earned income is at or above the applicable threshold,
- taxpayer fails the qualifying-child rules or the no-qualifying-child rules,
- taxpayer is required to file `Form 8862` and does not satisfy the recertification rules.

When the instructions say the taxpayer cannot claim EIC, `line 27a` must not carry an EIC amount and `line 27c` should be checked if instructed.

## 5. 2025 SSN And Child Rules

### 5a. Taxpayer / spouse SSN rule

To claim EIC, the taxpayer and, on a joint return, the spouse must each have a valid SSN by the due date of the 2025 return, including extensions.

Important 2025 guardrails:

- an `ITIN` does **not** satisfy the EIC SSN rule,
- if the taxpayer or spouse did not have a valid SSN by the due date, EIC cannot be claimed on either the original or an amended 2025 return,
- if the taxpayer or spouse became eligible by the due date for an unrestricted work-valid SSN but the card was not updated yet, Pub. 596 allows the claim.

### 5b. Child SSN rule

A qualifying child needs a valid SSN to increase the EIC under the child-based credit columns.

Important 2025 correction:

- if a child meets the qualifying-child tests for EIC purposes but does **not** have a valid SSN by the due date, the taxpayer may still be eligible for a **self-only EIC** if otherwise eligible.
- if the taxpayer has at least one child who meets the EIC qualifying-child conditions, `Schedule EIC` must still be completed and attached, even if that child does not have a valid SSN.

### 5c. Born-and-died-in-2025 rule

Schedule EIC allows `"Died"` instead of an SSN for a child born and died in 2025, with supporting records attached.

## 6. Qualifying Child And No-Child Rules

### 6a. Qualifying child path

A qualifying child for EIC must satisfy the 2025:

- relationship test,
- age / student / disability test,
- joint-return test,
- residency test.

For 2025, the child must have lived with the taxpayer in the **United States** for more than half of the year, subject to the usual temporary-absence, birth, death, foster-placement, adoption-placement, and kidnapping exceptions.

### 6b. More-than-one-person / tiebreaker rules

If the same child could qualify more than one person, only one person may use that child for EIC. The divorced-or-separated-parent dependency-release rules do **not** let the noncustodial parent claim EIC just because they can claim the child as a dependent or for CTC.

### 6c. No-qualifying-child path

If the taxpayer does not have a qualifying child with a valid SSN, EIC may still be available under the no-child rules. For 2025, that path requires additional age, dependency, qualifying-child-of-another, and U.S.-main-home tests.

## 7. Filing Status And Separated-Spouse Rules

Usually, a married taxpayer must file `MFJ` to claim EIC.

2025 exception:

- A married taxpayer not filing jointly may claim EIC if they meet the **special rule for separated spouses**.

That rule requires:

- a qualifying child who lived with the taxpayer in the United States for more than half of 2025, and
- either:
  - living apart from the spouse for the last 6 months of 2025, or
  - legal separation under state law under a written separation agreement or decree of separate maintenance, with no same-household status at year-end.

If this rule is satisfied, the taxpayer must check the dedicated **Dependents-section checkbox** for the special rule for separated spouses.

Important guardrail:

- a plain `MFS` return without this special-rule status cannot claim EIC.

## 8. Earned Income And Worksheet Routing

### 8a. Earned income

For 2025 EIC, earned income generally includes:

- `Form 1040 line 1z`,
- certain `Schedule 1` earned-income additions,
- net earnings from self-employment,
- statutory-employee income,
- other earned-income items recognized by the instructions.

It does **not** include items like:

- pensions,
- annuities,
- Social Security benefits,
- unemployment compensation,
- child support,
- alimony,
- most investment income.

### 8b. Combat pay and Medicaid-waiver interactions

- Nontaxable combat pay may be elected into earned income for EIC purposes; if elected, it is entered on `Form 1040 line 1i`.
- Medicaid-waiver payment handling follows the specific Step 5 worksheet logic already described in the 2025 Form 1040 instructions and should not be simplified away in implementation.

### 8c. Worksheet A vs. Worksheet B

- Use `Worksheet A` when the taxpayer answers **No** to the self-employment / clergy / statutory-employee routing question.
- Use `Worksheet B` when the taxpayer was self-employed, files `Schedule SE` because of clergy or church-employee income, or files `Schedule C` as a statutory employee.

Important guardrail:

- `line 27a` should be modeled as a worksheet-and-table output, not as a fixed algebraic formula.

## 9. Step 2 Investment-Income Screen And Pub. 596 Worksheet 1

The basic Form 1040 instructions screen uses:

```text
investment income = line 2a + line 2b + line 3b + max(line 7a, 0)
```

But the 2025 instructions and Pub. 596 require a broader approach in special cases.

Use `Pub. 596 Worksheet 1` when applicable, including when the taxpayer:

- files `Schedule E`,
- reports rental income from personal property not used in a trade or business,
- files `Form 8814`,
- has passive-activity income or loss,
- has `Form 4797` amounts affecting `line 7a`.

Important 2025 guardrail:

- do not hard-code the short `2a + 2b + 3b + 7a` screen as the only investment-income computation for EIC eligibility.

## 10. Schedule EIC, Form 8862, And IRS-Figured Credit

### 10a. Schedule EIC

For 2025:

- `Schedule EIC` is required if the taxpayer has a qualifying child for EIC purposes,
- it is attached to support the qualifying-child information,
- only up to three children need to be listed because three-or-more children already reaches the maximum-child category.

### 10b. Form 8862

`Form 8862` is required if EIC for a year after 1996 was reduced or disallowed for a reason other than a math or clerical error, unless one of the instruction exceptions applies.

### 10c. IRS-computed EIC

If the taxpayer wants the IRS to figure the credit:

- enter `EIC` on the dotted line next to `line 27a`,
- include any elected nontaxable combat pay on `line 1i`,
- still attach `Schedule EIC` if there is a qualifying child.

## 11. Line-Specific Semantics

### 11a. Line 27a

```text
Form1040.line27a = refundable earned income credit amount for 2025
```

This amount is later included in `Form 1040 line 32`.

### 11b. Line 27b

`Line 27b` is checked only for the clergy / religious-worker double-counting adjustment described in the 2025 instructions.

It is not:

- a separate payment amount,
- a general self-employment checkbox,
- an opt-in for all clergy filers regardless of Schedule SE overlap.

### 11c. Line 27c

Check `line 27c` if:

- the taxpayer does not want to claim EIC, or
- the instructions direct the taxpayer to check it because EIC cannot be claimed.

Common 2025 examples include:

- no valid taxpayer or spouse SSN by the due date,
- no qualifying EIC path after the eligibility flowchart,
- invalid no-child or qualifying-child path,
- nonresident-alien disqualification,
- investment income or income-threshold failure.

## 12. Summary Rule

For tax year 2025:

```text
Form1040.line27a = EIC amount determined under the 2025 eligibility rules, Worksheet A or B, and the 2025 EIC table
Form1040.line27b = clergy / Schedule SE overlap checkbox only
Form1040.line27c = opt-out / instructed-disallowance checkbox
```

And:

```text
Form1040.line32 = Form1040.line27a + Form1040.line28 + Form1040.line29 + Form1040.line30 + Form1040.line31
```
