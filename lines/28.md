# Form 1040 (2025) — Line 28: Additional Child Tax Credit (ACTC) from Schedule 8812

> Source of truth: 2025 Form 1040, the 2025 Instructions for Form 1040, the 2025 Instructions for Schedule 8812 (Form 1040), IRC §24, and the One Big Beautiful Bill Act (OBBBA) 2025 amendments to §24.

---

## 1. Line Identity

**Form 1040 line 28** is:

```text
Additional child tax credit from Schedule 8812
```

Exact mapping:

```text
Form1040.line28 = Schedule8812.line27
```

For 2025, line 28 is a **refundable credit** in the payments section.

It flows as follows:

```text
Form1040.line32 = Form1040.line27a + Form1040.line28 + Form1040.line29 + Form1040.line30 + Form1040.line31
Form1040.line33 = Form1040.line25d + Form1040.line26 + Form1040.line32
```

Line 28 is the **2nd addend** in line 32 (the refundable-credits subtotal).

---

## 2. What Line 28 Is And Is Not

`Line 28` is the **refundable additional child tax credit** computed on Schedule 8812 line 27. It is the spillover of unused nonrefundable CTC, capped at $1,700 per CTC-qualifying child via Part II-A or Part II-B.

It is not:

- the nonrefundable child tax credit (that is Form 1040 line 19),
- the credit for other dependents (`ODC`; nonrefundable; line 19 only),
- a direct count of children times a fixed dollar amount,
- an amount that can be computed independently from the rest of Schedule 8812.

Important 2025 relationship:

```text
Form1040.line19 = Schedule8812.line14   // nonrefundable CTC + ODC
Form1040.line28 = Schedule8812.line27   // refundable ACTC only
```

Only the **CTC portion** can generate ACTC. The `ODC` never produces line 28.

**★ INSEPARABLE from line 19** — a single helper (`computeSchedule8812`) produces both `line14` (→ line 19) and `line27` (→ line 28). See §13 for the implementation.

## 3. Core 2025 Rules

The ACTC for 2025 is available only if the taxpayer:

- has at least one qualifying child for `CTC` purposes,
- cannot use the full child tax credit on `line 19`, and
- satisfies the Schedule 8812 Part II rules.

2025 maximum ACTC amount per qualifying child (IRC §24(h)(5), as adjusted by OBBBA):

```text
maximum ACTC = $1,700 per CTC-qualifying child
```

2025 CTC per qualifying child (IRC §24, as amended by OBBBA 2025):

```text
$2,200 per qualifying child   // OBBBA Act of July 2025 raised the per-child CTC from $2,000 to $2,200 for 2025
```

OBBBA also extends the higher phaseout thresholds. For 2025:

```text
phaseoutThreshold = $400,000 if MFJ else $200,000
phaseoutRate      = $50 per $1,000 of MAGI over the threshold (5%)
```

ODC per other dependent (IRC §24(h)(4)) remains:

```text
$500 per other dependent
```

Important guardrail:

- the `$1,700` ACTC cap is a **ceiling**, not a direct formula for line 28.
- the actual amount still comes from Schedule 8812 Part II-A or Part II-B.

## 4. 2025 SSN And TIN Rules

### 4a. Taxpayer / spouse rule

For 2025, the SSN rule is the OBBBA "one valid SSN" rule.

To claim `CTC` or `ACTC`:

- on a **joint return**, only **one spouse** must have a valid SSN,
- the other spouse must have either an `SSN` or an `ITIN` issued on or before the due date of the return,
- if **neither** spouse has a valid SSN (both have ITINs), `CTC` and `ACTC` are not allowed.

A valid SSN for this purpose must be valid for employment and issued before the due date of the 2025 return, including extensions.

Implementation: enforced as the "G9" ITIN gate inside `computeSchedule8812` — both `you` and `spouse` SSNs are inspected; an identifier whose leading digit is `9` is treated as an ITIN.

### 4b. Qualifying child rule

Each child used for `CTC` / `ACTC` must have a valid SSN by the due date of the return, including extensions.

If a child does not have the required SSN:

- that child cannot be used for `CTC` or `ACTC`,
- that child may still potentially support `ODC` if the timely TIN rules are met.

## 5. Dependents-Section Checkboxes And Schedule 8812 Inputs

For 2025, Schedule 8812 Part I starts from the Form 1040 dependents section.

IRS rule:

```text
Schedule8812.line4 = count of "Child tax credit" boxes checked on Form 1040 / 1040-SR
Schedule8812.line6 = count of "Credit for other dependents" boxes checked on Form 1040 / 1040-SR
```

Important guardrail:

- you cannot check both boxes for the same dependent. The application enforces mutual exclusion via `DependentRecord.qualifiesForCTC()` and `qualifiesForODC()` predicates (per the 19 #5 sub-verification 2 closure).

Those checkbox counts feed the CTC / ODC computation in Part I, which then determines whether any unused CTC can flow into ACTC on line 28.

## 6. Opt-Out Checkbox On Form 1040 Line 28

The 2025 Form 1040 line 28 row includes a box:

```text
If you do not want to claim the ACTC, check here.
```

Important 2025 correction:

- this is a **Form 1040 line-28 opt-out checkbox**.
- it is **not** a Schedule 8812 line-15 election.
- Schedule 8812 line 15 is **reserved for future use** in 2025.

So for 2025:

- if the taxpayer elects not to claim ACTC, the line-28 checkbox is checked and `line 28` remains zero / blank,
- if the taxpayer does want ACTC, the checkbox is left unchecked and `line 28` is determined from Schedule 8812.

Implementation: `ctcScreeningTaxpayer.electsNoActc` is read at the top of `computeSchedule8812`; when `true` the helper short-circuits with `line27 = 0` and the wiring site at `TaxReturnComputeService.java:1885-1894` therefore does not set `payments.additionalChildTaxCredit`. The frontend mirrors the choice into the AcroForm field `unmapped_c2_14_0`.

## 7. Worksheet And Compute Order Requirements

The 2025 instructions explicitly require the return to be completed through certain lines before ACTC is figured.

Before completing Schedule 8812 Part II-A for a Form 1040 filer:

- complete Form 1040 through `line 27a`, and
- complete `Schedule 3 line 11`, if applicable.

This matters because the 2025 ACTC worksheets use:

- Form 1040 `line 27a` (EIC),
- Form 1040 `line 29` (refundable AOTC),
- Schedule 2 lines 5, 6, and 13, and
- Schedule 3 line 11.

Implementation order inside `prepare()`:

1. `computeForm8863` runs first (`TaxReturnComputeService.java:1749`) so that the refundable AOTC is set in `earlyPayments.americanOpportunityCredit` (`line 1774`) before Schedule 8812 reads it for Part II-B line 24.
2. EIC (`computeLine27aEIC`) is precomputed once inside the `earlyPayments` setup block (`line 1766`) so Part II-B line 24 can also read line 27a.
3. `computeSchedule8812` runs at `line 1817`, producing both `line14` (→ line 19) and `line27` (→ line 28).
4. `computeLine31ThroughLine38` (called from `prepare()` at `line 2062`) recomputes EIC into `payments.earnedIncomeCredit` and reads the already-stored ACTC.

Important 2025 guardrail:

- a tax engine should not compute line 28 in isolation before the upstream EIC / Schedule 2 / Schedule 3 / Form 8863 values needed by Schedule 8812 are available.

## 8. Part II-A And Part II-B Structure

For 2025, `line 28` can arise through either:

- Part II-A for all filers, or
- Part II-B for certain filers who have **three or more qualifying children** or are bona fide residents of Puerto Rico.

The trigger predicate (G11 closure 2026-04-20) is the union:

```text
runPartIIB = (numCtcChildren >= 3) OR isBonafidePuertoRicoResident
```

High-level structure:

```text
if Schedule8812.line12 <= Schedule8812.line14:
    line27 = 0     // no unused CTC left over
else:
    line17 = min(line16a, line16b)       // capped at $1,700 × numCtcChildren
    line20 = Part II-A 15% × max(0, earnedIncome − $2,500)
    if runPartIIB:
        line26 = Part II-B payroll-tax-based computation
        line27 = min(line17, max(line20, line26))
    else:
        line27 = min(line17, line20)
```

Important 2025 guardrails:

- Part II-B is **not optional** when its conditions are met.
- the Part II-B comparison uses current 2025 worksheet inputs, including EIC and refundable AOTC pre-set in `earlyPayments`.
- `line 28` is still the final Schedule 8812 line 27 amount regardless of which path produces it.

## 9. Part II-A Earned-Income Worksheet

Schedule 8812 Part II-A treats the unused CTC as refundable up to 15% of earned income over $2,500:

```text
line18a (earnedIncome) = Form 1040 line 1z (wages, salary, tips)
                       + Form 1040 line 1i (nontaxable combat pay election)
line19  = max(0, line18a − $2,500)
line20  = 15% × line19
```

Implementation reads `line 1z` (`form1040.income.totalWages`) and `line 1i` (`form1040.income.nontaxableCombatPayElection`) — the latter is the single source of truth for the combat pay election (see line 1i spec and the EIC line 27a §6).

---

## 10. Part II-B Payroll-Tax Worksheet (3+ children or Puerto Rico)

When `runPartIIB` is true:

```text
line21 = SS withheld (W-2 box 4 of both spouses on MFJ; one spouse otherwise)
       + tier 1 RRTA (when applicable)
       + half of self-employment tax (out of scope — SE not implemented)
line22 = Schedule 2 lines 5 + 6 + 13
line23 = line21 + line22
line24 = Form 1040 line 27a (EIC) + Form 1040 line 29 (refundable AOTC)
         ★ both read from earlyPayments — pre-set at TaxReturnComputeService.java:1766/1774
line25 = max(0, line23 − line24)
line26 = max(line20, line25)
```

Important 2025 guardrail:

- the line 24 inputs must be the **pre-set** values from `earlyPayments`; reading from final `payments` would be circular because `computeLine31ThroughLine38` runs after Schedule 8812.

## 11. Credit Limit Worksheet A Rules

The 2025 Schedule 8812 instructions use `Credit Limit Worksheet A` to bound line 14.

Current 2025 worksheet structure (CLW-A):

```text
wA_1 = Form 1040 line 18 (totalTaxBeforeCredits)
wA_2 = Schedule 3 line 1   (foreign tax credit)
wA_3 = Schedule 3 line 2   (child & dependent care credit)
wA_4 = Schedule 3 line 3   (education credits — nonrefundable LLC/AOTC)
wA_5 = Schedule 3 line 4   (retirement savings contributions credit)
wA_6 = Schedule 3 line 5b  (residential clean energy credit)
wA_7 = Schedule 3 line 6d  (elderly / disabled credit)
wA_8 = Schedule 3 line 6f  (clean vehicle credit)
wA_9 = Schedule 3 line 6m  (previously-owned clean vehicle credit)

worksheetA = max(0, wA_1 − (wA_2 + wA_3 + … + wA_9))
Schedule8812.line14 = min(Schedule8812.line12, worksheetA)
```

When Worksheet B applies (called from Form 8839 — see `lines/30.md` §8), it references the same Schedule 3 lines plus the Schedule 8812 line 14 result.

So a 2025 implementation must follow the current worksheet line references, not older 2024 mappings.

## 12. Form 2555 And Puerto Rico Rules

### 12a. Form 2555 block

If **either spouse** files Form 2555, ACTC is blocked:

```text
if Form2555Taxpayer present OR Form2555Spouse present:
    Form1040.line28 = 0
```

Implementation: `computeSchedule8812` receives both `form2555Taxpayer` and `form2555Spouse`. The call site at `TaxReturnComputeService.java:1777-1811` applies a defensive null-shadow on MFS to prevent the spouse's Form 2555 from leaking into the taxpayer's separate return (19 #1 closure 2026-05-14).

### 12b. Bona fide residents of Puerto Rico

For 2025, bona fide residents of Puerto Rico may claim ACTC via Part II-B if they had **at least one qualifying child** — the old "3 or more" gate no longer controls Puerto Rico eligibility (G11 closure 2026-04-20).

Implementation: the Part II-B trigger is

```text
runPartIIB = (numCtcChildren >= 3) OR isBonafidePuertoRicoResident
```

so a Puerto Rico bona fide resident with 1 or 2 qualifying children runs Part II-B even though they do not meet the 3-child gate.

## 14. Form 8862 And Improper-Claim Rules

For 2025, `Form 8862` may be required to claim `CTC`, `ACTC`, or `ODC` if one of those credits was denied or reduced for a year after 2015 for a reason other than a math or clerical error.

Important line-28 implication:

- if `Form 8862` is required and not properly filed, the return cannot allow the ACTC claim reflected on `line 28`. The application emits `FORM_8862_CTC_REQUIRED` as a blocking flag.

---

## 15. Refund Timing (PATH Act)

If a return properly claims `ACTC`, the IRS cannot issue the refund before **mid-February 2026**.

The hold applies to the **entire refund**, not just the portion attributable to line 28.

This is a filing / refund-timing rule, not a compute adjustment to line 28.

## 16. Validations / Flags

| Condition | Action |
|---|---|
| `electsNoActc = true` | Schedule 8812 short-circuits; line 28 not set |
| `ctcPreviouslyDenied` without valid Form 8862 | Emit `FORM_8862_CTC_REQUIRED` blocking flag; line 28 = 0 |
| Both spouses on MFJ have ITINs | G9 gate; line 28 = 0 |
| Either spouse files Form 2555 | Line 28 = 0 (IRC §24(d)(5)(B)) |
| `numCtcChildren == 0` | Only ODC may apply on line 19; line 28 = 0 |
| `MAGI ≥ phaseoutFullEliminationPoint` | Tentative credit fully phased out; line 28 = 0 |
| `actc > 0` | `payments.setAdditionalChildTaxCredit(actc)` |

Structural invariants:

- `line 28 ≥ 0`
- `line 28 ≤ $1,700 × numCtcChildren`
- `line 28 + line 19 ≤ line5 + line7` (total credits cannot exceed tentative credit)

## 17. Summary Rule

For tax year 2025:

```text
Form1040.line28 = Schedule8812.line27
```

Subject to these key 2025 rules:

- only unused CTC can become ACTC, never ODC,
- CTC per qualifying child is `$2,200` (OBBBA 2025),
- maximum ACTC is `$1,700` per qualifying child,
- the line-28 opt-out checkbox lives on Form 1040 (`unmapped_c2_14_0`); Schedule 8812 line 15 is reserved,
- on a joint return, only one spouse must have a valid SSN (the other may have a timely SSN or ITIN); both ITINs disqualifies,
- each child used for ACTC must still have a valid SSN,
- Form 2555 by **either** spouse blocks ACTC,
- Part II-B runs when `numCtcChildren ≥ 3` **OR** the filer is a bona fide resident of Puerto Rico,
- line 28 flows into line 32, then line 33.
