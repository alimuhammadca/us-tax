# Form 1040 (2025) — Line 29: Refundable American Opportunity Credit (AOTC) from Form 8863 line 8

> Source of truth: 2025 Form 1040, the 2025 Instructions for Form 1040, the 2025 Form 8863 and its instructions, IRC §25A, and Pub. 970.

---

## 1. Line Identity

**Form 1040 line 29** is:

```text
American opportunity credit from Form 8863, line 8
```

Exact mapping:

```text
Form1040.line29 = Form8863.line8
```

For 2025, line 29 is a **refundable credit** in the payments section (the 40% refundable portion of the AOTC).

It flows as follows:

```text
Form1040.line32 = Form1040.line27a + Form1040.line28 + Form1040.line29 + Form1040.line30 + Form1040.line31
Form1040.line33 = Form1040.line25d + Form1040.line26 + Form1040.line32
```

Line 29 is the **3rd addend** in line 32.

Important 2025 guardrail:

- line 29 is included **once** through line 32; it must not be added again separately on line 33.

---

## 2. What Line 29 Is And Is Not

`Line 29` is only the **refundable portion** of the American Opportunity Credit (AOTC) — Form 8863 line 8.

It is not:

- the full education-credit amount from Form 8863,
- the nonrefundable AOTC portion (which contributes to Form 8863 line 19),
- the Lifetime Learning Credit (LLC; nonrefundable only),
- a direct per-student fixed-dollar amount.

Important 2025 relationship:

```text
Form1040.line29  = Form8863.line8                 // refundable AOTC only
Schedule3.line3  = Form8863.line19                // nonrefundable education credits (AOTC remainder + LLC)
Form1040.line20  = Schedule3.line8                // includes Schedule 3 line 3 in the total
```

★ INSEPARABLE from line 20 — a single helper (`computeForm8863`) produces both. See §13.

So the refundable and nonrefundable education-credit paths must not be collapsed into one number.

---

## 3. Core 2025 Structure

The 2025 AOTC is computed on Form 8863 and then split between:

- a **refundable** part on `line 8`, and
- a **nonrefundable** part that contributes to `line 19` and then flows to Schedule 3 line 3 → Form 1040 line 20.

High-level structure:

```text
Part III lines 27-31 compute per-student AOTC amounts
Part I  lines 1-8    compute refundable AOTC
Part II lines 9-19   compute nonrefundable education credits (AOTC remainder + LLC)
Form1040.line29 = Form8863.line8
```

Important 2025 guardrail:

- Form 1040 line 29 must be derived from the finalized Form 8863 result, not recomputed independently on Form 1040.

---

## 4. 2025 AOTC Amount Rules

For 2025, the American opportunity credit is worth:

```text
100% of the first $2,000 of adjusted qualified education expenses
+ 25% of the next $2,000
```

So the maximum AOTC per eligible student is:

```text
$2,500 per student
```

After the phaseout calculation:

```text
Form8863.line7 = (sum of per-student line30) × phaseoutFraction
Form8863.line8 = 40% × line7        // refundable portion — UNLESS under-24 restriction (§6)
Form8863.line9 = line7 - line8      // remainder that continues into the nonrefundable path
```

Important guardrail:

- the refundable portion is normally **40%**, but it can be forced to zero by the special under-age-24 rule. When that restriction applies, `line8 = 0` and the full `line7` flows to the nonrefundable line 19.

## 5. 2025 MAGI Phaseout

For 2025, the American opportunity credit phaseout thresholds are:

| Filing status | No credit at or above | Phaseout band |
|---|---|---|
| `MFJ` | `$180,000` | `$160,000` to `< $180,000` |
| `Single` / `HOH` / `QSS` | `$90,000` | `$80,000` to `< $90,000` |

★ **G1 closure (2026-04-20)** — QSS uses the Single/HOH ceiling (`$90,000`), not the MFJ ceiling. The `isMfj` flag in the implementation is strict (Married filing jointly only); QSS falls through to the Single bucket.

On the 2025 form:

```text
Form8863.line2 = $180,000 if MFJ, or $90,000 otherwise
Form8863.line3 = MAGI
Form8863.line4 = line2 − line3
Form8863.line5 = $20,000 if MFJ, or $10,000 otherwise
Form8863.line6 = clamp([0,1], line4 / line5), HALF_UP-rounded to 3 decimal places
Form8863.line7 = line1TotalAotcBeforePhaseout × line6
```

★ **G-NEW-3 closure** — the rounding mode for line 6 is `HALF_UP` to 3 decimals (corrected from the earlier `ROUND_DOWN` per the 2025 Form 8863 line 6 instructions).

MAGI for 2025 generally starts from Form 1040 line 11b, with required add-backs for:

```text
+ magiAddBackForm2555ExcludedIncome       (Form 2555 line 45)
+ magiAddBackForm2555HousingExclusion     (Form 2555 line 50)
+ magiAddBackPuertoRicoExcludedIncome
+ magiAddBackForm4563ExcludedIncome       (American Samoa)
```

★ **G-NEW-12 closure** — when Form 2555 is filed, the application auto-fills `magiAddBackForm2555ExcludedIncome` and `magiAddBackForm2555HousingExclusion` from the computed `Form2555` object instead of relying on a manual entry on the personal form.

## 6. Under-Age-24 Refundable Restriction

For 2025, the refundable AOTC is blocked if all required conditions in the Form 8863 line 7 instructions are met.

The taxpayer does **not** qualify for the refundable AOTC if:

- they were:
  - under age 18 at the end of 2025, or
  - age 18 at the end of 2025 and earned income was less than half of support, or
  - over age 18 and under age 24 at the end of 2025, were a full-time student, and earned income was less than half of support, **and**
- at least one parent was alive at the end of 2025, **and**
- they are not filing a joint return for 2025.

If these conditions apply:

```text
skip Form8863.line8
enter Form8863.line7 on Form8863.line9
check the Form 8863 line-7 box
Form1040.line29 = 0
```

Implementation: a single self-reported boolean `refundableAotcRestrictionApplies` on the personal form forces `line8 = 0` and routes the full `line7` to the nonrefundable line 19. (G4 — deferred end-to-end derivation; the substantive eligibility test is self-attested.)

Important 2025 correction:

- this rule is not just an "under 24" rule in the abstract; it also depends on the parent-alive and joint-return conditions.

## 7. Who Cannot Claim The Education Credits

For 2025, the taxpayer cannot claim an education credit on the return if any of the following applies:

- taxpayer is claimed as a dependent on another person's return (G-NEW-5; IRC §25A(g)(3)),
- filing status is `MFS` (no exception path),
- taxpayer or spouse was a nonresident alien for 2025 and did not elect resident treatment where required (G-NEW-10; IRC §25A(g)(7)),
- taxpayer's TIN was not issued by the due date of the return including extensions (G-NEW-4; IRC §25A(g)(1)),
- MAGI is at or above the applicable ceiling,
- required TIN rules are not met for any claimed student.

Implementation enforces these as ordered hard gates inside `computeForm8863` (see §13):

| Order | Gate | Effect |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | `claimsEducationCreditsOnReturn != true` | return null silently |
| 2 | `claimedAsDependentOnAnotherReturn = true` | emit `EDUCATION_CREDITS_TAXPAYER_CLAIMED_AS_DEPENDENT` blocking; return null |
| 3 | `taxpayerOrSpouseDisqualifiedAsNonresidentAlien = true` | emit `EDUCATION_CREDITS_NONRESIDENT_ALIEN_INELIGIBLE` blocking; return null |
| 4 | `taxpayerTinIssuedByDueDate = false` | emit `EDUCATION_CREDITS_TAXPAYER_TIN_NOT_BY_DUE_DATE` blocking; return null |
| 5 | Form 8862 AOTC gate (when `aotcPreviouslyDenied = true`) | blocks AOTC for all students; LLC continues |
| 6 | 1098-T advisory (G-NEW-7) | emit `AOTC_1098T_NOT_RECEIVED_NO_ALTERNATIVE` non-blocking when no 1098-T and no alternative-docs election |
| 7 | MFS hard-disqualify | emit `EDUCATION_CREDITS_MFS_INELIGIBLE` blocking; return null (no exception path — distinct from EIC line 27a's MFS-with-exception remap) |

★ Convention 7 (MFS HARD-DISQUALIFY) is **unique** to line 29 in the workflow — it has no exception path.

## 8. TIN Rules For 2025

For 2025, both the filer and the student have TIN rules.

- If the taxpayer has not been issued a `TIN` by the due date of the 2025 return, including extensions, the AOTC cannot be claimed on either the original or an amended return.
- The AOTC also is not allowed for a student who has not been issued a `TIN` by that due date.

Important guardrail:

- this is stricter than a generic later-cure rule; getting the TIN after the due date does not revive the 2025 AOTC on an amended return.

The taxpayer-TIN check is enforced at the structural-gate level (`taxpayerTinIssuedByDueDate`). The per-student TIN check is captured in the per-student `studentTinLine21` field.

---

## 9. Form 1098-T And EIN Rules

For 2025, the general rule is that a taxpayer (or dependent) must have received Form 1098-T from the eligible educational institution to claim the education credit.

But the 2025 instructions also recognize exceptions, including cases where:

- the institution was not required to furnish Form 1098-T, or
- the institution was required to furnish it but did not do so before filing and the taxpayer followed the required request / cooperation steps and can substantiate enrollment and qualified expenses.

★ **G-NEW-7 closure** — the application emits `AOTC_1098T_NOT_RECEIVED_NO_ALTERNATIVE` (non-blocking) only when **both** of the following are true: `receivedAny1098TForClaimedStudents = false` **and** `usingAlternativeEnrollmentDocumentationForAnyStudent != true`. The advisory does not change the credit dollar amount; it nudges the user to supply documentation.

Important 2025 guardrails:

- do not overstate this as an absolute "1098-T always required with no exceptions" rule,
- for AOTC, the institution's `EIN` must be provided on Form 8863 Part III line 22 (G19/G20 closure).

## 10. Student-Level AOTC Eligibility

For 2025, a student qualifies for AOTC only if all required AOTC conditions are met, including:

- not having completed the first 4 years of postsecondary education before 2025 (`studentCompletedFirstFourYearsBefore2025Line25 = false`),
- AOTC not having been claimed for that student for 4 prior tax years (`aotcClaimedFourPriorYearsLine23 = false`),
- at least half-time enrollment for at least one academic period beginning in 2025 (`studentWasAtLeastHalfTimeLine24 = true`),
- no disqualifying felony drug conviction by the end of 2025 (`studentHadFelonyDrugConvictionLine26 = false`),
- required TIN and Form 1098-T / exception requirements per §9.

Important guardrail:

- a student disqualified from AOTC may still potentially be considered for LLC, but the same student cannot generate both credits for the same year. The `creditTypeRequested` per-student field (`"aotc"` or `"llc"`) enforces the choice.

---

## 11. Qualified Education Expenses

For 2025, AOTC qualified expenses generally include:

- tuition,
- required fees,
- course materials (books, supplies, equipment) needed for a course of study, whether or not purchased from the institution.

They do **not** include:

- room and board,
- insurance,
- medical expenses,
- transportation,
- similar personal, living, or family expenses.

Expenses must be adjusted for tax-free educational assistance and related coordination rules before the credit is computed. The per-student field is `adjustedQualifiedEducationExpenses`.

## 12. Form 8862 After Disallowance

For 2025, Form 8862 is required to claim AOTC if AOTC for a year after 2015 was denied or reduced for any reason other than a math or clerical error.

Implementation:

- when `aotcPreviouslyDenied = true`, the helper requires a valid `Form8862` with `claimsAOTC = true`,
- the per-student `Form8862.aotcStudentEligible` list controls which students can claim AOTC (★ Convention 6 — UNIQUE per-student granularity in the workflow),
- LLC is **not** affected by an AOTC Form 8862 gate; LLC continues.

If Form 8862 is required and not properly filed, `FORM_8862_AOTC_REQUIRED` is emitted (blocking) and AOTC is blocked for all students; LLC continues.

## 13. Credit Limit Worksheet And Nonrefundable Path

The 2025 Form 8863 uses a `Credit Limit Worksheet` for the nonrefundable education-credit path.

Current 2025 worksheet references:

```text
Worksheet line 4 = Form 1040 line 18 (totalTaxBeforeCredits)
Worksheet line 5 = total of Schedule 3 lines 1, 2, 6d, and 6l
Worksheet line 7 = smaller of worksheet lines 3 or 6
Form8863.line19 = worksheet line 7
```

The 2025 worksheet specifically references the listed Schedule 3 lines — not a broad grab-bag of every currently implemented nonrefundable credit.

This matters because stale worksheet references can incorrectly reduce or inflate the nonrefundable education-credit path. Line 19 then flows to Schedule 3 line 3 → Form 1040 line 20.

## 17. Summary Rule

For tax year 2025:

```text
Form1040.line29 = Form8863.line8
```

Subject to these key 2025 rules:

- line 29 is only the refundable AOTC,
- the normal refundable share is 40% of allowable AOTC,
- the refundable amount is forced to zero if the 2025 under-age-24 restriction applies (entire amount flows nonrefundable to line 19 → line 20),
- MFS filers cannot claim the education credits (no exception path),
- QSS uses Single thresholds (`$80k–$90k`, `$10k` range), MFJ uses `$160k–$180k` (`$20k` range),
- taxpayer and student TIN rules must be met by the due date,
- Form 1098-T is generally required but there are explicit IRS exceptions,
- line 29 flows into line 32, then line 33.
