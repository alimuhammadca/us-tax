# Form 1040 (2025) — Line 30: Refundable Adoption Credit from Form 8839 line 13

> Source of truth: 2025 Form 1040, the 2025 Form 8839, the 2025 Instructions for Form 8839, IRC §23, IRC §137 (employer-provided adoption assistance), and the One Big Beautiful Bill Act (OBBBA) 2025 amendments creating the refundable portion.

---

## 1. Line Identity

**Form 1040 line 30** is:

```text
Refundable adoption credit from Form 8839, line 13
```

Exact mapping:

```text
Form1040.line30 = Form8839.line13   // (= Form8839.part2Line13RefundableAdoptionCredit)
```

★ **NEW for tax year 2025** per the OBBBA Act 2025 — prior tax years (2024 and earlier) had no refundable adoption credit and line 30 was reserved.

For 2025, line 30 is a **refundable credit** in the payments section.

It flows as follows:

```text
Form1040.line32 = Form1040.line27a + Form1040.line28 + Form1040.line29 + Form1040.line30 + Form1040.line31
Form1040.line33 = Form1040.line25d + Form1040.line26 + Form1040.line32
```

Line 30 is the **4th addend** in line 32.

Important guardrail:

- line 30 is included once through line 32; it is not added separately again on line 33.

---

## 2. What Line 30 Is And Is Not

`Line 30` is only the **refundable** portion of the 2025 adoption credit.

It is not:

- the entire Form 8839 adoption credit,
- the nonrefundable adoption credit (that is Schedule 3 line 6c → Form 1040 line 20),
- the employer-provided adoption benefits exclusion (that produces taxable income on Form 1040 line 1f),
- a carryforward from prior years.

★ **Most co-outputs of any audited line** — line 30 is one of **three** co-outputs from a single Form 8839 computation:

```text
Form8839.line13 (refundable)              → Form1040.line30                  // ★ THIS LINE
Form8839.line18 (nonrefundable)           → Schedule3.line6c → Form1040.line20
Form8839.line31 (taxable employer-provided
                 adoption benefits)        → Form1040.line1f
```

Important 2025 rule:

- any prior-year adoption credit carryforward remains **nonrefundable** (line 15 → line 16) and cannot become part of line 30.

## 3. Core 2025 Structure

For 2025, Form 8839 splits the adoption-credit path into three pieces:

- **refundable** credit on `line 13` (NEW for 2025 per OBBBA), and
- **nonrefundable** credit on `line 18`, and
- **employer-provided benefits exclusion** in Part III producing taxable line 31.

High-level structure:

```text
Part II per-child loop (lines 2-11b):
    Line 2  = $17,280 maximum credit per child
    Line 3  = prior-year amount already used for the same child
    Line 4  = line 2 − line 3
    Line 5  = qualified adoption expenses (or $17,280 if domestic special-needs final 2025)
    Line 6  = min(line 4, line 5)        // credit base
    Line 10 = line 6 × phaseoutFraction
    Line 11a = max(0, line 6 − line 10)  // post-phaseout
    Line 11b = min(line 11a, $5,000)     // ★ refundable per-child cap (NEW for 2025 per OBBBA)

Part II aggregation (lines 11c-18):
    Line 11c = sum(per-child line 11b)
    Line 12  = sum(per-child line 11a)
    Line 13  = line 11c                  → Form1040.line30
    Line 14  = max(0, line 12 − line 13)
    Line 15  = prior-year nonrefundable carryforward
    Line 16  = line 14 + line 15
    Line 17  = Credit Limit Worksheet (or Worksheet B from Schedule 8812 instructions)
    Line 18  = min(line 16, line 17)     → Schedule3.line6c → Form1040.line20

Part III (employer-provided adoption benefits exclusion; lines 19-31):
    Line 23  = total W-2 box 12 code T benefits (SSN-filtered by isMfsReturn)
    Line 27  = phaseoutFraction (same as Part II)
    Line 30  = total excluded benefits (per-child cap $17,280 each)
    Line 31  = max(0, line 23 − line 30) → Form1040.line1f
```

Important 2025 guardrails:

- if employer-provided adoption benefits are involved (Part III line 23 > 0), Part III generally must be completed before Part II because reimbursed employer-paid expenses are not qualified adoption expenses for the credit;
- the instructions preserve a special-needs exception to that general sequencing rule.

## 4. Top-Level Formula

For tax year 2025:

```text
Form8839.line13 = Form8839.line11c
Form1040.line30 = Form8839.line13
```

And per child:

```text
Form8839.line11b (per child) = min(Form8839.line11a, $5,000)    // ★ NEW for 2025 per OBBBA
Form8839.line11c              = sum of all per-child line11b amounts
```

So the refundable credit is:

```text
Form1040.line30 = sum over eligible children of min(line11a_child, $5,000)
```

Important 2025 rule:

- the refundable cap is applied **per eligible child**, not once per return.

## 5. Per-Child Part II Rules

For each eligible child on Form 8839 Part II:

```text
Line 2  = $17,280 maximum credit per child
Line 3  = prior-year amount already used for the same child
Line 4  = line 2 − line 3
Line 5  = qualified adoption expenses for the child, subject to timing rules
Line 6  = smaller of line 4 or line 5
Line 10 = line 6 × phaseoutFraction       // §6 below
Line 11a = max(0, line 6 − line 10)
Line 11b = min(line 11a, $5,000)          // ★ refundable cap (OBBBA 2025)
```

Important 2025 line-5 rules:

- enter 2024 expenses if the adoption was not final by the end of 2025,
- enter 2024 and 2025 expenses if the adoption became final in 2025,
- enter 2025 expenses if the adoption became final before 2025,
- do **not** include expenses reimbursed by an employer under a written qualified adoption assistance program.

Special-needs rule (G-NEW-5 closure):

- if the taxpayer adopted a **U.S. child with special needs** and the adoption became final in 2025, line 5 is forced to `$17,280` (regardless of actual expenses, even zero). This allows the full current-year credit base for domestic special-needs adoptions.

Foreign-child rule:

- special rules apply for foreign children; the timing of when expenses become creditable differs from domestic adoptions, and the adoption generally must be final before the credit or exclusion is allowed (`adoptionFinal2025OrEarlier` flag).

More-than-six-children rule (G-NEW-1):

- the intake form supports up to 6 children. If more than 6 are claimed, an advisory directs the user to attach an additional Form 8839 per the 2025 IRS instructions ("more than 3" rule).

## 6. MAGI Phaseout

For 2025:

```text
Form8839.line7 = MAGI
Form8839.line8 = max(0, line7 − $259,190)
Form8839.line9 = min(1.000, line8 / $40,000)
phaseoutFraction = (1 − line9), clamped to [0, 1]
```

So the 2025 phaseout range is:

- starts at `$259,190`,
- fully phased out at `$299,190` (= $259,190 + $40,000).

For 2025 Form 8839 line 7, MAGI starts from Form 1040 line 11b and is increased by:

```text
+ Form 2555 line 45 (foreign earned income exclusion)
+ Form 2555 line 50 (foreign housing exclusion — employee)
+ Puerto Rico excluded income
+ Form 4563 line 15 (American Samoa)
+ Schedule 1 line 21 (student-loan interest deduction)        // 1f Gap #8 closure 2026-05-24
+ Schedule B line 3   (Series EE/I bond interest exclusion)   // 1f Gap #8 closure 2026-05-24
+ Schedule 1 line 24j (foreign-housing deduction — SE)        // 1f Gap #8 closure 2026-05-24
```

★ **G1 closure (2026-04-20)** — the application auto-derives MAGI from these upstream computed objects when the manual `magiForAdoptionBenefitsExclusion` field is absent. See `applyAdoptionCredit` at `TaxReturnComputeService.java:22289-22320`. The auto-MAGI also feeds the manual-vs-auto cross-check that emits `ADOPTION_MAGI_MANUAL_VS_AUTO_MISMATCH` (advisory) when the two values diverge by more than $100 (G-NEW-2 closure 2026-06-11).

## 7. Refundable vs. Nonrefundable Split

For 2025:

```text
Line 12 = total of all line 11a amounts          (per-child post-phaseout BEFORE refundable cap)
Line 13 = line 11c refundable adoption credit    → Form 1040 line 30
Line 14 = max(0, line 12 − line 13)              (carry into nonrefundable bucket)
Line 15 = prior-year adoption credit carryforward
Line 16 = line 14 + line 15
Line 17 = Credit Limit Worksheet result          (§8)
Line 18 = min(line 16, line 17)                  → Schedule 3 line 6c → Form 1040 line 20
```

Important 2025 rules:

- the refundable portion is only the current-year line-11b/11c amount,
- prior-year carryforwards enter on line 15 and stay in the **nonrefundable** path,
- unused nonrefundable credit may carry forward, but it does not become refundable later.

## 8. Credit Limit Worksheet (Line 17)

For 2025, Form 8839 line 17 is not discretionary. It is the amount from the Credit Limit Worksheet (CLW) in the Form 8839 instructions.

Current 2025 worksheet structure (CLW-B):

```text
Worksheet line 1 = Form 8839 line 16
Worksheet line 2 = Form 1040 line 18 (totalTaxBeforeCredits)
Worksheet line 3 = total of Form 1040 line 19 (CTC/ODC)
                 + Schedule 3 lines 1, 2, 3, 4, 5b, 6d, 6f, and 6m
Worksheet line 4 = worksheet line 2 − worksheet line 3
Worksheet line 5 = smaller of worksheet lines 1 or 4 = Form 8839 line 17
```

★ **G2/G3 closure** — Worksheet line 3 now includes Schedule 3 lines `1 + 2 + 3 + 4 + 5b + 6d + 6f + 6m` (current 2025 references). Earlier versions used a smaller, hand-picked subset that omitted line 3 (education credits) and line 6m (previously-owned clean vehicles); the audit replaced that with the full current 2025 set.

Special 2025 note:

- if the taxpayer is directed to complete Form 8839 through **Credit Limit Worksheet B** in the Schedule 8812 instructions, the worksheet substitutes line 14 of Credit Limit Worksheet B for Form 1040 line 19. The application reads `schedule8812.creditLimitWorksheetBLine14` when non-null (G7 partial).

## 9. Filing Status Rules

For 2025, the taxpayer may take the adoption credit or exclusion if all required conditions are met, including:

- filing status is `Single`, `HOH`, `QSS`, or `MFJ`, or
- the taxpayer is married but qualifies under the `Married Persons Not Filing Jointly` exception.

The 2025 exception requires all of the following:

- the general MAGI and child-information requirements are met,
- the taxpayer lived apart from the spouse during the last 6 months of 2025,
- the eligible child lived in the taxpayer's home more than half of 2025,
- the taxpayer provided over half the cost of keeping up the home.

★ **G4 closure (2026-04-20)** — for the **refundable Part II credit** (line 30) AND the Part III employer-benefits exclusion, the MFS exception is NOT honored; MFS filers are hard-blocked via `ADOPTION_BENEFITS_MFS_EXCLUSION_DISALLOWED` (BLOCKING). This was non-blocking before the fix. The Part II credit is similarly zeroed on MFS. Implementation: the MFS check inside `computeAdoptionBenefits` returns 0 for the refundable credit.

## 10. More Than Three Eligible Children

For 2025, the IRS form itself has three child columns, but the tax-law rule is broader.

If the taxpayer adopted or tried to adopt more than three eligible children:

- attach as many Forms 8839 as needed,
- enter `See Attached` by the caution below line 1,
- for Part II, complete child-level lines `2-6`, `10`, `11a`, and `11b` for each child,
- complete return-level lines `7-9`, `11c`, and `12-18` on only one Form 8839 using the combined totals.

The application supports up to 6 children in the intake form (`adoption-expenses`); if more are claimed, the advisory G-NEW-1 directs the user to attach additional Forms 8839 per the IRS rule.

## 12. Project Implementation Notes — Split-Stage Helper Pattern

These are implementation notes for this repo, not substantive IRS law.

★ **SPLIT-STAGE GATED CREDIT** — line 30 is the first audit with **two helper methods sharing state** via the `AdoptionComputation` object:

| Stage | Method | Location | When | Produces |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 (early) | `computeAdoptionBenefits(adoptionData, w2Entries, filingStatus, you, spouse, isMfsReturn, flags)` | `TaxReturnComputeService.java:18464` | called from `prepare()` at `line 424` BEFORE AGI is computed | `AdoptionComputation.refundableAdoptionCredit` (→ line 30), `AdoptionComputation.line1f` (→ line 1f), partial Form8839 model (Part II per-child + Part III computed) |
| 2 (later) | `applyAdoptionCredit(schedule3, adoption, form1040, schedule8812, form2555Taxpayer, form2555Spouse, puertoRicoExcludedIncome, form4563ExcludedIncome, schedule1, scheduleB, flags)` | `TaxReturnComputeService.java:22263` | called from `prepare()` at `line 1929` AFTER Schedule 3 lines 1–6b finalized | `Form8839.part2Line17CreditLimit` (CLW-B result), `Form8839.part2Line18NonrefundableAdoptionCredit` (→ Sched 3 line 6c → line 20). May also recompute the refundable line 13 when MAGI is auto-derived. |

Final line 30 wiring (`computeLine31ThroughLine38` at `TaxReturnComputeService.java:27236-27250`):

```java
if (adoption != null) {
    BigDecimal refundableCredit = null;
    if (adoption.form8839() != null
            && adoption.form8839().getPart2Line13RefundableAdoptionCredit() != null) {
        refundableCredit = adoption.form8839().getPart2Line13RefundableAdoptionCredit();
    } else if (adoption.adoptionCredit() != null) {
        refundableCredit = adoption.adoptionCredit();
    }
    if (hasPositiveAmount(refundableCredit)) {
        payments.setRefundableAdoptionCredit(refundableCredit);
    }
}
```

— preferring the `form8839.part2Line13` value if `applyAdoptionCredit` updated it via the G1 auto-MAGI recompute, otherwise falling back to the early-stage `adoption.adoptionCredit()`.

Other implementation notes:

- the project computes `Form8839.part2Line13RefundableAdoptionCredit` in `computeAdoptionBenefits()` and wires it to `Payments.refundableAdoptionCredit` → Form 1040 line 30,
- the nonrefundable adoption credit (line 18) is computed by `applyAdoptionCredit()` via Credit Limit Worksheet B and wired to `Schedule3NonrefundableCredits.adoptionCredit` → Schedule 3 line 6c → Form 1040 line 20,
- per-child Part III employer-benefits exclusion (lines 24–31) for up to 6 children is computed in `computeAdoptionBenefits` and flows to Form 1040 line 1f via `AdoptionComputation.line1f()`.

## 13. Statement Input — W-2 Box 12 Code T (Convention 5)

Part III line 23 is the total of employer-provided adoption benefits reported on W-2 box 12 code T across the appropriate spouses.

Implementation (`computeAdoptionBenefits`):

```java
sumW2AdoptionBenefitsForSsns(w2Entries, taxpayerSsn)                // isMfsReturn = true
sumW2AdoptionBenefitsForSsns(w2Entries, taxpayerSsn, spouseSsn)     // isMfsReturn = false
```

★ **Convention 5 — SSN-FILTERED STATEMENT AGGREGATION via `isMfsReturn`** — mirrors the line 1f.xlsx Issue #2 closure pattern. On MFS the spouse's W-2 box 12 code T must not aggregate into the taxpayer's own separate return; on all other filing statuses (including MFJ) both spouses' W-2s are summed.

## 16. Summary Rule

For tax year 2025:

```text
Form1040.line30 = Form8839.line13 = Form8839.line11c
```

Subject to these key 2025 rules:

- line 30 is only the **refundable** adoption credit (NEW for 2025 per OBBBA),
- the refundable amount is computed per child through `line 11b`, capped at `$5,000` per child,
- the current-year maximum adoption credit / exclusion amount is `$17,280` per child,
- the 2025 phaseout starts at `$259,190` MAGI and ends at `$299,190`,
- employer-reimbursed expenses are not qualified adoption expenses for the credit,
- prior-year carryforwards stay nonrefundable (line 15 → line 16, never line 13),
- MFS filers are hard-blocked from both Part II credit and Part III exclusion (G4 fix),
- more than three children require attached Forms 8839 under the IRS instructions,
- W-2 box 12 code T benefits are SSN-filtered by `isMfsReturn` (Convention 5),
- the helper is split-stage: `computeAdoptionBenefits` (early) + `applyAdoptionCredit` (later),
- line 30 is one of three co-outputs (also: Sched 3 line 6c → line 20; line 1f),
- line 30 flows into line 32, then line 33.
