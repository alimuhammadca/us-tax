# Form 1040 (2025) — Line 31: Amount From Schedule 3, Line 15

> Source of truth: 2025 Form 1040, 2025 Schedule 3, and the official 2025 instructions for the underlying source forms (Form 8962, Form 4868, Form 4136, Form 2439, IRC §1341, Form 3800, Notice 2018-39 for §965, etc.).

---

## 1. Line Identity

**Form 1040 line 31** is:

```text
Amount from Schedule 3, line 15
```

Exact mapping:

```text
Form1040.line31 = Schedule3.line15
```

For 2025, line 31 is part of the payments section.

It flows as follows:

```text
Form1040.line32 = Form1040.line27a + Form1040.line28 + Form1040.line29 + Form1040.line30 + Form1040.line31
Form1040.line33 = Form1040.line25d + Form1040.line26 + Form1040.line32
```

Line 31 is the **5th (final) addend** in line 32.

Important guardrail:

- line 31 is a **pure pass-through** from Schedule 3 line 15; it is not computed independently on Form 1040.

★ **STRUCTURALLY SIMPLEST payments-section computation in the workflow** — even simpler than line 25d (which was a 3-addend pure-sum). Line 31 is a single direct read with a null-when-zero gate.

## 2. Top-Level Formula

For tax year 2025:

```text
Schedule3.line15 = Schedule3.line9 + Schedule3.line10 + Schedule3.line11 + Schedule3.line12 + Schedule3.line14
Form1040.line31 = Schedule3.line15
```

And:

```text
Schedule3.line14 = Schedule3.line13a + Schedule3.line13b + Schedule3.line13c + Schedule3.line13d + Schedule3.line13z
```

So in expanded form:

```text
Form1040.line31 = line9 + line10 + line11 + line12 + line13a + line13b + line13c + line13d + line13z
```

★ **G-NEW-1 closure** — line 13c is included in the line 14 sum (it was previously omitted because Form 3800 is out of scope; the field path remains supported as a manual entry on the `31-other-payments` personal form).

Important 2025 rule:

- only **Schedule 3 Part II** feeds line 31;
- nonrefundable Schedule 3 credits from Part I flow to **Form 1040 line 20**, not line 31.

## 3. Schedule 3 Part II Structure For 2025

The 2025 printed Schedule 3 Part II is:

```text
Line 9   Net premium tax credit (from Form 8962)
Line 10  Amount paid with request for extension to file (Form 4868)
Line 11  Excess social security and tier 1 RRTA tax withheld
Line 12  Credit for federal tax on fuels (Form 4136)
Line 13a Form 2439
Line 13b Section 1341 credit for repayment of amounts included in income from earlier years
Line 13c Net elective payment election amount from Form 3800 Part III line 6 column (j)
Line 13d Deferred amount of net 965 tax liability
Line 13z Other refundable credits (write-in items)
Line 14  Total other payments or refundable credits (= line13a + 13b + 13c + 13d + 13z)
Line 15  Add lines 9 through 12 and 14                                  → Form 1040 line 31
```

Important guardrail:

- line 15 includes line 14 once; do not sum 13a–13z again separately after line 14 has already been formed.

## 5. Line 9: Net Premium Tax Credit

For 2025, Schedule 3 line 9 is the **net premium tax credit** from Form 8962.

Refundable path:

```text
If Form8962.line24 > Form8962.line25,
    Form8962.line26 = Form8962.line24 − Form8962.line25
    Schedule3.line9 = Form8962.line26
```

Repayment path:

```text
If Form8962.line25 > Form8962.line24,
    repayment does NOT go to line 31.
    It goes to Schedule 2 line 1a through Form 8962 Part III.
```

Important 2025 rule:

- the enhanced PTC rules still apply for 2025, so the old 400%-of-FPL cliff does not control the credit calculation in the way it did before the temporary law changes.

Important eligibility guardrail:

- married filing separately taxpayers generally cannot claim PTC, except for the statutory exceptions (domestic abuse / spousal abandonment) handled inside Form 8962.

## 6. Line 10: Amount Paid With Request For Extension To File

For 2025, Schedule 3 line 10 includes qualifying extension payments, such as:

- a payment with `Form 4868`,
- an electronic extension payment that serves as the extension request,
- a qualifying payment with `Form 2350`.

Important guardrails:

- this is a **payment** line, not an extension-approval line,
- payment-processing fees are not part of the creditable amount,
- an extension of time to file does not itself extend the normal due date for paying tax, except where a special rule applies.

---

## 7. Line 11: Excess Social Security And Tier 1 RRTA Tax Withheld

For 2025, Schedule 3 line 11 is the refundable excess of:

- Social Security tax withheld, and
- tier 1 RRTA tax withheld.

The 2025 employee Social Security limit is:

```text
wage base        = $176,100
employee rate    = 6.2%
maximum employee = $10,918.20
```

Important 2025 rule:

- on a joint return, compute the limit separately per spouse; do not combine spouses before applying the annual cap.

Implementation guardrail:

- do not state this as requiring "two distinct EINs" as a tax-law rule. The common real-world case is multiple employers, but line 11 is defined by **excess withholding**, not by an EIN-count test on the face of the law.

## 8. Lines 12, 13a–13z

For 2025:

```text
Line 12  = credit for federal tax on fuels (Form 4136)
Line 13a = Form 2439 undistributed long-term capital gains tax paid credit
Line 13b = Section 1341 credit (claim-of-right repayment)
Line 13c = net elective payment election amount from Form 3800 Part III line 6 column (j)
            (manual entry only — full Form 3800 OOS per G5)
Line 13d = deferred amount of net 965 tax liability
Line 13z = other refundable credits (write-in)
```

Important guardrails:

- line 13a is a refundable credit, not a Schedule D amount by itself; Schedule D handles the undistributed gain inclusion separately;
- line 13b is the specific claim-of-right repayment credit under IRC §1341;
- line 13z is a real active 2025 write-in line, not a permanently blank placeholder. Known 2025 write-in items include Form 8689 (U.S. Virgin Islands income allocation), which the 2025 IRS instructions identify as a line 13z source.

## 9. Project Implementation Notes

These are implementation notes for this repo, not substantive IRS law.

★ **NO dedicated helper method for line 31** — line 31 is a pure pass-through wired directly inside `computeLine31ThroughLine38` at `TaxReturnComputeService.java:27252-27260`:

```java
// ── Line 31: Schedule 3 line 15 (other payments and refundable credits) ─────────
BigDecimal line31 = null;
if (schedule3 != null && schedule3.getOtherPaymentsCredits() != null) {
    BigDecimal line15 = schedule3.getOtherPaymentsCredits().getTotalOtherPaymentsAndRefundableCredits();
    if (line15 != null && line15.compareTo(BigDecimal.ZERO) > 0) {
        line31 = line15;
    }
}
payments.setOtherPaymentsSchedule3(line31);
```

The actual Schedule 3 line 15 aggregation happens upstream in `finalizeSchedule3Totals` (`TaxReturnComputeService.java:26089`), which is covered by the 20 #5 VERIFIED CORRECT breadcrumb planted 2026-05-14.

Apply-methods that populate Schedule 3 Part II run BEFORE `finalizeSchedule3Totals`:

| Sched 3 line | Source method | Personal form / Statement |
|---|---|---|
| line 9 | `applyPremiumTaxCreditToSchedule3` | Form 8962 line 26 |
| line 10 | `applyForm4868ToSchedule3` | extension-payment data |
| line 11 | `computeExcessSocialSecurityTax` | W-2 box 4 |
| line 12, 13b, 13c, 13d, 13z | `applyOtherPaymentsFormToSchedule3` | `31-other-payments` personal form |
| line 13a | `applyForm2439CreditToSchedule3` | `form-2439` statement |

Then `finalizeSchedule3Totals` (called from `prepare()` at `line 2054`) computes:

```text
totalOtherPaymentsRefundableCredits = Schedule 3 line 14
totalOtherPaymentsAndRefundableCredits = Schedule 3 line 15
```

And finally `computeLine31ThroughLine38` reads `schedule3.otherPaymentsCredits.totalOtherPaymentsAndRefundableCredits` into `Payments.otherPaymentsSchedule3` for Form 1040 line 31.

Current app coverage for Schedule 3 Part II inputs:

- line 9 via `Form 8962`,
- line 10 via extension-payment data,
- line 11 via excess SS / RRTA compute,
- line 12 via the `31-other-payments` personal form (★ G3 fix — manual entry for Form 4136),
- line 13a via `2439` statement entries,
- line 13b, 13c, 13d via the `31-other-payments` personal form,
- line 13z via repeating `otherRefundableCreditItems`,
- line 13c — manual entry only; full Form 3800 OOS per G5.

★ Current app behavior note:

- the backend writes Form 1040 line 31 only when Schedule 3 line 15 is positive; otherwise it leaves the stored field null (null-when-zero convention).

## 10. MFS Mechanism (M2 Transitive Inheritance)

Line 31 has **no helper method** and therefore no direct MFS check at the wiring site. MFS protection is transitively inherited via:

- the upstream apply-methods (each with its own M3 or M4 mechanism for SSN/spouse filtering),
- `finalizeSchedule3Totals` (covered by the 20 #5 breadcrumb).

★ **M2 — transitive inheritance from `finalizeSchedule3Totals`.** Line 31 is the first M2 instance in the payments-section; M2 is section-agnostic and depends on the structural pass-through pattern, not the form section.

## 11. Downstream Form 1040 Flow

For 2025:

```text
Form1040.line31 = Schedule3.line15
Form1040.line32 = line27a + line28 + line29 + line30 + line31
Form1040.line33 = line25d + line26 + line32
```

Important guardrail:

- line 31 is part of the refundable-credit subtotal on line 32; it is not withholding and does not belong on line 25.

## 12. Validations / Flags

| Condition | Action |
|---|---|
| `Schedule3` or `Schedule3.otherPaymentsCredits` is null | `payments.otherPaymentsSchedule3 = null` |
| `Schedule3.line15 ≤ 0` | `payments.otherPaymentsSchedule3 = null` (null-when-zero) |
| `Schedule3.line15 > 0` | `payments.otherPaymentsSchedule3 = Schedule3.line15` |

Structural invariants:

- `line 31 ≥ 0` (sum of nonnegative inputs)
- `line 31 = Schedule3.line15` when positive; otherwise null
- Schedule 3 Part I credits do NOT feed line 31

No blocking flags are emitted at the line 31 wiring site. All Schedule 3 Part II validation happens upstream in the apply-methods + `finalizeSchedule3Totals`.

## 13. Summary Rule

For tax year 2025:

```text
Form1040.line31 = Schedule3.line15
```

With:

```text
Schedule3.line15 = line9 + line10 + line11 + line12 + line14
Schedule3.line14 = line13a + line13b + line13c + line13d + line13z
```

Subject to these key 2025 rules:

- line 31 is only the Schedule 3 Part II total,
- positive `Form 8962 line 26` goes to line 9 and then line 31,
- excess APTC repayment does not go to line 31 (it flows through Schedule 2 line 1a),
- excess Social Security / tier 1 RRTA withholding can create a refundable amount on line 11 (per-spouse capped at `$10,918.20`),
- line 12 is supported as a manual entry on `31-other-payments` (G3 fix — full Form 4136 not implemented),
- line 13c is supported as a manual entry on `31-other-payments` (G5 — full Form 3800 OOS),
- line 13z is an active write-in line,
- line 31 inherits MFS protection transitively via the upstream apply-methods (M2),
- line 31 flows into line 32, then line 33.
