# Form 1040 (2025) - Line 32: Total Other Payments and Refundable Credits

> Source of truth: 2025 Form 1040 and the 2025 Form 1040 instructions.

---

## 1. Line Identity

**Form 1040 line 32** is:

```text
Add lines 27a, 28, 29, 30, and 31. These are your total other payments and refundable credits
```

Exact mapping:

```text
Form1040.line32 = Form1040.line27a + Form1040.line28 + Form1040.line29 + Form1040.line30 + Form1040.line31
```

For 2025, line 32 is a **subtotal** in the payments section.

It flows as follows:

```text
Form1040.line33 = Form1040.line25d + Form1040.line26 + Form1040.line32
```

Important guardrail:

- `line 32` is not a standalone credit and not a write-in field; it is only the subtotal of lines `27a`, `28`, `29`, `30`, and `31`.

---

## 2. Top-Level Formula

For tax year 2025:

```text
Form1040.line32 = line27a + line28 + line29 + line30 + line31
```

Expanded source mapping:

```text
line27a = earned income credit
line28  = additional child tax credit
line29  = refundable American opportunity credit
line30  = refundable adoption credit
line31  = amount from Schedule 3, line 15
```

Downstream:

```text
Form1040.line33 = line25d + line26 + line32
```

Important 2025 rule:

- withholding on line `25d` and estimated tax payments on line `26` do **not** belong inside line `32`; they are added only on line `33`.

## 3. Upstream Components

### Line 27a

```text
Form1040.line27a = earned income credit
```

Computed by `computeLine27aEIC()`. 2025 max: $649 (childless) through $8,046 (3+ children). Investment income ceiling $11,950.

Lines 27b and 27c are **election indicators only** (checkboxes). Line 27b = clergy Schedule SE election; line 27c = prior-year earned income election. Neither is a separate dollar amount added to line 32. The formula "Add lines 27a, 28, 29, 30, and 31" uses only 27a as a dollar addend.

### Line 28

```text
Form1040.line28 = additional child tax credit from Schedule 8812, Part II-A
```

Computed by `computeSchedule8812()`. 2025 cap: $1,700 per qualifying child. ACTC floor = `max(0, earned_income − $2,500) × 15%`. Form 2555 filers are blocked.

### Line 29

```text
Form1040.line29 = refundable American opportunity credit from Form 8863 line 8
```

Up to 40% of the AOTC is refundable (max $1,000 per student). MAGI phaseout: Single $80k–$90k / MFJ $160k–$180k. MFS filers blocked.

### Line 30

```text
Form1040.line30 = refundable adoption credit from Form 8839, line 13
```

New for 2025. Computed per child: `line11b = min(line11a, $5,000)`. MAGI phaseout starts $259,190, ends $299,190. See `lines/30.md` for full Part II computation. Nonrefundable portion routes separately to Schedule 3 line 6c → Form 1040 line 20.

### Line 31

```text
Form1040.line31 = Schedule3.line15
```

Schedule 3 line 15 = lines 9 + 10 + 11 + 12 + 14 (where line 14 = sum of 13a–13z). See `lines/31.md` for full Part II detail.

Important guardrail:

- line `32` contains only **refundable credits / other payments** from these lines. It does not include nonrefundable credits from line `20` or total tax from line `24`.

## 4. Null And Zero Behavior

Tax-law arithmetic:

```text
line32 = sum of lines 27a, 28, 29, 30, and 31
```

Implementation guardrail for this repo:

- treat missing component values as zero for arithmetic via `safeAmount()`,
- the storage convention is **null-via-ternary at setter** (Convention 1):
  `payments.setTotalOtherPaymentsAndRefundableCredits(line32 > 0 ? line32 : null)`
  — if all component values are absent or zero, line `32` is stored as `null` rather than `0`, while still preserving correct line `33` math.

Important distinction:

- that null-storage behavior is an implementation choice, not a different IRS formula.

### 4a. Non-negativity guardrail (32.md Gap G)

Each addend (27a EIC, 28 ACTC, 29 refundable AOTC, 30 refundable Adoption Credit, 31 Schedule 3 line 15) is non-negative by IRS construction. The backend installs a defensive paper-trail guardrail at `TaxReturnComputeService.java:27281-27289`:

```text
if (any addend < 0 OR line32 < 0):
    LOG.warnf("Line 32 non-negativity guardrail tripped — line27a=… line28=… line29=… line30=… line31=… line32=…")
```

Computation continues; this is a defensive paper trail, not a hard block. It surfaces upstream regressions where a refundable-credit helper accidentally emits a negative value.

## 5. 2025 Structural Significance

For 2025, line `32` is the explicit bridge between:

- refundable credits / other payments on lines `27a-31`, and
- total payments on line `33`.

The correct 2025 payment flow is:

```text
line25d = total withholding
line26  = estimated tax payments
line32  = total other payments and refundable credits
line33  = total payments
```

Important guardrail:

- do not collapse line `32` conceptually into line `33`. They are separate IRS lines with different meanings.

---

## 6. Project Implementation Notes

These are implementation notes for this repo, not substantive IRS law:

- the backend stores line `32` in `Payments.totalOtherPaymentsAndRefundableCredits`,
- `TaxReturnComputeService.computeLine31ThroughLine38()` (entry point at line 27089) explicitly computes line 32 at lines 27270-27291:

```text
line32 = safeAmount(earnedIncomeCredit)        // line 27a
       + safeAmount(additionalChildTaxCredit)  // line 28
       + safeAmount(americanOpportunityCredit) // line 29
       + safeAmount(refundableAdoptionCredit)  // line 30
       + safeAmount(otherPaymentsSchedule3)    // line 31
```

- pure 5-addend sum with no helper method; safeAmount handles null upstream values,
- the null-via-ternary at setter (Convention 1, line 27290-27291) is the same mechanism as line 25d, 33, 35a — `compareTo(BigDecimal.ZERO) > 0 ? value : null`,
- the frontend `line32OtherPayments()` prefers the stored backend value and falls back to recomputing the subtotal client-side for backward compatibility with older saved returns,
- the old legacy `recoveryRebateCredit` framing is obsolete for 2025 and should not appear in line-32 documentation.

### 6a. MFS inheritance via M2 (transitive)

Line 32 has no per-spouse code or MFS-specific logic. All 5 addends (lines 27a/28/29/30/31) handle MFS protection upstream via M2 transitive inheritance from their respective sub-line MFS guards. Line 32 is a pure summation; MFS segregation happens before line 32 is formed.

### 6b. Withholding and estimated tax are NOT in line 32

Line 25d (withholding) and line 26 (estimated tax payments) are explicitly excluded from line 32. They are added separately at line 33. Mixing them into line 32 would double-count them at the line-33 pivot.

## 7. Validation Rules

For 2025:

- line `32` should never be negative if its component lines are correct,
- line `32` must equal the sum of `27a + 28 + 29 + 30 + 31`,
- line `33` must equal `25d + 26 + 32`,
- nonrefundable credits must not be pulled into line `32`.

Implementation guardrail:

- if a component line is blocked, ineligible, or opted out upstream, that component should become zero / absent before line `32` is formed.

---

## 8. Downstream Form 1040 Flow

For 2025:

```text
Form1040.line32 = line27a + line28 + line29 + line30 + line31
Form1040.line33 = line25d + line26 + line32
```

Then:

```text
If line33 > line24, overpayment / refund path begins at line34
If line24 > line33, amount owed path begins at line37
```

Important guardrail:

- line `32` is part of the refund / amount-owed calculation only through line `33`; it is not compared directly to line `24`.

---

## 9. Summary Rule

For tax year 2025:

```text
Form1040.line32 = Form1040.line27a + Form1040.line28 + Form1040.line29 + Form1040.line30 + Form1040.line31
```

With:

```text
Form1040.line33 = Form1040.line25d + Form1040.line26 + Form1040.line32
```

Subject to these key 2025 rules:

- line `32` is only a subtotal,
- it includes lines `27a`, `28`, `29`, `30`, and `31` only,
- it excludes withholding and estimated tax payments,
- it feeds line `33`, which is the total-payments line.
