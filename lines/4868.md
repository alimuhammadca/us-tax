# Form 4868 — Application for Automatic Extension of Time To File U.S. Individual Income Tax Return

**Tax year:** 2025
**IRS form:** Form 4868 (2025)
**Sources:** Form 4868 (2025) with instructions; J.K. Lasser's Your Income Tax 2025 Professional Edition (§§ 46.3, 46.4, 46.8, 46.9, 35.5, 35.7); Taxes For Dummies 2024 Edition (Chapters 4, 20, 29, 30)
**Attaches to:** Nothing — do NOT attach Form 4868 to the actual return when filed
**Destination:** Amount paid with Form 4868 (line 7) → Schedule 3 (Form 1040) line 10 → Form 1040 line 31 → applied against tax liability

```text
Form 4868 extends the time to FILE only — it does NOT extend the time to PAY
All tax owed is still due by April 15, 2026
```

---

## 1. What Form 4868 Is

Form 4868 is the "Application for Automatic Extension of Time To File U.S. Individual Income Tax Return." Filing it by April 15, 2026 gives an automatic **6-month extension** — to October 15, 2026 — to file the 2025 Form 1040.

**Critical distinction:** The extension is for **filing only**. Any tax owed for 2025 is still due by April 15, 2026. Failing to pay by April 15 triggers late-payment penalties and interest even when a valid extension has been filed.

Form 4868 is an administrative filing form — it has no tax computation lines and no output that flows to any tax schedule beyond crediting the payment amount on Schedule 3 line 10 of the eventual return.

---

## 2. Who Should File

File Form 4868 if you:

1. Need more time beyond April 15, 2026 to complete your 2025 Form 1040, and
2. Are not in a combat zone (combat zone personnel get an automatic extension without filing Form 4868), and
3. Are not using an electronic payment as a substitute (see §10).

**No reason or explanation is required.** The extension is automatic upon timely filing. The IRS does not approve or deny it.

**Do not file Form 4868 if:**
- You are making an **electronic payment** designated as an extension payment — that payment automatically constitutes a Form 4868 filing.
- You are a military member in a **combat zone** or qualifying hazardous duty area — the extension is automatic.
- You need to qualify for the **foreign earned income exclusion** and cannot meet the residency/presence test by April 15 — use **Form 2350** instead.

---

## 3. Per-Return Filing Rules

**One Form 4868 per return** — not per spouse, not per income source.

```text
Form4868 count per return = 0 or 1
```

- **MFJ:** One Form 4868 for the joint return. Both spouses' names and SSNs appear on the same form.
- **MFS:** Each spouse must file their own Form 4868 for their own separate return.
- **Single / HOH / QSS:** One Form 4868 for that return.
- **Estates and trusts (Form 1041):** Use Form 7004, not Form 4868.
- **Corporations:** Use Form 7004, not Form 4868.

---

## 4. Per-Spouse Rules (MFJ)

**Not required per spouse on MFJ.** One Form 4868 covers both spouses on a joint return.

The form has two SSN lines specifically for this purpose:
- **Line 2:** SSN shown first on the return (primary taxpayer)
- **Line 3:** Other spouse's SSN

If spouses file **MFS**, each must file their own Form 4868 for their own return.

**Joint↔Separate change after filing Form 4868:**
- If spouses each filed **separate** Forms 4868 but later file **jointly**: combine both extension payments on the joint return's Schedule 3 line 10.
- If spouses filed a **joint** Form 4868 but later file **separately**: the full extension payment may be placed on either separate return, or split between them however they agree.

---

## 5. Requires Information from Both Spouses?

**Yes, on MFJ — but only one Form 4868 is filed.**

The 2025 instructions require both spouses' names (entered in the order they will appear on the return) and both SSNs on lines 2 and 3. The financial estimates (lines 4–7) are combined joint amounts covering both spouses' income, withholding, and estimated payments. There is no per-spouse split.

Implementation rule:

```text
if filing_status == MFJ:
    create one joint Form4868
    collect taxpayer_name + spouse_name (in order they appear on return)
    collect taxpayer_ssn (line 2) + spouse_ssn (line 3)
    use combined joint estimates for lines 4-7
else:
    create one Form4868 for the filer only
```

## 6. Line-by-Line Completion

### Part I — Identification

| Line | Description |
|---|---|
| 1 | Name(s) and address. MFJ: enter both spouses' names in the order they will appear on Form 1040. If using an agent, enter agent's name and address. |
| 2 | SSN shown first on the return (primary taxpayer's SSN on MFJ) |
| 3 | Other spouse's SSN (MFJ only) |

*Note: fiscal-year filers must also enter their fiscal year begin/end dates.*

### Part II — Individual Income Tax

| Line | Description |
|---|---|
| 4 | **Estimate of total 2025 tax liability** — the amount expected on **Form 1040 line 24**. Enter "0" if you expect to owe nothing. Must be a reasonable good-faith estimate; an unreasonable estimate can invalidate the extension and expose the taxpayer to the failure-to-file penalty. |
| 5 | **Estimate of total 2025 payments already made** — the amount expected on **Form 1040 line 33, excluding Schedule 3 line 10**. Includes: W-2 Box 2 federal withholding + 1099 withholding + Form 1040-ES estimated tax payments. **Do not include the amount being paid with Form 4868 itself on this line.** |
| 6 | **Balance due** = line 4 − line 5, floored at zero. |
| 7 | **Amount you are paying with this form.** May be less than line 6 — you still get the extension even if you cannot pay in full. Pay as much as possible to minimize penalty and interest. |
| 8 | Check if you are **out of the country** on the regular due date and a U.S. citizen or resident alien (see §9). "Out of the country" means living/working outside the U.S. and Puerto Rico, or on military/naval duty outside the U.S. |
| 9 | Check if you file **Form 1040-NR** and do not receive wages subject to U.S. income tax withholding. |

**Key rule on line 4 estimate (J.K. Lasser § 46.3):** The estimate must be made in good faith. An extension based on an unreasonable estimate may be treated as invalid, potentially exposing the taxpayer to the failure-to-file penalty.

**Key rule on line 5 (IRS instructions):** Do not include the extension payment amount (line 7) in line 5. Line 5 is payments already made *before* this extension filing.

---

## 7. Formula Summary

Form 4868 has no tax computation — it contains only estimates:

```
line4 = estimated_total_tax_liability_2025        # → Form 1040 line 24
line5 = estimated_total_payments_2025             # → Form 1040 line 33, excluding Sched3.line10
        (excludes the extension payment itself)
line6 = max(0, line4 - line5)                     # estimated balance due
line7 = amount_taxpayer_chooses_to_pay_now        # any amount ≥ 0, ≤ line6 typical
```

**After the actual return is filed:**

```
Schedule3.line10 = amount_paid_with_Form4868_line7
                 + amount_paid_via_electronic_extension_payment
                 + amount_paid_with_Form2350
# Do NOT include any payment processing / convenience fee
```

## 8. Deadlines and Extension Period

| Event | Date (Tax Year 2025) |
|---|---|
| Original filing deadline | April 15, 2026 |
| Form 4868 must be filed by | April 15, 2026 |
| Extended filing deadline after Form 4868 | October 15, 2026 |
| Payment of all tax due (regardless of extension) | April 15, 2026 |

**Taxpayers outside the U.S.:** Original filing/payment deadline is June 15, 2026 (see §9). Filing Form 4868 by June 15 gives an additional 4-month extension to October 15, 2026.

---

## 9. Special Rules

### Taxpayers Outside the U.S.

U.S. citizens and resident aliens living and working **outside the U.S. and Puerto Rico** on April 15, 2026 automatically receive a **2-month extension** — to June 15, 2026 — for both filing and paying (no Form 4868 required for this initial 2-month period).

- Check **line 8** when filing Form 4868 to indicate out-of-country status.
- File Form 4868 by June 15, 2026 to get a further 4-month extension to October 15, 2026.
- Attach a statement to the eventual return explaining that you were outside the U.S. on the original due date.
- **Important distinction:** the automatic 2-month extension for those abroad extends **both filing and payment** — unlike the standard Form 4868 extension which extends filing only.

### Combat Zone / Qualifying Hazardous Duty Area

Military personnel serving in a **designated combat zone** or qualifying hazardous duty area receive an **automatic extension** of all tax deadlines — no Form 4868 required.

- Extension period: **180 days** after the last day of combat zone service (or last day of continuous hospitalization from a combat zone injury) **plus** the number of days remaining before the original filing deadline when the taxpayer entered the combat zone.
- Applies to filing, payment, refund claims, audits, and IRS collections.
- Also applies to **spouses** of combat zone personnel.
- Extensions are not a forgiveness of tax owed; tax owed is still due, just deferred.

### Active-Duty Military Payment Deferral (Non-Combat Zone)

Active-duty military members who cannot pay because of military service may defer payment by notifying the IRS in writing. Requirements:
- Performing active military service (part-time reserve service does not qualify).
- Service period exceeds 30 consecutive days.
- Mandated by the president or secretary of defense.
- After returning from active duty: **180 days** to pay the deferred amount before interest and penalties resume.

### Form 2350 — Foreign Earned Income Exclusion

Taxpayers who expect to qualify for the **foreign earned income exclusion** under the bona fide residence or physical presence test, but cannot meet the qualifying test by April 15, use **Form 2350** (not Form 4868) to get an extension to the date they can first claim the exclusion. Payments made with Form 2350 also flow to Schedule 3 line 10.

---

## 10. Electronic Payment as Form 4868 Substitute

**An electronic extension payment automatically constitutes filing Form 4868.** If you make a payment electronically via IRS Direct Pay, EFTPS, or credit/debit card and **designate the payment as an extension payment for tax year 2025**, you are treated as having filed Form 4868. No separate Form 4868 filing is needed.

```text
Pay via IRS Direct Pay / EFTPS / debit/credit card
Designate: "Extension payment" / "Tax year 2025"
→ Automatically satisfies Form 4868 filing requirement
→ No paper or e-filed Form 4868 needed
```

If you e-file Form 4868 separately, do not also mail a paper duplicate unless you are enclosing a check or money order.

---

## 11. How to File

| Method | Details |
|---|---|
| **Electronic payment (simplest when owing)** | Pay via IRS Direct Pay, EFTPS, or card with extension designation — automatically substitutes for Form 4868 |
| **E-file Form 4868** | Via tax software, tax professional, or IRS Free File at IRS.gov/FreeFile |
| **Paper mail** | Mail Form 4868 to the IRS address for your state (varies by state and by whether payment is enclosed). Do not use P.O. Box addresses if using a private delivery service. |

**Do NOT attach Form 4868 to Form 1040 when you eventually file.**

## 12. Penalty and Interest Consequences

### Late-Payment Penalty (extension filed, tax not paid by April 15)

- **Rate:** 0.5% per month (or part of month) on unpaid tax
- **Maximum:** 25% of unpaid tax
- **Safe harbor:** If at least **90%** of actual 2025 tax liability is paid by April 15, 2026, the late-payment penalty does **not** apply to the remaining balance — provided the balance is paid by October 15, 2026
- **Installment agreement:** If an IRS installment agreement is in effect, the failure-to-pay rate drops from 0.5% to **0.25% per month** while the agreement is in effect (J.K. Lasser § 46.9)
- **Reasonable cause:** The late-payment penalty may be avoided on reasonable cause grounds; the IRS will waive it for the extension period if 90% was paid by the due date

### Late-Filing Penalty (no extension filed, return filed late)

- **Rate:** 5% per month (or part of month) on unpaid tax
- **Maximum:** 25% of unpaid tax
- **Minimum penalty (2025 returns):** **$525** (for returns filed more than 60 days past the due date), or the amount of unpaid tax if less
- **Fraudulent failure to file:** 15% per month, maximum 75%
- Both the 5% failure-to-file and 0.5% failure-to-pay penalties can apply simultaneously, but the combined rate is capped: the 5% failure-to-file is reduced by the 0.5% failure-to-pay for any month both apply (effective: 4.5% + 0.5% = 5% maximum combined per month)
- **No penalty if no tax is due** — both penalties are computed on unpaid tax; if nothing is owed, no penalty accrues regardless of filing date

### Interest on Underpayments

- **Rate:** Federal short-term rate plus 3 percentage points, compounded daily
- **Starts:** From the original April 15 due date (not the extended October 15 date)
- **No safe harbor for interest** — interest accrues on any unpaid balance from April 15 regardless of the 90% payment safe harbor

### Key Takeaway

> Always file Form 4868 (or make an extension payment) even if you cannot pay. The failure-to-file penalty (5%/month) is **ten times** the failure-to-pay penalty (0.5%/month). Filing the extension eliminates the larger penalty even when payment is impossible.

---

## 13. What Happens If You Don't File and Don't Pay

1. Failure-to-file penalty begins: 5% per month on unpaid tax, up to 25%
2. Minimum penalty after 60 days late: **$525** (or unpaid tax if less)
3. Interest accrues from April 15 at the federal short-term rate + 3%
4. IRS may file a **substitute return** — typically less favorable than a self-prepared return
5. IRS collection sequence: four notices at five-week intervals → Automated Collection System (phone contact) → revenue officer (in-person contact) → potential levy, wage garnishment, or asset seizure

## 14. Schedule 3 Line 10 — Payment Credit on the Eventual Return

Any amount paid with or as a substitute for Form 4868 is a **payment toward 2025 tax** and must be credited on the eventual Form 1040:

```
Schedule3.line10 = amount_paid_with_Form4868_line7
                 + amount_paid_via_electronic_extension_payment
                 + amount_paid_with_Form2350
# Do NOT include any payment processing / convenience fee charged by the payment provider
```

Schedule 3 line 10 flows through:

```
Schedule3.line10 → Schedule3.line15 → Form1040.line31 → total payments → applied against tax liability
```

**No separate IRS form is filed with the return for line 10.** The taxpayer simply enters the payment amount on Schedule 3 line 10. The intake system needs to capture:

| Field | Description |
|---|---|
| `extension_payment_amount` | Dollar amount paid |
| `extension_payment_source` | `Form4868` \| `electronic_extension_payment` \| `Form2350` |
| `payment_date` | Date payment was made |
| `confirmation_number` | EFTPS / Direct Pay / card confirmation reference |
| `exclude_processing_fee` | Always true — do not include card/payment processing fees |

## 16. Compute Order / Integration Notes

Form 4868 is filed **before** the actual return and has no dependency on any other computed form. It feeds one value into the eventual return:

```
Form4868.line7 → Schedule3.line10 (on the eventual Form 1040)
```

All Form 4868 inputs are estimates at the time of filing. The actual return figures supersede them. The only lasting artifact is the payment amount on Schedule 3 line 10.

```text
line5 = estimated_total_payments_2025       # must exclude line7
line6 = max(0, line4 - line5)

# --- Validate ---
assert line5 does not include line7

```

## 18. Out of Scope

| Item | Reason |
|---|---|
| **State extension filings** | Form 4868 is federal only. Each state has its own extension rules; some honor the federal extension automatically, others require separate filings and payments. Check state-specific rules independently. |
| **Form 7004** | Extension for business returns (Form 1120, Form 1041, etc.) — separate form |
| **Form 2350** | Extension for taxpayers qualifying for foreign earned income exclusion — separate form; only the payment amount flows to Schedule 3 line 10 |
| **IRS installment agreements** | Process for paying outstanding tax balances post-filing; separate from Form 4868 |
| **Penalty abatement / reasonable cause** | IRS first-time penalty abatement and reasonable cause relief procedures; out of scope |
| **Form 1040-SS (U.S. territories)** | Uses different line references (Part I line 7 for tax; Part I line 12 for payments); out of scope |

## 20. Bottom-Line Answers

### How many Form 4868 per return?

**0 or 1 per return.** One form covers the entire return regardless of number of income sources. Multiple bonds, employers, etc. — still one Form 4868.

### Is it required per spouse?

**No** on MFJ — one Form 4868 covers both spouses. Both names and both SSNs appear on the single form (lines 2 and 3).

**MFS:** each spouse files their own Form 4868 for their own separate return.

### Does it require information from both spouses?

**Yes, on MFJ.** Both spouses' names and SSNs are required. The financial estimates (lines 4–7) are combined joint figures covering both spouses.

### Does it extend time to pay?

**No.** Tax owed is still due April 15, 2026. Exception: the automatic 2-month extension for taxpayers abroad extends both filing and payment to June 15, 2026.

### What is the 90% safe harbor?

If at least **90% of actual 2025 tax** is paid by April 15, 2026, the late-payment penalty (0.5%/month) does not apply to the remaining balance — provided the balance is paid by October 15, 2026. Interest still accrues on any unpaid balance from April 15.

### What is the minimum late-file penalty for 2025 returns?

**$525** (for returns filed more than 60 days past the due date), or the amount of unpaid tax if less.

### Where does the extension payment appear on the actual return?

**Schedule 3 line 10.** Sources: Form 4868 line 7 + any electronic extension payment + any Form 2350 payment. Exclude processing fees.
