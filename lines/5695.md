# Form 5695 — Residential Energy Credits

**Tax year:** 2025
**IRS form:** Form 5695 (2025), Attachment Sequence No. 75, OMB No. 1545-0074
**Sources:** `docs/IRS-Forms/f5695.pdf`; IRS Instructions for Form 5695 (2025); J.K. Lasser's Your Income Tax 2025 Professional Edition §§ 25.2–25.4, 17.5; IRS Notice 2023-65 (2023-42 IRB 1067); IRC §§ 25C, 25D
**Destination:** Schedule 3 (Form 1040), line 5a (Part I) and line 5b (Part II) → Form 1040 line 20

---

## 1. Line Identity

Form 5695 computes **two separate nonrefundable personal tax credits** authorized by the Inflation Reduction Act of 2022:

| Credit | Part | IRC § | Schedule 3 line | Carryforward? | Max annual credit |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Residential Clean Energy Credit | Part I (lines 1–16) | 25D | 5a | **Yes** | Unlimited (except fuel cell: $1,000/kW) |
| Energy Efficient Home Improvement Credit | Part II (lines 17a–32) | 25C | 5b | **No** | $3,200 ($1,200 + $2,000 buckets) |

Both credits are **nonrefundable**. Neither has an income limit or AGI phaseout.

---

## 2. Per-Return Filing Rules

### 2a. General rule

**One Form 5695 per return.** The header has a single SSN field (primary taxpayer). On MFJ returns, both spouses' qualifying costs are combined on the single form. There is no per-spouse column split.

```
Default: 0 or 1 Form 5695 per return
```

### 2b. Exceptions — when more than one Form 5695 is attached

| Situation | Rule |
|---|---|
| **MFJ, separate main homes (Part II)** | Complete lines 17a–30 on a separate Form 5695 for each main home; combine the line 30 totals onto one form; write "More than one main home"; attach both forms |
| **Joint occupants filing separately** | Each occupant completes their own Form 5695 and allocates the credit; applies to fuel cell (line 7c checkbox + statement) and Part II (line 32a checkbox + statement) |
| **More than one residence in Part I** | All costs on one Form 5695; attach a statement listing additional addresses if needed |

> **For implementation scope:** The MFJ-separate-main-homes scenario applies only to Part II lines 17–30. Part I costs for multiple residences are always pooled on one form.

---

## 3. Eligibility — "Principal Residence" vs. "Main Home"

These two standards apply in different parts of the form and are **not interchangeable**:

| Standard | Definition | Where required |
|---|---|---|
| **Principal residence** | The home where you live most of the time; renters qualify | Part I lines 1–5b (solar, wind, geothermal, battery) |
| **Main home** | Your primary dwelling; only one at a time | Part I lines 7–11 (fuel cell); all of Part II |

Key implications:
- A **renter** can claim Part I lines 1–5b for improvements to their rented principal residence.
- A **second home** can qualify for Part I lines 1–5b but **not** for Part II.
- The taxpayer's **main home** must be in the **United States** for both Part I fuel cell and all of Part II.

---

## 4. Part I — Residential Clean Energy Credit

**Credit rate: 30%** of qualified costs. No annual dollar cap (except fuel cell kilowatt cap). Credit is available at 30% through **December 31, 2032**, then steps down (26% in 2033, 22% in 2034).

**Qualified costs include:** property cost plus labor for onsite preparation, assembly, and original installation; piping or wiring to connect to the home where applicable.

**Subsidies:** Reduce qualified costs by any public utility subsidies, rebates, or purchase-price adjustments received.

**All Part I property must be new** (not used or refurbished).

### 4a. Property-specific standards

| Property | Line | Additional requirement |
|---|---|---|
| Solar electric | 1 | None (structural materials that also generate electricity may qualify; ordinary roofing does not) |
| Solar water heating | 2 | None |
| Small wind energy | 3 | None |
| Geothermal heat pump | 4 | Must meet Energy Star requirements in effect at time of purchase |
| Battery storage | 5b | **Capacity must be ≥ 3 kWh** (line 5a gating question) |
| Fuel cell | 8 | **Capacity ≥ 0.5 kW; electricity-only efficiency > 30%**; must be on taxpayer's **main home** |

### 4b. Line-by-line computation (lines 1–16)

**Section A — Non-fuel-cell clean energy property (lines 1–6b):**

```
Line 1    Qualified solar electric property costs
Line 2    Qualified solar water heating property costs
Line 3    Qualified small wind energy property costs
Line 4    Qualified geothermal heat pump property costs
Line 5a   GATING: Battery storage technology capacity ≥ 3 kWh? Yes/No
              If No → skip line 5b; cannot claim battery credit
Line 5b   Qualified battery storage technology costs (if line 5a = Yes)
Line 6a   = Lines 1 + 2 + 3 + 4 + 5b
Line 6b   = Line 6a × 30%
```

> "Skip lines 1 through 11 if you only have a credit carryforward from 2024."

**Section B — Fuel cell property (lines 7a–11):**

```
Line 7a   GATING: Fuel cell property installed on/in connection with
              main home in the US? Yes/No
              If No → skip lines 7b–11; go to line 12
Line 7b   Address of main home (one main home at a time)
Line 7c   CHECKBOX: Joint occupants — check and attach allocation statement
Line 8    Qualified fuel cell property costs
Line 9    = Line 8 × 30%
Line 10   Kilowatt capacity cap: (kW capacity of property on line 8) × $1,000
              If rated capacity < 0.5 kW → enter 0
Line 11   = min(line 9, line 10)
```

**Example:** 2 kW fuel cell, $20,000 cost → line 9 = $6,000; line 10 = $2,000; line 11 = $2,000.

**Carryforward and liability limit (lines 12–16):**

```
Line 12   Credit carryforward from 2024 Form 5695 line 16
Line 13   = Line 6b + Line 11 + Line 12
Line 14   Tax liability limit (from Residential Clean Energy Credit
              Limit Worksheet in instructions — see §8)
Line 15   = min(line 13, line 14) → enter on Schedule 3 line 5a
Line 16   Carryforward to 2026 = line 13 − line 15
              (if line 15 < line 13 → carries to next year's line 12)
```

**Part I carries forward indefinitely** — unused credit flows to the next tax year without expiration.

## 5. Part II — Energy Efficient Home Improvement Credit

**Credit rate: 30%** across all categories. Per-category caps and two annual aggregate bucket caps apply. **No carryforward** — unused Part II credit is permanently lost.

**Annual aggregate caps (non-cumulative; reset each tax year):**

| Bucket | Cap |
|---|---|
| All Section A + B items combined (lines 18–26) | **$1,200** |
| Heat pumps / heat pump water heaters / biomass (line 29) | **$2,000** |
| **Total Part II (line 30)** | **$3,200** |

**Qualified Manufacturer Identification Number (QMID):** Starting in 2025, most Part II items require a valid QMID for each unit claimed. The QMID is assigned by the manufacturer and confirms the product meets the applicable energy efficiency standard. The taxpayer **retains** manufacturer certification and QMID documentation in their records — it is not attached to the return unless a statement is otherwise required. Exception: insulation and air sealing (line 18) do not require a QMID.

**Labor costs** are included for Section B residential energy property: include cost of onsite preparation, assembly, and original installation.

### 5a. Section A gating (lines 17a–17e)

All three of lines 17a, 17b, 17c must be "Yes" to claim Section A improvements (lines 18–20):

```
Line 17a  GATING: Improvements installed in/on main home in US? Yes/No
Line 17b  GATING: Taxpayer is original user of the improvements? Yes/No
Line 17c  GATING: Components reasonably expected to remain in use ≥ 5 years? Yes/No
              If any of 17a/17b/17c = No → skip lines 18–28; go to line 21a
Line 17d  Address of main home ("one main home at a time")
Line 17e  Were any improvements related to construction of this home?
              If Yes → can only claim improvements NOT related to construction,
              even if made after moving in
```

### 5b. Insulation and air sealing — Line 18 (IECC standard; no QMID required)

```
Line 18a  Cost of insulation material or air sealing material/system
              specifically and primarily designed to reduce heat loss or gain;
              must meet IECC criteria in effect for 2009
Line 18b  = Line 18a × 30%   (no separate per-item cap; subject to $1,200 aggregate)
```

### 5c. Exterior doors — Line 19 (Energy Star certified; QMID required)

```
Line 19a  Cost of most expensive qualifying exterior door
Line 19b  QMID of that door
Line 19c  = Line 19a × 30%   (cap: $250 per door)
Line 19d  QMID(i) and cost; QMID(ii) and cost — two next most expensive doors
Line 19e  Cost of all other qualifying exterior doors (0 if none)
Line 19f  = Line 19d (total) + Line 19e
Line 19g  = Line 19f × 30%
Line 19h  = Line 19c + Line 19g   (aggregate cap: $500 for all exterior doors)
```

### 5d. Exterior windows and skylights — Line 20 (Energy Star certified; QMID required)

```
Line 20a  QMID(i)–(iv) and costs — four most expensive windows/skylights
Line 20b  Cost of all other exterior windows and skylights (0 if none)
Line 20c  = Line 20a (total) + Line 20b
Line 20d  = Line 20c × 30%   (aggregate cap: $600)
```

### 5e. Section B gating (lines 21a–21c)

```
Line 21a  GATING: Costs for qualified energy property installed in a US home
              used as a residence? Yes/No
              If No → skip lines 22–25 and 29; go to line 26
Line 21b  GATING: Taxpayer is original user? Yes/No
              If No → skip same lines; go to line 26
Line 21c  Address(es) of each home — multiple addresses permitted
              (Section B is broader than "main home"; any US residence qualifies)
```

### 5f. Central air conditioners — Line 22 (CEE highest efficiency tier; QMID required)

```
Line 22a  QMID and cost of most expensive qualifying central air conditioner
Line 22b  Cost of all others (0 if none)
Line 22c  = Line 22a + Line 22b
Line 22d  = Line 22c × 30%   (aggregate cap: $600)
```

Must meet the highest efficiency tier established by the Consortium for Energy Efficiency (CEE) as of January 1 of the purchase year.

### 5g. Natural gas, propane, or oil water heaters — Line 23 (QMID required)

```
Line 23a  QMID(i) and QMID(ii) and costs — two most expensive qualifying
              water heaters
Line 23b  Cost of all others (0 if none)
Line 23c  = Line 23a (total) + Line 23b
Line 23d  = Line 23c × 30%   (aggregate cap: $600)
```

### 5h. Natural gas, propane, or oil furnaces or hot water boilers — Line 24 (QMID required)

```
Line 24a  QMID and cost of most expensive qualifying furnace or boiler
Line 24b  Cost of all others (0 if none)
Line 24c  = Line 24a + Line 24b
Line 24d  = Line 24c × 30%   (aggregate cap: $600)
```

### 5i. Enabling property (electrical panels, subpanels, branch circuits, feeders) — Line 25 (QMID required)

```
Line 25a  GATING: Did you install enabling property AND the enabled qualifying
              improvement/energy property, both in tax year 2025? Yes/No
              ("See instructions if some property was installed in 2024")
              If No → skip lines 25b–25e; go to line 26
Line 25b  Code for type of enabled property (from instructions)
Line 25c  Cost of improvements or replacement of enabling property
Line 25d  QMID(i) and QMID(ii) of enabling property
Line 25e  = Line 25c × 30%   (aggregate cap: $600)
```

Both the enabling and enabled property must be installed in the **same tax year** (2025). If the enabled property was installed in 2024, see special instructions for the cross-year exception.

### 5j. Home energy audits — Line 26

```
Line 26a  GATING: Did you pay for a home energy audit that included an
              inspection of main home in US AND a written report by a
              certified home energy auditor? Yes/No
              If No → skip lines 26b–26c; go to line 27
Line 26b  Cost of home energy audit
Line 26c  = Line 26b × 30%   (aggregate cap: $150)
```

**Audit requirements:** The audit must include an inspection and written report identifying significant/cost-effective energy improvements; prepared by a **qualified home energy auditor** whose report includes their name, EIN/TIN, attestation, and certification-program name.

### 5k. $1,200 aggregate cap (lines 27–28)

```
Line 27   = Line 18b + 19h + 20d + 22d + 23d + 24d + 25e + 26c
Line 28   = min(line 27, $1,200)   ← $1,200 annual aggregate cap
```

### 5l. Heat pumps, heat pump water heaters, and biomass — Line 29 (separate $2,000 bucket; QMID required)

Items on line 29 are **excluded from the $1,200 cap** and have their own **$2,000 annual cap**:

```
Line 29a  QMID and cost of most expensive electric or natural gas heat pump
Line 29b  Cost of all other qualifying heat pumps (0 if none)
Line 29c  QMID and cost of most expensive electric or natural gas heat pump
              water heater
Line 29d  Cost of all other qualifying heat pump water heaters (0 if none)
Line 29e  QMID and cost of most expensive biomass stove or boiler
Line 29f  Cost of all other qualifying biomass stoves/boilers (0 if none)
Line 29g  = Lines 29a + 29b + 29c + 29d + 29e + 29f
Line 29h  = min(line 29g × 30%, $2,000)   ← $2,000 annual cap
```

Heat pumps must have a thermal efficiency rating of at least **75%** (J.K. Lasser §25.3).

### 5m. Aggregation and liability limit (lines 30–32)

```
Line 30   = Line 28 + Line 29h   (total Part II; max $3,200)
Line 31   Tax liability limit (from Energy Efficient Home Improvement Credit
              Limit Worksheet in instructions — see §8)
Line 32   = min(line 30, line 31) → enter on Schedule 3 line 5b

Line 32a  CHECKBOX: Joint occupants special rule (check and attach
              allocation statement showing each occupant's share)
Line 32b  CHECKBOX: Condominium or cooperative (fractional share of
              qualifying improvements)
```

## 8. Tax Liability Limitation Worksheets

Both worksheets start from **Form 1040 line 18** (total tax before credits) and subtract nonrefundable credits already applied earlier in the computation.

### Part I — Residential Clean Energy Credit Limit Worksheet (line 14)

Subtracts, in order, credits already claimed on Schedule 3:
- Schedule 3 line 6l (other nonrefundable credits)
- Schedule 3 lines 1, 2, 3, 4 (foreign tax credit; child/dependent care; education credits; saver's credit)
- Form 5695 **line 32** (Part II credit — computed first)
- Schedule 3 clean vehicle credits
- Form 1040 line 19 (child tax credit / ODC)
- Schedule 3 lines 6c, 6g, 6h (adoption credit; mortgage interest credit; qualified plug-in vehicle credit)

> **Part II must be computed before Part I** because Part II line 32 is subtracted in the Part I limitation worksheet.

### Part II — Energy Efficient Home Improvement Credit Limit Worksheet (line 31)

Also starts from Form 1040 line 18 and subtracts a shorter set of prior credits (foreign tax credit, child/dependent care, education credits, CTC/ODC, and certain other Schedule 3 credits). If the result is zero or less, enter 0 on lines 31 and 32.

### Dependency chain

| Step | Input |
|---|---|
| Part II lines 17–30 | User-entered costs + QMID; gating questions |
| Part II line 31 | Form 1040 line 18; prior Schedule 3 credits |
| **Part II line 32** | min(line 30, line 31) — **must complete Part II before Part I** |
| Part I lines 1–13 | User-entered costs + prior carryforward (line 12) |
| Part I line 14 | Form 1040 line 18; prior Schedule 3 credits including Part II line 32 |

**Upstream dependency:** Both worksheets require Form 1040 line 18 (line 16 + line 17 AMT) and all earlier Schedule 3 nonrefundable credits to be finalized before Form 5695 can be computed.

## 10. Special Cases

### 10a. Joint occupancy

When two or more people jointly occupy a home and each claims the credit independently:
- Each occupant completes **their own Form 5695** and claims an allocable share.
- For fuel cell property: check **line 7c** and attach an allocation statement.
- For Part II: check **line 32a** and attach an allocation statement.
- If the aggregate credits across occupants would exceed property/category caps, each must reduce their share proportionally.

### 10b. Condominium or cooperative

If the taxpayer owns a fractional share of qualifying property through a condo association or co-op:
- Check **line 32b** for Part II.
- Credit is based on the taxpayer's fractional ownership share of the improvement cost.

### 10c. Business use of property

If the same property serves both personal and business use:
- **≤ 20% business use:** Full qualified cost may be claimed for the credit.
- **> 20% business use:** Only the nonbusiness (personal) portion of the cost qualifies.
- **100% business use:** No personal credit; claim the business energy credit (§ 48) separately.

### 10d. MFJ spouses with separate main homes (Part II only)

- Complete separate Forms 5695 through **line 30** for each main home.
- Combine the two line 30 amounts on one form.
- Write "More than one main home" at the top of the combined form.
- Attach both completed forms to the return.

## 11. Interaction with Other Tax Items

### Basis of home

Tax credits claimed for residential energy improvements **reduce the cost basis of the home** by the amount of the allowable credit (J.K. Lasser §17.5). This affects the computation of gain or loss on a future sale. The credit does not increase income; it only reduces basis.

### Subsidies, rebates, and grants

Qualified costs **must be reduced** by any:
- Public utility subsidies for energy conservation measures.
- Rebates or incentives structured as purchase-price adjustments (including Inflation Reduction Act rebate programs).

State incentive payments that are not purchase-price reductions may be excludable from income without reducing the credit base — see Notice 2023-65 for the distinction.

### Utility-provided energy conservation subsidies

Amounts excluded from gross income under § 136 (utility-provided energy conservation subsidies) also reduce the cost basis of the qualifying property and therefore reduce the claimable credit base.

### Schedule A / deduction overlap

Costs used to compute either credit may not also be deducted on Schedule A. In practice, residential energy improvement costs are generally not deductible on Schedule A.

### Interaction with Schedule 8812 / ACTC

Per Schedule 8812 instructions, the residential clean energy credit (Part I) appears among the nonrefundable credits that reduce tax liability before computing the ACTC floor. This means Form 5695 Part I participates in the credit-ordering sequence that feeds into the child and dependent tax credit computation.

## 12. Substantiation — Documents to Retain

The following should be **kept in taxpayer's records** and produced on examination. Do **not** attach to return unless a statement is otherwise required:

- Contracts, invoices, and proof of payment
- Placed-in-service dates for each property/improvement
- Property specifications (capacity ratings, efficiency certifications)
- Manufacturer certifications confirming product meets applicable standard
- **QMIDs** for all qualifying Part II property
- Home energy audit report (if claiming line 26)
- Documentation of any subsidies, rebates, or incentives received
- Allocation statements for joint occupancy or multiple-home situations

## 14. Out of Scope

- **Business energy credit (§ 48):** Applies when property is placed in service in connection with a business, not a personal residence. Separate form.
- **New Energy Efficient Home Credit (§ 45L):** Claimed by contractors and builders — not by individual homeowners on Form 5695.
- **Form 8911 (Alternative Fuel Vehicle Refueling Property Credit):** EV charging infrastructure.
- **Form 8936 (Clean Vehicle Credit):** Electric vehicles.
- **Prior-law § 25C lifetime cap ($500):** Eliminated starting tax year 2023. Not applicable to 2025 returns.
- **2033+ phasedown of § 25D rate:** The 30% rate applies through 2032. Future rate changes (26% / 22%) are not implemented here.
- **QMID database validation:** The IRS has not published a centralized mandatory QMID lookup tool; the taxpayer self-certifies from manufacturer documentation. Online validation is out of scope.
- **Self-employment / Schedule C business property:** Solely-business property does not qualify; mixed-use property follows the ≤20% / >20% rule in §10c above.
- **American Samoa / Puerto Rico income exclusions** affecting the AGI used in credit limit worksheets.
- **Amortization of fuel cell property:** All costs are claimed in full in the year placed in service.
