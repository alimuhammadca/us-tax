Below is a **2025-tax-year, IRS-verified, developer-ready** rule map for **Form 1040 line 8** and its feeder, **Schedule 1 (Form 1040), line 10**.

Reviewed against:
- **2025 Form 1040 / 1040-SR instructions** (`i1040gi`)
- **2025 Schedule 1 (Form 1040)**
- **2025 Pub. 525** for miscellaneous-income detail
- Current implementation in `TaxReturnComputeService.java` (lines 13325-13755 for `computeOtherIncomes`; lines 7350-7597 for line 8 wire-up; lines 13755 for Schedule 1 line 10 sum).

# 1) Line identity (2025 Form 1040)

- **Form**: 1040 / 1040-SR
- **Year**: 2025
- **Line**: 8
- **Label**: Additional income from Schedule 1, line 10
- **Form text**: `Additional income from Schedule 1, line 10`

```text
Form1040.line8 = Schedule1.line10
```

Per the 2025 Schedule 1 form, line 10 is:

```text
Schedule1.line10 = line1 + line2a + line3 + line4 + line5 + line6 + line7 + line9
```

where:
- **line 9** is total other income from **lines 8a through 8z**; and
- **line 2b** is informational only (date of the divorce or separation agreement).

Line 8 is therefore a pure carryover from Schedule 1 Part I. It can be positive, zero, or negative depending on the signed items included on Schedule 1.

---

# 2) Special top-of-Schedule-1 field: Form 1099-K reported in error or for personal items sold at a loss

The 2025 Schedule 1 form has a separate entry space **above Part I** for:
- amounts reported on **Form(s) 1099-K** that were **included in error**, or
- amounts reported on **Form(s) 1099-K** for **personal items sold at a loss**.

Important implementation rule:
- this entry space is **not one of Schedule 1 lines 1 through 10**;
- it is **not included in the line 10 arithmetic**;
- it is a separate disclosure / matching field on the form.

Normalization rule:
- if the entire 1099-K amount was erroneous or from personal items sold at a loss, enter the full box 1(a) amount in that top entry space;
- if only part was erroneous or loss-related, enter only that part in the top entry space;
- any remaining amount must be reported elsewhere based on its character, such as business income, rental income, or capital gain.

Example implication:
- personal item sold at a loss and reported on 1099-K -> top Schedule 1 entry space only, not Schedule 1 line 8z.
- personal item sold at a gain and reported on 1099-K -> report as capital gain, generally on Form 8949 / Schedule D, not in the top entry space.

# 3) Exact 2025 Schedule 1 Part I structure

## 3.1 Lines 1 through 7

- **Line 1**: Taxable refunds, credits, or offsets of state and local income taxes
- **Line 2a**: Alimony received
- **Line 2b**: Date of original divorce or separation agreement (informational text)
- **Line 3**: Business income or (loss) from Schedule C
- **Line 4**: Other gains or (losses), with checkbox for **Form 4797** or **Form 4684**
- **Line 5**: Rental real estate, royalties, partnerships, S corporations, trusts, etc. from Schedule E
- **Line 6**: Farm income or (loss) from Schedule F
- **Line 7**: Unemployment compensation, with repayment checkbox and repayment amount field for a 2025 overpayment

## 3.2 Line 8 other-income sublines (8a through 8z; 8w/8x/8y do not exist)

- **8a** Net operating loss (NOL) deduction (negative entry)
- **8b** Gambling
- **8c** Cancellation of debt
- **8d** Foreign earned income exclusion **and** housing exclusion from Form 2555 (negative entry; both Form 2555 line 45 **and** line 50)
- **8e** Income from Form 8853 (Archer MSA)
- **8f** Income from Form 8889 (HSA)
- **8g** Alaska Permanent Fund dividends
- **8h** Jury duty pay
- **8i** Prizes and awards
- **8j** Activity not engaged in for profit
- **8k** Stock options
- **8l** Income from the rental of personal property if engaged in for profit but not in the business of renting such property
- **8m** Olympic and Paralympic medals and USOC prize money
- **8n** Section 951(a) inclusion
- **8o** Section 951A(a) inclusion (GILTI)
- **8p** Section 461(l) excess business loss adjustment
- **8q** Taxable distributions from an ABLE account
- **8r** Scholarship and fellowship grants not reported on Form W-2
- **8s** Nontaxable amount of Medicaid waiver payments included on Form 1040 line 1a or 1d (negative entry)
- **8t** Pension or annuity from a nonqualified deferred compensation plan or a nongovernmental section 457 plan (sourced from W-2 box 11)
- **8u** Wages earned while incarcerated (sourced from W-2 box 14 with inmate-wages flag)
- **8v** Digital-asset / pension-disaster ordinary income not reported elsewhere
- **8z** Other income, with type and amount listed

Then:

```text
Schedule1.line9  = sum(Schedule1.line8a through Schedule1.line8z)        # 23 sublines
Schedule1.line10 = sum(lines 1 through 7) + line9                        # 8 operands
Form1040.line8   = Schedule1.line10
```

# 4) Backend wiring

## 4.1 Orchestrator and call site

- **Orchestrator**: `computeOtherIncomes(...)` at `TaxReturnComputeService.java:13325` — aggregates taxpayer + spouse Schedule 1 Part I inputs into an `OtherIncomeComputation` value object.
- **MFS guard**: orchestrator takes a `boolean isMfsReturn` parameter and **null-shadows** `otherIncomeSpouse` and `employmentFormInmateWagesSpouse` on MFS. Single-guard cascade member.
- **Call site**: `TaxReturnComputeService.java:550` (inside `prepare()`).
- **Line 9 sum**: line 8 enters the line 9 chain at `TaxReturnComputeService.java:7439-7442` as the 8th and last operand.
- **Line 8 wire-up to Form 1040**: `income.setOtherIncomeSchedule1(line8)` at `TaxReturnComputeService.java:7597`.

## 4.2 Schedule 1 line 9 sum (23 sublines) and line 10 sum (8 operands)

- Line 9 = `sumAmounts(line8a, line8b, ..., line8v, line8z)` (null-as-zero).
- Line 10 = `addNonNull(addNonNull(...addNonNull(line1, line2a)..., line7), line9)` at `TaxReturnComputeService.java:13755` (null-preserving — returns null when ALL operands null, so no Schedule 1 is produced when there is no Part I input).

## 4.3 Line 8 override (test_cases/6a.md GAP T2.2)

`line8` accessor uses `firstNonNullAmount(otherIncome.line8FromSchedule1(), getAmount(socialSecurityTaxpayer, "manualOtherIncomeLine8"))` — when the taxpayer supplies a `manualOtherIncomeLine8` value on the Social Security personal form (used by imported-return scenarios), that value overrides the Schedule-1-derived line 8.

## 4.4 Pub. 915 SS-worksheet companion

`computeOtherIncomes` does **not** drive the Pub. 915 worksheet directly. The SS worksheet `worksheetLine3` includes Form 1040 line 8 directly (see `TaxReturnComputeService.java:14897` for the companion Pub. 915 subset accessor — but for adjustments only). For Schedule 1 Part I, the full line 10 flows through to line 8 and is reused as-is by Pub. 915.

# 6) Per-subline routing notes

## 6.1 Line 1: state/local refunds — auto-parse from 1099-G box 2

Per `TaxReturnComputeService.java:13421-13447` (closure of 8.md §4.7, 2026-06-07): the backend auto-parses 1099-G **box 2** entries (state and local income tax refunds) into Schedule 1 line 1. The user-fill amount overrides if present, but an advisory flag fires when the user-fill differs from the sum of uploaded box-2 amounts (consistent with the Pub. 525 tax-benefit-rule worksheet path producing a reduced taxable portion).

Key rules for line 1 itself:
- none of the refund is taxable if the taxpayer did not itemize in the year paid;
- none is taxable if the taxpayer deducted general sales tax instead of state and local income tax in that year;
- the standard worksheet in the Form 1040 instructions covers most 2024-refund cases; some taxpayers must use **Pub. 525** instead.

## 6.2 Lines 2a and 2b: alimony received

Taxable alimony belongs on line 2a only if it was received under a divorce or separation agreement:
- entered into on or before **December 31, 2018**, and
- not later modified to provide that the payment is not includible in income.

If the agreement was entered into after **December 31, 2018**, the alimony is not included on line 2a. Line 2b stores the original agreement date (the MFS guard nulls out the spouse-side date when filing MFS).

## 6.3 Line 3 / Line 6: Schedule C / Schedule F — OUT OF SCOPE blockers

When `hasScheduleCBusinessIncomeOutOfScope` or `hasScheduleFFarmIncomeOutOfScope` is true on the taxpayer or spouse form, the orchestrator emits a blocking `OTHER_INCOME_SCHEDULE_C_OUT_OF_SCOPE` or `OTHER_INCOME_SCHEDULE_F_OUT_OF_SCOPE` flag. Computation continues so the rest of Schedule 1 still assembles, but submission is gated.

## 6.4 Line 4: other gains or losses

Line 4 takes:
- **Form 4797, line 18b**, if Form 4797 is being filed; or
- **Form 4684, line 31**, if Form 4797 is not otherwise required.

The line 4 checkboxes identify which feeder form drove the amount.

## 6.5 Line 7: unemployment compensation

Use the actual 2025 unemployment amount, not blindly the 1099-G figure.

If the taxpayer repaid a **2025** overpayment in **2025**:
- subtract the repayment from the amount reported on line 7;
- check the repayment box on line 7; and
- enter the amount repaid in the entry space.

Repayments above $3,000 from a prior-year amount switch to the Pub. 525 claim-of-right rules rather than a simple netting.

## 6.6 Negative-entry sublines: 8a, 8d, 8s

These are signed reductions and are modeled as negative numbers.

- **8a** NOL deduction from a prior year.
- **8d** Form 2555 foreign earned income exclusion **and** housing exclusion (lines 45 + 50).
- **8s** nontaxable Medicaid waiver payments already included on Form 1040 line 1a or 1d.

## 6.7 Line 8g: Alaska Permanent Fund dividends

Alaska Permanent Fund dividends are still a distinct 2025 Schedule 1 line item; do not collapse them into 8z.

## 6.8 Line 8h / 24a, 8l / 24b, 8m / 24c linkage

These three pairs split inclusion from offset:
- jury duty pay belongs on **8h**, the give-back to the employer on **24a**;
- gross personal-property rental income belongs on **8l**, related deductible expenses on **24b**;
- Olympic / Paralympic medals and USOC prize money go on **8m** at gross; if AGI (including the amount) is at most **$1,000,000** (**$500,000** MFS), the nontaxable amount is backed out on **24c**.

## 6.9 Lines 8n / 8o: §951 / §951A

- **8n** from **Forms 5471, Schedule I**.
- **8o** from **Form 8992, Part II, line 5**.

Section 962 election: do **not** report on 8n or 8o; the related tax is computed at Form 1040 line 16 with an attached statement.

## 6.10 Line 8r: scholarships / fellowships

Includes amounts not reported on W-2. For degree candidates, only nonqualified-expense amounts (room, board, travel) belong here.

## 6.11 Line 8t: nonqualified deferred compensation

Sourced from W-2 box 11 (`employmentFormBox11NonqualifiedPlansTotal`). Single-source attribution per audit-trail discussion at `TaxReturnComputeService.java:19875`.

## 6.12 Line 8u: incarcerated wages

Sourced from W-2 box 14 entries flagged as inmate wages (`employmentFormInmateWages*`). MFS guard null-shadows the spouse-side W-2 box-14 inmate-wages aggregate.

## 6.13 Line 8v: digital assets / pension-disaster

Used for digital-asset ordinary income (forks, staking, mining) or pension withdrawals exempt from the early-distribution penalty under disaster relief. Do not use 8v for wages already on line 1a, capital gain or loss reported on Schedule D / Form 8949, or gifts and inheritances.

## 6.14 Line 8z: catch-all taxable income only

8z is for taxable income not reported elsewhere on the return. It is **not** a general dumping ground for items that already have a specific line, and **not** for nontaxable amounts (child support, many life insurance proceeds, gifts and bequests).

# 7) MFS guard

`computeOtherIncomes` is the eighth member of the single-guard MFS cascade. On MFS:
- the spouse-side `incomeOtherIncomeSpouse` map is null-shadowed at function entry;
- the spouse-side W-2-box-14 inmate-wages aggregate is null-shadowed;
- every taxpayer + spouse aggregator (`addNonNull(taxpayerValue, spouseValue)`) collapses to taxpayer-only;
- the Schedule C / F out-of-scope flags fire only from taxpayer-side input on MFS.

Lock-in test: `mfsExcludesSpouseOtherIncomeFromLine8`.

# 8) Validation and filing rules

## 8.1 Arithmetic validations

```text
validate Schedule1.line9  == sum(lines 8a through 8z)
validate Schedule1.line10 == line1 + line2a + line3 + line4 + line5 + line6 + line7 + line9
validate Form1040.line8   == Schedule1.line10        # unless manualOtherIncomeLine8 override is set
```

## 8.2 Structural validations

- If **line 2a** is nonzero, **line 2b** must have the original divorce or separation agreement date.
- If **line 7** reflects a 2025 overpayment repayment, the checkbox and repayment-amount entry should be populated.
- If **line 4** is used, at least one feeder-form indicator should be available for **4797** or **4684**.
- If **line 8z** is used, preserve the type-and-amount detail that the form requires.
- If the top **1099-K** entry space is populated, preserve that field even though it does not change line 10.

## 8.3 Schedule 1 attachment trigger

Generate / attach **Schedule 1** if any of the following are true:
- any Part I line is used,
- any Part II line is used,
- the top **1099-K** entry space is populated.

Do not use the narrower rule "attach Schedule 1 only if Form 1040 line 8 is nonzero." That misses valid 2025 cases where Schedule 1 is required for disclosure or Part II adjustments even if line 8 or line 10 is zero.

# 9) Out of scope

- **Line 3** Schedule C income or loss — blocking flag `OTHER_INCOME_SCHEDULE_C_OUT_OF_SCOPE`.
- **Line 6** Schedule F income or loss — blocking flag `OTHER_INCOME_SCHEDULE_F_OUT_OF_SCOPE`.
- **Line 8p** §461(l) excess business loss adjustment — modeled but driven by self-employment data that is out of scope.
- **Lines 8n / 8o** CFC inclusions — modeled but typically gated to deferred work.
- **Top-of-Schedule-1 1099-K entry-space** — not yet implemented as a separate disclosure field; out of scope per current spec §2. Verified gap, anti-fragmentation policy applied.

# 10) Implementation guardrails

- Do **not** add the top-of-Schedule-1 1099-K entry space into line 9 or line 10.
- Do **not** treat **8d** as only foreign earned income exclusion; it also includes the housing exclusion from Form 2555 (lines 45 + 50).
- Do **not** collapse Alaska Permanent Fund dividends into 8z.
- Do **not** net jury duty pay, personal-property-rental expenses, or Olympic / Paralympic medal exclusions directly on line 8; use 24a, 24b, and 24c.
- Do **not** put nontaxable items on 8z.
- Do **not** forget that 8a, 8d, and 8s are negative-entry lines.
- Do **not** assume Schedule 1 is unnecessary just because Form 1040 line 8 ends up as zero.
- Do **not** allow spouse-side Schedule 1 Part I data to leak into an MFS return — `computeOtherIncomes` is MFS-guarded.

# 11) Practical developer cheat sheet

- **Form 1040 line 8** = **Schedule 1 line 10** (override via `manualOtherIncomeLine8`).
- **Schedule 1 line 10** = lines 1-7 + line 9.
- **Schedule 1 line 9** = sum of 8a-8v + 8z (23 sublines; 8w/8x/8y absent).
- Top **1099-K** entry space is a separate disclosure field, not a line-10 input.
- **8a**, **8d**, **8s** are negative numbers.
- **8h / 24a**, **8l / 24b**, **8m / 24c** are inclusion / offset pairs.
- **1099-G box 2** auto-parses to Schedule 1 line 1; advisory flag if user-fill diverges from the parsed sum.
- **W-2 box 11** drives 8t; **W-2 box 14 inmate-wages** drives 8u.
- **Schedule C** and **Schedule F** are blocking out-of-scope conditions.
