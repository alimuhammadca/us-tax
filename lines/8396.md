# Form 8396 — Mortgage Interest Credit

**Tax year:** 2025
**IRS form:** Form 8396 (2025), Attachment Sequence No. 138, OMB No. 1545-0074, Cat. No. 62502X (created 8/28/25)
**Sources:** Form 8396 (2025) with instructions; J.K. Lasser's Your Income Tax 2025 Professional Edition § 25.14; Taxes For Dummies 2024 Edition (Ch. 16); Pub. 530 (Tax Information for Homeowners); Pub. 936 (Home Mortgage Interest Deduction); IRC § 25
**Attaches to:** Form 1040, 1040-SR, or 1040-NR
**Destination:** Form 8396 line 9 → Schedule 3 line 6g → Form 1040 line 20

---

## 1. Line Identity

Form 8396 computes the **Mortgage Interest Credit** for holders of **qualified Mortgage Credit Certificates (MCCs)** issued by state or local governmental units or agencies. It is a **nonrefundable** personal credit. Unused credit within the annual cap may be carried forward up to **3 tax years**.

The form also computes the **carryforward amounts** (Part II) to be used in 2026.

**Mandatory Schedule A interaction:** The tentative credit on line 3 **must** reduce the taxpayer's home mortgage interest deduction on Schedule A, even if part of line 3 is carried forward.

---

## 2. Who Can Claim the Credit

The credit can be claimed **only** if the taxpayer was issued a **qualified Mortgage Credit Certificate (MCC)** by a state or local governmental unit or agency under a qualified mortgage credit certificate program.

Additional requirements:
- The home to which the certificate relates must be the taxpayer's **main home**.
- The home must be **located in the jurisdiction** of the governmental unit that issued the certificate.
- The interest on the mortgage must **not** have been paid to a **related person**.

**Disqualified certificates — the following do NOT qualify:**
- Homestead Staff Exemption Certificates
- Certificates issued by the Federal Housing Administration (FHA)
- Certificates issued by the Department of Veterans Affairs (VA)
- Certificates issued by the Farmers Home Administration

**Property cost general rule (J.K. Lasser § 25.14):** Under most MCC programs, the qualifying principal residence may not cost more than **90% of the average area purchase price** (or **110%** in certain targeted areas). These limits are set by the issuing governmental authority, not the IRS.

---

## 3. Per-Return Filing Rules

```
Form 8396 count per return = one per MCC held (usually 0 or 1)
```

The form has one MCC number, one certificate credit rate, and one home address — it is tied to a single MCC and a single certified mortgage. Most taxpayers hold one MCC, so typically **one Form 8396 per return**.

If a taxpayer holds multiple MCCs (e.g., from different qualified programs on different properties — rare), a separate Form 8396 is filed for each certificate.

**One Form 8396 is not filed per spouse on a joint return.** For MFJ, both spouses' mortgage interest on the certified indebtedness is combined on a single Form 8396. The form header shows "Name(s) shown on your tax return" with **one SSN** (the primary filer's).

---

## 4. Per-Spouse and Joint-Return Rules

**Not required per spouse on a joint return.** One Form 8396 covers the joint return.

**MFJ:** Line 1 instructs: "If someone else (**other than your spouse if filing jointly**) also held an interest in the home, enter only your share of the interest paid." The carve-out for spouses on a joint return means both spouses' combined interest on the certified indebtedness is entered on line 1 without reduction.

**Does Form 8396 require both spouses' information on MFJ?** Often yes — the combined mortgage interest paid by either spouse is reported on line 1, and the Credit Limit Worksheet uses the joint Form 1040 line 18 (joint tax liability). No separate per-spouse fields exist on the form.

```
if filing_status == MFJ:
    create one Form8396
    use joint-return tax liability data
    include spouse mortgage/MCC information if jointly relevant
```

**MFS situation:** If spouses file separately and share one MCC on one property, the $2,000 cap and interest must be allocated between them in proportion to each spouse's ownership interest. Only one spouse's Form 8396 can claim the credit for that certificate (the one named on or entitled under the MCC). Each spouse files their own Form 8396 on their own separate return.

**Non-spouse joint owner (co-owner):** If someone other than the spouse (filing jointly) also held an interest in the home, line 1 is reduced to only the taxpayer's share of the interest. The $2,000 limit (when rate > 20%) is also allocated to each owner **in proportion to the interest held** (see Pub. 530, *Dividing the Credit*).

---

## 5. Certificate Credit Rate

The MCC credit rate is set by the issuing governmental authority and shown on the Mortgage Credit Certificate itself.

**Allowed range:** 10% to 50% (per form instructions line 2).

**Rate determines maximum credit:**
- Rate **≤ 20%:** No dollar cap. Credit = interest × rate (may exceed $2,000).
- Rate **> 20%:** Credit is **capped at $2,000** per year. Amounts above the cap **cannot be carried forward** — the $2,000 is a hard annual ceiling, not a deferral.

**Do not confuse** the certificate credit rate with the mortgage interest rate. Line 2 explicitly says "Do not enter the interest rate on your home mortgage."

---

## 6. Refinanced Mortgage Rules

A taxpayer can refinance without losing the MCC credit if the existing MCC is **reissued** and the reissued certificate meets **all five** conditions:

1. Issued to the holder(s) of the existing certificate for the **same property**.
2. **Entirely replaces** the existing certificate — the holder cannot retain any portion of the outstanding balance.
3. The **certified indebtedness** on the reissued certificate cannot exceed the outstanding balance shown on the existing certificate.
4. The **credit rate** of the reissued certificate cannot exceed the credit rate of the existing certificate.
5. The reissued certificate cannot result in a **larger line 3 amount** than would have been allowable under the existing certificate for any tax year.

**Original MCC cap calculation:** For each year, the taxpayer must determine the credit that would have been allowed under the original MCC (original scheduled interest × original credit rate). This may cap the line 3 amount even if the new loan has a lower interest rate.

**Mixed-year filing (two rates in one year):** Attach a statement showing separate line 1/2/3 calculations for the portions of the year each certificate was in effect. Combine for line 3 and write "see attached" on the dotted line next to line 2.

**Authority:** Regulations § 1.25-3(p).

## 7. Part I — Current-Year Mortgage Interest Credit (Lines 1–9)

### Line 1 — Interest Paid on Certified Indebtedness Amount

Enter the mortgage interest paid during 2025 on the **loan amount shown on the MCC** (certified indebtedness amount).

- **Source:** Usually **Form 1098 box 1** (Mortgage Interest Statement) or similar lender statement. Pub. 936 covers situations where Form 1098 is missing or was issued to another borrower.
- **Interest allocation (when mortgage > MCC certified amount):** If the MCC loan amount is less than the total mortgage balance, only a fraction of the total interest qualifies:

```
line 1 = total_mortgage_interest_paid
         × (certified_indebtedness_amount / original_mortgage_amount)
```

See Pub. 530 for the worked example of this allocation.

- **Non-spouse co-owner:** Enter only the taxpayer's share of the interest.
- **MFJ:** Include both spouses' combined interest — no reduction for the spouse.

### Line 2 — Certificate Credit Rate

Enter the rate shown on the Mortgage Credit Certificate. Valid range: 10%–50%. Do **not** enter the mortgage interest rate.

### Line 3 — Tentative Credit

```
If line 2 ≤ 20%:  line 3 = line 1 × line 2
If line 2 > 20%:  line 3 = min(line 1 × line 2, $2,000)
                  [or prorated share of $2,000 if non-spouse co-owner]
If refinanced with reissued MCC: see §6 above; original MCC rate may lower the cap
```

> **Mandatory Schedule A reduction:** If itemizing, **reduce** the home mortgage interest deduction on Schedule A by the line 3 amount. This reduction is required **even if part of line 3 is carried forward to 2026**.

### Lines 4–6 — Prior-Year Credit Carryforwards Brought Into 2025

| Line | Carryforward Year | Source |
|---|---|---|
| 4 | 2022 credit carryforward | 2024 Form 8396, line 16 |
| 5 | 2023 credit carryforward | 2024 Form 8396, line 14 |
| 6 | 2024 credit carryforward | 2024 Form 8396, line 17 |

### Line 7 — Total Available Credit

```
line 7 = line 3 + line 4 + line 5 + line 6
```

This is the total pool of current-year credit plus all eligible carryforwards available to offset 2025 tax liability.

### Line 8 — Tax Liability Limitation (Credit Limit Worksheet)

Line 8 is computed from the **Credit Limit Worksheet** (kept for records, not filed):

```
Worksheet step 1:  Form 1040 (or 1040-SR / 1040-NR) line 18
                   [total tax before credits]

Worksheet step 2:  Form 1040 line 19                          (CTC/ODC)
                 + Schedule 3 lines 1, 2, 3, 4                (foreign tax, child care, education, retirement saver)
                 + Schedule 3 line 6d                         (elderly/disabled credit)
                 + Schedule 3 line 6f                         (new clean vehicle, personal use)
                 + Schedule 3 line 6l                         (residential clean energy)
                 + Schedule 3 line 6m                         (previously owned clean vehicle)
                 + Form 5695 line 32                          (if applicable)

Worksheet step 3:  line 8 = max(0, step 1 − step 2)
```

**Schedule 8812 interaction:** If directed to complete Form 8396 by Credit Limit Worksheet B in the Schedule 8812 instructions, use Schedule 8812 Credit Limit Worksheet B line 14 in place of Form 1040 line 19 in step 2.

**Pre-Part I preparation note (top of form):** Before beginning Part I, figure the amounts of: credit for the elderly or the disabled, alternative motor vehicle credit, and qualified plug-in electric drive motor vehicle credit — because these feed into step 2 of the Credit Limit Worksheet.

### Line 9 — Current-Year Mortgage Interest Credit

```
line 9 = min(line 7, line 8)
```

**Enter line 9 on Schedule 3 (Form 1040) line 6g.**

If line 9 = line 7 → no unused credit; skip Part II.
If line 9 < line 7 → complete Part II to determine carryforwards to 2026.

---

## 8. Part II — Mortgage Interest Credit Carryforward to 2026 (Lines 10–17)

Complete only if **line 9 < line 7** (there is unused credit).

**Carryforward ordering rule:** The current-year credit (line 3) is used first, then prior-year credits beginning with the **earliest year** (oldest first).

```
Line 10  = line 3 + line 4           [current-year + 2022 carryforward pool]
Line 11  = line 7                    [total available]
Line 12  = max(line 9, line 10)      [larger of credit used or oldest pool]
Line 13  = line 11 − line 12         [remaining unused]
Line 14  2024 c/f to 2026 = min(line 6, line 13)
Line 15  = line 13 − line 14
Line 16  2023 c/f to 2026 = min(line 5, line 15)
Line 17  2025 c/f to 2026 = max(0, line 3 − line 9)
```

**Keep a copy of Form 8396** to figure the 2026 credit.

**Critical carryforward cap rule:** If the certificate credit rate exceeds 20%, **no amount over the $2,000 limit** (or the prorated $2,000 if co-owned) may be carried forward. The carryforward only applies to unused credit within the $2,000 cap.

**Carryforward horizon:** Up to 3 tax years or until used, whichever comes first. A 2025 credit (line 17) can be carried to 2026, 2027, or 2028 at most.

---

## 9. Full Line-by-Line Formula Summary

```
PART I:
line 1    Interest on certified indebtedness (Form 1098 box 1; allocated if mortgage > MCC amount)
line 2    Certificate credit rate (10%–50%, from MCC; NOT the mortgage rate)
line 3    = line1 × line2   [capped at $2,000 if rate > 20%]
          → Reduces Schedule A mortgage interest deduction by line 3
line 4    2022 carryforward (from 2024 Form 8396 line 16)
line 5    2023 carryforward (from 2024 Form 8396 line 14)
line 6    2024 carryforward (from 2024 Form 8396 line 17)
line 7    = line3 + line4 + line5 + line6
line 8    Credit Limit Worksheet = Form1040.line18
          − (line19 + Sch3.lines1+2+3+4+6d+6f+6l+6m + Form5695.line32)
          floored at 0
line 9    = min(line7, line8)   → Schedule 3 line 6g

PART II (complete only if line 9 < line 7):
line 10   = line3 + line4
line 11   = line7
line 12   = max(line9, line10)
line 13   = line11 − line12
line 14   2024 c/f to 2026 = min(line6, line13)
line 15   = line13 − line14
line 16   2023 c/f to 2026 = min(line5, line15)
line 17   2025 c/f to 2026 = max(0, line3 − line9)
```

## 10. Developer-Safe End-to-End Logic

```
if taxpayer_has_qualified_MCC == false:
    no Form8396

else:
    // Line 1 — allocate interest if mortgage exceeds certified amount
    if original_mortgage <= certified_indebtedness:
        line1 = all_interest_paid
    else:
        line1 = all_interest_paid × (certified_indebtedness / original_mortgage)

    line2 = MCC_rate

    // Line 3 — tentative credit with $2,000 cap if rate > 20%
    line3 = line1 × line2
    if line2 > 0.20:
        line3 = min(line3, 2000)

    // Lines 4–6 — prior-year carryforwards
    line4 = 2022_carryforward_from_2024_8396_line16
    line5 = 2023_carryforward_from_2024_8396_line14
    line6 = 2024_carryforward_from_2024_8396_line17

    line7 = line3 + line4 + line5 + line6
    line8 = CreditLimitWorksheet_output   // Form1040.line18 minus prior credits
    line9 = min(line7, line8)

    if line9 < line7:
        line10 = line3 + line4
        line11 = line7
        line12 = max(line9, line10)
        line13 = line11 − line12
        line14 = min(line6, line13)
        line15 = line13 − line14
        line16 = min(line5, line15)
        line17 = max(0, line3 − line9)

    Schedule3.line6g = line9

    if taxpayer_itemizes:
        ScheduleA.home_mortgage_interest -= line3   // reduce by tentative credit, not line9
```

All must be computed before line 8.

## 14. Interaction with Other Tax Items

### Schedule A — Mandatory Deduction Reduction
The tentative credit on **Form 8396 line 3** must reduce the home mortgage interest deduction on Schedule A. This is not optional and applies even when part of line 3 is carried forward rather than used in the current year. Failure to reduce Schedule A by line 3 double-counts the benefit.

```
ScheduleA.home_mortgage_interest_deduction -= Form8396.line3
```

### Credit Limit Worksheet — Prior Credits
The following credits consume tax liability before Form 8396 and therefore reduce line 8: Schedule 3 lines 1 (foreign tax), 2 (child/dependent care), 3 (education), 4 (retirement saver), 6d (elderly/disabled), 6f (clean vehicle personal), 6l (residential clean energy), 6m (previously owned clean vehicle), Form 1040 line 19 (CTC/ODC), Form 5695 line 32.

### Recapture — Form 8828
If the home is **sold within 9 years** of the MCC issuance date at a **gain**, the taxpayer may have to recapture some of the mortgage subsidy received. Recapture is computed on **Form 8828** (Recapture of Federal Mortgage Subsidy). Reference: Pub. 523 (*Selling Your Home*). The 9-year window begins at MCC issuance.

### AMT
Form 8396 is a nonrefundable personal credit and is not limited by AMT. The Credit Limit Worksheet uses Form 1040 line 18 (regular tax plus AMT if any), so AMT liability naturally reduces the credit available on line 8.

### Carryforward Interaction Across Years
Lines 14, 16, and 17 feed into next year's Form 8396 lines 6, 5, and 4 respectively. These must be tracked year-over-year. A 2025 credit (line 17) expires if unused after 2028.

## 15. Key Dollar Limits (2025)

| Item | Limit |
|---|---|
| Certificate credit rate range | 10%–50% |
| Annual credit cap (rate > 20%) | $2,000 |
| Annual credit cap (rate ≤ 20%) | None — credit = interest × rate (may exceed $2,000) |
| Amount above $2,000 that can carry forward | $0 — no carryforward above the cap |
| Carryforward period | Up to 3 tax years |
| Recapture window | 9 years from MCC issuance date |

---

## 16. Out of Scope

- **Non-qualifying certificates:** FHA, VA, Farmers Home Administration, and Homestead Staff Exemption Certificates do not qualify; no Form 8396 is filed for these.
- **Interest paid to related persons:** Ineligible; no credit.
- **Second homes / rental property:** Credit applies only to the taxpayer's main home.
- **Refinanced MCC that does not meet all five reissuance conditions:** Credit may be lost; determination is facts-and-circumstances.
- **Form 8828 recapture computation:** The 9-year recapture calculation is a separate form triggered only by a home sale event; not computed here.
- **Pub. 530 co-owner allocation worksheet (*Dividing the Credit*):** Interest allocation when MCC covers only part of the mortgage, and the co-owner $2,000 proportional split, are treated as user-entered inputs (user provides their allocated share on line 1).
