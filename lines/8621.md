Form **8621** is the IRS form used by a U.S. shareholder of a **PFIC** (Passive Foreign Investment Company) or **QEF** to report PFIC income, make elections, and in some cases compute the special **section 1291** tax and interest charge. The IRS requires it in several situations, including certain PFIC distributions, sales/dispositions, QEF or mark-to-market reporting, elections in Part II, and certain annual reporting under section 1298(f). ([IRS][1])

## How it relates to Form 1040 line 16

The direct line-16 connection is **Form 8621 Part V, line 16e**. The 2025 Form 8621 instructions say that if you have tax on **line 16e**, you report it on **Form 1040 line 16**, **check box 3**, and write **"1291TAX"** in the box-3 entry space. ([IRS][2])

Not every Form 8621 affects line 16. The line-16 connection is mainly the **section 1291 excess distribution / gain regime** in **Part V**. Other 8621 amounts can affect different parts of the return instead, depending on the PFIC method:

* **QEF** inclusions (section 1293) can feed ordinary income / long-term capital gain reporting.
* **Mark-to-market** under section 1296 can create ordinary income or deduction.
* Elections in Part II may affect basis and future-year treatment.
  So for your 1040 line-16 implementation, the specific mapping you need is:

```text
Form1040.line16 += Form8621.PartV.line16e
Form1040.line16_box3_checked = true
Form1040.line16_box3_text = "1291TAX"
```

## How many Forms 8621 are filed with a return?

The default rule is **one Form 8621 per PFIC**, not one per return and not one per expense item. The 2025 instructions say that a **separate Form 8621 must be filed for each PFIC** in which stock is held directly or indirectly, including each PFIC in a chain of ownership, unless an exception applies. ([IRS][3])

So the practical filing rule is:

```text
number_of_Form8621 = number_of_reportable_PFICs
```

If you own:

* 1 PFIC -> 1 Form 8621
* 3 PFICs -> 3 Forms 8621
* 1 top-level PFIC that itself owns 2 lower-tier PFICs and reporting is triggered for the chain -> potentially 3 Forms 8621 total, one for each PFIC in the chain. ([IRS][3])

## Is it per spouse?

Usually **not automatically one per spouse**. It is **per PFIC per shareholder**.

The 2025 instructions specifically say that **shareholders filing a joint return may file a single Form 8621 with respect to a single PFIC in which each joint filer owns an interest**. ([IRS][4])

So on an MFJ return:

* if both spouses jointly own **the same PFIC**, one Form 8621 for that PFIC can cover it;
* if spouse A owns PFIC X and spouse B owns PFIC Y, you generally need **separate Forms 8621 for each PFIC**;
* if both spouses each own interests in multiple PFICs, count by PFIC, not by spouse.
  The form itself also has a checkbox for shares **jointly owned with spouse**, which supports this structure. ([IRS][5])

## How to complete Form 8621

The exact lines you complete depend on **why** you are filing it. A good implementation starts by classifying the PFIC filing reason.

### Step 1: Identify the PFIC and the shareholder facts

Complete the header and Part I information for the PFIC:

* shareholder type,
* PFIC name/address,
* reference ID if any,
* class of shares,
* acquisition date,
* number of shares,
* value at year-end,
* whether shares are jointly owned with spouse,
* which regime applies: section 1291, section 1293 (QEF), or section 1296 (mark-to-market). ([IRS][5])

### Step 2: Decide which regime applies

You generally fall into one of these buckets:

1. **Section 1291 default regime** - excess distributions / gain on disposition
2. **QEF** regime - section 1293 inclusions
3. **Mark-to-market** regime - section 1296 inclusions/deductions
4. **Election-only / annual reporting** situations in Part II / Part I. ([IRS][1])

### Step 3: Complete the relevant parts

#### Part I - Summary information / annual reporting

Use this to identify the PFIC, holdings, value, and the applicable regime/election context. A single Form 8621 for one PFIC can include Part I plus later parts and elections. ([IRS][3])

#### Part II - Elections

Use Part II to make or maintain elections such as:

* **QEF election**
* election to extend time for payment on QEF inclusions
* **mark-to-market election**
* purging / deemed sale / deemed dividend type elections where applicable.
  These elections can require supporting statements from the foreign corporation or additional attachments depending on the election. ([IRS][3])

#### Part III - QEF income inclusions

If the PFIC is a **QEF**, report the shareholder's:

* pro rata share of **ordinary earnings**, and
* pro rata share of **net capital gain**.
  These are income inclusions under **section 1293** and generally feed the return's income lines, not Form 1040 line 16. ([IRS][6])

#### Part IV - Mark-to-market under section 1296

If a valid **mark-to-market election** applies, Part IV reports the annual inclusion or deduction under section 1296. This usually affects income directly, not Form 1040 line 16. ([IRS][3])

#### Part V - Section 1291 distributions and dispositions

This is the part most relevant to **Form 1040 line 16**.

You complete Part V when you have:

* an **excess distribution**, or
* gain treated as an excess distribution under section 1291.
  The 2025 instructions note new Part V line formatting, including a currency code entry and a USD conversion line for line 15e(2), then the form computes the section 1291 tax and interest charge through **line 16e**. The resulting **line 16e tax** is what goes to **Form 1040 line 16** as **1291TAX**. ([IRS][3])

### Step 4: Attach the form to the return

Attach Form 8621 to the shareholder's tax return by the due date, including extensions. If no income tax return is otherwise required, the instructions say to file Form 8621 directly with the IRS service center in Ogden. ([IRS][7])

## Developer-safe filing logic

A good rules-engine version looks like this:

```text
for each PFIC in taxpayer_holdings:
    if filing_trigger_exists_for_PFIC:
        create Form8621 for that PFIC

        complete Part I

        if election_made:
            complete Part II

        if QEF_reporting:
            complete Part III

        if MTM_reporting:
            complete Part IV

        if section1291_excess_distribution_or_gain:
            complete Part V
            Form1040.line16 += Form8621.line16e
            set Form1040.line16 box3 = "1291TAX"
```

On an MFJ return:

* if both spouses jointly own the same PFIC, one Form 8621 for that PFIC may be used;
* otherwise count by PFIC, not by spouse. ([IRS][4])

## What inputs you need to complete it

At minimum, for each PFIC:

* issuer identity and share class,
* direct vs indirect ownership,
* acquisition date,
* year-end share count/value,
* distributions received,
* sale/disposition details and gain,
* QEF annual information statement, if QEF,
* mark-to-market fair value data, if MTM,
* chain-of-ownership information if the PFIC is indirectly owned through another PFIC or pass-through. ([IRS][3])

## Bottom line

* **Form 8621 is generally per PFIC**, not per spouse and not per investment expense. ([IRS][3])
* On a joint return, spouses **may file a single Form 8621 for the same PFIC if both own an interest in that PFIC**. ([IRS][4])
* **Form 8621 line 16e** is the amount that feeds **Form 1040 line 16**, with **box 3 checked** and **"1291TAX"** entered. ([IRS][2])
