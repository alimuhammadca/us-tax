# Form 8801 — Credit for Prior Year Minimum Tax (Individuals, Estates, and Trusts)

**Tax year:** 2025
**IRS form:** Form 8801 (2025), Attachment Sequence No. 801, OMB No. 1545-0074
**Sources:** `docs/IRS-Forms/f8801.pdf`; IRS Instructions for Form 8801 (2025); J.K. Lasser's Your Income Tax 2025 Professional Edition § 23.4; IRC § 53
**Destination:** Schedule 3 (Form 1040), line 6b → Form 1040 line 20

---

## 1. Line Identity

Form 8801 computes the **Credit for Prior Year Minimum Tax** — the amount of prior-year AMT (from **deferral items** only) that can now offset current-year regular tax. It also computes any **credit carryforward to 2026**.

The credit is **nonrefundable**. Any excess over the current-year tax liability carries forward indefinitely to future years.

| Output | Line | Destination |
|---|---|---|
| Current-year minimum tax credit | Line 25 | Schedule 3 (Form 1040) line 6b |
| Credit carryforward to 2026 | Line 26 | Retained; enters next year's Form 8801 line 19 |

For estates and trusts, line 25 goes to Form 1041, Schedule G, line 2c instead.

---

## 2. When to File

File Form 8801 **only if line 21 is more than zero**. File if, for **2024**, the taxpayer had:

1. An AMT liability **and** adjustments or preferences **other than exclusion items** (i.e., deferral-item AMT), **OR**
2. A credit carryforward from **2024 Form 8801, line 26**, **OR**
3. An unallowed legacy **qualified electric vehicle credit** for line 20.

If line 21 is zero or less — **do not file Form 8801**.

### Deferral items vs. exclusion items

The minimum tax credit arises **only** from prior-year AMT caused by **deferral (temporary) items** — differences that reverse in a later year, such as:
- ISO exercise adjustment (§ 56(b)(3))
- Accelerated depreciation on personal property (§ 57(a)(7))
- Mining exploration/development costs
- Long-term contract adjustments

AMT from **exclusion (permanent) items** — such as the standard deduction add-back, percentage depletion, or tax-exempt interest from specified private activity bonds — is **not creditable** and does not generate a minimum tax credit. Part I isolates and removes the exclusion-item portion.

---

## 3. Per-Return Filing Rules

```
Form 8801 count per return = 0 or 1
```

| Filing situation | Rule |
|---|---|
| Single / HOH / QSS | At most one Form 8801 per return |
| MFJ | One **joint** Form 8801 for the return — not one per spouse |
| MFS | Each spouse may have their own Form 8801 on their own separate return |
| Estates / trusts | One Form 8801 per Form 1041 return |

**Form 8801 is not filed per spouse on a joint return.** The form attaches to a single return and uses return-level figures (Form 1040 line 16, Schedule 3 totals, Form 6251 line 9). The instructions explicitly reference "your (and your spouse's if filing jointly) 2024 Form 2555 amounts" — meaning both spouses' data is incorporated into **one** joint form.

## 5. Part I — Net Minimum Tax on Exclusion Items (Lines 1–15)

**Purpose:** Isolate the portion of 2024 AMT caused by **exclusion items**. That amount is the noncreditable portion. Part II then subtracts it from total prior-year AMT to find the creditable deferral-item AMT.

### Line 1
Combine **lines 1 and 2e** of your **2024 Form 6251**.
*(Estates and trusts: see instructions for the Schedule I equivalent.)*

### Line 2
Enter adjustments and preferences treated as **exclusion items** (see instructions). These are the permanent/structural AMT add-backs that do not reverse and are not creditable.

### Line 3
Minimum tax credit **net operating loss deduction** (MTCNOLD), if applicable. Entered as a negative amount (shown in parentheses on the form).

### Line 4
`= Lines 1 + 2 + 3`

- If **zero or less**: enter -0- here and on **line 15**, then go to Part II.
- If **more than $875,950** and you were **married filing separately for 2024**: see instructions for a special computation.

### Line 5 — Prior-year AMT exemption amount

Enter the applicable 2024 exemption:

| 2024 Filing Status | Exemption |
|---|---|
| Married filing jointly or qualifying surviving spouse | $133,300 |
| Single or Head of Household | $85,700 |
| Married filing separately | $66,650 |
| Estates and trusts | $29,900 |

### Line 6 — Prior-year exemption phaseout threshold

Enter the applicable 2024 phaseout threshold:

| 2024 Filing Status | Phaseout threshold |
|---|---|
| Married filing jointly or qualifying surviving spouse | $1,218,700 |
| Single, Head of Household, or Married filing separately | $609,350 |
| Estates and trusts | $99,700 |

### Line 7
`= Line 4 − Line 6`
If zero or less: enter -0- here **and on line 8**, then go to line 9.

### Line 8
`= Line 7 × 25% (0.25)`
(The exemption phases out at 25 cents per dollar of AMTI above the threshold.)

### Line 9
`= Line 5 − Line 8`
If zero or less, enter -0-. (This is the reduced exemption after phaseout.)

### Line 10
`= Line 4 − Line 9`
If zero or less: enter -0- here **and on line 15**, then go to Part II.
*(Form 1040-NR filers: see instructions.)*

### Line 11 — Tentative minimum tax on exclusion items

This is the most complex line in Part I. Compute differently based on 2024 facts:

**Case A — Form 2555 filed for 2024:**
See instructions for the Foreign Earned Income Tax Worksheet amount to enter.

**Case B — 2024 had capital gain distributions, qualified dividends, or Schedule D gains:**
If for 2024 you:
- Reported **capital gain distributions** directly on Form 1040/1040-SR/1040-NR line 7, **OR**
- Reported **qualified dividends** on Form 1040 line 3a, **OR**
- Had a gain on **both lines 15 and 16 of Schedule D (Form 1040)**

→ Complete **Part III** and enter the amount from **Part III line 55** here.

**Case C — All others (standard computation):**

| If line 10 is… | Then line 11 = |
|---|---|
| $232,600 or less ($116,300 or less if MFS for 2024) | Line 10 × 26% (0.26) |
| More than those amounts | (Line 10 × 28%) − $4,652 (−$2,326 if MFS for 2024) |

### Line 12
**Minimum tax foreign tax credit on exclusion items (MTFTCE).** Computed using separate Forms 1116 by category — see instructions for the six-step MTFTCE method. **Those Forms 1116 are kept in the taxpayer's records and are NOT attached to the return.**

### Line 13
`= Line 11 − Line 12`
*(Tentative minimum tax on exclusion items after the MTFTCE.)*

### Line 14
Enter the amount from **2024 Form 6251, line 10** (or 2024 Schedule I (Form 1041), line 53).
*(This is the prior-year actual AMT.)*

### Line 15 — Net minimum tax on exclusion items
`= Line 13 − Line 14`
If zero or less, enter **-0-**.

This is the noncreditable portion of 2024 AMT attributable to exclusion items.

---

## 6. Part II — Minimum Tax Credit and Carryforward to 2026 (Lines 16–26)

**Purpose:** Compute the available credit pool (lines 16–21), cap it against current-year regular tax minus tentative minimum tax (lines 22–24), and determine the allowable credit and carryforward.

### Line 16
Enter the amount from **2024 Form 6251, line 11** (or 2024 Schedule I (Form 1041), line 54).
*(This is the full prior-year AMT.)*

### Line 17
`= Line 15` (the exclusion-item portion, not creditable)

### Line 18
`= Line 16 − Line 17`
*(If less than zero, enter as a negative amount.)* This is the net deferral-item AMT portion — potentially creditable.

### Line 19 — 2024 credit carryforward
Enter the amount from **2024 Form 8801, line 26** (prior year's unused minimum tax credit carried forward).

### Line 20
Enter any **2024 unallowed qualified electric vehicle credit** (narrow legacy rule — see instructions).

### Line 21
`= Lines 18 + 19 + 20`
This is the total **available minimum tax credit pool**.

> **If line 21 is zero or less: stop here. Do not file Form 8801.**

### Line 22 — 2025 regular income tax liability minus allowable credits

For Form 1040 filers:

```
Line 22 = (Form 1040 line 16 + Schedule 2 line 1z)
          − (Form 1040 line 19
             + Schedule 3 lines 1 through 6z,
               EXCLUDING Schedule 3 line 6b [this credit]
               and Schedule 3 line 6k [Form 8912 credit])
```

If zero or less, enter **-0-**.

### Line 23
Enter the amount from **2025 Form 6251, line 9** (or 2025 Schedule I (Form 1041), line 52).
*(This is the current-year tentative minimum tax before credits.)*

### Line 24
`= Line 22 − Line 23`
If zero or less, enter **-0-**.

*(This is the excess of current-year regular tax over current-year tentative minimum tax — the amount available to absorb the prior-year minimum tax credit.)*

### Line 25 — **Minimum tax credit for 2025**
`= min(line 21, line 24)`

Enter this amount on **Schedule 3 (Form 1040), line 6b**.
*(Estates and trusts: Form 1041, Schedule G, line 2c.)*

### Line 26 — **Credit carryforward to 2026**
`= Line 21 − Line 25`

Keep this amount; it enters next year's **Form 8801, line 19**.

## 7. Part III — Tax Computation Using Maximum Capital Gains Rates (Lines 27–55)

Complete Part III **only if required** by line 11 (Cases A or B above) or by the Foreign Earned Income Tax Worksheet. Part III recalculates the line 11 tentative tax while preserving preferential capital-gain and qualified-dividend rates.

All computations in Part III use **2024 prior-year data**.

### Entry points

| Source | Line used |
|---|---|
| Standard entry | Line 8801 line 10 → Part III line 27 |
| Form 2555 for 2024 | Foreign Earned Income Tax Worksheet line 3 → Part III line 27 |

### Key Part III constants (2024)

**Line 34 — 0% capital gain rate threshold:**

| 2024 Filing Status | Amount |
|---|---|
| Married filing jointly or qualifying surviving spouse | $94,050 |
| Single or Married filing separately | $47,025 |
| Head of Household | $63,000 |
| Estate or trust | $3,150 |

**Line 40 — 20% capital gain rate threshold:**

| 2024 Filing Status | Amount |
|---|---|
| Single | $518,900 |
| Married filing separately | $291,850 |
| Married filing jointly or qualifying surviving spouse | $583,750 |
| Head of Household | $551,350 |
| Estate or trust | $15,450 |

**Lines 33 and 54 — ordinary income AMT rates (same as line 11 Case C above):**
- ≤ $232,600 (≤ $116,300 if MFS for 2024) → 26%
- Above → 28% minus $4,652 (minus $2,326 if MFS for 2024)

### Line-by-line summary (Part III)

```
Line 27   Form 8801 line 10 (or FEITW line 3 if Form 2555 for 2024)

Line 29   2024 Sched D (Form 1040) line 19, or Sched D (Form 1041) line 18b col(2)
              [skip if 2024 tax figured using QDCG Worksheet → carry line 28 to line 30]

Line 31   = smaller of line 27 or line 30

Line 36   = max(0, line 34 − line 35)
```

## 8. Compute Order and Dependencies

| Step | Requires |
|---|---|
| Part I lines 1–4 | 2024 Form 6251 lines 1, 2e |
| Part I lines 5–9 | 2024 filing status (for exemption and phaseout amounts) |
| Part I line 10 | Lines 4, 9 |
| Part I line 11 | Lines 10; 2024 QDCG/Sched D worksheets if applicable; Part III if applicable |
| Part I line 12 | Separate MTFTCE Form 1116 workpapers |
| Part I line 14 | 2024 Form 6251 line 10 |
| Part I line 15 | Lines 13, 14 |
| Part II line 16 | 2024 Form 6251 line 11 |
| Part II line 19 | 2024 Form 8801 line 26 |
| Part II line 21 | Lines 18, 19, 20 → **gating check: stop if ≤ 0** |
| Part II line 22 | 2025 Form 1040 line 16; Schedule 2 line 1z; Form 1040 line 19; Schedule 3 lines 1–6z (excl. 6b, 6k) — **all Schedule 3 credits except this one must be finalized first** |
| Part II line 23 | 2025 Form 6251 line 9 |
| Part II line 25 | Lines 21, 24 → **Schedule 3 line 6b** |
| Part II line 26 | Lines 21, 25 → carryforward |

**Critical upstream dependency:** Part II line 22 requires all other Schedule 3 nonrefundable credits to be computed and applied before Form 8801 can be finalized. Form 8801 is therefore computed **last** among Schedule 3 line 6 credits.

**Downstream:** Line 25 → Schedule 3 line 6b → Schedule 3 line 8 → Form 1040 line 20.

## 10. Out of Scope

- **Estates and trusts (Form 1041):** Line 25 goes to Schedule G line 2c rather than Schedule 3 line 6b. The Form 1041 path uses Schedule I instead of Form 6251; references to specific Schedule I line numbers are noted above but full Form 1041 support is not in scope.
- **Minimum tax foreign tax credit on exclusion items (MTFTCE):** The six-step MTFTCE computation using separate Forms 1116 is not implemented; line 12 will require manual user entry if applicable.
- **Form 1040-NR:** Line 10 has a special instruction for Form 1040-NR filers; not in scope.
- **MFS $875,950 threshold (line 4):** Special computation when MFS AMTI exceeds $875,950; rare edge case, deferred.
- **Legacy qualified electric vehicle credit (line 20):** Narrow legacy rule; captured as a user-entered field only.
- **AMT net operating loss (line 3 MTCNOLD):** Requires prior-year AMT NOL carryforward tracking; not implemented.
- **ISO spread, incentive stock options (Form 6251 line 2e):** No stock-option intake exists in this application.
