# Form 8814 — Parents' Election To Report Child's Interest and Dividends (2025)

For **tax year 2025**, you complete **Form 8814** only if a parent elects to report a qualifying child's **interest and dividends** on the parent's return so the child **does not have to file a separate return**. The child must meet all of these conditions: under **19** at year-end (or under **24** if a full-time student), only income from **interest and dividends** (including **capital gain distributions** and **Alaska Permanent Fund dividends**), **gross income under $13,500**, required to file a return, not filing a joint return, **no estimated tax payments** (including prior-year overpayment applied forward), and **no federal income tax withheld** from the child's income. The parent must also be the parent allowed to make the election.

## Who files it

Attach **Form 8814** to the parent's **Form 1040, 1040-SR, or 1040-NR** by the due date, including extensions. You file **one separate Form 8814 for each child** you elect for, and you may elect for one child but not another. If the parents file a joint return, use the joint return. If they do **not** file jointly, the return used depends on the family situation: often the parent with the **higher taxable income**, or the **custodial parent** in the divorced/separated cases described in the instructions. If more than one Form 8814 is attached, check **line C**.

## Before you decide to use it

The IRS warns this election can be worse than filing a separate return for the child. In particular, if the child has **qualified dividends** or **capital gain distributions**, the parent-election method can cost **up to $135 more tax**, because income between **$1,350 and $2,700** is taxed at **10%** on Form 8814, while a child's separate return may get lower preferential rates on those items. The election also causes the parent to lose certain deductions the child could have taken on the child's own return, such as the child's extra standard deduction for blindness, the penalty deduction for early withdrawal of savings, and the child's own itemized deductions. The IRS also notes that the parent's higher AGI can reduce or affect several deductions and credits.

## Inputs you need

You need the child's:

- **1099-INT** and **1099-OID** for taxable and tax-exempt interest
- **1099-DIV** for ordinary dividends, qualified dividends, and capital gain distributions
- any pass-through statements showing ordinary dividends through a partnership, S corporation, or estate/trust
- any nominee amounts
- any accrued-interest / amortizable-bond-premium / OID adjustments
- any facts about foreign accounts, foreign trusts, or private activity bond interest

The parent also needs their own filing facts to determine which parent's return is used and whether extra schedules are triggered.

## How to complete Form 8814, line by line

### Heading

Enter the parent name(s) and SSN exactly as shown on the parent return. If filing jointly, enter both names but the SSN of the person listed first on the return. Enter the child's name and SSN. Check **line C** if more than one Form 8814 is attached.

### Part I — Child's interest and dividends to report on your return

**Line 1a — Child's taxable interest**
Enter all the child's **taxable interest** for 2025. Do **not** include tax-exempt interest here. If the child received nominee interest that belongs to someone else, write the amount and **"ND"** on the dotted line and exclude it from the line 1a total. If there is accrued interest paid to the seller, amortizable bond premium, or OID adjustment, put the adjustment description on the dotted line and exclude the nontaxable portion from line 1a.

**Line 1b — Child's tax-exempt interest**
Enter the child's tax-exempt interest here, including exempt-interest dividends from a regulated investment company. If any of this tax-exempt interest came from **private activity bonds**, it can matter for **AMT**.

**Line 2a — Child's ordinary dividends**
Enter all ordinary dividends, including **Alaska Permanent Fund dividends** and ordinary dividends passed through from a partnership, S corporation, or estate/trust. If the child received nominee ordinary dividends, write the amount and **"ND"** on the dotted line and exclude it from the total.

**Line 2b — Child's qualified dividends**
Enter the child's qualified dividends from **1099-DIV box 1b**. Do **not** carry line 2b directly to the parent's Form 1040 line 3a. Instead, only the computed amount from **line 9** gets reported as the qualified-dividend amount on the parent return.

**Line 3 — Child's capital gain distributions**
Enter the child's capital gain distributions from **1099-DIV box 2a**. If nominee capital gain distributions were received for someone else, write the amount and **"ND"** on the dotted line and exclude it from the line 3 total. The amount eventually added to the parent's return comes from **line 10**, not necessarily the full line 3 amount.

**Line 4**
`line4 = line1a + line2a + line3`

- If the total is **$2,700 or less**, skip **lines 5–12** and go to **line 13**.
- If the total is **$13,500 or more**, **do not file Form 8814**; the child must file their own return.

**Line 5**
Base amount = **$2,700**.

**Line 6**
`line6 = line4 − line5`

**If both line 2b and line 3 are zero or blank**
Skip **lines 7–10**, enter **0** on **line 11**, and go to **line 12**.

**Line 7**
`line7 = line2b ÷ line4`
Enter as a decimal rounded to at least **three places**.

**Line 8**
`line8 = line3 ÷ line4`
Enter as a decimal rounded to at least **three places**.

**Line 9 — Qualified-dividend portion to the parent's return**
`line9 = line6 × line7`
This is the portion of the child's income that is treated as qualified dividends on the parent's return. Report it on the parent's **Form 1040 lines 3a and 3b**. If Schedule B is filed, include it on **Schedule B line 5** and identify it as from **Form 8814**; also include it on Form 1040 line 3a. If the amount from line 9 plus the parent's own dividends exceeds **$1,500**, Schedule B is required.

**Line 10 — Capital-gain-distribution portion to the parent's return**
`line10 = line6 × line8`
This is the portion of the child's capital gain distributions that goes to the parent return. Report it on **Schedule D line 13**, or on **Form 1040 line 7a** if Schedule D is not required. If Schedule D is not required, check the "**Schedule D not required**" box on Form 1040 line 7b when applicable. If the child's capital gain distributions include **collectibles gain**, **unrecaptured section 1250 gain**, or **section 1202 gain**, extra Schedule D worksheet allocations are required.

**Line 11**
`line11 = line9 + line10`

**Line 12 — Other income to parent's Schedule 1**
`line12 = line6 − line11`
Report this amount on the parent's **Schedule 1 line 8z**, and write **"Form 8814"** next to it. Do **not** include the child's line 2b qualified dividends or line 3 capital gain distributions again in line 12 / Schedule 1 line 8z. If you checked **line C** because more than one Form 8814 is attached, add the **line 12** amounts from all Forms 8814 and report the total on Schedule 1 line 8z.

### Part II — Tax on the first $2,700 of the child's interest and dividends

**Line 13**
Amount not taxed = **$1,350**.

**Line 14**
`line14 = max(0, line4 − 1350)`

**Line 15 — Extra tax added to the parent's line 16**
```
if line14 < 1350:
    line15 = line14 × 10%
else:
    line15 = 135
```
If you checked **line C** because more than one Form 8814 is attached, add the **line 15** amounts from all Forms 8814 and include the total in the tax on **Form 1040 line 16**. Check **box 1** on Form 1040 line 16.

## Where each Form 8814 amount goes on the parent return

| Form 8814 line | Parent return destination |
|---|---|
| Line 9 | Form 1040 lines 3a and 3b (Schedule B line 5 if Schedule B required) |
| Line 10 | Schedule D line 13, or Form 1040 line 7a if Schedule D not required |
| Line 12 | Schedule 1 line 8z, labeled "Form 8814" |
| Line 15 | Form 1040 line 16, check box 1 |

## Extra consequences to check

If you make the election:

- the parent may need **Schedule B Part III** if the child had a foreign financial account or certain foreign trust/grantor relationships
- tax-exempt interest from private activity bonds may affect **AMT**
- the child's line 12 income is included in the parent's **MAGI** and generally in the parent's **NIIT** base (except Alaska Permanent Fund dividends)
- the parent's AGI may rise enough to reduce deductions or credits

## Best-practice software rule

Before finalizing the election, compute tax **both ways**:

1. child files separately (possibly with Form 8615 if required), and
2. parent elects Form 8814.

The IRS explicitly recommends comparing the two because Form 8814 can be more expensive, especially when the child has qualified dividends or capital gain distributions.
