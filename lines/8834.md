# Form 8834 — Qualified Electric Vehicle Credit

**Tax year:** 2025
**IRS form:** Form 8834 (Rev. October 2024), Attachment Sequence No. 834, OMB No. 1545-1374, Cat. No. 14953G
**Sources:** Form 8834 (Rev. 10-2024) with instructions; 2025 Schedule 3 (Form 1040); IRS "About Form 8834" page; Taxes For Dummies 2024 Edition (confirms Schedule 3 line 6i = Form 8834, pre-2007 carryforward credit); J.K. Lasser's Your Income Tax 2025 Professional Edition (no dedicated section — QEV credit expired 2006); IRC § 30
**Attaches to:** Form 1040, 1040-SR, or 1040-NR (individuals); Form 1120 (corporations); Form 1041 (estates/trusts)
**Destination (individuals):** Form 8834 line 7 → Schedule 3 (Form 1040) line 6i → Schedule 3 line 8 → Form 1040 line 20

```text
Form 8834 is a legacy passive-activity-credit form
not a current-year vehicle-purchase credit form
```

---

## 1. Line Identity

Form 8834 computes the **Qualified Electric Vehicle (QEV) Credit** — specifically, **passive activity credits from prior years** that are allowed for the current tax year via Form 8582-CR (individuals/estates/trusts) or Form 8810 (corporations).

**Critical 2025 context:** The original QEV credit (IRC § 30) was available for qualifying electric vehicles placed in service **before January 1, 2007**. No new credits can be generated in 2025. In 2025, Form 8834 is used **only** to claim QEV passive activity credits released by Form 8582-CR or Form 8810 for the current year. For clean vehicles placed in service **after 2022**, use **Form 8936** instead.

The 2025 Schedule 3 identifies this line as: **"Qualified electric vehicle credit. Attach Form 8834."** (Schedule 3 line 6i)

The form has seven lines:

| Line | Purpose |
|---|---|
| 1 | QEV passive activity credits allowed for current year (from Form 8582-CR or 8810) |
| 2 | Regular tax before credits |
| 3a | Foreign tax credit |
| 3b | Certain allowable credits (see §7) |
| 3c | Line 3a + line 3b |
| 4 | Net regular tax = line 2 − line 3c (floored at 0) |
| 5 | Tentative minimum tax (Form 6251 line 9 for individuals) |
| 6 | Available credit capacity = line 4 − line 5 (floored at 0) |
| 7 | QEV credit = min(line 1, line 6) → Schedule 3 line 6i |

The credit is **nonrefundable**. If line 6 is less than line 1, the **unused credit is permanently lost** — no carryback or carryforward is allowed.

---

## 2. Who Can Claim / Eligibility in 2025

In 2025, Form 8834 may be filed only by a taxpayer who has **QEV passive activity credits released by Form 8582-CR (or Form 8810)** for the current year. Form 8834 itself does **not** have its own carryforward line — unlike Form 8859 (which carries a prior-year balance directly), the current-year allowed credit on line 1 comes entirely from Form 8582-CR. The IRS "About Form 8834" page confirms Form 8834 is for the credit **allowed for the current tax year**, not a direct prior-year carryover schedule.

**Original QEV credit eligibility (historical — for reference):**
- Vehicle must have been a **qualifying electric vehicle** as defined under IRC § 30.
- Vehicle must have been used **in a trade, business, or investment activity** if the credit was subject to passive activity limitations.
- Vehicle placed in service **before January 1, 2007** (credit expired for vehicles placed in service after 2006).

**What "passive activity credits from prior years" means:** If the original QEV credit was generated in a passive activity (e.g., a rental property or limited partnership with qualifying electric vehicles) and was suspended under IRC § 469 passive activity rules, it accumulated on Form 8582-CR. Those suspended credits are released in the year the taxpayer disposes of the passive activity or has sufficient passive income to absorb them. Form 8834 converts those released passive activity credits into a usable current-year credit.

**Do not use Form 8834:**
- For new clean vehicle credits for vehicles placed in service after 2022 — use **Form 8936**.
- For any direct (non-passive) QEV credit — the credit itself expired after 2006.

## 3. Per-Return Filing Rules

```
Form 8834 count per return = 0 or 1
```

One Form 8834 is filed per return (not one per spouse, not one per vehicle). Two reasons:

1. The form says **"Attach to your tax return"** in the singular.
2. It is a single return-level limitation form using return-level tax amounts on lines 2 through 6.

The form header shows "Name(s) shown on return" and a single identifying number (the primary SSN for individual filers). All QEV passive activity credits allowed for the year are aggregated on a single line 1.

A taxpayer files Form 8834 **only** if Form 8582-CR (or Form 8810) produces a QEV passive activity credit allowance greater than zero for the current year.

---

## 4. Per-Spouse and Joint-Return Rules

**Not required per spouse on a joint return.** One Form 8834 covers the MFJ return.

**MFJ:** Line 2 uses the joint return's regular tax (Form 1040 line 16 + Schedule 2 line 1z), line 3b uses the joint return's credits, and line 5 uses the joint return's TMT. Both spouses' combined data contributes indirectly through the joint return figures, but no separate per-spouse fields appear on the form.

**Does Form 8834 require both spouses' information on MFJ?** Yes, it can — because lines 2, 3b, and 5 all draw from joint-return tax and credit amounts. The passive activity credits on line 1 may also originate from a passive activity held by either or both spouses.

```
if filing_status == MFJ:
    create one Form8834
    line2 = joint Form1040.line16 + joint Schedule2.line1z
    line3b = joint return credits per §7
    line5 = joint Form6251.line9
else:
    use taxpayer-only return data
```

**MFS situation:** Each spouse files their own separate return with their own Form 8834 if they separately have QEV passive activity credits released from their own Form 8582-CR.

## 5. Original QEV Credit — Historical Context

The original qualified electric vehicle credit (IRC § 30) applied to qualifying electric vehicles placed in service before January 1, 2007.

| Item | Original Rules |
|---|---|
| **Credit amount** | 10% of the cost of the qualified vehicle |
| **Maximum credit** | Varied by vehicle type and year placed in service |
| **Phase-down** | Credit was phased down in 2004–2006 (75% / 50% / 25% of base) |
| **Credit type** | Nonrefundable; subject to passive activity limitations |
| **Carryforward** | None — unused credit (beyond passive activity suspension) is permanently lost |
| **Purchase deadline** | December 31, 2006 (no original credit after this date) |

These historical facts explain the **source** of any passive activity credit balance that may appear on line 1 of the 2025 form, but do not affect the 2025 computation mechanics.

## 6. How to Complete Form 8834 — Line by Line

### Line 1 — QEV Passive Activity Credits Allowed for Current Year

Enter the qualified electric vehicle passive activity credits allowed for the current tax year from:
- **Form 8582-CR** (Passive Activity Credit Limitations) — for individuals, estates, and trusts
- **Form 8810** (Corporate Passive Activity Loss and Credit Limitations) — for corporations

```
line1 = Form8582CR.allowed_QEV_passive_activity_credit
        (or Form8810 for corporations)
```

This is the **only** source for line 1. If neither Form 8582-CR nor Form 8810 produces a QEV allowance, no Form 8834 is needed.

### Line 2 — Regular Tax Before Credits

**Individuals (Form 1040, 1040-SR, or 1040-NR):**
```
line2 = Form1040.line16 + Schedule2.line1z
```
Form 1040 line 16 is the base regular tax. Schedule 2 line 1z is the alternative minimum tax (from Form 6251). Together they represent regular tax before any credits.

**Corporations (Form 1120):**
```
line2 = Form1120.ScheduleJ.line2
        (excluding base erosion minimum tax on line 1f)
```

**Estates and trusts (Form 1041):**
```
line2 = Form1041.ScheduleG.line1a
      + Form1041.ScheduleG.line1b
      + Form1041.ScheduleG.line1d
      + any Form8978 amount included on line1e
```

### Line 3a — Foreign Tax Credit

Enter the foreign tax credit (for Form 1040 filers: Schedule 3 line 1 / Form 1116 line 35 or simplified exception amount).

### Line 3b — Certain Allowable Credits

See §7 below for the full computation. For Form 1040 filers, this is a subset of nonrefundable credits already taken on the return, entered here to reduce available tax capacity before Form 8834.

### Line 3c — Total Prior Credits

```
line3c = line3a + line3b
```

### Line 4 — Net Regular Tax

```
line4 = max(0, line2 − line3c)
```

If line 4 is zero or less, enter -0- here **and** on line 7 (the credit is fully absorbed by prior credits; no Form 8834 credit is available).

### Line 5 — Tentative Minimum Tax (TMT)

Even if the taxpayer does not owe AMT, the TMT must be computed to determine the available credit capacity on line 6.

**Individuals:**
```
line5 = Form6251.line9
```

**Applicable corporations:**
```
line5 = Form4626.PartII.line9
```
Non-applicable corporations enter -0-.

**Estates and trusts:**
```
line5 = ScheduleI_Form1041.line52
```

### Line 6 — Available Credit Capacity

```
line6 = max(0, line4 − line5)
```

Line 6 is the excess of regular tax (after prior credits) over the tentative minimum tax. This is the maximum amount the QEV credit can reduce tax. If line 6 is zero or less, enter -0- here **and** on line 7.

### Line 7 — Qualified Electric Vehicle Credit

```
line7 = min(line1, line6)
```

**Enter line 7 on Schedule 3 (Form 1040) line 6i** (for individual filers).

> **Critical rule — no carryforward:** If line 6 is smaller than line 1, the unused credit is **permanently lost**. The unused or excess credit cannot be carried back or forward to other tax years. This is unlike most nonrefundable credits that allow carryforwards (e.g., Form 8396, Form 8859).

## 7. Line 3b — Allowable Credits Detail (Form 1040 Filers)

For **Form 1040, 1040-SR, or 1040-NR** filers, per the Form 8834 instructions:

```
line3b = Form1040.line19                // CTC / ODC
       + Schedule3.line2               // Child/dependent care credit
       + Schedule3.line3               // Education credits
       + Schedule3.line4               // Retirement saver's credit
       + Schedule3.line5a              // Residential clean energy credit
       + Schedule3.line5b              // Energy efficient home improvement credit
       + Schedule3.line7               // Subtotal of lines 6a–6z
       − Schedule3.line6a              // LESS: General business credit
       − Schedule3.line6b              // LESS: Prior year minimum tax credit
       − Schedule3.line6j              // LESS: Alt fuel vehicle refueling (personal-use)
       − Schedule3.line6k              // LESS: Credit to holders of tax credit bonds
```

Simplified: line 3b = Form 1040 line 19 + Schedule 3 lines 2–5 (with 5a and 5b explicit) + Schedule 3 line 7, reduced by lines 6a, 6b, 6j, and 6k.

**Credit ordering implication:** Credits excluded from line 3b (6a general business, 6b prior year min tax, 6j alt fuel refueling personal, 6k tax credit bonds) do **not** consume tax capacity before Form 8834. Credits included — 6c adoption, 6d elderly/disabled, 6e, 6f clean vehicle new, 6g mortgage interest, 6h DC homebuyer — **do** reduce available capacity before Form 8834 can apply. Form 8834's own result (line 6i) is being computed and is not yet included in line 3b.

## 8. Full Line-by-Line Formula Summary

```
INDIVIDUALS (Form 1040):

line1   = Form8582CR.allowed_QEV_passive_activity_credit

line2   = Form1040.line16 + Schedule2.line1z

line3a  = Schedule3.line1    // Foreign tax credit

line3b  = Form1040.line19
         + Schedule3.line2 + Schedule3.line3 + Schedule3.line4
         + Schedule3.line5a + Schedule3.line5b
         + Schedule3.line7
         − Schedule3.line6a − Schedule3.line6b
         − Schedule3.line6j − Schedule3.line6k

line3c  = line3a + line3b

line4   = max(0, line2 − line3c)

line5   = Form6251.line9     // Tentative minimum tax — always required

line6   = max(0, line4 − line5)

line7   = min(line1, line6)  → Schedule3.line6i

NOTE: If line4 = 0  → line7 = 0 (no credit available; enter -0- on lines 4 and 7)
      If line6 = 0  → line7 = 0 (no credit available; enter -0- on lines 6 and 7)
      If line6 < line1 → unused = line1 − line7 is LOST (no carryforward)
```

## 9. Developer-Safe End-to-End Logic

```
// Gate: only compute if passive activity credits were released by Form 8582-CR
if Form8582CR.allowed_QEV_passive_activity_credit == 0 or null:
    no Form8834 needed

else:
    line1 = Form8582CR.allowed_QEV_passive_activity_credit

    // Line 2 — regular tax before credits (individuals)
    line2 = Form1040.line16 + Schedule2.line1z

    // Line 3a — foreign tax credit
    line3a = Schedule3.line1

    // Line 3b — certain allowable credits (individuals)
    line3b = Form1040.line19
           + Schedule3.line2 + Schedule3.line3 + Schedule3.line4
           + Schedule3.line5a + Schedule3.line5b
           + (Schedule3.line7
              − Schedule3.line6a − Schedule3.line6b
              − Schedule3.line6j − Schedule3.line6k)

    line3c = line3a + line3b

    // Line 4 — net regular tax
    line4 = max(0, line2 − line3c)
    if line4 == 0:
        line7 = 0   // no credit available; stop here
    else:
        // Line 5 — tentative minimum tax (always required even if no AMT owed)
        line5 = Form6251.line9

        // Line 6 — available capacity above TMT
        line6 = max(0, line4 − line5)

        // Line 7 — allowed credit
        line7 = min(line1, line6)

    // Unused credit if line6 < line1 is permanently LOST — no carryforward
    Schedule3.line6i = line7
```

## 12. Compute Order and Dependencies

| Step | Requires |
|---|---|
| Line 1 | Form 8582-CR finalized for the current year |
| Line 2 | Form 1040 line 16 and Schedule 2 line 1z complete |
| Line 3a | Foreign tax credit (Schedule 3 line 1) finalized |
| Line 3b | Form 1040 line 19 + Schedule 3 lines 2–5, line 7 all finalized; excludes 6a, 6b, 6j, 6k |
| Line 4 | Lines 2 and 3c complete |
| Line 5 | Form 6251 completed — **always required** even if no AMT liability |
| Line 6 | Lines 4 and 5 complete |
| Line 7 | Lines 1 and 6 complete |
| Schedule 3 line 6i | Line 7 finalized |

**Critical ordering:** Form 8834 must be computed **after** all credits in line 3b have been finalized (Schedule 3 lines 2–5, lines 6c–6h, 6l–6m, and Form 1040 line 19), and **after** Form 6251 (TMT) has been computed. Form 8834 feeds Schedule 3 line 6i → line 7 subtotal → line 8 → Form 1040 line 20.

## 13. Interaction with Other Tax Items

### Form 8582-CR (Passive Activity Credit Limitations)

Form 8834 line 1 is entirely dependent on Form 8582-CR. The passive activity credit mechanism determines how much of the suspended QEV credit is released in the current year (e.g., due to disposition of the passive activity or sufficient passive income). Form 8582-CR must be completed first.

### Form 6251 (Alternative Minimum Tax)

Even if the taxpayer does not owe AMT, Form 6251 must be completed to determine the tentative minimum tax (TMT) for line 5. The TMT on line 5 reduces the available credit capacity on line 6. A taxpayer with a high TMT relative to regular tax may find that little or no credit is available.

### No Carryforward — Permanent Loss of Unused Credit

Unlike Form 8396 (3-year carryforward) and Form 8859 (indefinite carryforward), Form 8834 provides **no carryforward**. If the tax liability limit (line 6) is less than the passive activity credits available (line 1), the difference is permanently lost. The timing of passive activity disposals matters — the taxpayer should have sufficient tax capacity in the year the credit is released.

### Form 8936 — Distinction

Form 8834 is for the **old** QEV credit (pre-2007 vehicles, passive activity credits only). Form 8936 is for **current** clean vehicle credits (vehicles placed in service after 2022). These are entirely separate credits and separate forms with no overlap.

### Schedule 3 Line 6i Position

Form 8834 occupies **Schedule 3 line 6i**, which is part of the line 7 subtotal of "other nonrefundable credits." When computing Form 8834, line 6i is treated as not yet determined (= 0) to avoid circularity.

## 15. Out of Scope

- **New clean vehicle credits (post-2022):** Use Form 8936 for vehicles placed in service after 2022.
- **Direct (non-passive) QEV credits:** The original IRC § 30 credit expired after 2006 for non-passive uses; no direct credit available.
- **Form 8582-CR computation:** The passive activity credit limitation computation is on Form 8582-CR; Form 8834 consumes its output.
- **Corporate or estate/trust filers:** This spec covers individual Form 1040 filers. Lines 2, 3b, and 5 have different instructions for corporations (Form 1120 Schedule J) and estates/trusts (Form 1041 Schedule G / Schedule I). These are noted in §6 but not detailed.
- **AMT computation (Form 6251):** Form 6251 must be completed separately; Form 8834 consumes Form 6251 line 9. The Form 6251 computation is covered in the Line 17 spec.
- **Passive activity credit carryforward mechanics (Form 8582-CR):** Out of scope for this spec.

## 18. IRS Source Reference

**Form 8834 (Rev. 10-2024) — Notes and Specific Instructions:**

- **Notes (top of form):** "Use this form to claim qualified electric vehicle passive activity credits from prior years (allowed on Form 8582-CR or Form 8810 for the current year). The qualified electric vehicle credit was available for certain vehicles placed in service before 2007. Claim the credit for certain clean vehicles placed in service after 2022 on Form 8936."
- **Line 1:** "Enter the qualified electric vehicle passive activity credits allowed for your current tax year from Form 8582-CR, Passive Activity Credit Limitations (for individuals, estates, and trusts), or Form 8810, Corporate Passive Activity Loss and Credit Limitations (for corporations)."
- **Line 2 (individuals):** "Enter the sum of the amounts from Form 1040, 1040-SR, or 1040-NR, line 16; and Schedule 2 (Form 1040), line 1z."
- **Line 3b (individuals):** "Enter the total of any credits on Form 1040, 1040-SR, or 1040-NR, line 19; and Schedule 3 (Form 1040), lines 2 through 5, and 7 (reduced by any general business credit reported on line 6a, any credit for prior year minimum tax reported on line 6b, any alternative fuel vehicle refueling property credit (personal-use part) reported on line 6j, or any credit to holders of tax credit bonds reported on line 6k)."
- **Line 5:** "Although you may not owe alternative minimum tax (AMT), you generally must still figure your tentative minimum tax (TMT) to figure your credit."
- **Line 7:** "If you can't use part of the credit because of the tax liability limit, the unused credit is lost. The unused or excess credit can't be carried back or forward to other tax years."
