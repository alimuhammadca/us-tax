# Form 8859 — Carryforward of the District of Columbia First-Time Homebuyer Credit

**Tax year:** 2025
**IRS form:** Form 8859 (2025), Attachment Sequence No. 106, OMB No. 1545-0074, Cat. No. 24779G (created 8/7/25)
**Sources:** Form 8859 (2025) with instructions; 2025 Form 1040 instructions (Schedule 3 line 6h); IRC § 1400C; Taxes For Dummies 2024 Edition (carryforward-only; confirmed same mechanics); J.K. Lasser's Your Income Tax 2025 Professional Edition (no dedicated section — credit expired 2011)
**Attaches to:** Form 1040, 1040-SR, or 1040-NR
**Destination:** Form 8859 line 3 → Schedule 3 (Form 1040) line 6h → Schedule 3 line 8 → Form 1040 line 20

---

## 1. Line Identity

Form 8859 computes the **carryforward** of the District of Columbia first-time homebuyer credit. In 2025, the form is **carryforward-only** — no new credits may be claimed because the original credit expired after purchases made after December 31, 2011 (per IRC § 1400C). A 2025 Form 8859 may be needed only if the taxpayer has an **unused credit balance from a prior-year Form 8859 line 4**.

The 2025 Form 1040 instructions state this directly for Schedule 3 line 6h. Taxes For Dummies confirms: start with the remaining available credit from the prior year, apply the limitation, claim the smaller amount, and carry forward the rest.

Form 8859 computes:
- the **current-year usable portion** of the DC homebuyer carryforward, and
- the **unused amount carried to 2026**.

The form has four lines:

| Line | Purpose |
|---|---|
| 1 | Bring in carryforward from 2024 (prior-year Form 8859 line 4) |
| 2 | Tax liability limitation (Tax Liability Limit Worksheet) |
| 3 | Current-year allowable credit = min(line 1, line 2) → Schedule 3 line 6h |
| 4 | Remaining carryforward to 2026 = line 1 − line 3 |

The credit is **nonrefundable**. Any unused amount on line 4 carries forward indefinitely until fully used.

---

## 2. Who Can Claim / Eligibility in 2025

In 2025, only taxpayers with an **existing carryforward balance** from a prior-year Form 8859 (line 4 > 0) will file this form. No new credit is generated.

**Original eligibility (historical — for reference):**
- The home must have been a **first-time purchase** located in the **District of Columbia** (or certain other qualifying areas).
- The purchase must have occurred **on or before December 31, 2011**.
- The taxpayer (or spouse, if MFJ) must **not** have had an ownership interest in a principal residence in D.C. during the 1-year period ending on the date of purchase.

**Caution:** You may claim **only** a carryforward of any unused credit (shown on the prior year Form 8859 line 4). No new credits can be generated on the 2025 form.

---

## 3. Per-Return Filing Rules

```
Form 8859 count per return = 0 or 1
```

One Form 8859 is filed per return (not one per spouse). Two reasons:

1. The 2025 form says **"Attach to Form 1040, 1040-SR, or 1040-NR"** in the singular.
2. Line 2 uses the **return's** tax-liability limitation (not a per-spouse limitation) to determine the allowed amount.

The form header shows "Name(s) shown on return" and a single Social Security number (the primary filer's). A joint return files one Form 8859.

A taxpayer files Form 8859 **only** if they have a carryforward balance from a prior-year Form 8859 (line 4 > 0). Otherwise no Form 8859 is needed.

---

## 4. Per-Spouse and Joint-Return Rules

**Not required per spouse on a joint return.** One Form 8859 covers the MFJ return.

**MFJ:** Both spouses' combined carryforward is reported on a single Form 8859. The 2025 form and instructions do not separately track per-spouse carryforward balances on a joint return. The combined balance from line 4 of the prior-year joint Form 8859 is entered on line 1.

**Does Form 8859 require both spouses' information on MFJ?** Yes, it can — because line 2 is based on the **joint return's tax liability after other credits**, and the Tax Liability Limit Worksheet starts from **Form 1040 line 18** (which reflects both spouses' combined tax) and subtracts several return-level credits. On MFJ the result naturally depends on the joint return.

```
if filing_status == MFJ:
    create one Form8859
    line1 = combined carryforward from prior-year joint Form8859 line4
    use joint-return line 18 and joint-return credits in the limitation worksheet
else:
    use taxpayer-only return data
```

**MFS situation:** If spouses file separate returns and each holds their own carryforward balance, each spouse includes Form 8859 on their own separate return. The original MFS credit limit was $2,500 (vs. $5,000 for single/MFJ), which governs the historical per-spouse carryforward amounts.

**Transition from joint to separate (or vice versa):** Allocation of a joint carryforward when spouses change filing status is a facts-and-circumstances determination not addressed in the 2025 form instructions.

## 5. Original Credit — Historical Context

The original DC first-time homebuyer credit (IRC § 1400C) applied to qualifying purchases from August 5, 1997 through December 31, 2011.

| Item | Original Rules |
|---|---|
| **Maximum credit** | Smaller of $5,000 ($2,500 MFS) or the purchase price of the home |
| **Credit type** | Nonrefundable |
| **MAGI phaseout begins** | $70,000 (single); $110,000 (MFJ) |
| **MAGI phaseout complete** | $90,000 (single); $130,000 (MFJ) |
| **Carryforward** | Indefinite — until fully used |
| **Purchase deadline** | December 31, 2011 (no new credits after this date) |

These historical facts govern the **size** of a carryforward balance that may appear on line 1 of the 2025 form, but do not affect the 2025 computation mechanics. For 2025, you are only carrying forward the unused prior-year amount.

## 6. How to Complete Form 8859 — Line by Line

### Line 1 — Credit Carryforward from 2024

Enter the amount from **line 4 of the 2024 Form 8859**. This is the only carryforward source for the 2025 form.

```
line1 = prior_year_Form8859.line4
```

- If the taxpayer has no prior Form 8859, they have no carryforward and should not file Form 8859 in 2025.
- If the 2024 Form 8859 does not exist (e.g., the credit was fully used in a prior year), do not file.

### Line 2 — Tax Liability Limitation

Enter the amount from the **Tax Liability Limit Worksheet** (in the Form 8859 instructions — kept for records, not filed separately). See §7 below for the worksheet computation.

If the worksheet result is zero or less, enter $0 on lines 2 and 3.

### Line 3 — Current-Year Credit

```
line3 = min(line1, line2)
```

Enter the **smaller of line 1 or line 2**. Include this amount on **Schedule 3 (Form 1040), line 6h**.

### Line 4 — Credit Carryforward to 2026

```
line4 = line1 − line3
```

This is the unused credit balance that carries forward to 2026.

- **No expiration:** The unused balance on line 4 may be carried forward indefinitely until fully applied.
- **No carryback:** The unused credit cannot be carried back to prior years.
- **Keep a copy** of Form 8859 — next year's Form 8859 line 1 = this year's line 4.

---

## 7. Tax Liability Limit Worksheet (Line 2)

The worksheet limits the DC homebuyer credit to the tax remaining after higher-priority nonrefundable credits have consumed tax liability. The worksheet appears in the Form 8859 instructions (kept for records; not filed).

```
Worksheet step 1:  Form 1040 (or 1040-SR / 1040-NR) line 18
                   [total tax before credits]

Worksheet step 2:  Sum of the following credits if taken on the 2025 return:
                     Schedule 3 line 6l   (Form 8978 negative adjustment)
                   + Schedule 3 line 1    (Foreign tax credit)
                   + Schedule 3 line 2    (Child and dependent care credit)
                   + Schedule 3 line 6d   (Credit for the elderly or disabled)
                   + Schedule 3 line 3    (Nonrefundable education credits)
                   + Schedule 3 line 4    (Retirement savings contributions credit)
                   + Schedule 3 line 5b   (Energy efficient home improvement credit)
                   + Schedule 3 line 6m   (Credit for previously owned clean vehicles)
                   + Schedule 3 line 6f   (Clean vehicle credit — new)
                   + Form 1040 line 19*   (Child tax credit / credit for other dependents)
                   + Schedule 3 line 6g   (Mortgage interest credit)
                   + Schedule 3 line 6c   (Adoption credit)

Worksheet step 3:  line2 = max(0, step1 − step2)
```

**Schedule 8812 interaction:** If the Schedule 8812 instructions direct you to complete Credit Limit Worksheet B, use **Schedule 8812 Credit Limit Worksheet B line 14** in place of Form 1040 line 19 (*) in step 2 above.

**Credit ordering:** Form 8859 ranks after all credits listed in step 2 — Foreign tax credit (Sch3 line 1), child/dependent care (line 2), elderly/disabled (line 6d), education (line 3), retirement saver (line 4), energy efficient home improvement (line 5b), previously owned clean vehicle (line 6m), new clean vehicle (line 6f), CTC/ODC (Form 1040 line 19), mortgage interest credit (line 6g), and adoption credit (line 6c). All must be computed and finalized before line 2 of Form 8859 can be determined.

---

## 8. Full Line-by-Line Formula Summary

```
line 1    Credit carryforward from 2024 (prior-year Form 8859 line 4)
line 2    Tax Liability Limit Worksheet:
            = max(0, Form1040.line18
                  − (Sch3.line6l + Sch3.line1 + Sch3.line2 + Sch3.line6d
                  + Sch3.line3 + Sch3.line4 + Sch3.line5b + Sch3.line6m
                  + Sch3.line6f + adjusted_line19 + Sch3.line6g + Sch3.line6c))
line 3    = min(line1, line2)     → Schedule 3 line 6h
line 4    = line1 − line3         [carry forward indefinitely until used]

adjusted_line19 = Schedule8812.CreditLimitWorksheetB.line14
                  if Schedule8812 requires it
                  else Form1040.line19
```

## 9. Developer-Safe End-to-End Logic

```
// Gate: only file if a carryforward exists
if prior_year_Form8859_line4 == 0 or null:
    no Form8859 needed

else:
    line1 = prior_year_Form8859_line4

    // Schedule 8812 interaction: use CLW-B line14 if directed
    adjusted_line19 = Schedule8812.CreditLimitWorksheetB.line14
                      if Schedule8812_requires_CLW_B
                      else Form1040.line19

    // Tax Liability Limit Worksheet
    step1 = Form1040.line18

    step2 = Schedule3.line6l      // Form 8978 negative adjustment
          + Schedule3.line1       // Foreign tax credit
          + Schedule3.line2       // Child/dependent care
          + Schedule3.line6d      // Elderly/disabled
          + Schedule3.line3       // Education credits
          + Schedule3.line4       // Retirement saver
          + Schedule3.line5b      // Energy efficient home improvement
          + Schedule3.line6m      // Previously owned clean vehicle
          + Schedule3.line6f      // New clean vehicle personal
          + adjusted_line19       // CTC/ODC (or CLW-B line14)
          + Schedule3.line6g      // Mortgage interest credit
          + Schedule3.line6c      // Adoption credit

    line2 = max(0, step1 − step2)

    line3 = min(line1, line2)
    line4 = line1 − line3         // Store as 2026 Form 8859 line 1

    Schedule3.line6h = line3
```

## 12. Compute Order and Dependencies

| Step | Requires |
|---|---|
| Line 1 | User-provided carryforward from 2024 Form 8859 line 4 |
| Line 2 (worksheet) | Form 1040 line 18 + all credits listed in Tax Liability Limit Worksheet step 2 — **must be computed after all higher-priority nonrefundable credits are finalized** |
| Line 3 (current-year credit) | Lines 1 and 2 complete |
| Line 4 (carryforward) | Line 3 complete |
| Schedule 3 line 6h | Line 3 finalized |

**Critical ordering:** Form 8859 must be computed **after** all of the following credits have been finalized:
- Schedule 3 lines 1, 2, 3, 4, 5b, 6c, 6d, 6f, 6g, 6l, 6m
- Form 1040 line 19 (CTC/ODC via Schedule 8812)

Form 8859 feeds **Schedule 3 line 6h**, which feeds into **Schedule 3 line 7** (total nonrefundable credits subtotal) → **Schedule 3 line 8** → **Form 1040 line 20**.

## 13. Interaction with Other Tax Items

### Schedule 3 — Credit Sequencing

Form 8859 is one of the last nonrefundable credits in Schedule 3 Part I. Its Tax Liability Limit Worksheet subtracts all prior-sequence nonrefundable credits from tax liability, so taxpayers with high other credits may find their line 2 reduced to zero, preserving the full carryforward on line 4.

### No Schedule A Interaction

Form 8859 has no interaction with Schedule A. Unlike Form 8396 (mortgage interest credit), there is no mandatory deduction reduction associated with the DC homebuyer credit carryforward.

### AMT

The Tax Liability Limit Worksheet uses Form 1040 line 18, which includes AMT from Form 6251 if applicable. Taxpayers subject to AMT will have a higher step 1 in the worksheet, which generally benefits the DC homebuyer credit (more tax liability available for the credit to offset).

### Schedule 8812 Credit Limit Worksheet B

If the Schedule 8812 instructions direct the taxpayer to complete Credit Limit Worksheet B, use **Schedule 8812 CLW-B line 14** instead of Form 1040 line 19 in Tax Liability Limit Worksheet step 2. This affects taxpayers with Additional Child Tax Credit refundable computations.

## 15. Out of Scope

- **New DC first-time homebuyer purchases:** No new credit is available for 2025 or any year after 2011. Form 8859 is carryforward-only.
- **DC homebuyer credit recapture:** The original credit did not carry a recapture provision comparable to Form 8828 (mortgage interest). No recapture form applies.
- **Transition between MFJ and MFS:** Allocation of a joint carryforward when spouses change filing status is a facts-and-circumstances determination not addressed in the 2025 form instructions.
- **Original credit computation:** The income phase-out worksheet, purchase price limit, and first-time buyer eligibility determination applied only for purchase years (1997–2011). These are not part of the 2025 computation.
- **AMT limitation:** No separate AMT limitation applies to this credit. The credit is included in computing the tentative minimum tax base as a nonrefundable personal credit.

## 18. IRS Source Reference

**Form 8859 (2025) — Specific Instructions:**
- **Line 1:** "Credit carryforward from 2024. Enter the amount from line 4 of your 2024 Form 8859."
- **Line 2:** Tax Liability Limit Worksheet: step 1 = Form 1040 line 18; step 2 = sum of 12 specific prior credits; step 3 = max(0, step 1 − step 2); if zero or less, enter -0- on Form 8859 lines 2 and 3.
- **Line 3:** Smaller of line 1 or line 2 → Schedule 3 line 6h.
- **Line 4:** "You can carry forward any unused credit shown on line 4 until you have used all of it. You cannot carry the unused credit back to prior years."
- **Footnote:** Use Schedule 8812 Credit Limit Worksheet B line 14 instead of Form 1040 line 19 in step 2 of the worksheet if directed by the Schedule 8812 instructions.
