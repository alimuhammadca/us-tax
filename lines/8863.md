For **tax year 2025**, you generally file **one Form 8863 per tax return**, not one per spouse and not one per student. That single Form 8863 can cover **multiple students** by using a **separate Part III for each student**. The IRS form itself says: "Complete a separate Part III on page 2 for each student ... Use additional copies of page 2 as needed for each student." ([IRS][1])

## How many Form 8863s per return?

* **Usually one Form 8863 per return**
* If you are claiming credits for multiple students, still **one Form 8863**, but with:

  * one **Part III** per student, and
  * extra copies of page 2 if needed. ([IRS][1])

## What Form 8863 is for

Form 8863 is used to figure and claim the two education credits:

* **American Opportunity Credit (AOTC)** - partly refundable
* **Lifetime Learning Credit (LLC)** - nonrefundable only. ([IRS][2])

You can claim **both credits on the same return**, but **not for the same student**. ([IRS][2])

# Inputs you need before completing Form 8863

## Documents / statements

* **Form 1098-T** for each student, if issued
* School EIN (required for AOTC, and also when 1098-T was issued) ([IRS][2])
* Records of **qualified education expenses** actually paid in 2025
* Records of tax-free assistance that reduces eligible expenses:

  * scholarships / grants
  * employer educational assistance
  * veterans' benefits
  * tax-free distributions used for education
* Student eligibility facts:

  * at least half-time?
  * first 4 years completed?
  * felony drug conviction?
  * prior AOTC claimed for 4 years already?
* Return-level **MAGI** inputs (usually Form 1040 line 11b, with adjustments if filing Form 2555, Form 4563, or excluding Puerto Rico income) ([IRS][1])

## User inputs / facts

For each student:

* which credit is being claimed: **AOTC** or **LLC**
* adjusted qualified education expenses
* institution name, address, EIN
* whether Form 1098-T was received for 2025
* whether 2024 Form 1098-T had box 7 checked
* for AOTC:

  * whether the credit has already been claimed for the student in **4 prior tax years**
  * whether the student was enrolled at least half-time in a program leading to a credential
  * whether the student completed the first 4 years before 2025
  * whether the student had a felony drug conviction. ([IRS][1])

# How to complete Form 8863

## Step 1: Complete a separate Part III for each student first

The form requires you to finish **Part III for each student** before Parts I and II. ([IRS][1])

---

## Part III - Student and Educational Institution Information

### Lines 20-22: Student and school information

For each student:

* line 20: student name
* line 21: student SSN/TIN
* line 22: school information (up to two institutions on one Part III page), including:

  * name
  * address
  * whether 1098-T was received
  * whether prior-year 1098-T had box 7 checked
  * school EIN if required. ([IRS][1])

### AOTC eligibility questions

These determine whether you can use **lines 27-30** (AOTC) or must stop and go to line 31 (LLC only):

* line 23: AOTC already claimed for this student in 4 prior tax years?
* line 24: at least half-time in a program leading to a recognized credential?
* line 25: first 4 years already completed before 2025?
* line 26: felony drug conviction before end of 2025? ([IRS][1])

If any disqualifier applies, do **not** compute AOTC for that student; go to **line 31** for possible LLC. ([IRS][1])

---

## Step 2: Compute the student-level credit in Part III

### A) American Opportunity Credit - lines 27-30

If the student qualifies:

* **line 27** = adjusted qualified education expenses, capped at **$4,000**
* **line 28** = line 27 - 2,000 (not below 0)
* **line 29** = 25% of line 28
* **line 30** = line 29 + 2,000
  This gives the student's AOTC amount, up to **$2,500 per student**. The instructions and comparison table confirm the AOTC maximum is $2,500 per eligible student. ([IRS][1])

### B) Lifetime Learning Credit - line 31

If claiming LLC for the student:

* **line 31** = adjusted qualified education expenses for that student
  These student-level LLC amounts are later combined in Part II. ([IRS][2])

---

## Step 3: Complete Part I - Refundable AOTC

Part I is only for the **refundable piece** of the American Opportunity Credit.

### Part I flow

* **line 1** = total of all **Part III line 30** amounts for students claiming AOTC ([IRS][2])
* **line 2** = MAGI threshold:

  * **180,000** if MFJ
  * **90,000** if Single / HOH / QSS ([IRS][1])
* **line 3** = MAGI (generally Form 1040 line 11b, with special add-backs if filing Form 2555 / 4563 / Puerto Rico exclusion) ([IRS][1])
* lines 4-7 = phaseout computation
* **line 8** = refundable AOTC = **40% of line 7**
  The form says line 8 goes to **Form 1040 line 29**. ([IRS][1])

### Important refundable AOTC restriction

The instructions include a separate question set to determine whether a taxpayer **under age 24** can claim the refundable portion. In some cases, the allowable AOTC is still available, but **not the refundable part**. ([IRS][2])

---

## Step 4: Complete Part II - Nonrefundable education credits

This handles:

* the **nonrefundable part of AOTC**, and
* the **Lifetime Learning Credit**

### Part II flow

* **line 9** = line 7 - line 8 (remaining nonrefundable AOTC amount) ([IRS][1])
* **line 10** = total of all **Part III line 31** amounts for LLC students ([IRS][1])
* **line 11** = smaller of line 10 or **$10,000**
* **line 12** = 20% of line 11
* **line 13** = line 9 + line 12
* **line 14** = MAGI (same concept as Part I)
* lines 15-18 = phaseout computation
* **line 19** = nonrefundable education credits after applying the **Credit Limit Worksheet**
  The form/instructions say line 19 goes to **Schedule 3 (Form 1040), line 3**. ([IRS][1])

---

## Step 5: Complete the Credit Limit Worksheet

The instructions require the **Credit Limit Worksheet** to limit the nonrefundable amount on line 19.

The worksheet uses:

* **Form 8863 line 18**
* **Form 8863 line 9**
* **Form 1040 line 18**
* **Schedule 3 lines 1, 2, 6d, and 6l**
  and returns the amount that goes back to **Form 8863 line 19**. ([IRS][2])

---

# Final return mappings

## Refundable credit

* **Form 8863 line 8** -> **Form 1040 line 29** ([IRS][1])

## Nonrefundable credit

* **Form 8863 line 19** -> **Schedule 3 line 3** ([IRS][2])

# Eligibility rules you should enforce in software

## General

* MAGI limit for both credits in 2025:

  * **$180,000 MFJ**
  * **$90,000 Single / HOH / QSS** ([IRS][2])
* The law generally requires **Form 1098-T** unless the institution wasn't required to issue one and the taxpayer can otherwise substantiate enrollment and payment. ([IRS][2])
* To claim **AOTC**, the institution EIN must be provided on Form 8863. ([IRS][2])
* **Form 8862** may be required if AOTC was previously denied/reduced for reasons other than math/clerical error. ([IRS][2])
* You cannot claim **AOTC and LLC for the same student in the same year**. ([IRS][2])

## AOTC student-specific

A student is not eligible for AOTC if any of these apply:

* credit already claimed for 4 prior tax years
* not at least half-time
* already completed first 4 years
* felony drug conviction. ([IRS][1])
