# Form 8880 — Credit for Qualified Retirement Savings Contributions (Saver's Credit)

**Tax year:** 2025
**IRS form:** Form 8880 (2025), Attachment Sequence No. 54
**Sources:** `docs/IRS-Forms/f8880.pdf`; J.K. Lasser's Your Income Tax 2025 Professional Edition, Chapter 25 §§ 25.11–25.12
**Destination:** Schedule 3 (Form 1040), line 4 → Form 1040 line 20

---

## 1. Line Identity

Form 8880 computes the **non-refundable Credit for Qualified Retirement Savings Contributions**, commonly called the **Saver's Credit**. The maximum credit is **$1,000 per person** ($2,000 combined on an MFJ return).

The form has **two columns**:
- **(a) You** — taxpayer
- **(b) Your spouse** — complete only when filing a joint return (MFJ)

---

## 2. Per-Return vs. Per-Spouse Filing Rules

Form 8880 is filed **once per tax return**, not once per spouse.

```
Form 8880 count per return = 0 or 1
```

| Filing situation | Rule |
|---|---|
| MFJ | One joint Form 8880 using both columns (a) and (b) |
| Single / HOH / QSS | At most one Form 8880, column (a) only |
| MFS | At most one Form 8880 per separate return, column (a) only |

On a **joint return**, do **not** file two Forms 8880. File one and place:
- taxpayer amounts in column (a)
- spouse amounts in column (b)

Even though only one form is filed on MFJ, **both spouses' data are required** because each spouse may have independent contributions, line 4 cross-applies both spouses' distributions to both columns in certain years, and the form produces a single joint credit from both columns.

---

## 3. Eligibility — Hard Disqualifiers

The credit is **completely unavailable** if **either** of the following applies to the return:

### 3a. AGI exceeds the income ceiling (Form 1040 line 11a)

| Filing Status | Income Ceiling |
|---|---|
| Single / MFS / QSS | $39,500 |
| Head of Household | $59,250 |
| Married Filing Jointly | $79,000 |

> **Note:** If claiming any exclusion or deduction for foreign earned income, foreign housing, Puerto Rico income, or American Samoa income, refigure line 11a per Pub. 590-A before applying the ceiling.

### 3b. The contributing person is individually disqualified

A person **cannot** generate a qualifying contribution in their column if they:
1. Were **born after January 1, 2008** (must be age 18+ as of January 1, 2026), **OR**
2. Are **claimed as a dependent** on someone else's 2025 tax return, **OR**
3. Were a **full-time student** during any part of 5 or more calendar months of 2025.

**Full-time student definition:** Enrolled full-time at a school (including technical, trade, or mechanical schools), or took a full-time on-farm training course given by a school or government agency. Does **not** include on-the-job training, correspondence schools, or internet-only courses.

Each person's eligibility is evaluated independently per column. A disqualified spouse does not disqualify the taxpayer's column (a).

---

## 4. Qualified Contributions

### Line 1 — IRA and ABLE account contributions
- Traditional IRA contributions (rollovers excluded)
- Roth IRA contributions (rollovers excluded)
- ABLE account contributions made by the **designated beneficiary** (P.L. 119-21; permanent credit as of 2025)

### Line 2 — Employer plan elective deferrals and voluntary contributions
- Elective deferrals to 401(k), 403(b), governmental 457(b), SEP, SIMPLE, or federal TSP
- Designated Roth contributions under § 402A
- Voluntary employee contributions to a qualified retirement plan (§ 4974(c), including federal TSP)
- Contributions to a 501(c)(18)(D) plan

> **Excluded from line 2:** Contributions designated under § 414(h)(2) (mandatory employee contributions treated as employer contributions) are not voluntary and do not qualify.
>
> **W-2 box 12 codes** that indicate elective deferrals eligible for line 2: D, E, F, G, H, S.

---

## 5. Distribution Offset (Line 4)

Qualified contributions must be **reduced by distributions received** before the credit is calculated.

### Time window
Distributions received **after December 31, 2022** and **before the due date (including extensions) of the 2025 return** from any of:
- Traditional or Roth IRAs, ABLE accounts
- 401(k), 403(b), governmental 457(b), 501(c)(18)(D), SEP, SIMPLE, federal TSP
- Any qualified retirement plan (§ 4974(c))

### MFJ cross-column rule
Include **both spouses' distributions in both columns** (a) and (b).

**Exception:** Do not include the spouse's distributions in your column (a) for a year in which you and your spouse did **not** file a joint return.

*Example:* Taxpayer received $5,000 from a qualified plan in 2025. Spouse received $2,000 from a Roth IRA in 2023. They file jointly in both 2023 and 2025. Column (a) = $5,000; column (b) = $7,000 (both spouses' distributions combined, since a joint return was filed in 2023 when the spouse's distribution occurred).

### Distributions that are **NOT** counted as offsets
- Non-taxable rollovers or direct trustee-to-trustee transfers
- In-plan Roth rollovers (to a designated Roth account within the same plan)
- Conversions/rollovers from traditional IRA → Roth IRA, SIMPLE IRA, or SEP IRA designated as Roth
- Loans from a qualified employer plan treated as a distribution
- Excess contributions or deferrals (and income allocable to them)
- IRA contributions returned by the due date with allocable income
- Dividends on employer stock paid under § 404(k)
- Military retirement plan distributions (except federal TSP)
- Inherited IRA distributions received by a nonspousal beneficiary

## 6. Line-by-Line Computation

```
Line 1   IRA + ABLE contributions                          (per column)
Line 2   Employer plan deferrals + voluntary contributions (per column)
Line 3   = Line 1 + Line 2                                 (per column)
Line 4   Distributions after 2022 and before return due date (per column; MFJ: both spouses in both)
Line 5   = Line 3 − Line 4; if zero or less → enter 0     (per column)
Line 6   = min(Line 5, $2,000)                             (per column — $2,000 cap per person)
Line 7   = Line 6(a) + Line 6(b)   [non-MFJ: Line 6(a) only]
           If Line 7 = 0 → STOP; cannot take this credit
Line 8   AGI = Form 1040 line 11a
Line 9   Credit rate from table below based on Line 8 and filing status
           If Line 9 = 0.0 → STOP; cannot take this credit
Line 10  = Line 7 × Line 9
Line 11  = Tax liability limitation (Credit Limit Worksheet — see §8)
Line 12  = min(Line 10, Line 11)   ← final credit; enter on Schedule 3, line 4
```

## 8. Credit Limit Worksheet (Line 11)

The Saver's Credit is **non-refundable**. Line 11 caps the credit at remaining tax liability after prior Schedule 3 nonrefundable credits.

```
Worksheet step 1   Form 1040/1040-SR/1040-NR line 18 (total tax before credits)
Worksheet step 2   Sum of Schedule 3 credits: lines 1, 2, 3, 6d, and 6l
                   (foreign tax credit, child/dependent care, education credits,
                    elderly/disabled credit, other nonrefundable credits)
Worksheet step 3   Line 11 = step 1 − step 2
                   If step 3 ≤ 0 → STOP; cannot take this credit; do not file Form 8880
```

## 9. Compute Order and Dependencies

| Step | Input source |
|---|---|
| AGI ceiling check | Form 1040 line 11a |
| Lines 1–6: net contribution per column | User input (contributions, distributions) |
| Line 7: combined eligible amount | Lines 6(a) + 6(b) |
| Line 8: AGI | Form 1040 line 11a |
| Line 9: credit rate | Line 8 + filing status lookup |
| Line 10: preliminary credit | Line 7 × Line 9 |
| Line 11: tax liability limit | Form 1040 line 18; Schedule 3 lines 1–3, 6d, 6l |
| Line 12: final credit | min(line 10, line 11) → Schedule 3 line 4 |

**Upstream dependencies:** Line 16 (tax), Line 17 (AMT), Line 18 (total tax before credits), and Schedule 3 lines 1–3 / 6d / 6l must all be computed before the Credit Limit Worksheet can be evaluated.

**Downstream:** Form 8880 line 12 → Schedule 3 line 4 → Schedule 3 total → Form 1040 line 20.

---

## 10. Coordination with IRA Deduction

The Saver's Credit **may be claimed in addition to** any IRA deduction on Schedule 1 line 20. They are independent — claiming the credit does not reduce or affect the IRA deduction, and vice versa.

---

## 11. SECURE 2.0 Act — Future Phase-Out (Note for 2025)

Starting with **2027 tax returns** (filed in 2028), the Saver's Credit for retirement account contributions will be **replaced by a Saver's Match** — a government deposit made directly into the taxpayer's retirement account. From 2027, Form 8880 will only be used for ABLE account contributions; a new separate form will handle the saver's match.

For 2025 and 2026, Form 8880 applies in full as documented here.

## 13. Out of Scope

- Saver's Match computation (replaces the credit starting 2027 returns)
- Refigured AGI per Pub. 590-A for foreign earned income / Puerto Rico / American Samoa exclusion cases — deferred; use Form 1040 line 11a as-is unless adjusted by Form 2555
- Schedule R (Credit for Elderly or Disabled) — its effect on line 11 is handled by reading Schedule 3 line 6d directly in the Credit Limit Worksheet
