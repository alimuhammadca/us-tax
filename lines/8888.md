# Form 8888 — Allocation of Refund

**Tax year:** 2025
**IRS form:** Form 8888 (Rev. December 2025)
**Sources:** IRS Form 8888 (Rev. December 2025) and instructions; IRS Publication 17 (2024) Chapter 1; IRS Form 1040 Instructions 2025; J.K. Lasser's Your Income Tax 2025 Professional Edition §46.4; Taxes For Dummies 2024 Edition
**Attaches to:** Form 1040, 1040-SR, 1040-SS, or 1040-NR (filed with the return)
**Destination:** Directs IRS to split the **entire** refund among up to three direct-deposit accounts. The total of all allocations (line 5) must equal the refund on the return.

```text
IMPORTANT 2024/2025 CHANGE:
The savings bond purchase feature (I bonds, TreasuryDirect, paper bonds) is DISCONTINUED.
Form 8888 is now ONLY used to split a direct-deposit refund between
two or more accounts. The ENTIRE refund must be allocated — there is
no "remainder as check" option when using this form.
```

---

## 1. What Form 8888 Is

Form 8888 allows a taxpayer with a refund to split that refund among **up to three** bank, brokerage, or IRA/HSA/ESA accounts via direct deposit. Without Form 8888, a refund can only go to a single account (entered directly on Form 1040 lines 35b–d) or be issued as a paper check.

Form 8888 is **not a tax computation form**. It contains no tax calculations — it is a routing instruction form only.

### Key 2025 Change
The program allowing refunds to be deposited into TreasuryDirect® to purchase I bonds or Series EE paper bonds has been **permanently discontinued** as of the 2024 tax year. Form 8888 no longer has a savings bond section. **Line 4 is now reserved for future use.** Line 5 is the total of all account allocations and must equal the refund.

---

## 2. Cardinality

```text
Form 8888 count per return = 0 or 1
```

- **One form per return** regardless of filing status.
- **MFJ:** One Form 8888 covers both spouses' combined joint refund. Not one per spouse.
- **MFS:** Each spouse files their own separate Form 8888 for their own return.
- The form supports **2 or 3 accounts** in lines 1–3. If only 1 account is desired, do not use Form 8888 — request direct deposit on Form 1040 lines 35b–d directly.

---

## 3. Is Form 8888 Required Per Spouse?

**No.** On an MFJ return, one Form 8888 is filed for the joint return, not one per spouse. It allocates the joint refund using the return's combined name(s) and primary SSN.

**Does it require information from both spouses on MFJ?**
Yes — because it applies to the joint refund. The IRS specifically warns that on a joint return, the spouse may receive at least part of the refund if accounts are in separate names. The refund must go to accounts in the filer's name; deposits to accounts in another person's name (including a tax preparer's account) are prohibited.

---

## 4. When to Use Form 8888

Use Form 8888 when **all** of the following apply:

1. There is a **refund** on the return (Form 1040 line 35a > 0), **and**
2. The taxpayer wants the refund split into **exactly 2 or 3 direct-deposit accounts**, **and**
3. The taxpayer is **not** filing **Form 8379** (Injured Spouse Allocation).

**Do NOT use Form 8888 if:**
- The refund goes to only **one account** → use Form 1040 lines 35b–d directly; Form 8888 is not needed and should not be attached.
- There is **no refund** (balance due or zero).
- Filing **Form 8379** (injured spouse — IRS prohibits simultaneous use).
- The taxpayer wants a **paper check** for any portion of the refund — that is not supported by Form 8888 in 2025; if a check is desired, do not use Form 8888 at all and request a paper refund check instead.

---

## 5. Eligible Account Types

The IRS allows direct deposit of refunds to the following account types via Form 8888:

| Account Type | Notes |
|---|---|
| Checking | Standard bank checking account |
| Savings | Standard bank savings account |
| Traditional IRA | Account must be **established before** the return is filed. Deposit counts as a **prior-year (2025) contribution** only if deposited by **April 15, 2026** — extensions do not extend the IRA contribution deadline. Taxpayer must notify the trustee which year the contribution applies to. |
| Roth IRA | Same rules as Traditional IRA re: prior-year contribution timing and notification. |
| SEP IRA | Allowed; same direct deposit mechanics as traditional IRA. |
| Health Savings Account (HSA) | Allowed; standard HSA rules apply. |
| Archer MSA | Allowed. |
| Coverdell Education Savings Account (ESA) | Allowed. |
| Brokerage / mutual fund accounts | Standard brokerage accounts at U.S. financial institutions. |

**Not allowed:**
- **SIMPLE IRA** — explicitly prohibited by IRS instructions (IRS Form 1040 instructions: "not a SIMPLE IRA").
- Non-U.S. bank accounts — ACH direct deposit is U.S.-only.

**Important:** The PDF form only has "Checking" / "Savings" checkboxes (lines 1c, 2c, 3c). IRAs, HSAs, and ESAs use the financial institution's routing and account numbers; the institution's own account type classification distinguishes them. Typically check "Savings" for IRA/HSA accounts unless the institution specifies otherwise.

## 6. Form Gating Rules

| Rule | Detail |
|---|---|
| Refund required | Form 1040 line 35a must be > 0. If balance due or zero, form is not applicable. |
| Minimum 2 accounts | Form 8888 is only useful when splitting among 2 or 3 accounts. 1 account → use Form 1040 direct deposit lines instead. |
| Form 8379 conflict | Cannot use Form 8888 if also filing Form 8379 (Injured Spouse). The IRS cannot split a refund subject to injured-spouse allocation. |
| Entire refund must be allocated | The total of all account amounts (line 5) must **equal** Form 1040 line 35a exactly. Partial allocation is not permitted — if amounts do not match, the refund may be delayed or sent as a paper check. |
| Account ownership | Every account must be in the taxpayer's name (or both spouses' names on MFJ). Do not route to a tax preparer's account — doing so violates Circular 230 Reg. §10.31 and triggers a §6695(f) preparer penalty regardless of taxpayer consent. |
| Three-deposit-per-account limit | The IRS limits direct deposits to **3 refunds per calendar year** per single account or prepaid debit card. If this limit is exceeded, the IRS issues a paper check instead. |
| Whole dollars only | All amount fields (lines 1a, 2a, 3a) must be whole-dollar amounts (no cents). |
| Minimum $1 per account | Each deposit amount must be at least $1. |

## 7. Line-by-Line Completion

### Header

| Field | Description |
|---|---|
| Calendar Year | Tax year (2025). Pre-filled on printed form; software field: `page1_0_pg_header_0_calendar_year_0_f1_1_0` |
| Name(s) | Name(s) shown on the return. MFJ: both spouses' names. |
| SSN | Primary taxpayer's SSN (first SSN shown on the return). |

### Account 1 (Lines 1a–1d) — Required

| Line | Description |
|---|---|
| **1a** | Dollar amount to deposit into Account 1. Whole dollars only; minimum $1. |
| **1b** | **Routing transit number** — exactly 9 digits. First two digits must be **01–12 or 21–32** (U.S. Federal Reserve routing prefixes). |
| **1c** | **Account type** — check exactly one: ☐ Checking  ☐ Savings. |
| **1d** | **Account number** — up to 17 characters, letters and numbers allowed; hyphens are permitted; omit spaces and special symbols. |

### Account 2 (Lines 2a–2d) — Required if splitting into 2+ accounts

| Line | Description |
|---|---|
| **2a** | Dollar amount to deposit into Account 2. Whole dollars; minimum $1. |
| **2b** | Routing transit number — exactly 9 digits; first two digits 01–12 or 21–32. |
| **2c** | Account type: ☐ Checking  ☐ Savings. |
| **2d** | Account number — up to 17 characters; hyphens allowed; no spaces or special symbols. |

### Account 3 (Lines 3a–3d) — Optional (only for 3-way split)

| Line | Description |
|---|---|
| **3a** | Dollar amount to deposit into Account 3. Whole dollars; minimum $1. |
| **3b** | Routing transit number — 9 digits; first two digits 01–12 or 21–32. |
| **3c** | Account type: ☐ Checking  ☐ Savings. |
| **3d** | Account number — up to 17 characters; hyphens allowed; no spaces or special symbols. |

### Summary Lines

| Line | Formula | Description |
|---|---|---|
| **4** | — | **Reserved for future use.** Do not enter an amount. This line previously held the savings bond total; it is now blank/unused. |
| **5** | `line1a + line2a + line3a` | **Total allocated.** Must equal the refund amount on the tax return. If the total differs from the return's refund, the refund may be delayed. |

## 8. Formula and Decision Tree

```text
// Inputs
refund              = Form1040.line35a          // must be > 0

// Per-account inputs (up to 3)
deposit_amount_1    = user input                // whole dollars, >= 1
deposit_amount_2    = user input                // whole dollars, >= 1 (required for 2nd account)
deposit_amount_3    = user input                // whole dollars, >= 1 (optional 3rd account)

// Line 4: reserved — do not populate
line4               = [reserved for future use]

// Line 5: total allocation — MUST equal refund
line5               = deposit_amount_1 + deposit_amount_2 + deposit_amount_3

VALIDATION: line5 == refund   // exact match required
```

**Decision tree — when to use Form 8888:**

```text
if Form1040.line35a <= 0:
    → Form 8888 not applicable (no refund)

if Form8379 is filed:
    → Form 8888 not applicable (conflict)

if taxpayer wants 1 direct-deposit account only:
    → Do NOT use Form 8888
    → Use Form 1040 lines 35b–d directly

if taxpayer wants a paper check for any portion:
    → Do NOT use Form 8888
    → Request full paper refund check (no direct deposit) or use single-account direct deposit on Form 1040

if taxpayer wants 2 or 3 direct-deposit accounts:
    → Form 8888 required; attach to return
    → Total of all account amounts must equal Form1040.line35a
```

## 9. Routing and Account Number Validation Rules

| Field | Rule |
|---|---|
| Routing number length | Exactly 9 digits. |
| Routing number prefix | First two digits must be **01–12** or **21–32** (U.S. Federal Reserve district routing ranges). |
| Routing number correctness | The IRS does not perform a Mod-10 check at filing time. An incorrect routing number causes the deposit to fail; the IRS then mails a paper check. |
| Account number length | 1–17 characters. |
| Account number characters | Letters and numbers allowed. Hyphens are permitted. Do **not** include spaces or special symbols. |
| Account ownership | Account must be in the taxpayer's name. MFJ joint refunds may be rejected by some financial institutions if routed to an individual (non-joint) account — if rejected, IRS mails a paper check. |
| No preparer accounts | Routing to a tax preparer's account is prohibited (Circular 230 §10.31) and triggers a §6695(f) preparer penalty regardless of the taxpayer's consent. |

## 10. Rejection and Fallback Behavior

The IRS Form 8888 instructions specify explicit fallback rules when one or more accounts are invalid:

```text
If one or two deposit requests are invalid (wrong routing number, closed account, etc.):
    → The affected amount is redirected to the LAST VALID ACCOUNT listed on the form

If IRS processing is delayed or the entire Form 8888 cannot be processed:
    → The ENTIRE refund may be deposited into the LAST VALID ACCOUNT listed
```

**Implementation consequence:** The last account listed (Account 3 if present, otherwise Account 2) acts as the fallback destination for the entire refund in a worst-case scenario.

**UI warning to display:**
> "The last account you enter will receive any portion of the refund that cannot be deposited as requested — and may receive your entire refund if there is a processing issue. Make sure the last account listed is one you would accept for the full refund amount."

---

## 11. IRA / HSA / ESA Special Handling

If directing all or part of the refund to a Traditional IRA, Roth IRA, SEP IRA, HSA, Archer MSA, or Coverdell ESA:

1. The account must be **established before** the return is filed. You cannot open the account after filing and retroactively direct the refund there.
2. For IRA contributions: the deposit counts as a **prior-year (2025) contribution** only if it is **actually deposited by April 15, 2026**. Filing extensions do not extend the IRA contribution deadline.
3. The taxpayer must **notify the trustee/custodian** that the deposit should be applied as a 2025 contribution; otherwise the institution may treat it as a 2026 contribution.
4. IRA annual contribution limits apply. A refund deposit to an IRA cannot exceed the 2025 limit ($7,000; $8,000 if age 50+) net of any other contributions already made for 2025.
5. MFJ joint refund deposited into an individual IRA: some financial institutions reject deposits of a joint refund into an individual account (including an IRA). If rejected, the IRS will mail a paper check for that portion (or redirect to the last valid account — see §10).

## 12. Compute Order / Dependencies

```
Form1040.line35a (total refund)         ← must be computed first; Form 8888 is downstream
    ↓
Form8888 intake data loaded
    ↓
Form8888.line5  = account1Amount + account2Amount + account3Amount
    ↓
Validation: line5 == Form1040.line35a   ← blocking if mismatch
```

Form 8888 has no upstream tax computation of its own. It consumes only the final refund amount from Form 1040 and the bank routing information entered by the taxpayer.

## 15. Developer-Safe Implementation Summary

```text
// Gate checks
if refund_amount <= 0:
    do not use Form 8888

if direct_deposit_account_count == 1:
    do not use Form 8888
    use direct deposit fields on Form 1040 lines 35b-d instead

if Form8379 filed:
    Form 8888 not allowed

// Per-account validation
for each account in [account1, account2, account3] where amount > 0:
    require deposit_amount >= 1 (whole dollars)
    require routing_number length == 9
    require routing_number[0..1] in {"01".."12", "21".."32"}
    require account_type in {checking, savings}
    require account_number length in [1, 17]
    require account_number matches /^[a-zA-Z0-9\-]+$/ (no spaces or special symbols)

// Line 4: reserved — do not populate
line4 = null  // reserved for future use

// Line 5: total — must equal refund exactly
line5 = account1Amount + account2Amount + account3Amount
require line5 == refund_amount_from_return

// Fallback warning
last_valid_account acts as fallback destination for entire refund if processing fails
```

## 16. Out of Scope

| Feature | Reason |
|---|---|
| I bond / Series EE paper bond purchase | **Discontinued** — permanently removed for 2024+ returns |
| TreasuryDirect® direct deposit | **Discontinued** alongside savings bond feature |
| More than 3 accounts | IRS form supports maximum 3 direct-deposit accounts |
| Partial allocation (partial direct deposit + paper check) | Not supported by 2025 form; line 5 must equal full refund |
| Form 8379 (Injured Spouse) combined use | IRS prohibits simultaneous use |
| SIMPLE IRA deposit | Explicitly prohibited by IRS instructions |
| Non-U.S. bank accounts | Direct deposit is ACH-only; international accounts not supported |
| Routing of balance-due payment | Form 8888 is refund-only; payments use EFTPS / IRS Direct Pay |
