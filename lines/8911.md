# Form 8911 — Alternative Fuel Vehicle Refueling Property Credit

**Tax year:** 2025
**IRS form:** Form 8911 (Rev. December 2024), Attachment Sequence No. 151, OMB No. 1545-0123, Cat. No. 37721Q
**Sources:** Form 8911 (Rev. 12-2024) with instructions; Schedule A (Form 8911) draft (Rev. December 2025); 2025 Schedule 3 (Form 1040); Taxes For Dummies 2024 Edition (Schedule 3 line 6j; 6%/$100k business, 30%/$1,000 personal); J.K. Lasser's Your Income Tax 2025 Professional Edition (no dedicated section); IRC § 30C (as amended by the Inflation Reduction Act of 2022)
**Attaches to:** Form 1040, 1040-SR, or 1040-NR (individuals); also corporations and other filers
**Supporting schedule:** Schedule A (Form 8911) — one per qualifying property placed in service
**Destination (personal use):** Form 8911 line 10 → Schedule 3 (Form 1040) line 6j → Schedule 3 line 8 → Form 1040 line 20
**Destination (business/investment use):** Form 8911 line 3 → Form 3800, Part III, line 1s

> **Note on 20.md reference:** The existing `lines/20.md` cites "Form 8911 line 19" as the source for Schedule 3 line 6j. The current Form 8911 (Rev. 12-2024) has only 10 lines; the personal-use credit output is **line 10**. The "line 19" reference is stale and should be corrected to line 10 when line 6j is implemented.

---

## 1. Line Identity

Form 8911 computes the **Alternative Fuel Vehicle Refueling Property Credit** under IRC § 30C for taxpayers who placed qualified alternative fuel vehicle refueling property in service during 2025.

The form has two parts:

| Part | Lines | Coverage | Destination |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Part I** | 1–3 | Business/investment use portion | Form 3800, Part III, line 1s (general business credit) |
| **Part II** | 4–10 | Personal use portion | Schedule 3 (Form 1040) line 6j → Form 1040 line 20 |

**Supporting schedule:** The per-property credit computation is done on **Schedule A (Form 8911)**. One Schedule A must be completed for **each** qualified alternative fuel vehicle refueling property placed in service during the tax year. Totals from all Schedule A forms feed Form 8911 lines 1 (business credit) and 4 (personal credit).

Schedule A (Form 8911) has three parts:
- **Part I** — Property identification and eligibility (lines 1–7)
- **Part II** — Business/investment use credit computation (lines 8–16; feeds Form 8911 line 1)
- **Part III** — Personal use credit computation (lines 17–21; feeds Form 8911 line 4)

---

## 2. What Qualifies as Refueling Property

**Qualified alternative fuel vehicle refueling property** under IRC § 30C includes equipment used to store or dispense alternative fuel into a motor vehicle (or to recharge an electric vehicle). Qualifying alternative fuels include:

- **Electricity** (EV charging equipment — Level 1, Level 2, DC fast chargers)
- Ethanol with at least 85% ethanol content (E85)
- Natural gas (compressed or liquefied)
- Liquefied petroleum gas (LPG / propane)
- Hydrogen
- Biodiesel blends (B20 or higher)

**Eligibility requirements (2025 instructions):** Qualified refueling property must:
- Be placed in service during the tax year
- Have **original use** begin with the taxpayer (used property does not qualify)
- **Not** be used predominantly outside the United States
- Be located in an **eligible census tract** (required for property placed in service after 2022)
- If personal-use property: be installed on property used as the taxpayer's **main home**

**Termination date:** The credit **cannot** be claimed for property placed in service **after June 30, 2026** (per the law's shortened termination date).

**Location requirement:** For property placed in service after 2022, the refueling property must be located in a **low-income community** or a **non-urban census tract** (census tract not designated as an urban area by the Census Bureau). Taxpayers must verify the eligible census tract GEOID of the installation address. If the property is not in an eligible census tract (Schedule A line 6a = No), **stop** — the property does not qualify.

**Eligible uses:**
- Property used in a **trade or business** or for **investment purposes** (depreciable) → business/investment portion
- Property installed at the **taxpayer's main home** for personal use (non-depreciable) → personal-use portion

## 3. Per-Return Filing Rules

```
Form 8911 count per return    = 0 or 1
Schedule A (Form 8911) count  = one per qualifying property placed in service
```

One Form 8911 is filed per return regardless of how many qualifying properties were placed in service. Individual property calculations are done on separate Schedule A forms, and their totals consolidate onto the single Form 8911.

**Item A** on Form 8911: Enter the total number of qualified refueling properties and file a separate Schedule A for each one. If the only credit is from a pass-through line 2, enter 0 for Item A.

A taxpayer files Form 8911 **only** if they placed qualifying property in service during 2025 **or** have a business/investment credit from a partnership or S corporation (line 2).

**K-1 pass-through shortcut:** If the taxpayer's only Form 8911-related credit comes from a Schedule K-1 (Form 1065 box 15 code AO, or Form 1120-S box 13 code AO), most non-partnership/non-S-corp individual filers do **not** need to complete Form 8911 and may report the credit directly on **Form 3800 Part III line 1s**:

```
if only_source_is_passthrough_K1 and taxpayer_is_not_partnership_or_Scorp:
    skip Form8911
    report directly via Form3800
```

## 4. Per-Spouse and Joint-Return Rules

**Not required per spouse on a joint return.** One Form 8911 covers the MFJ return.

**MFJ:** Part II lines 5, 6, and 8 all draw from the joint return's tax and credit amounts. If either spouse installed qualifying property, both spouses' information contributes through the joint return figures. No separate per-spouse fields appear on the form.

**Does Form 8911 require both spouses' information on MFJ?** Yes, it can — because the personal-use limitation uses joint regular tax (line 5), joint credits (line 6b), and joint TMT (line 8). If either spouse owns or uses the property, or the property is jointly owned, it is handled on the single joint Form 8911 with its own Schedule A.

```
if filing_status == MFJ:
    create one Form8911 (with one Schedule A per property)
    Part II uses joint Form1040.line16 + joint Schedule2.line1z
    Part II uses joint Form6251.line9
else:
    use taxpayer-only return data
```

**MFS situation:** Each spouse files their own separate return with their own Form 8911 and Schedule A(s) for properties they installed or own.

## 5. Credit Rates and Dollar Limits

Credit rates depend on whether the property is **depreciable** (business use) or **non-depreciable** (personal use), and whether **prevailing wage and apprenticeship (PWA) requirements** are satisfied.

### Personal Use Property (non-depreciable — installed at main home)

| Condition | Credit Rate | Per-Item Cap |
|---|---|---|
| Standard (no PWA requirement for personal use) | **30%** of cost | **$1,000** per item |

### Business/Investment Use Property (depreciable)

| Condition | Credit Rate | Per-Item Cap |
|---|---|---|
| Base rate (PWA requirements NOT met) | **6%** of cost | **$100,000** per item |
| Enhanced rate (PWA requirements met) | **30%** of cost | **$100,000** per item |

**Taxes For Dummies confirms:** 6% credit with $100,000 maximum for depreciable property (base rate); 30%/$1,000 for non-depreciable personal-use property.

**Section 179 interaction:** Property cost must be **reduced by any § 179 deduction** taken before computing the credit (Schedule A line 10 − line 11 = line 12, then apply rate).

**Basis reduction:** The taxpayer must reduce the basis of the refueling property by the credit amounts on **Schedule A line 16** (business) and **Schedule A line 21** (personal), unless electing not to claim the credit.

**Recapture:** If the property later ceases to qualify, the credit may be subject to recapture.

## 6. How to Complete Schedule A (Form 8911) — Per Property

Complete one **Schedule A (Form 8911)** for each qualifying property before completing Form 8911 itself.

### Schedule A Part I — Property Information

| Line | Description |
|---|---|
| 1 | Registration number (if IRS-issued one is required for elective payment/transfer election) |
| 2 | Description of the property |
| 3 | Name and TIN of owner if different from filer |
| 4 | Location (address or coordinates) |
| 5 | Date construction began |
| 6 | Date placed in service |
| **6a** | Is the property located in an eligible census tract? (Yes/No) — **if No, stop; property does not qualify** |
| 6b | GEOID of eligible census tract |
| 7 | Certification or permit number, if any |

### Schedule A Part II — Business/Investment Use Credit (→ Form 8911 line 1)

| Line | Formula |
|---|---|
| 8 | Cost of property |
| 9 | Business/investment-use percentage |
| 10 | Line 8 × line 9 (business-use cost) |
| 11 | Section 179 deduction taken for this property (from Form 4562 Part I, if applicable) |
| 12 | Line 10 − line 11 |
| 13 | PWA requirements met? (Yes = 30% rate; No = 6% rate) |
| 14 | Line 12 × applicable rate (6% or 30%) |
| 15 | Maximum per-item cap: $100,000 |
| **16** | min(line 14, line 15) → carry to **Form 8911 line 1** |

**Mixed-use / mid-year change:** If use changes during the year, compute business percentage as months-in-business use ÷ 12.

**PWA enhanced rate:** Requires **Form 7220** for each property claiming the 30% business rate.

### Schedule A Part III — Personal Use Credit (→ Form 8911 line 4)

| Line | Formula |
|---|---|
| **17** | Was the refueling property installed on property used as your main home? (Yes/No) — **if No, stop; no personal-use credit** |
| 18 | Line 8 − line 10 (personal-use cost = total cost minus business-use cost) |
| 19 | Line 18 × 30% |
| 20 | Maximum per-item cap: $1,000 |
| **21** | min(line 19, line 20) → carry to **Form 8911 line 4** |

## 7. How to Complete Form 8911 — Line by Line

### Part I — Credit for Business/Investment Use Part of Refueling Property

#### Line 1 — Total Business Credit from Schedule(s) A
```
line1 = sum of Schedule A line 16 across all properties
```

#### Line 2 — Pass-Through Business Credits
```
line2 = refueling property credit from partnerships / S corporations (Schedule K-1)
```

#### Line 3 — Total Business/Investment Credit
```
line3 = line1 + line2
```

**Routing:**
- **Partnerships and S corporations not making a transfer election:** Stop here; report line 3 on Schedule K.
- **All others (including individuals):** Report line 3 on **Form 3800, Part III, line 1s**.

The business/investment portion is part of the **general business credit** (Form 3800) and has carryback (1 year) and carryforward (20 years) under general business credit rules. It does **not** flow directly to Schedule 3 line 6j.

---

### Part II — Credit for Personal Use Part of Refueling Property

#### Line 4 — Total Personal Use Credit from Schedule(s) A
```
line4 = sum of Schedule A line 21 across all properties
```

#### Line 5 — Regular Tax Before Credits (Individuals)
```
line5 = Form1040.line16 + Schedule2.line1z
```

#### Line 6a — Foreign Tax Credit
Enter the foreign tax credit (Schedule 3 line 1 / Form 1116).

#### Line 6b — Certain Allowable Credits
See §8 below for the full list.

#### Line 6c — Total Prior Credits
```
line6c = line6a + line6b
```

#### Line 7 — Net Regular Tax
```
line7 = max(0, line5 − line6c)
```
If zero or less, enter -0- and **stop here**. Do not file Form 8911 unless claiming a credit on line 3.

#### Line 8 — Tentative Minimum Tax
```
line8 = Form6251.line9    // individuals
```
Even if the taxpayer does not owe AMT, the TMT must be computed to determine the available personal-use credit capacity on line 9.

#### Line 9 — Available Credit Capacity
```
line9 = max(0, line7 − line8)
```
If zero or less, enter -0- and **stop here**. Do not file Form 8911 unless claiming a credit on line 3.

#### Line 10 — Personal Use Credit
```
line10 = min(line4, line9)
```

**Enter line 10 on Schedule 3 (Form 1040), line 6j.**

> **Critical rule — no carryforward:** If line 9 is smaller than line 4, the unused personal-use credit is **permanently lost** — no carryback or carryforward. This mirrors Form 8834's treatment. (The business-use portion on line 3 → Form 3800 does have carryforward/carryback rights.)

## 8. Line 6b — Allowable Credits Detail (Form 1040 Filers)

Per the Form 8911 instructions, for Form 1040, 1040-SR, or 1040-NR filers:

```
line6b = Form1040.line19                // CTC / ODC
       + Schedule3.line2               // Child/dependent care credit
       + Schedule3.line3               // Education credits
       + Schedule3.line4               // Retirement saver's credit
       + Schedule3.line5a              // Residential clean energy credit
       + Schedule3.line5b              // Energy efficient home improvement credit
       + Schedule3.line7               // Subtotal of lines 6a–6z
       − Schedule3.line6a              // LESS: General business credit
       − Schedule3.line6b              // LESS: Prior year minimum tax credit
       − Schedule3.line6k              // LESS: Credit to holders of tax credit bonds
```

Simplified: Form 1040 line 19 + Schedule 3 lines 2–5 + Schedule 3 line 7, reduced by lines 6a, 6b, and 6k.

**Credit ordering implication:** Credits excluded from line 6b (6a general business, 6b prior year min tax, 6k tax credit bonds) do **not** consume tax capacity before Form 8911. Credits included — 6c adoption, 6d elderly/disabled, 6f clean vehicle new, 6g mortgage interest, 6h DC homebuyer, 6i qualified EV — **do** reduce available capacity. Form 8911's own result (line 6j) is being computed and is not yet included in line 6b.

## 9. Full Line-by-Line Formula Summary

```
INDIVIDUALS (Form 1040):

// Schedule A per property (one per qualifying property):
schedA.line16[i] = min(cost × business_pct − sec179, 100000) × rate  // 6% or 30%
schedA.line21[i] = min((cost − cost×business_pct) × 30%, 1000)       // personal

// Form 8911 Part I — Business Use
line1  = sum(schedA.line16[i])
line2  = passthrough_credit_from_K1s
line3  = line1 + line2   → Form3800.PartIII.line1s

// Form 8911 Part II — Personal Use
line4  = sum(schedA.line21[i])
line5  = Form1040.line16 + Schedule2.line1z
line6a = Schedule3.line1
line6b = [see §8]
line6c = line6a + line6b
line7  = max(0, line5 − line6c)
         if line7 = 0: line10 = 0; stop (unless line3 > 0)
line8  = Form6251.line9
line9  = max(0, line7 − line8)
         if line9 = 0: line10 = 0; stop (unless line3 > 0)
line10 = min(line4, line9)   → Schedule3.line6j

NOTE: If line9 < line4 → unused personal-use credit is LOST (no carryforward)
      Business-use credit (line3 → Form3800) has 1-year c/b + 20-year c/f
```

## 10. Developer-Safe End-to-End Logic

```
// Gate: skip if no owned property and no K-1 pass-through
if no_qualifying_property AND no_passthrough_credits:
    no Form8911 needed

// K-1 pass-through only shortcut (individuals, not partnership/S-corp)
if only_source_is_passthrough_K1 and individual_return:
    skip Form8911; report directly on Form3800.PartIII.line1s

else:
    // Complete one Schedule A per property
    for each qualifying_property[i]:
        // Part I — Property eligibility gate
        if not in_eligible_census_tract:
            skip this property (does not qualify)

        // Part II — Business use
        business_cost = property.cost × property.business_pct
        schedA_line12 = business_cost − property.sec179_deduction
        rate = 0.30 if PWA_met else 0.06
        schedA_line16[i] = min(schedA_line12 × rate, 100000)

        // Part III — Personal use
        if property.at_main_home:
            personal_cost = property.cost − business_cost
            schedA_line21[i] = min(personal_cost × 0.30, 1000)
        else:
            schedA_line21[i] = 0

    // Form 8911 Part I
    line1 = sum(schedA_line16)
    line2 = passthrough_credit_from_K1s
    line3 = line1 + line2
    Form3800.PartIII.line1s = line3   // out of scope for personal 1040 compute

    // Form 8911 Part II
    line4 = sum(schedA_line21)
    line5 = Form1040.line16 + Schedule2.line1z
    line6a = Schedule3.line1
    line6b = [per §8]
    line6c = line6a + line6b
    line7 = max(0, line5 − line6c)
    if line7 == 0:
        line10 = 0
    else:
        line8 = Form6251.line9
        line9 = max(0, line7 − line8)
        line10 = min(line4, line9)
        // unused personal-use credit (line4 − line10) is permanently LOST

    Schedule3.line6j = line10
```

## 14. Interaction with Other Tax Items

### Business vs Personal Split

The form separates business/investment use (Part I → Form 3800) from personal use (Part II → Schedule 3 line 6j). A single piece of refueling property used for both purposes is allocated in Schedule A (business % on lines 9–16, remainder on lines 18–21).

### Form 3800 (General Business Credit)

The business/investment portion (line 3) flows into Form 3800 as part of the general business credit. Unlike the personal-use portion, the business-use credit has **carryback (1 year) and carryforward (20 years)** under the general business credit rules.

### No Carryforward for Personal Use

If the tax liability limit (line 9) is less than line 4, the unused personal-use credit is permanently lost — same treatment as Form 8834.

### Form 5695 (Residential Energy Credits) — Distinction

Form 8911 covers **EV charging and alternative fuel refueling equipment**. Form 5695 covers **residential energy property** (solar panels, heat pumps, insulation). These are separate credits. EV charging equipment at a personal residence uses Form 8911, not Form 5695.

### Basis Reduction

Basis of the property must be reduced by **Schedule A line 16** (business credit) and **Schedule A line 21** (personal credit), unless the taxpayer elects not to claim the credit. This affects future depreciation deductions for business-use property.

### AMT (Form 6251)

Form 6251 must be completed even if the taxpayer does not owe AMT — the TMT is needed for Part II line 8. A higher TMT reduces available personal-use credit capacity on line 9.

### Form 7220 (PWA Requirements)

Required for each property claiming the enhanced **30% business rate**. Without Form 7220, only the 6% base rate applies to business-use property.

## 16. Out of Scope

- **Business/investment use portion (Form 8911 Part I → Form 3800):** Out of scope for individual personal-return compute. Line 3 is noted but Form 3800 general business credit computation is not implemented.
- **PWA determination and Form 7220:** Whether PWA requirements are met is a user-attested fact; Form 7220 preparation is out of scope.
- **Census tract / location verification:** Whether the property is in a qualifying census tract is a user-attested fact (GEOID entered by user).
- **Pass-through credits (line 2):** K-1 allocated credits from partnerships and S corporations are out of scope (individual-return personal compute only).
- **Recapture computation:** Recapture of the credit if property ceases to qualify is not computed here.
- **Form 8910 (Alternative Motor Vehicle Credit):** Separate form for qualifying vehicles (not refueling property).
- **Elective payment and transfer elections:** Registration number requirements for these elections are not implemented.
