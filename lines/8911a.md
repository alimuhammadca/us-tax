# Form 8911 — Alternative Fuel Vehicle Refueling Property Credit

**Tax year:** 2025
**IRS form:** Form 8911 (Rev. December 2024), Attachment Sequence No. 151, OMB No. 1545-0123, Cat. No. 37721Q
**Sources:** Form 8911 (Rev. 12-2024) with instructions; Taxes For Dummies 2024 Edition (Schedule 3 line 6j, credit rates and limits); J.K. Lasser's Your Income Tax 2025 Professional Edition (no dedicated section found); IRC § 30C (as amended by the Inflation Reduction Act of 2022)
**Attaches to:** Form 1040, 1040-SR, or 1040-NR (individuals); also used by corporations and other filers
**Supporting schedule:** Schedule A (Form 8911) — one per qualifying property placed in service
**Destination (personal use):** Form 8911 line 10 → Schedule 3 (Form 1040) line 6j → Schedule 3 line 8 → Form 1040 line 20
**Destination (business/investment use):** Form 8911 line 3 → Form 3800, Part III, line 1s

> **Note on 20.md reference:** The existing `lines/20.md` cites "Form 8911 line 19" as the source for Schedule 3 line 6j. The current Form 8911 (Rev. 12-2024) has only 10 lines; the personal-use credit output is **line 10**. The "line 19" reference is stale and should be corrected to line 10 when line 6j is implemented.

---

## 1. Line Identity

Form 8911 computes the **Alternative Fuel Vehicle Refueling Property Credit** under IRC § 30C for taxpayers who placed qualified alternative fuel vehicle refueling property in service during 2025.

The form has two parts:

| Part | Coverage | Destination |
|---|---|---|
| **Part I** — Lines 1–3 | Business/investment use portion of the credit | Form 3800, Part III, line 1s (general business credit) |
| **Part II** — Lines 4–10 | Personal use portion of the credit | Schedule 3 (Form 1040) line 6j → Form 1040 line 20 |

**Supporting schedule:** The per-property credit computation is done on **Schedule A (Form 8911)**. One Schedule A must be completed for **each** qualified alternative fuel vehicle refueling property placed in service during the tax year. The totals from all Schedule A forms feed Form 8911 lines 1 (business credit) and 4 (personal credit).

Schedule A (Form 8911) has three parts:
- **Part I** — Property description and identification
- **Part II** — Business/investment use credit computation (feeds Form 8911 line 1)
- **Part III** — Personal use credit computation (feeds Form 8911 line 4)

---

## 2. What Qualifies as Refueling Property

**Qualified alternative fuel vehicle refueling property** under IRC § 30C includes equipment used to store or dispense alternative fuel into a motor vehicle (or to recharge an electric vehicle). Qualifying alternative fuels include:

- **Electricity** (EV charging equipment — Level 1, Level 2, DC fast chargers)
- Ethanol with at least 85% ethanol content (E85)
- Natural gas (compressed or liquefied)
- Liquefied petroleum gas (LPG / propane)
- Hydrogen
- Biodiesel blends (B20 or higher)

**Location requirement (post-IRA 2022):** For property placed in service after 2022, the refueling property must be located in a **low-income community** or a **non-urban census tract** (defined as a census tract not designated as an urban area by the Census Bureau, generally population under 20,000). Properties not meeting this requirement do not qualify for the personal-use credit. Taxpayers must verify the census tract of the installation address.

**Eligible uses:**
- Property used in a **trade or business** or for **investment purposes** (depreciable property) → business/investment portion
- Property installed at the **taxpayer's main home** for personal use (non-depreciable) → personal-use portion

---

## 3. Per-Return Filing Rules

```
Form 8911 count per return = 0 or 1
Schedule A (Form 8911) count = one per qualifying property placed in service
```

One Form 8911 is filed per return regardless of how many qualifying properties were placed in service. Individual property calculations are done on separate Schedule A forms, and their totals are consolidated onto the single Form 8911.

The form header shows "Name(s) shown on return" and a single identifying number (the primary SSN for individual filers).

A taxpayer files Form 8911 **only** if they placed qualified alternative fuel vehicle refueling property in service during 2025 **or** if they have a business/investment credit from a partnership or S corporation (line 2).

---

## 4. Per-Spouse and Joint-Return Rules

**Not required per spouse on a joint return.** One Form 8911 covers the MFJ return.

**MFJ:** The personal-use credit limitation in Part II uses the joint return's regular tax (Form 1040 line 16 + Schedule 2 line 1z) and the joint return's tentative minimum tax. No separate per-spouse fields appear on the form.

**Does Form 8911 require both spouses' information on MFJ?** Yes, it can — because Part II lines 5, 6, and 8 all draw from the joint return's tax and credit amounts. If either spouse installed qualifying property, both spouses' information is included through the joint return figures.

```
if filing_status == MFJ:
    create one Form8911 (with one Schedule A per property)
    Part II uses joint Form1040.line16 + joint Schedule2.line1z
    Part II uses joint Form6251.line9
else:
    use taxpayer-only return data
```

**MFS situation:** Each spouse files their own separate return with their own Form 8911 and Schedule A(s) for properties they installed or own.

## 5. Credit Rates and Dollar Limits

The credit rates depend on whether the property is **depreciable** (business use) or **non-depreciable** (personal use), and whether **prevailing wage and apprenticeship (PWA) requirements** are satisfied.

### Personal Use Property (non-depreciable — installed at main home)

| Condition | Credit Rate | Per-Item Cap |
|---|---|---|
| Standard (no PWA requirement for personal use) | **30%** of cost | **$1,000** per item of property |

### Business/Investment Use Property (depreciable)

| Condition | Credit Rate | Per-Item Cap |
|---|---|---|
| Base rate (PWA requirements NOT met) | **6%** of cost | **$100,000** per item of property |
| Enhanced rate (PWA requirements met) | **30%** of cost | **$100,000** per item of property |

**Taxes For Dummies confirms:** 6% credit with $100,000 maximum for depreciable property (base rate); 30%/$1,000 for non-depreciable (personal use) property.

**Basis reduction:** The taxpayer must **reduce the basis** of the refueling property by the amount of the credit claimed. This applies to both personal and business use property.

**Recapture:** If the property ceases to qualify (e.g., location requirement no longer met, property disposed of early), the credit may be subject to recapture.

## 6. How to Complete Form 8911 — Line by Line

### Step 1 — Complete Schedule A (Form 8911) for Each Property

Before completing Form 8911, complete a **separate Schedule A** for each qualified alternative fuel vehicle refueling property placed in service in 2025.

Each Schedule A:
- **Part I** identifies the property (address, date placed in service, type of fuel/property, cost)
- **Part II** computes the business/investment use credit for that property
- **Part III** computes the personal use credit for that property

The totals from all Schedule A forms are then carried to Form 8911 lines 1 and 4.

---

### Part I — Credit for Business/Investment Use Part of Refueling Property

#### Line 1 — Total Business Credit from Schedule(s) A

```
line1 = sum of Part II credits from all Schedule A (Form 8911) forms
```

Enter the total business/investment use credit computed across all Schedule A Part II calculations.

#### Line 2 — Pass-Through Business Credits

```
line2 = QEV refueling property credit from partnerships and S corporations
        (as reported on Schedule K-1)
```

Enter the alternative fuel vehicle refueling property credit allocated from partnerships or S corporations.

#### Line 3 — Total Business/Investment Credit

```
line3 = line1 + line2
```

**Routing:**
- **Partnerships and S corporations not making an election to transfer the credit:** Stop here; report line 3 on Schedule K.
- **All others (including individuals):** Report line 3 on **Form 3800, Part III, line 1s**.

> The business/investment use portion is part of the **general business credit** (Form 3800) and is subject to general business credit limitation and carryforward/carryback rules. It does **not** flow directly to Schedule 3 line 6j.

---

### Part II — Credit for Personal Use Part of Refueling Property

#### Line 4 — Total Personal Use Credit from Schedule(s) A

```
line4 = sum of Part III credits from all Schedule A (Form 8911) forms
```

Enter the total personal use credit computed across all Schedule A Part III calculations (30% × cost, capped at $1,000 per item).

#### Line 5 — Regular Tax Before Credits (Individuals)

```
line5 = Form1040.line16 + Schedule2.line1z
```

Form 1040 line 16 = regular income tax. Schedule 2 line 1z = AMT from Form 6251.

#### Line 6a — Foreign Tax Credit

Enter the foreign tax credit from the applicable return line (Schedule 3 line 1 / Form 1116).

#### Line 6b — Certain Allowable Credits

For Form 1040 filers, per the Form 8911 instructions, line 6b is the total of other nonrefundable credits that reduce regular tax before the refueling property credit. See §7 below for the full list.

#### Line 6c — Total Credits

```
line6c = line6a + line6b
```

#### Line 7 — Net Regular Tax

```
line7 = max(0, line5 − line6c)
```

If line 7 is zero or less, enter -0- and **stop here**. Do not file Form 8911 unless claiming a credit on line 3 (business use portion).

#### Line 8 — Tentative Minimum Tax

```
line8 = Form6251.line9    // individuals
```

Even if the taxpayer does not owe AMT, the TMT must be computed to determine the available personal-use credit capacity on line 9.

#### Line 9 — Available Credit Capacity

```
line9 = max(0, line7 − line8)
```

If line 9 is zero or less, enter -0- and **stop here**. Do not file Form 8911 unless claiming a credit on line 3.

#### Line 10 — Personal Use Credit

```
line10 = min(line4, line9)
```

**Enter line 10 on Schedule 3 (Form 1040), line 6j.**

> **If line 9 is smaller than line 4:** The unused personal-use credit is **lost** — it cannot be carried back or forward. This mirrors Form 8834's treatment. (The business-use portion on line 3 → Form 3800 does have general business credit carryforward/carryback rules.)

---

## 7. Line 6b — Allowable Credits Detail (Form 1040 Filers)

For **Form 1040, 1040-SR, or 1040-NR** filers, line 6b is the total of nonrefundable credits that rank before the alternative fuel vehicle refueling property credit (personal use), per the Form 8911 instructions. This follows the same structure as Forms 8834 and 8859:

```
line6b = Form1040.line19                    // CTC / ODC
       + Schedule3.line2                    // Child/dependent care credit
       + Schedule3.line3                    // Education credits
       + Schedule3.line4                    // Retirement saver's credit
       + Schedule3.line5a                   // Residential clean energy credit
       + Schedule3.line5b                   // Energy efficient home improvement credit
       + Schedule3.line7                    // Subtotal of lines 6a–6z
       − Schedule3.line6a                   // LESS: General business credit
       − Schedule3.line6b                   // LESS: Prior year minimum tax credit
       − Schedule3.line6k                   // LESS: Credit to holders of tax credit bonds
```

**Schedule 3 line 6j position note:** Form 8911 occupies Schedule 3 line 6j, which is part of the line 7 subtotal. When computing Form 8911 line 6b, line 6j (Form 8911's own output) is being determined and is treated as 0 to avoid circularity. Credits earlier in the sequence (6c adoption, 6d elderly/disabled, 6f clean vehicle new, 6g mortgage interest, 6h DC homebuyer, 6i qualified EV) do reduce available capacity before Form 8911 can apply.

> **Important:** The Form 8834 instructions explicitly list the credits to exclude from line 3b (6a, 6b, 6j, 6k). Form 8911 line 6b follows the same exclusion pattern per its own instructions. Confirm the exact list against the Form 8911 instructions when implementing — the instructions are not reproduced in the available PDF pages but follow the standard tax liability limitation worksheet pattern.

## 8. Full Line-by-Line Formula Summary

```
INDIVIDUALS (Form 1040):

// Schedule A per property (one per qualifying property):
schedA_business_credit[i]  = Part II of Schedule A i    // 6% or 30% × cost, capped $100k
schedA_personal_credit[i]  = Part III of Schedule A i   // 30% × cost, capped $1,000

// Form 8911 Part I — Business Use
line1   = sum(schedA_business_credit[i] for all i)
line2   = pass-through credits from K-1s
line3   = line1 + line2   → Form3800.PartIII.line1s

// Form 8911 Part II — Personal Use
line4   = sum(schedA_personal_credit[i] for all i)
line5   = Form1040.line16 + Schedule2.line1z
line6a  = Schedule3.line1    // Foreign tax credit
line6b  = [see §7]           // Other prior nonrefundable credits
line6c  = line6a + line6b
line7   = max(0, line5 − line6c)
          if line7 == 0: line10 = 0; stop (unless line3 > 0)
line8   = Form6251.line9     // Tentative minimum tax
line9   = max(0, line7 − line8)
          if line9 == 0: line10 = 0; stop (unless line3 > 0)
line10  = min(line4, line9)  → Schedule3.line6j

NOTE: If line9 < line4 → unused personal-use credit is LOST (no carryforward)
      Business-use credit (line3 → Form3800) has general business credit c/f rules
```

## 9. Developer-Safe End-to-End Logic

```
// Gate: only compute if qualifying property was placed in service
if no_qualifying_property_placed_in_service AND no_passthrough_credits:
    no Form8911 needed

else:
    // Complete one Schedule A per property
    for each qualifying_property:
        schedA_business_credit = compute_business_credit(property)   // Part II
        schedA_personal_credit = compute_personal_credit(property)   // Part III

    // Part I — Business use
    line1 = sum(schedA_business_credit)
    line2 = passthrough_QEV_refueling_credit_from_K1s
    line3 = line1 + line2
    Form3800.PartIII.line1s = line3    // out of scope for individual personal returns

    // Part II — Personal use
    line4 = sum(schedA_personal_credit)
    line5 = Form1040.line16 + Schedule2.line1z

    line6a = Schedule3.line1
    line6b = [prior nonrefundable credits per §7]
    line6c = line6a + line6b

    line7 = max(0, line5 − line6c)
    if line7 == 0:
        line10 = 0
    else:
        line8 = Form6251.line9
        line9 = max(0, line7 − line8)
        line10 = min(line4, line9)
        // unused personal-use credit (line4 - line10) is permanently LOST

    Schedule3.line6j = line10
```

## 12. Compute Order and Dependencies

| Step | Requires |
|---|---|
| Schedule A per property | Property cost, use %, census tract, PWA status |
| Line 1 (business credit total) | All Schedule A Part II computations complete |
| Line 4 (personal credit total) | All Schedule A Part III computations complete |
| Line 5 | Form 1040 line 16 and Schedule 2 line 1z complete |
| Line 6b | Form 1040 line 19 + Schedule 3 lines 2–5, 7 finalized |
| Line 7 | Lines 5 and 6c complete |
| Line 8 | Form 6251 completed — **always required** even if no AMT owed |
| Line 9 | Lines 7 and 8 complete |
| Line 10 | Lines 4 and 9 complete |
| Schedule 3 line 6j | Line 10 finalized |

**Critical ordering:** Form 8911 Part II must be computed **after** all credits that appear in line 6b have been finalized (Schedule 3 lines 2–5, line 6a–6i, 6k–6m, and Form 1040 line 19), and **after** Form 6251 (TMT) has been computed.

## 13. Interaction with Other Tax Items

### Business vs Personal Split

The form cleanly separates business/investment use (Part I → Form 3800) from personal use (Part II → Schedule 3 line 6j). A single piece of refueling property used for both purposes requires allocation in Schedule A.

### Form 3800 (General Business Credit)

The business/investment portion (line 3) flows into Form 3800 as part of the general business credit. Unlike the personal-use portion, the business-use credit has **carryback (1 year) and carryforward (20 years)** under the general business credit rules.

### No Carryforward for Personal Use

The personal-use portion (Part II) follows the same "no carryforward" rule as Form 8834 — if the tax liability limit (line 9) is less than line 4, the unused personal-use credit is permanently lost.

### Form 5695 (Residential Energy Credits) — Distinction

Form 8911 covers **refueling/charging equipment** at a home. Form 5695 covers **residential energy property** such as solar panels, heat pumps, and insulation. These are separate credits. EV charging equipment at a personal residence uses Form 8911, not Form 5695.

### Basis Reduction

The taxpayer must reduce the basis of the qualifying refueling property by the full credit amount claimed. This affects depreciation deductions for business-use property.

### AMT (Form 6251)

Even if the taxpayer does not owe AMT, Form 6251 must be completed to determine the TMT for Part II line 8. A higher TMT reduces available personal-use credit capacity on line 9.

## 15. Out of Scope

- **Business/investment use portion (Form 8911 Part I → Form 3800):** Out of scope for individual personal-return compute. Line 3 is noted but Form 3800 general business credit computation is not implemented.
- **Prevailing wage and apprenticeship (PWA) determination:** Whether the 6% or 30% rate applies to business property is a facts-and-circumstances determination not computed here.
- **Census tract / location requirement verification:** Whether the property is in a qualifying non-urban or low-income census tract is a user-attested fact.
- **Schedule A (Form 8911) detailed computation:** Schedule A is a separate IRS form not present in the IRS-Forms directory. Detailed Part I/II/III Schedule A line references are deferred until that form is available.
- **Pass-through credits (line 2):** K-1 allocated credits from partnerships and S corporations are out of scope.
- **Recapture computation:** Recapture of the credit if property ceases to qualify is not computed here.
- **Form 8910 (Alternative Motor Vehicle Credit):** Separate form for qualifying vehicles (not refueling property).
