# Form 8912 — Credit to Holders of Tax Credit Bonds

**Tax year:** 2025
**IRS form:** Form 8912 (Rev. December 2024), Attachment Sequence No. 912, OMB No. 1545-1456
**Sources:** Form 8912 (Rev. 12-2024) with instructions; 2025 Schedule 3 (Form 1040); Form 1097-BTC instructions; IRC § 54, § 54A–54F, § 6431; Taxes For Dummies 2024 Edition (confirms Schedule 3 line 6k = Form 8912; lists CREB, NCREB, QECB, QZAB, QSCB, BAB); J.K. Lasser's Your Income Tax 2025 Professional Edition (no dedicated section — too specialized/niche)
**Attaches to:** Form 1040, 1040-SR, or 1040-NR (individuals); Form 1120 (corporations); Form 1041 (estates/trusts)
**Destination (individuals):** Form 8912 line 12 → Schedule 3 (Form 1040) line 6k → Schedule 3 line 8 → Form 1040 line 20

```text
Form 8912 is a legacy nonrefundable credit for holders of tax credit bonds
issued on or before December 31, 2017 — no new bonds have qualified since 2018
```

---

## 1. Line Identity

Form 8912 computes the **Credit to Holders of Tax Credit Bonds** — a nonrefundable personal credit for individuals who hold certain government-issued tax credit bonds. The credit functions as a substitute for taxable interest: the bondholder reports the credit amount as **taxable interest income** on the credit allowance date, while simultaneously receiving an equal dollar-for-dollar tax credit.

**Critical 2025 context:** No bonds issued after December 31, 2017 qualify. As of 2025, all qualifying bonds are legacy instruments. Form 8912 is relevant only to taxpayers still holding pre-2018 bond portfolios.

**Qualifying bond types:**

| Bond Type | Abbreviation | Credit rate base |
|---|---|---|
| Clean Renewable Energy Bond (pre-10/4/08) | CREB | Treasury credit rate × principal × quarter |
| New Clean Renewable Energy Bond (post-10/4/08) | NCREB | Same; then × 70% at line 20 |
| Qualified Energy Conservation Bond | QECB | Same; then × 70% at line 20 |
| Qualified Zone Academy Bond | QZAB | Treasury AFR × principal × quarter |
| Qualified School Construction Bond | QSCB | Treasury credit rate × principal × quarter |
| Build America Bond | BAB | 35% × interest payable × 100% |

The 2025 Schedule 3 identifies this line as: **"Credit to holders of tax credit bonds. Attach Form 8912."** (Schedule 3 line 6k)

**Form structure — four parts:**

| Part | Lines | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| Part I — Current Year Credit | 1–6 | Aggregate credits from Parts III and IV; add carryforwards |
| Part II — Allowable Credit | 7–12 | Tax liability limitation; compute allowable credit |
| Part III — 1097-BTC Credits | 13–14 | Credits reported on Form(s) 1097-BTC received from issuers/pass-throughs |
| Part IV — Direct Bond Credits | 15–20 | Credits computed directly from bonds held without 1097-BTC |

---

## 2. Who Can Claim / Eligibility in 2025

Form 8912 may be filed in 2025 only by a taxpayer who:

1. Holds (or held during 2025) a qualifying tax credit bond issued **on or before December 31, 2017**, and
2. Has a credit allowance date (for CREB/NCREB/QECB/QZAB/QSCB: quarterly — March 15, June 15, September 15, December 15) or interest payment date (for BABs) falling within the 2025 tax year, **or**
3. Has a **carryforward** of unused credit from prior years (for NCREB, QECB, post-10/4/08 QZAB, QSCB, or BAB).

**Do not use Form 8912 for bonds issued after 2017.** The form includes an explicit Caution on both Part III and Part IV: "Do not enter bond credits for bonds issued after December 31, 2017."

**Intake validation gate:**

```text
bond_type in {CREB, NCREB, QECB, QZAB, QSCB, BAB}
and bond_issue_year <= 2017
```

**Interest income requirement:** The credit amount is treated as taxable interest income in the year of the credit allowance date. The holder must report the credit amount as interest income on Schedule B / Form 1040 line 2b, even though no cash is received.

---

## 3. Per-Return Filing Rules

**One Form 8912 per return.** Not per spouse; not per bond type. All bond credits are consolidated on one Form 8912.

```text
Form8912 count per return = 0 or 1
```

**Multiple bonds:** File separate Part III rows (one per Form 1097-BTC issuer) or separate Part IV sections (pages 2–3 replicated) for each bond with a different CUSIP, issuance date, maturity date, or credit rate. Attach as many copies of pages 2–3 as needed. The one Form 8912 aggregates all bonds.

**K-1 / pass-through credits:** If you receive a Form 1097-BTC from a pass-through entity (partnership, S corporation, estate, trust, RIC, REIT), report it on **Part III line 13** only. Do **not** also include the same bond on Part IV line 18 — anti-double-counting rule.

**Partnerships and S corporations:** Report Part I line 4 total on Schedule K instead of completing Part II. Must also issue Form(s) 1097-BTC to partners/shareholders.

**Estates and trusts:** Complete lines 5–6 to allocate credit between beneficiaries and the estate/trust before entering Part II.

---

## 4. Per-Spouse Rules (MFJ)

**Not required per spouse.** On a joint return, file **one Form 8912** for the combined return.

- **MFJ:** one joint Form 8912; the tax liability limitation uses the joint return's tax and credits.
- **MFS / separate returns:** each spouse files their own Form 8912 on their own separate return if they separately hold qualifying bonds.

There are no MFJ-specific provisions on Form 8912 itself. The tax liability limitation in Part II uses the joint return's Form 1040 line 16 and the joint Schedule 2 line 1z — these are inherently joint amounts.

---

## 5. Requires Information from Both Spouses?

**Yes, it can — but only one Form 8912 is filed.**

On MFJ, the tax liability limitation formula at Part II line 7 uses joint tax (Form 1040 line 16 + Schedule 2 line 1z), and lines 10a–10d subtract joint prior credits. If either spouse holds qualifying bonds or receives Form(s) 1097-BTC, all inputs flow to the single joint Form 8912. There is no spouse-specific split of bond credits on this form.

Safe implementation rule:

```text
if filing_status == MFJ:
    create one Form8912
    use joint-return tax limitation inputs
else:
    use taxpayer-only return data
```

## 6. Line-by-Line Computation

### Part I — Current Year Credit

| Line | Formula / Source |
|---|---|
| 1 | Total from Part III line 14 (1097-BTC credits) |
| 2 | Total from Part IV line 20 (direct bond credits) |
| 3 | Carryforward of unused credit from prior years (NCREB, QECB, post-10/4/08 QZAB, QSCB, BAB only — CREB and pre-10/4/08 QZAB cannot carry forward) |
| 4 | Line 1 + line 2 + line 3 (total current year credit) |
| 5 | Estates/trusts only: amount allocated to beneficiaries (must issue Form(s) 1097-BTC) |
| 6 | Estates/trusts only: line 4 − line 5 (amount retained by estate/trust) |

Partnerships and S corps: stop at line 4; report on Schedule K.
Individuals proceed to Part II using line 4.

### Part II — Allowable Credit (Tax Liability Limitation)

| Line | Formula / Source |
|---|---|
| 7 | Regular tax before credits: `Form1040.line16 + Schedule2.line1z` |
| 8 | AMT: `Form6251.line11` |
| 9 | `line7 + line8` |
| 10a | Foreign tax credit (Form 1116 line 35 or Schedule 3 line 1) |
| 10b | Certain credits: `Form1040.line19 + Schedule3.lines2–5b + Schedule3.lines6c–6j + Schedule3.lines6l–6z` |
| 10c | General business credit (Form 3800 amount) |
| 10d | Credit for prior year minimum tax (Form 8801 / Form 8827) |
| 10e | `line10a + line10b + line10c + line10d` |
| 11 | Net income tax: `line9 − line10e` |
| 12 | Allowable credit: `min(line4, line11)` → **Schedule 3 line 6k** |

**Note on line 10b inclusions:** Line 10b includes Schedule 3 lines 6c through 6j and 6l through 6z — meaning credits on line 6k (Form 8912 itself) are **not** subtracted from available capacity (Form 8912 cannot reduce its own base). Only credits that appear before line 6k in the nonrefundable credits chain reduce the available capacity.

**CREB pass-through additional limit:** If any of the credit on line 4 comes from CREB held through a pass-through entity, apply the additional formula per pass-through entity:

```
Allowable CREB pass-through credit = line11 × (taxable_income_from_pass_through / total_taxable_income)
```

If the pass-through taxable income is zero, no CREB credit is allowed from that entity for the year (and no carryforward is available for CREB).

### Part III — Credits from Form(s) 1097-BTC

Use this path when the taxpayer **received** one or more Forms 1097-BTC from bond issuers or pass-through entities.

For each Form 1097-BTC received, collect:
- issuer name
- issuer identifying number (EIN)
- unique identifier
- credit amount reported on Form 1097-BTC

| Line | Description |
|---|---|
| 13 | Table: (a) issuer name, (b1) issuer EIN, (b2) unique identifier, (c) credit amount per 1097-BTC |
| 14 | Sum of column (c) amounts → Part I line 1 |

Anti-double-count rule: if a partnership issues a Form 1097-BTC for a bond it holds, include only on Part III — **not** also on Part IV line 18.

### Part IV — Credits from Directly Held Bonds

Use this path when the taxpayer holds qualifying tax credit bonds but did **not** receive Form 1097-BTC.

For each bond, collect:
- bond issuer name, city/town, state
- issuer EIN
- bond issue date and maturity date
- disposal date, if applicable
- CUSIP number or required payment dates
- outstanding bond principal (quarterly bonds) or interest payable (BABs)
- credit rate (from Treasury Fiscal Data website, looked up by first binding contract date)
- allocation percentage

| Line | Description |
|---|---|
| 15a | Bond issuer name, city/state |
| 15b | Bond issuer EIN |
| 16a | Bond issue date |
| 16b | Bond maturity date |
| 17 | Date disposed (if redeemed, sold, or otherwise disposed in 2025) |
| 18 | Per-payment table: (a) CUSIP / payment dates, (b) outstanding principal or BAB interest payable, (c) credit rate, (d) (b)×(c), (e) % held per period, (f) (d)×(e) |
| 19 | Sum of column (f) amounts |
| 20 | Final credit amount after bond-type reduction (see §7) → Part I line 2 |

## 7. Formula Summary

### Part IV Line 20 — Bond-Type Reduction Rule

```
if bond_type in [NCREB, QECB]:
    line20 = line19 × 0.70
else:  # CREB, QZAB, QSCB, BAB
    line20 = line19
```

### Part IV Line 18(e) — Percentage Held

```
if bond_type in [BAB, pre-10/4/08 QZAB]:
    pct_held = 1.00  (100% — single annual/semi-annual payment)
else:  # quarterly credit bonds
    pct_held = 0.25 per credit allowance date
    # prorate if bond issued, redeemed, or matured mid-quarter
```

### Credit allowance dates (quarterly bonds)

March 15, June 15, September 15, December 15.

### BAB credit rate

Fixed at **35%** of interest payable (not principal).

### Overall allowable credit

```
line12 = min(line4_or_line6, line11)
```

### Carryforward

```
carryforward_to_next_year = line4 - line12
# Allowed for: NCREB, QECB, post-10/4/08 QZAB, QSCB, BAB
# NOT allowed for: CREB, pre-10/4/08 QZAB
# CREB/pre-10/4/08 QZAB: unused credit may be DEDUCTED instead (not carried forward)
```

## 8. Developer-Safe Pseudocode

### High-level entry point

```text
if taxpayer_has_no_qualifying_tax_credit_bonds:
    no Form8912

else:
    create one Form8912

    if Form1097_BTC_received:
        complete Part III
        line14 = sum(1097_BTC amounts)
        line1 = line14
    else:
        line1 = 0

    if qualifying_bonds_held_without_1097BTC:
        complete Part IV
        line19 = sum(principal_or_interest × credit_rate × pct_held for each payment)
        if bond_type in {NCREB, QECB}:
            line20 = 0.70 × line19
        else:
            line20 = line19
        line2 = line20
    else:
        line2 = 0

    line3 = carryforward_from_prior_year
    line4 = line1 + line2 + line3

    compute return-level limitation through line11
    line12 = min(line4, line11)

    Schedule3.line6k = line12
```

### Detailed computation

```python
def compute_form8912(
    credits_1097btc,         # list from Part III: each entry has amount
    direct_bonds,            # list from Part IV: each entry has type, principal/interest, rate, dates
    carryforward_prior,      # from prior year Form 8912 line 4 minus line 12
    form1040_line16,
    schedule2_line1z,
    form6251_line11,         # AMT (required even if no AMT owed)
    prior_credits_10b,       # Form1040.line19 + Sched3 lines 2–5b, 6c–6j, 6l–6z
    foreign_tax_credit,      # Form 1116 / Schedule 3 line 1
    form3800_credit,         # general business credit
    form8801_8827_credit,    # prior year minimum tax credit
):
    # Part III
    part3_total = sum(c.amount for c in credits_1097btc)

    # Part IV
    part4_total = 0
    for bond in direct_bonds:
        line19 = sum(
            bond.principal_or_interest * bond.credit_rate * bond.pct_held
            for payment in bond.payments
        )
        if bond.bond_type in ["NCREB", "QECB"]:
            line20 = line19 * 0.70
        else:
            line20 = line19
        part4_total += line20

    # Part I
    line1 = part3_total
    line2 = part4_total
    line3 = carryforward_prior
    line4 = line1 + line2 + line3

    # Part II — tax liability limitation
    line7 = form1040_line16 + schedule2_line1z
    line8 = form6251_line11
    line9 = line7 + line8
    line10e = foreign_tax_credit + prior_credits_10b + form3800_credit + form8801_8827_credit
    line11 = max(0, line9 - line10e)
    line12 = min(line4, line11)

    # Carryforward (CREB/pre-10/4/08 QZAB portion cannot carry forward)
    carryforward = line4 - line12

    return {
        "allowable_credit": line12,           # → Schedule3.line6k
        "carryforward_to_next_year": carryforward,
    }
```

## 11. Compute Order

Form 8912 depends on:

1. All prior nonrefundable credits on Schedule 3 (lines 2 through 6j) — must be computed first
2. Form 1040 line 16 (tax) and Schedule 2 line 1z
3. Form 6251 (AMT) — required even if result is zero
4. Form 3800 (general business credit) — if applicable
5. Form 8801 / Form 8827 — if applicable

Form 8912 must be computed **before** Schedule 3 line 8 aggregation but **after** all credits from Schedule 3 lines 6c–6j and 6l–6z (which reduce the Form 8912 liability limit via line 10b).

## 13. Dollar Limits, Rates, and Key Constants

| Item | Value |
|---|---|
| Qualifying bond cutoff | Must be issued **on or before December 31, 2017** |
| BAB credit rate | **35%** of interest payable (not principal) |
| NCREB and QECB reduction | Credit = computed amount × **70%** |
| QZAB (pre-7/1/99) AFR | 110% of long-term AFR compounded annually |
| Quarterly credit allowance dates | March 15, June 15, September 15, December 15 |
| Carryforward | **Indefinite** for NCREB, QECB, post-10/4/08 QZAB, QSCB, BAB |
| CREB carryforward | **None** — unused credit may be **deducted** instead |
| Pre-10/4/08 QZAB carryforward | **None** — deduction option same as CREB |
| Form 6251 requirement | Required even if AMT = $0 |

## 14. Out of Scope

| Item | Reason |
|---|---|
| **Bonds issued after 12/31/2017** | Legislatively excluded; these never qualify |
| **Qualified tax credit bonds acquired via secondary market after issuer already received payment** | Complex attribution rules; deferred to tax specialist |
| **CREB deduction election** | Taxpayer may deduct unused CREB credit rather than carry forward; the deduction mechanism is out of scope for the credit computation |
| **Accrual-basis interest income allocation on bond sale** | Mid-credit-period accrual and basis adjustment on secondary market sales; out of scope |
| **Corporate and estate/trust filers** | Only individual (Form 1040) filers in scope |

## 16. Bottom-Line Answers

### How many Form 8912 per return?

**0 or 1 Form 8912 per return.** Not per spouse. Not per bond. All qualifying bond credits (multiple 1097-BTCs, multiple directly held bonds) are consolidated inside the one Form 8912. Attach additional pages 2–3 as needed for multiple bonds.

### Is it required per spouse?

**No**, not on a joint return.

- **MFJ:** file **one** Form 8912 for the combined return.
- **MFS:** each spouse files their own Form 8912 on their own separate return if they individually hold qualifying bonds.

### Does it require information from both spouses?

**Yes, it can.** On MFJ, the tax liability limitation in Part II uses joint Form 1040 line 16 + joint Schedule 2 line 1z, and subtracts all joint prior nonrefundable credits. If either spouse holds qualifying bonds or receives Form(s) 1097-BTC, all credits are reported on the single joint Form 8912.

### Is there a carryforward?

**Yes — for most bond types.** NCREB, QECB, post-10/4/08 QZAB, QSCB, and BAB all carry forward indefinitely. **CREB and pre-10/4/08 QZAB do not carry forward** — unused credit from those bond types may be **deducted** instead.

### Does Form 6251 (AMT) need to be filed?

**Yes — always**, even if the taxpayer owes no AMT. Form 6251 line 11 is required to complete Form 8912 Part II line 8.
