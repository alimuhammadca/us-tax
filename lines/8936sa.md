# Schedule A (Form 8936) — Clean Vehicle Credit Amount

**Tax year:** 2025
**IRS form:** Schedule A (Form 8936) (2025), Attachment Sequence No. 69A, OMB No. 1545-2137, Cat. No. 93602W (created 8/21/25)
**Sources:** Schedule A (Form 8936) 2025; Form 8936 (2025); Instructions for Form 8936 (2025); J.K. Lasser's Your Income Tax 2025 Professional Edition § 25.16; Taxes For Dummies 2024 Edition (Ch. 16); IRC §§ 30D, 25E, 45W
**Destination:** Schedule A → Form 8936 → Schedule 3 line 6f (new personal use) / Schedule 3 line 6m (previously owned) / Form 3800 (business + commercial use)

---

## 1. Line Identity

Schedule A (Form 8936) computes the **Clean Vehicle Credit Amount** for one vehicle. It is a supporting worksheet attached to the main **Form 8936** and is completed separately for each qualifying clean vehicle placed in service during the tax year.

Three vehicle categories are covered — one per Schedule A:
- **Part II / Part III** — New Clean Vehicle (business/investment use split from personal use)
- **Part IV** — Previously Owned Clean Vehicle
- **Part V** — Qualified Commercial Clean Vehicle

**2025 acquisition cutoff:** All three credit paths require the vehicle to have been **acquired before October 1, 2025**. Vehicles acquired on or after that date cannot use this schedule.

---

## 2. Per-Vehicle Filing Rules

```
Schedule A (Form 8936) count = one per clean vehicle placed in service during the tax year
Form 8936 count per return   = 0 or 1
```

The form's own note (top of page 1) states:
> "Complete a separate Schedule A (Form 8936) for each clean vehicle placed in service during the tax year."

> "Individuals who transferred the credit to the dealer at the time of sale must file this schedule and Form 8936."

This means:
- One vehicle placed in service → one Schedule A.
- Two vehicles placed in service (e.g., one per spouse on a joint return) → two Schedule As, one Form 8936.
- Schedule A is **not** one per return and **not** one per spouse — it is **one per vehicle**.
- Even if the credit was transferred at the dealership (applied as a down payment), the taxpayer must still file Schedule A and Form 8936 to reconcile eligibility.

---

## 3. Per-Spouse and Joint-Return Rules

**Not required per spouse.** It is per vehicle. On a joint return, both spouses' vehicles each get their own Schedule A on the same return.

**MFS rule:** Only **one spouse** can claim the credit for a given vehicle. If spouses file separately, the claiming spouse must be the one listed on the dealer's seller report. The credit for a jointly placed-in-service vehicle may be claimed on **only one tax return**.

**Does Schedule A require information from both spouses?** Not on the Schedule A itself — it is vehicle-specific. However, the **MAGI eligibility check** on Form 8936 (lines 1–5) uses **joint MAGI** on a joint return, so both spouses' income determines whether each Schedule A is eligible.

```
if filing_status == MFJ:
    one Form 8936 + one Schedule A per vehicle
    MAGI check uses joint MAGI
    each spouse's vehicle gets its own Schedule A
else:
    taxpayer-only MAGI; one Schedule A per vehicle placed in service
```

## 4. Three Credit Paths and Vehicle Eligibility

### Path A — New Clean Vehicle (IRC § 30D) → Parts II and III

| Requirement | Rule |
|---|---|
| Manufacturer | Qualified manufacturer (IRS-registered) |
| Motor vehicle standard | Under Title II of the Clean Air Act |
| GVWR | Less than 14,000 lbs |
| Battery capacity | At least 7 kilowatt hours |
| Final assembly | North America |
| MSRP cap | $80,000 for vans/SUVs/pickups; $55,000 for all other vehicles |
| Acquisition date | After 2022 and before October 1, 2025 |
| Placed in service | During 2025 |
| Not resold | Within 30 days of placed-in-service date |
| Acquisition purpose | For use or to lease to others — not for resale |

**Maximum credit:** $7,500
- $3,750 if vehicle meets critical minerals requirement only
- $3,750 if vehicle meets battery components requirement only
- $7,500 if vehicle meets **both** requirements

**MAGI limits (checked on Form 8936, not Schedule A):**

| Filing Status | MAGI Ceiling |
|---|---|
| MFJ / QSS | $300,000 |
| HOH | $225,000 |
| Single / MFS | $150,000 |

### Path B — Previously Owned Clean Vehicle (IRC § 25E) → Part IV

| Requirement | Rule |
|---|---|
| Model year | At least 2 years earlier than the calendar year of purchase |
| Ownership | Taxpayer is not the original owner |
| Original sale price | $25,000 or less |
| First eligible claimant | First eligible individual to claim since August 16, 2022 (other than original owner) |
| 3-year lookback | No previously owned clean vehicle credit claimed for any vehicle in the 3-year period ending on this vehicle's purchase date |
| Not resold | Within 30 days of placed-in-service date |
| Acquisition purpose | For use — not for resale |
| Dependent | Cannot be claimed as a dependent on another person's return |
| Acquisition date | After 2022 and before October 1, 2025 |
| Placed in service | During 2025 |

**Maximum credit:** $4,000 (lesser of 30% × sales price or $4,000)

**MAGI limits (checked on Form 8936, not Schedule A):**

| Filing Status | MAGI Ceiling |
|---|---|
| MFJ / QSS | $150,000 |
| HOH | $112,500 |
| Single / MFS | $75,000 |

### Path C — Qualified Commercial Clean Vehicle (IRC § 45W) → Part V

| Requirement | Rule |
|---|---|
| Depreciation character | Subject to depreciation allowance (or tax-exempt entity exception applies) |
| Acquisition purpose | For use or to lease to others — not for resale |
| Acquisition date | After 2022 and before October 1, 2025 |
| Placed in service | During 2025 |

**Maximum credit:**
- $7,500 for vehicles with GVWR under 14,000 lbs
- $40,000 for vehicles with GVWR of 14,000 lbs or more

**Credit rate:**
- **15%** of (cost − § 179 deduction), limited to incremental cost — if vehicle is also powered by gas or diesel
- **30%** of (cost − § 179 deduction), limited to incremental cost — if vehicle is purely electric or fuel cell

No MAGI limit for the commercial path.

## 5. MAGI Definition

Applies to Paths A and B only:

```
MAGI = AGI
     + foreign earned income excluded (Form 2555)
     + income from Puerto Rico or American Samoa excluded from AGI
```

**Prior-year MAGI election:** The vehicle is disqualified only if **both** current-year and prior-year MAGI exceed the limit. Form 8936 checks current-year MAGI (lines 1–2) and prior-year MAGI (lines 3–5). A taxpayer whose income spiked in 2025 can still qualify based on their lower 2024 MAGI.

## 6. Part I — Vehicle Details (Lines 1a–7)

Every Schedule A begins here regardless of vehicle category.

| Line | Field | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| 1a | Year | Model year of the vehicle |
| 1b | Make | Manufacturer name |
| 1c | Model | Vehicle model |
| 2 | VIN | 17-character Vehicle Identification Number |
| 3 | Placed-in-service date | MM/DD/YYYY |
| 4a | Credit transferred to dealer? | Yes → enter transferred amount from seller's report; No → go to line 5 |
| 4b | Checkbox | Check if line 4a = Yes AND directed to do so by line 8a, 8d, 13a, or 13c |

**Routing (lines 5–7):**

```
Line 5: New clean vehicle acquired before 10/1/2025?
    Yes → Part II
    No  → Line 6

Line 6: Previously owned clean vehicle acquired after 2022 and before 10/1/2025?
    Yes → Part IV
    No  → Line 7

Line 7: Qualified commercial clean vehicle acquired after 2022 and before 10/1/2025?
    Yes → Part V
    No  → STOP — vehicle does not qualify; cannot use this schedule
```

## 7. Part II — Business/Investment Use Part of New Clean Vehicle (Lines 8a–11)

### Gating checks

| Check | Condition | If disqualified |
|---|---|---|
| Line 8a | Resold within 30 days? | Stop — no credit; if line 4a = Yes → Schedule 2 line 1b |
| Line 8b | Filing individual income tax return? | No → skip 8c/8d, go to 8e |
| Line 8c | Current-year MAGI (Form 8936 line 2) > Part II/III limit? | Yes → go to 8d; No → go to 8e |
| Line 8d | Prior-year MAGI (Form 8936 line 4) > Part II/III limit? | Yes → Stop; if line 4a = Yes → Schedule 2 line 1b |
| Line 8e | Acquired for use or to lease (not resale)? | No → Stop |

### Credit computation

```
Line 9   Tentative credit amount (from IRS chart / Form 8936 instructions; up to $7,500)
Line 10  Business/investment use percentage
Line 11  = line 9 × line 10%
         → Carry line 11 to Form 8936 Part II line 2
         If line 10 = 100% → stop here (no personal use; skip Part III)
         Otherwise → continue to Part III
```

---

## 8. Part III — Personal Use Part of New Clean Vehicle (Line 12)

```
Line 12  = line 9 (from Part II) − line 11 (from Part II)
         → Carry line 12 to Form 8936 Part III line 9
```

## 9. Part IV — Previously Owned Clean Vehicle (Lines 13a–17)

### Gating checks

| Check | Condition | If disqualified |
|---|---|---|
| Line 13a | Resold within 30 days? | Stop; if line 4a = Yes → Schedule 2 line 1c |
| Line 13b | Current-year MAGI > Part IV limit? | Yes → go to 13c |
| Line 13c | Prior-year MAGI > Part IV limit? | Yes → Stop; if line 4a = Yes → Schedule 2 line 1c |
| Line 13d | Previously owned credit claimed for another vehicle in prior 3 years? | Yes → Stop |
| Line 13e | Sales price > $25,000? | Yes → Stop |
| Line 13f | Acquired for use, not resale? | No → Stop |
| Line 13g | Can be claimed as dependent on another return? | Yes → Stop |

### Credit computation

```
Line 14  Sales price of the vehicle
Line 15  = line 14 × 30% (0.30)
Line 16  Maximum credit = $4,000  [fixed on the form]
Line 17  = min(line 15, line 16)
         → Carry line 17 to Form 8936 Part IV line 14
```

---

## 10. Part V — Qualified Commercial Clean Vehicle (Lines 18a–26)

### Gating checks

| Check | Condition | If disqualified |
|---|---|---|
| Line 18a | Elective payment election? | If yes, enter IRS-issued registration number |
| Line 18b | Subject to depreciation allowance? | No → Stop (unless exception applies) |
| Line 18c | Acquired for use or to lease (not resale)? | No → Stop |

### Credit computation

```
Line 18d  Also powered by gas or diesel? (Yes → 15% rate; No → 30% rate)
Line 18e  GVWR in pounds
Line 19   Cost or other basis of vehicle
Line 20   Section 179 expense deduction on this vehicle
Line 21   = line 19 − line 20
Line 22   = line 21 × 15%  [or × 30% if line 18d = "No"]
Line 23   Incremental cost of vehicle (from IRS table; excess cost over comparable non-electric)
Line 24   = min(line 22, line 23)
Line 25   Maximum credit:
              $7,500   if GVWR (line 18e) < 14,000 lbs
              $40,000  if GVWR (line 18e) ≥ 14,000 lbs
Line 26   = min(line 24, line 25)
          → Carry line 26 to Form 8936 Part V line 19
```

---

## 11. Full Formula Summary

```
NEW CLEAN VEHICLE (personal + business split):
  Tentative credit (line 9)   IRS chart (up to $7,500)
  Business credit (line 11)   = line 9 × business%   → Form 8936 Part II line 2
  Personal credit (line 12)   = line 9 − line 11      → Form 8936 Part III line 9

PREVIOUSLY OWNED CLEAN VEHICLE:
  Tentative (line 15)         = sales price × 30%
  Credit (line 17)            = min(line 15, $4,000)  → Form 8936 Part IV line 14

QUALIFIED COMMERCIAL CLEAN VEHICLE:
  Adjusted basis (line 21)    = cost − §179
  Rate-applied (line 22)      = line 21 × 15% [or 30%]
  Capped (line 24)            = min(line 22, incremental cost)
  Credit (line 26)            = min(line 24, $7,500 or $40,000) → Form 8936 Part V line 19
```

## 12. Output Mapping (Schedule A → Form 8936 → Return)

| Schedule A Part | Vehicle Type | Feeds Into | Final Destination |
|---|---|---|---|
| Part II line 11 | New — business use | Form 8936 Part II line 2 | Form 3800 → Schedule 3 line 6a |
| Part III line 12 | New — personal use | Form 8936 Part III line 9 → line 13 | **Schedule 3 line 6f** |
| Part IV line 17 | Previously owned | Form 8936 Part IV line 14 → line 18 | **Schedule 3 line 6m** |
| Part V line 26 | Commercial | Form 8936 Part V line 19 → line 21 | Form 3800 → Schedule 3 line 6a |

**Point-of-sale transfer recapture:**
- New clean vehicle: excess transferred amount → Schedule 2 line 1b
- Previously owned: excess transferred amount → Schedule 2 line 1c

**No carryforward:** Unused personal-use new clean vehicle credit (Form 8936 Part III line 13) and unused previously owned credit (Form 8936 Part IV line 18) are permanently lost if the tax liability limit is smaller than the credit amount.

## 13. How Form 8936 Aggregates All Schedule As

### Part I — MAGI eligibility (line 5 threshold chart)

Form 8936 checks both current-year and prior-year MAGI. Taxpayer qualifies if MAGI is within limits for **either** year.

### Part II — Business/investment use (new clean vehicle)

```
line 6  = sum of Part II line 11 from all Schedule A(s)
line 7  = passthrough K-1 business clean vehicle credit
line 8  = line 6 + line 7  → Form 3800
```

### Part III — Personal use (new clean vehicle)

```
line 9   = sum of Part III line 12 from all Schedule A(s)
line 10  = Form 1040 line 18 (total tax before credits)
line 11  = Schedule 3 lines 1–4, 5b, 6d, 6i, and 6m (prior nonrefundable credits)
line 12  = max(0, line 10 − line 11)
line 13  = min(line 9, line 12)  → Schedule 3 line 6f
```

If line 12 < line 9 → unused credit is **permanently lost** (no carryforward).

### Part IV — Previously owned clean vehicle

```
line 14  = sum of Part IV line 17 from all Schedule A(s)
line 15  = Form 1040 line 18
line 16  = certain Schedule 3 personal credits
line 17  = max(0, line 15 − line 16)
line 18  = min(line 14, line 17)  → Schedule 3 line 6m
```

If line 17 < line 14 → unused credit is **permanently lost** (no carryforward).

### Part V — Qualified commercial clean vehicle

```
line 19  = sum of Part V line 26 from all Schedule A(s)
line 20  = passthrough commercial clean vehicle credit
line 21  = line 19 + line 20  → Form 3800
```

## 17. Interaction with Other Tax Items

### Basis reduction
The vehicle's depreciable basis must be reduced by the credit amount before computing depreciation (IRC § 30D(f)(1)):
- New vehicle → reduce by Schedule A line 9 (tentative credit, before business/personal split)
- Previously owned → reduce by Schedule A line 17
- Commercial → reduce by Schedule A line 26

### Section 179 (commercial vehicles)
For Part V, any § 179 deduction already claimed on the vehicle reduces the cost basis before the credit rate is applied (line 20). The two items interact directly.

### Form 3800 (General Business Credit)
The business/investment use portion (Part II) and the commercial vehicle credit (Part V) are general business credits that flow through Form 3800 and are subject to general business credit ordering and carryover rules.

### Schedule 2 recapture (point-of-sale transfer)
If the credit was transferred to the dealer (line 4a = Yes) and the vehicle is later determined to be ineligible (e.g., resold within 30 days, MAGI too high), the transferred amount becomes a recapture liability on:
- **Schedule 2 line 1b** — new clean vehicle
- **Schedule 2 line 1c** — previously owned clean vehicle

### AMT
The personal use clean vehicle credit (Schedule 3 line 6f) and previously owned credit (Schedule 3 line 6m) are **not** limited by the AMT. They are nonrefundable credits against regular tax not subject to the AMT ordering rules that apply to general business credits.

---

## 18. Out of Scope

- **Vehicles acquired on or after October 1, 2025:** All three credit paths (lines 5, 6, 7) explicitly restrict to vehicles acquired before that date.
- **Leased vehicles (lessee):** The credit belongs to the lessor/dealer, not the lessee.
- **Resale within 30 days:** Automatic disqualification on lines 8a / 13a.
- **Final assembly outside North America:** Ineligible for the new clean vehicle credit.
- **Dependents:** Cannot claim the previously owned clean vehicle credit (line 13g).
- **Qualified plug-in vehicles placed in service before 2023:** Prior-law credit rules; not applicable to 2025 returns.
- **Commercial vehicle AMT interaction and Form 3800 multi-year carryover:** General business credit ordering and carryover tracking are out of scope.
