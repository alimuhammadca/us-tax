# Form 8962 — Premium Tax Credit (PTC)

**Tax year: 2025**
**Filed with: Form 1040, 1040-SR, or 1040-NR**
**IRS source: `C:\us-tax\docs\IRS-Forms\f8962.pdf`**
**Semantic assets: `C:\us-tax\pdfs\f8962_field_map_semantic.csv`**

---

## Overview

Form 8962 does two things:

1. Computes the **allowed Premium Tax Credit (PTC)** — a refundable credit that helps eligible individuals and families afford health insurance purchased through a Health Insurance Marketplace (Exchange).
2. **Reconciles** that allowed credit with any **advance payments of the premium tax credit (APTC)** already paid to the insurer on the taxpayer's behalf.

The result is either:
- An **additional credit** (`line24 > line25`) reported on Schedule 3 line 9, or
- A **repayment of excess APTC** (`line25 > line24`) reported on Schedule 2 line 1a (subject to an income-based cap).

---

## Who Must File

Per IRS instructions, you must file Form 8962 if:
1. You are claiming the PTC, **or**
2. APTC was paid for you or someone in your tax family, **or**
3. APTC was paid for someone you told the Marketplace would be in your tax family but no one actually included that person in a tax family on a return.

If APTC was paid for any individual in your coverage household, you **must** file even if you are otherwise not required to file a tax return.

---

## Eligibility: Applicable Taxpayer

You are an **applicable taxpayer** if ALL of the following are true:
- Your household income is at or above **100% of FPL** for your family size (see exceptions below for below-100% cases)
- No one can claim you as a dependent
- You are not filing MFS — unless you qualify under an exception (see Special Situations)
- You purchased coverage through a Marketplace (not directly from an insurer)
- No affordable employer-sponsored minimum essential coverage (MEC) was available to you

**IRA extension through 2025:** The Inflation Reduction Act removed the 400% FPL income ceiling. Taxpayers above 400% FPL can still claim PTC; the applicable figure simply caps at 0.0850 (8.50%), meaning they are expected to pay at most 8.50% of household income toward the benchmark plan.

---

## What You Need

- **Form 1095-A** for each Marketplace policy. If the Marketplace sent a **corrected** Form 1095-A, use only the corrected form — discard the original for that policy.
- Your Form 1040 return data: filing status, dependents, modified AGI.
- Facts about any **shared policies** (Part IV required if a policy covered people from more than one tax family).
- If you **married during 2025** and APTC was paid for someone in your tax family, you may elect the **alternative calculation for year of marriage** (Part V).

---

## Where Form 8962 Outputs Go

| Form 8962 Line | Amount | Destination |
|---|---|---|
| Line 26 (net PTC) | `line24 − line25` when line24 > line25 | Schedule 3 (Form 1040), line 9 |
| Line 29 (excess repayment) | `min(line27, line28)` when line25 > line24 | Schedule 2 (Form 1040), line 1a |
| Line 29 amount | Same repayment | Form 6251 line 10 (adds back to AMT base) |

Engine pseudocode:

```text
if line24 > line25:
    line26 = line24 - line25
    Schedule3.line9 += line26
elif line25 > line24:
    line27 = line25 - line24
    line28 = repayment_cap_from_table5  # or blank if 400%+ FPL
    line29 = min(line27, line28) if line28 else line27
    Schedule2.line1a += line29
else:
    line26 = 0  # no adjustment needed
```

# Part I — Annual and Monthly Contribution Amount (Lines 1–8)

Part I determines how much the household is expected to contribute toward benchmark (SLCSP) coverage based on income relative to the federal poverty line.

## Line 1 — Tax Family Size

Enter the number of individuals in the **tax family**: yourself + spouse (if MFJ) + dependents claimed on the return. Does **not** include:
- Anyone who can claim you (or your spouse on a joint return) as a dependent
- A spouse if filing MFS

## Lines 2a and 2b — Modified AGI

**Line 2a — Taxpayer's modified AGI** (use Worksheet 1-1):

```text
modified_agi = Form1040.line11 (AGI)
             + Form1040.line2a (tax-exempt interest)
             + Form2555.line45_or_50 (foreign earned income exclusion)
             + Form2555.line47 (foreign housing exclusion)
             + (Form1040.line6a - Form1040.line6b)  # nontaxable SS benefits
```

**Line 2b — Dependents' combined modified AGI** (use Worksheet 1-2 for each qualifying dependent):

Include the modified AGI of each dependent who is required to file a return for actual tax liability — **not** merely to recover withheld taxes.

## Line 3 — Household Income

```text
line3 = max(0, line2a + line2b)
```

Combine even if one value is negative. If the total is negative, enter zero.

## Line 4 — Federal Poverty Line

For **2025 returns**, use the **2024 federal poverty line** amounts from the table matching your state of residence on December 31, 2025. If you lived in Alaska or Hawaii at any point during the year, or if joint filers lived in different states, use the **higher** table.

| Family Size | 48 States + D.C. | Alaska | Hawaii |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | $15,060 | $18,810 | $17,310 |
| 2 | $20,440 | $25,540 | $23,540 |
| 3 | $25,820 | $32,270 | $29,770 |
| 4 | $31,200 | $39,000 | $36,000 |
| 5 | $36,580 | $45,730 | $42,230 |
| 6 | $41,960 | $52,460 | $48,460 |
| 7 | $47,340 | $59,190 | $54,690 |
| 8 | $52,720 | $65,920 | $60,920 |
| Each additional person | +$5,380 | +$6,730 | +$6,230 |

**Line 4 checkboxes:**
- **(a):** Check if household income is below 100% FPL but the Marketplace enrollment exception applies (APTC was paid based on projected 100%+ income)
- **(b):** Check if line 3 is zero
- **(c):** Check if using the Part V alternative calculation for year of marriage

## Line 5 — Household Income as Percentage of FPL

Use **Worksheet 2**:

```text
line5 = floor(line3 / line4 * 100)  # truncate decimals, do NOT round
```

If the result exceeds 400, enter **401**.

**Below 100% FPL special rule:** Some taxpayers below 100% FPL may still qualify:
- Lawfully present aliens ineligible for Medicaid due to immigration status (if APTC was paid), or
- Taxpayers whose Marketplace-estimated income was at least 100% FPL at enrollment (if APTC was paid).

If **neither exception applies and no APTC was paid**, stop — do not complete Form 8962. If APTC was paid, skip contribution lines and proceed to reconciliation (Part III) to determine repayment.

## Line 7 — Applicable Figure

Look up line 5 in **Table 2** of the IRS instructions. This decimal is the maximum share of household income the taxpayer is expected to contribute toward the benchmark plan.

| Line 5 (% of FPL) | Applicable Figure |
|---|---|
| 150 or less | 0.0000 |
| 175 | 0.0100 |
| 200 | 0.0200 |
| 225 | 0.0300 |
| 250 | 0.0400 |
| 275 | 0.0500 |
| 300 | 0.0600 |
| 325 | 0.0663 |
| 350 | 0.0725 |
| 375 | 0.0788 |
| 400 or more | 0.0850 |

The full Table 2 has a value for every integer from 100 to 400+. The step size is 0.0004 per percentage point from 150–300% and tapers differently above 300%; the table must be used — do not interpolate.

## Lines 8a and 8b — Contribution Amounts

```text
line8a = round(line3 * line7)     # annual contribution amount
line8b = round(line8a / 12)       # monthly contribution amount
```

# Part II — Premium Tax Credit Claim and APTC Reconciliation (Lines 9–23)

## Line 9 — Shared Policy / Year-of-Marriage Election

Line 9 is the gateway to Part IV and Part V. Before completing lines 10–23, determine:

- **Shared policy (Part IV needed):** If a Marketplace policy covered at least one individual who is NOT in your tax family (e.g., ex-spouse, or a child enrolled by one parent but claimed by the other), check **Yes** and complete **Part IV** first to compute allocated amounts. Return to Part II using the allocated figures.
- **Year-of-marriage election (Part V):** If you married during 2025 and wish to use the alternative calculation, check **Yes** and complete **Part V** before computing lines 12–23.

If neither applies, check **No** and proceed to line 10.

## Line 10 — Annual vs. Monthly Method

Check **Yes** (use **annual totals on line 11** only) if, for each policy:
- You were enrolled for all 12 months, **and**
- Form 1095-A Part III shows no month-to-month variation in columns A or B

Check **No** (complete **monthly lines 12–23**) if:
- You enrolled mid-year, changed plans, had a coverage gap, or coverage ended
- Your family composition or income changed during the year
- APTC amounts changed mid-year
- You married during the year (unless using Part V)
- The applicable SLCSP premium changed during the year

## Column Definitions (Lines 11–23)

All monthly lines and the annual line 11 share the same six columns:

| Column | Description | Source |
|---|---|---|
| **(a)** | Enrollment premium — actual monthly premium for all covered individuals | Form 1095-A Part III, Col A |
| **(b)** | Applicable SLCSP premium — second-lowest-cost silver plan for your area and family | Form 1095-A Part III, Col B (or corrected SLCSP if 1095-A is wrong/blank) |
| **(c)** | Monthly (or annual) contribution amount | Line 8b for monthly; line 8a for annual line 11 |
| **(d)** | Maximum premium assistance = `max(0, col_b − col_c)` | Computed |
| **(e)** | PTC allowed = `min(col_a, col_d)`. Reduce by QSEHRA permitted benefit if applicable; floor at zero. | Computed |
| **(f)** | Advance credit payments (APTC) received | Form 1095-A Part III, Col C |

**Core per-period formula:**
```text
col_d = max(0, col_b - col_c)
col_e = min(col_a, col_d)         # this is the allowed PTC for the period
```

**SLCSP correction:** If Form 1095-A column B is blank or incorrect (common when no APTC was paid, when coverage or family changed, or when shared policies apply), the taxpayer must determine the correct SLCSP premium from HealthCare.gov tools or the Marketplace directly.

## Line 11 — Annual Totals (Full-Year Path Only)

Use when line 10 is Yes:
- Column (a): Form 1095-A line 33A
- Column (b): Form 1095-A line 33B
- Column (c): line 8a
- Column (f): Form 1095-A line 33C

## Lines 12–23 — Monthly Rows (January–December)

Use when line 10 is No. Enter one row per covered month; leave uncovered months blank. Sources:
- Columns (a) and (b): Form 1095-A lines 21–32, columns A and B
- Column (c): line 8b (or recalculated for the specific month if circumstances changed mid-year)
- Column (f): Form 1095-A lines 21–32, column C

**Mid-year circumstance changes:** If household income, family size, or coverage changed, recompute the applicable figure using that month's income and family size to get a corrected monthly contribution for column (c).

## Unemployment Compensation Rule

If you received unemployment compensation for any week beginning in 2025, you may elect to treat household income as not exceeding 133% of the FPL for purposes of Worksheet 2. Because 133 < 150, this effectively sets the applicable figure to 0.0000 and reduces the required contribution to zero for those months.

## Lines 24 and 25 — Totals

```text
line24 = sum of col(e) from line11 or lines 12-23   # total PTC allowed
line25 = sum of col(f) from line11 or lines 12-23   # total APTC paid
```

# Part III — Reconciliation (Lines 26–29)

## Line 26 — Net Premium Tax Credit

| Condition | Action |
|---|---|
| line24 > line25 | `line26 = line24 − line25`; enter on **Schedule 3 line 9**; skip lines 27–29 |
| line24 = line25 | `line26 = 0`; stop |
| line24 < line25 | Leave line 26 blank; continue to line 27 |

## Line 27 — Excess Advance Payment

```text
line27 = line25 - line24    # only when line25 > line24
```

## Line 28 — Repayment Limitation

Look up line 5 in **Table 5** (Repayment Limitation). If line 5 is **400 or more**, leave line 28 blank — no cap applies and line 27 is fully owed.

**2025 Repayment Limitation (Table 5):**

| Household Income as % of FPL (line 5) | Single Filer | All Other Filing Statuses |
|---|---|---|
| Less than 200% | $375 | $750 |
| At least 200%, less than 300% | $975 | $1,950 |
| At least 300%, less than 400% | $1,625 | $3,250 |
| 400% or more | No cap — full repayment | No cap — full repayment |

**Exceptions — no cap applies regardless of income:**
- You are not lawfully present in the U.S. for the entire year
- Line 5 is 400 or more

## Line 29 — Repayment Amount

```text
line29 = min(line27, line28)    # if line28 is blank: line29 = line27
```

Enter line 29 on **Schedule 2 (Form 1040), line 1a**.

# Part IV — Allocation of Policy Amounts (Lines 30–33)

Complete Part IV when a single Marketplace policy covered individuals from **more than one tax family**. Common triggers:
- Divorce or legal separation during 2025
- Married filing separately
- A child enrolled by one parent but claimed on the other parent's return
- Any other situation where a policy covered someone outside your tax family

Up to **four policies** can be allocated on lines 30–33. If more than four policies require allocation, check **No** on the "all allocations complete" question and attach a statement with the same information.

## Columns for Each Allocation Line

The seven columns per allocation line — verified against `f8962_field_map_semantic.csv`:

| Column | Semantic Name | Description |
|---|---|---|
| (a) | `line3Xa_policy_number_NN` | Policy number from Form 1095-A box 2 |
| (b) | `line3Xb_other_taxpayer_ssn_NN` | SSN of the primary filer in the other tax family |
| (c) | `line3Xc_premium_allocation_pct_NN` | Your share (%) of enrollment premiums (col A) |
| (d) | `line3Xd_slcsp_allocation_pct_NN` | Your share (%) of SLCSP premiums (col B) |
| (e) | `line3Xe_advance_payment_allocation_pct_NN` | Your share (%) of APTC (col C) |
| (f) | `line3Xf_allocation_start_month_NN` | First month allocation applies (01–12) |
| (g) | `line3Xg_allocation_stop_month_NN` | Last month allocation applies (01–12) |

## Allocation Situations

**Situation 1 — Divorce or legal separation during the year:**
Both taxpayers may agree on any percentage from 0%–100%, but must use the **same percentage for all three amounts (cols c, d, e)**. If they cannot agree, each allocates **50%**.

**Situation 2 — Married filing separately at year-end:**
Allocate 100% of each enrolled individual's amounts to the spouse whose tax family that individual belongs to.

**Situation 3 — No APTC was paid; shared policy:**
Allocate enrollment premium and SLCSP amounts based on the proportion of enrollees in each tax family. APTC percentage is 0% (no advance payments to split).

**Situation 4 — All other shared policies:**
Allocate based on actual membership of each tax family for each month. Both taxpayers must agree on percentages; if they cannot agree, each allocates 50%.

After completing Part IV, recompute monthly totals in lines 12–23 columns (a), (b), and (f) using the allocated amounts.

# Part V — Alternative Calculation for Year of Marriage (Lines 34–35)

Optional — reduces excess APTC repayment for taxpayers who married during 2025 and had APTC computed on single-filer projected income.

## Eligibility (Table 4)

All of the following must apply:
1. You married during 2025 (were **not** married on January 1, 2025)
2. APTC was paid for at least one spouse for at least one pre-marriage month
3. You are filing a joint return for 2025
4. Both spouses were residents of the same state throughout 2025

## Line 34 — Election Checkbox

Check **Yes** to elect the alternative calculation. Check **No** to use the standard calculation.

## Lines 35a–35h — Alternative Monthly Inputs

Part V uses a single line 35 with sub-fields a through h — verified against `f8962_field_map_semantic.csv`:

| Line | Semantic Field | Description |
|---|---|---|
| 35a | `line35a_alternative_calculation_months_taxpayer` | Number of alternative months for taxpayer (months before marriage with APTC) |
| 35b | `line35b_months_in_coverage_taxpayer` | Months enrolled in Marketplace plan (pre-marriage, taxpayer) |
| 35c | `line35c_alternative_calculation_months_spouse` | Number of alternative months for spouse |
| 35d | `line35d_months_in_coverage_spouse` | Months enrolled in Marketplace plan (pre-marriage, spouse) |
| 35e | `line35e_monthly_contribution_taxpayer` | Alternative monthly contribution for taxpayer (recomputed using taxpayer-only income/family size) |
| 35f | `line35f_monthly_slcsp_premium_taxpayer` | Applicable SLCSP premium for taxpayer's pre-marriage months |
| 35g | `line35g_monthly_contribution_spouse` | Alternative monthly contribution for spouse |
| 35h | `line35h_monthly_slcsp_premium_spouse` | Applicable SLCSP premium for spouse's pre-marriage months |

The alternative calculation computes PTC separately for each spouse for pre-marriage months using single-filer income and family size. If the result produces lower repayment than the standard calculation, use Part V results for lines 12–23 pre-marriage months.

**Important:** If you elect the alternative calculation and line 24 would otherwise exceed line 25, line 26 is forced to **0** — the election eliminates any net credit overage.

For detailed monthly computations, see **IRS Publication 974** (Premium Tax Credit).

# Special Situations

## MFS — Married Filing Separately

MFS filers generally **cannot** claim the PTC and must repay all APTC received. Two exceptions:
- **Exception 1 (living apart):** You lived apart from your spouse for the last 6 months of 2025, paid more than half the cost of maintaining a home for yourself and a qualifying person, and can effectively file as HOH.
- **Exception 2 (domestic abuse / abandonment):** You are a victim of domestic abuse or spousal abandonment. Write "Exemption" on the dotted line next to Line 10. This exception is limited to 3 consecutive tax years.

If APTC was paid and you are MFS without an applicable exception, you still complete Form 8962 to determine repayment — set line 24 to **0** and proceed to Part III.

## QSEHRA

If your employer provided a Qualified Small Employer Health Reimbursement Arrangement (QSEHRA), reduce your monthly PTC (column e) by the monthly permitted QSEHRA benefit amount, floor at zero. Write "QSEHRA" in the top margin of Form 8962.

## Below-100% FPL With APTC Paid

If household income fell below 100% FPL but APTC was paid based on projected income of 100%+, you are still treated as an applicable taxpayer. Your repayment is **capped** by Table 5 — you do not owe the full excess.

## Coverage Gaps

For months with no Marketplace coverage, leave all columns blank. No PTC is allowed and no APTC should have been paid for those months.

## Corrected Form 1095-A

If you receive a corrected Form 1095-A after already filing, you must generally amend your return using the corrected data.

# Computation Order

1. Collect all **Form 1095-A** documents; use corrected versions if received.
2. Confirm whether you must file Form 8962 (see Who Must File).
3. **Part I:** Compute modified AGI (Worksheet 1-1) → line 2a; dependents' MAGI (Worksheet 1-2) → line 2b; household income → line 3; FPL lookup → line 4; FPL % (Worksheet 2) → line 5; applicable figure (Table 2) → line 7; contribution amounts → lines 8a, 8b.
4. **Line 9 gateway:** If shared policy → complete **Part IV** first. If year-of-marriage election → qualify with Table 4, then complete **Part V**.
5. **Line 10:** Decide annual (line 11) or monthly (lines 12–23) path.
6. **Part II:** Complete lines 11 or 12–23; compute totals → lines 24 and 25.
7. **Part III:** Compare line 24 vs. line 25:
   - If line 24 > line 25 → line 26 → **Schedule 3 line 9**.
   - If line 25 > line 24 → lines 27–29 → **Schedule 2 line 1a**.

# Developer-Safe Formulas Summary

```text
# Part I
line3  = max(0, line2a + line2b)
line8a = round(line3 * line7)
line8b = round(line8a / 12)

# Part II — per period (annual or each month)
period_d = max(0, period_b - period_c)
period_e = min(period_a, period_d)

line24 = sum(period_e for all covered periods)
line25 = sum(period_f for all covered periods)

# Part III
if line24 > line25:
    line26 = line24 - line25
    Schedule3.line9 += line26
elif line25 > line24:
    line27 = line25 - line24
    line28 = repayment_cap_from_table5  # None if line5 >= 400
    line29 = min(line27, line28) if line28 is not None else line27
    Schedule2.line1a += line29
    Form6251.line10 += line29           # AMT add-back
else:
    line26 = 0
```

# Special Coding Rules

- **MFS without exception:** Set line 24 = 0 before Part III; compute full repayment.
- **100% allocation to other taxpayer:** If another taxpayer takes 100% of a shared policy, your line 1 may be 0; Part IV handles the 100% allocation and your Part II columns will be zero.
- **Multiple 1095-A forms:** Sum the allocated amounts from all policies into each month's columns (a), (b), (f) before computing (d) and (e).
- **Line 5 = 401 (above 400%):** Table 5 does not apply; line 28 is blank; line 29 = line 27 (full repayment).
- **Line 5 < 150:** Applicable figure = 0.0000; line 8a = 0; line 8b = 0; monthly contribution is zero so the full SLCSP premium is potentially creditable.

# Key 2025 Constants

| Constant | Value |
|---|---|
| Maximum applicable figure | 0.0850 (8.50%) at 400%+ FPL |
| Applicable figure at <150% FPL | 0.0000 |
| Unemployment compensation income threshold | 133% of FPL |
| Repayment cap — <200% FPL, single | $375 |
| Repayment cap — <200% FPL, other | $750 |
| Repayment cap — 200–299% FPL, single | $975 |
| Repayment cap — 200–299% FPL, other | $1,950 |
| Repayment cap — 300–399% FPL, single | $1,625 |
| Repayment cap — 300–399% FPL, other | $3,250 |
| Repayment cap — 400%+ FPL | No cap (full repayment) |

*Repayment caps are inflation-adjusted annually. Values above are 2025 per IRS Form 8962 instructions. For reference: 2024 caps were $950/$1,575 (single) vs. $1,900/$3,150 (other) for the 200–400% brackets.*
