Form **8978** is **not** per spouse and **not** per investment. It is a **partner-level form** used when an individual partner receives **Form 8986** from a partnership that pushed out audit or AAR adjustments under the BBA partnership rules. The IRS says partners (other than pass-through partners like partnerships or S corporations) use Form 8978 and Schedule A (Form 8978) to refigure tax for the affected years and intervening years based on the pushed-out adjustments. ([IRS][1])

## What Form 8978 is for

Use Form 8978 when you receive one or more **Forms 8986** and need to calculate the **additional reporting year tax** from those partnership-related adjustments. It is only for **income tax** changes; the instructions specifically say related **non-income-tax** changes, such as self-employment tax, are handled on an amended return for the first affected year. Since you said self-employment is out of scope, you can ignore that branch. ([IRS][2])

## How it relates to Form 1040 line 16

For an individual filing Form 1040, the key output is:

```text
if Form8978.line14 > 0:
    Form1040.line16 += Form8978.line14
```

The 2025 Form 1040 instructions also say that if **Form 8978 line 14 is positive**, you report that liability on the appropriate line of the return; for Form 1040 this is part of the **tax** section. If **Form 8978 line 14 is negative**, it does **not** reduce line 16 directly; instead, the 2025 Form 1040 instructions route a negative Form 8978 adjustment through **Schedule 3 line 6l**, with a worksheet if the negative amount exceeds the available tax. ([IRS][2])

So the practical 1040 mapping is:

```text
if Form8978.line14 >= 0:
    include in Form1040.line16
else:
    include via Schedule3.line6l workflow
```

## How many Forms 8978 are filed with one return

The filing count is driven by **source type** and **number of applicable years**, not by spouse or by investment.

### Base rule

If all adjustments come from one source type and fit in the form's year columns, you usually file:

* **one Form 8978**
* **one Schedule A (Form 8978)**

### Separate form for each source type

If the taxpayer has adjustments from:

* a **BBA audit**, and
* an **AAR filing**,

then the IRS requires **separate Forms 8978 and separate Schedules A** for each source type. ([IRS][2])

### Additional forms for more than 4 years

Each Form 8978 and its Schedule A only provide for **4 years of reporting**. If there are **more than 4 applicable tax years**, the IRS requires **additional Forms 8978 and Schedules A**. You then **add all line 14 amounts** from all Forms 8978 and report the total on the return. ([IRS][2])

### Joint return / spouse question

It is **not one per spouse by default**. Form 8978 is tied to the **partner who received Form 8986**. On a joint return, if only one spouse is the partner receiving the pushout adjustment, that spouse's Form 8978 is attached to the joint return. If both spouses separately received Form 8986 as separate partners, then you could have multiple Forms 8978 on the same joint return - but that is because there are **multiple partner-level adjustment streams**, not because the form is inherently "per spouse."

### Not per investment

Form 8978 is **not** organized per stock, fund, or investment holding. It is organized around **Forms 8986 / partnership adjustment sources**.

## How to complete Form 8978

At a high level, you do this:

### 1) Gather the source documents

You need:

* all relevant **Forms 8986**
* one or more **Schedule A (Form 8978)** to list the year-by-year adjustments
* the originally filed return data for each affected/intervening year
* any statements needed to recompute partner-level tax attributes (for example, suspended losses or section 199A schedules, if relevant) ([IRS][2])

### 2) Separate adjustments by source

Split the Forms 8986 into:

* **AAR-related**
* **BBA audit-related**

Each source type gets its **own** Form 8978 + Schedule A package. The instructions say if both source types exist, complete the **AAR-related Form 8978 first**, then carry its results into the "previously reported" amounts on the audit-related Form 8978. ([IRS][2])

### 3) Complete Schedule A (Form 8978)

Schedule A is where you list the pushed-out adjustments by year. It also captures related adjustments not directly shown on Form 8986 but needed because partner-level attributes changed. ([IRS][2])

### 4) Complete Form 8978 main lines

The instructions' example shows the structure:

* Use Schedule A results to populate Form 8978 lines for corrected tax and related amounts.
* **Line 11** = corrected tax liability for the affected year.
* **Line 12** = income tax as previously reported.
* **Line 13** = increase or decrease to tax.
* **Line 14** = total increase/decrease to tax after combining the year columns. ([IRS][2])

That is the key output line for the return.

### 5) Compute penalties and interest if applicable

If penalties shown on Form 8986 apply, compute them and enter them on the penalty lines. Interest is computed from the due date of the affected-year return up to payment date. The example in the instructions shows attaching a separate penalty calculation statement when needed. ([IRS][2])

### 6) Add together all Form 8978 line 14 amounts

If you have multiple Forms 8978:

* sum all **positive and negative** line 14 amounts,
* route positive totals to **Form 1040 line 16**,
* route negative totals through the **Schedule 3 line 6l** rules. ([IRS][2])

## Developer-safe filing logic

```text
group Forms8986 by source_type (AAR vs BBA audit)

for each source_type:
    split into batches of up to 4 applicable years
    create one Form8978 + ScheduleA per batch
    compute line14 for each form

total_form8978_adjustment = sum(all Form8978.line14)

if total_form8978_adjustment > 0:
    Form1040.line16 += total_form8978_adjustment
elif total_form8978_adjustment < 0:
    route through Schedule3.line6l workflow
```

## Bottom line

* **Per spouse?** No, not inherently.
* **Per investment?** No.
* **Per partnership adjustment source / Form 8986 stream?** Yes.
* **Multiple forms on one return?** Yes, if:

  * there are both **AAR** and **BBA audit** adjustments,
  * or there are **more than 4 applicable years**,
  * or separate partner-level adjustment streams exist on a joint return. ([IRS][2])
