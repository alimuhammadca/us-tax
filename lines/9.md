# 1) Line identity (2025 Form 1040)

- **Form**: 1040 / 1040-SR
- **Year**: 2025
- **Line**: 9
- **Label**: Total income
- **Printed instruction**: `Add lines 1z, 2b, 3b, 4b, 5b, 6b, 7a, and 8. This is your total income.`

The official form defines line 9 entirely by arithmetic. There is no separate worksheet or alternate calculation for line 9 itself.

---

# 2) Exact 2025 computation

```text
Form1040.line9 =
    Form1040.line1z
  + Form1040.line2b
  + Form1040.line3b
  + Form1040.line4b
  + Form1040.line5b
  + Form1040.line6b
  + Form1040.line7a
  + Form1040.line8
```

For a joint return, each feeder line already reflects the combined taxpayer-and-spouse amount, so line 9 is just the sum of the final computed joint feeder lines.

---

# 3) What is included and excluded

## 3.1 Included feeder lines

Only these eight lines flow into line 9:
- **1z** Wages, salaries, tips, etc.
- **2b** Taxable interest
- **3b** Ordinary dividends
- **4b** Taxable IRA distributions
- **5b** Taxable pensions and annuities
- **6b** Taxable Social Security benefits
- **7a** Capital gain or (loss)
- **8** Additional income from Schedule 1, line 10

## 3.2 Companion lines that do not enter line 9

These informational or upstream companion lines are **excluded** from the line-9 arithmetic:
- **2a** tax-exempt interest
- **3a** qualified dividends (subset of 3b; double-count prevention)
- **4a** gross IRA distributions
- **5a** gross pensions and annuities
- **6a** total Social Security benefits (only 6b enters line 9)
- **1i** nontaxable combat pay election (EIC / 8812 earned-income marker only)
- **6c, 6d, 7b**-checkboxes — Boolean fields, structurally ineligible to enter a numeric sum
- **7b** child-amount `line7bChildAmountFromForm8814Line10` — numeric **but** already inside line 7a (double-count prevention)

# 4) Signed behavior

Line 9 is not guaranteed to be positive.

Sources of negative feeders:
- **line 1f** can be negative (special-needs adoption per IRC §137(a)(3));
- **line 4b** can be negative for certain rollover scenarios;
- **line 7a** can be negative — capital losses flow through capped at $3,000 / $1,500 MFS per IRC §1211(b);
- **line 8** can be negative because Schedule 1 contains negative-entry items (NOL 8a, Form 2555 exclusion 8d, Medicaid waiver back-out 8s, §461(l) excess business loss 8p).

Implementation rule:

```text
line9 = exact_sum_of_the_eight_feeder_lines
```

Do **not** clamp line 9 to zero. The current code uses `addNonNull` (algebraic, null-preserving) followed by `roundMoney` (HALF_UP, sign-preserving). Line 15 is where `max(0, ...)` applies, not line 9.

# 5) Upstream dependencies

Line 9 has no standalone source documents of its own. It depends entirely on upstream computed return lines.

## 5.1 Required computed inputs

- `line1z`, `line2b`, `line3b`, `line4b`, `line5b`, `line6b`, `line7a`, `line8`

## 5.2 Typical upstream forms and statements

These are not direct line-9 inputs, but they drive the feeder lines:
- Forms **W-2**, **Form 8919**, and wage adjustments -> line 1z
- Forms **1099-INT / 1099-OID**, Schedule B when required -> line 2b
- Form **1099-DIV**, Schedule B when required -> line 3b
- Form **1099-R**, Form 8606, rollover / QCD logic -> line 4b
- Form **1099-R**, pension annuity basis rules, rollover / PSO logic -> line 5b
- Forms **SSA-1099 / RRB-1099**, Social Security worksheet / Pub. 915 -> line 6b
- Form **1099-DIV box 2a**, Schedule D, Form 8949, Form 2439, K-1 capital items, carryovers -> line 7a
- **Schedule 1** sources (unemployment, scholarships, digital-asset income, NOL etc.) -> line 8

## 5.3 Compute-order rule

Do not compute line 9 until all eight feeder lines are final. Several feeders depend on later branching logic:
- line 4b and 5b can change based on rollover / basis / exclusion rules;
- line 6b depends on the Social Security taxable-benefit computation;
- line 7a depends on Schedule D and capital-loss limitation logic;
- line 8 depends on Schedule 1 Part I totals.

# 6) Backend wiring

## 6.1 Primary computation site

The expression is preceded by a long verification block (the "13-audit consolidation FINAL") that documents inclusion and exclusion citations for every operand and companion line.

## 6.2 Persistence

`income.setTotalIncome(line9)` at `TaxReturnComputeService.java:7600` (conditional on non-null).

## 6.3 Form 8814 augmentation pattern

When a parent elects Form 8814 and the child's qualified-dividend / capital-gain distributions push the parent's return through Form 8814 line 12 (the unallocated remainder), the parent's totals must reflect the child amount **without** re-running every orchestrator. The augmentation at `TaxReturnComputeService.java:949-970` does this in place:

```java
BigDecimal sched1Line10Delta = subtractNonNegativeAllowNegative(sched1Line10After, sched1Line10Before);
// ...
income.setOtherIncomeSchedule1(sched1Line10After);
income.setTotalIncome(roundMoney(addNonNull(
    income.getTotalIncome(),                  // current line 9 value
    sched1Line10Delta                         // delta from Schedule 1 line 8z child push
)));
// ...
adjustments.setLine11aAdjustedGrossIncome(agiAfter);
adjustments.setLine11bAmountFromLine11aAdjustedGrossIncome(agiAfter);
```

Key points:
- the augmentation **does not** re-sum all 8 feeders; it adds the Schedule-1-line-10 delta to the current `totalIncome`;
- AGI is also updated in lockstep (`line11a` and `line11b` both repointed to the post-augmentation value);
- the child amount also flows through line 7a (and the dual-path 7a routing), so the augmentation only handles the Schedule 1 line 8z slot, never the line 7a slot;
- `setLine7bChildAmountFromForm8814Line10` at `TaxReturnComputeService.java:7593` records the entry-space disclosure value but does **not** add to line 9 (double-count prevention).

## 6.4 No standalone MFS guard at line 9

Line 9 has no orchestrator — it inherits MFS protection from the 13 upstream feeder orchestrators (1c-1i + computeInterestIncome + computeIraDistributions + computePensionAnnuities + computeSocialSecurityBenefits + computeCapitalGainLoss + computeOtherIncomes). On MFS, each feeder produces a taxpayer-only value, so the line-9 sum is taxpayer-only by construction.

# 7) What line 9 is **not**

This is where implementations often drift.

## 7.1 Not adjusted gross income

AGI is computed at line 11a:

```text
Form1040.line11a = Form1040.line9 - Form1040.line10
```

where line 10 is adjustments to income from Schedule 1, line 26.

## 7.2 Not taxable income

Taxable income is computed at line 15 after standard or itemized deductions, QBI, and additional deductions. Line 9 is only the pre-adjustment total-income figure.

## 7.3 Not provisional income for Social Security

The Pub. 915 SS worksheet does **not** use line 9. Its provisional-income line is constructed without line 6b (to break circular dependency on the SS taxability that is being computed). Per `TaxReturnComputeService.java:14897` it uses a Pub-915-subset accessor for the Schedule 1 line 10 input.

## 7.4 Not §6012 gross income for the filing-requirement test

IRC §6012 uses a separate "gross income" concept. The product does not implement the §6012 filing-requirement decision.

# 8) Validation rules

## 8.1 Arithmetic integrity

```text
validate line9 == line1z + line2b + line3b + line4b + line5b + line6b + line7a + line8
```

## 8.2 Feeder completeness

Every feeder line should be defined before summation. A missing feeder should be treated as **0** by `addNonNull`, not as a hard error. When **all** feeders are null, line 9 is null and the Income object is not persisted.

## 8.3 Companion-line exclusion guardrail

The Java type system prevents Boolean companions (6c, 6d, 7b checkboxes) from being added to the numeric sum. The verification block enumerates:
- line 2a (tax-exempt) excluded;
- line 3a (qualified dividends subset) excluded — double-count prevention;
- line 4a / 5a / 6a (gross — only taxable portion enters) excluded;
- line 1i (combat pay election) excluded — Boolean / EIC marker.
- line 7b's `line7bChildAmountFromForm8814Line10` excluded — double-count prevention.

## 8.4 Signed-number guardrail

Do not reject a negative line 9 merely because the label is "Total income."

# 9) Output and filing effect

Line 9 itself does not create any attachment or schedule. It is simply a carryforward computation on Form 1040.

Any attachments are driven by its feeder lines, for example:
- **Schedule 1** for line 8 / line 10;
- **Schedule D / Form 8949** for line 7a when required;
- **Form 8606** for line 4b when required;
- **Schedule B** for lines 2b / 3b when required.

# 10) Implementation guardrails

- Do **not** include informational companion lines (**2a, 3a, 4a, 5a, 6a, 1i, 7b** child-amount) in line 9.
- Do **not** compute line 9 before lines **4b, 5b, 6b, 7a, and 8** are finalized.
- Do **not** floor negative results to zero at line 9.
- Do **not** confuse line 9 total income with AGI, taxable income, or Social Security provisional income.
- Do **not** treat line 9 as creating its own filing requirement apart from the underlying feeder lines.
- When applying the Form 8814 augmentation, update `totalIncome` and **both** `line11aAdjustedGrossIncome` and `line11bAmountFromLine11aAdjustedGrossIncome` so AGI carry-forward stays consistent.

# 11) Practical developer cheat sheet

- **Line 9** = **1z + 2b + 3b + 4b + 5b + 6b + 7a + 8**.
- Use the taxable feeder lines, not their informational companions.
- **Line 7a** and **line 8** may be negative, so line 9 may also be negative.
- Line 9 feeds AGI, but it is **not** AGI.
- Compute line 9 only after all eight feeder lines are final.
- The Form 8814 augmentation at `TaxReturnComputeService.java:949-970` updates line 9 + AGI in place when the child amount lands in Schedule 1 line 8z.
