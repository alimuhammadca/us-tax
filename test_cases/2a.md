## Test 1 — Line 17: Single $80k wages: AMT is null (income below exemption)

**Command (isolated):**

```powershell
cd C:\us-tax\us-tax-be\e2e
npx playwright test --workers=1 --output=test-results-2a-t1 -g "Single \$80k wages: AMT is null" line17-amt.spec.ts
```

**Purpose.** Lock in the Form 6251 exemption mechanic: a Single filer with $80,000 of ordinary wages and no AMT preferences (no Private Activity Bond interest, no incentive stock options, no etc.) has Alternative Minimum Taxable Income below the 2025 Single exemption of $88,100, so the tentative minimum tax is zero, the AMT addition on Form 1040 line 17 is null/zero, and the application should NOT generate Form 6251 for the return. A regression that produced AMT for every filer (e.g., by failing to apply the exemption, or by setting Form 6251 line 1 to gross income instead of taxable income) would be caught here.

## Test 2 — Line 17: Single $200k wages + $100k Private Activity Bond interest: AMT is positive and Form 6251 generated

**Command (isolated):**

```powershell

npx playwright test --workers=1 --output=test-results-2a-t2 -g "Single \$200k wages \+ \$100k PAB interest" line17-amt.spec.ts
```

**Purpose.** Lock in the AMT preference mechanic for Specified Private Activity Bond Interest (the subset of tax-exempt interest reported on 1099-INT box 9 and 1099-DIV box 13). A Single filer with $200,000 of ordinary wages and $100,000 of Private Activity Bond Interest has Alternative Minimum Taxable Income that exceeds the 2025 Single exemption, so the tentative minimum tax is positive and exceeds regular tax — meaning AMT is positive and Form 6251 must be generated and attached to the return.

The test is the principal downstream lock-in for Line 2a's box 9 / box 13 routing. A regression that omitted box 9 from the Form 6251 line 2g feed (or added it to Line 2a instead of routing it through `form6251Line2g`) would change Form 6251 line 2g away from $100,000 and would change AMT from positive to zero or near-zero.

**Notable seeding choice.** The 1099-INT seeds Box 9 ($100,000) WITHOUT seeding Box 8 (Tax-exempt interest). Per IRS Form 1099-INT instructions, Box 9 is a *subset* of Box 8, so a real 1099 would normally have both. The test isolates the AMT preference by seeding only Box 9 — this is acceptable test data even though it does not match a real 1099 statement, because the application's Box 8 / Box 9 carve-out logic processes them independently. The result: Line 2a is computed from Box 8 (which is null, so Line 2a is null), and Form 6251 line 2g is computed from Box 9 (which is $100,000). In a real return with both boxes set, Line 2a would equal Box 8 and Form 6251 line 2g would equal Box 9 — the math the test asserts would be identical.

- `TaxReturnComputeService.java:6814` reads `box9 = parseAmount(entry.get("specifiedPrivateActivityBondInterestAmount"))` = `$100,000`.

- Apply 26% rate, get tentative minimum tax = $50,999.
- Subtract regular tax (approximately $37,067), get AMT (line 11) ≈ $13,932 (subject to rounding).
- Because AMT > 0, Form 6251 is generated and `form6251.line2gPrivateActivityBondInterest` is set to $100,000.

### Implemented follow-up tests (2026-06-02)

All 6 recommended follow-up tests were implemented in the same spec file (`line17-amt.spec.ts`).

**Important correction (2026-06-02).** First-pass hand calculations for these tests omitted a step the application correctly performs: for non-itemizers, the standard deduction is added back to Alternative Minimum Taxable Income (AMTI) at Form 6251 line 2a (the "G1 2025 fix" at `TaxReturnComputeService.java:12081-12090`, applied 2026-04-18). Form 6251 builds line 4 AMTI from line 1b (Adjusted Gross Income minus the non-senior portion of deductions) + line 2a (standard deduction add-back) + line 2g (Private Activity Bond preference) — NOT from taxable income + preferences. Net effect: AMTI = AGI + $100,000 PAB = $300,000, regardless of filing status (since deductions cancel out). After running the suite, the bounds and hand-computation comments were corrected to match the application's correct 2025 behavior.

## Glossary

- **Form 1040** — the standard United States individual federal income tax return.
- **Form 1099-INT** (Interest Income) — the IRS information return reporting interest income.
- **Form 1099-DIV** (Dividends and Distributions) — the IRS information return reporting dividends and exempt-interest dividends from mutual funds.
- **Form 6251** (Alternative Minimum Tax — Individuals) — the form that computes Alternative Minimum Tax (AMT), an alternative tax system that runs parallel to regular income tax. The AMT ensures that high-income filers cannot use preference items to avoid paying any federal tax.
- **Alternative Minimum Tax (AMT)** — the additional tax owed when the tentative minimum tax (computed on a broader income base with fewer preferences) exceeds the regular tax. Reported on Form 1040 line 17 via Schedule 2 line 1.
- **Alternative Minimum Taxable Income (AMTI)** — taxable income recomputed with certain preferences added back and certain deductions disallowed. The base on which the tentative minimum tax is computed.
- **AMT exemption** — the amount subtracted from AMTI before applying the AMT rate. For 2025, $88,100 for Single, $137,000 for Married Filing Jointly, $68,500 for Married Filing Separately, $88,100 for Head of Household, and $137,000 for Qualifying Surviving Spouse. Phased out at higher AMTI.
- **AMT exemption phaseout** — the reduction of the exemption by 25 cents per dollar of AMTI above the phaseout threshold ($626,350 Single / Head of Household, $1,252,700 Married Filing Jointly / Qualifying Surviving Spouse, $626,350 Married Filing Separately, for 2025).
- **AMT rate brackets** — 26% on the first $239,100 (2025) of AMTI in excess of the exemption, and 28% on the remainder.
- **Specified Private Activity Bond (PAB)** — a bond issued by a U.S. state, U.S. territory, or political subdivision for a private purpose (e.g., a privately-operated airport, a stadium, an industrial-development project). Interest from such bonds is exempt from regular income tax under Internal Revenue Code §103 but is treated as a preference item for Alternative Minimum Tax under Internal Revenue Code §57(a)(5).
- **1099-INT box 9 (Specified Private Activity Bond Interest)** — the subset of the box 8 tax-exempt interest that came from Specified Private Activity Bonds. Already included in box 8; the IRS uses box 9 as a flag for AMT preference.
- **1099-DIV box 13 (Specified Private Activity Bond Interest Dividends)** — the analogous subset for mutual fund exempt-interest dividends. Already included in box 12; the IRS uses box 13 as a flag for AMT preference.
- **Tentative minimum tax** — the AMT rate times the AMTI after exemption. The amount the filer would owe if the AMT system were the only tax system. AMT itself is the excess of tentative minimum tax over regular tax.
- **Internal Revenue Code §55** — the statute defining the Alternative Minimum Tax.
- **Internal Revenue Code §57** — the statute listing AMT preference items, including Specified Private Activity Bond Interest at §57(a)(5).
- **Tax Cuts and Jobs Act (TCJA)** — the 2017 federal tax reform legislation that, among other changes, suspended the standard deduction add-back for AMT through tax year 2025 and increased the AMT exemption amounts. Tax year 2025 is still under TCJA rules; sunset (absent congressional action) was anticipated for tax year 2026.
- **Publication 17 — Your Federal Income Tax** — the IRS's master guide for individuals filing Form 1040.
- **Internal Revenue Code** — Title 26 of the United States Code; the statute defining federal tax law.
- **`addNonNull(a, b)`** — application helper that returns `a + b` when both are non-null, the non-null operand when one is null, or `null` when both are null.
- **`subtractNonNegative(a, b)`** — application helper that returns `max(0, a − b)` when both are non-null; handles null appropriately.
- **`roundMoney(value)`** — application helper that rounds a BigDecimal to the nearest dollar (half-up).
