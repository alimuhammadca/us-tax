## Per-test catalog

### 1. line33 — `line33 = line24: neither refund nor amount owed when payments exactly equal tax`

**Spec:** `line33-refund-owed.spec.ts:28`

**Purpose:** Verify the edge case where total payments (line 33) exactly equal total tax (line 24) — neither refund nor amount-owed must be populated.

**Inputs (two-pass fixture to discover the exact tax amount first):**
- Filing status Single, age ≥ 24 (default DOB seeds the taxpayer ID and address).
- Pass 1: W-2 with `wagesTipsOtherCompAmount = 100000`, `federalIncomeTaxWithheldAmount = 0`. `employment-income-taxpayer.hasEmploymentIncome = true`. Compute → record `taxAfterCredits` (line 22) which equals `totalTax` (line 24) for a W-2-only return.
- Pass 2: clear data; re-seed identical fixture with `federalIncomeTaxWithheldAmount = taxAfterCredits` from pass 1. Compute.

**IRS rule (i1040gi_2025 page 100, line 34 paragraph):** "If line 33 is more than line 24, subtract line 24 from line 33. This is the amount you overpaid." Conversely (line 37 paragraph): "If line 24 is more than line 33, subtract line 33 from line 24. This is the amount you owe." When neither inequality holds (line 33 equals line 24 exactly), no overpayment and no balance due. The 2025 Single standard deduction is $15,750; for $100,000 wages, line 15 taxable income = $84,250, regular tax via the 2025 tax computation worksheet ≈ $13,449. Setting withholding to the exact tax produces line 33 = line 24.

**Code path:** `computeLine31ThroughLine38()`. After computing `line33`, the comparison `line33.compareTo(totalTax) > 0` and `totalTax.compareTo(line33) > 0` both return false, so neither the refund branch nor the amount-owed branch sets its respective object on `form1040`. Both `refund` and `amountOwed` remain null.

**Assertions and verdict:**

| # | Assertion | Verdict |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | `firstResult` truthy + `taxAmount > 0` | ✅ OK — establishes the pass-1 tax baseline |
| 2 | `result` truthy after pass 2 | ✅ OK |
| 3 | `refund === null \|\| undefined` | ✅ OK — matches IRS rule (no overpayment) |
| 4 | `amountOwed === null \|\| undefined` | ✅ OK — matches IRS rule (no balance due) |

Net: 4/4 OK. The two-pass pattern correctly handles the discovery of the exact tax amount before the comparison.
