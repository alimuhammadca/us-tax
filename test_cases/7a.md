### Purpose

Lock in the client-side save-guard at `form-capital-gain-loss-taxpayer.component.ts:865` (`isValid()`). When the user affirms "I received a 1099-DIV with capital-gain distributions" AND "I uploaded the 1099-DIV" but no 1099-DIV statement actually exists in the Statements section, the form must refuse to save. This is a **UX guardrail**, not a tax-computation test. The IRS-related rule it implicitly protects is: *the return must not be assembled with received-but-unuploaded capital statements* (otherwise Schedule D / Form 8949 / line 7a values silently understate or omit reportable capital activity).
