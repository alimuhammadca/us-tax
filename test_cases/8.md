**Form 8814 arithmetic crib-sheet** (used repeatedly below):

- `line4 = line1a + line2a + line3`
- `line6 = line4 − $2,700` (only when line4 > $2,700)
- `line7ratio = line2b ÷ line4` (qualified-dividend share)
- `line8ratio = line3 ÷ line4` (cap-gain-distribution share)
- `line9 = line6 × line7ratio` (routes to parent's Form 1040 line 3a AND line 3b — qualified dividends are a subset of ordinary dividends)
- `line10 = line6 × line8ratio` (routes to parent's Form 1040 line 7a / Schedule D line 13)
- `line12 = line6 − line9 − line10` (routes to parent's Schedule 1 line 8z)
- `line14 = min($1,350, line4 − $1,350)` capped non-negative
- `line15 = min($135, 10% × line14)` (routes to parent's Form 1040 line 16 box 1)
- Election denied if `line4 ≥ $13,500`

**Form 8814 qualifying-child gate** (added in 7b.md G2 closure, `TaxReturnComputeService.java:11070`): requires (a) relationship in `EIC_QUALIFYING_RELATIONSHIPS` (Son, Daughter, Stepchild, Brother, Sister, Half brother, Half sister, Stepbrother, Stepsister, Foster child, or descendant of any of those); AND (b) age tests — under 19, OR under 24 + full-time student, OR permanently and totally disabled. A missing DOB conservatively fails the gate.

**Medicaid waiver crib-sheet:** under IRS Notice 2014-7, qualified Medicaid waiver payments to a live-in caregiver are excluded from gross income. The amount enters Form 1040 line 1d AND exits as a negative Schedule 1 line 8s, producing zero net AGI impact. If the IRC §131(c) per-individual cap applies (`>5` qualified individuals), the exclusion is fraction-capped at `5 / N` of the total qualified amount; the cap-excess remains taxable.

**Form 4852 inmate-wages crib-sheet:** inmate wages are reclassified out of Form 1040 line 1a (subtracted to avoid double-count, clamping to zero) and into Schedule 1 line 8u (Wages while incarcerated), making them ineligible for Earned Income Credit per the 2025 line 8u IRS labelling.

**§17 non-overrideable blockers in scope:** the Medicaid waiver Schedule C blocker, the W-2-box-1 overlap blocker, and the §131(c) Code II missing-qualified blocker are all registered in `NonOverrideableFlags.CODES`. Even when the caller passes `overrideFlags=true`, the compute endpoint still returns 409 with the flag — locking in the "no risk of audit or loss" principle.

- **Line 7** (Form 1099-G box 1 unemployment, netted for same-year 2025 repayment) = `$200 − $20 = $180`. Per Publication 525, repayments ≤ $3,000 paid back in the same calendar year as the overpayment are netted directly on line 7.
- **Line 8a** (Net Operating Loss carryover) = `−$10`. Per the IRS line 8a instruction the value must be entered as a negative number; the code negates the absolute value of whatever the user types, so `10` becomes `−10`.

- **Schedule 1 line 10** = `1 + 2a + 5 + 7 + 9 = 100 + 25 + 50 + 180 + 10 = 365`.
- **Form 1040 line 8** = Schedule 1 line 10 = `365`.

### IRS rule that applies
